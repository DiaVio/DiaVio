The driver of V2 admitted to attempting to make a left turn in front of oncoming traffic, misjudging the gap and the speed of V1. This decision error, along with the driver's inattention due to talking to his passenger, led to the collision. Additionally, V2 was the vehicle that cut in front of V1, indicating a failure to yield the right of way. On the other hand, the driver of V1 was traveling below the speed limit and was aware of the oncoming traffic. She stated that V2 cut in front of her before she had a chance to react. The driver of V1 had the right of way and was not at fault for the accident.
