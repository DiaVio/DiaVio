This crash occurred in the early evening on the right roadside of a two-lane, undivided rural roadway. The north/south roadway consisted of one lane in each direction with a posted speed limit of 64kmph (40 mph). The bituminous roadway was dry, straight, and level with no traffic control devices. Traffic flow was normal and there were no adverse atmospheric conditions at the time of the crash.

V1, a 2001 Chevrolet Prizm, was southbound in lane one traveling near the speed limit. V1 drifted off the roadway to the right side and struck a utility pole. After impact, the vehicle rotated clockwise approximately 90 degrees before coming to final rest. The vehicle had to be towed due to damage to its front and right sides. Police Reported Travel Speed is 64 kmph for V1.

The driver of V1, a 60 year-old male, sustained unknown injuries and was taken to the hospital for treatment. There was one other occupant in the vehicle located in the front right seat. The occupant also suffered unknown injuries, and was taken to the hospital for treatment. The driver stated that he became very tired while driving and fell asleep as his vehicle drifted off the roadway.

The Critical Precrash Event for V1 was coded this vehicle traveling, off the edge of the roadway to the right. The Critical Reason for the Critical Precrash Event was coded as a driver related factor; sleeping, that is, actually asleep. Associated factors for this crash include a change in sleep patterns as the driver was unable to take his normal afternoon nap, as well as several physical factors such as strenuous non-work activities (sanding and cleaning his deck the day of the crash) and the fact he was not wearing his prescription lenses that correct a hyperopic (far-sighted condition).
