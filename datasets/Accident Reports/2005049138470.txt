This single vehicle collision occurred mid-morning off the roadway, near the intersection of two, two-way, two lane, undivided roadways. Both roads were dry, traveled concrete. The east bound travel direction had a -1.6% grade. The posted speed limit is 48 kmph (30 mph). There were no adverse weather conditions.

Vehicle one (V1) was a 1991 Mazda 626 driven by a 30 year-old female was traveling eastbound in the first travel lane. V1 departed the roadway on the east edge of the southeast corner, an end departure. The front of V1 struck a tree after crossing a vacant lot. V1 then rotated clockwise and came to rest off the roadway facing southeast. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

The driver was transported to a medical facility from the scene and then later expired so no interview was obtained with the driver. A surrogate interview obtained some information about the driver. Per the police report, the driver became ill while driving and passed out. The driver suffered with asthma. She takes Benadryl medication and had recently begun taking steroids.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: this vehicle traveling, off the edge of the road on the right side. The critical reason for the critical event was coded as a driver related factor: unknown critical non-performance.
