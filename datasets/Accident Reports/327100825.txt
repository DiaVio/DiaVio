This case involves a severe front-left corner impact with a tree. The case occupant is the 26-year-old male driver. He was restrained by a three-point belt and the steering-wheel air bag deployed. The case vehicle, a 2008 Volkswagen GTI 2-door hatchback was westbound on a dry, level, two-lane asphalt roadway. It was daylight and cloudy. As the case vehicle was negotiating the right-sweeping curve, it crossed into the southbound lane and departed the roadway on the left side of the road hitting and knocking down a small wood pole (<10-cm) with its the front-left corner. It then struck a larger tree with its front-left corner. It rotated counterclockwise approximately 40 degrees as it rebounded off the tree and came to rest facing southwest. The 26-year-old male driver was the sole occupant of the vehicle. He was restrained by a three-point belt and the steering-wheel air bag deployed. He was transported to a level-one trauma center and enrolled as a case occupant.
