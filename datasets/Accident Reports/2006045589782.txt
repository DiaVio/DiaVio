Case #2006-45-039

The crash occurred on a north/south two-lane residential minor arterial roadway. The road curved to the left (west) as it traveled southward with a radius of curvature of 375 meters and a negative grade of 3% and superelevation of 4% to the left. The posted speed limit was 80kph (50mph). Conditions were daylight and dry on a weekday morning with an air temperature of 32 degrees at the time of the crash up from the overnight low of 28 degrees. Roads were dry on this particular morning however, a water line had broken just off the west side of the road and water had spilled into the road and frozen near the apex of the curve. Before road crews could apply salt to melt the ice there were three crashes on the curve due to the ice. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

Vehicle #1 was a 1994 Geo Tracker traveling south on the roadway. Near the apex of the curve it crossed the ice and the driver lost control. The Tracker (V1) rotated counterclockwise and departed the left (east) side of the road. It struck a chain link fence that was 5.4 meters off the road with its right side before contacting a utility pole guide wire with its left side. Vehicle #1 came to rest facing northwest against the guide wire. The Tracker (V1) was towed due to damage.

Vehicle #1 was driven by a 23-year old female that was belted. She was transported to a local hospital with minor injuries. Two male children, ages 5 and 3, were restrained in child seats in the second row and were not injured. The vehicle was not equipped with airbags. The driver stated she lost control because of the ice on the road. Police charged her with driving without a valid license.

The Critical Precrash Event for Vehicle #1 was this vehicle control loss due to poor road conditions (ice). The Critical Reason for the Critical Event was an environment factor, slick roads. There were no other associated factors.
