This two-vehicle crash occurred on a grass median-divided, straight trafficway. There are three lanes in each direction. The westbound lanes have a five percent uphill grade and the eastbound lanes have a five percent downhill grade. The speed limit for this roadway is 72 kmph (45 mph). It was dark with no streetlights on at the time of the crash with no adverse atmospheric conditions.

Vehicle one (V1), a 2003 Jeep Liberty, was westbound in lane three. Vehicle two (V2), a 1993 Toyota Camry, was eastbound in an unknown eastbound lane. V2 traveled off the left side of the road and through the median. V2 then entered the westbound lanes and the front struck the front of V1. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Unknown for V1 and Unknown for V2.

The driver of V1 was a thirty-six-year-old female and the driver of V2 was a twenty-four-year-old male traveling with two passengers, a nineteen-year-old female and a nineteen-year-old male. V2 driver's autopsy indicates positive results for amphetamine, cannabinoids and methamphetamine. Neither driver nor the passengers were interviewed; no further information is available for these drivers.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "other vehicle encroachment; from opposite direction - over left lane line." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was "not coded to this vehicle."

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded as "this vehicle traveling; over the lane line on left side of travel lane." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded as "driver related factor; type of driver error unknown."
