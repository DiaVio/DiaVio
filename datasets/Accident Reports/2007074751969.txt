This single-vehicle crash occurred near a four-legged intersection. The east/west roadway is undivided, straight, has an uphill grade and consists of one lane in each direction. The eastbound travel lane is controlled by a stop sign. The speed limit for this roadway is 40 kmph (25 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.

Vehicle one (V1), a 2005 Kia Spectra, was eastbound in lane one. V1 turned right at the intersection and traveled over the curbed median on the north/south roadway. The back left wheel struck the curb. V1 continued to the south and the front struck a signpost on the median. V1 continued to the southwest and off the right side of the southbound lane. The front of V1 struck a utility pole. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

The driver of V1, a twenty-year-old male, had just picked up his two female teenage friends from work and they were on the way to the friend's house. This driver was healthy, well rested and comfortable with his vehicle and passengers. He rarely travels this route. This driver stated that he was thinking about a personal problem prior to the crash. He also stated that he was being pursued by another vehicle. A witness to the crash stated that there was no vehicle following V1 and that V1 was speeding when it approached the intersection.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "this vehicle traveling; off the edge of the road on the left side." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "driver related factor; too fast for curve/turn." This driver's speed, his failure to obey a traffic control device, his focus on a personal problem and his poor directional control were associated factors in this crash.
