This crash occurred in the center median of a four-lane, controlled access, interstate highway. The north/south highway consisted of two lanes in each direction divided by a wide grassy center median with a metal guardrail running down the center. The bituminous highway was dry, straight, and level with a posted speed limit of 113 kmph (70 mph). At the time of the mid-day, mid-week crash the weather was clear and the traffic volume was moderate.

V1, a 2002 Ford Focus 4 door, was southbound in lane two of the above described roadway. As V1 began to pass a non-contact medium/heavy truck that was southbound in lane 1, the non-contact vehicle began encroaching into V1's lane. The driver of V1 steered left to avoid the truck; departed the highway to the left onto the median. The front left of V1 struck the median guardrail and rotated counterclockwise coming to rest in the median facing north-northeast. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

The driver of V1, a 42-year-old female, was accompanied by 12-month-old female passenger in a child restraint in the backseat. She advised that she was traveling at the posted speed and was familiar with the roadway. She was not in a hurry and she was coming from work, which she had just resumed after a vacation.
