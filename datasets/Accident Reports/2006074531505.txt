This two-vehicle crash occurred on a controlled-access, straight, divided roadway. There are four travel lanes with an 8% uphill grade in the area of the crash. There are paved shoulders on both sides of the travel lanes. The speed limit on this roadway is 89 kmph (55 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with rush hour congestion and no adverse atmospheric conditions.

Vehicle one (V1), a 2000 Chrysler Concorde, was westbound in lane one. Vehicle two (V2), a 1990 Ford Tempo, was parked and unoccupied on the right shoulder. V1 traveled onto the shoulder and the front struck the back of V2. V1 then rotated clockwise and traveled down a grass embankment. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The driver of V1, a sixty-two-year-old male, was traveling from work to his home. He was familiar with his vehicle and this roadway. This driver had bronchitis for two days and was taking an antibiotic and Dayquil prior to the crash. He stated that he had recently learned that his brother has terminal cancer. This driver stated that he was glancing at the vehicles to his left at the time of the crash.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: this vehicle traveling; over the lane line on right side of travel lane. The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as a driver related factor: external distraction.
