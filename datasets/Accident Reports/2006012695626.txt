The crash occurred off the south side of a three-lane roadway. There was one eastbound lane and one westbound lane divided by a left-turn lane. This was a straight, bituminous roadway with a posted speed limit of 40 kmph (25mph) and an uphill grade. It was afternoon on a weekday the road was dry and the sky was clear. Traffic was light.

V1, a 1992 Chevrolet Lumina Van with one occupant had just turned right from a side street. She was traveling eastbound in lane one. The driver stated that her door had come open while driving. She tried to pull the door shut and continue driving, but lost control. V1, departed the roadway on the right side and contacted it's front to a bridge. The driver of V1 fell out of the vehicle and it continued to fall down the embankment and roll right five quarter turns. V1 came to final rest on its on it's right side in the river. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

The driver of V1 was a 16-year old female who reported minor injuries, and was treated on-scene. She advised that she had just left her mother's work and was on her way home. She advised that she was traveling between 18-32 kmph (11-20 mph) prior to her door coming open and her loosing control.

The Critical Precrash Event for the driver of V1 was “this vehicle traveling: off the edge of the road on the right side”. The Critical Reason for the Critical Precrash Event was “interior distraction”. Some associated factors for this vehicle were, vehicle deficiency door latch, the drivers inexperience, insufficient braking and steering, and a wheel deficiency. It should be noted that the researcher had inspected the latch on the door and found it was not working. This means that the driver was holding the door for the entire trip.
