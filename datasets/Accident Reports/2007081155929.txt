This multiple-vehicle crash occurred a in the early morning daylight hours .The location was in the northbound lanes of a busy interstate highway. This roadway was a bituminous four-lane divided roadway. The weather was raining, and the roadway was wet with poor visibility. The posted speed limit was 97 kmph (60 mph).

Vehicle one (V1) was a 2004 Toyota Corolla sedan driven by a forty four (44) year old male with one passenger. V1 was traveling northbound in lane three, and preparing to change lanes to the left into the car-pool lane. The driver of V1 checked, saw nothing and proceeded. He steered to his left into the right side of vehicle two (V2), a Toyota Tacoma pickup driven by a forty six (46) year old male with one passenger. V2 was traveling at a much higher rate of speed. V2's driver steered to the left, then came back to the right and proceeded to hit the left side rear of vehicle three (V3), a 2001 Chevrolet Silverado pickup driven by a fifty (50) year old male. V3 was used as a lunch wagon and was stopped in its traffic lane. V2 then drove up on the left side of vehicle four (V4), a 1998 Lincoln Town Car driven by a thirty three (33) year old male, who also had stopped in the travel lane. V2 finally came to rest after crashing into the rear of vehicle five (V5), a 1990 GMC Sierra pickup driven by a forty seven (47) year old female who was stopped in her travel lane. V5 was pushed forward after the rear impact with V2 and subsequently contacted vehicle six (V6) in its rear plane. V6 a 1986 Nissan pickup driven by a forty eight (48) year old female that was stopped in its travel lane. V1 was driven from the scene. V2, V4, V5 and V6 were towed due to damage. The police report did not indicate the tow status of V3. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: this vehicle traveling, over the lane line on the left side of travel lane. The critical reason was coded as a driver related factor: inadequate surveillance (e.g., failed to look, looked but did not see).

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded: other vehicle encroachment; from adjacent lane (same direction) - over right lane line. The critical reason was not coded to this vehicle.

V3, V4, V5 and V6 were not involved in the first harmful event. The critical reason was not coded to any of these vehicles.
