This two-vehicle crash occurred on a jersey barrier-divided, straight trafficway. The eastbound lanes are level and consist of four through lanes with narrow shoulders on both sides. The speed limit for this roadway is 89 kmph (35 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions. Traffic was congested at the time of the crash.

Vehicle one (V1), a 2007 Honda Fit, was eastbound in lane three. Vehicle two (V2), a 2000 Chevrolet C1500 pickup, was eastbound in lane three in front of V1. The front of V1 struck the back of V2. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The driver of V1, a twenty-one-year-old female, was on her way from her home to an appointment with her college professor. She was healthy, well rested and familiar with her vehicle. She travels this route weekly. This driver stated that she "blacked out" prior to the crash.

The driver of V2, a thirty-nine-year-old male, and his twenty-one-year-old male passenger were on their way from their work headquarters to a customer's house. This driver was well rested, healthy and comfortable with his vehicle and passenger. He travels this route daily. This driver stated that he did not see V1 prior to the crash.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "other motor vehicle in lane; traveling in same direction with lower steady speed." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "driver related factor; too fast for conditions - congested traffic."

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded as "other motor vehicle in lane; traveling in same direction with higher speed." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V2 was "not coded to this vehicle."
