This two-vehicle crash occurred in a four-legged, traffic signal-controlled intersection. Both roadways are undivided, straight and consist of one lane in each direction with parking lanes on both sides. The eastbound approach has a downhill grade. The westbound approach has an eight percent uphill grade. The speed limit on this roadway is 48 kmph (30 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.

Vehicle one (V1), a 1997 Dodge Caravan, was eastbound in lane one. Vehicle two (V2) was westbound in lane one. No information is available for this vehicle because it left the scene immediately after the crash. V2 turned left in the intersection and the front struck the front of V1. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The driver of V1, a twenty-year-old male, was traveling with an eighteen-year-old female, from a store to their home. This driver was well rested, healthy and comfortable with his vehicle and passenger. He travels on this roadway daily. This driver stated that he had no indication that V2 was going to turn left and therefore he did not attempt any avoidance maneuvers.

No information is available for the driver of V2.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: other vehicle encroachment; from opposite direction - over left lane line. The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was not coded to this vehicle.

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded: this vehicle traveling; turning left at intersection. The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded as a driver related factor: type of driver error unknown.
