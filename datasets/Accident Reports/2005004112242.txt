The crash occurred off the edge of a two-lane roadway to the right. Conditions were dark with minimal lighting on a dry road in the evening on a weekday. The posted speed limit was 40 kmph (25 mph).

V1 was a 2004 Chevy Cavalier coupe traveling eastbound in the right lane approaching a stop sign with a left turn only. V1 did not see the stop sign and swerved left and hit a tree that was off the right side of the roadway. V1 came to final rest up against a tree and a fence facing eastbound. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

V1 was driven by an 18-year-old male who was not injured. He stated that he was looking for his friend's house and did not see the stop sign. The driver applied the brakes and steered left but lost control and the front of V1 struck a tree and a fence. V1 was traveling between 34 and 48 kmph (21 and 30 mph) at the time of impact. V1 was towed due to damage.

The Critical Precrash Event was this vehicle traveling off the edge of the road on the right side. The Critical Reason for the Critical Precrash Event was a decision error, traveling too fast for conditions. Associated factors coded to this vehicle were driver recognition factors, conversing with friends, looking for address, unfamiliar with roadway and being an inexperienced driver. He had only been driving for 7 months. Highway related factors include the road being poorly lit and no sign telling you of a sharp left curve ahead.
