This single-vehicle crash occurred on a jersey barrier-divided, straight trafficway. The westbound lanes in the precrash area are straight, have a three percent uphill grade and consist of five through lanes. The speed limit for this roadway is 97 kmph (60 mph). Traffic was congested at the time of the crash. This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.

Vehicle one (V1), a 1995 Chevrolet Lumina, was westbound in lane five. A non-contact vehicle (NCV) was westbound in lane four. The NCV began to change lanes to the left. The driver of V1 steered left and traveled onto the left shoulder. The driver steered right, reentering the roadway, and then steered abruptly left. V1 rotated counterclockwise and traveled off the left side of the road. The front and then the right side struck the jersey barrier. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

The driver of V1, a twenty-five-year-old female, was on her way home from work. She was healthy, well rested and familiar with her vehicle. She travels this route daily. This driver stated that she "lost control."

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "this vehicle traveling; off the edge of the road on the left side." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "driver related factor; poor directional control (e.g., failing to control vehicle with skill ordinarily expected)."
