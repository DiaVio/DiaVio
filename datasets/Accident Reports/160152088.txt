This frontal crash involves a 22-year-old unrestrained male without air bag deployment who sustained serious injury. The crash occurred during nighttime hours in dry and clear weather. The collision occurred on a two lane, two-way bituminous roadway. The collision occurred on a straight, two lane, two-way roadway with no traffic control devices in the area. The case vehicle (V1) was a 2005 Mitsubishi Lancer sedan that was being driven eastbound. The crash occurred when V1 encountered a large horse in the roadway and struck the horse with the front of his vehicle. The body of the horse struck the windshield and windshield header before falling off the vehicle and coming to rest in the roadway. After this event, V1 went off the roadway to the left where it traveled up an embankment and struck a fence and the three small trees with its front. V1 came to final rest off the roadway to the left next to the three trees. V1 was being driven by a 23-year-old male case occupant who was unrestrained and did not have the available steering wheel mounted air bag deploy. The case occupant was seriously injured and was transported to a level-one trauma center by helicopter for treatment.
