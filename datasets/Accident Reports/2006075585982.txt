This two-vehicle crash occurred on a straight, four-leg, residential intersection. South and northbound traffic has one lane in each direction with stop signs for controls. East and westbound traffic has one bike lane, one through lane and one left turn lane in each direction with no controls. The speed limit for southbound traffic is 40 kph (25 mph) and for eastbound traffic is 48 kph (30 mph) and there were no adverse weather conditions at the time.

Vehicle one, a 1993 Honda Accord, was stopped southbound in lane one. Vehicle two, a 1998 Ford F-150, was eastbound in lane one going straight. Vehicle two made a wide right turn from the stop sign. Vehicle one impacted with its front to vehicle two's left side. Vehicle one rotated counter clockwise and came to rest on its wheels facing east in the bike lane. After the impact, vehicle two rotated counter clockwise and partially entered the bike lane. Vehicle two, still rotating counter clockwise-entered lane one again and entered into the left turn lane of westbound travel. Vehicle one was driven from the scene. Vehicle two was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Unknown for V1 and Unknown for V2.

The driver of vehicle one, a sixty-two year old female was not interviewed. There is no known sleep or driving information for this driver. This driver was not transported for injuries so there is no know medical information.

The driver of vehicle two, a twenty-two year old male was heading from his home to the store. This driver had been driving for about ten minutes at the time of the crash. This driver drives this vehicle daily and over this road on a daily basis.

The critical pre-crash event for vehicle one was coded as “this vehicle traveling: turning right at intersection”. The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event was attributed to this vehicle but due to a lack of interview and medical information was coded as “driver related factor: type of driver error unknown”.
