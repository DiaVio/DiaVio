This three-vehicle crash occurred on a divided, level, straight, access-controlled roadway. The southbound lanes consist of five through lanes with shoulders on both sides. The speed limit on this roadway is 97 kmph (60 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions. Traffic was heavy due to the morning rush hour.

Vehicle one (V1), a 1998 Mercury Mystique, was southbound in lane four. V2, a 2006 Subaru Tribeca, was southbound in lane three. V1, a 2000 Toyota Camry, was in lane three in front of V2. The traffic in lane four slowed down and V1 moved into lane three after looking to the rear and the right to ensure the lane was clear. When the driver of V1 realized that V2 and V3 were slowing for the traffic ahead, he applied the brakes in an attempt to avoid a collision. The front of V1 struck the back of V2. V2 was pushed forward and the front of V2 struck the back of V3. V1 and V2 were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The driver of V1, a twenty-nine-year-old male, was on his way from home to work. He was healthy and familiar with his vehicle. He drives this route daily. This driver stated that he was "tired and late for work." He was in a hurry and did not notice that the traffic in lane three was slowing.

The driver of V2, a fifty-five-year-old male, was on his way from home to work. He was healthy and comfortable with his vehicle. He drives this route daily.

The driver of V3, a twenty-two-year-old female, was on her way from home to work. She has been diagnosed with depression and takes Prozac as well as birth control pills. She is otherwise healthy and was well rested at the time of the crash. She was very familiar with her vehicle and her route.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "other motor vehicle in lane; traveling in same direction while decelerating." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "driver related factor; misjudgment of gap or other's speed."

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded as "other motor vehicle in lane; traveling in same direction with higher speed."

The critical pre-crash event for V3 was coded as "other; not involved in first harmful event." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V2 and V3 was coded as "not coded to this vehicle."
