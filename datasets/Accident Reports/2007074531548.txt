This single-vehicle crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions. The roadway is undivided, straight, level and consists of one lane in each direction with a parking lane on each side. The speed limit for this roadway is 48 kmph (30 mph).

Vehicle one (V1), a 1998 Plymouth Neon, was northbound in lane one. V1 traveled off the left side of the roadway and the front left wheel struck a curb. V1 continued on and the front struck a building, a newspaper vending machine and a soda vending machine. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

The driver of V1, a twenty-four-year-old male, had just dropped his father off and was on his way to pick up his mother. He was familiar with the vehicle but rarely travels this route. This driver is prescribed Depakote for his epilepsy, but stated that he was not taking it three times each day as prescribed. He also stated that he had been drinking the night before and was "still a little hung over" and felt fatigued. He also stated that it was hot outside and he felt began to feel ill. He further stated that he turned the radio up loud to keep himself awake but remembered the vehicle "drifting" prior to his blacking out due to an epileptic seizure.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "this vehicle traveling; off the edge of the road on the left side." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "driver related factor; heart attack or other physical impairment of the ability to act." This driver's fatigue and failure to take his medication as prescribed were associated factors in this crash.
