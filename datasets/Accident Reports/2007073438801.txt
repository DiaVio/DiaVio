This single-vehicle crash occurred at night with streetlights on with no adverse atmospheric conditions. The roadway is level, undivided, curved to the left (the radius of curvature is 50 meters) and consists of one lane in each direction. The speed limit for this roadway is 32 kmph (20 mph).

Vehicle one (V1), a 2004 Chevrolet Malibu, was southbound in lane one. V1 traveled off the right side of the roadway, skidding longitudinally. The right side of V1 struck a tree. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is No PAR obtained (created) for V1.

The driver of V1, a fifteen-year-old female, was traveling with two sixteen-year-old female passengers in the second seat. She was healthy and well rested. This unlicensed driver had borrowed her mother's car without permission and was in a hurry to get home before her mother woke up. She had six months of driving experience and stated that she did not feel comfortable with her passengers. This driver further stated that she was talking to and looking at her passengers when V1 traveled off the road. She steered left and applied the brakes in an attempt to avoid the crash.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "this vehicle control loss due to traveling too fast for conditions." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as " driver related factor; too fast for curve/turn." This driver's inexperience, her distraction caused by her passengers, her emotional state and her poor directional control were associated factors in this crash.
