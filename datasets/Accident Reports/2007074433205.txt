This two-vehicle crash occurred on a straight, level, undivided roadway that consists of one lane in each direction with a guardrail on the west side. This roadway was wet with icy patches at the time of the crash. The speed limit for this roadway is 56 kmph (35 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.

Vehicle one (V1), a 2002 Nissan Pathfinder, was southbound in lane one. Vehicle two (V2), a 1992 Geo Metro, was northbound in lane one. V1 lost control as it traveled over a bridge and moved into the opposing travel lane. The driver of V1 steered right as V1 approached the guardrail. V1 rotated clockwise and the left side struck the guardrail. V1 moved to the southeast and the front struck the front of V2. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The driver of V1, a forty-seven-year-old female, was running some work errands and had just left a bank. She was well rested, healthy and familiar with her vehicle. She travels this route monthly. This driver stated that she was unable to control her vehicle once she hit the ice patch.

The driver of V2, a fifty-seven-year-old male, was on his way from a restaurant to work. He was healthy, well rested and familiar with his vehicle. This driver stated that his crash with V1 "happened so fast" and he did not have time to avoid the crash.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "this vehicle control loss due to poor road conditions (wet road with icy patches)." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "environment related factor; slick roads (low friction road surface due to ice, loose debris, any other cause)."

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded as "not involved first harmful event." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V2 was "not coded to this vehicle."
