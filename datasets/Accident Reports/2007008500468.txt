This crash occurred in the southbound lanes of a six-lane roadway divided by a grass median. There were three southbound lanes and three northbound lanes. This was a straight roadway with a slight uphill grade. The roadway was bituminous with a posted speed limit of 89 kmph (55 mph). It was mid evening on a weekday, traffic was heavy, and conditions were clear.

V1 was a 2004 Toyota Corolla, driven by a 24 year-old female. V1 was traveling southbound in lane one of the roadway engaged in a phone conversation on her cell phone when she lost control of her vehicle and it suddenly left the roadway on the right side. It began to rotate clockwise as it traveled through a field and impacted a light pole on the driver's side door. The driver was pronounced dead at the scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is Unknown for V1.

The Critical Precrash Event for V1 was "this vehicle traveling, off the edge of the road on the right side." The Critical Reason for the Critical Precrash Event was "driver related factor, internal distraction." The police reported that the driver was talking on her cell phone at the time of the crash. The witness stated that there were no outside factors that would have made the driver swerve off of the road so suddenly, even though the traffic flow was heavy.
