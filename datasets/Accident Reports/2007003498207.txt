The crash occurred on an east/west 4-lane undivided roadway with two lanes and a bicycle lane in both directions. This was a straight, level, bituminous roadway with a speed limit of 48 kmph (30 mph). It was the morning hours of a weekday, and the road was dry and it was clear with no adverse conditions.

V1, a 2006 Chrysler Sebring four door sedan with one occupant, was traveling westbound in lane one. V2, a 2003 Chevrolet Cavalier four door sedan with one occupant was facing west in the parking lane and attempted to make a U-turn. As V2 pulled out, the front of V1 impacted the left side of V2. Both vehicles came to final rest shortly after impact. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

V1, the Chrysler Sebring, was driven by a 21 year-old male who reported minor injuries and was transported to a medical facility. The driver confirmed to the police that V2 was making a U-turn when they collided.

The Critical Pre-crash Event for V1 was "Other vehicle encroachment, from adjacent lane (same direction) - over right lane line." The Critical Reason for the Critical Event was not coded to this vehicle. The driver had the right of way, and possible associated factors were coded unknown because no interview was obtained.

V2, the Chevrolet Cavalier, was driven by a 24 year-old male who reported minor injuries but refused medical attention. He stated to the police that he was making a U-turn when he collided with V1. The driver was issued a citation for an unknown violation.

The Critical Pre-crash Event for V2 was "This vehicle traveling over the lane line on left side of travel lane." The Critical Reason for the Critical Event was coded as an unknown driver error. The driver did not have the right of way, and an associated factor coded to the driver was an illegal maneuver, because he initiated an illegal turn. Other possible associated factors were coded unknown because no interview was obtained.
