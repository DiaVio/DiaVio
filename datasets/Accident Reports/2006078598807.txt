This two-vehicle crash that occurred during early morning daylight hours at a four-way intersection that is controlled by posted stop signs for the east/west traffic. There is one northbound and one southbound lane. The speed limit on both roadways is posted 80 kmph (50 mph). The road surface were dry, level asphalt and the sky was clear.

Vehicle one (V1), a 1995 Chrysler Cirrus Lx sedan driven by a 15 year-old female with two passengers, was traveling eastbound approaching the intersection. Vehicle two (V2), a 1977 Chevrolet Flatbed Utility pickup driven by a 48 year-old male with two passengers, was traveling northbound through the intersection at about 56 kmph (35 mph). V1 traveled into the intersection and was struck on the right side by the front of V2. The impact caused V1 to rotate counterclockwise. The right side of V1 side slapped the left side of V2. V1 continued to travel through the intersection in a northeasterly direction. V1 traveled off the edge of the roadway and came to rest off the east edge of the north/south roadway to the north of the intersection facing in a northeasterly direction. V2 rotated clockwise and continued to travel in a northeasterly direction through the intersection and came to rest in the intersection facing a northeasterly direction. Both vehicles were towed from the scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is 24 kmph for V1 and 24 kmph for V2.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: this vehicle traveling crossing over passing through intersection. The critical reason was coded to V1 as a driver related factor: misjudgment of gap or other's speed. An additional factors for V1: the driver was not licensed, inexperienced, pregnant and talking with her passenger.

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded: other vehicle encroachment; from crossing street, across path. The critical reason was not coded to this vehicle.
