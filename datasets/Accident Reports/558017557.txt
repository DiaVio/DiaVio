The case vehicle, a 2002 Chevrolet Cavalier sedan was northbound on a two-lane roadway and stopped at a four-leg intersection which is a four-way stop controlled by stop signs. Vehicle two (V2), a 1991 Oldsmobile Cutlass Calais was eastbound on the intersecting roadway approaching the intersection. Vehicle three (V3), a 1990 Nissan Maxima was westbound and stopped at the same intersection. The case vehicle entered the intersection and was struck on the left side by V2. The impact caused the case vehicle to rotate slightly counterclockwise and yaw to the right and strike the left-front corner of V3. After the second minor impact the case vehicle rotated slightly clockwise before coming to rest at the northeast corner of the intersection. V2 rotated counterclockwise and came to rest on the east side of the intersection, in the eastbound lane.

The case occupant is the unrestrained 52-year-old male driver. The vehicle was not equipped with a side-impact airbag. He was the sole occupant of the vehicle. He was transported to a to a level-one trauma center.
