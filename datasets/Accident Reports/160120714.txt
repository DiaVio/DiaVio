Vehicle 1 (V1) is a 2000 Chrysler LHS, four-door (case study vehicle). Vehicle 2 (V2) is a 2002 Toyota Camry, four-door. V1 was occupied by a female, age 44 as its driver (case study participant). The case study participant was wearing the available lap and shoulder belt. The frontal air bags deployed as a result of impact. The crash occurred during the hours of dusk. The weather at the time of the crash was clear and dry. The roadway surface was dry. The posted speed limit is 45 mph (72 kmph). The crash occurred on a five lane divided trafficway. The trafficway is divided by a raised concrete median. The trafficway travels in a north-south direction. The northbound direction consists of two through lanes and a dedicated left turn lane. The southbound direction consists of two through lanes. The northbound direction has a left handed curve and the southbound direction has a right hand curve. The roadway is level in the area of the crash. V1 was traveling southbound in the left through lane, where the driver was reported to be possibly ill. V2 was traveling northbound in the left through lane. V1 crossed into the northbound lanes. V1's front struck the front of V2. The impact caused V1 to rotate clockwise and come to rest in the right through lane of the northbound lanes. V2 rotated clockwise coming to rest in the dedicated left turn lane of the northbound lanes. The case study participant was transported by air to a trauma center.
