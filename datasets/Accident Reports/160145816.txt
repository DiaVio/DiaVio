This frontal crash involves an unrestrained 35-year-old female driver with frontal air bag deployment who sustained serious injury. The crash occurred during daylight hours in dry and clear weather. The collision occurred on a four lane, one-way roadway, part of a seven lane, divided trafficway. The case vehicle (V1) is a 2003 Chrysler Sebring sedan and was being driven west in lane three. The crash occurred when the driver of V1 fell asleep and drifted into the center median where she struck a pole attached to a traffic light with her front. V1 came to final rest in the median. V1 was being driven by a 35-year-old female case study occupant who was the sole occupant of the vehicle. The driver of V1 was unrestrained but both frontal air bags deployed during impact. The case occupant was seriously injured and was transported to a level-one trauma center for treatment.
