The crash occurred in the northbound lanes of an intersection. This was a five-lane roadway with two northbound lanes, two southbound lanes and a left-turn lane. This was a level, bituminous roadway with a posted speed of 89 kmph (55 mph). The northbound side of the intersection had a 2% curve to the left and the southbound side was straight. It was late evening on a weekday and the road was dry and the sky was cloudy. Traffic was flowing.

V1, a 2005 Chevrolet Equinox four-door with one occupant was traveling southbound in lane three. V2, a 2001 Ford Escape four-door with three occupants was traveling northbound in lane two. V1 was following the car in front of him and attempted to turn left at the intersection. The driver of V1 did not realize that he did not have the right-of-way. V1 followed the vehicle through the intersection. The driver of V2 stated that as she was approaching the intersection she saw the vehicle ahead of V1 turning and tapped her brakes. She did not see V1 until it was already in her path. The front of V2 contacted the right of V1 before they both came to final rest in the roadway. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

V1, the Chevrolet Equinox, was driven by a 20-year old male who reported minor injuries, but refused treatment. He advised that he had gone with his mother to pick-up her car from work. They had left her work and he was following her home. He advised that he was traveling between 18-32 kmph (11-20 mph) prior to being contacted. He did not attempt any avoidance actions because he did not see V2 until he was contacted.

The Critical Precrash Event for the driver of V1 was this vehicle traveling: turning left at intersection. The Critical Reason for the Critical Precrash Event was a driver related error: External distraction because he was focusing on the car ahead of him instead of the roadway. An associated factor for V1 was a sight restriction from the vehicle he was following.

V2, the Ford Escape was driven by a 41-year old female, who reported injuries and was transported to a local trauma facility. She advised that her and her family had just left dinner and was on their way to Staples. She advised that she was traveling between 67-80 kmph (41-50 mph) when she made the evasive action of applying the brakes. She could not avoid contacting V1. An associated factor was a sight restriction from the vehicle in front of V1.

The Critical Precrash Event for the driver of V2 was other vehicle encroachment: from opposite direction over left lane line. The Critical Reason was not coded to this vehicle.
