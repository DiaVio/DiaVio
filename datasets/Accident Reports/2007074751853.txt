This two vehicle crash occurred in the early morning of a snowy, foggy day in darkness. The roadway was a 3-lane, one-way, wet asphalt roadway with a posted speed limit of 56 kmph (35 mph) and a grade of 5%. At the time of the crash a thick fog had set in, along with light snow.

Vehicle one (V1), a 2003 Chevrolet Cavalier 2 door coupe driven by a thirty eight (38) year old female, was traveling east in lane three. Vehicle two (V2), a 2003 Ford Explorer SUV driven by a thirty three (33) year old male, was traveling east in lane two. As both vehicles approached the intersection, V2 turned left from the second lane into the path of V1. The front of V1 contacted the left side of V2. After impact V1 came to final rest in the intersection, V2 came to a controlled stop on the east side of the northbound lanes. V1 was towed due to damage. V2 was driven from the scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: other vehicle encroachment; from adjacent lane (same direction) - over right lane line. The critical reason was not coded to this vehicle. Medications taken by the driver include Ibuprofen and Vitamins.

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded: this vehicle traveling, turning left at intersection. The critical reason for the critical event was coded to V2 as a driver related factor: inadequate surveillance (e.g., failed to look, looked but did not see). An associated factor for V2's driver was inattention- he explained that he was on his way from home going to get breakfast at a store, but thought he was on his way to work. None of the lanes of this one-way street are designated or marked as turn lanes at this intersection. This driver tried to turn from the middle travel lane across the path of V2, an illegal turn from the wrong lane. This driver stated that he never saw V1 prior to initiating the turn.
