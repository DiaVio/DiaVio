This two-vehicle crash occurred in a four-legged, traffic signal-controlled intersection. The east/west trafficway is straight, divided by a curbed median and consists of two lanes in each direction with a left turn lane at the intersection. Both approaches have a five percent downhill grade. The roadways were wet at the time of the crash. The speed limit for this roadway is 64 kmph (40 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.

Vehicle one (V1), a 2004 Ford F150 pickup, was eastbound in lane two. Vehicle two (V2), a 2004 Chevrolet Cavalier, was westbound in lane three. V2 turned left in the intersection and the right side was struck by the front of V1. V2 rotated clockwise and the left side struck a street sign. V2 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The driver of V1, a fifty-one-year-old male, had left work and was on his way to pick up his wife to take her for a routine medical test. He was healthy, well rested and familiar with his vehicle. He travels this route daily. He takes the medication Allegra. This driver stated that he saw V2 but thought that the driver would stop.

The driver of V2, a twenty-one-year-old female, was not interviewed. No further information is available for this driver.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "other vehicle encroachment; from opposite direction - over left lane line." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was "not coded to this vehicle." This driver's assumption that V2 would yield the right-of-way was an associated factor in this crash.

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded as "this vehicle traveling; turning left at intersection." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded as "driver related factor; type of driver error unknown."
