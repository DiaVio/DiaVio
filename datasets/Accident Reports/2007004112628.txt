This crash occurred in the early morning of a weekday on the roadside of a 4-lane roadway with a divided concrete median. The east/west roadway had two lanes going in each direction. The bituminous roadway was a wet, straight, and level with a posted speed limit of 80 kmph (50 mph). The traffic flow was congested due to rush hour and there were on-and-off rain showers all morning and the skies were cloudy at the time of the crash. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

V1, a 1998 Ford Expedition, large utility vehicle, was traveling southbound in lane two intending on making a left turn at the intersection. The driver made a left at the intersection and after completing the turn skidded clockwise, departing the roadway and striking a utility pole on the left front side of the vehicle. V1 came to final rest at the original point of impact with the pole.

V1 was driven by a 22-year-old female who reportedly had a small abrasion on her left hand and was treated on scene but refused to be transported to the hospital. The driver was an unlicensed driver. The driver was leaving home and on her way to work. When she was negotiating the turn she began to lose control and attempted to brake and steer to left to avoid going off the roadway but was unsuccessful and struck a pole.

The Critical Pre-crash Event for V1 was coded this vehicle traveling, turning left at intersection. The Critical Reason for the Critical Pre-crash Event was coded as, "too fast for conditions-wet roads". The only associated factors that were coded to this driver were rush hour congestion, wet roads, and rain and cloudy weather conditions.
