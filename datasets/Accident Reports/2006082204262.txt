This two vehicle collision occurred shortly before noon during daylight hours. The collision occurred in an intersection with a stop sign for the North/South lanes only. The pre-crash roadway is straight, dry and level. Both roadways are paved with concrete. There is a posted speed limit of 48 kmph (30 mph) for both vehicles.

Vehicle one (V1), a 1975 Dodge Dart driven by a 70 year-old male with one passenger, was traveling southbound on the roadway in lane one wanting to go straight at the intersection. Vehicle two (V2), a 1998 Nissan Pathfinder driven by a 59 year-old male with one passenger, was traveling westbound in lane 1. V1 was stopped at the sign but was unable to see around the big box truck parked on the north/east corner. V1 continued to roll forward for a better view of the westbound traffic. Not seeing V2, V1 entered the intersection. The front of V2 impacted the left front panel and the driver door of V1 causing them to deflect and come to rest on the south/west corner. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Driver and passenger of V1 and driver of V2 were transported to a local medical facility. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: this vehicle traveling; crossing over (passing through) intersection. The critical reason for the critical event was coded to V1 as an environment related factor: view obstructed by other vehicle.

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded: other vehicle encroachment: from crossing street, across path. The critical reason for the critical event is not coded to this vehicle. This vehicle's driver also was effected by the sight line obstruction of the parked vehicle.
