This single vehicle crash occurred shortly after noon during daylight hours on a two-way road in an urban residential neighborhood. The road is not physically divided two-way traffic and has no deficiencies noted. Road surface asphalt, level and dry. The posted speed limit is 32 kmph (20 mph).

Vehicle one (V 1), a 1981 Chevrolet Cheyenne pick-up truck with one occupant, was traveling east bound when the driver drove off the left side of the road and into a building. The front of vehicle one crashed into the building. According to the police on scene, the driver fled the scene with unknown injuries, and V 1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is No PAR obtained (created) for V1.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: this vehicle traveling off the edge of the road on the left side. The critical reason was coded as: type of driver error unknown. Although the driver was not interviewed, physical evidence indicate the vehicle was tracking when it departed the roadway to the left. The bed of the truck was loaded with miscellaneous metal items, most likely being taken to repair or be sold for scrap. There was no indication that a load shift or tire problem occurred prior to impact, although the right rear tire was underinflated. Poor directional control of the vehicle was exhibited by the driver for departing the roadway suddenly; however, the reason for this could not be determined.
