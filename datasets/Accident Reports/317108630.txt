This case involved a 30 year old unrestrained male driver of a 2010 Hyundai Accent four door sedan (V1). V1 was equipped with frontal and side airbags, none of which deployed in the crash. The crash occurred at night on a dark, unlit rural roadway. The two lane roadway ran east and west and curved slightly to the right with a negative 2% grade. The posted speed limit was 89 kph (55 mph). V1 was traveling east at a reported speed of 72 kph (45 mph) when a deer ran across the road into its path of travel. The driver of V1 swerved to the right to avoid striking the deer and departed the roadway. The driver attempted to regain control of the vehicle and crossed the roadway and departed the left side. V1 traveled over an embankment and struck the ground with it frontal plane. V1 rotated counterclockwise approximately 30 degrees and came to rest. V1 was towed from the scene due to disabling damage. The driver of V1 was able to extricate himself from the vehicle. He was transported by ground ambulance to a level one trauma center and hospitalized for treatment of injuries sustained in the crash.
