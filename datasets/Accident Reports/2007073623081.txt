This two-vehicle crash occurred in a four-legged, four-way stop intersection. Both roadways are straight, level, undivided and consist of one lane in each direction with left turn lanes at the intersection. The speed limit for these roadways is 40 kmph (25 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.

Vehicle one (V1), a 2007 Ford Edge, was stopped at the stop sign, eastbound in lane one. Vehicle two (V2), a 1998 Chevrolet Lumina, was southbound in lane one. V1 entered the intersection and the front struck the right side of V2. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The driver of V1 was a twenty-five-year-old female traveling with one passenger. The driver of V2 was an eighty-one-year-old female. These drivers were not interviewed. No further information is available for these drivers.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "this vehicle traveling; crossing over (passing through) intersection." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "driver related factor; type of driver error unknown."

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded as "other vehicle encroachment; from crossing street, across path." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V2 was "not coded to this vehicle."
