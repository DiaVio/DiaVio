This crash occurred in the southbound lane of a two-lane undivided roadway. The roadway curved to the left, had a downhill grade, was bituminous, and had a posted speed limit of 56 kmph (35 mph). It was early afternoon on a weekday and conditions were clear.

V1 was a 1999 Ford Taurus, driven by a 21 year-old male. V1 was traveling southbound in lane one negotiating a left curve, when it went off of the road to the right and contacted a utility pole. V1 then began rotating clockwise and impacted an embankment on the right side of the road, where it came to final rest. Police Reported Travel Speed is Unknown for V1.

The Critical Precrash Event for V1 was “this vehicle control loss due to, tire blow out.” The Critical Reason for the Critical Precrash Event was “vehicle related factor, tires wheels failed.” The driver stated that as he was traveling down the hill, he felt the right front tire blow out, and the vehicle pulled to the right. He attempted steering to the left to get back onto the road but was unable to avoid the utility pole. Upon inspection of the right front tire, it could be clearly seen that the sidewall had indeed suffered damage. The tire had only 1/32” of tread and had been worn unevenly. Evidence on the tire suggested that the damage was not sustained from the utility pole.
