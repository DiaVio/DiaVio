This two-vehicle crash occurred on a level, undivided, straight roadway. This roadway consists of two lanes in each direction. There are elevated train support columns between lanes one and two in both directions. The speed limit on this roadway is 48 kmph (30 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions. Traffic was congested at the time of this crash.

Vehicle one (V1), a 2000 Ford Ranger, was westbound in lane two. Vehicle two (V2), a 2006 Jeep Cherokee, was westbound in lane two in front of V1. V2 stopped due to traffic and the front of V1 struck the back of V2. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The driver of V1, a thirty-four-year-old male, provided only a partial interview. He stated that he steered to the left to avoid V2 in an attempt to avoid this crash. No further information is available for this driver.

The driver of V2, a thirty-four-year-old male, was not interviewed. No further information is available for this driver.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "other motor vehicle in lane; other vehicle stopped." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "driver related factor; inadequate evasive action, e.g. braking only, not braking and steering."

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded as "other motor vehicle in lane; traveling in same direction with higher speed." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V2 was "not coded to this vehicle."
