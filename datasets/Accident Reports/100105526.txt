This case involves a belted driver responding to a near side impact with a large pickup truck. This crash occurred at the four-way intersection of a two residential streets. The north/south, street had a single lane for travel in each direction, accommodating parallel parking, and separated by painted double yellow lines. The straight roadway had an elevation gain from south to north. The east/west, street had a single lane for travel in each direction, without lane division, and parallel parking. This roadway was straight and level. The intersection was controlled by stop signs at the east and west sides. The weather was clear and the bituminous roadways dry during the afternoon, weekday crash. The case vehicle (V1), a 2001 Toyota Camry 4-door sedan, was traveling westbound on its approach to the intersection intending to travel across (straight). V1 was equipped with lap and shoulder belts and driver and passenger frontal impact air bags. The 88-year-old, female, driver (case subject/sole occupant) was using the belt restraint as evidenced by the belt being cut (spooled out). The opposing vehicle (V2), a 1992 Ford F-150 pickup, was traveling northbound on its approach to the intersection intending to travel straight through. Beginning from a stop, V1 proceeded into the intersection simultaneously with V2. The front of V2 struck the left side of V1. The impact deflected V1 to the northwest as it rotated counterclockwise. V1 came to rest along the west roadside facing southeast. The impact deflected V2 to the northwest as it rotated counterclockwise, slightly. Both vehicles were towed from the scene, V1 due to disabling damage. The driver of V1 (subject) was transported to a trauma facility due to "B" injuries as rated by police.
