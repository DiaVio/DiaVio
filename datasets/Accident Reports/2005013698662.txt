The crash occurred on a snow-covered two-lane median divided highway during the hours of dark. The posted speed limit for this road is 113kmph (70mph). The atmospheric conditions at the time of the crash were poor as a heavy and blowing snow was falling. These conditions caused asphalt highway to become almost completely covered.

V1, a 1990 Ford Bronco, was traveling southbound in the right lane at a speed of approximately 72kph (45mph). A non-contact vehicle in front of V1 slowed down for some reason, V1 attempted to pass this vehicle on the left side. As V1 moved into the left lane the driver lost control of the vehicle as it began skidding counter-clockwise. V1 went into the median were it rolled over three-quarter turns coming to final rest on it's left side. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

V1 was driven by a 42 year-old male who claimed to be in good health despite taking a prescription medication for an unknown condition. This medication has possible side effects of drowsiness and fatigue. He was wearing his prescription lenses that corrected a hyperopic (far-sighted) condition. The driver then stated that the vehicle began a counter-clockwise rotation as he moved into the left lane and that is all he can remember. The driver of V1 had to be extracted from the vehicle, as he could not get out. The driver of V1 was transported to the hospital with moderate injuries and V1 was towed due to damage.

The Critical Precrash Event coded for V1 was this vehicle's control loss due to traveling to fast for conditions. The Critical Reason coded for V1 is also traveling to fast for conditions, as the road was snow covered. Associated factors for this vehicle include a decision error as V1 was traveling too fast for conditions, as the driver did not realize caution was required. There was also a vehicle related factor as the vehicle was disengaged from four-wheel drive.
