This case occupant is a 59 year old male driver involved in moderate severity near side impact crash. He was wearing the manual lap/shoulder belt and both the seatback and left roof rail airbags deployed on impact. He sustained moderate injuries and was transported to the trauma center. This near-side intersection crash occurred during daylight hours with dry roadway conditions and overcast skies. Vehicle one (V1), a 2009 Honda Civic two door coupe, was eastbound in lane one of two eastbound lanes on a four lane, two way street with no traffic controls for vehicles traveling this direction. Vehicle two (V2), a 1999 Nissan UD1200 incomplete truck/van was southbound on a two lane, two way street and was stopped at a stop sign at this intersection. V2 entered the intersection traveling southbound as V1 was crossing through the intersection eastbound. The front of V2 impacted the left side of V1. V1 rotated slightly counterclockwise and continued to the east side of the intersection and came to final rest in lane one. V2 stopped near the point of impact and came to final rest in the intersection facing south. V1 had to be towed due to damage and V2 was driven away. The driver of V1 is the case subject and he is a 59 year old male who was wearing the manual lap/shoulder belt. His seatbelt retractor and buckle pretensioners actuated on impact and his seatback mounted side impact and roof side rail mounted curtain air bags deployed. This driver was injured and transported to the trauma center. No other occupants were in V1.
