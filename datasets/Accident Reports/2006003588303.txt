This crash occurred in the late evening of a holiday weekday in the middle of an urban intersection. The intersection consisted of a southbound one-lane, one-way, roadway and a two-lane east/west roadway controlled by a properly functioning traffic signal. Both roadways were bituminous, straight, and level with a posted speed limit of 48 kmph (30 mph). The traffic flow was normal but the roadway was wet as it was raining at the time of the crash.

V1, a 1998 Nissan Altima four door with one occupant, was southbound approaching the intersection where it intended to turn left. V2, a 1999 Pontiac Grand Prix four door with one occupant, was also southbound behind V1. As V1 slowed in the intersection to make its turn the front of V2 contacted the back of V1. Both vehicles came to final rest a few meters near the point of impact. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The driver of V1, a 39 year-old male, suffered minor injuries but was not transported to a medical facility. He stated that he was going to a friend's home to have dinner before he was struck in the rear by V2 as he was making a left hand turn. V1 was towed due to damage.

The Critical Precrash Event for V1 was coded other motor vehicle in lane, traveling in same direction with higher speed. The Critical Reason for the Critical Event was not coded to this vehicle. The only associated factors coded to V1 were the rain and wet roads.

The driver of V2, a 43 year-old male, was uninjured in the crash. He declined to participate in the study. V2 was driven from the scene.

The Critical Precrash Event for V2 was coded other motor vehicle in lane, traveling in same direction while decelerating. The Critical Reason for the Critical Event was coded as an unknown driver error. The only known associated factors for V2 were the rain and wet roads.
