This two-vehicle collision occurred mid-afternoon at a four-leg intersection. Both roadways are five lanes, undivided, dry, level and made of asphalt. The intersection is controlled by a on-color signal, which was working properly at the time. Both roadways are designated 56 kmph (35 mph). The north/south roadway has bike lanes and parking lanes in both directions.

Vehicle one (V1) is a 2002 Volkswagen Jetta 4-door sedan, driven by a twenty two (22) year old female was traveling east in the number two lane. Vehicle two (V2) is a 1998 Chevrolet Venture LS mini van, driven by a seventy (70) year old male. V2 was stopped for traffic light in the number two lane, who then traveled into the intersection going straight. After V1 entered the intersection, its front plane impacted V2's left plane. V2 rotated counterclockwise to face west at final rest in the intersection. V1 came to rest facing northeast in the intersection. V1 was towed from the scene, V2 was driven from the scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: this vehicle traveling, crossing over (passing through) intersection. The critical reason for the critical event was coded to V1 as a driver related factor: internal distraction. The driver was holding her cell phone and talking while driving and entered the intersection on a red light. Her inattention caused her a failure in recognition of the traffic controls and also the presence of V1, inadequate surveillance.

The critical pre-crash event for V2 coded other vehicle encroachment from crossing street across path. Critical reason not coded to this vehicle.
