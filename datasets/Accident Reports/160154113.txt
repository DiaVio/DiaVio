This head-on crash involves a 31-year-old restrained male driver with deployed air bag who sustained serious injury and a 32 year-old male restrained right front passenger with air bag deployment who sustained serious injury. The crash occurred during nighttime hours in dry and clear weather. The collision occurred on a two lane, tree lined roadway. The lanes are delineated by a double yellow line and is level. The case vehicle (V1), a 2008 Acura TL, was traveling east and was approaching a left hand curve in the roadway. The crash occurred when vehicle (V1) entered the left hand curve and ran off the roadway to the right and struck a tree head-on where the vehicle came to rest. Post crash the vehicle caught fire which was contained to the engine compartment. The vehicle was towed due to damage. V1 was driven by a 31-year-old case study occupant. He was properly wearing her three-point lap and shoulder belt and had his frontal air bag deploy. The case vehicle also contained a 32-year old case study occupant right front passenger who was properly belted and had his frontal air bag deploy. The case occupant was seriously injured and was transported to a level-one trauma center for treatment.
