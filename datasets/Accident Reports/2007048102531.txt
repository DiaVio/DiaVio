This two-vehicle crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions. The roadway is undivided and consists of one lane in each direction. In the precrash area, the eastbound lane is curved to the left (the radius of curvature is 222 meters and the superelevation is six percent) and has a nine percent downhill grade. The speed limit for this roadway is 56 kmph (35 mph).

Vehicle one (V1), a 2000 Chevrolet Malibu, was eastbound in lane one. V1 traveled off the right side of the road. The driver of V1 steered left and V1 returned to the road and crossed the center line. The driver of V1 then steered right and V1 rotated clockwise, traveling off the right side of the road. The left side of V1 struck an embankment. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 72 kmph for V1.

The driver of V1, a twenty-one-year-old female, was on her way to work from her home. This driver was not interviewed but her friend stated that she drives this route and her vehicle daily. No further information is available for this driver.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "this vehicle traveling; off the edge of the road on the right side." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "driver related factor; overcompensation." Speed was an associated factor in this crash.
