This single-vehicle crash occurred on a straight trafficway that is divided by a grass median. The two eastbound lanes are level with shoulders on both sides and a guardrail on the left side. The speed limit for this roadway is 121 kmph (75 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.

Vehicle one (V1), a 1997 Honda Accord, was eastbound in lane two. V1 traveled off the left side of the road. The driver of V1 steered right and V1 returned to the roadway, rotating clockwise. V1 continued rotating and moving eastbound, then traveled off the right side of the road. The left side of V1 struck a concrete embankment and V1 rolled over to the left eight quarter-turns. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

The driver of V1, a twenty-year-old male, was traveling with a twenty-three-year-old female passenger. This driver was not interviewed. His medical report indicates the use of cannabinoids. No further information is available for this driver.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: this vehicle traveling; off the edge of the road on the left side. The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as a driver related factor: overcompensation. This vehicle's speed and the driver's use of cannabinoids were associated factors in this crash.
