This severe frontal head-on crash involved a 37 year old restrained male who was the front right passenger in a 2008 Cadillac CTS four door sedan. He sustained serious injuries and was transferred to a trauma center. This crash occurred on a two lane, two way roadway at night in a dark area, and it was raining with a wet roadway surface. The roadway curves in the area of the crash and has narrow paved shoulders and a gravel shoulder on the east side. Vehicle one (V1), a 2008 Cadillac CTS four door sedan was northbound and negotiating the curve to the left. Vehicle two (V2), a 2008 Chevrolet Cobalt two door coupe was southbound negotiating the curve to the right. V2 continued straight in the curve and crossed the centerline into the northbound lane. The front of V1 impacted the front of V2. V1 rotated clockwise about 120 degrees and came to final rest facing southeast still in the northbound lane near the point of impact. V2 moved laterally, rotated slightly clockwise and came to final rest facing south on the gravel area near the east edge of the roadway. Both vehicles were severely damaged and towed from the scene. The front right passenger in V1 is the case occupant and he is a 37 year old male, who was wearing the manual lap/shoulder belt with pretensioner actuation. In his seating position, both the instrument panel mounted front airbag and right roof side rail mounted curtain air bag deployed on impact. He sustained serious injuries and was transported and hospitalized at the trauma center. The driver of V1 was a 64 year old male, who was not wearing his seatbelt. His frontal steering column mounted airbag and left roof side rail curtain deployed on impact. The driver was fatally injured. No other occupants were in V1.
