Case #2007-13-014

This crash occurred off the right side of a three-lane, curved to the left, positive divided, dry asphalt highway that had a posted speed limit of 113Kmph (70Mph). The crash occurred during daylight hours and there were no adverse conditions present at the time of the crash. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

V1 a 1996 Pontiac Sunfire was negotiating a curve in the third lane when a gust of wind blew a construction sign over and into her lane. V1 steered hard to the right, and lost control of the vehicle. V1 then departed the roadway rolling over and coming to final rest on its roof. V1 was towed from the scene with moderate damage, and the driver of V1 was transported to the hospital with minor injuries.

The driver of V1 a 39 year-old female stated that as she was negotiating the curve a strong gust of wind blew a sign into her lane. She then stated that she steered hard to the right to avoid the sign, but lost control of her vehicle. She then stated that the next thing she could remember was that she was stopped and upside down.

The Critical Pre-Crash Event for V1 is “object in roadway”. The Critical Reason for the Critical Event coded for V1 is “overcompensation”.
