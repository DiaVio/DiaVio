This crash occurred at night on a weekday evening along a straight, unlighted four-lane asphalt roadway. There was an uphill grade of 2%. The speed limit was 55mph (89 kph).

Vehicle 1 was a 2000 Jeep Cherokee driven by a 25 year-old female. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

Vehicle 1 was initially traveling in the left of two northbound lanes. The driver of Vehicle 1 and a witness both indicated that Vehicle 1 was traveling approximately 25mph (40kph). As the vehicle changed lanes to the right, another vehicle passed on the left. The driver of Vehicle 1 felt that the passing vehicle was about to “cut her off” and she swerved to the right and departed the right road edge. The front right of Vehicle 1 contacted the end of a guardrail and the vehicle rolled onto its left side. The vehicle came to rest in its original travel lane.

The Critical Precrash Event was vehicle 1 departing the right road edge. The Critical Reason for the Critical Precrash Event was panic/freezing.
