This crash occurred in the evening of a weekday in the southbound lane of a suburban roadway. The north/south roadway consisted of one lane in each direction divided by a center two-way left turn lane. The bituminous roadway was dry, straight, and level with a posted speed limit of 56 kmph (35 mph). The traffic flow was normal and there were no adverse atmospheric conditions at the time of the crash.

V1, a 2006 Mercedes Benz M-series SUV, was southbound in lane one. V2, a 2004 Hyundai Elantra, was stopped in the southbound lane ahead of V1. V3, a 2005 Chevy Monte Carlo was also stopped in the southbound lane ahead of V2 waiting for a properly functioning traffic signal to change so it could proceed through the intersection. V1 failed to stop behind the vehicles in front of it and struck V2 in the rear. The force of the impact pushed V2 into the rear of V3. All three vehicles came to a rest in lane one near their respective points of impact. V1 and V3 were driven from the scene while V2 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The driver of V1, a 54-year-old male, was not injured in the crash. Numerous attempts to contact this driver proved unsuccessful. Therefore an interview was not obtained.

The Critical Precrash Event for V1 was coded other motor vehicle in lane, other vehicle stopped. The Critical Reason for the Critical Event was an unknown driver error. There were no known associated factors coded to V1, as an interview was not obtained with the driver.

The driver of V2, a 29-year-old female, was transported for treatment of minor injuries to her lower back. A surrogate interview obtained with the driver's mother. She stated that her daughter was just sitting at the intersection when V1 failed to stop and struck her.

The Critical Precrash Event for V2 was coded other motor vehicle in lane, traveling in same direction with higher speed. The Critical Reason for the Critical Event was not coded to this vehicle. There were no known associated factors coded to V2 and the driver was not thought to have contributed to the crash.

The driver of V3, a 19-year-old female, who along with the 19-year-old female in the right front passenger seat were transported for treatment of minor injuries. The vehicle was removed by driver's mother. After numerous attempts the driver of V3 could not be located for an interview.

The Critical Precrash Event for V3 was coded as other, not involved in the first harmful event. The Critical Reason was not coded to this vehicle. There were no known associated factors coded to V3 and the driver was not thought to have contributed to the crash.
