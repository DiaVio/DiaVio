This crash occurred in a 4-way intersection on a two-lane undivided east/west roadway. The roadway had a downhill grade in the eastbound direction, was straight, bituminous, and had a posted speed limit of 56 kmph (35 mph). It was mid-morning on a weekday and conditions were clear.

V1 was a 1991 Buick Century 4-door sedan driven by a 70 year-old female. V2 was a 1988 GMC S10 pickup truck driven by a 49 year-old male. V1 was traveling in the westbound lane approaching the four-way intersection. V2 was traveling eastbound approaching the same intersection. V1 made a left hand turn at the intersection at the same time that V2 was passing through the intersection, impacting V2 on the left side. After impact V2 began rotating counterclockwise, over a small island, and then came to final rest on the side of the road next to the eastbound lane. V1 continued its turn southbound and came to final rest off of the road next to the southbound lane. Police Reported Travel Speed is 56 kmph for V1 and 56 kmph for V2.

The Critical Pre-crash Event for V1 was "this vehicle traveling, turning left at intersection." The Critical Reason for the Critical Pre-crash Event was "driver related factor - inadequate surveillance, looked but did not see." The driver stated to the officer in charge that she did not see V2 until she began to turn. No interview was obtained, so no associated factors could be coded.

The Critical Pre-crash Event for V2 was "other vehicle encroachment from opposite direction-over left lane line." The Critical Reason was not coded to this vehicle. There were no associated factors coded to this vehicle.
