This crash occurred in the intersection of two similar roadways, both being five-lane, two-way, non-divided asphalt city roadways with a posted speed limit of 64 Kmph (40 Mph). The crash occurred during daylight hours, and there were no adverse conditions present at the time of the crash.

V1 a 2000 Mercury Mountaineer was stopped facing east waiting for traffic to clear. V2 a 1996 Oldsmobile Bravada was traveling west bound in the second lane. V1 proceeded to make its left turn and came into contact with V2. V2's front bumper hit V1's right side, V1 rotated clockwise and rolled 1 quarter turn in the middle of the intersection. Both vehicles were towed with moderate damage. The driver of V1 and the passenger of V2 were transported to the hospital with moderate injuries. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The driver of V1 a 70 year-old female stated that she stopped because she had a flashing red turn light. She then stated that traffic cleared so she started to make the left turn when she was struck in the right side by V2. She states that she never saw V2.

The Critical Pre-Crash Event coded for V1 is " this vehicle traveling, turning left at an intersection". The Critical Reason for the Critical Event coded for V1 is "Driver related, inadequate surveillance, looked but did not see".

The driver of V2 a 29 year-old female stated that she saw V1 waiting to turn. She then stated that when she was almost to the intersection, V1 turned right in front of her. She said that she hit the brakes as hard as she could but could not avoid the collision.

The Critical Pre-Crash event coded for V2 is "other motor encroachment, from opposite direction-over left lane line". V2 was not assigned a Critical Reason.
