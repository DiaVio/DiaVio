This single vehicle-crash happened on a weekday during early morning daylight hours. The location was a three lane divided (no positive barrier) straight asphalt roadway. In the median are light poles mounted on three feet high concrete bases. It was an uphill (slope 4.1 %) roadway. The weather was clear and the roads dry. The posted speed limit for the roadway was 64 kph (40 mph). The morning rush hour had not started and there was medium traffic on the roadway.

The vehicle (V1), a 1996 Plymouth Breeze, with one occupant, was traveling east in the second lane of the roadway. The driver tried to avoid an object in the roadway by steering to the left. The vehicle drove across lane three and departed the roadway to the left and entered the median. The front of V1 impacted the concrete base of a light pole located on the median. The impact caused V1 to rotate clockwise. V1 entered the westbound lanes of traffic as it continued to rotate and the vehicle entered and crossed the three lanes of the westbound traffic. V1 came to rest in lane one of the westbound traffic lanes, facing west. During the impact a fire ignited inside the engine department of V1. V1 was towed from the scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

A 38-year old female was the driver of V1. She has a valid driver's license. She is in good health, takes no medication and was well rested. She is familiar with the vehicle and drives on this roadway on a daily basis. She spoke little English and was able to answer any questions but without detailed description. She basically said there was something in the roadway she tried to avoid. No such object was found on scene.

The critical per-crash event for V1 was coded "object in roadway." The critical reason was coded "driver related factor - overcompensation." In attempting to avoid the object in the roadway, the driver overcompensated and the vehicle entered the median.
