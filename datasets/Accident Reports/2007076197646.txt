This single-vehicle crash occurred on an undivided, level roadway. This roadway consists of one lane in each direction and curves to the right in the northbound direction. The speed limit on this roadway is 56 kmph (35 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.

Vehicle one (V1), a 1995 Chevrolet K2500 pickup, was northbound in lane one. A group of wild pigs crossed the road and the driver of V1 steered to the right to avoid them. The driver of V1 then steered left to return to the road and the right side of V1 struck a guardrail. V1 returned to the road, crossed both lanes and traveled off the left side of the road. The front of V1 struck a dirt embankment and V1 rolled over to the right one quarter-turn. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 48 kmph for V1.

The driver of V1, a sixty-four-year-old female, was traveling from her home to drop off her seventeen-month-old granddaughter at a day care facility. She was healthy, well rested and comfortable with her vehicle and passenger. She travels this route daily. This driver stated that she did not realize that she was so close to the guardrail when she was returning to the road.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: object or animal; animal in roadway. The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as a driver related factor; poor directional control (e.g., failing to control vehicle with skill ordinarily expected).
