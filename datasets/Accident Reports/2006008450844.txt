This crash occurred on a weekend morning on the right shoulder of a two-way two lane rural undivided roadway. The bituminous roadway was dry, straight, and level with a posted speed limit of 40kmph (25mph). The traffic flow was normal and there were no adverse atmospheric conditions at the time of the crash.

V1, a 1987 Porsche 944 coupe was eastbound traveling in lane one. As V1 emerged from under a highway overpass, a deer appeared in the roadway. V1 steered right, departing the roadway. V1 impacted a utility pole with the front of the vehicle. The vehicle came to rest blocking the eastbound lane and partially on the roadside. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 40 kmph for V1.

The driver of V1 was a belted 42 year-old male who was uninjured in the crash. After numerous attempts the driver of V1 could not be located making an interview impossible.

The Critical Precrash Event for V1 was coded as an animal in the roadway. The Critical Reason for the Critical Precrash Event was coded as a driver related factor, incorrect evasive action. There were no other associated factors coded in addition to the incorrect evasive action, as an interview was not obtained for the driver of V1.
