The crash occurred on a level two lane undivided roadway with a posted speed limit of 89 KPH (55 MPH). It was cloudy, the roadway dry and it was daylight at the time of this weekend afternoon crash. The traffic flow was normal for that time of day.

V1, a 1997 Toyota 4-Runner, was traveling east on the roadway. V1 crossed into the westbound lane and off the left side of the roadway. V1 continued along a small ditch struck a tree stump with its front and ripped the left front tire off. V1 continued through small bushes, struck and snapped a small tree with its front. V1 continued into a ditch, struck an 18 cm diameter tree with its front and came to rest facing in an easterly direction. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

V1, the Toyota 4-Runner, was driven by a belted 38-year-old female who was not injured. Driver stated to police that she had fallen asleep due to self-proclaimed sleep deprivation. No further information could be obtained as the driver could not be contacted or located.

The Critical Precrash Event for V1 was this vehicle traveling off the edge of the roadway on the left side. The Critical Reason for the Critical Event was sleeping, that is, actually asleep, a driver non-performance error. This was chosen because the driver stated to the police she had fallen asleep. An associated factor coded to this driver was fatigue.
