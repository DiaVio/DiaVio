This two-vehicle crash occurred early evening daylight hours, on a dry, level, two-lane, bituminous roadway that runs north to south and has a posted speed limit of 89 kmph (55 mph). This roadway intersects with a dry, downhill, bituminous roadway that runs east to west. This intersection was controlled by a flashing caution light (yellow). There were no adverse atmospheric conditions present at the time of the daylight crash.

Vehicle one (V 1), a 1989 Dodge Dakota pickup driven by a 59 year-old female was traveling southbound in lane one. Vehicle two (V 2), a 2002 Mercedes-Benz ML 320 SUV driven by a 62 year-old female with one passenger was stopped facing southbound in lane one attempting to make a left turn at the intersection. While V 2 was stopped, the front of V 1 contacted the rear . V 1 came to rest in lane one facing south and V 2 came to rest facing south on the west shoulder of the southbound side of the roadway. The driver of V 1 advised that she was traveling southbound in lane one and she guessed that V 2 was stopped and she hit V 2 from the rear. The driver of V 2 advised that she was stopped with her left turn signal on for about 20-30 seconds prior to the impact. The driver of V 1 was arrested after the crash for driving under the influence, however, no BAC was recorded. Both vehicles were towed from the scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is 89 kmph for V1 and 89 kmph for V2.

The pre-event movement for V 1 coded going straight, category other motor vehicle in lane other vehicle stopped. Critical reason driver related factor "misjudgment of gap or other's speed".

The pre-event movement for V 2 coded stopped in traffic lane, category other motor vehicle in lane traveling in same direction with higher speed. Critical reason not coded to this vehicle. Associated factor- driver of V 1 had poor surveillance.
