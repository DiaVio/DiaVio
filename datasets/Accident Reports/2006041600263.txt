This crash occurred in the late afternoon of a holiday in the center median of a divided urban roadway. The east/west roadway consisted of three lanes in each direction divided by a grassy tree-lined median. The bituminous roadway was dry, straight, and level with a posted speed limit of 56kmph (35 mph). Traffic flow was light as it was a holiday and there were no adverse atmospheric conditions

V1, a 2000 Jeep Cherokee, was westbound traveling in lane three. An unidentified non-contact vehicle was westbound in lane two alongside of V1. The non-contact vehicle began moving over into lane three (that was still occupied by V1). The driver of V1 braked hard and steered left to avoid the collision and departed the roadway. V1 began rotating clockwise until its tires dug into the grass of the median. V1 rolled right roughly 38m (125ft.) until it came to final rest Police Reported Travel Speed is 56 kmph for V1.

The driver of V1 was a 28-year-old woman who was on her way to a friend's home with her 11-year-old son. She stated she was driving along in lane three when out of the corner of her right eye she noticed another vehicle encroaching into her lane. She said she started beeping her horn to alert the driver she was there. The other vehicle continued creeping over into lane three. To avoid a crash she steered to the left and hit her brakes. She stated that she lost control of her vehicle, left the roadway and rolled over in the grassy median. She and her son were treated on scene and V1 was towed due to damage.

The Critical Precrash Event for V1 was coded as this vehicle traveling, over the lane line left side of travel lane. The Critical Reason was coded as a driver related error, overcompensation. In addition to the overcompensation an internal distraction was coded as an associated factor as the driver was engaged in conversation with her child.
