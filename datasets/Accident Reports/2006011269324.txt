This crash occurred on at weekday afternoon at an intersection of a five lane, two- way, bituminous roadway, and a commercial driveway. The roadway was dry, straight, and level with a posted speed limit of 56kmph (35mph). There were no traffic control devices on the roadway. The lighting conditions were daylight with cloud cover.

V1, a 1990 Pontiac 6000 LE, was eastbound in lane 3 (the designated left turn lane). V2, a 2004 Chevrolet Colorado pickup, was westbound in lane 1 of the same roadway. As V1 turned left across the two westbound lanes, V2 front contacted V1 right. V1 rotated clockwise 270 degrees clockwise and came to rest in the parking lot he was entering, facing west. V2 exited the roadway to the right and came to rest in an adjacent parking lot facing west. Both vehicles were towed due to damage and the driver of V1 was injured and transported. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

V1 was driven by a 73 year-old male who reported he was in fair health with a history of heart disease for which he had a pacemaker and took medication. He suffered from a hyperopic condition and was not wearing his corrective lenses. The driver of V1 was not in a hurry and said he was not fatigued. He was operating the vehicle with a driver's license that had been revoked since 1972 in one state and his current license from another state was suspended. V1 appeared old with many parts in disrepair.

The Critical Pre-crash Event was coded this vehicle traveling, turning left at intersection and the Critical Reason for Critical Pre-crash Event was misjudgment of gap or other's speed. Some factors that may have contributed to the cause of the crash include the driver's misjudgment of gap distance, his lack of a valid driver's license, the poor condition of the vehicle and his decision to proceed with insufficient clearance. As well as a vision impairment as the driver was not wearing his glasses at the time of the crash.

V2 was driven by a 36 year-old male who stated he was in good health and out running some errands. The driver was properly licensed for and familiar with his vehicle. He was not exceeding the speed limit or in a hurry. The driver suffered no injuries but his vehicle was towed due to damage.

The Critical pre-crash event was coded as other vehicle encroachment, from opposite direction-over left lane line. The Critical Reason for Critical Pre-crash Event was not coded to this vehicle. There were no associated factors coded to this vehicle, as it was not thought to have contributed to the crash.
