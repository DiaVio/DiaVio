This two-vehicle crash occurred on a jersey barrier-divided, straight trafficway in an interchange area. The southbound travel lanes have an uphill grade and consist of two through lanes. The speed limit for this roadway is 89 kmph (55 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.

Vehicle one (V1), a 2003 Ford Explorer, was southbound in lane one. Vehicle two (V2), a 2000 Pontiac Grand Prix, was southbound in lane two. V1 traveled to the left and the front struck the right side of V2. V2 traveled to the left and the front struck the median jersey barrier. The driver of V1 steered to the right and then to the left. V1 rotated counterclockwise and rolled over to the right four quarter-turns. The back of V1 struck a guardrail on the right side of the road. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The driver of V1, a fifty-nine-year-old male, was on his way home from work. He was well rested, healthy and familiar with his vehicle. He travels this route daily. This driver stated that he did not see V2 before he started to change lanes to the left. His medical record indicates a positive test for alcohol.

The driver of V2, a twenty-one-year-old male, was not interviewed. No further information is available for this driver.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "this vehicle traveling; over the lane line on left side of travel lane." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "driver related factor; inadequate surveillance (e.g. failed to look, looked but did not see)."

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded as "other vehicle encroachment; from adjacent lane (same direction) - over right lane line." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V2 was "not coded to this vehicle."
