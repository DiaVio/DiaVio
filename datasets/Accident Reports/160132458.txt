Vehicle one (V1), a 2000 Toyota MR2 Spyder convertible, was traveling west in lane four of a four lane, one-way trafficway, part of a six lane, two-way roadway under dark, rainy conditions and was approaching an intersection. Vehicle two (V2), a 2006 Mazda 6 sedan, was traveling east in lane three of a four lane, one-way trafficway approaching the same intersection. V1 attempted to turn left at the intersection and the front of V1 contacted the front of V2. Both vehicles came to final rest in the southeast portion of the intersection and both vehicles were towed due to damage. The drive and sole occupant in V1 (Case study occupant) was a 66 year-old male who was properly wearing a lap and shoulder belt and had both frontal airbags deploy. The driver of V1 was transported to a level-one trauma center for treatment.
