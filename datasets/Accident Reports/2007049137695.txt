The single vehicle crash occurred on a divided, north/south highway. The median is grass with a barrier consisting of a three-cable system supported by metal posts. The southbound roadway was straight, dry, with a positive grade and consists of three through lanes. An asphalt shoulder is located between lane three and the barrier. The posted speed limit is 97 kmph (60 mph). Traffic was light at the time of the crash. This crash occurred during weekend daylight hours with clear skies.

Vehicle one (V1), a 1995 Honda Civic, was southbound in the third travel lane. The driver departed the roadway on the left side and the front of the vehicle struck the post and cable traffic barrier. The vehicle came to rest against the barrier facing south. The vehicle was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

The driver of V1, a twenty-six year old female, was heading home from a friend's residence. This driver had been driving for about five minutes, drives the route monthly and is familiar with the vehicle. The driver stated that a truck in the second lane attempted to merge left into her lane. She steered to the left and departed the roadway on the east side. The front of V1 struck a cable and post traffic barrier. V1 came to rest off the roadway facing south.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: this vehicle traveling-off the edge of the road on the left side. The critical reason for V1 was coded as a driver related factor: overcompensation.
