This two-vehicle crash occurred in the early afternoon during daylight hours. The location was an inter-city residential arterial with no traffic control and numerous private driveways along both sides of the roadway. The roadway was a bituminous two lane, two ways, undivided arterial with paved shoulders and bike lanes on both sides. The location of the crash was a right curve with a positive grade. The surface was dry, and it was overcast. The posted speed limit is 56 kmph (35 mph).

Vehicle one (V1) was a 2005 Toyota Camry 4 door sedan. V1 was driven by a seventy five (75) year old male who was traveling southbound behind Vehicle two (V2), a 2004 Chevrolet Malibu Classic 4 door sedan driven by a fifty (50) year old male. The driver of V1 took his eyes off the roadway to look at two dogs being walked on the east shoulder moving southbound. When the driver of V1 looked back at the road V2 had slowed to almost a stop in order to make a left turn into the driveway of his residence. V1 struck the rear of V2. On impact V2 was pushed forward and across the northbound lanes and came to rest with the front of the vehicle on the shoulder facing east. The driver of V2 stated he was steering left on impact. V1 stopped on impact, then the driver drove to the shoulder and made a controlled stop. According to the witness statements there were no brake lights visible for either vehicle prior to the impact. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 56 kmph for V1 and 56 kmph for V2.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: other motor vehicle in lane, traveling in same direction with a lower steady speed. The critical reason for the critical event was coded to V1 as a driver related factor: external distraction.

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded: other motor vehicle in lane, traveling in same direction with higher speed. The critical reason was not coded to this vehicle.
