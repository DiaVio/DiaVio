This two vehicle crash occurred during early evening daylight hours at a four leg intersection with the east/west traffic controlled by stop signs. Road surface is level dry asphalt. The posted speed limit 48 kmph (30 mph).

Vehicle one (V1) a 1997 Volkswagen Gulf Station wagon driven by a 19 year-old male was heading south in the second lane. Vehicle two (V2) a 2003 Dodge Caravan mini van driven by a 21 year-old female with five passengers was heading north in the second lane. V1 was going to turn left at the intersection. when V1 turned left it impacted with the left front bumper of V2. V1 continued going east and stopped next to the south curb facing east. V1 was towed due to damage to the radiator. V2 after impact continued northeast coming to final rest at the corner. V2 left the scene under own power. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: this vehicle traveling, turning left at intersection. The critical reason for the critical event was coded to V1 as a driver related factor: misjudgment of gap or others speed. Additional factors for V1 driver was a unlicensed driver with 1 year driving experience. At the time of the crash an outdoor birthday party was being held on the southwest corner, with lots of people and loud music. At the time of the interview the driver did not mention any distractions; however, at the scene of the crash the driver, who spoke Spanish kept gesturing towards the house with the party. It was a distraction during the interview with the police, as the driver kept looking at the party as the police officer was in the process of gathering information.

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded: other vehicle encroachment; from opposite direction, over left lane line. The critical reason was not coded to this vehicle.
