This two-vehicle crash occurred on a two-lane, one-way, access-controlled entrance ramp. This roadway curves to the right, has a downhill grade and has jersey barriers on both sides. The speed limit on this roadway is 97 kmph (60 mph) and an advisory speed of 48 kmph (30 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours and it was raining.

Vehicle one (V1), a 1999 BMW 528i, was northbound in lane two. Vehicle two (V2), a 1996 Chevrolet Blazer, was northbound in lane one. V1 began to rotate clockwise and traveled off the right side of the roadway. The front of V1 struck the jersey barrier. V1 continued rotating, traveling to the north and across both travel lanes. The back of V1 struck the left jersey barrier. V1 then traveled across the roadway again and the front struck the right jersey barrier again. The front of V2 then struck the right side of V1. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The driver of V1, a fifty-six-year-old male, was traveling from his home to his office. He was well rested, healthy and familiar with his vehicle. He travels this route daily. This driver stated that he was traveling 64-72 kmph (40-45 mph).

The driver of V2, a twenty-five-year-old male, was traveling with a twenty-five-year-old male passenger, from his home to a friend's home. This driver was healthy and well rested. No further information is available for this driver.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: this vehicle control loss due to- traveling too fast for conditions. The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as a driver related factor: too fast for curve/turn.

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded: other; not involved in first harmful event. The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event was not coded to this vehicle.
