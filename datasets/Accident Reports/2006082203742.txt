This single-vehicle crash occurred on the approach to a four-legged, traffic signal-controlled intersection. The north/south roadway is level, straight and undivided and consists of two lanes in each direction. The speed limit on this roadway is 48 kmph (30 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.

Vehicle one (V1), a 2000 Ford Crown Victoria, was southbound in lane one. Traffic ahead of V1 stopped for a red traffic signal and the driver of V1 steered to the right to avoid the stopped vehicles. V1 traveled off the right side of the roadway and the front struck a building and a low concrete wall. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

The driver of V1, a forty-one-year-old male, was traveling with his twenty-eight-year-old wife, from a store to their home. He was healthy, well rested and comfortable with his vehicle and passenger. He travels this route daily. This driver stated that he was daydreaming and when he realized that the traffic was stopping, he steered right and accelerated rather than applying the brakes.

The pre-event movement for V1 was coded as "successful avoidance maneuver to a previous critical event." The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "this vehicle traveling; off the edge of the road on the right side." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "driver related factor; poor directional control (e.g. failing to control vehicle with skill ordinarily expected)." The driver's inattention was an associated factor.
