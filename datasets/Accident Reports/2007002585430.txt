The crash occurred on a level two lane undivided roadway with a posted speed limit of 89 KPH (55 MPH). The roadway curved to the right with a radius of curvature of 1,125 meters and a superelevation of .9%. It was cloudy, the roadway dry and it was daylight at the time of this early weekend morning crash.

V1, a 1992 Toyota pickup, was traveling north on the roadway negotiating the curve to the right. V1 went off the right side of the roadway and struck a rock embankment with its front. V1 continued, struck a delineator with its front and then rolled over to the left four-quarter turns. V1 struck the rock embankment during the rollover. V1 continued back onto its wheels and came to rest in the northbound lane facing in southeasterly direction. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

V1, the Toyota pickup, was driven by an unbelted 36-year-old male who was transported, treated and released for minor injuries. Driver stated to emergency personnel that he had looked down and reached for something when he drove off the road and the crash occurred. No additional information was obtained as the driver refused an interview. Official records indicated that this driver took no medications.

The Critical Precrash Event for V1 was this vehicle traveling off the edge of the road on the right side. The Critical Reason for the Critical Precrash Event was an internal distraction, a driver related factor. This was chosen because the driver had looked down and reached for something taking his attention away from the driving task. Police did not consider drugs or alcohol to be involved and ordered no tests.
