This single vehicle crash occurred a couple hours before mid-night on a two lane county roadway. This roadway has various grades, crests and curves. The posted speed limit for this roadway is 80 kph (50 mph) with warning signs posted for the curves and speed reduction signs of 40 kmph (25 mph) just prior to this event, crash occurred during darkness with no adverse weather or road conditions.

Vehicle one (V1) a 1986 Nissan 300ZX two door hatch back driven by a 14 year-old female with two passengers was traveling eastbound going downgrade with an "S" type curve. The driver departed the right side off the road edge and impacted a large rock with the right front. V1 then rotated clockwise 360 degrees back onto the roadway coming to a rest on the eastbound lane heading in a northerly direction. V1 was towed from the scene due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 40 kmph for V1.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: this vehicle traveling, off the edge of the road to the right. The critical reason for the critical event was coded as a driver related factor: too fast for curve/turn.
