This case involves two case occupants, the first is the driver and the second is the right front passenger. The driver is a 50 year old male and the right front passenger is a 51 year old female. Both front seat occupants were restrained by the available 3-point manual belts. There was also a 70 year old unrestrained female occupant in the right rear seat. The crash occurred during daylight hours and the asphalt road was dry. The case vehicle (V1), a 2006 Kia Rio, was traveling west through an interchange area intending to go straight. V2 is a 1997 Mercury Grand Marquis which was exiting the interstate to go east. V2 was southbound exiting the interstate and was in the process of turning left to proceed east when it was struck in the left front quarter panel by the front of V1. V1 then rotated counterclockwise while V2 rotated clockwise sideslapping with the left rear quarter panel of V2 coming in contact with V1's right rear wheel and wheel well. The case vehicle came to rest on the channelized concrete divider in the center of the interchange and V2 traveled across the center and came to rest in the eastbound lanes. Both vehicles were towed from the scene due to disabling damage. The driver and right front passenger were transported to a level one trauma center and hospitalized due to serious injuries. The right rear passenger was treated and released from the emergency department.
