This case involves an 81 year old female driver of a 2007 Saturn Ion. The Saturn (V1) was struck in the left side by the front of (V2) a 1997 Ford Crown Victoria. The crash occurred during daylight hours on a dry asphalt roadway. There were no adverse weather conditions. V1 traveling west, was exiting a private business parking lot entering the northbound side of a four-lane divided highway. The driver of V1, for unknown reasons, attempted a left turn into oncoming traffic. V2 was traveling north in the left lane of the straight and level divided highway. The front of V2 struck V1 in the left rear door. V1 rotated counterclockwise and departed the roadway going into the grass median for final rest. V2 continued forward coming to rest at the edge of the median. Both vehicles were towed due to disabling damage and the case subject was transported by ground ambulance to a level one trauma center where she was hospitalized for treatment of her injuries.
