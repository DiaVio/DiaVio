This two-vehicle crash occurred on a two-lane freeway entrance ramp. This roadway is one-way, curved to the right, has an uphill grade and consists of two lanes that merge into one lane on the freeway. The speed limit for this roadway is 72 kmph (45 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions. Traffic was congested at the time of the crash.

Vehicle one (V1), an unknown tractor-trailer, was in lane two on the ramp. Vehicle two (V2), a 2000 Jeep Cherokee, was in lane one on the ramp. V2 stopped due to congested traffic on the freeway. V1 moved to the right and the front struck the left side of V2. V2 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is No PAR obtained (created) for V1 and No PAR obtained (created) for V2.

V1left the scene. No further information is available for this driver.

The driver of V2, a thirty-eight-year-old male, was on his way home from work. He was healthy, well rested and familiar with his vehicle. He travels this route daily. This driver stated that he was stopped and waiting to enter the freeway when V1 struck his vehicle.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "this vehicle traveling; over the lane line on right side of travel lane." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "unknown reason for critical event."

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded as "other vehicle encroachment; from adjacent lane (same direction) - over left lane line." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V2 was "not coded to this vehicle."
