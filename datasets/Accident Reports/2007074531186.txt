This two-vehicle crash occurred in a traffic signal-controlled intersection. The roadways are divided, level, and straight and were dry at the time of the crash. The lanes approaching the intersection (from all directions) consist of two through lanes, a left-turn-only lane and a right-turn-only lane. The speed limit on these roadways is 72 kmph (45 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.

Vehicle one (V1), a 2005 Chrysler Town and Country, was northbound in lane two, approaching the intersection. Vehicle two (V2), a 1999 Dodge Caravan, was stopped westbound in lane four. The traffic signal changed to a left-turn arrow for V2. V2 proceeded to start a left turn. Both drivers noticed the other at the last minute and applied the brakes. The front of V1 struck the left side of V2. Both vehicles came to rest in the intersection, V1 facing north and V2 facing northwest. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The driver of V1, a fifty-one-year-old female, was traveling from her workplace to a store. She was well rested and familiar with her vehicle. She drives this roadway daily. She had been suffering with a migraine headache for three days and was taking Fiorinal (Butalbital/APAP/Caffeine). Some of the side effects listed for this medication are drowsiness, tiredness and an inability to concentrate. This driver stated that she "was just kind of daydreaming" while driving.

The driver of V2, a twenty-eight-year-old female, was traveling from her workplace to a bank. She was healthy, well rested and comfortable with her vehicle. She drives this roadway weekly. She currently takes Doxycycline and birth control pills.

The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "driver related factor; inattention (i.e. daydreaming)." The pre-event movement for V2 was coded as "stopped in traffic lane." The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded as "other vehicle encroachment; from crossing street, across path." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded as "not coded to this vehicle."
