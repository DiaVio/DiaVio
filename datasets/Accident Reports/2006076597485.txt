This single vehicle crash occurred on an undivided three lane asphalt state highway which runs east and west and has a posted speed limit of 89 kmph (55 mph). In the area of the crash, there are one eastbound and two westbound lanes; the downgrade for eastbound traffic is 5 percent and the road curves to the left. At the time of this mid-morning crash, the traffic was light and there were no adverse atmospheric conditions or road defects.

Vehicle one (V1), a 1996 Plymouth mini van, was eastbound, drifted right off the south edge of the road and impacted a rock embankment with the front plane. V1 then entered a clockwise rotation, rolled left two quarter-turns, impacted a guardrail with the top plane, rolled an additional quarter-turn left, then one more back to the right as it re-entered the roadway, then came to rest on its roof in the eastbound lane facing generally west. The V1 driver, a 22 year old female who was in the third trimester of a pregnancy stated that she was on a long trip and that her most recent leg began when she picked up her passenger, a 68 year old female, approximately 129-kilometers (80-miles) from the crash scene. The V1driver added that she felt sleepy and did in fact fall asleep before the crash occurred. She also stated that she has gestational diabetes and was taking pre-natal vitamins. Both V1 occupants were transported for treatment and V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 113 kmph for V1.

The Critical Event was coded as This Vehicle Traveling - Off the edge of the road on the right side. The Critical Reason was coded as a Driver Related Factor - Sleeping, that is, actually asleep.
