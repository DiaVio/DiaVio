This crash occurred on an undivided two-lane roadway with one lane in each direction. This roadway had paved asphalt shoulders with guardrails on both sides of the roadway. The crash occurred on a sweeping right curve. The roadway was level. The radius of curvature of the roadway was 375 meters with a superelevation of 7 percent. The speed was posted at 89kmph (55mph). The crash occurred on the weekend in the late afternoon hours. The weather was overcast.

Vehicle #1, a 2000 Chevrolet Impala 4-door sedan, was traveling north on the roadway negotiating the curve to the left. For unknown reasons, the vehicle crossed the centerline of the roadway and then continued to the left where it traveled off the roadway onto the left shoulder. The driver then realized the vehicle was out of its lane and braked and steered to the right trying to get back to its lane. This last action caused the front of V1 to strike the front of Vehicle #2, a 2006 Dodge Ram pick-up truck, which had been traveling in the southbound lane. V1 rotated clockwise 180 degrees to face to the south for final rest. The pick-up rotated counterclockwise and came to rest facing east at the guardrail on the east side of the roadway. The driver and passenger had to be extracted from V1 and were transported to a local trauma unit for their injuries. The driver of V2 was also transported a local trauma unit. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is No PAR obtained (created) for V1 and No PAR obtained (created) for V2.

A 44-year-old female drove V1. The driver's husband gave the interview stating he did not want his wife traumatized any more than she was at the time. He stated that his wife had been in good health and was taking medication for diabetes. He stated she does wear corrective lenses for nearsightedness. He stated that his wife and 14-year old son had gone to a craft show to help her sister with her craft booth that day. He stated they left the house very early and had left the craft show about one hour before the show ended. He stated that they think his son had fallen asleep on the way home and was sleeping at the time of the crash. He stated that his wife does not remember much after that. He did not know why his wife crossed the centerline.

A 61-year-old male drove V2. He reported being in good health but taking medication for blood pressure. He also stated he wears corrective lenses for farsightedness. He stated he had gone to the store to pick up some groceries to make chili. He stated he had left the store and had never accelerated to the posted speed because he only lived one-half mile down the roadway. He stated he could see V1 crossing the centerline of the roadway. He also stated he could see the vehicle behind V1 slowing and pulling to the side of the roadway. He stated he started to move to the oncoming traffic lane as the driver looked straight at him and steered back into his lane of travel. He stated he braked hard and steered to the left but there was no time to really react. He stated he received injuries to his wrist and cuts on his fingers. He also stated he lost his ring in the crash.

The Critical Pre-crash for V1 was coded this vehicle traveling over the lane line on left side of travel lane. The Critical Reason for the Critical Event was coded as type of driver error unknown. Associated factors coded to this driver include use of medications for diabetes, driver fatigue after working all day on her feet, and incorrect evasive action for steering to the right at the last moment rather than simply braking.

The Critical Pre-crash Event for V2 was coded other vehicle encroachment from opposite direction - over left lane line. The Critical Reason for the Critical event was not coded to this vehicle. The only associated factor coded to this driver was the use of prescription medication to control high blood pressure.
