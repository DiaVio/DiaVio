This crash occurred on an ice covered two-lane, non-divided, rural roadway with a posted speed limit of 89 kmph (55 mph). The crash occurred during daylight hours, and there were no adverse conditions present at the time of the crash.

V1 a 1991 GMC Sonoma was traveling north bound in the right curb lane. V2 a 2005 Buick Park Avenue was slowing down in the right curb lane. V1 contacted V2 in the rear bumper with its front bumper. Both vehicles were towed from the scene, and the driver of V1 was transported to the hospital with minor injuries. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The driver of V1 a 47 year-old males stated that he was on his way home from work when he noticed the car in front of him slowing down. He stated that he applied the brakes but the tires locked up on the ice. He then stated that he started to pump the brakes to stop but could not avoid hitting V1.

The Critical Pre-Crash Event Coded for V1 is "other motor vehicle in lane, traveling in the same direction while decelerating". The Critical Reason for the Critical Event Coded for V1 is "Traveling too fast for conditions".

The driver of V2 a 76 year-old female stated that she was on her way to a clients house for a appointment when she was hit in the rear buy another vehicle. She then stated that she did not see V1 until after being contacted.

The Critical Pre-Crash Event Coded for V2 is " other motor vehicle in lane, other vehicle traveling in the same direction with higher speed". V2 was not assigned a Critical Reason.
