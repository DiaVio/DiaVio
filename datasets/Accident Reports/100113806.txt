This case involved the belted driver of a small passenger car responding to a frontal impact with a parked semi-trailer. The case vehicle frontal impact air bags deployed. The subject sustained severe injury(s) as a result. This single vehicle crash occurred on an east/west, six-lane, divided trafficway. There were three lanes for travel in each direction with on street parking along each side, divided by a raised curb median. The trafficway was straight and relatively level at the crash site. The weather was clear and the bituminous roadway dry, during the early morning (dark, street lights), weekend crash. The case vehicle (2003 Nissan Sentra, 4-door sedan) was traveling westbound in the number-one (right) lane. The vehicle was equipped with lap and shoulder belts (pretentioners) and first row frontal impact air bags. The 45-year-old male driver (case subject/sole occupant) was determined to have been using the belt restraint. The case vehicle was traveling straight when the driver believes he became distracted, possibly by on-coming headlights, when he allowed the vehicle to track diagonally out of the number-one lane, to the right. The vehicle struck the back of a semi-trailer with its front right. The vehicle's first row frontal impact air bags deployed. The vehicle rotated clockwise approximately 45-degrees were it came to rest adjacent to the impact, facing northwest. The vehicle was towed due to disabling damage. The subject was transported to a trauma facility due to a 'severe' injury.
