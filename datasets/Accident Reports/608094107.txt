This case involves a minor rollover impact to the right side of the case vehicle with the ground. The case occupant is the 43-year-old female driver. She was not restrained by a three-point belt, and no air bags deployed. The case vehicle a 2003 Jeep Grand Cherokee 4x4 SUV was southbound on an icy, two-lane road negotiating a right sweeping curve on a 4% downgrade. The driver lost control of the case vehicle as it approached a four-legged intersection. It rotated counterclockwise, traversed the intersection and slid into the curb at the southwest corner tripping the vehicle, causing it to rollover right side leading. The case vehicle rolled three quarter turns before coming to final rest at an estimated 50 feet from the tripping point. The 43-year-old female driver was the sole occupant of the case vehicle. She was not restrained by a 3-point belt and no air bags deployed. She was transported to an outside facility before being transferred to a level-one trauma center and enrolled as a case occupant.
