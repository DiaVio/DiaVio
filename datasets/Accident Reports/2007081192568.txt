This single vehicle crash occurred during the late morning daylight hours . The location was an inter-city arterial in a residential area. The roadway was a two lane, two way, undivided bituminous road with paved shoulders on both road sides. The surface was wet except for the travel areas. It had been raining, but at the time of the crash it was cloudy and dry. There was a hillcrest and then a negative - 7% grade for westbound traffic. The posted speed limit is 48 kmph (30 mph).

Vehicle one (V1) was a 2003 Honda Odyssey mini-van driven by a thirty six (36) year old female with one passenger. They were traveling westbound up a hill. As V1 crested the hill the driver stated she saw a small animal enter the roadway from her right. She applied the brakes and tried to steer right, but her vehicle kept going straight, so she steered back to the left. The driver lost control and the vehicle rotated in a counter-clockwise yaw off the roadway on the south shoulder into a ditch. The front of the vehicle struck the ditch, and then the vehicle continued rotating and the right side front struck a small tree. The right side passenger area struck two small trees, the vehicle continued to rotate counter-clockwise and came to rest facing north with the back of V1 in the ditch. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

The driver of V1 was on her way to a birthday party and was running late. She stated that she takes the medication Cymgalca for depression. The critical pre-crash event was coded: object or animal, animal in roadway. The critical reason for the critical event was a driver related factor: incorrect evasive action. Associated factors were overcompensation and poor directional control.
