This crash occurred in the late evening of a weekend in the center median of a divided 6-lane highway. The highway featured three northbound lanes and three southbound lanes separated by a depressed grassy median. The roadways in both directions were bituminous, dry, straight, and level. There were no adverse atmospheric conditions and the street was dark but lighted.

V1, a 2005 Ford F-150 pick up truck, was southbound in lane three when its right rear tire blew out causing the driver to lose control. V1 yawed slightly counterclockwise and departed the roadway to the left. As V1 left the roadway its left front tire slid down the embankment of depressed median damaging the tire and the axle. V1 came to final rest in the median and was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is No PAR obtained (created) for V1.

Upon arrival at the scene, the State Trooper was preparing to leave and the occupant was not there. A PAR was not able to be located for this crash and the driver could not be located. The trooper was able to relay that the driver had stated that the tire of the vehicle blew out and he had lost control of the vehicle.

The Critical Precrash Event for V1 was coded this vehicle loss control due to a blow out of a tire. The Critical Reason for Precrash Event was a vehicle related factor tires failed. There are no known associated factors coded to the driver, as he could not be interviewed.
