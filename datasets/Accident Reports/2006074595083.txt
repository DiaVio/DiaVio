This two-vehicle crash occurred in a traffic signal-controlled intersection. The north/south roadway has three lanes, one in each direction and a left-turn lane, and is straight with a slight downhill grade for the southbound lanes. The east/west roadway has five lanes, two in each direction and a left-turn lane, and is straight with a slight uphill grade for the westbound lanes. The speed limit for both roadways is 56 kmph (35 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.

Vehicle one (V1), a 1998 Jeep Cherokee, was southbound in lane one, approaching the intersection. V2, a 2000 Dodge Stratus, was westbound in lane two. The driver of V2 failed to notice that his traffic signal was red. In the intersection, the front of V1 struck the right side of V2. Both vehicles came to rest west of the intersection. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The driver of V1, a forty-two-year-old female, was traveling from work to her home. She was well rested and very familiar with her vehicle and her route. She noticed that her light was green and proceeded through the intersection without looking to either side.

The driver of V2, an eighteen-year-old male, was traveling from work, making a delivery. He was well rested but was experiencing a headache, for which he had taken Aspirin. He was very familiar with his vehicle and his route. He did not notice the traffic signal at the intersection.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: other vehicle encroachment, from crossing street- across path. The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event not coded to this vehicle.

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded: this vehicle traveling; crossing over (passing through) intersection. The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded as a driver related factor: inadequate surveillance (e.g. failed to look, looked but did not see.
