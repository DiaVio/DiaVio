The case involves a single vehicle impacting a large tree with its front end. The moderate impact resulted in severe injuries to the restrained 67-year-old female driver of the vehicle (case occupant). The case vehicle a four-door 2002 Suzuki Esteem was eastbound on a wet two-lane asphalt roadway with a 4% downgrade. It was raining and dark but the roadway was lighted. The driver experienced a hypoglycemic episode and lost control of the vehicle. The vehicle crossed the westbound lane, ran over the north curb and struck a 62-cm tree with the left side of its front end and came to a rest facing northeast. The case vehicle was towed due to damage. The 67-year-old female driver was not restrained by a three-point belt but the steering-wheel air bag deployed. The 82-year-old female right-front passenger was restrained by a three-point belt and the mid-instrument panel air bag deployed. The right-front passenger sustained police reported non-incapacitating injuries and was tranported to a local hospital. The driver was transferred to a level-one trauma center with serious injuries and was enrolled as a case occupant.
