This crash involved a 88 year old female in a moderate severity frontal crash. She was the front right passenger restrained with a lap/shoulder belt and the pretensioners actuated and the front instrument panel airbag deployed. She sustained serious injuries and was airlifted to the trauma center. This crash occurred at an intersection during daylight hours with clear, dry conditions. Case vehicle one (V1), a 2010 Dodge Journey four door hatchback was travelling southbound on a two lane, two way highway where it curved to the left with a downgrade and then intersected with a two lane, two way roadway from the west with no traffic controls for north and south traffic. Vehicle two, a 2006 Chrysler 300 four door sedan, was heading northbound on the same roadway that curved to left with an upgrade and came to a stop to turn left at this intersection. Vehicle three (V3), a 1995 Toyota Tacoma pickup, was travelling northbound behind V2 and struck the front of V3 into the back of V2. This forced V2 into the southbound lane where the front of V2 impacted the front of V1 as it entered the intersection. Post impact V2 rotated counterclockwise and V1 rotated clockwise and the right side back corner of V2 impacted the left side of V1. V1 then came to final rest facing west over the double yellow line of the intersecting road. V2 continued to the southwest corner of the intersection and impacted the face of the guard rail and came to final rest against it facing southwest. V3 came to final rest facing mainly north in the northbound travel lane. All three vehicles were towed due to damage. The case occupant was an 88 year old female who was restrained with the manual lap/shoulder belt and on impact the belt pretensioners actuated and the frontal instrument mounted air bag deployed in her position. She was airlifted to the trauma center and hospitalized. The driver of V1 was an 83 year old male who was wearing the manual lap/shoulder belt and on impact the belt pretensioners actuated and the frontal steering column mounted airbag deployed. He was treated and released with minor injuries. No other occupants were in V1.
