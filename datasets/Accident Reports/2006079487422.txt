This two-vehicle crash occurred on a seven-lane roadway. This roadway is straight and level with no defects. The speed limit is 64 kmph (40 mph). There was rush hour congestion on the road at the time of the crash. This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.

Vehicle one (V1), a 1999 Toyota Corolla was westbound in the second lane. Vehicle two (V2), a 2001 Honda Passport was stopped facing west in the second lane. The front plane of V1 impacted the rear plane of V2. V1 was towed with major damage. V2 was driven with moderate damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The driver of V1, a thirty-nine year old female, refused to be interviewed. There is no known pre-crash information for this driver.

The driver of V2, a twenty-three year old male, was on his way home from school. This driver had been driving for twenty-five minutes at the time of the crash. His arm was in a sling from a previous injury. This driver stated that before the crash he looked back and saw V1 coming and tried to steer left to avoid but was still impacted. This driver drives this vehicle daily and over this road on a daily basis.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: other motor vehicle in lane, other vehicle stopped. The critical pre-crash event was attributed to V1 and was coded as a driver related factor: type of driver error unknown.

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded: other motor vehicle in lane, traveling in the same direction with higher speed. The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event was not coded to this vehicle.
