This two vehicle daytime collision occurred mid-morning. The collision occurred at a four-way intersection, on a five lane, undivided, dry, level, asphalt roadway. The roadway is designated 56 kmph (35 mph). The intersection is controlled by a on-color signal that was working properly at the time.

Vehicle one (V1), a 2004 Ford Focus 4-door wagon driven by a seventeen (17) year old female, was traveling north in the number three (left turn) lane. Vehicle two (V2), a 2000 Hyundai Sonata 4-door sedan driven by a nineteen (19) year old female, was traveling south in the number two lane. As V1 negotiated a left turn the front of V2 contacted the right side of V1. V1 came to rest west of the intersection facing northeast in the eastbound lanes. V2 came to rest facing west in the intersection. V1 and V2 were towed with moderate damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: this vehicle traveling, turning left at intersection. The critical reason for the critical event was coded to V1 as a driver related factor: false assumption of other's actions. The driver of V1 only gave a partial interview. The driver said that she saw the other vehicle but thought that it would stop for the yellow light. When she realized it was not going to stop she attempted to accelerate but missed the pedal and was unable to get out of the way.

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded: other vehicle encroachment; from opposite direction - over left lane line. Critical reason not coded to this vehicle.
