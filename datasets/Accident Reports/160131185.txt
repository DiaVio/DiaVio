Vehicle One (V1), a 2003 Dodge Grand Caravan minivan, was traveling south in lane four of a six lane, two-way roadway under dry, daylight conditions and approaching an intersection. Vehicle Two (V2), a 1997 Dodge Ram 3500 pickup truck, was traveling north in lane two of the same roadway approaching the same intersection. V1 attempted to make a left turn at the intersection and the right front of V1 contacted the front of V2. Both vehicles came to final rest in the intersection and both vehicles were towed due to damage. The driver of V1 (case study occupant) was an unbelted, 45-year-old male and had both the frontal air bags deploy. The right front passenger of V1, a 39 year-old female, was wearing the available three-point lap and shoulder belt. The driver and (case study occupant) of V1 was transported by helicopter to a level-one trauma center for treatment. The passenger in V1 was transported to a local hospital by ambulance for treatment. The driver of V2, a 71-year-old male, was uninjured and not transported.
