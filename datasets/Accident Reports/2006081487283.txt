5A two-vehicle intersection collision occurred a couple hours before midnight on a Monday. The collision occurred at a busy intersection with traffic signals. The north/south roadway has 4 northbound lanes with a slight uphill grade prior to the intersection. The east/west roadway is also divided with 4 eastbound lanes. It was raining heavily, and the roadway was wet. Both directions of the roadway were straight with bituminous paving. The posted speed limit for vehicle one (V1) is 72 Kmph (45mph). The posted speed limit for vehicle two (V2) is 56 Kmph (35 Mph). Access to the roadway is not controlled.

V1, a 1990 Ford Bronco Utility with two occupants, was headed northbound in lane 2. As V1 entered the intersection, the traffic control signal was red. V2, a 2007 Honda Accord 4-door sedan with one occupant, was traveling eastbound in lane two, on a green light. The front of V1 impacted the right front corner of V2. Both vehicles came to rest in the intersection. The drivers of both vehicles were transported to local hospitals due to injuries. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. A police officer witnessed the crash and stated on the report that the driver of V1 ran the red light. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The driver of V1 was a 34 year-old female whose pre-crash factors are not known, as she was not interviewed.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded "This Vehicle Traveling: crossing through (passing through) intersection”. The critical reason for the critical event assigned to this driver is "illegal maneuver”.

The driver of V2 was a 24 year-old female whose pre-crash factors are not known, as she was not interviewed.
