This two-vehicle crash occurred in a four-legged intersection. The north/south roadway is straight, level, undivided, consists of one lane in each direction and is controlled by stop signs. The east/west roadway is straight, level, and consists of two lanes in each direction and a center two-way left turn lane. The speed limit for these roadways is 72 kmph (45 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.

Vehicle one (V1), a 1995 Jeep Cherokee, was stopped northbound in lane one. Vehicle two (V2), a 1994 Dodge Dakota, was westbound in lane two. There were non-contact vehicles in the left turn lane of the westbound roadway. V1 entered the intersection and the right side was struck by the front of V2. The vehicles rotated and the right side of V1 struck the left side of V2. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The driver of V1, a seventeen-year-old female, was on her way home from a local hospital. She was healthy, well rested and familiar with her vehicle. She travels this route weekly. She had eight months of driving experience at the time of the crash. This driver stated that she was thinking about how her father was going to react to a minor crash that she had earlier in the day. She also stated that she did not see V2 prior to the crash.

The driver of V2, an eighteen -year-old female, was not interviewed. She told the officer at the scene that she did not see V1 before she entered the intersection. No further information is available for this driver.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: this vehicle traveling; crossing over (passing through) intersection. The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as a driver related factor: inattention (i.e. daydreaming). This driver's inexperience was an associated factor in this crash.

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded: other vehicle encroachment; from crossing street, across path. The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event was not coded to this vehicle.
