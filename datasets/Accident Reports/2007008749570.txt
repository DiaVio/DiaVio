This crash occurred in the eastbound lane of a two-lane undivided roadway. There was one eastbound lane and one westbound lane. The roadway was straight, with a downhill grade, bituminous, and had a posted speed limit of 56 kmph (35 mph). It was evening on a weekday and conditions were clear.

V1 was a 1998 Oldsmobile Intrigue, driven by an 18 year-old male heading west. V2 was a 2007 Chevrolet Cobalt, driven by a 64 year-old female heading east. V1 was traveling westbound when the vehicle stalled and began drifting into the eastbound lane. V1 impacted the front of V2, which was stopped in the eastbound lane. Police Reported Travel Speed is 40 kmph for V1 and 40 kmph for V2.

The Critical Precrash Event for V1 was "this vehicle traveling, over the lane line on left side of travel lanes." The Critical Reason for the Critical Precrash Event was "vehicle related factor, engine failure." Reading the EDR confirmed that the power to the vehicle was indeed cut off before the impact. The driver attempted to steer to the right and brake, but the lack of power steering and braking made it impossible for him to avoid the collision.

The Critical Precrash Event for V2 was "other vehicle encroachment, from opposite direction-over left lane line. The Critical Reason was not coded to this vehicle. There were no other factors coded to this vehicle.
