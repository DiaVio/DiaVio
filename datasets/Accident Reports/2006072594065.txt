This single vehicle crash occurred around midnight on a four-lane northbound expressway with shoulders on the east and west sides, curving to the right and is divided by a concrete traffic barrier. The roadway is level, has no deficiencies and was dark but lit by street lamps. The east side of the expressway also has a concrete traffic barrier lining the roadway. The speed limit for the roadway was 89 kmph (55 mph). Marked city police cruisers were pursuing the driver at the time.

Vehicle one (V1), a 2004 four-door Chevrolet Trail Blazer with a 25-year-old male driver, was traveling northbound in lane one of the expressway evading marked police cruisers. The vehicle ran off the right side of the roadway where the front right corner struck the concrete barrier. The right rear corner then sideswiped the barrier, as the vehicle was re-directed across the roadway where the front left corner struck the concrete barrier in the median. The left side then side-slapped the barrier. It is unknown where the vehicle came to rest. The driver was transported to a local hospital with non-incapacitating injuries and the vehicle was towed due to extensive damage. Alcohol tests were performed with unknown results. The police reported the driver to be under the influence of drugs/alcohol and the driver was also cited for failure to reduce speed to avoid a crash. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

Marked cruisers were pursuing the driver, a 25-year-old male, at the time of the crash. He was not available for an interview. Medical records could not be located at the trauma center. The critical pre-crash event was coded "this vehicle traveling-off the edge of the road on the right side". The critical reason was coded "external distraction".
