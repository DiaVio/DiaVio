This single-vehicle crash occurred just past dawn on a six-lane, downhill, right curve, bituminous interstate highway. The highway generally runs east/west; however, at this location runs northeast/southwest. Both east and westbound lanes consist of three through lanes that are separated by a depressed grassy median. There are warning signs for the eastbound lanes that warn truckers of steep grades and sharp curves ahead. The speed limit on the highway is 89 kmph (55 mph) and there were no adverse atmospheric conditions present.

Vehicle one (V1), a 1992 Chevrolet S-10 Blazer 4x4, was driven by a 56 year-old male who was traveling eastbound in lane three negotiating a right curve. The driver fell asleep and traveled off the left side of the road. V1 crossed the depressed grassy median and entered the paved shoulder and part of lane three of westbound traffic. The driver over corrected to the right and began to rotate clockwise back into the depressed grassy median. The driver once again over corrected to the left and the vehicle began to rotate counterclockwise. The wheels of V1 dug into the soft dirt and caused the vehicle to roll to the right It rolled four quarter turns and came to rest on its wheels facing northwest in the middle of the grassy median. V1 was towed due to severe top damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 89 kmph for V1.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: this vehicle traveling, off the edge of the road on the left side. The critical reason for the critical event was coded to V1 as a driver related factor: sleeping, that is actually asleep. Due to work, this driver only slept for 5.5 hours two nights before the crash. He had been awake for 24 hours when the crash occurred and works 12 hours per day.
