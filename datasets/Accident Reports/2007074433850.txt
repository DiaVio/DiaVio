This two-vehicle crash occurred curbed median-divided trafficway, on the approach to a traffic signal-controlled intersection. The eastbound lanes are level, curve to the right and consist of four through lanes and a left turn lane. The speed limit for this roadway is 72 kmph (45 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions. Traffic was congested at the time of this crash.

Vehicle one (V1), a 2002 Pontiac Grand Am, was eastbound in lane four. Vehicle two (V2), a 2002 Jeep Liberty, was eastbound in lane three. According to witnesses and the driver of V2, V1 moved to the right and the front struck the back of V2. V1 then rolled over to the left two quarter-turns and was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The driver of V1, a forty-nine-year-old female, had left work and was going to stop at a store on her way home. She was well rested, in good health and familiar with her vehicle. She travels this route daily. This driver has high blood pressure, high cholesterol and a seizure disorder and takes the medications Micardis, Zocor and Carbatrol. This driver stated that she thought that V2 moved into her lane.

The driver of V2, a thirty-year-old female, was on her way home from a store. She was healthy, well rested and familiar with her vehicle. She travels this route daily. This driver stated that she was decelerating due to slowing traffic and did not see V1 prior to the crash.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "other motor vehicle in lane; traveling in same direction while decelerating." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "driver related factor; inattention."

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded as "this vehicle traveling; this vehicle decelerating." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V2 was "not coded to this vehicle."
