Vehicle 1 (V1) is a 2002 Pontiac Grand Am, two-door (case study vehicle). Vehicle 2 (V2) is a 1998 Ford Taurus, four-door. V1 was occupied by a male, age 41 as its driver and lone occupant (case study participant). The case study participant was wearing the available lap and shoulder belt and the frontal air bags did deploy as a result of impact.

The crash occurred during the hours of dawn. The weather at the time of the crash was clear and dry. The roadway surface was dry. The posted speed limit is 55 mph (88 kmph).

The crash occurred on a two lane rural route. The roadway is hilly and curvy. The westbound lane is straightaway on a negative grade. The eastbound lane is a left hand curve with a positive grade. The lanes are divided by a double yellow line. A small shoulder exists in both directions. The roadway is bordered by a heavily wooded area.

V1 was traveling westbound and V2 was traveling eastbound V2 crossed into V1's lane. V2 struck the front end of V1 with its front end. The impact caused V1 to rotate counterclockwise and come to rest partially on the shoulder of the westbound lane. V2 rotated counterclockwise and came to rest in the eastbound lane of travel.

The case study participant was transported by ground to a local emergency room then transferred to a trauma center by air.
