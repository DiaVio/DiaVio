This crash occurred on a weekday morning at the junction of an on-ramp and two westbound lanes of an interstate highway. The concrete roadway was wet and it was raining at the time. There was a grade of -1.6 percent and the roadway was straight but the entrance ramp curved to the right. The speed limit was 89 kmph (55mph).

V1 was a 1996 Buick Roadmaster station wagon driven by an 18-year old female. Police Reported Travel Speed is No PAR obtained (created) for V1.

As the driver of Vehicle 1 negotiated the curve of the entrance ramp, she accelerated heading westbound to enter the interstate. The vehicle began to rotate clockwise and crossed both travel lanes. The back left of Vehicle 1 swiped the concrete barrier on the left side of the travel lanes. Vehicle 1 then crossed the travel lanes in a northerly direction and then the right shoulder. The front of the vehicle contacted the other concrete barrier and V1 came to rest straddling lane 1. The driver stated that she fishtailed due to the wet roadway and was unable to regain control. She indicated that surrounding traffic was not a factor in the crash.

The Critical Pre-crash Event was Vehicle 1 traveling off the edge of the road on the left side. The Critical Reason for the Critical Pre-crash Event was poor directional control. Associated factors coded to this driver include traveling too fast for conditions, driver inexperience, wet roadways and rain.
