This crash occurred in the southbound lanes of a two lane highway. There is one northbound and one southbound lane. This road curved to the right, somewhat leveled, bituminous roadway with a posted speed limit of 72 kmph (45 mph). It was early afternoon on a weekday and the road was dry and the sky was clear. Traffic flow was minimal.

V1, a 2001 Chevy Tahoe with 4-doors, was traveling southbound in lane 1. V1 after passing through the intersection kept going straight even thought the road curved to the right. She departed the roadway to the left and struck the guardrail and telephone pole with the right front corner of her vehicle. The car then rolled over on the right side while rotating around the telephone pole. The car ended up on its roof after the left front corner contacted the guardrail. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

The 2001 Chevy Tahoe was driven by a 44 year-old female who was transported from the scene due to injuries. She stated that she had just come back from the doctors office for a regular visit. She says that she knows that on that certain road she should take extra precaution because she knows that there are frequent accidents there and also because the roads are windy and narrow. She stated that she wasn't really concentrating like she should have been and just lost control of the car. She doesn't remember rolling over but does remember hitting the telephone pole. The driver has had "ups and downs" when it comes to her health. She is a diabetic and takes insulin. She stated after she left the Doctors office, she was just ready to go home. She may have been thinking about her ongoing health issue.

The Critical Precrash Event for V1 was “this vehicle traveling off the edge of the road on the left side”. The Critical Reason for the Critical Precrash Event was a driver related factor “inattention”.
