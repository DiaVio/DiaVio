This crash occurred in the intersection of two roadways, one being a five-lane undivided asphalt rural roadway with a posted speed limit of 89 kmph (55 mph), and the other being a three-lane undivided asphalt rural roadway with a posted speed limit of 72 kmph (45 mph). The intersection was controlled by traffic signals in all directions. The crash occurred during the daylight hours, and there were no adverse conditions present at the time of the crash.

V1 was a 2007 Chevrolet Trailblazer utility vehicle traveling westbound in the second lane approaching the intersection. V2, a 2001 GMC Sierra pickup truck, was traveling northbound in the right curb lane approaching intersection. V3, a 2000 Chevrolet Silverado pickup truck, was stopped in southbound second lane waiting to turn left. V1 proceeded through the intersection on a red light, striking V2 in the right side rear. This impact caused V2 to rotate clockwise, and its left rear side struck the front of V3. V3 was pushed backward by the impact, and V2 rolled over one quarter turn onto its left side. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The driver of V1 was a 21-year-old male who stated that he fell asleep while traveling to a doctor's visit. The driver of V1 also stated that he does not remember anything before the impact with V2. The Critical Pre-Crash Event coded for V1 was "this vehicle traveling crossing over (passing through) intersection." The Critical Reason for the Critical Pre-Crash Event coded for V1 was "Driver-Related - Asleep, that is actually sleeping." The driver of V1 had been awake for more than 19 hours when the crash occurred. An associated factor coded to the driver of V1 was an illegal maneuver for failing to obey a traffic control device.

The driver of V2, a 65-year-old male, stated he was on his way home, had the green light and proceeded into the intersection. The driver also stated he does not remember crash events after entering the intersection until speaking with EMS personnel. The Critical Pre-Crash Event coded for V2 was "other vehicle encroachment - from crossing street across path." V2 was not assigned a Critical Reason. Associated factors coded to V2 include several medical conditions and the use of prescription medications to treat these conditions.

The driver of V3, a 61-year-old male, stated that he was waiting to turn left when he saw V1 run the stoplight and strike V2. He then saw V2 roll over onto his vehicle. No Critical Pre-Crash Event and no Critical Reason were coded for V3 because V3 was not involved in the first harmful event. No associated factors were coded to the driver of V3.
