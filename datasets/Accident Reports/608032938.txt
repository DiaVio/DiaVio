This case involves a primary front-end plane to front-end plane impact resulting in serious injuries to the restrained 20-year-old female driver. The case vehicle a 2-door 2001 Pontiac Grand Am was eastbound on a dry, level asphalt two-lane roadway. Vehicle two (V2) a two-door 2006 Chevrolet Monte Carlo was westbound on the same roadway. It was daylight and there was no adverse weather. V2 drifted over the center line into the path of the case vehicle. The driver of V2 attempted to avoid the collision by braking and steering right. The driver of the case vehicle attempted to avoid the collision by braking. The front of V2 struck the front of the case vehicle in an off-set mode. The impact caused the case vehicle to rotate counterclockwise into the eastbound lane where it came to rest facing north. V2 rotate clockwise and also came to rest in the eastbound lane facing north. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. The 20-year-old female driver was the sole occupant. She was restrained by a lap and shoulder belt and the steering-wheel air bag deployed. She was transported to a level-one trauma center and enrolled as a case occupant.
