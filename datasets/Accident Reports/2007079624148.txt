This two-vehicle crash occurred in a four-legged, traffic signal-controlled intersection. The roadways west and south of the intersection are undivided, straight, level and consist of one lane in each direction. The speed limit for these roadways is 56 kmph (35 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.

Vehicle one (V1), a 2004 Dodge Ram pickup, was eastbound in lane one. Vehicle two (V2), a 2006 Chevrolet Malibu, was northbound in lane one. In the intersection, the front of V1 struck the left side of V2. V2 rotated counterclockwise and traveled toward the northeast corner of the intersection and the back struck the curb. The left side of V2 then struck a utility pole. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The driver of V1, a sixty-five-year-old male, was traveling from his home to have lunch with his seventy-year-old male passenger. This driver was well rested and comfortable with his vehicle and passenger. He takes vitamins, Lovastatin and an unknown prostate medication. This driver stated that he did not see V2 until they were both in the intersection and at the last minute, applied the brakes in an attempt to avoid the crash.

The driver of V2, a thirty-one-year-old female, was not interviewed. No information is available for this driver.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "this vehicle traveling; crossing over (passing through) intersection." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "driver related factor; inadequate surveillance (e.g. failed to look, looked but did not see)."

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded as "other vehicle encroachment; from crossing street, across path." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V2 was "not coded to this vehicle."
