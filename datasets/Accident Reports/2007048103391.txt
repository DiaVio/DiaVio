This single-vehicle crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions. The roadway was level, straight and divided by a grass median. The westbound lanes consist of two through lanes. The speed limit for this roadway is 105 kmph (65 mph).

Vehicle one (V1), a 2001 Hyundai Sonata, was westbound in lane two. A non-contact vehicle in lane one began to change lanes to the left. The driver of V1 applied the brakes and steered left and then right. V1 rotated clockwise and traveled off the right side of the roadway. After rotating through the grassy roadside, the front of V1 struck a small embankment. V1 then rolled over to the right two quarter-turns and came to rest on its top. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 121 kmph for V1.

The driver of V1, a thirty-year-old female, was traveling from her home to her friend's house. She was healthy, well rested and familiar with her vehicle. She drives this route weekly. This driver stated that she had been traveling at 121 kmph (75 mph).

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "other vehicle encroachment; from adjacent lane (same direction) - over right lane line." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "driver related factor; too fast to be able to respond to unexpected actions of others."
