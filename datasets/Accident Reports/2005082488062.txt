This two vehicle daytime collision occurred shortly past noon. The collision occurred at an intersection comprised by a west/east roadway and a north/south roadway. Both roadways are two-way, two lane and bituminous. The north/south roadway is controlled by a stop sign. Both roadways were dry, level, free of congestion, poor weather conditions, and/or road deficiencies at the time of the collision. Both roadways are designated as 25 mph (40 kph) zones.

Vehicle one (V1), a 2005 Chevrolet Impala, driven by a 28 year-old male was traveling southbound. Vehicle two (V2), a 2001 Chevrolet Monte Carlo, driven by a 29 year-old male with three passengers was traveling eastbound. As the vehicles entered the intersection the front of V1 impacted the left side of V2. V1 rotated counterclockwise continuing in a southeast direction. The back of V1 end swiped a stop sign on the southeast corner of the intersection. V1 came to rest up on the southeast corner facing northwest. V2 continued after impact in a southeast direction departing the east side of the roadway. The front of V2 impacted a chain link fence and the left side of V2 impacted a small tree. V2 came to rest up against the stoop of a house facing southeast. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The pre-event movement for V2 coded going straight, category other vehicle encroachment from crossing street across path. Critical reason not coded to this vehicle.
