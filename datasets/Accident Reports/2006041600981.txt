The crash occurred in the westbound lanes of an urban roadway that was level and straight. There were three lanes running westbound with lane three being a left turn only lane. There were three lanes running eastbound with lane three being a left turn only lane. There was a posted speed limit 48 KPH (30 mph) traffic flow was normal. The crash occurred on a weekday morning and the skies were clear.

Vehicle #1 was a 2005 Monte Carlo that was driving by a 22-year-old female who was traveling in lane two going the speed limit who stated in the interview that V2, a 1995 Toyota Corolla that was driven by a 49-year-old male suddenly came to a stop for no apparent reason. V1 was unable to stop in time without making contact with V2. There were no evident damages to V2 and the driver complained of neck pain but refused treatment from EMS. His vehicle was not towed due to damages. V1 was not injured but her vehicle was towed due to damages as she was losing engine and transmission fluids. This crash was a typical rear-end collision. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was another vehicle stopped in lane. The critical reason was driver related, inattention. She was cited a ticket for causing the crash which was failure to use due care. There were no other associative factors related to the driver.

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was another vehicle in lane traveling at a higher rate of speed. The critical reason was not related to this driver, as he was not thought to have contributed to the crash. He was cited a ticket for no proof of insurance. Although, he was not thought to have contributed to the crash it is unknown as to why he came to a sudden stop for no reason. There were no associative factors related to the driver.
