This three vehicle daytime collision occurred at 1607 hours. The collision occurred on the northbound lanes of a divided interstate highway, in the third of three lanes. The pre-crash roadway for all vehicles is straight and level, dry worn concrete paving. The roadway has a posted speed limit of 96 Kmph (60mph).

Vehicle 1 (V 1), a 1994 Chevrolet Astro Van, driven by a 34 year-old male was traveling northbound on the roadway in lane three. Vehicle 2 (V 2), a 2004 Chevrolet Silverado Large pickup, driven by a 35 year-old male was traveling northbound in lane three. Vehicle 3 (V 3), a 2000 Toyota 4Runner compact utility vehicle, driven by a 29 year-old male was traveling northbound in lane 3. V 3 slowed for traffic congestion, and V 2 slowed behind V 3 within one car length. V 1 did not slow, and the front of V 1 collided off-center with the rear of V 2. This impact forced V 2 into V 3, although the damage to the front of V 2 and the rear of V 3 was not noticeable. V 1 was towed due to disabling damage, and the driver of V 2 was transported to a local medical facility. V-1,V-3 were driven from the scene. The driver of V 1 could not be interviewed due to a language barrier, and no interpreter could be contacted. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
