This case involves a severe, right off-set, impact to the front of the case vehicle with a large tree. The case occupant is the 31-year-old male driver. He was not restrained by a three-point belt, but the steering-wheel air bag deployed. The case vehicle a 2005 Ford F-150 4x2 pick-up truck was northbound on a dry, level, asphalt road. It was dark, but lighted and clear. It must be noted that there was an unoccupied and unrestrained convertible seat in the right front seat. The case vehicle crossed the center line, drove off the road, struck a sign-post, and a wooden rail fence, before striking a tree in a right, off-set, frontal-impact mode. The 31-year-old male driver was the sole occupant of the vehicle. He was not restrained by a three-point belt but the steering-wheel air bag deployed. The 31-year-old male driver self-extricated from the vehicle, and was admitted to a local hospital. He was transferred to a level-one trauma center four-and-a-half hours post-crash and enrolled as a case occupant.
