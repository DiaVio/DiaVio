This crash occurred on a cloudy late afternoon day with no adverse weather conditions present. All three vehicles were traveling west in the fourth lane of a five lane concrete, level divided straight expressway with no controls. The speed limit on the roadway was 97 kmph (60 mph). The crash occurred during rush hour congestion.

Vehicle three (V3), a 1996 Honda Accord 4 door sedan with two occupants, was stopped in westbound lane four because of traffic congestion ahead. Vehicle two (V2), a 1998 Ford F150 pickup with one occupant, was also in lane four behind V3 and was decelerating to come to a stop behind V3. Vehicle one (V1), a 1995 Ford F150 pickup with one occupant, was behind V2 also in lane four. The front of V1 struck the rear of V2 that moved forward and the front of V2 struck the rear of V3. All three vehicles came to rest in lane four. V1 and V2 were towed from the scene; V3 was driven. The driver of V1 received minor injuries and was not transported. Police Reported Travel Speed is Unknown for V1 and Unknown for V2.

A 37 year-old male who takes only an unknown arthritis medication along with Pepcin drove V1. The driver had 6.5 hours of sleep the night before the crash. The driver also had been working 10 hours at a construction site before going home. At the time of the crash the driver of was heading west with the cruise control on listening to easy listening music in rush hour traffic. The driver was conscious of the heavy traffic and was in no hurry but failed to recognize that traffic was slowing down in his lane up ahead. He braked prior to impact but could not avoid V2. He drives the route daily and is familiar with the vehicle.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: other motor vehicle in lane, traveling in same direction while decelerating. The critical reason was coded to V1 as a driver related factor: inattention (i.e., daydream).

A 28 year-old male drove V2. He was traveling west, going home after putting in 10 hours on a construction site. The driver was aware that traffic had slowed down in the lane he was driving in and had slowed down to less than 16 kmph (10 mph). The driver stated that he did not hear any braking before he was s truck by the front of V1.

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded: this vehicle traveling-this vehicle decelerating. A critical reason was not assigned to this vehicle.

A 23 year-old female drove V3. She takes birth control, vitamins and antibiotics. V3 also had a 28 year-old male passenger, the fiance of the driver. The driver was traveling west and had stopped in the fourth lane due to traffic congestion ahead. The driver and passenger were heading to a bridal shower for the driver in a city about 2 hours away. The driver was somewhat excited about the shower, but was still irritated with the passenger about an argument they had the night before. At the time of the crash the driver was talking to the passenger, heard the collision between V1 and V2 but did not have time to track the sound before being struck.

V3 was not involved in first harmful event and the critical reason was not coded to this vehicle.
