Vehicle 1 (V1) is a 2004 Nissan Maxima, four-door (case study vehicle). Vehicle 2 (V2) is a 2000 Land Rover Range Rover, four-door. V1 was occupied by a female, age 60 as its driver (case study participant). The case study participant was not wearing the available lap and shoulder belt. The right side curtain air bag and the front right seat back thorax bag deployed as a result of impact. The crash occurred during the hours of daylight. The weather at the time of the crash was clear and dry. The roadway surface was dry. The posted speed limit is 45 mph (72 kmph). The crash occurred on a six lane divided trafficway. The roadway is divided by a raised concrete median. The roadway travels in a north-south direction. The northbound roadway consists of two through lanes and a dedicated right turn lane. The southbound roadway consists of three through lanes. The northbound direction has a negative grade and the southbound direction has a positive grade. The roadway is straight in the area of the crash. V1 was traveling northbound in the left through lane. V2 was traveling southbound in the left through lane. V1 crossed into the southbound lanes. V1 was profiling the right side of the vehicle as it entered the southbound lanes. V2's front struck V1's left side. V1 and V2 then rotated towards each other and V1's right side struck the left side of V2. The case study participant was transported by air to a trauma center.
