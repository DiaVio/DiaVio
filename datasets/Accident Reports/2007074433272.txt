This two-vehicle crash occurred in a four-legged, traffic signal-controlled intersection. Both trafficways are straight and divided by a curbed median. The northbound approach has an uphill grade and consists of two through lanes, a left turn lane and a right turn lane. The westbound approach has a downhill grade and consists of two through lanes, two left turn lanes and a right turn lane. The speed limit for these trafficway is 72 kmph (45 mph). It was dark with streetlights on at the time of the crash with no adverse atmospheric conditions.

Vehicle one (V1), a 1998 Suzuki GSX motorcycle, was traveling northbound in lane three at a high rate of speed, fleeing from police. Vehicle two (V2), a 2001 Pontiac Grand Am, was westbound in lane three. In the intersection, the front of V1 struck the left side of V2. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The driver of V1, a nineteen-year-old male, was not interviewed. A surrogate was interviewed and provided minimal information. According to this surrogate, this driver had no training and little experience on motorcycles. No further information is available for this driver.

The driver of V2, a twenty-three-year-old female, was on her way home from her boyfriend's house. She was healthy, well rested and familiar with her vehicle. She travels this route daily. This driver stated that she did not see V1 prior to the crash.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: this vehicle traveling; crossing over (passing through) intersection. The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as a driver related factor: aggressive driving behavior.

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded: other vehicle encroachment; from crossing street, across path. The critical reason for the critical event was not coded to this vehicle. This driver's inadequate surveillance was an associated factor in this crash.
