Vehicle 1 (V1) is a 1996 Acura Integra, four-door (case study vehicle). Vehicle 2 (V2) is a 1993 Ford Escort, station wagon. V1 was occupied by a female, age 18 as its driver (case study participant). The case study participant was wearing the available lap and shoulder belt and the frontal air bags did deploy as a result of impact.

The crash occurred during the hours of dusk. The weather at the time of the crash was clear and dry. The roadway surface was dry. The posted speed limit is 40 mph (64 kmph).

The crash occurred on a four lane roadway. The roadway is divided by a double yellow line. The roadway travels in a north-south direction. The southbound direction has a right hand curve with a negative grade. The northbound direction is a straightaway with a positive grade.

V1 was traveling northbound in the right through lane and V2 was traveling southbound in the left through lane. V2 crossed into the northbound lanes. V2 impacted V1's frontal plane with the front of V2. The impact caused V1 to be forced rearward and rotate counterclockwise coming to rest on the shoulder of the northbound lane. V2 came to rest in the left through lane of the southbound lanes.

The case study participant was transported by ground to a level-one trauma center.
