This single-vehicle crash occurred in a rural area on a four-lane roadway with a jersey-barrier wall dividing the northbound and southbound lanes. The southbound asphalt roadway curves left with a radius of 372 meters and slopes uphill. The speed limit for the area is 104 kph (65 mph) with an advisory speed of 96 kph (60 mph) for the curve. The crash occurred during early morning darkness with no adverse weather or road conditions.

Vehicle #1, a 1996 Jeep Cherokee was southbound in the first lane when it departed the right road edge and contacted a guardrail post with its front bumper plane. Vehicle one rotated counter-clockwise and again contacted the guardrail with its front bumper plane and came to rest on the dirt shoulder facing east. Vehicle one was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 89 kmph for V1.

The driver of vehicle one, a 24-year-old male, had started this trip three and one-half hours prior to the crash and had traveled 320 km (200 miles). He was commuting to work from his home in another town. He works an average of 9 hours each weekday. He travels home for the weekend on Fridays and returns to work early on Mondays. He stated that his cell phone had been placed originally on the dashboard but had fallen onto the passenger floorboard. It began to ring and he looked down into the dark interior to see where it had fallen and reached for it. That is when his vehicle departed the right road edge onto the gravel shoulder. He attempted to steer left but did not avoid the end of the guardrail and struck it with the front of his vehicle. This driver drives this vehicle daily and over this road on a weekly basis.

The critical pre-crash event for vehicle one was coded as "this vehicle traveling: off the edge of the road on the right side". The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for vehicle one was coded as "driver related factor: internal distraction".
