This two-vehicle crash occurred during daylight hours in a four-legged intersection. Both roadways are straight and two-lane. The eastbound lane is uphill and the southbound lane is downhill at this location. There are stop signs for east and westbound traffic. The speed limit for east/west traffic is 40 kmph (25 mph) and for north/south traffic is 48 kmph (30 mph). There were no adverse weather conditions at the time of the crash.

Vehicle one (V1), a 1994 Ford F-250 pickup, was eastbound approaching the intersection, intending to continue in a straight path. Vehicle two (V2), a 1994 Gillig city bus, was southbound traveling through the intersection. V1 failed to stop at the intersection and the front struck the right side of V2. V1 rotated clockwise and came to rest facing southeast at the southwest corner of the intersection. V2 came to a controlled stop south of the intersection. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The driver of V1, a twenty-eight-year-old male, was driving a company vehicle from his home to work. He was well rested and very familiar with the route. He claimed that he braked for the stop sign, but there is no evidence to support this statement.

The driver of V2, a thirty-five-year-old male, was driving a city-owned transit bus between stops. There were three passengers on the bus at the time of the crash. He was comfortable with the passenger load and had been driving a bus for five years. He had gotten married four days prior to the crash and may have been distracted by this recent life change.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: this vehicle traveling, crossing over (passing through) intersection. The critical reason for the event was attributed to V1 and was coded as a driver related factor: illegal maneuver.

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded: other vehicle encroachment; from crossing street, across path. The critical reason was not coded to this vehicle.
