This two-vehicle crash occurred at the intersection of a five-lane road and a two-lane road. Both roads are straight and level. The speed limit for the five-lane road is 56 kmph (35 mph). This intersection is controlled by a tri-light traffic signal. The crash occurred in early morning hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.

Vehicle one (V1), a 1999 Honda Civic was eastbound in the third lane, a left turn lane. Vehicle two (V2), a 2002 Ford F 150 was westbound in the second lane. As V1 made a left turn its front plane was impacted by the front plane of V2. V1 rotated clockwise and came to rest facing southeast in the intersection. V2 headed southwest and came to rest facing southwest in the intersection. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The driver of V1, a twenty-seven year old female was on her way to work at the time of the crash. This driver had been driving for twenty minutes at the time of the crash. This driver has twelve years of driving experience. She wears glasses for a myopic condition but it is not a restriction on her license. She was listening to the radio at the time. She stated that she feels "grumpy" in the morning and had not had any coffee yet that day. She was also facing east at an early hour of the day, which could have contributed to some sun glare. This driver drives this vehicle daily and over this road on a daily basis..

The driver of V2, a forty-five year old male refused to be interviewed. There is no known pre-crash information for this driver.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: this vehicle traveling, turning left at intersection. The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as a driver related factor: false assumption of other's actions.

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded: other vehicle encroachment; from opposite direction - over left lane line. The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event was not coded to this vehicle.
