Vehicle #1 (case vehicle) was a 1998 Ford Escort, four-door sedan. Vehicle # 2 was a 2003 Nissan 350Z, two-door coupe. There was only one occupant in the case vehicle with the front left occupant (driver) being our case study participant: Driver - was a 55-year-old female/ Redesigned Frontal air bag (steering wheel) with a manual lap and shoulder belt were available/Redesigned Frontal air bag did deployed and it appears the manual lap and shoulder belt was in use. This crash occurred at 15:20 hrs in the afternoon. The roadway was dry and there were no adverse weather conditions. The crash occurred at a four-legged intersection with two north/southbound lanes controlled by a stop sign and two east/westbound lanes. The involved roadways were level with a bituminous construction and a speed limit of 40kmph (25mph). Vehicle #1 (case vehicle) was initially traveling southbound on a two-lane roadway. V2 was initially traveling westbound on a two-lane roadway. As V1 entered the intersection (after stopping at a stop sign) it was impacted on the left side by the front of V2. After initial impact, V1 and V2 side slapped before traveling off the southwest road edge before traveling to final rest. V1 traveled to its final resting position on the sidewalk of the southwest corner of the intersection. V2 traveled to its final resting position on the southwest quadrant of the intersection after impacting an iron fence. The driver of V1 was extricated from the vehicle and then transported from the scene to a local trauma center where she was admitted for 5 days for injuries received in the crash.
