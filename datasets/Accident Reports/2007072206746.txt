This two-vehicle crash occurred in a four-legged, traffic signal-controlled intersection. The north/south roadway is straight, level, undivided and consists of two lanes in each direction with a left turn lane at the intersection. The east/west roadway is straight, level, undivided and consists of one lane in each direction with parking on both sides. The speed limit for these roadways is 48 kmph (30 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.

Vehicle one (V1), a 2000 Ford Focus, was northbound in lane two. Vehicle two (V2), a 2001 Ford Escape, was westbound in lane one. In the intersection, the front of V2 struck the right side of V1. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The driver of V1, a forty-seven-year-old female, was traveling with a forty-six-year-old male. This driver was healthy and comfortable with her vehicle and passenger. She stated that she was looking down and adjusting the radio as they approached the intersection. She further stated that she did not see V2 prior to the crash.

The driver of V2, a twenty-seven-year-old female, was on her way from her home to a store with her fifty-year-old mother. She was healthy and comfortable with her vehicle and passenger. This driver stated that she saw V1 just prior to the crash and applied the brakes in an attempt to avoid the crash.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "this vehicle traveling; crossing over (passing through) intersection." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "driver related factor; internal distraction."

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded as "other vehicle encroachment; from crossing street, across path." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V2 was "not coded to this vehicle."
