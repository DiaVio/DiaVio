This two vehicle crash occurred during mid-afternoon daylight hours on a cool, clear day. At a tri-color traffic signal controlled intersection. Five lanes road surface level, dry concrete with a slight uphill grade. The posted speed for both vehicles was 72 kmph (45 mph).

Vehicle one (V 1) a 2000 Honda Accord 4 door sedan driven by a 32 year male was traveling south in lane two. Vehicle two (V 2) a 2001 Pontiac Grand Am sedan driven by a 64 year-old female was traveling north in the left turn lane. As V 1 approached the intersection he saw the approaching V 2, V 1 noticed that V 2 was not stopping or slowing V 1 slammed on his brakes at this time to avoid hitting V 2. The front of V 1 impacted the left side of V 2 both vehicles came to rest in the intersection. Both vehicles were towed from the scene due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: other vehicle encroachment (from opposite direction), over left lane line. The critical reason was not coded to this vehicle.

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded: this vehicle traveling, turning left at intersection. The critical reason for V2 was a driver related factor: inadequate surveillance. The driver had just finished getting directions to return home.
