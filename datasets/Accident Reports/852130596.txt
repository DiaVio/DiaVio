This crash occurred during daylight hours at an intersection controlled by stop signs for the north and south directions. The weather was clear and the roadway was dry. Case vehicle one (V1), a 2002 Subaru Forester was traveling northbound through the intersection on a two lane, two way roadway. Vehicle two (V2), a 1984 GMC pickup truck was traveling eastbound through the same intersection on a two lane, two way roadway. Just prior to the intersection V2 locked up the brakes and steered to the left slightly before the front of V2 impacted the left side front half of V1. V1 was then deflected clockwise with V2 rotating counterclockwise and then the right side of V2 impacted the left back corner of V1. V2 continued to rotated clockwise and the right side quarter panel then impacted the back right bumper of V1. V1 then departed the roadway northeasterly on the northeast corner of the intersection and had minor impacts involving the front into a barbed wire fence, the left side swiping a light pole and then finally the left tire and rim rolled into a the base of tree trunk where it came to final rest facing northeast. V2 also departed the roadway on the northeast corner into the ditch where the right side also impact the bob wire fencing and it came to final rest facing south in the ditch after rotating about 270 degrees clockwise from point of impact. Both vehicles were damage and towed. The CIREN case occupant is the driver of V1 and the only person in the vehicle. The case occupant is 39 year old female who was wearing the manually lap/shoulder belt and the frontal steering column mounted air bag deployed on impact with V2. The case occupant had severe injuries and transported to the trauma center.
