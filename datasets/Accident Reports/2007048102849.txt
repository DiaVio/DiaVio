This two-vehicle crash occurred on a median-divided roadway in a four-legged intersection. The westbound lanes are straight, level and consist of two through lanes and a left turn lane. The eastbound lanes are straight, have an uphill grade and consist of two through lanes, a right turn lane and a left turn lane. The speed limit on this roadway is 80 kmph (50 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.

Vehicle one (V1), a 1994 Saturn SL, was stopped westbound in lane three, waiting to turn left in the intersection. Vehicle two (V2), a 1999 Chevrolet Tahoe, was eastbound in lane two. V1 turned left and the right side was struck by the front of V2. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 16 kmph for V1 and 16 kmph for V2.

The driver of V1, a forty-eight-year-old male, was on his way back to work from lunch. He was healthy, well rested and familiar with his vehicle. He travels this route daily. This driver stated that he in a hurry because he was late returning to work. He did make any attempts to avoid this crash.

The driver of V2, a thirty-eight-year-old male, was on his way home from work. He was well rested, in good health and familiar with his vehicle and the roadway. This driver saw V1 but did not think that it would turn in front of him. By the time this driver realized that V1 was making the turn, it was too late to make any avoidance maneuvers.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "this vehicle traveling; turning left at intersection." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "driver related factor; inadequate surveillance (e.g. failed to look, looked but did not see)."

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded as "other vehicle encroachment; from opposite direction - over left lane line." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V2 was "not coded to this vehicle."
