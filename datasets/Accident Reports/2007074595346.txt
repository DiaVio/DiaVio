This single-vehicle crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions. The roadway is a one-way ramp that is level, curves to the left and consists of one through lane. On the right side of this road, the grass embankment has a steep downhill grade. The speed limit for this roadway is 89 kmph (55 mph).

Vehicle one (V1), a 1992 Buick Century, was northbound in lane one. V1 traveled off the right side of the road (skidding longitudinally), down the embankment and the front struck a wooden utility pole. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

The driver of V1, a thirty-two-year-old female, had left home, met her boyfriend to get a check and was on the way to a bank. She had her dog in the car with her. This driver was awakened fifteen minutes prior to this trip. She was taking an antibiotic and Advil for dental problems. She only drives this vehicle once a month and this was her first time on this road. This driver stated that she was talking to and looking at the dog just prior to the crash.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "this vehicle control loss due to traveling too fast for conditions." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "driver related factor; too fast for curve/turn." This driver's focus on her dental problems and the distraction of the dog were associated factors in this crash.
