The crash occurred off the left side of a four-lane highway with a 14-meter wide grass median, spotted with small trees, dividing the four southbound lanes from the four northbound lanes. The skies were cloudy on this well-traveled, straight, level, bituminous road. The posted speed was 89 kmph (55 mph) on a weekday at dawn during the early commuting hours.

V1, a 2001 Lexus GS 300 four-door sedan was driven by a 70 year-old male. He stated that he had just left his home, on his way to play golf, when the crash occurred. He was traveling south in lane four at a rate of 89 kmph (55 mph), when a flock of geese, suddenly appeared, walking across the road. He braked and swerved left onto the grass median in an unsuccessful attempt to avoid the geese, lost control of his vehicle and ran into a tree. The driver was not injured. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

After providing the information leading to the crash the driver suddenly became very hostile and told the state trooper to send the researcher away. The state trooper tried to assure him of the researches credentials, but this only upset him further.

The Pre-Crash Event for V1 was " Animal in roadway." The Critical Reason for the Pre-Crash Event was "Incorrect evasive action". Associated factors for the driver include wearing golf shoes while driving, inadequate surveillance, poor directional control (V1 traveled an additional 30 meters after hitting the geese before he hit the tree) and animals on the road.
