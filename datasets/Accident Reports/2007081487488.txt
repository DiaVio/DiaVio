his two-vehicle collision occurred in the early afternoon during daylight hours, The collision occurred just north of an intersection. The north-south leg of the intersection has 5 lanes, and the posted speed limit is 64 kmph (40 mph). The pre-crash roadway is straight and level, with bituminous paving. It was clear, and almost dry, it had rained earlier. Access to the roadway is not controlled.

Vehicle one (V1), a 2000 Honda Civic coupe driven by a thirty nine (39) year old male was headed southbound in lane 1. As V1 approached the intersection the light changed from green to yellow, he stopped fast not to go through the light. Vehicle two (V2), a 1993 Ford Club Wagon Van driven by a twenty nine (29) year old male traveling in same direction in same lane was unable to stop causing the front of V2 to hit the rear of V1. After impact V1 and V2 came to final rest near the point of impact. V1 was towed from the scene, V2 was driven from the scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: other motor vehicle in lane, traveling in same direction with higher speed. The critical reason for the critical event was not coded to this vehicle.

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded: other motor vehicle in lane, traveling in same direction while decelerating. The critical reason for the critical event was assigned to V2 as a driver related factor: following too close to be able to respond to unexpected actions.
