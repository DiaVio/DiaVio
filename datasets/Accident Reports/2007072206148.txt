This three-vehicle crash occurred on the approach to a four-legged, traffic signal-controlled intersection. The roadway is straight, level, undivided and consists of two left turn lanes in each direction. The speed limit for this roadway is 48 kmph (30 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.

Vehicle one (V1), a 1993 Ford Thunderbird, was eastbound in lane two. Vehicle two (V2), a 1993 Jeep Cherokee, was stopped eastbound in lane two in front of V1. Vehicle three (V3), a 2000 Chevrolet Cavalier, was stopped eastbound in lane two in front of V2. The front of V1 struck the back of V2. V2 moved forward and the front struck the back of V3. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is No PAR obtained (created) for V1 and No PAR obtained (created) for V2.

The driver of V1, a fifty-eight-year-old female, travels this route weekly and was familiar with her vehicle. She stated that she applied the brakes but they did not work.

The driver of V2, a forty-year-old female, was on her way to work from her home. She was healthy, well rested and familiar with her vehicle. She travels this route daily. This driver did not see V2 prior to the crash.

The driver of V3, a thirty-year-old male, was not interviewed. No further information is available for this driver.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: other motor vehicle in lane; other vehicle stopped. The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as a vehicle related factor: brakes failed.

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded: other motor vehicle in lane, traveling in same direction with higher speed. The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V2 was not coded to this vehicle.

V3 was not involved first harmful event. The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V3 was not coded to this vehicle.
