This case involved a 60 year old female driver of a 2006 Buick Terraza minivan (V1). She was unrestrained and the frontal airbags did not deploy in this crash. Side impact airbags located in the front outboard seat backs are optional on this model but were not installed on this vehicle. V1 was involved in a T-bone type crash with a 1995 Honda Civic 4-door sedan (V2). The crash occurred at the intersection of two two-lane rural roadways. The dry asphalt roadways were straight and level with the east/west roadway was controlled by stop signs. The north/south roadway was uncontrolled. The crash occurred at dusk and it was cloudy. V1 was traveling west through the intersection when it was contacted in the left side by the front of V2. After impact V1 rotated 45 degrees counterclockwise and departed the northwest corner of the intersection. While on the roadside V1 impacted a wooden utility pole with its right side before coming to rest against the pole. V2 rotated counterclockwise after impact. V2 then traveled off the northwest corner of the intersection where it came to rest facing northwest. Both vehicles were towed from the scene due to disabling damage. The driver of V1 was transported by ground ambulance to a level one trauma center for treatment of serious injuries sustained in the crash.
