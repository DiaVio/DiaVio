This crash occurred on a five-lane undivided north/south roadway at an entrance to a parking lot. There were two lanes in each direction with a two-way left turn lane in between. The roadway was straight, level, dry, asphalt. The speed was posted at 72 kmph (45 mph). The weather was cloudy. This crash occurred on a weekday evening while still daylight. There were no traffic control devices involved.

Vehicle #1, a 2003 Chevrolet Silverado pick-up truck, was exiting the store parking lot attempting a left turn onto the north/south roadway. The driver, an 18-year old male, did not stop to check traffic before pulling out and the front of V1 struck the right front of Vehicle #2, a 2005 Dodge Durango utility vehicle that was traveling south in lane one. After contacting V2, V1 rotated clockwise and came to final rest facing southeast across lane #2 and the left turn lane. Vehicle #2 rotated clockwise and drove up on the west curb to final rest facing southwest. The driver and occupant of V2 suffered minor injuries but were not transported from the scene. The driver and occupant of V1 were not injured in the crash. Both vehicles had to be towed from the scene due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The driver of V1 reported being in good health and not taking any medications. He also stated he does not wear corrective lenses. He stated he was driving the passenger's vehicle at the time of the crash. He stated he did not have a license and has never taken driver's training. He stated he was only driving because his passenger had just been arguing with her father on the phone and the passenger was upset and stated she could not drive. She asked him to try to drive her home. He stated as he was exiting the parking lot he never noted the southbound vehicle. He stated he had looked to the left then the right as they started into the roadway and were struck. He stated the vehicle rotated and came to final rest in the middle of lane 2 and the left turn lane. He stated he was just banged up in the crash but did not need to be taken to the hospital.

A 37 year-old male drove V2. He reported that he was in good health and taking no medications. He does not wear corrective lenses. He stated he and his daughter had just come from the computer store and were on their way home. He stated as he had turned onto the roadway he was checking traffic to his left and as he looked back forward "there was a blue flash and bang." He stated his vehicle rocked up on its side and then back down and rotated clockwise and came to final rest on the west curb. He stated he and his daughter were not injured.

The Critical Pre-crash Event for V1 was coded this vehicle turning left at intersection. The Critical Reason for the Critical Event was coded as inadequate surveillance, failed to look to side. Associated factors coded to the driver include fatigue, conversation with the passenger, looking at the passenger, driver inexperience, unfamiliarity with the roadway (first time on roadway) and unfamiliarity with the vehicle (first time driving it).

The Critical Pre-crash Event for V2 was other vehicle encroachment from driveway turning into opposite direction. The Critical Reason was not coded to this vehicle. Associated factors coded to the driver include conversation with the passenger and an exterior distraction because the driver was checking his side mirror for traffic in the left lane.

A witness who was traveling north in lane two intending on turning left into the parking lot saw V1 come up to the edge of the north/south roadway. She stated that the male driver of V1 looked to his right at her and then accelerated as if he wanted to get onto the roadway before she turned in front of him. She stated that the driver of V1 never looked to the left and did not stop before pulling out.

Another witness who was traveling north in lane two saw V1 leave the parking lot and begin to turn left. He also stated that V1 never stopped before pulling out. He said that V2 swerved to avoid V1 but could not avoid V1.
