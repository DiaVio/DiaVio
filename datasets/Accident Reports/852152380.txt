This case occupant, a 55 year old female, is the driver in a frontal impact who was not belted with a frontal air bag deployment and sustained serious injuries. This crash occurred at a traffic signal controlled intersection at night with street lights and clear, dry conditions. Vehicle one (V1), a 1997 Dodge Stratus was traveling eastbound in lane 2 of a four lane two way street and crossing over straight through the intersection. Vehicle two (V2), a 2003 Honda Civic two door hatchback, was traveling westbound on the same street and in the left turn lane and entered the intersection to make a left turn. The front of V1 impacted the right side of V2 in the intersection. V2 rotated counterclockwise and V1 rotated clockwise and both came to final rest facing approximately southeast in the intersection. Both vehicle were disabled and towed. The driver of the V1 is the case occupant who was a 55 year old female, and was not belted and the frontal steering column mounted airbag deployed. She was transported initially to a local hospital before being transfer to the CIREN trauma center. No other occupants were in V1.
