This two-vehicle crash occurred on a straight, level, undivided roadway. This roadway consists of two lanes in the northbound direction and three lanes (one through lane, one left turn lane and one right turn lane) in the southbound direction. The speed limit for this roadway is 48 kmph (30 mph). It was dark with streetlights on at the time of the crash with no adverse atmospheric conditions. Traffic was congested at the time of the crash.

Vehicle one (V1), a 2002 Ford Taurus, was northbound in lane two. Vehicle two (V2), a 2005 Dodge Stratus, was southbound in lane two. Non-contact vehicles were stopped southbound in lane three. V1 turned left into a driveway (between two of the non-contact vehicles) and the front struck the front of V2. V2 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The driver of V1, a twenty-one-year-old female, was stopping at a bank on her way from her home to a friend's house. She was healthy, well rested and familiar with her vehicle. She travels this route weekly. This driver stated that she was in a hurry because she was trying to get to the bank before it closed. She further stated that she did not see V2 until just before the crash.

The driver of V2, a nineteen-year-old male, was not interviewed. No further information is available for this driver.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "this vehicle traveling; turning left at intersection." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "driver related factor; turned with obstructed view."

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded as "other vehicle encroachment; from opposite direction - over left lane line." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V2 was "not coded to this vehicle."
