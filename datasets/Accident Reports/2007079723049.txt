This two-vehicle crash occurred in a four-legged, traffic signal-controlled intersection. The east/west roadway is level, straight and divided by a curbed median. The approach lanes in both directions consist of three through lanes and a left turn lane. The speed limit on this roadway is 56 kmph (35 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.

Vehicle one (V1), a 1989 Toyota Corolla, was westbound in lane three. Vehicle two (V2), a 1994 Mercedes Benz 300SE, was eastbound in lane four. In the intersection, V2 turned left and the right side was struck by the front of V1. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The driver of V1, a twenty-one-year-old male, was traveling with two female passengers. He was healthy, well rested and comfortable with his vehicle and passengers. He travels this route daily. This driver stated that he saw V2 in the turn lane but assumed that the driver would wait until V2 had passed through the intersection.

The driver of V2, an eighteen-year-old female, was traveling with a thirteen-year-old female passenger. This driver was not interviewed; no further information is available for this driver.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "other vehicle encroachment; from opposite direction - over left lane line." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was "not coded to this vehicle."

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded as "this vehicle traveling; turning left at intersection." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded as "driver related factor; unknown decision error."
