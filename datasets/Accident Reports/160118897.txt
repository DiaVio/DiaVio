Vehicle 1 (V1) is a 2001 Hyundai Elantra, four-door (case study vehicle). Vehicle 2 (V2) is a 1998 BMW 740iL, four-door. V1 was occupied by a male, age 58 (study participant) as its driver and lone occupant. The study participant was wearing the available lap and shoulder seat belt. The frontal air bags did not deploy as a result of the impact. The crash occurred during the hours of daylight. The weather at the time of the crash was cloudy and dry. The trafficway surface was dry. The posted speed limit is 45 mph (72 kmph). The crash occurred at interchange. The roadway travels primarily north and south. The roadway is divided by a concrete barrier. The northbound direction consists of four through lanes and an off ramp. The roadway has a large right shoulder and a small left shoulder with a concrete barrier. The roadway has a positive grade of 2% in the area of the crash. V1 was traveling northbound in the right through lane. V2 was traveling northbound in the second right through lane. V2 changed lanes to the right, pulling in front of V1. V1's driver applied the foot brake in an attempt to stop but struck the rear of V2 with the front of V1. The study participant was transported by ambulance to a trauma center.
