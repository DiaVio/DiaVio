This two-vehicle crash occurred during early morning hours on a straight, dry, five-lane, state highway approaching an on-colors controlled intersection with pedestrian signals. The highway runs east and west at the location of the crash. Westbound traffic lanes consists of two through lanes and one left and one right turn lane. It also had a steep downhill grade. Eastbound traffic lanes consists of two through lanes and one left turn lane. The speed limit on the highway is 72 kmph (45 mph) and there were no adverse weather conditions at the time.

Vehicle one (V1), a 1996 Ford E350 cargo van driven by a 32 year-old male, was traveling westbound in lane two going straight. Vehicle two (V2), a 2003 Kenworth Aerocab T600 pulling one trailer, was driven by a 44 year-old male. V2 was stopped eastbound in lane two. The light turned red and a northbound non-contact vehicle started to enter the intersection. The northbound vehicle stopped as soon as they saw V1 coming down the hill. Driver of V1 braked and steered to the left to avoid the northbound vehicle. V1 then entered lane three, the left turn lane for eastbound traffic, and impacted head-on with V2. V1 rotated slightly clockwise and came to rest on its wheels facing southwest in lane two of the eastbound traffic. V2 came to rest on its wheels at impact facing east in lane two. V1 was towed from the scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is 72 kmph for V1 and 72 kmph for V2.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: this vehicle traveling, over lane line on left side of travel lane. The critical reason for the critical event was coded to V1 as a driver related factor: internal distraction. The sun had hit V1's left outside mirror, which blinded the driver of V1 for a second or two, so he grabbed in his vehicle for his sunglasses.

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded: other motor vehicle encroachment, from opposite direction - over left lane line. The critical reason was not coded to this vehicle.
