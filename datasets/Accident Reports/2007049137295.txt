This two-vehicle crash occurred in a four-legged, traffic signal-controlled intersection. The north/south roadway is straight, level and divided by a raised median. The southbound lanes consist of three through lanes and a left turn lane. The speed limit on this roadway is 56 kmph (35 mph). The eastbound roadway is one-way, straight, has an uphill grade and consists of two lanes. The speed limit on this roadway is 72 kmph (45 mph). This crash occurred at dawn with no adverse atmospheric conditions.

Vehicle one (V1), a 1997 Ford Taurus, was eastbound in lane two. Vehicle two (V2), a 2001 Ford Escort, was southbound in lane three. In the intersection, the front of V1 struck the right side of V2. V2 continued southbound and the front struck a pole in the median. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The driver of V1, a fifty-year-old male, was on his way to work from his home. He was healthy, well rested and familiar with his vehicle. He travels this route daily. This driver works ten-to-twenty hours each day for a total of sixty hours in the previous seven days. He stated that he didn't see V2 in time to attempt any avoidance maneuvers.

The driver of V2, a twenty-two-year-old female, was traveling with a thirty-two-year-old male passenger, from a friend's house to her home. She was healthy and comfortable with her vehicle and passenger. She rarely travels this route. This driver stated that she was talking to her passenger, but noticed V1 just before entering the intersection and applied the brakes in an attempt to avoid the crash.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "this vehicle traveling; crossing over (passing through) intersection." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "driver related factor; inadequate surveillance (e.g. failed to look, looked but did not see)."

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded as "other vehicle encroachment; from crossing street, across path." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V2 was "not coded to this vehicle."
