This two-vehicle crash occurred on a jersey barrier-divided, straight trafficway. The northbound lanes are level and are divided by a jersey barrier into two express lanes and two local lanes. The speed limit for this roadway is 89 kmph (55 mph). Traffic was congested at the time of the crash. This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.

Vehicle one (V1), a 1995 Mack tractor towing one trailer, was northbound in local lane two. Vehicle two (V2), a 1995 Lincoln Mark VIII, was northbound in local lane one. V1 attempted to change lanes to the right and the front struck the left side of V2. V2 rotated counterclockwise and traveled to the left. The front and right side of V2 struck the jersey barrier divider. V2 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The driver of V1, a thirty-nine-year-old male, was traveling with his twelve-year-old son. They had dropped off a load of cargo and were on their way to pick up more cargo. This driver was healthy, well rested and comfortable with his vehicle and passenger. He had been driving a truck for nine years and travels this route weekly. This driver stated that he did not see V2 prior to the crash.

The driver of V2, a fifty-year-old male, was not interviewed. No further information is available for this driver.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "this vehicle traveling; over the lane line on right side of travel lane." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "driver related factor; inadequate surveillance (e.g. failed to look, looked but did not see)."

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded as "other vehicle encroachment; from adjacent lane (same direction) - over left lane line." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V2 was "not coded to this vehicle."
