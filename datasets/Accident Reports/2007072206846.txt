This single-vehicle crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions. In the precrash area, the north/south roadway is straight, undivided and consists of one lane in each direction. In the southbound direction, the travel lane has a downhill grade. In the crash area, this roadway crosses under railroad tracks. In this area, there is a concrete barrier with steel pillars between the lanes. The speed limit for this roadway is 48 kmph (30 mph).

Vehicle one (V1), a 2007 Chrysler Sebring, was southbound in lane one. V1 traveled to the left, up on the barrier and the front struck the center steel pillar. V1 then rolled over to the right two quarter-turns. The back then struck the center concrete barrier. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Unknown for V1.

The driver of V1, a forty-nine-year-old male, was traveling with a forty-three-year-old female passenger. This driver was not interviewed. No further information is available for this driver.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "this vehicle traveling; off the edge of the road on the left side." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "driver related factor; type of driver error unknown."
