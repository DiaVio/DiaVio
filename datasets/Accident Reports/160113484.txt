Vehicle 1 (V1) is a 2002 Volkswagen Jetta, four-door (case study vehicle). Vehicle 2 is a 2001 Ford F450, tow truck (V2). V1 was occupied by a male, age 26 as its driver, (case study participant). V1's occupant was wearing the available lap and shoulder belt and the frontal air bags didn't deploy, but the driver's side airbag and inflatable curtain did deploy as a result of impact.

The crash occurred during the hours of darkness. The weather at the time of the crash was clear and dry. The roadway surface was dry. The posted speed limit is 55 mph (89 kmph).

The crash occurred at an intersection controlled by a stop sign. The northbound lanes consist of two through lanes and a dedicated left turn lane. The southbound lanes consist of two through lanes and a dedicated left turn lane. The north and southbound lanes are divided by a grassy median. The intersecting roadway consist of a two lane road, divided by a double yellow line. In the area of the crash the southbound side has a positive grade.

V1 was traveling east on a two lane undivided roadway and was stopped at the stop sign waiting to turn left and proveed north. V1 turned left and was struck by the front of V2 which was traveling in the southbound right through lane of a divided north and south trafficway. . V2 was traveling southbound in the right through lane. V1 turned into the path of V2, the front plane of V2 impacted the left side of V1. The impact caused V1 to rotate counterclockwise coming to rest in the grass median just south of the intersection. V2 continued skidding after impact and came to rest in the grass median.

The case study participant was transported by air to a trauma center.
