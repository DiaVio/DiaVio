This single vehicle ran off the left side of the roadway crash occurred mid-morning on a two lane, two-way interstate roadway. There are two westbound lanes and two eastbound lanes that are separated by depressed earthen median. This was a straight level, bituminous roadway with a posted speed limit of 120 KHP (75 MPH). It was daylight, the road was dry and the sky was clear.

Vehicle one(V-1), a 2000 Ford Excursion 4X4 suv, driven by a 19 year-old female with two passengers was traveling eastbound at approximately 120 kph (75 MPH) in the number two lane with three occupants. The tread on the right rear tire began to separate. The vehicle traveled in the number two lane for approximately 58 meters before the vehicle began to rotate counterclockwise. The vehicle traveled for approximately 74 meters on the roadway while rotating counterclockwise. The vehicle departed the left side of the roadway and traveled in the earthen median for approximately 23 meters until it tripped on the right wheels. The vehicle rolled to it's right one quarter turn and slid on it's right side for approximately 13 meters before it came to rest on it's right side facing in a northerly direction. The vehicle was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.
