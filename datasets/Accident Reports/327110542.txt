This case involves a 'T'-type side impact to the right side of a passenger by the front of a school bus. Both the 51-year-old female driver and the 25-year-old female right-front passenger are case occupants. Neither the driver nor the right-front passenger was restrained by a three-point belt, but the seat-back mounted and curtain airbags deployed. The case vehicle a 2007 four-door Honda Accord was southbound in the left-turn lane of a divided, dry, six-lane, concrete road approaching a controlled four-leg intersection. Vehicle two (V2), a 2011 school bus was in the inside northbound through lane approaching the same intersection. It was daylight and clear. The case vehicle made a left turn through the intersection and crossed the path of V2. The front of V2 struck the right side of the case vehicle and pushed it across the intersection over the curb at the northeast corner and into a utility pole, cracking the pole. The case vehicle came to rest against the pole facing east-southeast. V2 came to rest against the case vehicle facing northeast. The unrestrained 51-year-old female driver and unrestrained 25-year-old female right-front passenger were the only occupants of the case vehicle. Both the driver and right-front passenger sustained multiple moderate injuries and were transported to a level-one trauma center, where they were enrolled as case occupants.
