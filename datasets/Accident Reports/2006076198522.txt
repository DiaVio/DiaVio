This is a single-vehicle crash that occurred in a rural area on a four-lane divided asphalt roadway with an extended median. The two southbound lanes curve right and are uphill at the pre-crash location. The speed limit for the area is 104 kph (65 mph). The crash occurred during daylight with no adverse weather or road conditions present.

Vehicle one, a 2004 Dodge Neon was southbound in the second lane passing a tractor-trailer that was in the first lane. As vehicle one was passing the tractor-trailer, the tractor-trailer began to change lanes to the left, apparently unaware of the location of vehicle one to its left. To avoid contact with the tractor-trailer, vehicle one steered left and applied his brakes and departed the road edge with its left tires. Vehicle one lost control and came back to the right. Vehicle one crossed the roadway and departed the right side. The front of vehicle one impacted a small tree. Vehicle one then impacted the ground with its undercarriage and then a second tree with its front plane. Vehicle one came to rest of the right shoulder. Vehicle one was towed due to undercarriage damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 121 kmph for V1.

The driver of Vehicle one, a twenty-four year old male had traveled twelve miles after getting off of work. He had worked a twelve-hour night shift and was traveling to his residence in another town. He stated he had ten hours of sleep the day before, waking up at 1500 for his shift that began at 1900. This was his first night shift after having seven days off and working the day shift before that. This driver drives this vehicle daily and over this road on a daily basis.

The critical pre-crash event was coded for vehicle one was coded as "this vehicle traveling: off the edge of the road on the right side". The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event was coded as "driver related factor, poor directional control".
