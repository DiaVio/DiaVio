This crash occurred on a weekend evening on a two-way, two lane roadway. The driver was driving southbound, negotiating a curve when he lost control of the vehicle he was driving. The roadway was dry asphalt. The speed that was posted was 56kmph (35mph) the advisory speed was posted at 48kmph (30 mph) for the curve. The Slope was level on the roadway and the S. E. was -12/122 to the curve. The vehicle in the crash came to final rest on the east side of the roadway after making contact with a group of trees.

The Vehicle driven was titled as a 1977 Chevrolet T-Bucket. This vehicle was driven by a 59 year-old male whom was DOA at the scene of the crash. This researcher found that the driver was negotiating a curve in the roadway, the backend of the vehicle lost traction in the curve sliding off the asphalt onto the gravel shoulder. As the driver tried to regain control of the vehicle it came back onto the roadway going across both lanes of travel then leaving the east side of the roadway and shoulder traveling across the grass to contact the front of the vehicle to the trees ejecting the driver at final rest. Police Reported Travel Speed is Unknown for V1.

The Critical Precrash Event for this vehicle was coded this vehicle traveling off the edge of the road on the left side. The Critical Reason for the Critical Precrash Event was coded as performance error, overcompensation. This researcher found that this driver was in good health at the time of the crash. His daughter stated that while going through his home after the funeral she found out that he was a boarder line Diabetic. Another associating factor was that the vehicle was not equipped with seat belts. This researcher had found out that this driver lived in the area of the crash at one time and may have been going to visit someone. He knew the roadway very well so this researcher feels the driver may have been speeding at the time of the crash.
