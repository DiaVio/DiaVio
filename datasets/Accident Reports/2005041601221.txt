This crash occurred in the westbound lanes of a high-volume, high-speed divided interstate during morning rush hour on a weekday. The bituminous roadway featured four dry, straight, and level lanes with a posted speed limit of 105 kmph (65 mph). The traffic flow was normal for that time of day. V1, a 2000 Buick Century was in lane two traveling westbound at approximately 105 kmph. V2, an unknown vehicle that fled the scene, was traveling in lane three slightly behind V1. V2 began moving into lane two and impacted V1 on its left rear side. After impact, the driver of V1 lost control of the vehicle and departed off the right side of the roadway and came to final rest 16 meters down a grassy embankment.

V1 was driven by a 68-year-old woman in good health and was in the middle of running a few errands that morning. She stated that she never drives this route but did this morning because of several accidents on her normal route. The driver was not injured but V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 89 kmph for V1 and 89 kmph for V2.

The Critical Precrash Event for V1 was coded as other vehicle encroachment from the adjacent lane over the left lane line. The Critical Reason was not coded to this vehicle. The only associated factor was an experience factor because the driver of V1 stated that she rarely drives this road. However V1 was not thought to have contributed to the crash.

The Critical Precrash Event for V2 was coded as this vehicle traveling, over the lane line right side of travel lane. The Critical Reason is unknown as V2 fled the scene. It is unknown if there were any associated factors as neither the driver or vehicle could be located.
