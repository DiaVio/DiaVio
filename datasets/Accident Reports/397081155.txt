This crash occurred on an undivided urban trafficway. The trafficway is situated in a north to south configuration and is comprised of 2 lanes. There is also a 4-leg intersection at the location however it played no part in the crash circumstances. At the time of the crash it was daylight, the weather was clear and the road surface was dry. Traffic for that location at that time of the day is light. Involved in the crash was the case vehicle (V1) a 1998 Ford Explorer 4-door SUV equipped with 3-point manual restraints for each outboard seating position. In addition to the manual restraints V1 is also equipped with a driver and right passenger frontal impact air bags, both of which deployed in the crash. The driver and lone occupant of V1 was a 46-year-old male who was using the available manual restraint at the time of the crash. V1 was traveling in a northerly direction in the proper lane. As V1 was approaching an intersection, the driver reportedly fainted. V1 departed the north-south roadway at the right edge, prior to entering the intersection. V1 continued forward, entered the east-west trafficway and crossed the east leg of the intersection. V1 then departed the east-west trafficway at the north edge of the eastern leg of the intersection. At that time V1 struck and penetrated a chain link fence and continued traveling in a northerly direction. V1 continued traveling north in the grass field that borders the east side of the roadway. V1 continued forward another 40 meters and struck a fruit tree with the front of the vehicle. V1 came to final rest still engaged with the tree. The driver of the case vehicle did not loose consciousness in the crash. He was awake upon first observation by the paramedics, however he did not remember the crash nor how it occurred. He was removed from the vehicle by the paramedics and transported to the trauma center via helicopter.
