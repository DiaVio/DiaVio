This two-vehicle crash occurred on an undivided roadway with no controls. This roadway is straight and has a 1.6% downgrade. The traffic lanes consist of one lane in each direction, northbound and southbound. This roadway has a posted speed limit of 48 kmph (30 mph). This crash occurred during nighttime hours, in the late evening on a weekday. Traffic flow was light and the dark, but unlighted roadway was wet from an earlier rain.

Vehicle one (V1), a 1999 Volkswagen Passat with one occupant, was traveling southbound in lane one. V1 crossed the northbound lane, departed the roadway on the left, and impacted vehicle two (V2), a 2000 Ford Ranger, with its front. V2 was parked and unattended facing north just off the roadway on the east side. V1 came to final rest at the approximate point of impact facing south with its right tires resting on the east road edge. V2 was pushed backward into a chain link fence and came to final rest facing north-northeast on the east side of the roadway. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 48 kmph for V1 and 48 kmph for V2.

The driver of V1, a twenty-eight-year-old female, was going from a bar to her home. She stated that she felt fatigued at the beginning of the trip and fell asleep. She said she had worked a full day and had a few drinks before driving home that night. She is in good health, feels very comfortable with her vehicle, and drives this route daily.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: this vehicle traveling, off the edge of the road on the left side. The critical reason for the critical event for V1 was coded as a driver related factor: sleeping, that is, actually asleep. Driver fatigue was coded as an associative factor.
