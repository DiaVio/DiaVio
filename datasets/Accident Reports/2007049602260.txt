This single-vehicle crash occurred on a ramp between two access-controlled trafficways. This ramp is one-way, has an uphill grade, curves to the left (the radius of curvature is approximately fifty meters and the superelevation is eight percent) and consists of two lanes. There are jersey barriers on both sides of this roadway. The speed limit on this roadway is 97 kmph (60 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours and it was raining.

Vehicle one (V1), a 2005 Suzuki XL7, was westbound in lane one. This vehicle's left rear tire was the recommended tire size but was underinflated. The other three tires were not the recommended tire size and were overinflated. V1 began to rotate counterclockwise, traveling to the left. The front and then the back of V1 struck the left jersey barrier. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

The driver of V1, a twenty-three-year-old female, was on her way to work from her home. She was healthy, well rested and familiar with her vehicle. She travels this route daily. This driver stated that she was not in a hurry and that she lost control because the road was wet.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as " this vehicle control loss due to traveling too fast for conditions." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "driver related factor; too fast for conditions." Associated factors were the driver's poor directional control, the vehicle's mismatched tires and the wet road.
