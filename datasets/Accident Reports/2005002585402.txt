The crash occurred on a level two lane undivided roadway with a posted speed limit of 35 MPH (56 KPH). The road was curved slightly left with a radius of curvature of 2,812 meters and no superelevation. The weather was clear, the roadway dry and it was daylight at the time of this weekday afternoon crash.

Vehicle #1, a 1996 Buick Skylark, was traveling south on the two-lane roadway negotiating the slight curve. Vehicle #1 drove off the right side of the roadway, down a slight slope and struck a telephone pole guide wire anchor with its front. Vehicle #1 rotated counterclockwise and came to rest facing east. Vehicle #1 was towed due to damage. Police did not consider either alcohol or illegal drugs to be involved and ordered no tests. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

The Buick Skylark (Vehicle #1) was driven by a belted 19 year old male who was transported, treated and released at a local hospital for minor injuries. The driver was unable to be contacted for additional information. The medical indicated that the driver said "he realized that he was going to hit the telephone pole, swerved and hit side guard" and also stated during interview that "maybe he should have hit the pole."

The Critical Precrash Event for Vehicle #1 was that this vehicle was traveling off the edge of the road on the right side. The Critical Reason for the Critical Event was unknown type of driver error. This was chosen because there was no additional information available, however, from scene evidence there were no braking or avoidance maneuvers indicated. It should be noted that the medical indicated that the driver had a history of seizures and asthma, but he denied taking any type of medications.
