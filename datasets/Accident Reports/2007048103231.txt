This single-vehicle crash occurred at night (dark with no streetlights) with no adverse atmospheric conditions. The roadway is straight, level and consists of one lane in each direction. There are paved shoulders, driveways and ditches on both sides of this roadway. The speed limit for this roadway is 89 kmph (55 mph).

Vehicle one (V1), a 1988 Ford Bronco, was southbound in lane one. V1 traveled off the right side of the roadway into the ditch. V1 continued southbound and the front struck the edge of the pavement above the culvert. V1 rotated counterclockwise and then clockwise while continuing southbound. V1 rolled over to the left two quarter-turns. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 129 kmph for V1.

The driver of V1, a forty-seven-year-old male, was on his way home from a fishing trip. He was familiar with his vehicle and travels this route daily. This driver has hypertension and post-traumatic stress disorder and takes Lortab and Xanax. He stated that he did not remember any of the precrash or crash events. The police officer indicated that this vehicle was traveling at an estimated speed of 129 kmph (88 mph).

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "this vehicle traveling; off the edge of the road on the right side." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "driver related factor; poor directional control (e.g., failing to control vehicle with skill ordinarily expected)." Speed and this driver's medication use were associated factors in this crash.
