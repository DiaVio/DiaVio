This was a single vehicle with roll-over an impact to a large conifer tree with the vehicles top. This crash occurred shortly after noon on rural two lane asphalt roadway this roadway runs north to south and is bordered by large mature pine trees. This roadway has varying curves and grades. The posted speed limit is 89 kmph (55 mph) with no emergency pull out lanes. This crash occurred during daylight with good visibility and no adverse weather.

Vehicle one (V1) a 2001 Chevrolet S Blazer 4 door SUV driven by a 69 year-old female was traveling northbound in the right curb lane, crossed the centerline and exited the left side road edge. V1 rode the left shoulder some 30 meters (98 feet) then steered right back onto the roadway where it began to rotate clockwise. V1 then steered left and rotated counter clockwise and exited the left road edge while in a CCW yaw. V1 tripped and rolled over then spun clockwise into a large tree and rolled to a stop on its wheels heading in a southerly direction. Vehicle one was towed from the scene due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 89 kmph for V1.

Since the driver was the only occupant and was fatally injured no pre-crash information could be obtained. Her medical history did not point to any serious problems.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: this vehicle traveling, off the edge of the road on the left side. The critical reason for the critical event was coded as a driver related factor: type of driver error unknown. The driver's husband reported that the driver was on her way home from shopping. She takes an unspecified medication for high cholesterol, but was otherwise in good health and had sufficient sleep the previous night. His wife did have a cell phone but he did not know if it was in use. She drives the route daily and was familiar with driving the vehicle.
