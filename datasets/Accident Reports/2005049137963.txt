This collision occurred at the intersection of two, two-way, two lane, undivided roadways. Both roads were dry, traveled concrete. The northbound travel direction had a +4.1% grade. The eastbound travel direction had a +8.2% grade. The posted speed limit was 48 kmph (30 mph). There were no adverse weather conditions. The collision occurred in the early evening hours on a sunny, clear day.

Vehicle one (V1) was a 1991 Honda Accord stopped at a stop sign northbound in the first lane. Vehicle two (V2) was approaching the intersection eastbound in the first lane. V1 entered the intersection without the right of way and was struck on the left side by the front of V2. V1 came to rest in the roadway facing east. V2 came to rest in the intersection facing northwest. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The driver of V1 was a 30-year-old female who was returning to her residence from a friend's house. She is familiar with the vehicle but rarely drives the route. The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: this vehicle traveling, crossing over (passing through) intersection. The critical reason for the critical event was coded to V1 as a driver related factor: inadequate surveillance (e. g., failed to look, looked but did not see).

The driver of V2 was a 23-year-old female who was returning home from work. She was familiar with the vehicle and the route traveled. The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded: other vehicle encroachment; from crossing street, across path. The critical reason was not coded to this vehicle.
