This two-vehicle crash occurred in a four-legged intersection. Both roadways are straight, undivided and consist of one lane in each direction. The eastbound approach is controlled by a stop sign, has a three percent uphill grade and the speed limit is 40 kmph (25 mph). The southbound approach is controlled by a traffic signal, has a three percent uphill grade and the speed limit is 48 kmph (30 mph). There are buildings and shrubbery on the sides of both of these roadways. This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.

Vehicle one (V1), a 1993 Hyundai Elantra, was eastbound in lane one at a high rate of speed. Vehicle two (V2), a 1995 Geo Prizm, was southbound in lane one. In the intersection, the front of V2 struck the left side of V1. V1 rotated counterclockwise and the right rear wheel struck a curb. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The driver of V1, a twenty-one-year-old male, was not interviewed. This driver was reportedly fleeing an earlier incident.

The driver of V2, a fifty-eight-year-old female, was traveling from home to work. She was healthy, well rested and comfortable with her vehicle. She travels this route daily. This driver stated that she proceeded through the intersection because her traffic signal was green and that she never saw V1 prior to the crash.

The pre-event movement for V1 was coded as "going straight." The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "this vehicle traveling; crossing over (passing through) intersection." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "driver related factor; aggressive driving behavior." The sightline restriction caused by the buildings and shrubbery was an associated factor in this crash.

The pre-event movement for V2 was coded as "going straight." The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded as "other vehicle encroachment; from crossing street, across path." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V2 was "not coded to this vehicle." The sightline restriction caused by the buildings and shrubbery was an associated factor in this crash.
