This single-vehicle crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions. The roadway has one lane in each direction and was dry at the time of the crash. There is a left curve with a 224 m radius of curvature and a 5% upgrade in the precrash area. The speed limit on this roadway is 72 kmph (45 mph).

Vehicle one (V1), a 2004 Ford Explorer, was southbound in lane one. V1 traveled off the right side of the roadway and the front struck a tree. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 64 kmph for V1.

The driver of V1, a thirty-three-year-old male, was traveling with a thirty-one-year-old female, from his mother's workplace to his home. He is familiar with his vehicle and drives this route daily. This driver stated that he has narcolepsy and a seizure disorder, but takes no medication. He also stated that he fell asleep and was still sleeping when his vehicle struck the tree.

The pre-event movement for V1 was coded as "negotiating a curve." The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "this vehicle traveling; off the edge of the road on the right side." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "driver related factor; sleeping, that is, actually asleep."
