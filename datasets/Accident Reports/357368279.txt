This case involves an impact between the front of a passenger car (case vehicle) and a left side of another passenger car. The unrestrained 54-year-old female right-front passenger is the case occupant. The case vehicle, a 2009 Chevrolet Cobalt sedan, was traveling westbound in the inside lane of a wet, bituminous, four-lane divided level traffic-way and approaching a four-leg intersection. Vehicle two (V2), 1998 Ford Contour sedan, was travelling eastbound in the inside lane of the same roadway and was approaching the same intersection. It was daylight and rainy. V2 made a turn left and entered the path of the case vehicle. The front of the case vehicle struck the right side of V2. After impact the case vehicle rotated clockwise and came to rest in a parking lane on the north side of the intersection. V2 rotated counterclockwise and came to rest off the roadway to the northwest of the intersection. Both vehicles were towed due to disabling damage. The unrestrained 80-year-old male driver had the benefit of a deployed steering-wheel air bag. He sustained police reported minor injuries. The case occupant is the 54-year-old female right-front passenger. She was unrestrained by the available three-point belt and the instrument panel mounted frontal airbag deployed during the impact. The case occupant was transported to a level-one trauma center with moderate injuries and enrolled as a case occupant.
