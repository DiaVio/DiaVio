The crash occurred off the right side of a two-lane undivided roadway. Conditions were daylight and dry in the afternoon on a weekday. The posted speed limit was 40 KMPH (25 MPH).

V1, a 1987 Toyota 4-Runner pickup truck, was traveling northbound when the front right tire blew out. The driver tried to pull over and bring the vehicle to a stop; however, the vehicle veered right, jumped the curb, and then the front right of the vehicle struck a utility pole. V1 came to final rest facing eastbound. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

V1 was driven by a 37-year old male who was transported with moderate injuries prior to the researcher's arrival. The driver spoke little English and a police officer translated the interview. He stated that his steering wheel was shaking and he braked and tried to pull over to the right but his vehicle jumped the curb and struck the pole. He estimated his speed at impact to be between 21 and 30 MPH. V1 was towed due to damage.

The Critical Precrash Event for V1 was when the vehicle lost control due to a blow out to the right front tire. The Critical Reason for the Critical Precrash Event was a vehicle related factor - tire failed. The driver was familiar with the roadway and felt rested prior to the crash. No additional associated factors were coded to this driver.
