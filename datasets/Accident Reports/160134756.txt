Vehicle one (V1), a 2005 Toyota Corolla sedan, was stopped at a red light facing west waiting to make a left turn in lane three of a three lane, one way roadway. Vehicle two (V2), a 2005 Sterling medium/heavy flatbed, was traveling north in lane three of the northbound leg of a six lane, divided roadway. V1 attempted to make a left turn at the intersection and the front of V2 contacted the left side of V1. V1 came to rest in the northwest portion of the intersection and V2 came to rest in the northeast portion of the intersection. V1 was towed due to damage, V2 was not towed. The driver of V1, a 29-year-old female and case study occupant was properly wearing her three-point lap and shoulder belt and had her B-pillar mounted pretensioner fire. The driver of V1 was transported to a level-one trauma center for treatment.
