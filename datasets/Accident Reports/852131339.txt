This two vehicle head on crash occurred on the three westbound lanes of a divided freeway. The roadway was wet, weather was clear and it was dark with street lights. The case vehicle one (V1), a 2005 Chevrolet Colorado pickup truck was traveling in the westbound lane three. Vehicle two (V2), a 1994 Subaru Legacy wagon, was eastbound traveling opposite V1 in the same lane. Driver of V1 attempted to brake prior to the full front of V1 impacting the full front of V2. V1 rotated clockwise and the left back quarter panel impacted the concrete barrier adjacent to this lane. V1 came to final rest facing west, northwest with the back left up on the top of the barrier. V2 rotated slightly clockwise and came to final rest facing southeast in lane 3 and partially into lane 2. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. The driver and front right passenger in V1 are both CIREN subjects with no other passengers in V1. The driver is a 21 year old male who was not wearing a safety belt and the front steering column mounted air bag deployed. The front right passenger is a 20 year old male who was not wearing a safety belt and the instrument panel mounted air bag deployed. Both case occupants in V1 were transported to the trauma center with serious injuries.
