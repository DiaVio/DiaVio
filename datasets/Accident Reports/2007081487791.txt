This two-vehicle crash occurred shortly after noon during daylight hours . The location was a rural intersection. The eastbound section of roadway was a bituminous five-lane undivided roadway. The westbound was a four-lane undivided roadway. The impact location is in the intersection. The weather was cloudy and raining and the roadway was wet. Visibility was fairly clear. The posted speed limit was 64 kmph (40 mph).

Vehicle one (V1), a 2001 Chrysler Town & Country van driven by a seventy seven (77) year old female, was in lane three turning left from the westbound direction to travel southbound. Vehicle two (V2), a 1998 Saturn SL1 sedan driven by a twenty nine (29) year old female was traveling eastbound in lane two. As both vehicles entered the intersection the front of V2 impacted the right side of V1. After impact V1 came to final rest facing southeast in the intersection, V2 came to final rest facing east, and both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The driver of V1's pre-crash factors are unknown as she was unable to be reached for interview. The driver of V2's pre-crash factors are also unknown as she was unable to be reached for interview.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: this vehicle traveling, turning left at intersection. The critical reason was a driver related factor: false assumption of other's actions. According to the police report the driver of V1 thought she had a green arrow, but a witness refuted this indicating V2 had a green light. This driver falsely assumed V2 would yield the right of way to her vehicle.

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded: other vehicle encroachment; from opposite direction - over left lane line. A critical reason for the critical event was not assigned to this vehicle.
