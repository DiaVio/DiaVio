This crash occurred in the late evening of a weekday on the northbound side of the center median of fully controlled divided (concrete barrier wall) highway. The northbound direction of the highway featured three bituminous, dry, straight, and level, lanes with a posted speed limit of 113kmph (70 mph). It was dark but there were no adverse atmospheric conditions at the time of the crash.

V1, a 1994 Pontiac Grand Am was northbound in lane three traveling over 113kmph (70 mph). As V1 continued northbound the right rear wheel separated from the vehicle causing the driver to loss control. The vehicle departed the roadway to the left where it struck the concrete barrier wall. As V1 continued down the wall it eventually rolled 4-quarter turns to the right before coming to final rest facing west partially on the roadway. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

The driver of V1 was a belted 18 year-old male who on his commute home from school. He was rested and was wearing his prescription lenses that correct a myopic (near-sighted) condition. He was uninjured in the crash and refused treatment. V1 was traveling in lane three when the driver stated that he had heard a loud bang come from the back of his car. He applied the brakes and steered right but overcompensated and struck the barrier wall.

The Critical Precrash Event for V1 was coded this vehicle loss of control due to a disabling vehicle failure: the wheel breaking off. The Critical Reason for the Critical Precrash Event was a vehicle related error, wheels/tires failed. The only associated factor for this crash was inexperience as the driver had only been driving for 12 months.
