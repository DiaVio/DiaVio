This crash occurred off to the right side of the roadway. The roadway that was being traveled is a two-way, two-lane, non-divided rural asphalt roadway with a posted speed limit of 89 Kmph (55 Mph). The crash occurred during daylight hours, and there were no adverse conditions present at the time of the crash.

V1 a 2003 Pontiac Bonneville was traveling southbound in the right curb lane, when two deer jumped out in front of the vehicle. V1 steered to the right, departing the roadway and struck a tree. V1 was towed from the scene with moderate damage, and the lone occupant was uninjured. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

The driver of V1 a 43 year-old male stated that he was going slow looking out the right side of his vehicle at potential deer hunting locations. The driver then stated that he looked back forward and there were two deer standing in the road. He stated that he steered to the right, went off the roadway and hit a tree.

The Critical Pre-Crash Event coded for V1 is " Animal in roadway". The Critical Reason for the Critical event coded for V1 is "Driver related, inadequate evasive action, braking".
