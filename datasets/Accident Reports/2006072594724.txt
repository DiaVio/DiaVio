This two-vehicle crash occurred mid after-noon during day-light hours at a driveway on a two-lane east/west road.. The east and westbound travel lanes were divided by a painted median and are straight, level and of bituminous construction. The east/west roadway has a posted speed limit of 48 kmph (30 mph). At the time of the crash, the weather was clear, traffic was not congested, and there were no sight restrictions or road defects.

Vehicle one (V 1), a 1991 Nissan Sentra 2 door, was southbound entering the roadway from the private drive intending to turn left and go eastbound. Vehicle two (V 2), a 1993 Ford Aerostar van, was westbound in lane one of the roadway. The front of V 2 collided with the left side of V 1 in the westbound lane. The impact pushed V 1 a short distance to the west and it came to rest facing generally south/southwest in the westbound lane. The impact with V 1 caused V 2 to rotate somewhat counterclockwise and V 2 came to rest a short distance beyond the point of impact in the original lane of travel facing generally southwest. V 1 was towed due to damage; V 2 was driven from the crash scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: other - leaving driveway, turning left. The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event was coded as a driver related factor: type of driver error unknown. Due to a lack of driver interview it could not be determined if the driver made a recognition or decision error when he entered the cross street. It is also unknown if there were any interior or exterior distractions; or if the driver misjudged the speed of the other vehicle, or failed to look or looked, but did not see V2.

The critical pre-crash event for V 2 was coded: other vehicle encroachment; from crossing street, turning into opposite direction. The critical reason was not coded to this vehicle.
