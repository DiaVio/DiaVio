This one-vehicle collision occurred on a one-way, one-lane, bituminous roadway. This road is straight and uphill with a six and one half percent slope. This crash occurred during nighttime hours with street lighting. The speed limit on the road is 40 kmph (25 mph). There were no adverse atmospheric conditions at the time of the crash.

Vehicle one, a 1993 Geo Prizm was northbound in the first lane. As the vehicle was traveling down hill on the road the driver tried to brake and the vehicle did not slow. The driver did not want to get to the intersection without stopping. The driver turned right at an alley and departed the left side of the alley and impacted a building with the front plane. Vehicle one came to rest against the building. Vehicle one was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

The driver of vehicle one, a sixteen-year-old female was heading to a friends house to swap out this car. The driver knew that this car had degraded brakes. This driver had been driving for only a few minutes at the time of the crash. This driver stated that she turned right into this alley because she did not want to get to the intersection without brakes. This driver turned right into the driveway but went wide and hit the building. This alley has a gate across it and the car would not have gone too far down the alley. This driver stated that she tried both foot brakes and the emergency brake. This driver drives this car rarely and over this road rarely.

The critical pre-crash event for vehicle one was coded: this vehicle traveling, turning right at intersection. The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event was coded as a driver related factor: poor directional control.
