This crash occurred in the early morning of a weekend, on a two-lane roadway. The roadway curved to the right, with a slight uphill grade, before it became straight and level. After the curve, the roadway passed through a small tunnel under an overpass. The roadway had a posted speed limit of 64 kmph (40 mph). It was early morning on a weekend and conditions were clear.

V1 was a 2000 Mercury Sable, driven by an 83 year-old male. V1 was traveling southbound, negotiating a right hand curve when the vehicle left the roadway on the right side. It then impacted three signposts and a concrete base to an overpass. The vehicle continued through the tunnel, crossing over the center line and impacted the left side of the tunnel. The vehicle then crossed over the center line of the roadway again, impacted the right side of the tunnel and came to final rest. Police Reported Travel Speed is 56 kmph for V1.

The Critical Precrash Event for V1 was "this vehicle traveling, off the edge of the road on the right side." The Critical Reason for the Critical Precrash Event was "driver related factor, sleeping that is actually asleep." The driver of the vehicle did not sleep well the night before and was still very drowsy. He woke up at 06:00 to go to church. According to his wife, she did not think that he should have been driving because he was still so tired. The driver fell asleep at the wheel before he left the roadway, as he was negotiating the curve.
