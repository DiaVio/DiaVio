This crash occurred at noon involving one passenger car and a utility truck at a intersection without traffic lights. The speed limit for the roadway is 54 kmph (35 mph). The road is a 4-lane divided (no positive barrier) concrete roadway for both vehicles The road surface was dry and there were no detrimental atmospheric conditions at the time of the crash.

V 1, a 2000 Cadillac Catera driven by a 65 year-old male was traveling east in the third lane of the eastbound lanes. V 2, a 2002 Ford F 350 driven by a 70 year-old male with one passenger was traveling east in the second lane. At the intersection V 2 attempted to turn left to a north bound roadway. V 2 attempted this turn from lane 2 unaware of traffic in lane 3. The right front corner of V 1 impacted the left rear corner of V 2. V 1 under rode the left rear corner of V 2. V 2 continued in its left turn movement , dragging V 1 into the intersection, before disengaging with V 1. V 2 came to rest in the first lane of the westbound lanes of traffic, facing west. V 1 came to rest within the intersection, facing west. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

V 1 was towed from the scene. The driver was transported to a medical facility.

V 2 was able to drive off the scene with minor damage to the rear bumper.

The pre-event movement for V 1 was coded going straight. The critical pre crash event was codedother vehicle encroaching into the lane, from same direction, over the right lane line. No critical reason for the critical event was coded to this vehicle.

The pre-event movement for V 2 was coded going straight. The critical pre crash event was coded this vehicle traveling, over the left lane line of traffic. The critical reason for the critical event was coded driver related factor "illegal maneuver". (This vehicle tried to turn left from the wrong lane of traffic).
