The case vehicle a 2005 2-door Scion tC hatchback was traveling in the outside northbound lane of an asphalt four-lane roadway with a painted median. Vehicle two (V2) a Lexus RX 300 SUV was traveling in the inside southbound lane of the same roadway. As it was negotiating a left-sweeping curve, the case vehicle struck the east barrier curb with its right wheels and rode up over it. The driver lost control and the vehicle entered a counter-clockwise yaw, crossed the painted median and entered the path of V2. The front of V2 struck the right quarter-panel of the case vehicle. The impact caused the case vehicle to rotate clockwise and ride over the

west barrier curb, back end leading. The case vehicle came to a rest on the roadside, against a fence, facing east.

The 23-year-old female driver and the case occupant, the 30-year-old female right-front passenger were both wearing three-point belts. There were no air bag deployments. The driver was taken to a local hospital with minor injuries and treated and released. The case occupant was also taken to a local hospital with serious injuries and later transferred to a level-one trauma center.
