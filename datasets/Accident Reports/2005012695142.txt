V1, a 1997 Pontiac Grand Prix, was traveling westbound on the entrance ramp of a highway that was one-lane, one-way, icy concrete roadway. The Driver of V1 stated she thought she might be traveling too fast for conditions. She tapped the brakes and began rotating in a counterclockwise direction. The vehicle departed the roadway on the right side and contacted it's left side to a breakaway light post. The vehicle then contacted it's left side to a tree before coming to final rest off the road.

The driver of V1 was a 51-year old female who reported that she had been traveling between 32-40 kmph (20-25 mph) prior to the crash. She had no health problems. She had said she takes medication for neck pain. She was rested and traveling to pick up a present. She did not sustain any injuries from the crash. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

The Critical Precrash Event for the driver of V1 was when she lost control of her vehicle due to traveling too fast for conditions. The driver of V1 reported that as she noticed that cars had to merge ahead so she attempted to slow down by tapping the brakes. This action sent her vehicle into a skid. Associated factors include condition of the roadway (slick) and the weather (sleet).
