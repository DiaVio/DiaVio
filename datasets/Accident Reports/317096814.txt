This case involved a 19 year old restrained driver of a 2007 Chevrolet Cobalt two door coupe (V1). He was restrained by the available three point lap and shoulder belt and had a deployed frontal airbag. There were no side impact airbags available in this vehicle. The crash occurred during daylight hours on a dry asphalt two lane roadway. The road was level and curved to the left. The posted speed limit was 72 kph (45 mph). V1 was traveling south at approximately 97 kph (60 mph) as it negotiated the left hand curve. V1 went into a counterclockwise yaw and skidded off the left side of the roadway. V1overturned onto its right side and continued a total of four quarter turns, coming to rest back on its wheels facing west. V1 was towed from the scene due to disabling damage. The driver of V1 was transported by ground ambulance to a level one trauma center for treatment of his injuries.
