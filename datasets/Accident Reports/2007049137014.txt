The single vehicle crash occurred on a curbed median-divided, east/west roadway. There are three travel lanes for both eastbound and westbound traffic. The roadways were straight, level, dry and of concrete construction. There are no traffic controls at the scene. The posted speed limit is 64 kmph (40 mph). Traffic was light at the time of the crash. This crash occurred during daylight hours with clear skies.

Vehicle one (V1), a 2006 Cadillac CTS, was westbound in the third lane. V1 departed the roadway onto the median, crossed the eastbound lanes and departed the roadway on the south side. The front of the vehicle struck an approximately six foot high brick wall. V1 rotated counterclockwise and came to rest off the roadway facing south. V1 was towed due to damage. The driver of V1 was transported. . Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

The driver of V1, a twenty-nine year old male, was not interviewed. The police report and medical record state that the driver suffered a seizure, leading to the critical event. The driver takes Dilantin to control the seizures.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: this vehicle traveling, off the edge of the road on the left side. The critical reason for V1 was coded: driver related factor-heart attack or other physical impairment of the ability to act.
