This two-vehicle crash occurred on a two-lane, straight, level roadway. There is a driveway n the east side of this roadway. The speed limit on this roadway is 89 kmph (55 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.

Vehicle one (V1), a 1995 Mitsubishi 3000GT, was southbound in lane one. Vehicle two (V2), a 1994 Ford Tempo, was northbound in lane one. V1 turned left into the driveway and the right side was struck by the front of V2. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 24 kmph for V1 and 24 kmph for V2.

The driver of V1, a twenty-nine-year-old male, was traveling from his home to deliver the car to its new owner. He was healthy, well rested and familiar with his vehicle. This was the first time that he drove on this roadway. This driver stated that he turned left into the driveway because he needed to turn around and travel northbound. He also stated that he never saw V2.

The driver of V2, a seventy-one-year-old male, was traveling from the local area to his home in another state. He was healthy, well rested and familiar with his vehicle. This was the first time that he drove on this roadway. This driver stated that he noticed V1 coming into his lane and applied the brakes, but slid into the crash with V1.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "this vehicle traveling; turning left at intersection." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "driver related factor; inadequate surveillance (e.g. failed to look, looked but did not see)."

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded as "other vehicle encroachment; from opposite direction - over left lane line." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V2 was "not coded to this vehicle."
