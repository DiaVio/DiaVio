This is a single vehicle crash that occurred on a divided four-lane interstate in the two eastbound lanes. It was a dry, asphalt roadway and occurred during early morning daylight hours. The posted speed limit on the roadway is 121 kmph (75 mph) There were no roadway deficiencies and no sight restrictions noted at the scene. There is a depressed dirt median that separates the eastbound and westbound roadway. There were no adverse weather conditions.

Vehicle one (V1) was a 1996 GMC Safari mini van driven by a 37 year-old male with six passengers. V1 was traveling eastbound in the number two lane at a driver reported speed of approximately 105 kmph (65 MPH) when the tread on the right rear tire separated from the tire. The vehicle continued to travel eastbound in the number two lane for a short distance before the vehicle traveled into the number one lane. The driver then steered the vehicle to the left causing the vehicle to rotated counter clockwise and travel back into the number two lane. V1 continued to rotate clockwise and then began to slide laterally until it departed the left side of the roadway where it tripped and landed on it's top. The vehicle slid across the dirt median on it's top in a northeasterly direction and rolled to it's left a quarter turn onto it's left side and then back onto it's wheels where it came to rest near the north edge of the median facing in a northeasterly direction. The tread on the left rear tire was missing and the tire was deflated. The tread was located at the scene and the depth of the tread was measured at 7mm (9/32"). The vehicle was towed due to damage. The driver and one additional passenger were transported to a local medical facility. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

The critical pre-crash event was coded: this vehicle control loss due to, blow out/flat tire. The critical reason for the critical event was coded as a vehicle related factor: tires/wheels failed.
