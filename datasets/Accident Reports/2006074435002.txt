This two-vehicle collision occurred in the intersection of two five-lane roadways. Both roads are straight and level with a speed limit of 40 kmph (25 mph). The northbound road has two lanes in either direction and a left turn lane at the intersection. The westbound road has five lanes all in the westbound direction. This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.

Vehicle one (V1), a 2002 Honda Civic was traveling north in the first lane. Vehicle two (V2), a 2002 Dodge Caravan was traveling west in the fourth lane. The front of V1 impacted the left of V2. Then both vehicles rotated and side slapped V1's right plane to V2's left plane. V1 came to rest in the first southbound lane facing west. V2 came to rest in the second northbound lane. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The driver of V1, a twenty-four year old female was on her way to an appointment. This driver had been driving for almost one hour at the time of the crash. This driver was thirty-five weeks pregnant at the time of the crash was taking pre-natal vitamins. This driver stated that as she was driving she was scanning left, right and forward looking for her appointment. This driver drives this vehicle daily and only rarely over this roadway.

The driver of V2, a sixty-seven year old male was heading to an appointment at the hospital at the time of the crash. This drive had been driving for over one hour at the time of the crash. This driver is on multiple medications for rheumatoid arthritis. This driver drives this vehicle daily and over this road on a monthly basis.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: this vehicle traveling, crossing over [passing through] intersection. The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as a driver related factor: external distraction.

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded: other vehicle encroachment; from crossing street, across path. The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event was not coded to this vehicle.
