This case involves a full-frontal mode impact involving a passenger car and a city bus. The 81-year-old female driver of the passenger car is the case occupant. She was not restrained by a three-point belt, but the steering-wheel mounted airbag deployed. The case vehicle, a 2005, four-door Toyota Corolla was travelling northeast in the curb lane of a dry, level, divided, seven-lane, asphalt traffic-way approaching a controlled four-leg intersection. Vehicle two (V2), a city bus was travelling northeast in the same lane approaching the same intersection, and slowing to a stop. The front end of the case vehicle struck the back end of the bus. After impact both vehicles came to rest facing northeast in the curb lane. The 81-year-old female driver was not restrained by a three-point belt. She was the only occupant of the case vehicle. The driver sustained serious injuries and was transported to a level-one trauma center and enrolled as a case occupant.
