This two-vehicle crash occurred in the morning during daylight hours The roadway was a bituminous six-lane undivided roadway, with a negative grade, and just coming out of a long sweeping curve to the left. The weather was clear and dry, and visibility was clear. The posted speed limit was 56 kmph (35 mph).

Vehicle one (V1), a 2000 Dodge Neon sedan driven by a twenty five (25) year old female, was traveling southbound in lane one, approaching stopped traffic. Vehicle two (V2), a 2006 Mitsubishi Endeavor SUV driven by a twenty one (21) year old female, was stopped facing southbound ahead of V1, in lane one. As V1 approached vehicle two, the driver of V1 had a sneezing attack, and the front of V1 struck the rear of V2. V2 drove over to the right shoulder. V1 remained stalled where it was at impact until the tow truck arrived and removed it. V2 was not disabled and was driven away from the scene. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The driver of V2's pre-crash factors are mostly unknown as she was not interviewed.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: other motor vehicle in lane, other vehicle stopped. The critical reason was coded to V1 as a driver related factor: internal distraction. Driver was having a sneezing fit which impaired his ability to see V2 and stop his vehicle. An additional factor for V1 was sun glare.

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded: other motor vehicle in lane, traveling in same direction with higher speed. A critical reason was not assigned to this vehicle.
