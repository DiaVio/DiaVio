This two-vehicle crash occurred on an undivided, level, straight, seven-lane roadway. The eastbound lanes consist of three through lanes and a left turn lane. The speed limit on this roadway is 64 kmph (40 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.

Vehicle one (V1), a 2005 GMC C2500 pickup, was eastbound in lane one. Vehicle two (V2), a 1992 TMC metro bus, was eastbound in lane one in front of V1. V2 decelerated to stop at the bus stop to pick up a passenger. The front of V1 struck the back of V2. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The driver of V1, a thirty-year-old male, was on his way to work from his home. He was well rested, healthy and familiar with his vehicle. He travels this route daily. This driver stated that he was playing with a squeezable stress ball and dropped it on the floor. He leaned down to retrieve the ball. When he looked back up at the roadway, V2 was slowing. He steered left and applied the brakes in an attempt to avoid the crash.

The driver of V2, a thirty-two-year-old male, was traveling between bus stops. He was in good health, well rested and comfortable with his vehicle and passengers. He has diabetes and takes Metformin. This driver has nine years of experience driving buses and travels this route several times each month.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "other motor vehicle in lane; traveling in same direction while decelerating." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "driver related factor; internal distraction."

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded as "other motor vehicle in lane; traveling in same direction with higher speed." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V2 was "not coded to this vehicle."
