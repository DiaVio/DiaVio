This single vehicle crash occurred during early evening darkness on a two-lane asphalt rural roadway with a drainage ditch on both sides. At the time of this crash the roadway had snow on the edges and ice patches in places. Posted speed limit is 40 kmph (25 mph).

Vehicle one (V1), a 1985 Dodge 3/4 ton truck, driven by a 24 year-old male traveling eastbound and had just exited a right hand curve when it steered right off the road edge after encountering a patch of ice on the roadway and rode the drainage ditch a short distance. V1 then steered left back onto the roadway and crossed the roadway exiting the left hand road edge. After exiting this roadway, V1 sideswiped a delineator with its left door mirror, then sideswiped a medium sized tree with its left rear fender, then impacted a larger tree with left-front bumper plane. coming to rest against the second tree heading east. V1 was towed for disabling damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 64 kmph for V1.

The driver of V1 had a police reported BAC result of 0.23. The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: this vehicle control loss due to - traveling too fast for conditionsThe critical reason for the critical event was coded as a driver related factor: poor directional control. Associative factors include: driving under the influence of alcohol; some family issues including concerns about a sick father.
