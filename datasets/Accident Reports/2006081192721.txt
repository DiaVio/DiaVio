This single vehicle rollover crash occurred at the end of the morning rush hour in daylight hours. The location was a single lane bituminous entrance ramp to an interstate highway in an urban area. It was cloudy, the roadway was wet and it had been raining intermittently all morning. The roadway was a right curved circular on-ramp with a positive grade and super elevation. The radius of curvature was 96 meters and the super elevation was 11 %. The posted speed limit and advisory speed limit is unknown.

Vehicle one (V1), a 1989 Chevrolet S-10 Blazer, was traveling on the on-ramp from eastbound to northbound on the interstate. For unknown reasons the driver lost control of the vehicle, the vehicle rotated clockwise while traveling off the roadway onto the right shoulder and sod roadside. The vehicle rolled left four-quarter turns and came to rest on its wheels facing south. The vehicle was towed due to damage. The driver sustained possible injuries. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

The driver, a 36 year-old male was not available for interview.

The critical pre-crash event was coded "this vehicle loss of control-unknown cause of control loss." The critical reason was coded "type of driver error unknown." The driver lost control while rounding the curve and the vehicle ran off the inside of the curve and rolled over.
