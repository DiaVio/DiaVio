This two-vehicle collision occurred at the intersection of a two-way five-lane road with a two-way turn lane in the center, and a two-way side street. The intersection is controlled by a color signal for the main road, but only stop signs for the side street. Both roads are paved with bituminous asphalt. The speed limit for the both roads is 48 kmph (30 mph). At the time of the crash the sky was overcast but there was no precipitation.

Vehicle one (V1), a 1995 Toyota Camry was leaving a parking lot and entering the side street. Vehicle two (V2), a 2004 Volkswagen Jetta was southbound in the second lane. V1 entered the side street and began to turn left onto the main road. The front of V2 struck the left side of V1, and the two cars came to rest perpendicular and touching in lane two. V1 was towed due to disabling damage, but V2 was driven from the scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The driver of V1, a thirty-eight year old male was not interviewed. There is no known pre-crash information for this driver. The police report did indicate that following this crash; this driver was constantly on the cell phone. It is unknown if the phone was in use during the crash.

The driver of V2, a twenty-four year old female gave a partial interview. There is very little known pre-crash information.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: this vehicle traveling, turning left at intersection. The critical reason for the critical event was attributed to this vehicle but due to a lack of an interview was coded as a driver related factor: type of driver error unknown.

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded: other vehicle encroachment; from crossing street, turning into opposite direction. The critical reason for the critical event is not coded to this vehicle.
