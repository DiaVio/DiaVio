This single-vehicle crash occurred at night (dark with no streetlights) with no adverse atmospheric conditions. The roadway is undivided and consists of two lanes in each direction. The northbound lanes have a three percent downhill grade and are curved to the left (the radius of curvature is 39 meters and the superelevation is three percent). The speed limit for this roadway is 72 kmph (45 mph).

Vehicle one (V1), a 1987 Pontiac 6000, was northbound in lane one. V1 traveled over the left lane line and then back into lane one. V1 rotated clockwise off the right side of the road. The left side of V1 struck a pole and the front of V1 struck a building. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is No PAR obtained (created) for V1.

The driver of V1 left the scene prior to police arrival. No information is available for this driver.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "this vehicle traveling; over the lane line on left side of travel lane." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "driver related factor; overcompensation."
