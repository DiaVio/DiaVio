This crash occurred on a two-lane undivided roadway. There was one eastbound lane and one westbound lane. The roadway had an uphill grade of 8 percent, curved to the left, was bituminous, and had a posted speed limit of 40 kmph (25 mph). It was early in the morning on a weekday, the road was icy, and the sky was clear.

V1 was a 1995 Volkswagen Jetta III driven by a 22-year-old female. V2 was a 1985 Oldsmobile Delta 88 that was legally parked off of the road on the left side. V1 was traveling westbound negotiating a left hand curve when it hit a patch of ice. The ice caused V1 to rotate in a counterclockwise direction, crossing over the lane line to the left and then off the left side road edge where it impacted V2. Police Reported Travel Speed is 40 kmph for V1 and 40 kmph for V2.

The Critical Pre-crash Event was "this vehicle control loss due to poor road conditions (icy roads)." The Critical Reason for the Critical Pre-crash Event was an environmental factor - slick roads. The ice accumulation on the road was caused by poor drainage, as related by an officer on the scene. According to the officer this was a common occurrence. Several attempts were made to contact the driver of V1, without success. Without an interview, no additional associated factors could be coded to the driver.
