This single vehicle crash occurred on a divided, level, straight roadway. The trafficway consists of two westbound lanes and two eastbound lanes that are divided by a depressed dirt median. There are paved (with rumble strips) and unpaved shoulders on both sides of the travel lanes. The posted speed limit is 121 kmph (75 mph). The crash took place during daylight hours and there were no adverse atmospheric or road conditions.

Vehicle one (V1), a 2003 GMC Envoy, was westbound in the number two lane with the cruise control set at 117 kmph (73 mph). The vehicle traveled off the left side of the roadway onto the shoulder. The driver steered to the right and the vehicle returned to the roadway and began to enter lane one. The driver steered to the left and the vehicle began to rotate counterclockwise. The vehicle departed the left side of the road and rolled two quarter turns to the right, still rotating counterclockwise. The vehicle rolled back on its wheels and came to rest in the median facing north. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

The driver of V1, a sixty-nine-year-old female, was traveling with her seventy-year-old husband, from their home to a vacation in another state. She stated that she was looking out of her left window into the desert. She said that she was well rested and had been driving for approximately two hours. She was very comfortable with the route and her vehicle. The driver said that she takes medication for asthma and emphysema.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: this vehicle traveling, off the edge of the road on the left side. The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event was coded to V1 as a driver related factor: inattention (i.e., daydreaming).
