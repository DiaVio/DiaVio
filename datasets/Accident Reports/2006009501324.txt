This crash occurred during the afternoon hours on a weekend at the intersection of two roadways. The east-west roadway was eight lanes divided by a median, there were 4 east and 4 westbound lanes, and it had a radius of curvature of 113 meters. The north -south roadway was three lanes and undivided. The posted speed limit was 89kmph (55mph).

V1, a 1999 Toyota 4 Runner was traveling east in lane two intending on going straight through the intersection. V2, a 1997 Honda Accord was traveling west in lane four, intending on turning left as she approached the intersection. V2 began her turn left and contact was made to front planes of both vehicles. V2 rotated counterclockwise and came to rest in the intersection. V1 deflected from impact, and departed the roadway to the right. V1 then rolled four quarter turns to the right and came to rest in the grass adjacent to the roadway. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

V1 was driven by a 50 year-old male who refused to be interviewed.

The Critical Pre-crash Event for this vehicle was coded “other vehicle encroachment from opposite direction - over left lane line”. The Critical Reason was “not coded to this vehicle”.

V2 was driven by a 58 year-old female who stated that she was on her way to a family gathering for the holiday. She had been driving about half an hour before the crash. She was trying to make the left turn and had the green arrow and was almost through the intersection when she was hit by the truck. She spun around and remembered hearing the truck make a loud noise when it rolled over.

The Critical Pre-crash Event for this vehicle was coded “this vehicle traveling turning left at intersection”. The Critical Reason was coded “False assumption”.
