This two vehicle daytime collision occurred just past noon on a five lane, divided, dry, level, asphalt highway. The highway has designated speed limit of 104 kmph (65 mph). A concrete center divider borders the left shoulder. A wood/metal guardrail borders the right shoulder. There is a slight curve to the right near the final rest areas but not in the pre-crash.

Vehicle one (V1) is a 2003 Toyota Corolla 4-door sedan, driven by a thirty six (36) year old female with two passenger was traveling west in the number four lane. Vehicle two (V2) is a 1994 Toyota Corolla 4-door sedan driven by a fifty nine (59) year old male was traveling west in the number three lane. As V1 changed lanes to the right, V2's front plane impacted V1's right plane. Both vehicles headed in a northwest direction. V2 rolled over towards its left and came to rest on its roof. V1 and V2 came to rest facing north in the number one lane. V1 was towed with moderate damage. V2 was towed with rollover damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 105 kmph for V1 and 105 kmph for V2.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: this vehicle traveling, over the lane line on the right side of travel lane. The critical reason for the critical event was assigned to V1 and coded as a driver related factor: type of driver error unknown. Due to lack of interview most factors are unknown and it could not be determined if the critical reason was due to a driver recognition or decision factor.

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded: other motor vehicle encroachment, from adjacent lane (same direction) - over the left lane line. The critical reason was not coded to this vehicle.
