This case involves a primary front end impact resulting in severe injuries to the 36-year-old female driver (case occupant). The case vehicle a four-door 2002 Honda Accord was in the inside northbound lane of a wet, divided, four-lane, asphalt traffic-way. It was dark but lighted and it was raining. The driver of the case vehicle lost control and the case vehicle went over the curb and sideswiped a 15-cm tree and ran over two tree stumps before it struck a 38-cm tree with its left-front bumper. The case vehicle rotated counterclockwise around the tree before it came to rest facing northeast. The vehicle was towed due to damage. The sole occupant was the 35-year-old female driver. She was not restrained by a three-point belt, but the steering-wheel air bag deployed. She was transported to a level-one trauma center with severe injuries.
