This single-vehicle crash occurred during daylight hours while it was snowing. The roadway is straight, level, undivided and consists of one lane in each direction. The speed limit for this roadway is 48 kmph (30 mph).

Vehicle one (V1), a 2000 Buick Century, was westbound in lane one. A dog entered the roadway from the right side. The driver of V1 steered left and applied the brakes. V1 rotated counterclockwise and traveled off the left side of the road. The front of V1 struck a tree. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

The driver of V1, a twenty-one-year-old male, was traveling with his twenty-two-year-old male friend. They had dropped the driver's aunt off at work and were on their way to the passenger's house. This driver was healthy, well rested and comfortable with his vehicle and passenger. He travels this route weekly. This driver stated that he was traveling at 48-64 kmph (30-40 mph).

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "object or animal; animal in roadway." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "driver related factor; overcompensation." The snow-covered roadway and the vehicle's speed were associated factors in this crash.
