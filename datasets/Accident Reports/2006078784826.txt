This two-vehicle crash occurred early in the morning on a two lane, two-way roadway divided by a centerline stripe. There is one northbound and one southbound lane. This was a straight level, bituminous, dry roadway with a posted speed limit of 80 kmph (50 mph). It was an early morning and the road was dry and the sky was clear.

Vehicle one (V1), 2004 Ford Focus LX 4door driven by a 22 year-old female, was traveling southbound at approximately 72 kmph (45 mph). Vehicle two (V2), a 2000 Ford F-150 4X2 pickup driven by a 35 year-old male, was traveling northbound at approximately 80 kmph (50 mph). V1 departed the southbound lane and began to travel in the northbound lane. In order to avoid being struck by V1, V2 steered to the left and traveled over the left lane line in a northwesterly direction. The driver of V1 then woke up and steered V1 to the right back into the southbound lane and locked up her brakes. The vehicle skidded for about 22-meters (72-feet) before it struck the right side of V2. The impact caused V2 to rotate counterclockwise and off the edge of the road in a northwesterly direction. The vehicle tripped on it's left side wheels and rolled for 3 quarters turns before it came to rest in a field on it's right side facing in a southeasterly direction. Police Reported Travel Speed is 72 kmph for V1 and 72 kmph for V2.

V1 came to rest in the southbound lane facing in a southerly direction. Both vehicles were towed from the scene.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: This vehicle traveling, over lane line on left side of travel lane. The critical reason coded driver related factor-sleeping, that is, actually sleeping. The driver had no sleep within in the last 24 hours and also worked an 8 hours shift during that time. She also played softball for 2 hours the night before the crash).

The critical pre crash event for V2 was coded: other motor vehicle in lane-traveling in opposite direction . The critical reason was not coded to this vehicle.
