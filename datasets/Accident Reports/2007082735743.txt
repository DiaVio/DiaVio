This single vehicle collision occurred heading south on a north/south arterial roadway. In the area of the crash the roadway has three lanes; one north bound, one south bound, and a center left turn lane. The road is paved with concrete, has a 3% upgrade and a curve to the left. The speed limit for the roadway is 56 kmph (35 mph). At the time of the crash the sky was clear and the roadway was dry.

Vehicle one (V1), a 1999 VW New Beetle driven by a forty eight (48) year old female, was heading south in lane one. The driver of V1 had not held her course through the full curve to the left. Her vehicle went off the road to the right, and immediately impacted a utility pole with the front of her vehicle. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: this vehicle traveling, off the edge of the road on the right side. The critical reason for the critical event was coded as a driver related factor: internal distraction. V1's driver had admitted to the investigating officer that she was eating just prior to the crash.

The associative factors coded to V1 include: other non-driving activity - eating or drinking; and, a performance error - poor directional control (e.g. failure to control vehicle with skills ordinarily expected). Medical records indicate the driver was taking the following medications: Ambien and Vicodin.
