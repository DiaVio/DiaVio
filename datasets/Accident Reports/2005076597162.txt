This two-vehicle crash occurred just past noon during daylight hours in a four-legged intersection. This intersection is in a rural area with stop signs controlling the east to west traffic lanes. Clear visibility and no adverse weather. The north to south roadway is on a downgrade of -8 percent with a wood fence on the right road edge blocking the view of the west travel lane. The east to west travel lanes have stop signs clearly posted.

Vehicle one (V1), a 2001 Oldsmobile Aurora 4 door sedan driven by a 69 year-old female was traveling eastbound approaching this intersection. The driver was not familiar with this roadway and looking for a road sign at this intersection. As she entered this intersection, she failed to stop at the stop sign and was impacted on the left front fender by Vehicle two (V2) a 2002 Dodge Grand Caravan driven by a 44 year-old male with five passengers was traveling south approaching the intersection. V1 after impact rotated clockwise and came to a controlled stop off the west side road edge heading in a southerly direction. V2 came to final rest in the intersection. Both vehicles were towed from the scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is 40 kmph for V1 and 40 kmph for V2.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: this vehicle traveling; crossing over (passing through) intersection. The critical reason for V1 was coded as a driver related factor: external distraction. An associative factors for V1: inadequate surveillance, and inattention.

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded: other vehicle encroachment; from crossing street, across path. The critical reason was not coded to this vehicle.
