This 3-vehicle rear-end configuration crash occurred during mid-afternoon daylight hours on a 6-lane asphalt roadway just past an intersection in a congested traffic area. The eastbound roadway consisted of 2 travel lanes which were straight, uphill and of bituminous construction. The westbound roadway consisted of 4 travel lanes including a left-turn only lane and a right-turn only lane. The speed limit in the crash area is 72 kmph (45 mph). The crash occurred with no adverse weather or roadway conditions.

Vehicle one (V1), a 1997 Chevrolet K-3500 pickup driven by a 21 year-old male was traveling eastbound in the #2 lane after just passing through a traffic light-controlled intersection when its front end contacted the rear end of Vehicle two (V2), a 1997 Ford F-350 pickup driven by a 47 year-old male. The front of V2 was pushed into the rear end of Vehicle three (V3), a 2005 Toyota Sequoia driven by a 52 year-old male with two passengers. All three vehicles came to rest in the #2 eastbound lane in the area of impact. V1 was towed from the scene. V2 and V3 were driven from the scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is 16 kmph for V1 and 16 kmph for V2.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: other motor vehicle in lane, other vehicle stopped. The critical reason was attributed to V1 as a driver related factor: internal distraction. The driver states his hat fell on the floor and he was bent over picking it up.

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded: other motor vehicle in lane, traveling in same direction with higher speed. The critical reason was not coded to this vehicle.

V3 was not involved in first harmful event. The critical reason was not coded to this vehicle.
