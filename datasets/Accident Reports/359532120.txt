This moderate severity frontal crash involved a 55 year male driver who was restrained with the manual lap/shoulder belt and the front steering column and knee bolster airbags deployed. This crash occurred in the northbound, one way lanes of a divided freeway with a paved shoulder on the east side. At the time of the crash, it was daylight with clear, dry conditions. Vehicle one (V1), a 2011 Toyota Prius four door hatchback was traveling in lane one of five lanes. V2, a 1989 Chevrolet S10 pickup truck was parked facing north on the paved shoulder and was not occupied. The driver of V1 experienced a medical issue, causing him to lose consciousness. V1 then drifted to the right, out of lane one and onto the paved shoulder. The front of V1 then impacted the back of V2. Both vehicles moved forward after impact, and came to final rest facing north on the shoulder. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. The driver of V1 is the case occupant who is a 55 year old male who was wearing the manual lap/shoulder belt. His seat belt retractor pretensioner actuated on impact and his frontal steering column and knee bolster mounted airbags deployed. He sustained serious injuries and was transported and hospitalized at a trauma center.
