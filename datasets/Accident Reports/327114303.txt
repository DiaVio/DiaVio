This case involves an impact to the right side of a compact SUV by a small pick-up truck. The restrained 66-year-old female driver is the case occupant. The available left side-impact airbags did not deploy. The case vehicle, a 2006 Honda CRV SUV was northbound in the left-turn lane of a dry, level, divided, four-lane, asphalt roadway approaching a controlled four-leg intersection. Vehicle two (V2), a 1994 Ford Ranger extended cab 4 x 4 pick-up truck was southbound approaching the same intersection. As the case vehicle made a left turn it was struck on the right-side by the front of V2. Both vehicles came to rest near the median on the south side of the intersection; the case vehicle was facing west and V2 was facing south-southwest. The restrained 66-year-old female driver and the unrestrained 83-year-old male right-front passenger were the only occupants of the case vehicle. The driver sustained moderate injuries and was transported to a level-one trauma center and enrolled as a case occupant. The right-front passenger was fatally injured.
