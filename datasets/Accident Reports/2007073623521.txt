This two-vehicle crash occurred in a four-legged intersection. The north/south roadway is undivided and consists of one lane in each direction. The northbound approach curves to the left, has a five percent downhill grade and is controlled by a stop sign. The east/west roadway is straight, level and undivided. The eastbound approach consists of one travel lane. The speed limit for this roadway is 48 kmph (30 mph). It was dark with street lights on at the time of the crash with no adverse atmospheric conditions.

Vehicle one (V1), a 2006 Volkswagen Jetta, was stopped northbound in lane one. Vehicle two (V2), a 1997 Ford Escort, was eastbound in lane one. V1 entered the intersection and the left side was struck by the front of V2. V1 traveled to the northeast corner of the intersection and the left side struck a mailbox. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The driver of V1, a twenty-five-year-old male, was not interviewed. No further information is available for this driver.

The driver of V2, a thirty-nine-year-old female, was on her way home from a restaurant. She was healthy, well rested and familiar with her vehicle. She travels this route daily. This driver stated that she did not see V1 until it entered the intersection and she steered to the left in an attempt to avoid the crash.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: this vehicle traveling; crossing over (passing through) intersection. The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as a driver related factor: type of driver error unknown.

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded: other vehicle encroachment; from crossing street, across path. The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V2 was not coded to this vehicle. This driver's inadequate surveillance was an associated factor in this crash.
