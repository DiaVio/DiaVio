This two-vehicle crash took place in the evening on a weekend. The crash occurred in the southbound lanes of a divided north/south roadway in a 72 kmph (45 mph) zone, consisting of three dry, level, bituminous lanes (six lanes overall, divided by a raised median). At the time of the crash, it was dark, and the roadway was illuminated by streetlamps. There were no adverse weather conditions and the roadway was free of defects.

Vehicle one (V1), a 2004 Landrover Discovery 4x4 SUV, was traveling southbound in the third lane. Vehicle two (V2), a 2005 Honda Element SUV, was traveling southbound ahead of V1 in the same lane at a constant speed. The front of V1 impacted the back of V2. Both vehicles came to rest in their original lane. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The 27-year-old female driver of V1 refused to participate in an interview. She reportedly told the police on-scene that she saw the vehicle ahead of her and tried to slow, but that her brakes were unresponsive and she could not stop in time. There is no evidence to substantiate the claim of brake failure. The critical precrash event for V1 was: Other motor vehicle in lane, traveling in same direction with lower steady speed. The critical reason for the event was attributed to V1, and was coded: Driver related factor, type of driver error unknown.

The 44-year-old female driver of V2 could not be interviewed due to her inability to understand English. She was able to relate to police that she was traveling at a constant speed when she was impacted from behind. The critical precrash event for V2 was: Other motor vehicle in lane, traveling in same direction with higher speed. The critical reason for the event was not attributed to V2.
