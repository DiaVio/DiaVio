This single-vehicle crash occurred in a rural area on a two-lane asphalt roadway with various curves and elevations. In the area of the crash, the eastbound lane curves right then straightens out and is uphill. There is a steep downhill embankment bordering the right side of the roadway after the right curve with guardrail protection. The speed limit is 89 kmph (55 mph). The crash occurred just past noon during daylight with no adverse weather or road conditions.

Vehicle one (V1), a 2005 Honda Odyssey minivan, was eastbound when it gradually departed the right road edge contacting the end of the guardrail with its front-end plane. V1 continued forward where it traveled down the steep embankment impacting trees with its front end and left side planes, and impacting the ground with its undercarriage. V1 came to rest at the bottom of the embankment facing east. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 72 kmph for V1.

The driver, a seventy one (71) year-old female, stated to police that she did not know what happened and did not recall driving off the road. She remembered going down the embankment. She suffers from diabetes, heart disease, abnormal heart rhythm, for which she takes numerous medications for including Coumadin, Dilantin, Digitek, Lasix and Colestid.

A witness stated he was following V1 for sometime when she pulled off onto the right shoulder. He went around her then he noticed she drove onto the road again and was traveling behind him. He saw her gradually depart the right road edge and impact the guardrail before going down the embankment. He stopped and went down to help.

The critical pre-crash event was coded: this vehicle traveling, off the edge of the road on the right side. The critical reason for the critical event was coded as a driver related factor: heart attack or other physical impairment of the ability to act.
