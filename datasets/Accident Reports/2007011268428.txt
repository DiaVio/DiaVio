The crash occurred on a two-way, two-lane, bituminous roadway with a posted speed limit of 72 kph (45 mph). Conditions at the time of the weekend afternoon crash were daylight, cloudy and dry.

V1, a 2006 Dodge Dakota 4 door club cab 4 WD pick up truck driven by an 18 year-old male was south bound on the roadway traveling 45 mph. The driver stated that he was on his way home after working 10.5 hours and fell asleep. The road was a downhill 1% slope, initially straight and then curved left where v1 left the roadway. The witness stated that v1 was behind him and moved to the left and the witness thought v1 was attempting to pass him. V1 then cut to the right and went off of the roadway on the right side. The right side of v1 sideswiped a plastic mailbox. V1 continued off of the road into a ditch and the front of v1 struck a 20 cm diameter tree. V1 began to roll to the left and stopped 1/8 of the way when the top of the hood on the left side struck a wood utility pole 20 cm in diameter. V1 came to a stop in the ditch off the right side of the road facing southwest. V1 was towed from the scene due to damage. The fully restrained driver was transported by a relative to a local hospital for treatment. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

The Critical Precrash Event coded to v1 was 'this vehicle traveling off the edge of the road on the right side'. The Critical Reason coded to this driver was 'a driver related critical non-performance error of sleeping/actually asleep'. The contributing factor coded to this driver was 'fatigue'.
