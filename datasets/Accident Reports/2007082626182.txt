This two-vehicle daytime rear-end collision occurred on an on ramp to a major highway. The on ramp curves to the right with a yield sign. The main highway also curves to the right and creates a blind corner for the on ramp. The road is paved with concrete and has an unknown uphill grade. The speed limit for the road is 64 kmph (40 mph). At the time of the crash the sky was cloudy with no adverse conditions.

Vehicle one (V1), a 1999 Mazda Miata coupe driven by a twenty three (23) year old female with one passenger, Vehicle two (V2), a 2002 Mini Cooper coupe driven by a thirty (30) year old female was heading north up the on ramp. V2 was merging and saw another vehicle coming around the corner so it came to a stop; V1 was looking rearward to merge and did not realize V2 had come to a stop. V1 then rear-ended V2. Both vehicles came to rest at point of impact. V1 was towed due to disabling damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: other vehicle in lane, other vehicle stopped. The critical reason for the critical event is coded to V1 as a driver related factor: external distraction. The driver of V1 had assumed V2 had merged and was looking rearward to see if traffic was clear without looking forward.

The driver of V2 was unable to be located for an interview after numerous attempts. The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded: other motor vehicle in lane, traveling in same direction with higher speed. The critical reason was not coded to this vehicle.
