This two-vehicle collision occurred in the late afternoon during daylight hours. The collision occurred in a busy intersection. The east-west leg of the intersection has four lanes, the north-south leg has five lanes, and the posted speed limit is 56 kmph (35 mph). The pre-crash roadway is straight and level, with bituminous paving. It was clear and dry.

Vehicle one (V1) was a 1999 Acura 3.2 TL 4-door sedan driven by a forty six (46) year old female with one passenger. V1 was traveling eastbound in lane one. Vehicle two (V2) was a 1996 Toyota Camry 4-door sedan driven by a twenty two (22) year old female with one passenger. V2 was southbound in lane one. V2's light turned to green, and as V2 accelerated into the intersection, the front of V2 impacted the left side of V1. V1 was towed from the scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The driver of V1's and the driver of V2's pre-crash factors are mostly unknown, as they was not reached for interview.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: this vehicle traveling, crossing over (passing through) intersection. Critical reason for the critical event coded driver related factor "aggressive driving behavior". According to police report, the driver of V1 was speeding and had disregard for the traffic signal. V1 entered the intersection after V2 had already entered it. Inadequate surveillance was considered an associative factor for this driver.

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded: other vehicle encroachment; from crossing street, across path. The critical reason for the critical event was not coded to this vehicle.
