This crash occurred in the eastbound lane of a two lane undivided roadway just prior to a work zone. This was a straight, level, bituminous roadway with a posted speed of 56 kmph (35mph). It was early afternoon on a weekday and the road was dry. Traffic flow was light and moving easily.

V1, a 1994 Chevrolet S10 pickup truck with one occupant, was traveling east when the operator lost consciousness and departed the roadway to the right and contacted a utility pole. Police Reported Travel Speed is Unknown for V1.

V1 was driven by a 21-year old male who sustained lacerations to his face. He advised that he is a carpenter who was laid off and returned back to work and developed severe flu symptoms and was sent home early. He believes he blacked out prior to the accident and had no recollection of the crash events.

The Critical Pre-crash Event for V1 was this vehicle traveling off the road on the right side. The Critical Reason for the Critical Pre-crash event was a critical non-performance error, other critical non-performance error, severe flu symptoms leading to a blackout. An associated factor was driver fatigue, the driver is a carpenter and is required to lift heavy pieces of wood, also, this was his first day back to work after being laid-off.
