Vehicle one (V1), a 2003 Infiniti FX35 and the case study vehicle, was traveling south in lane one of a two lane, two way roadway under rainy, daylight conditions. Vehicle two (V2), a 1999 Ford F350 pickup truck, was traveling north in lane two of the same roadway. Vehicle three (V3), a 2002 four door Ford Focus, was traveling north in front of V2 and was slowing to make a left turn. V2 swerved to miss V3 and the front of V2 contacted the front of V1. V1 rotated counterclockwise after impact and came to rest off the roadway one the western side,. After impact V2 rotated counterclockwise, striking V3, and both V2 and V3 came to final rest in the roadway. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. The driver and sole occupant of V1, a 42 year old female, was wearing a lap and shoulder belt and had both frontal airbags deploy as well as the driver side seat mounted thorax bag. The driver of V1 was transported to a level one trauma center with serious injuries.
