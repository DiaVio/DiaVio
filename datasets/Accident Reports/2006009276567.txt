This crash occurred in the northbound lanes of a 3-way intersection of a roadway. The northbound lane has a slight curve to the left. The roads were dry. The pavement is bituminous and the weather was clear. Traffic flow is at a minimum.

V1, a 2006 Chrysler Town and Country with 4-doors and 1 occupant, was traveling westbound into the intersection in lane one. V2, a 1997 Jeep Grand Cherokee with 3 occupants, was traveing northbound in lane one. V1 proceeded into the intersection without yielding to V2, causing it to strike V2 on its front right side. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

V1, the 2006 Chrysler Town and Country, was driven by a 27 year old female who was not injured. 5th attempt to talk to driver came up somewhat successful. Driver had said she would call back and has not. More attempts were made and came up unsuccessful. Driver of V1 approved of interview if authorized by attorney. Attorney (case manager) has been difficult to reach as well.

Tow lot attendant had stated that when she had talked to driver of V1 about obtaining her vehicle, she had stated that the driver of V1 stated that she did not know that there was a stop sign at that intersection.

The Critical Pre-crash Event for V1 was “this vehicle traveling turning right at the intersection”. The Critical Reason for Critical Pre-crash Event was a “driver related error, unknown recognition error”.

V2, the 1997 Jeep Grand Cherokee, was driven by a 34 year old female who was transported due to injuries. There were two occupants also in the vehicle ages 33, and 9. The 9-year old was transported to the hospital as well. Contact with driver of V2 was successful but would only do interview if attorney approved. Several attempts to contact attorney (case manager) has come up unsuccessful.

The Critical Pre-crash Event for V2 is “other vehicle encroachment from crossing street turning into same direction”. The Critical Reason was not coded to this vehicle.
