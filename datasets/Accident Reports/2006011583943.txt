Summary 038

Vehicle 1, a 2003 Honda Civic 4dr, was westbound in lane 2 of 2 westbound lanes of a 4 lane, bituminous, dry, level, straight, interstate roadway with a positive barrier separating the westbound and eastbound lanes. At the time of the crash it was daylight with heavy rush hour traffic with a posted speed limit of 113 kmph (70mph). V1 could not stop in time for slowing traffic in lane 2, steered right into lane 1 then steered left so as not to exit the roadway to the right. V1 exited the roadway to the left and struck the median wall with its front. V1 then rotated counterclockwise and while traveling backwards, struck the median wall again with its right rear. V1 came to rest against the median wall facing east. The 27-year-old female driver of v1 refused treatment. V1 was towed. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

A complete driver interview and vehicle inspection was obtained for v1. The driver was properly licensed for and familiar with the vehicle. She was also familiar with the road and its traffic patterns. The Pre-event movement of v1was the successful avoidance maneuver to a previous critical event, the Critical pre-crash event was This vehicle traveling off the edge of the road on the left side and the Critical reason for the critical pre-crash event was this vehicle going too fast to be able to respond to unexpected actions of others. Some factors which may have contributed to the cause of the crash include rush hour traffic congestion, the driver's failure to look far enough ahead, impending problem masked by traffic flow pattern, following too closely and too much speed for traffic conditions.
