The crash occurred near the intersection of two roadways. The north/south roadway was a two-way, five-lane undivided street. The east/west roadway was a two-way, three-lane undivided street. The intersection was controlled by a stoplight. These were straight, level, asphalt roads, with a north/south posted speed of 56 kmph (35 mph). Conditions were daylight on a weekend morning with dry roads and clear skies. Traffic flow was normal.

V1, a 2002 Mazda Protégé sedan with one occupant, was traveling northbound in lane one, approaching the intersection. The driver fell asleep as he entered the intersection. The vehicle began drifting to the right. Just after passing through the intersection, the vehicle departed the right side of the roadway. The front of V1 made contact with a concrete utility pole. The vehicle was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is No PAR obtained (created) for V1.

The Mazda Protégé was driven by a 28-year old male, who was not injured. He stated that he was on his way home from a thirteen-hour shift at work, and was exhausted. The driver explained that he worked two jobs, one of which as a bicycle-taxi driver at a nearby casino. He said that the bicycle-taxi job was particularly fatiguing the night before the crash. In addition, the driver decided to forego the routine cup of coffee he uses to help stay awake after such a busy shift. The driver indicated that he fell asleep behind the wheel before reaching his destination, crashing into a pole.

The Critical Precrash Event for V1 was "this vehicle traveling, off the edge of the road on the right side." The Critical Reason for the Critical Event was a driver-related critical non-performance error, sleeping. The driver was actually asleep at the time of the crash. An associated factor for this driver was fatigue.
