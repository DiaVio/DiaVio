The crash occurred in the westbound lane of a three lane undivided roadway. There was one westbound lane and two east bound lanes. At 100 meters east of the crash scene the westbound lane lines disappear to make one large lane. This was an 8% downhill grade, straight, bituminous roadway with a posted speed limit of 56 kmph (35 mph). It was early afternoon on a weekday and the road was dry and skies were clear. Traffic flow was light and moving freely.

V1, a 1995 Oldsmobile Cutlass Supreme sedan with one occupant, was traveling west in lane one. V2, a 2002 Pontiac Grad Prix sedan with one occupant, was traveling west in lane one behind V1. While traveling west, the driver of V1 noticed the brakes were not working properly. The driver left the roadway and entered a church parking lot in attempt to stop the vehicle. When this failed the driver reentered the westbound roadway. The driver of V2 had not noticed V1 reenter the roadway struck the left rear quarter panel of V1 with the front of V2. V1 continued across the two eastbound lanes and struck an embankment with the front of V1, coming to rest on the eastbound shoulder facing west. V2 came to rest in the westbound lane. Police Reported Travel Speed is Unknown for V1 and Unknown for V2.

V1, the Oldsmobile Cutlass, was driven by an 88 year old male who reported no injuries. He advised he was attempting to slow his vehicle for the downhill section of roadway and realized his brakes were not working. In an attempt to stop his vehicle he entered a parking lot and pumped his brakes. When the vehicle did not slow down he reentered the roadway but did not look to see if there was traffic coming. He was struck in the rear by V2 and continued until the vehicle impacted an embankment .

The Critical Precrash Event for V1 was “this vehicle traveling unknown travel direction”. The Critical Reason for the Critical Precrash Event was vehicle related, “brake failure”. Associated factors would be inadequate surveillance before entering the roadway, failing to look to the side. The driver also had a hearing impairment that could have prevented him from hearing the brake sensors, indicating it was time to replace the brakes.

V2, the 2002 Pontiac Gran Prix, was driven by a 50 year old female who was transported from the scene due to a complaint of pain in the lower back and whiplash. She stated she did not notice the other vehicle until just before the crash and she could not stop in time.

The Critical Precrash Event for V2 was “other vehicle encroachment from driveway, intended path not known”. The Critical Reason for the Critical Precrash Event was not coded to this vehicle. The driver was suffering from anxiety, GERD, bipolar disorder, asthma, hysterectomy, and gastric bypass. She was taking medication for these conditions.
