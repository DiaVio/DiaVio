This two-vehicle crash occurred just before noon on a dry, two-lane, asphalt undivided, highway. The highway runs east/west at this point and has one lane eastbound and one lane westbound with a rumble strip present in the middle. The highway winds through a canyon and curves at the point of the crash. There are no traffic controls present at the scene. The speed limit is 64 kmph (40 mph) and there were no adverse atmospheric conditions present.

Vehicle one (V1), a 2003 Ford Crown Victoria Police Interceptor, driven by a 34 year-old female with one passenger was parked on the right shoulder of the highway and its driver had just arrested someone for driving under the influence of alcohol. Vehicle two (V2), a 1997 Ford Explorer, driven by a 58 year-old female with one passenger was traveling eastbound in lane one, negotiating a right curve. V1 initiated a u-turn from the right shoulder across the highway intending to travel eastbound. As V1 entered the eastbound lane its right side was contacted with the front of V2. The impact caused V1 to rotate counterclockwise. It came to rest facing east on the westbound shoulder and partly in lane one. After the impact, V2 traveled forward and came to rest in the eastbound shoulder and eastbound lane one. V2 had moderate frontal damage and was driven from the scene. V1 sustained moderate right side damage and was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: this vehicle traveling, over the lane line on left side of travel lane. The critical reason for the critical event was coded as a driver related factor: internal distraction, due to the prisoner in rear seat.

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded: other vehicle encroachment; from crossing street, turning into same direction. The critical reason was not coded to this vehicle.
