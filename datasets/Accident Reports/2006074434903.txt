This single vehicle crash occurred on a four-lane divided roadway. This roadway is curved to the left for westbound traffic. The road has two travel lanes in either direction. This road has a speed limit of 64 kmph (40 mph). This crash occurred during nighttime hours with additional lighting.

Vehicle one, a 1999 Nissan Altima was westbound in the first lane. Vehicle one departed lanes to the left then back to the right. Vehicle one then departed the road and began to rotate clockwise. The left of vehicle one impacted a utility pole and vehicle one rolled over. Vehicle one came to rest on its roof in the first westbound lane. Vehicle one was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

The driver of vehicle one, a thirty-five year old male had just picked up his daughter and was heading home. It is unknown how long he had been driving on this particular portion of the trip but he left home to pick up his daughter about one half hour before the crash. This driver indicated that he had been out of work for several months and had a two-hour job interview this morning. This driver stated that after the interview he had one drink. Medical records indicated a BAC of .307. This driver indicated that he typically picks up his daughter from day care. He stated that while they drive back he constantly talks to her, and is looking at her in the rearview mirror. This driver drives this vehicle daily and over this road on a daily basis. This vehicle had several tires with low tire pressure and low tread depths.

The critical pre-crash event for vehicle one was coded: this vehicle traveling off the edge of the road on the right side. The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for vehicle one was coded as a driver related performance factor: poor directional control, e.g., failed to control vehicle with skill ordinarily expected. This lack of control was related to the driver's consumption of alcohol. The driver may also have passed out due to alcohol and then awakened.
