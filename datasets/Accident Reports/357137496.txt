This case involves an impact to the right side of a van (far-side relative to the case occupant). The 36-year-old male driver is the case occupant. He was not restrained by a three-point belt and the frontal impact air bags did not deploy. No side impact air bags were available. The case vehicle a 2009 GMC 3500 van was northbound on a snowy/slushy, two-lane asphalt road. It was dawn and snowing. The driver lost control and the case vehicle entered a counterclockwise rotation, departed the roadway off the left shoulder and struck a tree with its right-front door. The 36-year-old male driver was the only occupant of the case vehicle. He was not restrained by a three-point belt and the frontal impact air bags did not deploy. He was transported to an outside hospital before being transferred to a level-one trauma center with serious injuries and enrolled as a case occupant.
