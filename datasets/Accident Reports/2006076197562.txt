This single-vehicle crash occurred in a rural area on a four-lane divided asphalt roadway with an extended median. There are two lanes in either direction. Where vehicle one departed the roadway a left turn bay begins. The southbound lanes are straight and slightly downhill and there is a rumble strip present on the right shoulder. The speed limit for the area is 104 kph (65 mph). The crash occurred during daylight with no adverse weather or road conditions present.

Vehicle one, a 2005 Cadillac CTS was southbound in the first lane. Vehicle one gradually crossed the southbound fog line to the right, contacted the rumble strip then steered left. The vehicle rotated counter-clockwise as it crossed both travel lanes to the left. Vehicle one departed the road edge and contacted a series of small bushes in the dirt median with its right side plane before contacting a large mesquite tree with its rear right side plane. Vehicle one then rotated clockwise and continued forward where it came to rest in the median facing southeast. Vehicle one was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Unknown for V1.

The driver of vehicle one, a forty-one year old male refused to be interviewed. Medical reports and evidence inside of the vehicle revealed the driver to be diabetic requiring daily insulin injections and at the time of the crash, he was extremely hyper-glycemic with a glucose level of 336. Medical reports also state he may have had an alcoholic withdrawal seizure and his ETOH level was .329. The prescription drug "Zoloft" was located inside the vehicle which is consistent with the alcoholic withdrawal program per the Medical Records.
