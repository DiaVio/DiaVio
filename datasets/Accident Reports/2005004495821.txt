The crash occurred in the eastbound lane of a five-lane roadway. Conditions were dry with sun glare in the early morning on a weekday. The posted speed limit was 45 MPH.

V1 was a 2001 Nissan Altman sedan traveling eastbound in the 1st travel lane intending on going straight. V2, a 1994 Honda Accord station wagon, was stopped in traffic in the eastbound lane directly in front of V1. The front of V1 struck the back of V2 in the eastbound lane. Both vehicles came to final rest facing eastbound. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

V1 was driven by a 17-year-old male who sustained moderate injuries but refused medical attention. He stated that he was on his way to school and had some fog on his windshield and there was real bad sun glare. The driver of V1 also stated that he never saw V2. V1 did not take any avoidance actions and was traveling between 41-50 MPH at the time of impact. V1 was towed due to damage.

The critical precrash event for V1 was other motor vehicle in lane, other motor vehicle stopped. The critical reason for the critical precrash event was weather related; glare from the sun. Associated factors coded to this driver include inadequate surveillance (looked but did not see), inexperienced driver (the driver had his license less than one year), and an environmental factor (sun glare).

V2 was driven by a 76-year-old female who sustained moderate injuries and was transported to a local hospital. She stated that she was stopped in traffic when V1 crashed into the back of her vehicle. V2 did not take any avoidance actions. V2 was towed due to damage.

The critical precrash event for V2 was other vehicle in the travel lane, traveling in the same direction with higher speed. No critical reason for the critical precrash event was coded to this driver. No associated factors were coded to this driver.
