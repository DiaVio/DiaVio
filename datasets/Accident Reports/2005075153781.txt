This two-vehicle crash occurred just past noon on a dry, straight, level, bituminous, two-lane road with a center two way left turn lane. The roadway runs east and west. There is one through lane in each direction with a center two-way left turn lane. Private driveways line the north and south sides of the street. There are no traffic controls at the scene. The speed limit on the road is 30 mph (48 kph) and there were no adverse weather conditions present at the time.

Vehicle one, a 1992 Ford E-150 Econoline passenger van, driven by a 43 year-old male was traveling eastbound in lane one. Vehicle two, a 1994 Ford Explorer, was parked in a driveway on the north side of the roadway facing north. Vehicle one began to swerve on the roadway. It veered to the left and traveled across the center two-way left turn lane and westbound lane one. It then departed the roadway where its front contacted some bushes, fence and it rode up on an embankment. Vehicle one vaulted off of some stairs into a driveway. The front of vehicle one then contacted the left side of vehicle two. Vehicle one continued forward where its right side contacted a utility pole. Vehicle one came to final rest against the utility pole facing east. Vehicle two, after impact, rotated counterclockwise and came to final rest in the driveway facing northwest. Vehicle one was towed from the scene due to moderate undercarriage damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 48 kmph for V1 and 48 kmph for V2.
