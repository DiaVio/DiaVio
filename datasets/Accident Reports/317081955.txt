This case involves a 68 year old female restrained front right passenger of a 2006 Hyundai Sonata four-door sedan (V1). There were three other occupants in the vehicle, the 46 year old female driver, an 8 year old male in the left rear seat and a 10 year old male in the right rear seat. The other vehicle involved in the crash was a 2008 Chevrolet Cobalt four-door sedan (V2). V1 was traveling north at approximately 80 kmph (50 mph) on the dry asphalt roadway as it entered the intersection. The roadway was straight with a slight uphill grade and had no controls for the north/south traffic lanes. V2 was going west on the intersecting roadway which was straight and level also traveling at the posted speed limit of 80 kph (50 mph). The east/west lanes were controlled by stop signs which were functioning propely. As V1 traveled through the intersection, it was struck in the right side by the front of V2. V1 rotated approximately 70 degrees clockwise and departed the west side of the roadway and traveled down a small embankment to final rest. V2 continued in a northwesterly direction and came to rest at the top of the embankment. Both vehicles were towed from the scene due to disabling damage. All four occupants of V1 were restrained by the 3-point lap and shoulder belts. The driver and front right occupant had deployed frontal airbags. The right side front seat mounted airbag and the right front and rear inflatable curtain airbag also deployed The front right passenger and the rear right passenger were transported by air to a level one trauma center and hospitalized due to the severity of their injuries. The driver and rear left passenger were transported by ground ambulance were treated and released from the trauma center.
