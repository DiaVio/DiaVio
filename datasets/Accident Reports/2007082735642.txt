This two-vehicle daytime collision occurred on a north/south roadway in the morning daylight hours. The roadway has two lanes of travel in the area of the crash, one north bound and one southbound. The speed limit for the asphalt paved northbound roadway is 48 kmph (30 mph). At the time of the crash the sky was clear and the roadway was dry.

Vehicle one (V1), a 2005 Honda Odyssey mini van driven by a thirty three (33) year old male, was turning left onto the northbound roadway from an intersecting roadway controlled by a stop sign. Vehicle two (V2), a 1979 Chrysler New Yorker sedan driven by a fifty (50) year old male, was headed north in lane one of the northbound roadway going straight with no traffic controls. V1, after backing out of the day care near the scene came to a stop at the intersection. He then checked the roadway and did not notice V2 headed northbound in his intended travel lane. The driver of V1 claims a utility pole and his vehicles A pillar may have contributed to lack of vision to V2. V1 then turned left into the number one travel lane of the northbound roadway. The front of V1 impacted the left side of V2. V2 continued north and leaving the road to the left and coming to final rest in the swale. V1 came to rest facing north just north of the intersection. V2 was towed from the scene; V1 was driven under its own power. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The driver of V2 was never located for inspection or interview.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: this vehicle traveling, turning left at intersection. The critical reason for the critical event was coded to V1 as a driver related factor: inadequate surveillance (e.g., failed to look, looked but did not see).

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded: other vehicle encroachment; from crossing street, turning into same direction. There is no critical reason for the critical event coded to this vehicle.
