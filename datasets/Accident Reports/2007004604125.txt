The crash occurred in the southbound lanes of a six-lane north/southbound roadway. The roadway consisted of three southbound lanes, two northbound and a center turn lane. All lanes were bituminous and level with a posted speed limit of 72 kmph (45 mph). It was night on a weekday. The roads were dry and the sky was clear. Traffic flow was light.

V1, a 2005 Nissan Murano utility vehicle with one occupant, was traveling north with the intention of turning left from the turn lane into a driveway. V2, a 2003 Toyota Matrix hatchback with two occupants, was traveling south in lane two when V1 turned in front of it, causing V2 to strike V1 on the right side. V2 attempted to avoid V1 by applying the brakes. V1 was unable to take any avoidance action. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The driver of V1, a 27-year-old male who reported no injuries, stated that he saw V2 approaching, but misjudged the distance and the speed of V2 when he attempted to turn left in front of V2. V1 made no avoidance maneuver and was struck on the right side by V2. The driver claimed to have had his headlights on and left turn signal activated.

The Critical Pre-crash Event for V1 was "This vehicle traveling-turning left at intersection." The Critical Reason for the Critical Event was "Driver Related Factor- Misjudgment of gap or other's speed." No additional associated factors were assigned.

V2 was driven by a 55-year-old male and occupied by a 51-year-old female. Both reported complaints of pain to the chest and were transported to the hospital. The driver stated that he was traveling south when V1 suddenly turned in front of him and that V1 did not have its headlights on at the time of the crash. V2 struck V1 on the right side. The driver of V2 applied his brakes in an attempt to avoid V1.

The Critical Pre-crash Event for V2 was "Other vehicle encroachment from opposite direction-over the left lane line." No Critical Reason was coded to this vehicle. Associated factors coded to the driver of V2 include the use of prescription medications to control several medical conditions, including coronary artery disease, hypertension, high cholesterol, and a disabling back injury.
