This single-vehicle crash occurred in the late evening on a city street intersection in an urban neighborhood. The north/south roadway intersects with an east/west roadway. The east/west roadway ends at the intersection and has posted stop sign. The bituminous roadway is dry, straight, uphill and has a posted speed limit of 56 kmph (35 mph). The conditions were clear but dark with limited street lighting.

V1, a 1993 Dodge Spirit with one occupant, was westbound when it failed to stop at the intersection and continued to depart the end departure. The front of V1 struck a tree (40 cm in diameter) and the driver fled the scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is 48 kmph for V1.

The driver of V1 is a 35-year-old male that has a criminal history for selling crack, did not own this vehicle and was not interviewed for this case. The Critical Precrash Event for V1 was coded this vehicle traveling end departure. The Critical Reason for the Critical Event was coded unknown recognition error.
