This crash occurred at the intersection of two asphalt roadways. It was mid-afternoon on a weekday. It had just stopped raining and the roadway was wet. The posted speed limit was 35mph (56 kph.)

Vehicle 1 was a 2002 Dodge Dakota Pickup driven by a 21 year-old male. This vehicle was traveling south along a two-lane roadway. Vehicle 2 was a 2000 Honda Civic driven by a 34 year-old female. This vehicle was traveling north on the same roadway. Police Reported Travel Speed is 24 kmph for V1 and 24 kmph for V2.

Vehicle 1 turned left at an intersection and the front of Vehicle 1 contacted the front of Vehicle 2.

The Critical Precrash Event was vehicle 1 turning left at the intersection into the path of Vehicle 2.

The Critical Reason for the Critical Precrash Event for Vehicle 1 was inadequate surveillance. The driver of Vehicle 1 stated that he checked for opposing traffic but did not see vehicle 1. The driver of Vehicle 1 indicated that a school bus and SUV ahead of him limited his view of opposing traffic.
