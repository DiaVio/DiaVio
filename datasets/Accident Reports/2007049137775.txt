This two-vehicle crash occurred in a four-legged, traffic signal-controlled intersection. The north/south trafficway is straight and divided by a curbed median. The southbound roadway has a downhill grade and consists of three through lanes and a left turn lane. The northbound roadway has an uphill grade and consists of three through lanes, a left turn lane and a right turn lane. The posted speed limit is 56 kmph (35 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with clear skies.

Vehicle one (V1), a 1993 Chevrolet Silverado pickup, was southbound in the fourth travel lane. Vehicle two (V2), a 1998 Jeep Cherokee, was northbound in the third travel lane. As V1 attempted a left turn in the intersection, the front of V2 struck the right side of V1. V1 rotated counterclockwise and came to rest in the intersection facing east. V2 rotated clockwise and came to rest facing northeast in the intersection. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The driver of V1, a forty-year-old male, was not interviewed. No further information is available for this driver.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: this vehicle traveling-turning left at intersection. The critical reason for V1 was coded: driver related factor; type of driver error unknown.

The driver of V2, a forty-six year old male, was heading from home to work. This driver had been driving for about five minutes, drives the route daily and is familiar with the vehicle. The driver stated the light was green. He did not notice V1 until he entered the intersection and did not have time to attempt evasive maneuvers.

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded: other vehicle encroachment; from opposite direction, over left lane line. The critical reason for V2 was not coded to this vehicle.
