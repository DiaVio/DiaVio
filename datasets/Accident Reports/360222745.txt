This case occupant is a 50 year old male driver, restrained with a manual lap/shoulder belt, with no air bag deployments and who sustained serious injuries. This crash occurred during daylight hours with clear weather conditions and straight and level, dry concrete travel lanes. In the crash area, there is a narrow paved strip to the west of the travel lanes, followed by a jersey barrier. Vehicle one (V1), a 2007 Saturn Sky two door convertible, was northbound in lane four of a five lane, one way, interstate freeway. Further north, there was a non-contact vehicle in V1's travel lane that began to decelerate rapidly. The driver V1 changed lanes to the right, but lost control and began to rotate counterclockwise. V1 crossed over lanes four and five while in a yaw and V1's right side front impacted the jersey barrier, resulting in minor damage. V1 continued to rotate counterclockwise and the right side back impacted the jersey barrier, resulting in minor damage to the body of V1, but moderate damage to the right rear tire. V1 came to final rest across lane five, facing southeast. V1 was towed due to damage. The case occupant is the driver of V1, a 50 year old male, who was restrained with the available manual lap/shoulder belt. There were no air bag deployments. This driver sustained serious injuries. There were no other occupants in the vehicle.
