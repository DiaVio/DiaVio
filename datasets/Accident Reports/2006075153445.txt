This two-vehicle crash occurred in a four-legged, traffic signal-controlled intersection. The north/south roadway is straight and undivided. The northbound approach has a 6.6 percent downhill grade and consists of three through lanes and a left turn lane. The southbound approach has a 6.6 percent uphill grade and consists of three through lanes and a left turn lane. The speed limit on this roadway is 64 kmph (40 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.

Vehicle one (V1), a 2002 Volkswagen Beetle, was northbound in lane four. Vehicle two (V2), a 1994 Toyota 4Runner, was southbound in lane two. V1 turned left in the intersection and the front struck the left side of V2. V2 rotated counterclockwise and traveled off the southwest corner of the intersection. The left side of V2 struck an electrical box and V2 rolled over three quarter-turns. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 16 kmph for V1 and 16 kmph for V2.

The driver of V1, a twenty-one-year-old male, had just taken his mother to work and was on his way home. He was healthy and well rested. He has asthma and takes Pirbuterol. This driver was driving this vehicle for the first time, rarely travels this route and has been driving for one year. He stated that he did not see V2 prior to the crash.

The driver of V2, a twenty-six-year-old female, was traveling with a twenty-six-year-old male and two young children. They were on their way home from the driver's mother's workplace. This driver was healthy, well rested and comfortable with her vehicle and passengers. She travels this route weekly.

The pre-event movement for V1 was coded as "going straight." The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "this vehicle traveling; turning left at intersection." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "driver related factor; inadequate surveillance (e.g. failed to look, looked but did not see)." This driver's inexperience was an associated factor.

The pre-event movement for V2 was coded as "going straight." The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded as "other vehicle encroachment; from opposite direction - over left lane line." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V2 was "not coded to this vehicle."
