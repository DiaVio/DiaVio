This two-vehicle crash occurred mid-morning during daylight hours .The location is an urban industrial area with an arterial road with curbs and sidewalks. The roadway is a four lane, undivided, two way with a very sharp curve with an -8% negative grade for eastbound traffic. The road surface was dry bituminous. The weather was clear. The posted speed limit for both vehicles was 56 kmph (35 mph). There was an advisory 32 kmph (20 mph) speed sign for eastbound traffic approaching the curve. There were Chevron warning signs posted for the curve.

Vehicle one (V1), a 1997 Lincoln Town Car sedan driven by a fifty five (55) year old male, was traveling eastbound down the hill approaching the curve. The driver was traveling too fast and drifted across the centerline and struck vehicle two (V2) head-on. V2, a 1989 Chevrolet S-10 pickup driven by a thirty nine (39) year old male, was traveling westbound in the curb lane (lane one) approaching the curve. The driver stated she saw V1 drifting across the centerline as it exited the curve. The driver of V2 swerved to the left to try to avoid hitting V1 but was unsuccessful. The right front of V1 struck the right front of V2. On impact both vehicle separated and stopped in the roadway. V1 was driven away from the scene. V2 was towed due to damage. Neither driver was interviewed and no pre-crash information was obtained. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: this vehicle traveling, over the lane line on left side of travel lane. The critical reason for the critical event was coded to V1 as a driver related factor: too fast for a curve/turn.

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded: other vehicle encroachment; from opposite direction, over left lane line. The critical reason was not coded to this vehicle.
