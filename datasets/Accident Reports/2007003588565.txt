This crash occurred in the northbound lanes of a two-way roadway with three lanes in the south direction and five lanes in the north direction. This was a straight, level, and bituminous roadway with a speed limit of 48kmph (30mph). The roadway was snow and ice covered, it was the morning hours of a weekday and the sky was clear with no adverse conditions.

V1, a 2002 Toyota Camry four door with two occupants, was southbound in lane two. V2, a 1993 Buick LeSabre four door, was parked and unoccupied in the parking lane of the northbound roadway. V1 hit a patch of ice and lost control and crossed the roadway into the northbound lanes. The front of V1 impacted the left side of V2, causing the left side of V1 to contact the left side of V2. Then the front of V1 impacted a tree on top of the sidewalk. Both vehicles came to a final rest partially on the sidewalk after the impact. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The driver of V1, a 51-year-old female, reported no injuries. A 16 year-old male occupant also sustained reported no injuries. She advised that she was commuting from home and heading to a doctor's appointment. The driver stated that her vehicle slipped on ice and she lost control and went over the median before impacting V2 and then a tree. She stated that she did not attempt any avoidance maneuvers.

The Critical Precrash Event for V1 was coded this vehicle control loss due to, traveling too fast for conditions. The Critical Reason for the Critical event was coded as a Driver Related factor, too fast for conditions (slush and ice). Associated factors include driver fatigue (the driver only had 6.5 hours of sleep opposed to her normal 8), traveling too fast for conditions (not realizing that caution was required), and panic/freezing as she relinquished control of the vehicle. The slick surface of the roadways due to icy conditions was also coded as an associated factor.
