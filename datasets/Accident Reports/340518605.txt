This case involves an impact between the front of a compact 2005 Hyundai Tucson sport utility vehicle (SUV) and the rear end of a parked Chrysler Town and Country minivan. The case occupant is the driver of the SUV. The case vehicle, a 2005 Hyundai Tucson SUV(V1) was travelling in the outside eastbound lane of a slush covered, 3% positive incline, asphalt four-lane divided trafficway with legal parking lanes approaching a four-leg intersection. It was daylight and snowing. Vehicle 1 drifted into the adjacent parking lane and struck the rear end of a parked 1998 Chrysler Town and Country minivan (V2). The impact pushed V2 into a 2002 Ford Taurus(V3) parked in front of it. V1 came to rest facing east in the parking lane. The unrestrained 18 year-old male driver was the only occupant of V1. He had the benefit of deployed frontal-impact air bag. The driver was transported to a level-one trauma center with serious injuries where he was enrolled as a case occupant.
