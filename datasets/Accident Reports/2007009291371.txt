The crash occurred on a level, two-lane, rural road divided by double-yellow lines in the southbound direction with a right curve prior to the crash site. The posted speed limit was 48 kmph (30 mph). Traffic was light, the sky was clear and the road was dry during this weekday, early afternoon period.

V1, a 1992 four-door Honda Civic, was driven by an 18-year-old female traveling with four male friends varying in age from 13 to 17 years. The driver stated that she had just picked up her friends fifteen minutes prior to the crash and was going to the mall. She was traveling in lane one at an unknown speed negotiating a right curve when V1 skidded left out-of-control. She braked and continued to steer right, attempting to regain control of the vehicle. V1 continued skidding left over the double-yellow lines across the northbound lane and off the road to the left. V1 traveled off the road, rolled to the right and skidded for several meters before going up an incline and then tipping back to the left. V1 came to rest with its left side elevated at a 45-degree angle against a tree facing east. No injuries were reported. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

A police witness traveling in his cruiser ahead of V1 spotted V1 in his rear-view mirror. He stated that he saw V1 come around the curve at a speed of 128 kmph (80 mph). When the driver of V1 spotted his vehicle the officer stated that her eyes grew large and V1 lurched as the driver slammed on the brakes. The driver of V1 lost control, skidded left off the road and overturned.

The Critical Pre-Crash Event for V1 was "This vehicle control loss due to traveling too fast for conditions." The Critical Reason for the Critical Pre-Crash Event was "Other decision error - slammed on brakes in response to seeing a police car." Associated factors include driver age, inexperience, passenger load, an exterior factor (the police car), and excess speed.
