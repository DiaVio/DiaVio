The crash occurred in the northbound lane of a two-lane undivided north/south roadway. The roadway was dry, straight, and bituminous roadway with an uphill grade of 6 percent in the northbound direction and a posted speed limit of 64 kmph (40 mph). It was early evening on a weekday and the weather was clear. The roadway was dark.

V1, a Mercury Sable 4-door sedan with one occupant, was traveling northbound when a deer ran into the path of the vehicle. The deer made contact with the front of the vehicle, went onto the hood, and struck the right side of the windshield. The driver side and the passenger side airbags deployed. The vehicle came to rest on the roadway after leaving a skid mark. Police Reported Travel Speed is 56 kmph for V1.

The Mercury Sable was driven by a 53 year-old female who was transported to the hospital for neck and back pain. According to the police report, the vehicle was traveling 56 kmph (35 mph). The driver stated she slammed on her brakes but it was too late to avoid the deer. No interview was obtained due to the driver not returning voicemail messages.

The Critical Pre-crash Event for V1 was "Animal in roadway." The Critical Reason for the Critical Event was coded unknown due to the fact that an interview was not obtained with the driver. No associated factors could be coded.
