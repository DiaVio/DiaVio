This three vehicle daytime collision occurred just past noon at a four-way intersection. Both roadways are seven lanes, dry, level asphalt with a two-way left turn lane. The roadway is designated 56 kmph (35 mph). The collision occurred just after an intersection. The intersection is controlled by a on-color signal that was working properly at the time. The weather was clear and dry at the time.

Vehicle one (V1) is a 1998 Ford escort ZX2 2-door coup, driven by an unlicensed thirty three (33) year old female who was traveling east in the number two lane. Vehicle two (V2) is a 2006 Toyota Scion TC 2-door hatchback, driven by a thirty three (33) year old female who was traveling east in the number one lane. Vehicle three (V3) is a 1989 Dodge Caravan was legally parked in the southeast corner parking lot. Just after passing through the intersection V1 attempted a right turn into the private driveway from the number two lane. The right side of V1 was impacted by the front of V2. V1 continued southeast impacting the left side of V3 with its front plane. V1 came to rest at impact with V3 facing southeast. V3 came to rest facing east in the parking stall. V2 came to rest facing southeast in the driveway. V2 was driven from the scene with moderate damage. V1 was towed from the scene with moderate damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: this vehicle traveling, turning right at intersection. The critical reason for the critical event was coded to V1 as a driver related factor: inadequate surveillance (e.g., failed to look, looked but did not see). The driver said he never saw V2 prior to turning in front of V2. An associated factor for V1, was turning from the wrong lane.

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded: other vehicle encroachment; from adjacent lane (same direction) - over left lane line. The critical reason was not coded to this vehicle.
