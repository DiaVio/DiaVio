This single vehicle crash occurred during early evening daylight hours on a straight, divided, six-lane highway with warning signs of deer crossing the highway. The highway generally runs east and west, but runs northwest and southeast at the location of the crash. There are three through lanes in each direction. The speed limit is 110 kmph (65 mph) and there were no adverse weather conditions at the time.

Vehicle one (V1) a 2001 Ford Taurus sedan driven by a 43 year old female was traveling northwest in lane three. A non-contact vehicle was traveling in the same direction in lane two. The non-contact vehicle started to enter lane three. V1 started to accelerate and move to the left. The driver lost control while off the left side of the highway. V1 re-entered the highway and started rotating counter clockwise, running off the left side of the highway again and impacted a breakaway pole with its right side. V1 rotated clockwise and broke the pole from its base. V1 came to rest facing west in the median on its wheels facing southwest. V1 was towed due to damage to the right side. The driver was transported to a trauma center and hospitalized with major injuries. Police Reported Travel Speed is 32 kmph for V1.

The driver is a nurse who works three days a week, the night shift and had worked 24 hours that week. The date of the crash was her first day off. She had a good sleep that morning and felt rested at the start of the trip.

The pre event movement coded successful avoidance maneuver, critical event category other vehicle encroachment from adjacent lane over right lane line. Critical reason driver related factor, critical performance factor of overcompensation. Sped up when she saw non-contact vehicle enter her lane steered left & then right.

Additional factor of poor directional control.
