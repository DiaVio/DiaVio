This single-vehicle crash occurred on an undivided roadway. This roadway consists of one lane in each direction, curves to the right and has a downhill grade of -6.5 percent. A ditch and a steep uphill embankment are on the right side of the roadway. The left side of the roadway has a narrow dirt shoulder and then a 55 percent downhill embankment. The speed limit on this roadway is 56 kmph (35 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.

Vehicle one (V1), a 2001 Toyota 4-Runner, was northbound in lane one. V1 traveled off the left side of the roadway and overturned six quarter-turns down the embankment. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

The driver of V1, a fifty-year-old male, had driven approximately 209 kilometers (130 miles) from his girlfriend's house and was on his way to his work site. He was healthy and comfortable with his vehicle. He travels on this roadway several times each month. This driver stated that he had been on vacation and had made a 2,092 kilometer (1,300 mile) road trip during the previous two weeks. He stated that he was very tired and had taken a short nap 48 kilometers (30 miles) prior to the crash. This driver further stated that he was thinking about his vacation and just fell asleep.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: this vehicle traveling, off the edge of the road on the left side. The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as a driver related factor: sleeping, that is, actually asleep.
