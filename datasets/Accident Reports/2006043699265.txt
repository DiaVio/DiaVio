The crash occurred in the center median and east roadside of a divided, four-lane highway. The highway consisted of two northbound lanes divided from two southbound lanes by a large grass median with a center guardrail. The bituminous roadway was dry and level with a posted speed limit of 88 kmph (55 mph) and a 3% downhill grade (in the northbound direction). Conditions were daylight and dry with clear skies. The setting was mid-afternoon hours on a weekday.

Vehicle one (V1), a 1999 Hyundai Elantra sedan, was northbound in the second lane. V1 lost control and began skidding, traveling off the left side of the road into the grass median. V1 struck the guardrail with its left side. After striking the guardrail, V1 traveled back across the roadway and off the right roadside. V1 traveled down an embankment and struck a large tree roughly 20m (67ft.) off the roadway before coming to final rest against the tree. Police Reported Travel Speed is 80 kmph for V1.

The driver of V1, a 37-year old male, was transported to a local trauma center with non-incapacitating injuries. He was unable to be located for an interview. The investigating officer estimated his travel and impact speeds to be 80 kmph (50 mph). V1 was towed due to damage.

The Critical Precrash Event for V1 was coded this vehicle traveling, off the edge of the road on the left side. The Critical Reason was unknown. The only known associated factors include the driver's use of a learner's permit, indicating inexperience and the dangerously worn tires of the vehicle.
