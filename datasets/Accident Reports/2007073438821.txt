This single-vehicle crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions. The roadway is a controlled-access trafficway exit ramp that has an uphill grade, curves to the left and consists of two lanes. The speed limit for this roadway is 89 kmph (55 mph) with an advisory speed of 72 kmph (45 mph).

Vehicle one (V1), a 2002 Mitsubishi Montero, was eastbound in lane one. V1 traveled off the right side of the roadway and the front struck a signpost. V1 then rolled over to the left four quarter-turns. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

The driver of V1, a thirty-seven-year-old female, was "just driving" and "killing time" while traveling from her cousin's house to her aunt's house. She was healthy, well rested and familiar with her vehicle. She travels this route weekly. This driver stated that she was looking at a vehicle in lane two and thought that it was going to move to the right. She steered to the right and traveled off the road. She further stated, "After I ran off the road, there wasn't anything I could do."

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "this vehicle traveling; off the edge of the road on the right side." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "driver related factor; poor directional control (e.g., failing to control vehicle with skill ordinarily expected)." This vehicle's speed was an associated factor in this crash.
