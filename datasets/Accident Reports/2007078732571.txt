This single-vehicle crash occurred on a divided, straight trafficway. The westbound lanes have a downhill grade and consist of two through lanes with paved shoulders on both sides. The speed limit for this trafficway is 121 kmph (75 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.

Vehicle one (V1), a 1965 Chevrolet pickup, was westbound in lane one when the left rear tire tread separated. V1 rotated clockwise and traveled off the right side of the roadway. V1 rolled over four quarter-turns and was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Unknown for V1.

The driver of V1, a fifty-four-year-old female, was traveling with a sixteen-year-old male, five-year-old female and an eighty-year-old male. They were on their way home from a vacation and had been traveling for three-to-four hours at the time of the crash. This driver had been driving for approximately thirty minutes on this leg of the trip. She was healthy, well rested and comfortable with her vehicle and passengers. She rarely travels this route. This driver stated that she was traveling at 126 kmph (78 mph) at the time of the crash. When the tire tread separated, she steered to the right.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: this vehicle control loss due to - left rear tire tread separated. The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as a driver related factor: poor directional control (e.g., failing to control vehicle with skill ordinarily expected).
