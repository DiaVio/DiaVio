This crash occurred on a weekday afternoon in the southbound lanes of a divided four-lane roadway. The roadway consisted of two lanes in each direction divided by a grassy median. The bituminous roadway was straight and level with a posted speed limit of 88 kmph (55 mph). The traffic flow was slightly restricted due to the presence of construction and the roadway was wet from a rain that was falling at the time of the crash.

V1, a 1992 Oldsmobile Cutlass Ciera with two occupants, was southbound in lane two. V2, a 2005 Pontiac Montana minivan with one occupant, was also southbound in lane two ahead of V1. V1 was traveling at a higher rate of speed than V2 and did not slow down in time to avoid striking V2. The front of V1 made contact with the rear of V2. V1 was towed due to damage while V2 was driven from the scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is 80 kmph for V1 and 80 kmph for V2.

The driver of V1, a 20-year-old male, was transported along with his passenger for treatment of non-incapacitating injuries. After numerous attempts an interview could not be obtained with the driver of V1.

The Critical Precrash Event for V1 was coded other motor vehicle in lane, traveling at a steady lower speed. The Critical Reason was an unknown driver error. The rain and wet roads were the only known associated factors.

The driver of V2, a 38-year-old male, was uninjured and drove his vehicle from the scene. He informed this researcher that V2 was his company vehicle and it was against company policy for allow it to be inspected or for him to answer any questions about the crash.

The Critical Precrash Event was coded other motor vehicle in lane, traveling at higher speed. The Critical Reason for the Critical Event was not coded to this vehicle. The rain and wet roads were the only known associated factors.
