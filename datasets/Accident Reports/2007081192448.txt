This single vehicle crash occurred eight days before the vehicle was discovered at the bottom of a ravine. The time of day and weather conditions are unknown. The posted speed limit is 80 kmph (50 mph). The location is state route in a rural area. There are narrow shoulders on both roadsides and a drainage ditch. The road at the location of the crash is a bituminous and level two lane, two way with a slight left curve.

Vehicle one (V1), a 2007 Honda Element SUV, driven by a thirty three (33) year old female was traveling southbound approaching the curve. Scene evidence indicates the vehicle departed the roadway to the right and the right side wheels dropped off into a ditch. The vehicle followed the ditch until the ditch ended. The front of V1 struck a dirt embankment and vaulted V1 into a ravine. At some point the vehicle rotated counter-clockwise and rolled towards its left. The vehicle came to rest at the bottom of the ravine on its left side facing away from the road. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

The driver was wearing a lap and shoulder belt and according to the news media had minor injuries. She was in critical condition when she was extracted from her vehicle due to the length of time trapped in the vehicle, 8 days. There was no interview because the driver refused. According to the driver's husband in a news interview, she was on her way home from work traveling her normal route.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: this vehicle traveling, off the edge of the road on the right side. The critical reason for the critical event is coded as a driver related factor: type of driver error unknown.
