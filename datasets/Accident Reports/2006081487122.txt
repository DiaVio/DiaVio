A two-vehicle intersection collision occurred in the early evening hours, on a Friday. The collision occurred in a four-way intersection. The intersection is not controlled. The roadway is a two-way, eight-lane undivided roadway. The pre-crash roadway is level and straight, with bituminous paving. The posted speed limit for the roadway is 80 Kmph (50mph). Access to the roadway is not controlled.

Vehicle one (V1), a 1995 Ford Taurus 4 door sedan with one occupant, was originally headed north in lane four and then initiated a left turn at the uncontrolled intersection. Vehicle two (V2), a 1988 Honda Accord with one occupant, was traveling southbound in lane three. When V1 turned left, V2 attempted to steer right and braked with lock up. The front of V2 impacted the right side of V1. Both vehicles came to rest in the intersection. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Neither driver was transported. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The driver of V1 was a 33 year-old male whose pre-crash factors are unknown, as he was not interviewed. The driver stated to the investigating officer he did not see V2 prior to turning.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded “This Vehicle Traveling-turning left at intersection”. The critical reason for the critical event assigned to this driver is “Driver Related Factor: Inadequate Surveillance-failed to look, looked but did not see”.

The driver of V2 was a 40 year-old male whose pre-crash factors are unknown, as he was not interviewed.

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded “Other Motor Encroachment: from opposite direction - over left lane line”. The critical reason for the critical event assigned to this driver is “Not coded to this vehicle”.
