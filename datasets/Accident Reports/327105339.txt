This case involves a moderate to severe, centered, frontal-impact with a tree by a passenger car. The case occupant is the 18-year-old male driver of the passenger car. He was restrained by a three-point belt, and the steering-wheel air bag deployed. The case vehicle, a 2000 Ford Mustang convertible, was southbound in the curb lane of a dry, concrete, divided, four-lane road with a slight down-grade. It was dark but lighted and clear. The case vehicle struck a temporary spare tire in the roadway. The impact caused the driver to lose control of the vehicle as it entered a clockwise yaw. The case vehicle drove over the curb and struck a tree with its front end knocking it over. The case vehicle came to rest straddling the roadside and the curb lane facing southwest. The sole occupant was the18-year-old male driver. He was wearing a three-point belt and the steering-wheel air bag deployed. He was transported to a level-one trauma center and enrolled as a case occupant.
