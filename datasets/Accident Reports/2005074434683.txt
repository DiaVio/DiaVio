This single vehicle crash occurred shortly before noon. The roadway is a four-lane, divided, two-way, straight, and concrete roadway with a 7% vertical profile. No adverse weather conditions were present at the time of collision. Posted speed limit is 64 kmph (40 mph).

Vehicle one (V1) a 1994 Toyota Camry sedan driven by a 29 year-old female was traveling west in unknown lane when she experienced an epileptic seizure. V1 crossed the median across the eastbound lanes, mounted the south side curb hitting a chain link fence with the left front continuing west and striking a pole with the right front. V1 came to final rest at the pole impact. V1 was towed due to damage and the driver was transported to a local medical facility. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: this vehicle traveling, off the edge of the road on the left side. The critical reason for the critical event was coded as a driver related factor: heart attack or other physical impairment of the ability to act. Associative factors assigned are as follows: driver suffers from epilepsy and experienced a seizure while driving and could not remember how she arrived at the scene of the collision. The driver takes the medication Topomax for this condition. The driver expressed being under a lot of stress due to having difficulties at school, working several jobs to make ends meet, and having to take care of family members at home.
