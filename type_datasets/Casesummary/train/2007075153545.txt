This single-vehicle crash occurred on a one-way entrance ramp to a jersey barrier-divided, straight trafficway. This ramp is level, curves to right and consists of one lane. The speed limit for this roadway is 105 kmph (65 mph). There is a jersey barrier with a snow bank on the right side of the right shoulder. This crash occurred during daylight hours, it was raining and the road was covered with slush.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2001 Ford Escape, was eastbound on the entrance ramp. V1 rotated clockwise and departed the right side of the roadway. The front of V1 struck the jersey barrier and V1 rolled over to the left two quarter-turns. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 48 kmph for V1.
The driver of V1, a twenty-two-year-old male, was on his way home from work. He was healthy, well rested and familiar with his vehicle. He rarely drives this route. This driver stated that he tapped the brakes due to traffic on the main lanes and then lost control.
