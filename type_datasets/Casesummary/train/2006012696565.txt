V1, a 2005 Jeep Cherokee Laredo, was traveling northbound on a four-lane, one-way, dry asphalt roadway in daylight conditions. The posted speed limit was 40 kmph (25 mph). V1 was approaching an intersection attempting to go straight. V2, a 1993 Pontiac Bonneville, was traveling eastbound on a similar roadway, approaching the same intersection, also intending to go straight. The posted speed limit on the eastbound roadway was 48 kmph (30 mph). The driver of V1 thought that he had a green light and drove through the red light. The front of V1 contacted the right side of V2. V1 then struck a light pole with its front before coming to final rest off the roadway. V2 spun around clockwise and departed the roadway. V2 returned to the roadway where it came to final rest.
The driver of V1 was a 66-year old male who reported that he had been traveling between 50-64Kmph (31-40mph) prior to the crash. He had no health-related problems and had taken no medication prior to the crash. He was rested and traveling to the office. He was wearing his prescribed lenses that corrected a hyperopic (far-sighted) condition. He was uninjured and refused treatment. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V2 was a 22-year old male who reported that he had been traveling between 34-48Kmph (21-30mph) prior to the crash. He had no health-related problems and had taken no medication prior to the crash. He was rested and traveling home from school. He does not wear corrective lenses. He was uninjured and refused treatment.
