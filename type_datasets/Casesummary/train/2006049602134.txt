This two-vehicle crash occurred in a four-leg intersection. Both roadways are two-lane, undivided, straight and have a slight upgrade. The north/south roadway is controlled by stop signs and the speed limit is 40 kmph (25 mph). The speed limit for the east/west roadway is 56 kmph (35 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1995 Pontiac Bonneville, was southbound in lane one. Vehicle two (V2), a 2005 Ford Taurus, was westbound in lane one. In the intersection, the front of V1 struck the right side of V2. V2 traveled in a northwesterly direction and the front left wheel struck the south curb. V2 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, an unidentified male, drove away from the scene; no information is available for this driver.
The driver of V2, a thirty-two-year-old female, was traveling, with two female relatives, from visiting a relative in the hospital to their home. She was healthy, well rested and familiar with her vehicle. This driver rarely travels on this roadway.
