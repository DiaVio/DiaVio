This two-vehicle crash occurred in the morning daylight hours at a Tee intersection. The east/west roadway is a three-lane, two-way, undivided, level, straight, bituminous roadway. The north/south roadway intersection the east/west roadway from the south and is a two way, two lane undivided asphalt roadway. Northbound traffic is controlled by a stop sign. The posted speed limit on both roadways is 48 kmph (30 mph).
There were no adverse weather conditions. Police Reported Travel Speed is 16 kmph for V1 and 16 kmph for V2.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2005 Harley Davidson motorcycle with one male occupant, was headed north and was stopped for the stop sign. The driver initiated a left turn onto the cross street. Vehicle two (V2), a 1999 Pontiac Grand Am with two occupants, was westbound in lane one approaching the intersection. The front of V2 struck the left side of V1. V1 was knocked onto its side and moved east coming to final rest partially off the roadway in lane one of the westbound lanes facing southeast. V2 crossed the eastbound lane and departed the north road edge and stuck a fence. V2 came to final rest in the back yard of a private residence facing north. Both vehicles were towed due to disabling damage. The driver of V1 was transported.
The driver of V2, a 24 year-old female, was traveling home from a restaurant with a 25 year-old female passenger. The driver was arrested for DUI. The breath test reported a blood alcohol concentration of 0.11. The passenger stated that they had been out all night celebrating, had no sleep, and they were on their way home. The passenger stated that V1 came out of nowhere and they hit it. The driver drives the route daily but this was the first time she had driven the vehicle. The driver did not possess a valid driver's license; it had been suspended or revoked for an unknown reason.
