This single-vehicle crash occurred just past noon during daylight hours. The location was an interstate highway. The roadway was a concrete five lane level roadway in the area just past an off-ramp. The roadway was straight and level. The weather was clear and dry. The posted speed limit was 97 kmph (60 mph).
Vehicle one (V1), a 1999 Ford Explorer 4 door SUV driven by a twenty one (21) year old female with one passenger, was traveling northbound in lane one attempting to exit just past the off ramp. V1 drove into a raised berm of the adjacent roadway on the right shoulder . After impacting the berm, the vehicle returned to the roadway and began to rotate counter-clockwise. V1 subsequently rolled to the right five-quarter turns. V1 came to rest on the right side facing southwest straddling lane one and two. The driver and passenger of the vehicle were both pregnant, and were transported to the hospital. The vehicle was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.
A partial interview with the driver was obtained. After the driver departed the roadway onto the paved shoulder & impacted the 15 centimeter (6") tall berm of the adjacent roadway. she steered to the left in an attempt to return to the travel lanes and subsequently rolled the vehicle over.
