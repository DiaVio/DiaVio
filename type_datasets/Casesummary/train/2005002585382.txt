The crash occurred on a two lane undivided roadway with a posted speed limit of 55 MPH (89 KPH). The roadway was straight with a grade of -2.3%. There was a black on yellow “Y” intersection warning sign for both vehicles. The weather was clear, the roadway dry and it was daylight at the time of this weekday afternoon crash.
Vehicle #1, a 1997 Plymouth Neon, was traveling north on the two lane roadway approaching the intersection. Vehicle #2, a 1987 Ford F-350 stake body, was stopped facing north waiting to make a left hand turn. Vehicle #1 struck Vehicle #2 in rear with front. Both vehicles came to rest facing north. Vehicle #1 was towed due to damage. Police did not consider either alcohol or illegal drugs to be involved and ordered no tests. Police Reported Travel Speed is Unknown for V1 and Unknown for V2.
The Plymouth Neon (Vehicle #1) was driven by a belted 76 year old female who was transported, treated and released for minor injuries. Both airbags deployed. She stated to police that she did not realize that Vehicle #2 was stopped. She attempted to brake, but not in time to avoid the collision. There was no interview obtained and no further information available.
The Ford F350 stake body (Vehicle #2) was driven by a belted 40 year old male who was treated at a local hospital the following day for shoulder pain. The belted 18 year old male passenger complained of pain, but declined medical attention. The driver stated that he was stopped with his directional on for approximately 1 minute waiting for traffic to clear prior to making a left hand turn. He advised that he caught the movement of Vehicle #1 just prior to impact in his side mirror, but was unable to take any avoidance maneuvers. He also stated that he was not in a hurry, had just finished lunch and was going to look at a vehicle with his passenger. He does not wear corrective lenses and does not take any type of medications.
