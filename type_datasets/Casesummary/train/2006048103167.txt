This single-vehicle crash occurred in the mid-afternoon a clear day. The crash occurred on a two-lane, two-way, dry, level, bituminous, roadway that curves sharply to the right for westbound travel. There were curve-warning chevrons on the left shoulder of the road and the posted speed limit was 72 kmph (45 mph). The radius of this curve is 87 meters (285 feet), with a super elevation of +7% (9/122 cm). There were no adverse atmospheric conditions present at the time of the crash.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1991 Buick Century was traveling westbound in lane one. As V1 was negotiating the curve, it departed lane one to the left. V1 then departed the roadway to the left. V1 continued off of the road for approximately 25 meters (82 feet) before the front of V1 struck a cement bridge. Post-impact, V1 rotated counterclockwise coming to rest facing westbound in lane two. V1 contained one occupant who was pronounced dead at the scene and V1 was towed due to disabling damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 80 kmph for V1.
The driver of V1 was a 73-year-old male. The driver was a retired commercial vehicle driver. The driver also had severe diabetes and was legally blind. A surrogate for the driver advised that the driver of V1 had been without his insulin for the 2-3 days prior to the crash.
