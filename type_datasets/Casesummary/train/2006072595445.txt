This single vehicle crash occurred on an eight-lane roadway. This road has eight-lane with four in each direction before the intersection and six lane of travel after the intersection. This intersection is controlled by a traffic signal, which was functioning properly at the time of the crash. The roadway was straight, level, and had no visible deficiencies. The speed limit for this roadway is 56 kmph (35 mph). This crash occurred during nighttime hours with additional lighting and no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one, a 1988 Ford E150 Econoline 4x2 Regular Cargo van was traveling eastbound in lane one prior to the intersection. Vehicle one departed the right side of the roadway on the other side of the intersection. The driver steered back to the left and impacted a fire hydrant with its front plane. Vehicle one reentered the roadway and then steered back to the right. Vehicle one then departed the roadway again and impacted a large pole with its front plane. Vehicle one rotated clockwise and rolled over two quarter turns. As vehicle one came to rest, its back plane impacted a park bench. Vehicle one came to rest on its roof off the roadway to the right. Vehicle one was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is No PAR obtained (created) for V1.
The driver of vehicle one is a male with an unknown age. There was no PAR obtained for this crash and no interview for this driver. There is no known pre-crash information for this driver.
