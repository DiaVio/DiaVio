This two-vehicle collision occurred just before noon It was sunny and warm at the time. The collision occurred at a four-leg intersection. The north/south roadway has five lanes with left-turn pockets. The east/west roadway has five lanes with left turn pockets. Both roadways are dry, level and made of asphalt. Both roadways are designated 56 kmph (35 mph). Both roadways are undivided. The intersection is controlled by a on-colors signal, which was functioning properly at the time.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1997 Jaguar XK8 2-door convertible driven by a twenty five (25) year old female, was traveling north in the number three left-turn lane. Vehicle two (V2) is a 2002 Volkswagen Golf GLS, 4-door sedan driven by a fifty four (54) year old male who was traveling south in the number two lane. As V1 made a left turn its front plane was impacted by V2's front plane. V1 rotated counter clockwise and was pushed in a southerly direction. V1 came to rest facing south just south of the intersection. V2 came to rest facing southwest in the intersection. V1 was towed with minor damage. V2 was towed with moderate damage. The damage for V1 was actually moderate or major. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
