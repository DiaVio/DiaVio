The crash occurred in the newly painted bicycle eastbound lane of a two-lane two-way roadway that is not physically divided. There was one travel lane, one bicycle lane, and a parking lane in each direction of travel. The roadway was straight and had an uphill grade measurement of +1/61. It was daylight and cloudy with rain. The speed limit was 48kmph (30mph).
V1 was a 2006 Honda Passport that was attempting to merge from the parking/bicycle lanes into the eastbound travel lane. V2 was a 1989 Chevrolet G-Series passenger Van that was in the eastbound travel/bicycle lanes. The frontal plane of V2 impacted the rear plane of V1, and both vehicles came to a final rest shortly after. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
V1, the Honda Passport, was driven by a 40 year-old male who did not sustain any injuries. He stated that he was driving normally and ready to merge into the travel lane when he was impacted by V2 from the rear. The Honda was not towed due to damage.
V2, the Chevrolet Van, was driven by a 43 year-old male who did not sustain any injuries. He stated that he tried to brake however he was unable to stop in time to avoid striking V1 due to a brake problem.
