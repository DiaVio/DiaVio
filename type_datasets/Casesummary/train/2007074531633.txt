This two-vehicle crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions. In the precrash area, the east/west trafficway is straight, divided by a curbed median and consists of two lanes in each direction. In the westbound direction, the roadway has a downhill grade. At the crash site, this trafficway becomes a five-lane roadway divided by a center left-turn lane. The speed limit for this roadway is 64 kmph (40 mph).
Vehicle one (V1), a 1999 Chevrolet S10 Blazer, was westbound in lane two. Vehicle two (V2), a 1993 Ford Ranger, entered lane one from a driveway, traveling westbound. A non-contact vehicle (NCV) was westbound in lane one behind V2. V2 moved into lane two in front of V1 and then stopped at the end of the curbed median to make a u-turn. The front of V1 struck the back of V2. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, a twenty-year-old female, had just taken her son to a daycare center and was on her way to work. She was healthy, well rested and familiar with her vehicle. She drives this route daily. This driver stated that she did not see V2 prior to the crash.
The driver of V2, a forty-six-year-old female, was traveling with her ten-year-old daughter and the daughter's ten-year-old female friend. They were on their way from a store to a parent-teacher conference. This driver was well rested, healthy and comfortable with her vehicle and passengers. She travels this route weekly. This driver stated that she did not see V1 prior to the crash.
The vision restriction created by the NCV was an associated factor for both drivers in this crash.
