The crash occurred on the shoulder of a northbound, residential roadway. The roadway had travel lanes in the north and the southbound directions with parking shoulders on each side. The posted speed limit was 56 kmph (35 mph) and the northbound approach was on a 1 % downhill grade. Conditions were daylight, wet, cloudy, and raining. The setting was mid-afternoon on a weekend.
Vehicle one was a 2001 Buick LeSabre sedan traveling north in lane one. Vehicle two was a 2007 Ford Escape sport utility parked facing north on the right shoulder. V1 traveled to the right out of the travel lane and impacted the back of v2 with its front plane. V2 was then pushed onto the curb where it struck a roadway sign and a couch. V2 came to rest on top of the couch. V1 came to rest in the roadway facing northeast. Police Reported Travel Speed is 72 kmph for V1 and 72 kmph for V2.
The Buick LeSabre (v1) was driven by a 37-year old female who was transported to a local trauma center with non-incapacitating injuries. Her two children who were unrestrained in the back seat were also transported. She was taken to jail after being treated for her injuries. She was located later and stated that she was turned around speaking to her kids just before the crash. She said the parked vehicle was in a location where vehicle's usually don't park. She estimated her travel and impact speeds to be 48 kmph (30 mph). She didn't have a chance to react in avoidance. The investigating officer estimated her travel and impact speeds to be 72 kmph (45 mph). The Buick sedan was towed due to damage.
The Ford Escape (v2) was unoccupied and legally parked. No Critical Precrash Event or Reason were coded for v2.
