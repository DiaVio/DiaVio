This single vehicle crash occurred in a rural area on a three-lane asphalt roadway. The eastbound uphill roadway curved left, straightened out for 100 meters, and then curved right. The speed limit for the area is 104 kmph (65 mph). The crash occurred during darkness with no lighting present. The weather was clear and there were no adverse road conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1996 Chevrolet Corsica 4-door sedan, was eastbound in the number one lane when the driver fell asleep and gradually crossed the number two eastbound lane, the westbound lane and departed the westbound road edge. The front struck a metal post on the westbound shoulder, three bushes, then the vehicle traveled down a short, steep embankment. The front struck the bottom of the embankment. V1 then rolled end-over-end onto its top where it came to rest facing south. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 105 kmph for V1.
The twenty-three-year-old, female driver had started the trip about an hour after midnight and had traveled 161 kilometers (100 miles) when the crash occurred. The crash location was not discovered until over four hours after it had occurred that same morning. The driver stated she normally feels drowsy, and had just 2.5 hours of sleep in the 24 hours preceding the crash. She had been awake for 21.5 hours when the crash occurred. She fell asleep while driving. The driver was transported by helicopter for incapacitating injuries.
