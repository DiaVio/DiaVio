This two-vehicle collision occurred on a westbound two-way, five-lane road. The pre-crash roadway for both vehicles has a -6% downhill grade and wet bituminous pavement. The roadway has a speed limit of 48 kph (30mph). This crash occurred during evening hours with additional lighting and a light rain.
Vehicle one, a 1984 Volvo 240 sedan with passenger was westbound in the second lane. Vehicle two, a 2005 BMW X3 SUV was stopped at the red light before the intersection in the second lane. Vehicle one did not come to a stop, and struck the rear of vehicle two, pushing it into the intersection. Vehicle one was towed due to damage and vehicle two was driven from scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of vehicle one, a twenty-four year old female stated she was changing lanes and was looking in her blind spot to make sure it was clear. She never saw vehicle two until she hit it. This driver had just picked up the passenger at a bar. This passenger had called this driver because she was having some trouble at the bar. This driver had been driving for about seven minutes at the time of the crash. The driver of this vehicle was not taking any medications. This driver had a blood alcohol level of 0.17. This driver drives this vehicle daily and over this road on a weekly basis. This driver indicated that the passenger windshield wiper does not work on this vehicle.
The driver of vehicle two, a thirty-four year old female was heading home from work. This driver had been driving for about twelve minutes at the time of the crash. This driver stated that she did not see vehicle one. This driver was taking Levoxyl as a medication. This driver drives this vehicle daily and over this road on a daily basis.
