A three-vehicle rear-end collision occurred in the evening daylight hours on a Thursday. The collision occurred on an interstate highway that was congested at the time. The roadway is a divided highway with four travel lanes in the northbound direction. The pre-crash roadway is straight and a -3% downhill grade, with bituminous paving. The weather was clear and the roadway was dry. The posted speed limit for the roadway is 97 kmph (60 mph). Access to the roadway is controlled.
Vehicle three (V3), a 1997 Toyota Avalon with one occupant, was stopped in lane three because of congested traffic ahead. Vehicle two (V2), a 2001 Mazda Protégé with one occupant, was behind V3 decelerating to a stop. Vehicle one (V1), a 1992 Lexus LS400 with one occupant, was also traveling in lane three behind V2. The driver of V1 failed to stop in time and the front of V1 struck the rear of V2. V2 was propelled forward where the front of V2 under rode the frame of V3 as the vehicles came to rest together. V2 and V3 were towed from the scene. The driver of V2 was transported. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1 is a 36 year-old male whose is reportedly in good health with no hearing or sight restrictions, he is in overall good health and does not take any medications. He was traveling home from his friend's house, and had been on the road for 40 minutes. He is very comfortable with his vehicle, and familiar with the roadway.
The driver of V2 is a 33 year-old male whose pre-crash factors are unknown as he was unable to be reached for interview.
The driver of V3 is a 36 year-old male whose pre-crash factors are unknown as he was unable to be reached for interview.
