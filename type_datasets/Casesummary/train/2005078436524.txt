This is single vehicle end departure crash that occurred during morning daylight hours on an urban intersection. There are no centerline stripes to divide the travel lines on both roadways. Road surface dry asphalt. Traffic approaching the intersection from the west are controlled by a stop sign. There were no environmental factors that contributed to the crash. The speed limit on both roadways is posted 40 kmph (25 MPH).
Vehicle one (V1), a 1997 GMC 1/2 ton pickup driven by a 32 year-old male, was traveling eastbound on a roadway has a posted stop sign prior to entering the intersection. V1, was intending on turning right when it stopped and then proceeded through the intersection and struck a fence on the east side of the north/south roadway. The vehicle then struck a tree that was to the east of the fence in a residential yard. The driver of V1, stated that he left his residence at about 15 -20 minutes prior to the crash and was going to drop some mail off at the post office and then get his vehicle's alignment checked. The driver stated that he thought he stopped at the post office but realized that he had not stopped because the mail was still in his vehicle. The driver stated that he does not recall some events of the crash. The driver stated that he does remember stopping for the stop sign but does not remember what happened after he stopped. The driver also stated that he has a history of having seizures and has been diagnosed with spinal meningitis as a child. The driver stated that he has never had a seizure while he is driving. A surrogate interview was conducted on the driver and the surrogate statements confirmed that the driver has a history of seizures. The driver takes the medication Zonegran, an anticonvulsant medication prescribed to prevent seizures. During the time on scene, the driver became more coherent. V1 was towed from the scene there were no reported injuries. Police Reported Travel Speed is 16 kmph for V1.
