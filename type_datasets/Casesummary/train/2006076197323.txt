This two-vehicle crash occurred in an urban area on a state highway consisting of two through lanes in each direction and a 2-way center turn lane. The highway is asphalt, straight and level, has full access, and a posted speed limit of 72 kmph (45 mph). At the time of this early morning crash there were no adverse atmospheric conditions or road defects.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1995 Toyota Camry, was exiting a private drive located to the north of the highway, intending to turn left. As V1 entered the highway, the front of V1 struck the right side of V2. The V1 driver, an 18 year old female, could not be reached for an interview. V1 was towed due to damage, but the V1 driver was not transported for treatment. The driver of V1 stated to the investigating officer, that she intended to turn left out of the driveway and never saw V2 approaching. Based on the time & location of this crash, sun-glare was considered a factor for this driver. Police Reported Travel Speed is 16 kmph for V1 and 16 kmph for V2.
Vehicle two (V2), a 1993 Jeep Grand Cherokee, was westbound in lane one intending to go straight. The V2 driver, an 18 year old female, that as she noticed V1 begin to enter the road into her path, she attempted to avoid the collision by steering left, but was not successful, and the right side of V2 was struck by the front of V1 and V2 was rotated clockwise, then tripped and rolled over one quarter-turn onto the driver's side. The V2 driver stated that she was commuting from her residence to school and was familiar with the road. V2 was towed due to damage, but the V2 driver was not transported for treatment.
V1 and V2 could not be located for an inspection.
