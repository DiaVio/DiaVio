This single-vehicle crash occurred during early evening hours, just after the sun set. The crash happened on a divided, five-lane, level, bituminous roadway on a right curve. There are three lanes for eastbound traffic, two through lanes and one left turn lane. There are two through lanes for westbound traffic. There are black and yellow curve warning arrows that line the median for eastbound traffic. The speed limit is 64 kph (40 mph) and there were no adverse atmospheric conditions present at the time.
Vehicle one, a 1996 Toyota Avalon sedan, driven by an eighteen-year-old male with three passengers, was traveling eastbound in lane two, traveling at a high rate of speed. The driver lost control of the vehicle. It traveled to the left and then back to the right. The driver then over-corrected and the vehicle began to rotate counterclockwise. The vehicle crossed all lanes of eastbound traffic and the front right tire struck a curbed median. It continued to rotate counterclockwise and the right rear wheel struck the same median and the vehicle rode up onto the median. The vehicle rolled to the right, two quarter turns. During the rollover, the back of the vehicle struck a tree on the median. Vehicle one came to rest on its roof facing northwest in lane two of westbound traffic. Vehicle one was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 113 kmph for V1.
The driver was not interviewed. No further information is available for this driver
