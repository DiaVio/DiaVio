This three-vehicle collision occurred during daylight hours on a cloudy weekend morning. The crash occurred on a straight, level stretch of a five-lane, two-way, bituminous north-south roadway, in the area of an intersection with an east-west roadway. The intersection is controlled by a tri-light traffic signal, and the speed limit is 56 kmph (35 mph). There was heavily congested traffic in the area of the intersection. At the time of the crash, there were no adverse weather conditions, and the roadway was free of defects.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2000 Toyota Sienna minivan, was traveling southbound in the second lane, traversing the intersection on a green light. Vehicle two (V2), a 1999 Chrysler LHS 4-door sedan, was ahead of V1 in the same lane. Vehicle three (V3), a 2001 BMW X5 compact SUV, was ahead of V2 in the same lane. V3 came to a sudden stop due to congested traffic. V2 came to a stop behind V3. Just past the intersection, the front of V1 impacted the back of V2. V2 moved ahead and impacted the back of V3. All three vehicles came to rest in their original lane. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The 37-year-old female driver of V3 reported that she was able to come to a complete stop when traffic suddenly stopped in front of her. She looked in the rear-view mirror and saw V2 come to a complete stop before the impacts occurred. V3 was not involved in the first harmful event.
