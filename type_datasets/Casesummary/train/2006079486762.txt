A two vehicle crash occurred shortly past noon during daylight hours. The crash occurred near an on ramp of a five lane divided, dry, level, highway comprised of concrete with an asphalt shoulder. The roadway is designated 105 kmph (65 mph). The highway curves to the right. The on ramp starts as two lanes then merges to one. The on ramp is made of asphalt and has a downhill grade. A gore point with a raised asphalt curb separates the highway and the on ramp. In the gore point area there are some small bushes and grass. The right side of the on ramp also has a raised curb.
Vehicle one (V1) a 1994 Toyota Camry sedan driven by a 61 year-old male with three passengers was traveling south in the number two lane. Vehicle two (V2) a 2002 Mercedes E 320 sedan driven by a 42 year-old female with one passenger was traveling south in the number two lane of the on ramp. V1 lost control and began to rotate clockwise. V1 then impacted the right side curb with its left side as it departed the roadway. V1 then began to roll left V1 landed on its roof as its front plane impacted V2's left mirror. V1 came to rest on its roof facing west. V2 came to rest on the right shoulder facing southwest. V1 was towed from the scene, V2 left scene under own power. Police Reported Travel Speed is 105 kmph for V1 and 105 kmph for V2.
The driver of V1 had just left a fast food restaurant after a 3-hour drive. He has an ulcer and high blood pressure. He takes Tagament (cimetidine), Vonnatal, Atenolol, and herb supplements. He last took his medication on Thursday. He has an astigmatism and was wearing his glasses at the time of the crash. He has a restriction on his license for corrective lenses.
V2 was not involved in first harmful event, and the critical reason was not assigned to this vehicle. The driver of V2 indicated that prior to the crash she was singing church songs with her daughter.
