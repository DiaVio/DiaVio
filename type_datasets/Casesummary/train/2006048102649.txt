This three-vehicle crash occurred on a divided, straight, level roadway on the approach to a traffic signal-controlled intersection. The eastbound lanes in the precrash area consist of two through lanes, a left turn lane and a right turn lane. The speed limit on this roadway is 72 kmph (45 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with rush hour congestion and no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2002 Ford F150 pickup, was eastbound in lane three. Vehicle two (V2), a 1996 Cadillac Deville, and vehicle three (V3), a 2004 Chevrolet Avalanche, were in front of V1 and stopped for the traffic signal. The front of V1 struck the back of V2. V2 was pushed forward and the front struck the back of V3. V2 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 64 kmph for V1 and 64 kmph for V2.
The driver of V1, a forty-two-year-old male, was traveling with a female passenger, from a store to a bank. He was healthy, well rested and familiar with his vehicle. He drives this roadway weekly. This driver stated that he just didn't notice that V2 was stopped.
The driver of V2, a sixty-seven-year-old female, was traveling from work to home. She was well rested, healthy and comfortable with her vehicle. This driver drives this roadway daily.
The driver of V3, a thirty-three-year-old male, was not interviewed. No information is available for this driver.
