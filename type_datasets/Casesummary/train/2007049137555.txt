The two-vehicle rear-end crash occurred on a two-lane, one-way, westbound roadway at the intersection with a seven-lane, divided, north/south roadway. The roadway was straight, level, wet with an asphalt surface. Overhead traffic control lights control traffic at the intersection. The posted speed limit is 56 kph (35 mph). Traffic was light at the time of the crash. This crash occurred during morning daylight hours with a light rain falling.
Vehicle two (V2), a 2005 Chevrolet Colorado, with four occupants, was stopped in lane one facing west, waiting for the traffic signal to turn green for his direction of travel. Vehicle one (V1), a 2007 Ford Taurus, with one occupant, was westbound in the first travel lane, approaching the intersection. The front of V1 struck the back of V2. V1 and V2 came to rest in lane one facing west. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, an eighteen year-old male, was heading home from taking his mother to work. This driver had been driving for about ten minutes and drives the route weekly. He had rented the vehicle earlier that day and claimed to be comfortable with the vehicle. He was listening to the radio at the time. The driver stated he applied the brakes and steered to the right in an attempt to avoid the crash. The police report indicated that this driver was not attentive to the driving task.
The driver of V2, a thirty-eight year old male, was heading from home to a friend's residence. This driver had been driving for about ten minutes, drives the route weekly and is familiar with the vehicle. The driver stated he had stopped at the red light and when he looked into his rearview mirror he saw V1. He did not attempt any evasive maneuvers.
