This single-vehicle crash occurred during morning daylight hours on a weekend. The crash occurred on a straight stretch of a five-lane dry, level, bituminous, positively divided expressway. At the time of the crash, there were no adverse atmospheric conditions, and the roadway was free of defects.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2003 GMC Yukon, was traveling northbound in the second lane of the freeway. A non-contact vehicle was traveling abreast of V1 in the third lane. The non-contact vehicle moved right and began to enter the second lane. The driver of V1 engaged the brakes and steered hard left as the non-contact vehicle moved in front of V1. V1 lost traction and moved left in a counter-clockwise yaw. V1 departed the road to the left side, where the front of V1 impacted the concrete traffic barrier. V1 demolished a section of the barrier and rode up onto the barrier before coming to rest. The frontal air bags deployed at impact. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.
Data recovered from the vehicle shows the vehicle was traveling 82 mph several seconds prior to impact. The 44-year-old female driver of V1 was in good health and alert at the time of the collision. She was traveling with two passengers, but reports no conversation or other source of distraction. The vehicle was traveling in excess of the posted speed limit prior to the critical event. V1's tires were found to be slightly under-inflated.
