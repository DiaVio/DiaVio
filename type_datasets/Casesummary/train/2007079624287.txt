This two vehicle daytime collision occurred at a four-leg intersection comprised of a north/south roadway and an east/west roadway. The north/south roadway is undivided, dry, level, asphalt, and consists of five lanes. The east/west roadway is divided, dry, level, and asphalt. The east leg has four westbound lanes (including a left-turn pocket) and three eastbound lanes that are separated by a raised concrete island. The west leg has three westbound lanes and four eastbound lanes (including a left-turn pocket) that are separated by a raised concrete island. Both roadway are designated 56 kmph (35 mph) zones. The intersection is controlled by on-color signals that were working properly at the time of the collision. The crash occurred during the morning rush hour in daylight hours.
Vehicle one (V1) is 2000 Dodge Dakota pickup truck, driven by a forty nine (49) year old male who was traveling westbound in the number one lane. Vehicle two (V2) is a 1997 Ford Explorer SUV driven by a forty (40) year old male who was traveling eastbound in the number four (left-turn) lane. As V2 turned left at the intersection, the front plane of V1 contacted its right plane. V2 then rolled unto its left plane. V1 came to rest facing northbound blocking the southbound number three lane. V2 came to rest facing northbound on its left plane. V1 received major damage and was towed from the scene. V2 received major (rollover) damage and was towed from the scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
There was a partial sight line restriction for both drivers that was crated by the vehicle in Westbound lanes 2 & 3 that were stopped at the red light.
