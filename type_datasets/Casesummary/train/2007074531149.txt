This two-vehicle crash occurred in a four-legged intersection. The north/south roadway is undivided, level and straight and consists of one lane in each direction with a left turn lane at the intersection. The speed limit for this roadway is 72 kmph (45 mph). The east/west roadway is undivided, level, consists of one lane in each direction and is controlled by stop signs. The speed limit for this roadway is 40 kmph (25 mph). It was dark with streetlights on at the time of the crash with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2001 Ford Ranger, was southbound in lane one. Vehicle two (V2), a 2001 Oldsmobile Alero, was stopped eastbound in lane one. V2 entered the intersection and the left side was struck by the front of V1. V2 rotated counterclockwise, traveled to the southeast and the back right wheel struck the curb. V2 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, a twenty-two-year-old male, had picked up two male friends and they were on their way to a nearby bar. This driver was healthy, well rested and comfortable with his vehicle and passenger. He travels this route daily. This driver stated that he saw V2 when he was fifty feet from the intersection and assumed that V2 would wait until he passed. When V2 entered the intersection, the driver of V1 applied the brakes and steered to the left (moved into lane two) in an attempt to avoid the crash.
The driver of V2, a sixteen-year-old male, had been at a party and was on the way to take his fourteen-year-old male passenger home. This driver was in good health. He stated that he was tired and this was his first time traveling this route. He had two months of driving experience at the time of the crash. This driver stated that he saw V1 when it was fifty feet from the intersection and thought he had time to safely cross the intersection. He accelerated in an attempt to avoid the crash.
