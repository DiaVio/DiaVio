The crash occurred in the westbound lane of a two-lane east/westbound roadway. The roadway was bituminous with a 5% uphill grade and a posted speed limit of 64 kph (40 mph). It was afternoon on a weekday. The road was dry. Traffic flow was moderate due to nearby police activity.
V1, a 2000 Dodge Dakota with two occupants, was traveling west when the driver glanced in his rearview mirror to look at a police motor vehicle stop (driver is a part time police office). The driver looked forward just before impact with V2. V2, a 2006 Toyota Avalon with one occupant was stopped in traffic waiting to make a left turn when it was struck from behind by V1. No avoidance maneuvers were attempted Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
V1 was driven by a 20-year-old male who reported a complaint of pain. V1 was also occupied by an 18-year-old male who reported a complaint of pain, but was transported to the hospital. The driver (a part time police office) stated that he was coming home from work when he passed a police motor stop and as he was looking at the stop in his rearview mirror he hit V2. V1 made no avoidance maneuver.
V2 was driven by a 49-year-old female who reported a complaint of pain. Driver stated that she was stopped in traffic waiting to make a left turn when she was struck from behind by V1. Driver made no avoidance maneuver.
