Case Summary 2006-05-10
This midday two-vehicle crash occurred on a two-way, two-lane roadway, which was not physically divided. The roadway was dry, and there were no adverse atmospheric conditions. The gradient for both vehicles involved was determined to be level. No traffic control devices were present at the crash location. The posted speed limit for the roadway was 72 kmph (45 mph). Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
Vehicle one (V1), a blue 1997 Oldsmobile Cutlass, was traveling north on the roadway intending to continue straight. Vehicle two (V2), a yellow 2002 Chevrolet School Bus was heading south on the roadway, also intending to continue straight. Vehicle one crossed full double barrier lines, entering the path of V2. The front left of V1 made contact with the front left of V2. The impact caused vehicle one to rotate counter clockwise approximately 110 degrees before coming to final rest on the roadway facing southwest. Vehicle two rotated counter clockwise approximately 90 degrees before coming to final rest off of the roadway facing east. As V2 left the roadway, the right side of the vehicle made contact with a utility pole where it came to final rest. Both vehicles were towed from the scene due to damage.
