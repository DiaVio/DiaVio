It was dark with streetlights on with no adverse atmospheric conditions at the time of this single-vehicle crash. The roadway is straight, level, undivided, consists of two lanes in each direction and was ice-covered at the time of the crash. The speed limit for this roadway is 48 kmph (30 mph).
Vehicle one (V1), a 1991 Toyota Camry, was turning right, leaving a carwash on the west side of the roadway. The accelerator stuck and the driver was unable to apply the brakes so he steered to the right. V1 slid off the right side of the road and the right rear wheel struck the curb. V1 continued to the southwest and the front struck a sign post with a concrete base. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.
The driver of V1, a seventy-nine-year-old male, was on his way home from the car wash. He was familiar with his vehicle and travels this route daily. This driver stated that he had five hours of sleep (rather than his normal six hours). He has untreated sleep apnea and stated that he felt fatigued when he woke up. This driver has chronic obstructive pulmonary disease and is on oxygen and takes the following medications: Advair, Coumadin, Xopenex, Spiriva, Lasix, Centrum multi-vitamins, Mirapex, Cardizem, Propafenone, and Potassium. This driver stated that when the accelerator stuck, he was not able to apply the brakes in time to avoid a crash on the roadway so he steered to the right.
