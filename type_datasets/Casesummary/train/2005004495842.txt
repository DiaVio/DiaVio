The crash occurred in the eastbound lane of a three-lane intersection controlled by a traffic control signal. Conditions were daylight and dry in the early evening on a weekday. The posted speed limit was 45 MPH.
V1 was a 1997 Plymouth Seabreeze sedan turning right at the intersection controlled by a traffic control signal. V2, a 2005 Ford Focus hatchback, was stopped at the red traffic control signal in the 2nd travel lane facing eastbound. V3, a 2002 Chevy Express Van, was also stopped at the red traffic control signal in the 1st traffic lane facing eastbound. The front of V1 struck the left side of V2. V2 was then pushed into V3. The right side of V2 struck the left side of V3 in the eastbound lane. V1 came to final rest facing westbound. V2 and V3 came to final rest facing southeast. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
V1 was driven by a 44-year-old male who sustained moderate injuries and was transported to a local hospital. He stated that he was talking to the passenger, when he realized his brakes were not working properly. He made a wide right hand turn and struck V2. Prior to the impact the driver of V1 applied his foot brakes and hand brakes but both were not working properly. V1 was traveling between 31-40 MPH at the time of impact. V1 was driven away from the scene.
V2 was driven by a 33-year-old female who was not injured. She stated that she was stopped at the red traffic control signal when she saw V1 heading towards her left side. The driver of V1 did not take any avoidance actions. V2 was towed due to damage.
V3 was driven by a 48-year-old female who sustained minor injuries but was not transported. She stated that she was stopped at the red traffic control signal when she saw V2 come into her lane and strike her vehicle. The driver of V3 did not take any avoidance actions. V3 was driven away from the scene.
No critical precrash event was coded for V3 because this vehicle was not involved in the first harmful event. Therefore, no critical reason was coded to this driver.
