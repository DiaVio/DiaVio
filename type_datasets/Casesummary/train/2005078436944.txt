This is a two-vehicle rear-end configuration crash that occurred in the eastbound # 2 lane of a two-way, four-lane, straight, level, dry, asphalt roadway. The roadway has two westbound and two eastbound lanes. The lanes are separated by painted centerline stripe. The crash was intersection related. The intersection where the crash occurred is controlled by stop signs to enter the west/east roadway. The speed limit on the roadway is posted 52 kmph (35 mph). There were no environmental factors and no sight line restrictions that contributed to this crash. It was dark at the time of the crash, but the area was illuminated by street lights.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2003 Nissan Sentra was traveling eastbound in the second lane. Vehicle two (V2), a 1997 Dodge pickup was traveling eastbound in the #2 lane and stopped to make a left turn at the intersection. The front of V1 impacted the rear of V2. Both vehicles came to rest in the area of impact. V1 was not towed and left the scene before police arrived. V2 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 32 kmph for V1 and 32 kmph for V2.
The driver of V1, a 56-year-old male, stated that V2 stopped suddenly and that he applied his brakes and his vehicle skidded for about 9 meters (30 feet) before it struck the rear of V2. This driver has no health related issues, does not work and received plenty of sleep the previous night. This driver drives this vehicle daily and over this road on a daily basis.
There is no known information about the driver of V2 or any occupants.
