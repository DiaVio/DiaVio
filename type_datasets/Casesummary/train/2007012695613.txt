The crash occurred at an intersection on a 5-lane undivided roadway. The intersection was controlled by a traffic signal. There were two eastbound lanes and two westbound lanes divided by a left-turn lane. This was a straight, bituminous roadway with a level grade and a posted speed of 72 kmph (45 mph). It was early evening on a weekday and the road was dry and the sky was clear. Traffic was flowing.
V1, a 1998 Volkswagen Beetle two door coupe with one occupant, was traveling eastbound in lane two. V1 was approaching the intersection intending to go straight. V2, a 2004 Chevrolet Malibu four door sedan with one occupant, was traveling westbound in lane three. V2 was approaching the intersection intending to turn left. The driver of V1 stated that she saw V2 as she approached the intersection and thought he was going to stop. She stated that she saw him slow down and then accelerate at the last minute. She stated that she did not have time to attempt any avoidance actions. The driver of V2 stated that he approached the intersection and did not see V1 so he initiated the turn. He did not attempt any avoidance actions. The front of V1 contacted the right of V2. Both vehicles came to final rest in the intersection. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
V1, the Volkswagen Beetle, was driven by a 56-year old female. She reported minor injuries and was treated on scene. She stated that she had just left the store and was on her way home. She stated that she had been traveling between 67-80 kmph (31-40 mph) when she contacted V2.
V2, the Chevrolet Malibu, was driven by a 39-year old male. He reported no injuries and refused treatment. He stated that he had just left home and was heading to the gas station. He stated that he had decelerated to between 2-16 kmph (1-10 mph) prior to being struck.
