A two-vehicle crash occurred at the intersection of a 6-lane asphalt roadway and a 4-lane asphalt roadway with left-turn only lanes. The westbound 6-lane roadway is straight and downhill. The eastbound lanes are straight and uphill. The intersection is controlled by a traffic control lights for all travel directions. The eastbound and westbound left-turn lanes are controlled by a left-turn green arrow that becomes a solid green, indicating left turns are to yield to right-of-way traffic. The speed limit for westbound traffic is 72 kmph (45 mph) and 64 kmph (40 mph) for eastbound traffic. The crash occurred at dawn with no adverse weather or road conditions present.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1990 black Ford Ranger pickup with a camper shell, was eastbound facing the sunrise in the left-turn lane preparing to turn north behind another vehicle. The green left-turn arrow had changed to solid green and V1 turned left immediately after the other vehicle turned and was impacted on the right side by Vehicle two (V2). V1 rotated counter-clockwise and was pushed into a guardrail in the northwest corner of the intersection and came to rest. V2, a 1993 dark blue Chevrolet S-10 Blazer, was westbound in the #3 lane approaching the intersection with a green light. V2 entered the intersection and its front-end plane impacted the right side plane of V1. V2 rotated clockwise and came to rest in the middle of the intersection facing east. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
