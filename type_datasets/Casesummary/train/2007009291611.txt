The crash occurred in the southbound lanes of a four-lane roadway with a 10-meter wide dirt and grass strip dividing the two southbound lanes from the two northbound lanes. This was a well-traveled, straight, level, bituminous road with a traffic signal 48 meters south of the crash site. The skies were clear on this weekday at dusk. Traffic was heavy during this commuting period. The posted speed limit was 89 kmph (55 mph).
V1, a 2000 Toyota Corolla sedan, was driven by a 37 year-old female traveling with her eleven year-old daughter. The driver stated that she had just picked up some food from a drive-thru one mile north and was on her way to drop off her daughter when the crash occurred. She had been traveling in lane two, traveling at a rate of about 64 kmph (40 mph). She was traveling behind V2 when V2 suddenly braked, coming to an abrupt halt. She braked unsuccessfully, crashing into the rear of V2 with the front of V1. She had no idea at the time why V2 would suddenly stop; her view of upcoming events was completely blocked by V2. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
V2, a 2006 Lincoln Mark LT pick-up truck, was driven by a 52 year-old male. The driver stated that he was on his way home from after shopping when the crash occurred. He had just stopped behind V3 when the front of V1 crashed into the rear of V2. This impact forced V2 forward into the rear of V3. The driver did not see V1 prior to the crash.
V3, a 2005 Chevrolet Suburban utility vehicle, was driven by a 19 year-old male. He had just left home on his way to a friend's home when the front of V2 struck the back of his vehicle. Prior to the crash, he had been stopped behind a long line of other vehicles at the red light. He stated that he was stopped less then ten seconds when V2 struck the back of his vehicle. He had not seen anything prior to the crash.
