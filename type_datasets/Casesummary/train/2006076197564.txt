This is two-vehicle crash occurred in a rural area at the intersection of two two-lane asphalt roadways. The roadways are straight and level. The intersection is controlled by stop signs for the north and south bound directions. The speed limit for the area is 56 kmph (35 mph). The crash occurred during daylight with no adverse weather or road conditions present.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2007 GMC Yukon was southbound stopped at the required stop sign in the first lane. Vehicle two (V2), a 1989 Cadillac Sedan Deville was westbound in the first lane. V1 proceeded through the intersection and its front-left corner contacted the right-front corner of V2. V1 rotated clockwise and came to rest on the eastbound shoulder facing west. V2 rotated counter-clockwise and went into a ditch where it impacted a wooden telephone pole with its front-end plane. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, a sixty-nine year old female was driving to the hospital for a doctor appointment. She had traveled from her residence in another town that was 64 kilometers (40 miles) away. Her vehicle is new and this is the first large sport utility vehicle she has driven. This driver stated that she was stopped and did not see the other vehicle. She also stated that she believed that the intersection was a four-way stop. She does not drive on this roadway very often. This driver was taking Lisinopril and Potassium Chloride.
The driver of V2, a thirty-eight year old female was driving a passenger home after they both worked a 12-hour graveyard shift 80 kilometers (50 miles) away. She was fatigued but saw V1 stopped at the stop sign and assumed that V1 would remain stopped until they passed by. This driver was taking Tylenol as a medication. She drives this vehicle on a daily basis and over this roadway regularly.
