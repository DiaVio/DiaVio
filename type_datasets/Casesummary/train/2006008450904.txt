This crash occurred in the evening of a weekday on the right roadside of a two-way, two-lane undivided rural roadway. The north/south roadway consisted of one lane in each direction and featured a posted speed limit of 56kmph (35 mph). The bituminous roadway featured a slight curve to the left and a negative grade of 3.2%. The roadway was wet from a previous rainfall. At the time of the crash it was daylight but cloudy and the traffic flow was normal.
V1, a 1997 GMC Safari extended wheelbase all wheel drive van, was southbound. As V1 negotiated the curve its left rear tire blew out causing the vehicle to swerve to the right, depart the roadway and hit a telephone pole. Upon impact the van skidded clockwise and came to rest in the southbound lane facing northeast. Police Reported Travel Speed is 64 kmph for V1.
V1 was driven by a 17-year-old female who reported no injuries. She advised that she was returning home from work and traveling about 64kmph (40mph). Suddenly her left rear tire blew out and caused her to leave the roadway to the right and hit a telephone pole with the front end of the van. V1 was towed due to damage.
