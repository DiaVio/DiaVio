A two-vehicle crash occurred several hours after dusk. The location is a busy inner-city four-way intersection with traffic signals and crosswalks. The roadway surface was wet bituminous. It was dark with streetlights and it was raining heavy at the time of the crash. The posted speed limit for both vehicles was 56 kmph, or 35-mph.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2000 Oldsmobile Alero four door sedan, was traveling southbound in lane two of a five-lane arterial approaching the intersection. Vehicle two (V2), a 1998 Toyota Camry four door sedan, was traveling northbound in lane four of a six-lane arterial. As V1 entered the intersection, V2 had pulled forward and started to turn left on a green arrow from northbound to go westbound. V1's front impacted the front right of V2. On impact V1 rotated counterclockwise and V2 rotated counterclockwise. V1's front then struck V2's right side. V1 was driven away from the scene; V2 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
