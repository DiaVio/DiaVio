This single-vehicle crash occurred during daylight hours and it was raining. The north/south roadway is undivided, straight and consists of one lane in each direction. The southbound lane has a five percent uphill grade. There is a ditch along the west side of this roadway. The speed limit for this roadway is 89 kmph (55 mph).
Vehicle one (V1), a 1996 Toyota Camry, was southbound in lane one. V1 traveled off the right side of the road and the front struck a culvert. V1 rotated clockwise and the left side struck a mailbox post. V1 then rolled over to the left eight quarter-turns. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 89 kmph for V1.
The driver of V1, a twenty-three-year-old male, was ejected and died after transportation to a local hospital. A friend was interviewed and stated that this driver was well rested, familiar with the vehicle and travels this route monthly. According to medical records, this driver has ingested Amphetamines, Benzodiazepines, Methadone and Marijuana. No further information is available for this driver.
