This five-vehicle crash occurred in a four-legged, traffic signal-controlled intersection. The north/south roadway is level, straight and undivided. The northbound approach consists of three through lanes and a center left turn lane. The southbound approach consists of two through lanes and a center left turn lane. The speed limit on this roadway is 48 kmph (30 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2005 Volvo S40, was northbound in lane four. Vehicle two (V2), a 1970 Ford F350 pickup, was southbound in lane two. Vehicle three (V3), a 2000 Jeep Cherokee, vehicle four (V4), a 1979 Oldsmobile Cutlass and vehicle five (V5), a 2003 Kia Spectra were parked and unoccupied in a parking lot southwest of the intersection. V1 turned left in the intersection and the front struck the front of V2. V2 continued to the southwest and into the parking lot. The front of V2 struck the right side of V3, the back of V4 and the left side of V5. V1 and V2 were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, a twenty-two-year-old female, was traveling from a work meeting back to work. She was healthy, well rested and familiar with her vehicle. She travels this route daily. This driver stated that the traffic signal was turning yellow and she thought that V2 would stop for the yellow light. This driver further stated that she was thinking about the meeting that she had just attended.
The driver of V2, a seventy-two-year-old male, was not interviewed; no further information is available for this driver.
