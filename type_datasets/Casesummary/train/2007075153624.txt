This single-vehicle crash occurred on an eight lane divided Interstate roadway. The westbound direction consisted of three through lanes and one entrance ramp lane. This roadway was straight, uphill, bituminous and with a posted speed limit of 89 kmph (55 mph). The crash occurred during the early morning daylight hours with moderate traffic congestion. Road conditions at the time of the crash were ice and slush on roadway. It was snowing at the time.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2001 Chevrolet Silverado with only the driver present, was westbound in lane two. V1 began to lose control and rotate clockwise. V1 then departed the original traffic lane on the right side, entered the paved shoulder and then entered the entrance ramp. The vehicle departed the ramp and ran off the right side of the roadway where the left side struck the end of a W-beam guardrail. After impact, V1 continued to travel down an embankment before coming to rest on all four tires facing west. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 97 kmph for V1.
The driver of V1 was a thirty-three-year-old female who was on her way to work from home when the crash occurred. The driver stated she was going approximately 80-97 kmph (50-60 mph) when she lost control due to the ice on the roadway. She was rested, drives this route daily and is very comfortable with the vehicle. She also reported being in good health and does not take any medication.
