The crash occurred in the morning hours of a weekday in the right parking lane of a two-way north/south roadway, with a single lane of travel in each direction. The roadway was straight and level, and it was daylight and clear with no adverse conditions. Traffic conditions were normal and the speed limit was 48kmph (30mph).
V1, a 1997 Nissan Altima four door with a single occupant, was traveling southbound when it lost control and the front impacted the back of V2, a 1989 Toyota Camry four door that was legally parked with no occupants present. V2's frontal plane contacted the rear plane of V3, a 2001Toyota Camry four door that was legally parked with no occupants present. V3's frontal plane then contacted the rear plane of V4, a 1997 Mercedes Benz E320 four door that was legally parked with no occupants present. V1 came to a final rest shortly after impact and V2, V3 and V4 came to final rest a few feet forward. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
V1 was driven by a 27 year-old female who sustained minor injuries and was transported to a hospital. The driver could not be located to participate in the study. The driver stated to the Police that an unknown white van traveling northbound made a turn in front of her causing to lose control and impact V2. The Nissan was towed due to damage. V2, V3 and V4 were not towed.
