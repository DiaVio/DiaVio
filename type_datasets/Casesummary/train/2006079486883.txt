This single vehicle crash occurred at dusk on a Sunday evening. The crash occurred on a dark five lane divided, dry, level, asphalt highway with a curve to the left. The speed limit on this roadway is 105 kmph (65 mph).
Vehicle one is a white 2003 Toyota Tundra 4x2 access cab pick-up, traveling east in the number one lane. V1 departed the roadway to the right and impacted a cinder block wall with its front right plane. V1 came to rest on the right shoulder and partially in lane one, facing east. The driver was not reported as injured. No airbags deployed in the crash. V1 was towed with major damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.
The driver and sole occupant of V1 was a 21-year old male traveling home from his parent's home after a family gathering. He had consumed one beer, 3 hours prior to the collision. He said that his front right tire blew out and caused him to veer to the right. There was no evidence that the tire blew out before impact. There were no pre-impact tire marks and officers at the scene all agreed, the damage to the tire appeared to be from the impact with the wall. The driver's cell phone was found outside the vehicle after the crash; the driver said it was in his right visor before the crash. Officers checked the cell phone for calls and times; there was one missed call, at the same time as the crash. The driver was reaching for his cell phone and veered to the right, which caused him to impact the cinder block wall.
