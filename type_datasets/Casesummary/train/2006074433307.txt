This two-vehicle crash occurred in a four-legged, traffic signal-controlled intersection. The east/west roadway is level, straight and divided by a curbed median. Both approach roadways consist of two through lanes and a left turn lane. The speed limit on this roadway is 64 kmph (40 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1994 Plymouth Voyager, was stopped in eastbound lane three, intending to turn left at the intersection. Vehicle two (V2), a 1998 Chevrolet Cavalier, was westbound in lane one. V1 entered the intersection, turning left and the right side was struck by the front of V2. V1 rotated clockwise and the left rear wheel struck the median curb on the north/south road. V1 continued rotating and the left side struck a sign on the median. V2 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, a twenty-five-year-old female, was traveling with her four-year-old son, to their home after interviewing a babysitter. This driver was healthy, well rested and comfortable with her vehicle and passenger. She rarely drives this route. This driver stated that she was lost and looking for the signs directing her back to the freeway. When she noticed a sign, she immediately turned left toward it without paying attention to the traffic signal.
The driver of V2, a twenty-four-year-old female, was traveling from work to a friend's house. She was well rested, healthy and familiar with her vehicle. She travels this roadway weekly. This driver stated that she applied the brakes when she realized that V1 was entering the intersection, but skidded and could not stop prior to the crash.
