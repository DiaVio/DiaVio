The crash occurred in the middle of a four-way intersection. The north/south roadway was an undivided four-lane roadway with lanes one and three being left and right turn-only lanes with the middle lane continuing straight. The intersection was controlled by traffic lights. The roadway was straight and level. The conditions were dry and clear on a weekday morning. The posted speed was 56 kmph (35 mph).
V1 was a 1998 Chevrolet Astro van that was traveling southbound in lane three, attempting to make a left hand turn. V2 was a 2003 Lincoln LS sedan that was traveling northbound in lane two attempting to go straight through the intersection. V1 and V2 both had a solid green light. V1 began to make a left turn and entered the intersection when V2 was passing through the intersection and hit V1 head on. Both vehicles were towed due to damages but no one was injured. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
V1 was driven by a 54-year-old male who had just left his office and was on his way to another work site. The driver stated that he was decelerating into the intersection and had a green light. He stated that he looked to the right and went to turn when V2 just came out of nowhere and hit him head on. Both vehicles sustained major front-end damage.
V2 was driven by a 20-year-old male who was on his way to drop off his friend at his house. He stated that he was in the middle through lane and was attempting to pass through the intersection when V1 just turned left into him. The driver hit his brakes to try to avoid the crash but was unsuccessful. He stated that he saw V2 approaching the intersection but didn't think it would turn.
