This single vehicle crash occurred on a four-lane interstate highway. This road is curved and level. A guardrail separates the roadway. The speed limit is 104 kmph (65 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2006 Dodge Stratus was northbound in the number four lane. As V1 was traveling, it entered the west edge median. The left plane of V1 contacted the metal/wood guardrail. V1 proceeded to skid across all lanes. V1 departed the roadway as it rotated clockwise and down the east edge dirt embankment. V1 rolled left as it proceeded to contact a chain-link fence. V1 came to rest facing southwest on its wheels. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 121 kmph for V1.
The driver of V1, a forty-eight year old male, was traveling home after being in a distant city. The driver was driving a rental car, which he had been driving for a week. The driver has been on disability for seven to eight years because of a dislocated disc. The disc is located at his fifth vertebrae. The disc was dislocated during a work-related injury. The driver also has diabetes, high cholesterol, and high blood pressure. He takes Lipitor and Zocor. The driver wears reading glasses. The driver had just left a rest stop after buying some supplies. The driver had an alcoholic beverage on his lap as he was driving. He stated that he was reaching for the beverage when he lost control of the vehicle. A sobriety test yielded a BAC result of .03. The driver stated that he had passed the interchange he had planned to take, but was not lost. The route he was on would simply take longer. The driver was fatigued at the time of the collision. His last period of sleep started on two days before the crash at 1730 and ended on the day before the crash at 0400 hours, thirty-three hours previous.
