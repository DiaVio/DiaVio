This two-vehicle crash occurred on the merge lane from a one-way access ramp as it paralleled a four-lane divided expressway. The speed limit on the ramp and roadway is 80 kmph (50 mph). The weather was clear, the crash occurred in the daylight hours on a dry, straight, level bituminous roadway.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1999 Dodge Neon was southbound on the shoulder of the access ramp approaching the merge onto the expressway. Vehicle two (V2), a 2002 Chevrolet Silverado was southbound in the entrance lane. V1 began to pass V2 on the right. As V1 was passing V2, V1 changed lanes to the left and the left rear of V1 impacted the right front of V2. V1 then crossed both lanes of traffic and the front plane impacted the center barrier. V1 rolled over and came to rest on its right side on the left shoulder. V2 pulled off the road to the right. V1 was towed due to damage. V2 was not towed. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, a twenty-one year old female was not interviewed. Very little is knows about this driver. It is known that previous to this crash on this day, that this driver struck a five year old pedestrian in a school zone about 1.6 kilometers (1 mile) away. This driver fled the scene. At the time of this crash, a witness to the original crash was following this driver. While fleeing the original crash this driver exhibited many aggressive driving behaviors.
The driver of V2, a twenty-eight year old male was heading to a friends house after picking his son up from school. This driver had been driving for about fifteen minutes at the time of the crash. This driver was taking Vicoden as a medication. This driver had not worked in about one week and was preparing for Hernia surgery at the end of this week. This driver saw V1 coming up from behind but was unable to do anything as V1 tried to pass in front of him. This driver drives this vehicle daily and over this road on a weekly basis.
