This three-vehicle crash occurred on a straight, level, one-way, one-lane entrance ramp to a jersey barrier-divided trafficway. The speed limit for this roadway is 89 kmph (55 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions. Traffic was congested at the time of the crash.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1991 Toyota Corolla, was westbound in lane one. A non-contact vehicle (NCV) was in front of V1. Vehicle two (V2), a 2006 Ford Taurus, was in front of the NCV. Vehicle three (V3), a 1998 Ford Taurus, was in front of V2. V2 and V3 slowed down. The NCV moved across the gore into the main lanes. The front of V1 struck the back of V2. V2 moved forward and the front struck the back of V3. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, a fifty-nine-year-old male, was on his way home from work. He was healthy and familiar with his vehicle. He travels this route daily. This driver stated that he was tired because he had worked all night and was on his way home to go to bed. He applied the brakes (skidding longitudinally) and steered to the right when the NCV moved and he could see that V2 and V3 were slowing down.
The driver of V2, a seventy-one-year-old male, was on his way to work from his home. He was well rested, in good health and familiar with his vehicle. He travels this route daily. He has diabetes and takes the medications Glucophage and Advil as well an unspecified high blood pressure medication. This driver stated that he slowed down when he noticed V3 slowing.
The driver of V3, a twenty-three-year-old male, was on his way to work from his home. He was healthy, well rested and familiar with his vehicle. He travels this route daily. This driver stated that he was having engine problems and was trying to restart his vehicle. He was concerned because he was late for work.
