The crash occurred on the roadside of the westbound lanes of a three-lane east/westbound roadway. The roadway was bituminous and level, with a posted speed limit of 72 kmph (45 mph). It was night on a weekday. The roads were dry and the sky was clear. Traffic flow was light.

V1 then departed the roadway on the right hand side, striking a traffic sign, a utility pole and a fence before coming to rest. V2, a 2005 Dodge Ram pickup truck with one occupant, was traveling east when the falling power lines from the pole struck by V1 struck the roof of the vehicle causing minor damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
V1, driven by a 16-year-old female her passenger, a 50-year-old female that were both transported to the hospital. She had been involved in a motor vehicle crash a few minutes earlier where the airbags had deployed and she became disoriented and panicked, leaving the scene of the crash. The driver also stated that her passenger had lost consciousness and she was trying to revive her while she continued to travel westbound. At this time V1 drifted off the roadway to the right. The driver stated that she attempted to stop but the brakes seemed to not be functioning correctly and she struck a traffic sign, continued west until striking a utility pole at which time V1 spun counterclockwise and struck a fence before coming to rest.
V2, driven by a 43-year-old male who reported no injuries, stated to law enforcement personnel that he was traveling east when the power lines from the utility pole stuck by V1 fell and contacted the roof of his truck. V2 made no avoidance maneuver.
