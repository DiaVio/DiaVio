A two-vehicle collision occurred in the early morning hours, on a Friday. It was dark, the skies were cloudy and the streets were dry prior to the collision, and began raining following the collision. The collision occurred on a busy congested interstate highway, in the southbound lanes. This is an eleven-lane divided highway with six southbound lanes. The pre-crash roadway is an uphill grade and straight, with bituminous paving. The posted speed limit for the roadway is 97 kmph (60 mph). Access to the roadway is controlled.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2006 Kenworth Tractor Trailer, was headed south in lane two. Vehicle two (V2) a 1997 Ford Ranger was ahead of V1, also traveling southbound in front of V1. V2 slowed for traffic ahead. The driver of V1 internally distracted, looked up and saw V2 slowing, or stopping. V1 attempted to avoid V2, by steering to the left and braking. The front right corner of V1 impacted the rear left of V2. The driver of V2 was transported due to injuries. V1 was driven from the scene, and V2 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1 was a 35 year-old male who is reportedly in good health, with no hearing or sight restrictions. He stated that he was looking at his directions, and when he looked up he noticed the other vehicle's brake lights. He attempted to avoid hitting V2, but was unsuccessful. He was on his way to pick up a load, he is from out of town, and this was his first time on the roadway. He reported to be well rested, and comfortable with the vehicle. He also stated that his mother-in-law had passed away prior to his leaving town, however this was not on his mind that morning, it just came to his mind when asked if there was any concerns in his family.
The driver of V2 was a 50 year-old male whose pre-crash factors are unknown, as he was not interviewed.
