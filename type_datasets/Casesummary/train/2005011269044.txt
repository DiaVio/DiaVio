Summary 075
V1, a 2000 Ford Econoline van, was westbound in lane 1 of a 2 way, 2 lane, bituminous, ->2% grade, straight roadway. At the time of the crash the roadway was dry and it was dark. V1 exited the roadway to the left, v1, front, struck a tree, then continued straight. V1, front, then struck another tree with it's front left, ramped to the right, rolled over 1 quarter turn, struck another tree then came to rest on it's right side. The driver was uninjured. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.
A complete driver interview and vehicle inspection was obtained for v1. The driver stated that 3 deer were entering the roadway from his right and he swerved left to avoid them. Evidence at the scene did not support this scenario but indicated instead a gradual run off road. There was no evidence of attempted braking either on the road or off. The driver was properly licensed for and familiar with his vehicle. He was not found to be familiar with the roadway. The Pre-event movement of v1 was going straight, the Critical pre-crash event was v1 traveling off the edge of the road on the left side and the Critical reason for the critical pre-crash event was poor directional control. Some factors contributing to the crash include impairment of the driver by alcohol, open intoxicant in vehicle at the time of the crash (observed on-scene by the researcher), driver fatigue and his unfamiliarity with the roadway.
