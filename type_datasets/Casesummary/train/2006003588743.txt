The crash occurred at a signalized intersection of two one-way roadways. Both roadways were straight and level, and it was daylight and clear with no adverse conditions. The speed limit for both roadways was 48 kmph (30 mph).
Vehicle #1 was a 2004 Ford F-550 tow truck that was traveling westbound on the one-way roadway with one lane of travel. Vehicle #2 was a 2005 Ford F-250 pickup traveling northbound in the right lane of a one-way roadway with two lanes of travel. At the intersection, the front of Vehicle #1 impacted the right side of Vehicle #2, sending Vehicle #2 into a clockwise rotation. While rotating, the left side of Vehicle #2 impacted a pedestrian who had entered the roadway at the northwest corner of the intersection. The left rear corner of Vehicle #2 then struck Vehicle #3., a legally parked and unoccupied 1992 International school bus. Vehicle #2 came to a final rest with its back bumper against the back of Vehicle #3. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The Ford F-550 (Vehicle #1) was driven by a 44-year old male who did not sustain any injuries. He stated that he had the traffic light in his favor when Vehicle #2 disregarded the traffic control signal. Vehicle #1 was not towed due to damage.
The Ford F-250 (Vehicle #2) was driven by a 37-year old male who did not sustain any injuries. The driver declined to participate in the study and Vehicle #2 was towed due to damage.
The pedestrian who was involved in Event #2 sustained incapacitating injuries and was transported to a medical facility.
