The crash occurred on a two-lane undivided level roadway with a posted speed limit of 89 KPH (55 MPH). Vehicle 1 was negotiating a curve to the right with a superelevation of 4%. The weather was clear, the roadway dry and it was daylight at the time of this early afternoon weekend crash.
Vehicle 1, a 1988 Nissan Maxima, was traveling north on the two-lane undivided roadway negotiating the curve to the right. The operator of Vehicle 1 observed a non-contact vehicle in her lane slowing or stopping. Operator of Vehicle 1 braked and steered to the left in an attempt to avoid the non-contact vehicle. Vehicle 1 began to rotate counterclockwise, corrected right and then began to rotate clockwise. Vehicle 1 continued across the southbound lane and off the left side of the roadway striking a 48 cm diameter tree with its left side. Vehicle 1 rotated counterclockwise and came to rest facing in a westerly direction. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.
The Nissan Maxima (Vehicle 1) was driven by a belted 26-year-old female who was airlifted to a trauma center with severe bleeding to the head. The belted 5-year-old front seat passenger was transported, treated and released at a local hospital for minor bleeding to the head. The PAR indicated unsafe speed as a contributing factor. There was no additional information available. Vehicle 1 was towed due to damage.
