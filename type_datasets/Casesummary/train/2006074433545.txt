This two-vehicle crash occurred in a four-way, traffic signal-controlled intersection. The north/south roadway is level, constructed of concrete and consists of four lanes, two in each direction. The northbound lanes are slightly curved to the left and the southbound lanes are straight. The speed limit on this roadway is 56 kmph (35 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1995 Mazda MX-3, was northbound in lane two, intending to turn left (west) at the intersection. V2, a 1993 Dodge Caravan, was southbound in lane one on the same roadway. A non-contact vehicle, a large truck, was southbound in lane two turning left (east) at the intersection. The driver of V1 failed to notice V2 and began the left turn to the westbound roadway. The driver of V2 applied the brakes and steered right, but the right side of V1 was struck by the front of V2. The vehicles came to rest in the southwest corner of the intersection, V1 facing northeast and V2 facing southwest. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, a twenty-six-year-old male, was traveling with a twenty-three-year-old female passenger. They had just left their home and were on the way to the laundromat. This driver works night shifts and had awakened feeling rested approximately one hour prior to the crash. He is very familiar with his vehicle and the route. He claimed that he was not able to see around the non-contact truck that was turning left.
The driver of V2, a twenty-four-year-old female, was traveling with a thirty-year-old male passenger as well as three young children. They were driving from a store to their home and had been on this trip for approximately ten minutes. This driver was well rested and was very comfortable with her vehicle and passengers but not very familiar with the roadway.
