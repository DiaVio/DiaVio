The crash occurred off the right side of an eastbound four-lane highway with a posted speed of 105 kmph (65 mph). A guardrail divided the four eastbound lanes from the four westbound lanes. It was mid-afternoon on a weekday; the road was dry and there was mud and standing water on the roadside. This was a straight, level bituminous section of roadway approaching a left curve.
V1, a 1993 four-door Lexus ES 300, was driven by a 70-year-old male. The driver stated that he had left a shopping center 40 minutes prior to the crash and had traveled about 30 miles when he fell asleep. The driver woke when the vehicle hit the guardrail four meters off the right side of the highway. He stated that his speed was 105 kmph (65 mph) prior to falling asleep and that he had felt tired when he left the shopping center. The driver stated that he was uninjured and refused treatment although he had visible facial injuries caused by the airbag. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.
