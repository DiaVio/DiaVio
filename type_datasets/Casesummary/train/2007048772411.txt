This two-vehicle crash occurred on an undivided, straight, level, two-lane roadway. The speed limit for this roadway is 89 kmph (55 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2002 Ford Escort, was northbound in lane one. Vehicle two (V2), a 1998 Mercury Grand Marquis, was northbound in lane one in front of V1. The front of V1 struck the back of V2. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 89 kmph for V1 and 89 kmph for V2.
The driver of V1, a twenty-one-year-old female, was traveling from her home to a friend's house for lunch. She was well rested, healthy and familiar with her vehicle. She travels this route weekly. This driver stated that she was looking at the scenery on the sides of the road. When she realized that V2 was traveling slower, she applied the brakes (skidding longitudinally) in an attempt to avoid the crash.
The driver of V2, a seventy-six-year-old female, was on her way home from church. She was healthy, well rested and familiar with her vehicle and route. This driver stated that she did not see V1 prior to the crash.
