The crash occurred in the westbound lanes of a three-lane roadway. There was one eastbound and two westbound lanes. The roadway had a positive barrier that ended part way up the hill. The concrete roadway was uphill (8.3% grade) with a left hand curve and a posted speed limit of 72 kmph (45 mph). It was afternoon on a weekday, the road was dry and skies were cloudy. Traffic was light and moving freely.
V1, a 1999 Dodge Neon four door with one occupant, was traveling westbound in lane one. V2, a 2000 Ford Windstar minivan with one occupant, was traveling west in lane two. As the vehicles traveled up the hill, the jersey barrier came to an end and V1 initiated an illegal U-turn. The left front of V1 contacted the right front of V2. The vehicles side slapped V1's left rear contacted V2's right rear. V1 came to rest on the roadway and backed off the side of the road. V2 came to rest on the westbound shoulder. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
V1, the Dodge Neon, was driven by a 20 year-old female who was transported with unknown injuries. No interview could be obtained for V1. Police on scene related that the driver of V1 had missed a turn a few miles back and was trying to turn around as soon as possible.
V2, the Ford Windstar, was driven by a 55 year-old female who reported no injuries. She advised that she had noticed V1 driving in an erratic manner and was keeping an eye on it. V1 then passed V2 on the right. As they passed the point in the road where the jersey barrier ended, V1 initiated an illegal U-turn and contacted V2 twice.
