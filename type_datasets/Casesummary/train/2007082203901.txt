This two vehicle daytime collision occurred in the intersection of a four lane arterial road with a center turn lane, and a two-lane city street. The road is paved with concrete and is straight with a downhill grade of - 6% east/west and controlled by stop sign, north/south level . The speed limit east/west is 40 kmph (25 mph) and north/south the speed limit is 18 kmph (30 mph). At the time of the crash the sky was partly cloudy.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2000 Ford Mustang coupe driven by a thirty nine (39) year old male with one passenger, was traveling east. Vehicle two (V2), a 1998 Nissan Pathfinder SUV driven by a twenty eight (28) year old male, was traveling north. V1 was approaching the intersection in lane one and had stopped at the stop sign. V1 began to cross the intersection after yielding to two or three cars. V2 was traveling in lane one as V1 pulled out; the front of V2 impacted the left side of V1. V1 continued southeast jumped a curb, impacted a wooden post with its left front quarter panel and came to rest in a parking lot. V2 continued southeast and impacted a utility pole with its front. Both vehicles were towed due to damage and both drivers were transported due to injury. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
