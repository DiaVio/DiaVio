This two-vehicle crash occurred in a four-legged, traffic signal-controlled intersection. The east/west trafficway is straight, divided by a curbed median and both directions consist of two through lanes and a left turn lane. The eastbound travel lanes are level and the westbound lanes have a downhill grade. The speed limit for this roadway is 64 kmph (40 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1995 BMW 740IL, was eastbound in lane two. Vehicle two (V2), a 2001 Mercury Sable, was stopped westbound in lane three. V2 entered the intersection turning left and the front struck the front of V1. Both vehicles towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, a twenty-five-year-old female, was traveling with a forty-nine-year-old female passenger, from her home to a store. This driver was healthy, well rested and comfortable with her vehicle and passenger. She travels this route several times each month. This driver saw V2 waiting to turn left but thought that it would wait until V1 had passed through the intersection. She applied the brakes and steered to the right in an attempt to avoid the crash.
The driver of V2, a sixty-three-year-old female, was on her way to lunch from her home. She was in fair health, well rested and familiar with her vehicle. She travels this route daily. This driver has multiple sclerosis and diabetes and takes the medications Zoloft, Baclofen, Copaxone, Metformin, Celebrex, Aciphex, Detrol, Propranolol, calcium, vitamins and aspirin. This driver saw V1 but thought that V1 was stopping for the traffic signal.
