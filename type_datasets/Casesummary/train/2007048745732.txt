This two-vehicle crash occurred on a clear morning. The crash occurred on a two-lane, dry, uphill, bituminous roadway that runs north to south and was under construction. This roadway intersected with a dirt/gravel, straight, level roadway that runs east to west and was also under construction. A stop sign controlled traffic heading westbound at the intersection. The posted speed limit for this roadway was 56 kph (35 mph). Roadwork warning signs and traffic barrels were present at the location of the crash.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2005 Ford Explorer with one occupant, was traveling westbound in lane one attempting to make a left turn to travel south. Vehicle two (V2), a 2005 Chevrolet Cobalt with two occupants, was traveling northbound in lane one. As V1 entered the intersection the front of V2 struck the left side of V1. V1 came to rest in the southbound lane facing east and V2 came to rest in the northbound lane facing northwest. V1 was driven from the scene and V2 was towed due to disabling damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 8 kmph for V1 and 8 kmph for V2.
The driver of V2 was a healthy 43-year-old male who drives the vehicle and roadway on a daily basis. He was commuting to work when the crash occurred. The driver of V2 advised that V1 pulled into the intersection and he struck her on the left side. This driver applied the brakes (skidding longitudinally) but could not stop in time.
