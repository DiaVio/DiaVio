The crash occurred on a four lane urban roadway. There were two lanes running southbound and two lanes running northbound separated by a curbed raised median. The conditions were dry and clear on a weekend afternoon. The roadway was straight and level. The posted speed was 56 KPH (35 MPH).
Vehicle 1 was a 2007 Dodge Ram Truck that was driven by a 54-year-old male traveling southbound in lane one. The traffic had come to a stop and was backed up for the traffic at the traffic light. V2, a 1998 Chevy Geo Metro that was driven by a 86-year-old woman had also come to a complete stop when she was hit from behind and her vehicle was pushed into V3, a 2006 Ford Van that was also stopped in lane one, the driver was a 38-year-old male. V2 was the only vehicle that was towed due to damages. Police Reported Travel Speed is Unknown for V1 and Unknown for V2.
V1, the 2007 Dodge Ram truck was a rental truck that the driver rented for his work. He had just made a right turn onto the street where the crash had occurred and spilled his coffee on the floor. He stated in the interview that the traffic had stopped and he went to hit the brake but his foot slipped off the brake because of the wet coffee making the pedal slick. He was unable to avoid the crash.
V2, the 1998 Chevy Metro that was driven by the 86-year-old woman stated in her interview that she was on her way home with her granddaughter as she had just gotten to town to visit her grandma and were returning home form lunch. They stated in the interview that they were at a stop and they heard a crash and saw the truck hit them from behind and they were pushed under the rear of the van that was in front of them.
V3, the 2006 Ford Van was driven by a 38-year-old male who had stopped in lane one. He was on his way to meet a client for a job, as he was a painter. He was in his work van and was hit from behind. He stated there was nothing he could do but luckily there was no visible damage to the back of the vehicle. The van was not towed due to damages.
