This two-vehicle crash occurred on an southbound, eight lane, divided, straight, level roadway at the intersection of a three lane, one-way, straight roadway with a 6.6% downhill grade. The southbound lanes consist of three through lanes and a right turn lane. Traffic at the intersection is controlled by traffic control signals. The speed limit on the roadways is 48 kmph (30 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with light traffic. There were no adverse weather conditions at the time of the crash.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1998 Chevrolet Silverado pulling a flat bed gooseneck trailer, was southbound in lane two. Vehicle two (V2), a 2005 Dodge Durango, was westbound in lane three. V1 entered the intersection on a red light and the front of V2 struck the left side of V1. V2 rotated 180 degrees counter clockwise and came to rest facing east in the intersection. V1 came to a controlled stop in southbound lane three. V1 did not sustain disabling damage. V2 was towed due to damage. Neither occupant was transported. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, a forty-four year-old male, was traveling from picking up a trailer to home. He was well rested, in good health and comfortable with his vehicle. He drives this route rarely. This driver stated that he was unsure of the color of the traffic light when he entered the intersection. He noticed V2 just before he entered the intersection. He applied the brakes in an attempt to avoid the crash.
The driver of V2, a twenty-one year-old male, was traveling from home to work. He was well rested, in good health and comfortable with his vehicle. He drives this route daily.
