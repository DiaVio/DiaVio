This single vehicle daytime collision occurred mid-morning. The collision occurred on the southbound lanes of a north/south interstate highway. The northbound lanes are dry, concrete, curve left, and have a 3.2% upgrade slope. The number three lane of the highway is a high occupancy vehicle (HOV) lane. The east edge of the highway is bordered by a concrete median. East of the east median exists a metal/wood guardrail. East of the guardrail exists grass covered embankment. West of the highway exists an asphalt shoulder. West of the asphalt shoulder exists a dirt/grass embankment with an upgrade slope. The highway is designated a 104 kmph (65 mph) zone.
Vehicle one (V1) is a 2002 Ford Mustang, two-door coupe, driven by an eighteen-year-old female was traveling southbound in the number two lane. As V1 negotiated the curve, it began to change lanes to the right. The left rear tire then suffered tread separation causing the vehicle to lose control. The vehicle traveled across all lanes towards the west edge shoulder. The front plane of V1 contacted the dirt/grass hillside as it traveled up the embankment. The left plane of V1 then contacted the hillside as it traveled down the embankment. The vehicle came to rest on the west edge shoulder facing southbound. V1 was towed from the scene due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 113 kmph for V1.
