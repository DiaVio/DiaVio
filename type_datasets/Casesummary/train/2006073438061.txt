This single vehicle crash occurred on a four-lane two-way roadway. This road is straight and level. It had rained previously and the bituminous roadway was wet at the time. Traffic was very light. The speed limit on the roadway is 32 kmph (20 mph). This crash occurred during nighttime hours.
Vehicle one, a 1995 Chevrolet Blazer was northbound in the first lane. Vehicle one departed the roadway to the right, climbed the curb and struck a utility pole with the front end. The utility pole sheared off at the base and vehicle one began a clockwise rotation, coming to final rest on the roadway against the curb facing southeast. Vehicle one was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.
The driver of vehicle one, a forty-eight year old female had just left a tavern with the passenger and was heading to take the passenger home. This driver had only been driving for a few minutes. This driver indicated that she was talking to the passenger before the crash but does not remember anything leading up to the crash. This driver had a BAC of 0.191. This driver takes Ambien as a medication. This driver drives this vehicle daily and over this road on a daily basis.
