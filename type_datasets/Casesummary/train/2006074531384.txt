This two-vehicle crash occurred in a traffic signal-controlled intersection. The roadway south of the intersection was level, straight and consisted of one lane in each direction and a left-turn lane. The speed limit on this roadway is 48 kmph (30 mph). The roadway east of the roadway was level, straight and consisted of two lanes in each direction and a left-turn lane. The speed limit on this roadway is 56 kmph (35 mph). It was dark with streetlights on at the time of the crash and there were no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1993 Ford Thunderbird, was stopped in northbound lane one at the intersection, waiting for the signal to turn green. V2, a 1992 Chevrolet S10 pickup, was westbound in lane two, approaching the intersection. The driver of V2 saw that the signal was yellow and attempted to cross the intersection before it turned red. The north/south signal turned green and V1 proceeded through the intersection. The front of V1 struck the back left side of V2. Both vehicles rotated counterclockwise. V1 came to rest in the northwest corner of the intersection facing northwest. V2 came to rest in the westbound lanes facing southeast. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, a forty-five-year-old female, was traveling with a four-year-old female, from her home to a relative's home. She was well rested, healthy and familiar with this roadway. This was the first time she drove this vehicle, but she stated that she felt comfortable with it.
The driver of V2, an eighteen-year-old female, was traveling with an eighteen-year-old female, from a school function to her home. She was well rested and healthy. She is familiar with this roadway but had only been driving for one month. Her inexperience is an associative factor in this crash.
