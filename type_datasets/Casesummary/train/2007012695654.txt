The crash occurred off the left side of the northbound lanes of a six-lane highway divided by a grass median. There were three northbound lanes and three southbound lanes. This roadway had a pre-crash curve to the right with a radius of 2250m, a superelevation of 4 percent, and a posted speed of 113 kmph (70 mph). It was morning on a weekday and the road was icy and the sky was clear. Traffic was flowing.
V1, a 1994 Saturn SL four-door sedan with one occupant, was traveling northbound in lane three. The driver of V1 stated that she was negotiating a curve and saw a snowdrift in her lane of the roadway up ahead and she did not know how deep it was. She attempted to slow down by applying the brakes. When she contacted the snow she started to fishtail. She stated that she took her foot off the brake and attempted to correct by steering left and right. V1 lost control and departed the roadway on the left side, skidding clockwise. V1 rolled left two-quarter turns before coming to final rest on its roof facing east. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.
V1 was driven by a 19-year old female. She reported injuries and was transported to a local trauma facility. She stated that she had left her boyfriend's house two hours before the crash and was on her way home. She stated that she had been traveling at approximately 113 kmph (70 mph) prior to losing control.
