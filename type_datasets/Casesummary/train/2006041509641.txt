The crash occurred in the westbound lanes of a five-lane, undivided roadway. There were two eastbound lanes and three westbound lanes. This was a straight, level, asphalt roadway, with a posted speed of 64 kmph (40 mph). Conditions were dark with streetlights, on a weekday evening. The skies were clear and the roadway was dry. Traffic flow was normal.
V1, a 2001 Chevy Silverado 1500 with two occupants, was traveling westbound in lane one. V2, a 1992 Toyota Previa with two occupants, was stopped in lane one for a drawbridge signal, ahead of V1. V3, a 2002 Toyota 4-Runner with one occupant, was stopped in lane one for the same drawbridge signal, ahead of V2. V1 ran into the back of V2, pushing V2 into the back of V3. Police Reported Travel Speed is 56 kmph for V1 and 56 kmph for V2.
V1, the Chevy Silverado, was driven by a 45-year old male, who was not injured. The driver could not be located for interview.
V2, the Toyota Previa, was driven by a 54-year old male, who was not injured. He stated that he was taking his neighbor to church, when he had to stop for the drawbridge. The driver indicated that the driver of V1 got out of his truck and didn't seem to understand what had just happened. He said that the other driver was extremely intoxicated, and began to walk away, down the sidewalk.
V3, the 4-Runner, was driven by a 34-year old female, who was not injured. She stated that she was picking up a friend from church. She had just left her house, and saw that the bridge was up. Traffic was backed up. She saw the driver of V2 change lanes and get behind her. She said that she was stopped for one to two minutes at the most, before the driver of V1 hit the vehicle behind her, pushing that vehicle into her. She indicated that the driver of V1 appeared to be very intoxicated, walking away from the scene.
