This two-vehicle crash occurred in a rural area on a four-lane divided highway with an extended median. The two-lane northbound asphalt roadway was straight and level. The crash occurred during daylight with no adverse weather conditions. The posted speed limit is 104 kmph (65 mph).
Vehicle one (V1), a 1996 Mazda 626 was northbound in the first lane. Vehicle two (V2), a 2005 GMC Sierra K-2500 pickup towing a flatbed trailer with a tandem axle was stopped on the paved right shoulder of the northbound roadway. The front of V1 impacted the tandem tires on the right side of V2's trailer. V1 rotated 360 degrees in a clockwise direction and came to rest on the right side shoulder facing north. V2 remained at the point of impact. V1 was towed due to damage. V2 was not towed. Police Reported Travel Speed is 97 kmph for V1 and 97 kmph for V2.
The driver of V1, a forty-two year old male, gave a partial interview. It was discovered that this driver was suffering from an unknown mental disorder. This driver was supposed to be taking Risperidone for this illness but was not taking the medications regularly. This driver was heading to a job interview about 160 kilometers (100 miles) away at the time of the crash. This driver was late for the interview. The driver indicated that he did not remember the crash. This driver drives this vehicle daily but this was his first time on this roadway. He had 4 hours of sleep in the previous 24 hour period, so fatigue was considered a factor for him. The driver tested negative for alcohol consumption at the medical facility.
