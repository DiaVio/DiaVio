This two-vehicle crash occurred on a jersey barrier-divided, straight trafficway in an interchange area. The westbound travel lanes are level and consist of three through lanes with shoulders on both sides. The speed limit for this roadway is 89 kmph (55 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1990 Saab 900, was westbound in lane three. Vehicle two (V2), a 1999 International straight truck, was westbound in lane two. V1 moved to the right and the right side struck the left side of V2. V1 then traveled to the left and the front struck the median jersey barrier. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, a twenty-seven-year-old female, was not interviewed. No further information is available for this driver.
The driver of V2, a fifty-seven-year-old male, was traveling between jobs. He was healthy, well rested and comfortable with his vehicle and cargo. He travels this route daily. He had been driving a truck for more than five years. This driver stated that he did not see V1 prior to the first impact.
