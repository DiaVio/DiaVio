This three-vehicle crash occurred at the intersection of a four-lane, divided, east/west roadway and a two-lane, undivided, north/south roadway. For westbound traffic, there are two travel lanes, no traffic controls and a right curve with a radius of 188m (617 ft.). Both roadways were level, dry and of asphalt construction. The posted speed limit is 80 kph (50 mph). Traffic was congested at the time of the crash. This crash occurred during daylight hours with clear skies.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2002 Toyota Tacoma with one occupant, was westbound in the second travel lane. Vehicle two (V2), a 1986 Oldsmobile Cutlass, was stopped westbound in the second travel lane ahead of V1. Vehicle three (V3), a 1994 Cadillac DeVille, was stopped westbound in the second travel lane ahead of V2. The front of V1 struck the rear of V2. V2 was moved forward, where the front of V2 struck the back of V3. All three vehicles came to rest in the second lane, forward of the points of impact. V1 and V2 were towed due to damage. V3 was driven from the scene without disabling damage. The driver of V2 was transported. Police Reported Travel Speed is No PAR obtained (created) for V1 and No PAR obtained (created) for V2.
The driver of V1, a twenty-seven year old male, was heading from one job site to another. V1 was intending to merge into the first lane and was looking into the outside rearview mirror to see if he could begin to merge. When he turned his attention back to the front, the driver stated he was approximately 20 feet from the back of V2. The driver stated he applied the brakes and attempted to steer to the left prior to impact.
The driver of V2 was an unidentified male who was transported due to injury. He was not interviewed.
The driver of V3, a sixty-seven year old male, stated he had been stopped in the lane for 1-2 seconds when the accident occurred. He was waiting to turn left at the intersection. The driver could not be located at a later time to complete the interview.
