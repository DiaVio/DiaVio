This single-vehicle crash occurred late in the afternoon with no adverse atmospheric conditions. The roadway is undivided and consists of two lanes in the westbound direction and one lane in the eastbound direction. The westbound lanes have an uphill grade and curve to the right in the pre-crash area. There is a rock embankment on the north side of this roadway. On the south side of this roadway is a steep, downhill, dirt/rock embankment that leads to a dirt fire road. There are trees on the south side of the fire road. The speed limit for this roadway is 40 kmph (25 mph).
Vehicle one (V1), a 2007 BMW 550I, was westbound in lane two. A coyote entered the roadway from the right side. The driver of V1 steered left and V1 departed the roadway on the left side. The front of V1 struck a boulder as V1 traveled down the embankment. V1 crossed the fire road and the front struck a tree. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.
The driver of V1, a thirty-seven-year-old male, was on his way home from a business appointment. He was well rested, healthy and familiar with his vehicle. He has high cholesterol and takes the medication Lipitor. He rarely travels this route. This driver stated that he was traveling at approximately 72 kmph (45 mph) and thinking about an upcoming free dinner. When he saw the animal, he steered to the left but did not apply the brakes. This driver further stated that he "blacked out," that it was dark when he woke up and he could not find his cell phone. He spent the night in his vehicle. He called for help when it became light again and he was able to locate his cell phone.
