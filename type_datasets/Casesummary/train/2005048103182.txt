This rear end type crash occurred at approximately 0800 hours on a dry, straight level, four-lane, divided, bituminous roadway. This roadway runs north to south with a speed limit of 89kmph (55mph). Both vehicles were traveling north at the time of the crash. There are two traffic lanes in each direction of travel with no shoulders. There were no adverse atmospheric conditions at the time of the crash.
Prior to the crash, there was a motorist stopped in the southbound side of the roadway, either in lane two or in the median. Vehicle two was traveling northbound and the driver stopped, partially off of the roadway, but mainly in lane two to determine if the motorist needed assistance. The vehicle, a 1996 Chevrolet K1500 pickup, was stopped for about one minute with its emergency flashers on. Vehicle one, a 1994 Chrysler Town and Country minivan, was traveling northbound in lane two. While vehicle two was stopped, the front of vehicle one contacted the back of vehicle two. Vehicle two rotated counter clock-wise and came to rest in the median facing southwest. Vehicle one deflected towards the right and came to rest in lane one of the northbound side of the roadway. Vehicle one contained three occupants. One of the passengers was transported to a local hospital by ambulance for treatment. Vehicle two contained one occupant who was transported by private vehicle to a local hospital for treatment. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Unknown for V1 and Unknown for V2.
