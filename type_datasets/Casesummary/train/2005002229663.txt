This crash occurred on a two lane undivided roadway with a slight curve to the left prior to the crash site. The roadway was level and the posted speed limit was 72KMPH (45MPH) It was raining and the roadway was wet at the time of the crash. This crash occurred on a weekday afternoon.
Vehicle #1 a 2005 Subaru Forrester was traveling east on the two lane roadway when the driver lost control of the vehicle and overcompensated his steering, going off the roadway to the right and impacting with a utility pole. The top of the utility pole then broke off and contacted with the top of Vehicle #1. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.
The Subaru Forrester, (Vehicle#1) was driven by a belted 83 year old male who refused treatment at the scene. The driver stated he felt the back end of his vehicle “hydroplaning”, going to the right and so he steered left, then right in a effort to correct it, and went off the road. It should be noted here that the driver takes prescription medication for gout and a diuretic for high blood pressure. This driver also walks with a cane on his left side.
The Police did not consider alcohol or illegal drugs to be a factor in this crash and ordered not tests for this driver.
