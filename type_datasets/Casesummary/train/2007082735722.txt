This two-vehicle daytime collision occurred on a north/south roadway. The roadway has five lanes in the area of the crash. Two lanes are northbound, two lanes are southbound and one lane is a center two-way left turn lane. The road is paved with concrete, level, and curves to the right for the southbound lanes; the road is straight and level for the northbound lanes. The speed limit for the roadway is 48 kmph (30 mph). At the time of the crash the sky was clear, the roadway for the southbound traffic was icy at the curve; the roadway for the northbound traffic was dry.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2004 Chevy pickup driven by a fifty three (53) year old female with one passenger, was heading south in lane one. Vehicle two (V2), a 2000 Chevy Malibu sedan driven by a seventy four (74) year old male with one passenger, was heading north in lane two. V1, while traveling south, had come into contact with ice in the curve. V1 lost control and spun around clockwise and traveled into the northbound lanes. V2 was traveling north in lane two and its front impacted with the right side of V1 with V2's front. V2 was towed due to vehicle damage. V1 was driven from the scene. The driver of V1 was not transported to a hospital, while the driver of V2 was transported to a hospital for treatment. The driver of V2 refused an interview. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
