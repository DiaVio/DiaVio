The crash occurred at a signalized intersection of two one-way roadways. Both roadways were straight and level, and it was daylight and clear with no adverse conditions. The speed limit on both roadways was 48 kmph (30 mph).
Vehicle #1 was a 1998 Ford Explorer that was traveling eastbound on a one-way roadway with one lane of travel. Vehicle #2 was a 1999 Chevrolet Cavalier that was traveling northbound in the right lane of a one-way roadway with three travel lanes. At the intersection, the front of Vehicle #1 impacted the left front side of Vehicle #2. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The Ford Explorer (Vehicle #1) was driven by a 35-year old female who sustained minor injuries but was not transported to a medical facility. The driver stated to the police that Vehicle #2 disregarded the traffic control signal. The Ford was not towed due to damage. The Chevrolet Cavalier (Vehicle #2) was driven by a 29-year old female who sustained minor injuries and was transported to a medical facility. The occupants of Vehicle #1, a 10-year old female, a 7-month old male, and a 9-year old male, did not sustain any injuries but were transported to a medical facility. The driver stated to the police that she "blew a red light," causing the crash. The Chevrolet was towed due to damage.
