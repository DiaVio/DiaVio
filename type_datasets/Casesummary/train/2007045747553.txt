Case 2007-143
The crash occurred on a narrow undivided two-lane north/southbound roadway with one lane (2.8m wide) in each direction. Approximately 50m prior to the crash the road curved to the right with a radius of curvature of 81m. The asphalt road was straight, dry and level where the crash occurred. The posted speed limit was 48kmph/30mph. There were no traffic control devices. The crash occurred on a weekday afternoon while traffic was light under clear sunny skies. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.
V1, a 1995 Olds Ciera four-door with one occupant was traveling southbound as it entered the straight section of road. V1 drove off the right side of the road and over a mound of roadside dirt that caused it to go slightly airborne. Its front right struck a 40cm tree (Event 1) and then continued traveling down a steep embankment where it 's right side struck two more smaller trees (Event 2) before coming to rest on the embankment against a fourth tree facing southwest approximately 8m west of the road and 15m from the road departure point.
V1, the Olds Ciera, was driven by an 83-year old male who was coded for possible injuries and transported to a hospital. The driver reported that he was traveling approximately 32kmph/20mph while returning home from shopping. He said he drove this road rarely. He driver said that as he exited the curve into the straight-away section he saw two oncoming non-contact vehicles and that the second one (a truck) straddled the double yellow lane marker ahead. He could not say how far away the truck was. The driver added that he steered right and felt his vehicle go off the right side of the road where he lost control and crashed.
