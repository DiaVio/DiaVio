The crash occurred on a north / south, rural arterial road that was two way and two lane. There was a curve right in the area that the crash occurred, as well as another switchback curve to the left (if headed south). The road was bordered on the right side (if southbound) by trees and a creek. The radius of curvature for the first curve (right) was 142.4 meters, the second (left) was 97.8 meters. The posted speed limit was 48 KPH / 30 MPH. Conditions were daylight and dry in the early afternoon on a weekday.
Vehicle 1 was a 1988 Nissan Maxima southbound on the arterial road. After negotiating the curve right it failed to negotiate the curve left and went off the right side of the road and into the trees. It struck a tree with its front and then rolled one quarter turn to its left, and coming to rest partially suspended in the air over the creek by the underbrush that accumulated under the front end when it left the roadway. Vehicle 1 was towed due to damage Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.
Vehicle 1 was driven by a 51 year old belted female who was uninjured in the crash.
