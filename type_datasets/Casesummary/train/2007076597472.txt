This single vehicle crash occurred on a wet one-way divided road. This road is in a rural area with a -6 percent downgrade on a right hand curve. This crash occurred during the late afternoon with a heavy rainfall and light traffic conditions. The posted speed limit is 89 kmph (55 mph) with curve warning signs prior to this curve reducing the speed to 64 kmph (40 mph). This roadway has guard railing on the left side and a drainage ditch with a vertical embankment on the right road edge.
Vehicle one (V1), 2002 Jeep 4 door Cherokee SUV, was southbound negotiating a right hand curve in a heavy rain when V1 began to rotate clockwise. A female driver steered left to counter this rotation but V1 continued to rotate clockwise and exited the right hand road edge. V1 impacted a dirt embankment with its front plane then rotated clockwise and impacted the embankment a second time with the left rear bumper. V1 bounced off the embankment and came to rest on its wheels off the roadway heading in a westerly direction. V1 was towed for disabling damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 89 kmph for V1.
The driver, a thirty two (32) year-old female, was slightly injured and transported to a local hospital where she was treated and released. The driver was not interviewed. The police reported a speed of 89 kmph (55 mph) for this vehicle. The vehicle was inspected after the crash and the tires had 8 millimeters (10 32nds of an inch) of tread depth, but it is unknown what all of the tire pressures were due to crash damages.
