This two-vehicle crash occurred on a seven lane, divided, straight north/south roadway at the intersection of a two lane, undivided, straight east/west roadway. The westbound lanes have a positive grade while the northbound lanes have a negative grade. The northbound lanes consist of three through lanes and a left turn lane. The speed limit on this roadway is 56 kph (35 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with light traffic. There were no detrimental weather conditions at the time of the crash. East/west traffic is controlled by stop signs.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1998 Ford Escort, was stopped westbound in lane one. Vehicle two (V2), a 2005 Honda Pilot, was northbound in lane three. V1 attempted to make a left turn across the path of V2 into the southbound lanes. The front of V1 struck the right of V2. Both vehicles moved in a northwesterly direction before coming to rest in the intersection facing northwest. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, a sixteen year-old female, was traveling from home to school. She was healthy, well rested and familiar with her vehicle. She drives this route daily. This driver stated that there was a bus stopped in the first northbound lane that obstructed her view of the northbound lanes. She did not see V2 prior to impact and attempted no evasive maneuvers. She had been stopped at the stop sign for more than ten seconds. She has been driving for less than 6 months.
The driver of V2, a thirty-four year-old female, was traveling from home to work. She was healthy, well rested and familiar with her vehicle. She drives this route daily. This driver also stated that there was a bus stopped in the first northbound lane that obstructed her view of V1.
