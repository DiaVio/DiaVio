This three-vehicle collision occurred shortly after noon in daylight hours The collision occurred on an interstate highway. The roadway is a seven-lane divided highway with four travel lanes in the northbound direction. The pre-crash roadway is straight and level with concrete paving. The weather was clear and the roadway was dry. The posted speed limit for the roadway is 97 kmph (60 mph). Access to the roadway is controlled. Traffic flow was stop & go and congested.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2003 BMW 745i sedan driven by a forty four (44) year old male, was headed north in lane three. Vehicle two (V2), a 2005 Honda Element driven by a fifty two (52) year old male, was ahead of V1 in lane three. Vehicle three (V3), a 2005 Nissan pathfinder driven by a thirty two (32) year old male, was traveling ahead of V2 in lane three. Traffic ahead of V3 stopped, and V3 stopped abruptly, and so did V2. V1 did not stop in time and the front of V1 impacted the rear of V2, pushing V2 forward into the rear of V3. V1 was towed due to damage and V2 was also towed unknown if due to damage. V3 was driven from the scene Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1's pre-crash factors are mostly unknown as he refused to participate in the study. The driver of V2's pre-crash factors are mostly unknown as he was unable to be reached for interview. According to the police report the investigating officer listed V1 to be exceeding a reasonably safe speed as a contributing factor. The driver of V3 stated that traffic was crawling & stop & go. He observed V2 come to a sudden stop behind his vehicle and then V1 "creamed him" (V2). The driver of V3 tried to move forward to avoid the impact from V2 but was unable to get out of the way. The driver of V3 was talking on his cell phone while he observed what was going on behind him in his rear view mirror.
