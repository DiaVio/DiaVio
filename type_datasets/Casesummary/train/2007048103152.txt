This two-vehicle crash occurred in a four-legged, traffic signal-controlled intersection. The north/south trafficway is level, straight and divided by a curbed median. The southbound lanes consist of two through lanes, two left turn lanes and a right turn lane. The northbound lanes consist of two through lanes, one left turn lane and a right turn lane. The speed limit for this trafficway is 80 kmph (50 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1999 Mercury Sable, was southbound in lane four. Vehicle two (V2), a 1009 GMC Sierra K1500 pickup, was northbound in lane two. V1 turned left in the intersection and the front struck the front of V2. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Unknown for V1 and Unknown for V2.
The driver of V1, a thirty-nine-year-old female, was traveling from a doctor's office to a restaurant with two teenage passengers. She was well rested, in good health and comfortable with her vehicle and her passengers. She takes Synthroid and Pepcid for a thyroid condition and acid reflux. This driver stated that she was focused on finding the restaurant. This was the first time she had driven on this roadway.
The driver of V2, an eighteen-year-old male, was on his way to lunch from work. He was healthy, well rested and familiar with his vehicle. He travels this route daily. This driver stated that he did not see V1 prior to the crash.
