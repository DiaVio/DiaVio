This single-vehicle crash occurred in a rural area on a two-lane asphalt roadway. The southbound lane curves left and is downhill. The radius of curvature was 568 meters (1863 feet) with a cross slope of 4 %. The speed limit is 88 kph (55 mph) for the area. The crash occurred during darkness with no lighting available. There were no adverse weather or road conditions present.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2003 Chevrolet S-10 Blazer with one occupant, was southbound, negotiating the left curve when the vehicle gradually departed the right road edge and continued until the right side sideswiped the rock embankment. The vehicle initiated a clockwise rotation where the front of the vehicle again struck the embankment. The vehicle then rolled left four-quarter turns, coming to rest on its wheels in the northbound lane facing southwest. V1 was towed from the scene. The driver was transported and admitted to a trauma center. Police Reported Travel Speed is Unknown for V1.
The driver of V1 was a 78 year-old male. The driver was not available for an interview. The report completed by the investigating officer was positive for alcohol.
Medical reports indicate a significant medical history for alcohol intake, chronic kidney disease with an elevated creatinine and benign prostatic hyperplasia. Medication consisted of Terazosin. He was quite combative in the emergency department. His blood/alcohol was 0.185.
