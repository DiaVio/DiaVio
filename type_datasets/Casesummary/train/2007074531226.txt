This two-vehicle crash occurred in a four-legged, traffic signal-controlled intersection. The east/west trafficway is straight and divided by a curbed median. The westbound approach lanes are level and consist of two through lanes, a right turn lane and a left turn lane. The eastbound approach lanes have an eleven percent downhill grade and consist of one through lane, one through/right turn lane and a left turn lane. The speed limit for this trafficway is 64 kmph (40 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1999 Honda Civic, was stopped westbound in lane four. Vehicle two (V2), a 2003 Dodge Durango, was eastbound in lane two. V1 entered the intersection, turning left, and the front struck the front of V2. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, an eighteen-year-old male, was on his way to a friend's house from his home. He was healthy, well rested and familiar with his vehicle. He travels this route weekly. This driver stated that he was looking to the left as he made his turn and did not see V2 until "the last second."
The driver of V2, a thirty-four-year-old male, was traveling with his thirty-four-year-old wife and two young children. They had left their home and were on their way to the driver's mother's house. This driver was well rested and comfortable with his vehicle and passengers. He travels this route daily. According to this driver's medical record, he has high blood pressure and a seizure disorder and is prescribed the medications Clonidine, Zoloft and Benicar. He had not taken the Benicar for two days prior to the crash. This driver stated that he was traveling at five miles-per-hour over the speed limit and saw V1 as he was approaching the intersection. When he realized that V1 was entering the intersection, he applied the brakes, skidding longitudinally.
