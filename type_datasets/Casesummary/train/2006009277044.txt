This crash occurred in the morning of weekday in the eastbound lanes of a divided four-lane roadway. The bituminous north/south roadway featured two lanes in each direction and a posted speed limit of 80kmph (50 mph). The roadway was dry, straight, and level. It was daylight and there were no adverse atmospheric conditions at the time of the crash.
V1, a 1985 Lincoln Town car, was traveling north in lane one of the roadway. V2, a 1987 Chevrolet Pick-up was also traveling north in lane one ahead of V1. As V1 continued northbound its water pump burst causing windshield to cloud up. The driver was unable to see in front of ahead of him and struck V2 in the rear. Both vehicles rolled forward slightly before coming to final rest in the roadway near the point of impact. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
V1 was being driven by a 19 year-old male on his morning commute to work. He stated he heard a loud pop when he turned on the heat in his car and the windshield quickly fogged up. The next thing he knew he had rear-ended the truck in front of him. He was uninjured, but his vehicle was towed due to damage.
V2 was driven by a 44 year-old male who became agitated during the interview and refused to cooperate further. Before ending the interview he stated that he saw V1 out of his rearview mirror come up behind him and fail to stop before crashing into him. The driver of V2 was not injured but the back window of V2 was shattered rendering the car disabled and it was towed from the scene.
