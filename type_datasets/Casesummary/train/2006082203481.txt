This four-vehicle crash occurred on an entrance ramp. This roadway is two lanes in the northbound direction and converges into one lane prior to an access-controlled roadway. The precrash area is on a hillcrest and there is a warning sign indicating the lane merger prior to this area. The speed limit on this roadway is 97 kmph (60 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2006 Ford Fusion, was northbound in lane one. Vehicle two (V2), a 2004 Volvo S40, was northbound in lane two. Vehicle three (V3), a 2000 Ford F250 pickup, was northbound in lane two in front of V2. Vehicle four (V4), a 1997 Chevrolet Astrovan, was northbound in lane two behind V2. V1 moved into lane two due to the merger into one lane. V2 and V3 stopped due to a disabled non-contact vehicle. The front of V1 struck the back of V2. The front of V2 then struck the back of V3. The front of V4 then struck the back of V1. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, a seventy-one-year-old male, was traveling with his forty-one-year-old wife, from their home to a ballet production. He was healthy, well rested and comfortable with his vehicle and his passenger. He travels on this roadway daily. This driver stated that he was looking to the left to facilitate his lane change. When he looked forward again, he noticed that V2 and V3 had stopped and applied the brakes in an attempt to avoid a crash.
The driver of V2, a twenty-one-year-old male, was traveling from his home to his girlfriend's home. He was well rested, healthy and familiar with his vehicle. This driver travels this route daily.
The driver of V3, a forty-seven-year-old male, and the driver of V4, a forty-six-year-old male who was traveling with five passengers, were not interviewed; no further information is available for these drivers.
