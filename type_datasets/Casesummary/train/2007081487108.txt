This two-vehicle crash occurred a couple of hours past noon during daylight hours. The location was a busy arterial in a residential neighborhood in an intersection controlled by overhead on-color signals. The north south roadway was a bituminous two lane roadway with one lane in each direction. The eastbound roadway is a one-way roadway, with three lanes approaching the intersection, and four coming out of the intersection. The crash occurred in the intersection. The weather was clear and dry and visibility was clear. The posted speed limit was 40 kmph (25 mph).
Vehicle one (V1), a 1990 Acura Integra sedan driven by a sixteen (16) year old male, was eastbound in lane three. Vehicle two (V2), a 1997 Honda Civic sedan driven by a forty four (44) year old female, was traveling southbound crossing through the intersection on a green light. As V1 entered the intersection, the front of V1 impacted with the right side front of V2. Both vehicles then side slapped. V2 came to rest facing northeast in lane four on the east leg of the intersection. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1's pre-crash factors are unknown as his father refused to have him participate in the interview. A witness listed in the police report stated that he was following V1 and he observed the light change to red and then V1 entered the intersection without any avoidance actions taken.
