This single-vehicle crash occurred during the hours of darkness with no adverse atmospheric conditions. The roadway is divided, straight, level and was dry at the time of the crash. There are two through lanes in each direction. On the right side of the roadway, there is a narrow paved shoulder and then a grassy downhill embankment with trees at the bottom. The speed limit on this roadway is 89 kmph (55 mph).
Vehicle one (V1), a 1999 Oldsmobile Alero, was eastbound in lane one. The right front tire blew out and V1 traveled off the right side of the roadway, down the embankment, rotating clockwise. The left side of V1 struck a tree. V1 continued rotating and the right side struck two more trees before V1 came to rest facing north. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 89 kmph for V1.
The driver of V1, a twenty-six-year-old female, was traveling with a three-year-old female, from a friend's house to her home. She was healthy, well rested and familiar with her vehicle. She drives this route often.
