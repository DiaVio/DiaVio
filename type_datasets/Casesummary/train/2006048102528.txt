This two-vehicle crash occurred in an intersection during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions. The east/west roadway is level and consists of one lane in each direction. This roadway is at a forty-five degree angle (southwest) on the west side of the intersection. This roadway is controlled with stop signs and a flashing red beacon. The northbound approach roadway is straight and level and consists of one lane in each direction and a right turn lane. A flashing yellow beacon controls this roadway. The speed limit on both roadways is 89 kmph (55 mph).
Vehicle one (V1), a 2000 Nissan Frontier, was westbound, stopped at the stop sign. Vehicle two (V2), a 2005 Harley Davidson motorcycle, was northbound in lane two. V1 proceeded across the intersection. The driver of V2 applied the brakes and slid into the intersection. The front of V2 struck the left side of V1. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 16 kmph for V1 and 16 kmph for V2.
The driver of V1, an eighty-seven-year-old male, was traveling from his home to his friend's house. He was in fairly good health and well rested. He was familiar with his vehicle and drives this route weekly. This driver stated that he stopped to check for cross traffic and did not see V2.
The driver of V2, a fifty-five-year-old male, was not interviewed. No information is available for this driver.
