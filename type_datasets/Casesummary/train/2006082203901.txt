The crash occurred in the intersection of two one-lane roadways. There were three northbound and two westbound lanes. The northbound lanes had an uphill grade of 2% and the westbound lanes had an uphill grade of 6%. Both roads have straight, concrete roadways with a posted speed limit of 48 kmph (30 mph). It was daytime on a weekday when the road was dry and the sky was cloudy.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2005 Toyota Scion was westbound on the roadway in the first lane. Vehicle two (V2), 2006 Chevrolet Trailblazer was northbound in the second lane. Both vehicles approached the intersection at roughly the same time. Both vehicles entered the intersection to go straight through and the front of V2 impacted the left side of V1. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1 was a fifty-eight year old male. The NASS researcher was unable to obtain an interview from the driver on-scene due to the driver being transported for injuries. The driver could not be reached on later attempts.
The driver of V2, a thirty-five year old male was on his way back to the hotel to pick up his wife. This driver had spent the day with his son shopping. This driver had been driving for about five minutes at the time of the crash. This driver stated that he did not see the other vehicle coming. This driver was on a trip from another state and had driven this vehicle daily for the week he had been in town and over this road daily while in town.
