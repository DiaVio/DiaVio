This two-vehicle crash occurred just after dawn on a clear morning. The crash occurred in lane-two on a two-lane interstate exit ramp. The roadway is bituminous, level, curves to the right, and has a super elevation of 2%. The speed limit is 65 mph (105 kmph). There were no adverse conditions at the time.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2000 Ford Explorer was traveling south in lane two. Vehicle two (V2), an unknown large truck was southbound in lane one. Both vehicles were going at approximately the same speed as they entered the exit ramp. V2 changed lanes to the left and encroached into V1's travel lane. The left side of the trailer of V2 struck the right front side of V1. V2 then left the scene of the crash. V1 departed the left road edge and struck a guardrail causing V1 to roll to its left along it longitudinal axis two quarter turns for approximately fourteen meters were it came to final rest partially in lane two on its top facing northwest. V1 was towed due to disabling damage and the driver was transported to a local trauma center with incapacitating injuries. Police Reported Travel Speed is Unknown for V1 and Unknown for V2.
V1 contained one occupant, a 27 year-old female. The driver advised that the tractor/semi-trailer changed lanes and she steered left in an attempt to avoid the collision. The vehicle was forced off the roadway into the roadside and guardrail. She is familiar with the vehicle and drives the route daily. She was attentive to the driving task.
