A single vehicle ran off roadway crash occurred on a Saturday evening during the hours of darkness. The crash occurred in the five dry, level, asphalt eastbound lanes of a divided highway in the gore area at an exit off-ramp. The roadway is designated 105 kmph (65 mph.). There were no detrimental environmental, traffic or atmospheric condition at the time. The roadway is illuminated at night by overhead lights.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1992 Honda Accord EX 2-door coup with one occupant, was traveling east in the number one lane. V1 departed the roadway onto the gore point on the right side where the front plane impacted a wooden exit sign. V1 then continued forward where the front left plane struck the end of a guardrail. V1 rotated counter clockwise as it came to rest in the gore area facing north. V1 was towed with major damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 105 kmph for V1.
The driver of V1 is a 32-year old male with 10 years of driving experience in Korea and 2 years in the USA. He has a myopic (near sighted) condition. He has a restriction on his license for corrective lenses. He was wearing his prescription glasses at the time. Fatigue was considered a factor for this driver, who was asleep before departing the roadway. His last main sleep was 17.5 hours prior to the crash, and his normal daily sleep is 4 hours in length. He had slept two hours the morning of the crash and took two 15-minute naps during the day. He spends most of his waking hours at his church doing odd jobs. The day of the crash he spent 5 hours planting flowers at the church. He drives the route daily and is familiar with the vehicle.
