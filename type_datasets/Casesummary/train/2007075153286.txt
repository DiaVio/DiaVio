This single-vehicle crash occurred on a four lane divided roadway. The northbound direction of the roadway consisted of two through lanes. This roadway was curved right, downhill and bituminous with a posted speed limit of 72 kmph (45 mph). The crash occurred during the early evening hours. The roadway was wet with visible icy road treatment and it had just started to snow lightly.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1988 Toyota 4-Runner with only the driver present was northbound in lane one. The driver of V1 was negotiating a curve when V1 started to rotate clockwise. V1 continued to rotate clockwise before departing the roadway on the right side. When leaving the roadway V1 struck a delineator post with its left side before rolling one quarter turn onto its left side. V1 came to rest on its left side facing west and was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 80 kmph for V1.
The driver of V1, a twenty-two-year-old female, was on her way to the airport from home when the crash occurred. She stated she had a scheduled time to be at the airport but had enough time and was not in a hurry. She stated she drives this route monthly and is very comfortable with this vehicle. She did state however that her tires were pretty bald and her speedometer did not work in the vehicle at the time. She stated being rested and in good health at the time of the crash. The investigating officer indicated that the driver was traveling over the posted speed limit.
