The crash occurred off the right side of a two lane undivided roadway. It had one northbound lane and one southbound lane. This was a straight, dirt roadway with a positive grade of two percent and a speed of 89 kmph (55 mph). It was afternoon on a weekday and the road was icy and the sky was clear. Traffic was sparse.
V1 was a 1997 Chevrolet C2500 pickup with one occupant. V1 was traveling northbound in lane one. A non-contact vehicle was approaching V1 headed southbound. Due to the narrow roadway the driver of V1 stated that he steered to the right a little to let the other vehicle pass by. V1 then lost control and started to skid off the right side of the roadway. The driver of V1 applied the brakes but could not avoid striking a utility pole with the front of V1. V1 came to final rest at the pole. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.
The driver of V1 was a 61-year old male who reported injuries and was transported to a local trauma facility. He stated that he had come from the grocery store and was on his way home. He stated that he had been traveling between 34-48 kmph (21-30 mph) prior to losing control. He applied the brakes but could not avoid contacting the pole.
