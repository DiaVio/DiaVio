The crash occurred in the eastbound lanes of a two-way, four-lane, residential roadway. There were two eastbound lanes on a 3% uphill left curve and two westbound lanes on a 3% downhill right curve. The roadway had a posted speed limit of 56kmph (35 mph). Conditions were daylight and dry with cloudy skies. The setting was early afternoon hours on a weekday.
V1, a 1991 Nissan pickup, was westbound in lane two. V2, a 2000 Toyota Tacoma T100 pickup towing a loaded, open trailer was eastbound in lane two. V3, a 2001 Dodge Durango, was eastbound in lane two, traveling behind V2. V1 drifted the centerline and impacted the left plane of the trailer being hauled behind V2 with its front plane. V1 continued forward into the front of V3, striking it head on. V1 and V3 came to rest in the eastbound lanes near the point of impact. V2 traveled approximately 50 meters with its damaged trailer before coming to rest in the eastbound lane two. The trailer axle was destroyed causing a couple of laundry machines to fall onto the roadway. Police Reported Travel Speed is 56 kmph for V1 and 56 kmph for V2.
V1 was driven by a 38-year-old male who was transported to a local trauma center with non-incapacitating injuries. He was unable to be located for an interview. The investigating officer estimated his travel and impact speeds to be 56kmph (35 mph). V1 was carrying a large amount of heavy cargo in its bed, which shifted into the rear of the passenger cabin after impact. The pickup was towed due to damage.
V2 was driven by a 65-year-old female who was uninjured. She stated that she was heading to the tire store when another vehicle crossed into her lane and struck the trailer she was towing. She honked her horn in an effort to avoid the collision. She estimated her travel and impact speeds to be 40kmph (25 mph). The pickup was not towed due to damage.
V3 was driven by an 35-year-old male who was transported to a local trauma center with non-incapacitating injuries. He was unable to be located for an interview. The investigating officer estimated his travel and impact speeds to be 56kmph (35 mph). The sport utility was towed due to damage.
Neither Critical Precrash Event nor a Critical Reason were coded to this vehicle, as it was not involved in the first harmful event. No other associated factors could be coded to this driver.
