This two-vehicle crash occurred in a four-leg intersection. Northbound had one right turn lane, two through lanes and one left turn lane. Southbound had three through lanes and one left turn lane. The roadway for vehicle one was straight, level, bituminous and with a posted speed of 64 kmph (40 mph). The roadway for vehicle two was straight, uphill, bituminous and with a posted speed of 64 kmph (40 mph). It was mid-afternoon on a weekend. The road was dry and the sky was clear. Traffic flow was congested.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2005 Nissan four-door Maxima with two occupants, was northbound in lane four, the left turn lane, turning left. Vehicle two (V2), a 1995 Chevrolet S-10 with two occupants was southbound in lane one going straight. Southbound lanes two and three had stopped to let V1 make its turn. V1 was impacted in the front by V2's front, which was in lane one going straight. Neither driver did anything to avoid the crash. After the impact, V1 rotated counterclockwise and came to rest on its wheels facing southwest at the southwest corner of the intersection. After the impact, V2 traveled southwest and came to rest on its wheels facing southwest on the sidewalk of the southwest corner next to V1. V1 was towed from the scene with moderate frontal damage. V2 was also towed from the scene with moderate frontal damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 16 kmph for V1 and 16 kmph for V2.
