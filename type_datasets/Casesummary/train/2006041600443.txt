This crash occurred on a weekend afternoon on a high-occupancy, high-volume interstate highway. The northbound direction of traffic consisted of four lanes flanked by paved shoulders and divided from southbound traffic by a concrete barrier wall. The bituminous highway was dry, straight, and level with a posted speed limit of 105kmph (65 mph). The traffic flow was normal and there were no adverse atmospheric conditions at the time of the crash.
V1, a 2002 Ford Taurus was northbound in lane one traveling near the speed limit as it approached V2, a 1999 Dodge Intrepid that was disabled and parked on the right shoulder. V1's right side struck the left rear of V2 as it passed causing damage to both vehicles. After contact V1 came final rest facing northbound on the shoulder. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is No PAR obtained (created) for V1 and No PAR obtained (created) for V2.
The driver of V1, an 80-year-old male, was on his way home with his son. He claimed the disabled vehicle was sticking into the lane one but he thought he could maneuver around it. Neither he nor his son were injured in the crash. He was cited for failure to drive within a single lane. The driver was unable to be interviewed and his son could only provide limited information.
