mThis two-vehicle rear end crash occurred during early morning hours on a highway. The location was a four-lane concrete, divided (with positive barrier) westbound highway. The speed limit is 80 kmph (50 mph). There was medium traffic at the time of the crash. The roadway was illuminated. The weather was clear and the roads dry.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2003 Mitsubishi Galant with one occupant was traveling west in the third lane of traffic. Vehicle two (V2), a Ford Explorer, with at least one male occupant was traveling ahead of V1. V2 slowed for an unknown reason. The driver of V1 applied his brakes but the front of V1 impacted the rear of V2. Both vehicles came to rest within lane three of the highway, facing west. V1 was towed from the scene. V2 did not remain at the scene. The vehicle drove off the scene and was not identified. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
A 26-year old male drove V1. He was heading from a customer site to another site. He had driven for about 20 minutes to this location. He estimated he was following V2 at a distance of 8 meters (26 feet), when V2 slowed for no apparent reason. He said there was no traffic in front of V2. V2 did not come to a complete stop before the impact. At the gap and speed estimated by the driver, he did not have time to react to the maneuver of V2 prior to impact.
V2 or the driver of V2 was never identified. No information is available.
