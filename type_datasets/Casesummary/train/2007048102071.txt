This two-vehicle crash occurred on an undivided, two-lane roadway. In the northbound direction, this roadway curves to the left (the radius of curvature is 347 meters and the superelevation is seven percent) and has a downhill grade. The southbound travel lane curves to the right and has an uphill grade. The speed limit on this roadway is 72 kmph (45 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1994 Ford Mustang, was northbound in lane one traveling at approximately 89 kmph (55 mph). Vehicle two (V2), a 2005 Ford F150 pickup, was southbound in lane one. V1 traveled off the right side of the roadway. The driver of V1 steered left and lost control. V1 returned to the roadway and then crossed the centerline rotating counterclockwise. The front of V2 struck the right side of V1. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 89 kmph for V1 and 89 kmph for V2.
The driver of V1, a seventeen-year-old male, was traveling with his seventeen-year-old girlfriend, from her house to their friend's house. He was healthy, well rested and familiar with his vehicle. He travels this route weekly. This driver had been driving for one year.
The driver of V2, a forty-eight-year-old male, was on his way home from work. He was in fairly good healthy, well rested and comfortable with his vehicle. He travels this route daily. This driver has heart disease and takes Plavix. This driver stated that when he saw V1 lost control, he applied the brakes and steered to the right in an attempt to avoid the crash.
