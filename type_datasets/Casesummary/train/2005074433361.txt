This single vehicle crash occurred early evening on an interstate roadway during rush hour traffic. No adverse weather conditions present at the time of collision. Concrete roadway has a 5% slope and five lanes of travel. A positive concrete barrier is to the left of roadway (south side). Posted speed limit 96 kph (60 mph).
Vehicle one (V1), a 1985 GMC Jimmy 4x4, driven by a 26 year-old female was traveling west in lane 5 when it suddenly swerved left and contacted concrete barrier with left front corner of vehicle. Left tires of vehicle traveled up the barrier to the point of almost clearing the barrier. Vehicle then ricocheted off the barrier while the left rear corner contacted the barrier. Vehicle then traveled across lane 5, 4, 3, and partially into 2 before coming to rest facing north. According to witness statement, it appeared V1 driver failed to notice traffic slowing down and swerved left to avoid collision with vehicles ahead of V1. Witness stated seeing V1's brake lights while it was in a slight counterclockwise yaw. V1 was towed from the scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.
The pre-event movement for V1 coded going straight, category this vehicle traveling over the lane line on left side of travel lane. Critical reason for the critical event driver related factor "heart attack or other physical impairment of the ability to act".
Associative factors assigned to driver are as follows: driver experienced a physical impairment induced by a partial low blood sugar blackout while driving in rush hour traffic. Also, driver has high blood pressure. Driver takes medication for both migraine and high blood pressure conditions.
