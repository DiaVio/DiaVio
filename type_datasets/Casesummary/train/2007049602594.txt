This single vehicle crash occurred on a weekday during morning daylight hours. The location an entrance ramp leading to a divided, access-controlled trafficway. The entrance ramp and the highway both had concrete traffic barriers on both sides of the travel lanes. The ramp had a slight curve to the right and an uphill grade. The weather was clear and the roads dry. The posted speed limit for the highway was 97 kmph (60 mph). The advisory speed posted for the entrance ramp was 64 kmph (40 mph). There were no adverse weather conditions at the time of the crash.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2003 Mazda Tribute Minivan, was traveling north on the entrance ramp to the thru traffic lanes on the highway. V1 departed the roadway on the left side and the front left corner struck the western concrete traffic barrier. V1 continued to move north and the front right corner of V1 struck the east side barrier. V1 continued to move along the barrier for 16.5 meters (54 feet) until it came to rest on the shoulder of the highway. The vehicle was towed from the scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.
The driver of V1 was a 52-year old female. She was heading from home to work. She drives on this roadway to her work on a daily basis. She was in good health and does not take any medication. The driver said she had no explanation as to why she lost control of her vehicle. She claimed she did not fall asleep and said she was well rested that morning. During the interview, she asked if one would notice or remember when falling asleep for a moment. She stated that she worked extended hours (up to 10 hours a day) for the last four weeks prior to the crash. She gave a range of time as to when she usually goes to sleep and what times she gets up in the morning, less than seven hours each night. This driver's fatigue was a factor in this crash.
