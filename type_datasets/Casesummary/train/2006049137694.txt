The single vehicle crash occurred on a three-lane divided urban roadway without a positive barrier. Northbound traffic has a curve to the left with a radius of 804 meters (2636 feet) at the point of the road departure. The road was dry, travel polished concrete with a -4.9% grade. The posted speed limit is 56 kph (35 mph). Traffic was light at the time of the accident. This crash occurred during daylight hours with clear skies.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1995 Nissan Pathfinder with four occupants, was northbound in the third travel lane. V1 departed the travel lane on the right side, passed through lane two and lane one and departed the roadway on the right side. The front of V1 impacted a tree with an approximate diameter of 60 cm. V1 continued northbound, became airborne as it crossed over a creek, landed on the other side and continued northbound to final rest approximately twenty meters farther. The vehicle was towed due to damage. Three of the four occupants were transported with minor injuries. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.
The driver of V1, a thirty-four year old female, was heading home from picking-up her children from school at the time of the crash. This driver had been driving for about five minutes at the time. She stated that she had fallen asleep. The driver stated that she had been up late the previous night studying. The driver stated that the last thing she remembers was starting downhill in the third lane. This driver drives this vehicle daily and over this road on a daily basis.
