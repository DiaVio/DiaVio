This crash occurred in the northbound lane of a eight-lane roadway divided by a grass median and a guardrail. There were five northbound and three southbound lanes. This was a straight, level, bituminous roadway with a posted speed limit of 89 kmph (55mph). It was late evening on a weeknight and the road was dry and the sky was clear.
V1 a 2003 Jeep Sahara was traveling eastbound in lane three proceeding to turn left at the intersection. V2 a 1994 Pontiac Bonneville was traveling northbound in lane two at a constant rate of speed. V1's front contacted V2's left side. Final rest for V1 and V2 was in lane one of the northbound lane on the northeast corner of the intersection. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
V1, a 2003 Jeep Sahara was driven by a 17 year-old female. The driver of V1 stated that she was looking at the wrong light when she entered into the intersection. She stated that the light she was looking at was green so she proceeded to turn into the intersection. As a result, V1 struck V2 on its left side causing severe damage to V2.
V2, a 1994 Pontiac Bonneville was driven by a 20 year-old male that is physically handicapped, the gas and brake is operated by a special instrument for the handicapped. The driver of V2 stated that he doesn't remember what happened at the time of the crash. The driver of V2 was transported to the hospital due to injuries and his vehicle was towed due to severe damage.
