This single vehicle crash occurred on a two way, two-lane roadway near a three-legged intersection area. This roadway is constructed of asphalt with a concrete sidewalk on the east side. On this walkway is a metal utility pole that is located near the curb edge of this sidewalk. The posted speed limit is 48 kmph (30 mph) with a downgrade of -4 percent. 50 meters (164 feet) prior to this crash area this roadway curves left for the southbound traffic. This crash occurred during darkness with overhead street lighting.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1989 Chevrolet 4 door sedan, was southbound and had just exited the left hand curve when a dog crossed the street in front of this vehicle. The driver swerved left to avoid the animal. The driver stepped on the accelerator instead of the brake pedal; causing V1 to jump the curb and impact a metal utility pole. V1 came to rest at the point of impact with the pole and the pole was knocked over. The vehicle was towed due to damage. The 16 year-old driver was injured and transported to a medical facility. A 16 year-old passenger was not injured and not treated. Police Reported Travel Speed is 48 kmph for V1.
The interview for this crash was refused.
