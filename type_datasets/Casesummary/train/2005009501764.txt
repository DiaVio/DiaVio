This crash took place during the early afternoon of a weekday at an intersection of two roads and a driveway controlled by a traffic signal. The bituminous, dry north/south roadway was two-way, undivided and had four lanes with a posted speed of 56kmph (35 mph). The west roadway was two-way undivided with three lanes.
V1, a 2005 Dodge Caravan, was stopped for the red signal preparing to turn right from the driveway exit. V2, a 2000 Ford Expedition, was traveling north in lane two, approaching the intersection while negotiating a slight curve left. V3, a 2000 Saturn SL2, was traveling south in lane two approaching the same intersection. V4, a 1997 GMC C-series truck, was traveling south in lane one. As V1 turned right on the red signal and attempted to enter lane two it was struck by V2. V2 rotated approximately 30 degrees counterclockwise, crossing the centerline contacting V3. V3 was than pushed into lane one where it was struck in the back plane by V4. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
V1 was driven by a 59 year-old female who claimed to be in fair health and was wearing her glasses that corrected a hyperopic (far-sighted) condition. She stated that she was conversing with an occupant about recent financial problems just prior to initiating the turn right. She also stated that she saw V2 but thought that she had time to pull out and complete the turn. Both occupants suffered possible injuries and were transported for treatment. V1 was towed from the scene.
V2 was driven by a 44 year-old male in good health but fatigued as he was heading home. He stated that he was driving a little fast but saw V1 and did not think that the vehicle would attempt the turn until he passed. His vehicle collided with V1 as he traveled through the intersection. The driver was uninjured but V2 was towed due to damage.
V4 was driven by a 43 year-old male who refused to be interviewed. This vehicle was not involved in first harmful event and not thought to have contributed to the crash.
