The crash occurred in the westbound lane of a two-lane, two-way rural road. This was a straight, gravel/slag roadway with a posted speed of 89 kmph (55 mph). At the time of the crash it was daylight, raining and the road was wet.
Vehicle 1, a 1998 Ford Explorer, was westbound. V1 drifted to the right side of the road, the driver steered left, and the vehicle went into a counter-clockwise yaw and exited the roadway to the left. V1 continued in a counter-clockwise yaw off-road to left, struck a tree >10cm with its front and rotated further counter-clockwise. V1 struck a stump with its right side causing it to roll over 2 quarter turns to the right. V1 came to rest on its roof facing west. The driver was uninjured and the vehicle was towed. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.
V1, the Ford Explorer, was driven by a 16 year-old female driver who had a valid, graduated driver's license and was alone at the time of the crash. She stated that she was westbound on the right side of the road and it had been raining for a few days. It was raining when her car got “sucked” to the soft mud on the right edge of the road. She said she was doing about 64 kmph (40 mph). She “cranked” the steering wheel to the left and tried braking, the car went into a counter-clockwise yaw and slid off the road to the left and struck a stump with the right side causing it to flip onto it's roof. She was belted and got out the passenger side.
