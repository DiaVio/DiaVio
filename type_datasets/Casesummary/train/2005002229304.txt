This crash occurred on a two lane undivided roadway, curving to the right. with a posted speed llmit of 89KMPH (55MPH). The weather was cloudy and misting after a moderate rain and the roadway was wet. This crash occurred on a weekend evening.
Vehicle #1, a 1993 Mazda 626, was traveling north on the two lane undivided roadway when it approached the curve at an unsafe speed. The vehicle went over the left lane line, across the southbound lane and departed the roadway impacting and breaking a guardrail with the left front, then the left rear. The vehicle then continued across both north and south bound lanes and impacted with a guardrail on the northbound side, with its front right. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.
The Mazda 626 was driven by a 22 year old belted female who was transported to an area hospital, treated and released.
She stated she was going 45MPH ((72KMPH) and was in the turn when the vehicle started to skid. She stated she tried braking and steering to the right but it didn't help. It should be noted at this time that very recently painted roadway lines were extremely slippery and caused several rescue workers to slide and fall down. It may be noted that the lead tire, the Left Front tire, had only 1mm of tread at the time of the crash.
It should also be noted that this driver has had to take a 6 hour mandatory driving test two times.
It should be noted here there were no warning signs on the northbound side of the roadway, however there was a posted warning sign of 50MPH (80KMPH) on the southbound side. The roadway is bituminous on either side of the bridge and concrete on the bridge itself.
The police did not consider alcohol or illegal drugs to be a factor in this crash.
