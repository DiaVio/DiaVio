The crash occurred in the northbound lanes of a six-lane divided roadway. The road had three northbound lanes and three southbound lanes. The roadway is straight, level and bituminous. The posted speed limit is 56 kmph (35 mph). It was the early evening on a weekday. Traffic flow was light.
V1, a 2000 Ford F-150 pick-up truck was traveling north in lane three. V2, a 2000 Buick Century 4-door sedan was traveling north in lane three ahead of V1. V3, a 2000 Toyota Camry was traveling north in lane two next to V2. V1's front contacted V2 in the rear. V2 lost control of its vehicle and then struck V3. V1 and V2 came to a rest in lane 3. V3 was not on scene upon arrival. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
V1, a 2000 Ford F-150 pick-up truck was driven by a 28-year old male who was not injured. V1 was traveling behind V2 in lane 3 at a constant rate of speed. The driver of V1 stated that he did not know that V2 had slowed down and he's not sure why. He said he slammed on his brakes but it was to late.
V2, the 2000 Buick Century 4-door sedan was driven by a 51 year old female. The driver was reluctant to give an interview because her attorney told her not to speak to anyone, but did state that she was slowing down due to a traffic signal when she saw the lights of V1 from her rear view mirror approaching and not slowing down. She said she braced and felt the impact and was dazed. She also stated that she didn't know that she had struck another vehicle until she received the crash report. The driver of V2 was transported due to possible injuries.
V3, the 2000 Toyota Camry was driven by a 26-year old male. This driver also refused an interview. He stated that the vehicle was his aunts car and he wasn't supposed to be in that area where the crash occurred. Request to see the vehicle was denied by owner. She stated that her nephew associates with the "wrong kind of people" and she doesn't want any strangers at her residence looking at the vehicle. The driver of V3 was not injured from the crash.
V3 was not involved in the first harmful event.
