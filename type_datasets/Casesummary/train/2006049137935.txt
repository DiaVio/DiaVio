This two-vehicle crash occurred in a four-legged, traffic signal-controlled intersection. The north/south roadway is straight and divided by a curbed median. The speed limit on this roadway is 64 kmph (40 mph). The northbound approach lanes have a 3.3% downhill grade and consist of four through lanes and a left turn lane. The southbound approach lanes have a 2.5% uphill grade and consist of four through lanes and a left turn lane. It was dark with streetlights on at the time of the crash and there were no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1992 GMC Jimmy, was northbound in lane five. Vehicle two (V2), a 1988 Cadillac Deville, was southbound in lane two. V1 turned left at the intersection and the front of V2 struck the right side of V1. V1 traveled off the northwest corner of the intersection and the front struck a building. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, a thirty-nine-year-old male, was not interviewed. No information is available for this driver.
The driver of V2, a twenty-six-year-old male, was traveling with a sixteen-year-old male, from a store to his home. He was healthy, well rested and familiar with his vehicle. This was the first time that this driver traveled on this roadway. He stated that he did not realize that V1 was going to turn left until it was too late to attempt to avoid a crash.
