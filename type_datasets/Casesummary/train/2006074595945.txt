This two-vehicle crash occurred in a four-legged, traffic signal-controlled intersection. The east/west roadway is undivided, level and consists of five lanes with a left turn lane at the intersection. Both approaches have a left curve with a radius of curvature of sixty-five meters. The speed limit on this roadway is 64 kmph (40 mph). It was dark with streetlights on at the time of the crash and there were no adverse atmospheric conditions. Traffic was congested due to rush hour.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2002 Ford Taurus, was eastbound in lane two. Vehicle two (V2), a 1997 Chevrolet S10 pickup, was westbound in lane three. V2 turned left in the intersection and the front struck the front of V1. The vehicles rotated and the left side of V1 struck the right side of V2. V1 continued off the southeast corner of the intersection, down an embankment and the front struck a ditch. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, a sixty-three-year-old female, was traveling from a friend's home to a store. She was in fairly good health, well rested and comfortable with her vehicle. This driver stated that she was lost and did not know what street she was on. She stated that she saw V2 but thought he would wait for her to pass through the intersection before making his turn. She steered to the right in an attempt to avoid the crash. This driver has depression and takes the medications Atenolol, Loestrin-FE, Lexapro, Singulair, Advil, Combivent, Tylenol and Vitamins.
The driver of V2, a -year-old male, had left work and was going to stop at a video rental store on his way home. He was healthy, well rested and comfortable with his vehicle He travels this route daily. This driver indicated that his traffic signal turned yellow and he thought that V1 would stop prior to the intersection. He applied the brakes in an attempt to avoid the crash.
