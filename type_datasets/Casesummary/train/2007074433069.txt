This two-vehicle crash occurred in a four-legged intersection with road construction in the area. Both trafficways are divided by a curbed median. The westbound approach consists of two lanes, a downhill grade, curves to the left and is controlled by a stop sign. The speed limit for this roadway is 40 kmph (25 mph). The northbound approach consists of two lanes, is level and curves to the left. The speed limit for this roadway is 56 kmph (35 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2007 Mini Cooper, was westbound in lane two. Vehicle two (V2), a 2001 Toyota Highlander, was northbound in lane two. V1 entered the intersection, turning left and the left side was struck by the front of V2. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, an eighty-two-year-old female, was on her way from her home to a friend's house to play bridge. She was well rested and familiar with her vehicle. She travels this route daily. This driver had left her husband home alone and she was concerned about him because he has cancer. She stated that her health was good, but her medical records indicate that she has allergies and high cholesterol and takes the medications Zyrtec, aspirin, Zetia, Nulab and vitamins. This driver further stated that she looked left and then right before entering the intersection, but did not see V2 prior to the crash.
The driver of V2, a sixty-nine-year-old female, was running a work-related errand. She was healthy, well rested and familiar with her vehicle. She travels this route daily. This driver stated that she saw V1 but thought that it would yield the right-of-way.
