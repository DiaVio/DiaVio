This two-vehicle crash occurred on a state highway. This roadway is undivided with two lanes of travel, one lane each in the northbound and southbound directions. This roadway is level, curves to the east and has a posted speed limit of 48 kmph (30 mph). There is a pedestrian crosswalk sign at the crash site which states that vehicles must stop for pedestrian traffic in the crosswalk. This crash occurred in mid-afternoon on a weekday with moderate traffic flow.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2002 Ford E-250 large utility van with two occupants, was traveling northbound in lane one. Vehicle two (V2), a 1998 Dodge Ram with two occupants, was traveling northbound in lane one in front of V1. V2 stopped behind non-contact vehicles stopping for pedestrian traffic in the crosswalk. The driver of V1 applied the brakes and steered to the right to avoid a collision, but the front of V1 impacted the rear of V2. Both vehicles came to rest at the point of impact. V1 was towed due to damage. No one was transported. Police Reported Travel Speed is 48 kmph for V1 and 48 kmph for V2.
The driver of V1, a forty-nine-year-old male, was commuting from a job site to his home with his co-worker. He and his partner were talking prior to the crash about the aggressive actions of the driver of V2 at a stoplight a few kilometers/miles before. He said he saw V2 brake suddenly, but was unable to stop in time due to his heavy cargo load. He was well rested, very comfortable with his vehicle, in good health, and had driven this route twice daily in the two weeks before the crash, but had rarely driven it before that.
The driver of V2, a seventeen-year-old male, was driving from his girlfriend's house to his own house. He was well rested, in good health, very comfortable with his vehicle, and drives this route daily. He has been driving for nine months. He was conversing with the occupant at the time. He saw V1 just prior to impact. He had been stopped for 1-2 seconds before impact.
