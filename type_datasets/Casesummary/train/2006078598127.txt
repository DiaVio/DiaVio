This is a two-vehicle crash that occurred at dawn during daylight hours on a two lane, one-way roadway. The roadway is an exit ramp that meets at a four-way intersection. The roadway is straight, level and constructed of bituminous asphalt. The speed limit for the roadway is not posted. It was a morning crash. The roadway was dry and the sky was clear. The intersection where the crash occurred is controlled by a traffic light on colors.
Vehicle one (V-1), a 2005 Nissan Titan 4wd pickup driven by a 29 year-old male had exited the interstate traveling eastbound approaching the intersection in the #1 lane. Vehicle two (V-2), a 1993 Nissan Sentra 2 door driven by a 36 year-old male was stopped at the intersection on a red light waiting to turn left. V-1 approached the intersection and struck the rear of V-2. The vehicles came to rest in the intersection. The vehicles were removed from the intersection and stopped in the #1 eastbound lane of the cross street. The driver of V 1 stated that he had exited the interstate and was approaching the intersection. He said that he observed V-2 stopped in the #1 lane. He said that he was not paying attention and thought the light turned green. but that as he got closer to V-2, he realized that it was not moving. He said that he slammed on the brakes (no lock up) and struck the rear of V-2. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
