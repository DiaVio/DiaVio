The crash occurred in the intersection of two one-way roadways. The southbound roadway had two travel lanes, and the eastbound roadway had one travel lane. The roadway was straight and level, and it was clear with no adverse conditions. It was a weekend afternoon and the speed limit was 48kmph (30mph).
V1, a Lincoln Town Car four door was traveling southbound. V2, a 1992 Toyota Land Cruiser was traveling eastbound. At the intersection, the front of V1 contacted the left side of V2, and causing V2 to roll over three quarter-turns. V1 came to a final rest south of the intersection and V2 came to a final rest on its left side approximately 25 meters east of the intersection. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
V1, the Lincoln Town Car was driven by a 28 year-old male who did not sustain any injuries. The driver advised that he was going home from work. He stated that he was traveling straight with the green light when the front of his vehicle was struck by V2. The Lincoln was driven from the scene.
V2, the Toyota Land Cruiser was driven by a 31 year-old male who sustained incapacitating injuries and was transported to a medical facility. The driver stated to the Police that V1 had run the light and struck his vehicle. The driver was issued a citation for driving without a license. No interview was obtained and the Toyota was towed due to damage.
