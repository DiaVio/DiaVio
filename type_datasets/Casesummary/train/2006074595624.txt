This single-vehicle crash occurred on a two-lane, one-way, level, left-curved entrance ramp, leading to an access-controlled roadway. There are jersey barriers on both sides of the ramp and on the right side of the access-controlled roadway. The speed limit on this roadway is 89 kmph (55 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1993 Honda Civic, was westbound in lane two. V1 rotated clockwise and traveled off the right side of the roadway. The front of V1 struck the jersey barrier. V1 continued westbound and crossed the lanes of the access-controlled roadway. V1 then departed the roadway on the right. The front V1 then struck the jersey barrier on the right of the access-controlled roadway. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.
The driver of V1, an eighteen-year-old male, was traveling with an eighteen-year-old male passenger. They were traveling from a friend's home to a gas station. This driver stated that he was in a hurry because he was afraid that the vehicle would run out of gas. He was healthy, well rested and familiar with the vehicle. However, he stated that he was not comfortable with the vehicle because it had oversized tires installed on it. This driver also stated that he had "smoked a little marijuana earlier."
