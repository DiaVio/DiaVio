This crash occurred on a two lane undivided roadway with a posted speed limit of 64 kmph (40 mph). The roadway curved to the left with a radius of curvature of 96 meters and a superelevation of 3.7%. The roadway had a - 4.6% grade. There was a black on yellow curve warning sign with a suggested speed of 32 kmph (20 mph) prior to the curve. The weather was clear, the roadway dry, but it was dark and unlighted at the time of this weekday afternoon crash.
Vehicle 1 (V1), a 2001 Chevrolet Astro Van, was southbound on the roadway approaching the curve to the left. As V1 entered the curve it drove off the right side of the roadway into a ditch. V1 continued along the ditch until it struck a 35 cm diameter pole with its front. V1 then rotated clockwise before coming to final rest facing south. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.
The driver of V1, a belted 48-year-old male, was treated at the scene for minor injuries (the PAR indicates no injuries). The belted 54-year-old male right front seat passenger was not injured. The driver stated that as he traveled down the roadway at approximately 25-30 MPH, a deer came out from his right. He steered left and braked to avoid the deer, but went off the road and struck the pole. He indicated that he did not drive this roadway often. He stated that he took no medications but wore corrective lenses for an unknown condition. He had been talking to his passenger prior to the crash. No additional information was obtained as the driver could not be re-contacted or located to complete the interview.
