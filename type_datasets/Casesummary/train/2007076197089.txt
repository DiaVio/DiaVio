This single vehicle crash occurred in a rural mountainous area on a 3-lane not physically divided asphalt roadway with various curves and elevations. The single eastbound lane curves right then straightens out and is downhill. A rocky embankment borders the eastbound roadway. The speed limit for the area is 89 kmph (55 mph) with an advisory speed of 80 kmph (50 mph) posted for the curve. The crash occurred during daylight with no adverse weather or road conditions present.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2005 GMC Sierra 2500 Heavy Duty pickup, was eastbound when it gradually departed the eastbound road edge and impacted the rocky embankment with its front-end plane, right front tire and right rear tire. The truck rotated clockwise and came to rest facing west across the eastbound lane. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 97 kmph for V1.
The driver of V1, a seventy two (72) year-old male, stated he fell asleep and woke up after contacting the embankment. He stated that he and his wife were on their way home from visiting relatives overnight in another town. He had driven for 2 hours and was feeling drowsy but felt he could make it home safely. He is in good health and his truck was in good working condition. His wife was sleeping in the right front seat when the crash occurred.
