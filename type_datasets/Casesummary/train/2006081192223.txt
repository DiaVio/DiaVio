This single vehicle crash occurred shortly after noon on a Friday. The location was an arterial in a commercial/residential area at a three way T-intersection. The approach roadway was a four lane, undivided, bituminous, two way with curbs and sidewalks on both roadsides. There was a slight curve to the left with a radius of 667 meters (2188 feet) with a negative grade approaching the intersection. At the intersection, traffic in lane one is required to turn right with a traffic island channeling the vehicles. It was raining and the road was wet. The posted speed limit was 56 kmph (35 mph).
Vehicle one (V1), a 1981 Ford Mustang three door hatchback with one occupant, was traveling northbound in the outside right turn only lane. V1 failed to negotiate the right turn, jumped the curb and struck a metal light pole located on the traffic island head-on. V1 stopped on impact. There was no driver interview and a complete PAR was not obtained. Based on the scene evidence V1 had been traveling to fast to negotiate the turn. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.
The driver, a 17 year-old male, was injured and transported to an unknown medical facility. He was not available for interview. The driver was inexperienced and failed to slow for the turn.
