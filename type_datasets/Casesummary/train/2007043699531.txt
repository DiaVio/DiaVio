The crash occurred in the northbound lane of a two-lane, rural roadway with grass shoulders. There was a left curve in the northbound direction. The roadway had a posted speed limit of 72 kmph (45 mph) and was on level grade. The curve had a positive superelevation of 3%. Conditions were daylight and dry with clear skies. The setting was early afternoon hours on a weekday.
Vehicle one was a 2001 Sterling L9500 dump truck traveling northbound in lane one. Vehicle two was a 1998 Jeep Cherokee sport utility traveling southbound in lane one. Both vehicles approached a curve. V2 crossed the center line and struck the left rear axles of v1. V1 continued northbound and came to rest on the west shoulder of the roadway some distance past the crash site. V2 came to rest facing southbound in the northbound lane one. Police Reported Travel Speed is 72 kmph for V1 and 72 kmph for V2.
The Sterling L9500 (v1) was driven by a 38-year old male who was uninjured. He stated that he was heading to a jobsite when an oncoming vehicle crossed into his lane. He steered right and honked, but could not avoid the crash. He estimated his travel and impact speeds to be 72 kmph (45mph). The Sterling dump truck continued in service.
The Jeep Cherokee (v2) was driven by a 59-year old female who was treated on scene for possible injuries. She stated that she was heading to work when a crash occurred. She was just going straight and is not sure how the crash occurred. She estimated her travel and impact speeds to be (72 kmph) 45 mph. The Jeep sport utility was towed due to damage.
