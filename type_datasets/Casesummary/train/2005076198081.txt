This single vehicle rollover crash occurred in a rural area on a four-lane divided roadway with an extended median. The two southbound lanes curve left and are on a downhill elevation. The speed limit for the area is 88 kmph (55 mph). The crash occurred during daylight with no adverse weather or road conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2005 GMC Yukon XL, was southbound in the second lane when it departed the right road edge with its right side tires. The driver steered left and crossed both southbound lanes and departed the roadway on the left side where it traveled along the dirt and grass median for approximately 40 meters (131 feet). The driver of V1 then steered right and returned to the second southbound lane where it abruptly steered left again which caused V1 to trip, flip and roll 2 quarter turns. V1 came to rest on its top in the median facing north. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 89 kmph for V1.
The driver of V1, a fifty-five year old female, had just dropped her grandson off at school and was heading to a location to pickup an artificial limb for her husband. This driver had been driving for about forty minutes at the time of the crash. This driver does not remember what occurred during the crash but believes she blacked out. This driver was taking the following medications: Advair, Albuterol, Celebrex, Glucophage, Lorazepam. It is unknown how often this driver drives this vehicle or over this roadway.
