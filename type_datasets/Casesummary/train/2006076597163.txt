This two-vehicle crash occurred on a 5-lane asphalt state highway. The highway runs east and west, consists of two travel lanes in each direction and a two-way center turn lane. The posted speed limit on the highway is 72 kmph (45 mph) and there was full access to the highway in the area where the crash occurred. At the time of this mid-day crash, there were no adverse atmospheric conditions or road defects but traffic was heavy.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1983 Mitsubishi compact pickup, was exiting a private driveway intending to turn left. The V1 driver, a 59 year old female who was familiar with the road, had stopped to wait for traffic, then entered the roadway and the front of V1 and right side of V2 collided in the number two eastbound lane. After the impact with V2, V1 crossed the highway then came to a controlled stop off the north side of the highway. The V1 driver stated that she had looked in both directions while stopped then entered the roadway, and did not see V2 until just before the crash occurred. V1 was not towed due to damage and the driver was not transported. The Driver could not remember the names of medications she takes for high blood pressure & a heart condition. Police Reported Travel Speed is 24 kmph for V1 and 24 kmph for V2.
Vehicle two (V2), a 2005 GMC full size pickup, was eastbound in the number two lane going straight when the right side of V2 and front of V1 collided. After the impact, V2 came to a controlled stop, then turned around and returned to the crash scene. The V2 driver, a 33 year old male, stated that as he approached the area of impact he noticed that the V1 driver was looking to the right, away from him but thought that the V1 driver had seen him and was waiting for him to pass before entering the road. He added that he was surprised that V1 intended to turn left because of the heavy traffic that was present when the crash occurred. V2 was towed due to damage but the driver was not transported.
