The crash occurred at the intersection of a two-way, five-lane, straight, level, bituminous urban roadway with a posted speed limit of 56 kmph (35 mph) and a driveway. The bituminous driveway, an exit from a drive-through coffee shop, was one-way, one lane, straight and level with a statutory speed limit of 40 kmph (25 mph). Conditions at the time of the weekday, morning crash were daylight, cloudy and dry.
V1, a 2006 Toyota Sienna LE minivan driven by a 38 year-old female exiting the coffee shop, and on her way home, was turning left onto the roadway. The driver stated that she stopped, looked left, right and then left again, thought it was clear, looked straight ahead and then pulled out onto the road. At the last moment the driver of V1 saw V2 approaching on the roadway from her left. The driver of V1 accelerated and steered right in an attempt to avoid V2. The left side of V1 was struck by the front of V2 in the center lane. V1 rotated counterclockwise after the impact and came to rest facing northeast in lane one of the eastbound lanes. V1 was driven from the scene. The fully restrained driver and the 1.5 year old males in child safety seats in the third row center and right seats, denied injuries at the scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
V2, a 1993 Pontiac Grand Am GT 2 door sedan driven by a 66 year-old male on his way from home to his work to pick up papers, was westbound on the road in lane one traveling 40 mph. The driver of V2 stated that there were no other vehicles around him and V1 pulled out in front of him. He swerved left into lane two and applied the brakes (full ABS application). V2 rotated counterclockwise after the impact and came to rest facing south in lane one of the eastbound lanes. V2 was towed from the scene due to damage. The fully restrained driver was transported from the scene due to injuries.
