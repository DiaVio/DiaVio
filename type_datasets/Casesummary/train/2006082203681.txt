This two-vehicle crash occurred on a divided, level, access-controlled trafficway. The southbound roadway consists of three through lanes, curves to the right and is bordered by a jersey barrier on the right. A jersey barrier in the precrash area and then a guardrail in the area of events two, three and four are on the left (median) side of this roadway. The speed limit on this roadway is 97 kmph (60 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2004 Honda Civic, was southbound in lane one. Vehicle two (V2), a 2001 Chrysler PT Cruiser, was southbound in lane one in front of V1. V1 changed lanes to the left. The driver noticed a traffic cone in the middle of lane two and abruptly steered right to avoid it. V1 traveled off the right side of the roadway and the driver steered left. The right side of V1 struck the jersey barrier. V1 reentered the roadway, crossed all lanes and the front, then the left side struck the guardrail in the median. V1 again reentered the roadway and the front struck the left side of V2. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, a twenty-nine-year-old female, was traveling from her home to a store. She was in fairly good health, well rested and familiar with her vehicle. She travels this roadway several times each month. This driver has epilepsy and seasonal allergies and takes the medication Tegretol.
The driver of V2, a sixty-three-year-old male, was traveling from his home to a doctor's office. He was well rested and comfortable with his vehicle. He travels this route monthly. This driver has high blood pressure and takes Atenolol and Arava. He stated that he did not see V1 until just before the impact.
