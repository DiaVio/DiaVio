The crash occurred in the T-intersection of two roadways. The northbound roadway was one lane with one lane of travel. The east/west roadway had one lane in each direction. It was straight and level, and it was dark out but street lamps illuminated the roadways. It was clear with no adverse conditions. The speed limit was 48kmph (30mph), and it was during the evening hours of a weekend.
V1, a 2000 Nissan Maxima four door with one occupant, was traveling northbound in lane one. V2, a 1995 Chevrolet Lumina four door with four occupants, was traveling westbound in lane one. V1 stopped, and proceeded to make a right turn at the intersection, when the left side of V1 impacted the left side of V2. Both vehicles came to a final rest shortly after impact. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
V1, the Nissan Maxima, was driven by a 34 year-old male who reported no injuries. The driver advised that he was on his way home after visiting a friend's house. He advised that he was making a right turn at the intersection, going eastbound, when V2, traveling westbound was speeding in the middle of the road.
V2, the Chevrolet Lumina, was driven by a 59 year-old male who reported no injuries. The 70 year-old male, 60 year-old female, and a 58 year-old female occupants also reported no injuries. The driver advised that he was coming from his home and going to drop off his friends at their home. He advised that he was traveling straight in his lane when the driver of V1 made a wide turn at the intersection, crossing into the westbound lane and impacting his vehicle.
