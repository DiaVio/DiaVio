This two-vehicle crash occurred in a T-type intersection. The north/south roadway is divided and straight. The northbound lanes have a 4% upgrade and consist of two through lanes with no shoulders. The speed limit on this roadway is 64 kmph (40 mph). The east/west roadway (on the right side of the northbound lanes) is divided, curved right and has a 5% upgrade. There are two westbound lanes that are controlled by a stop sign at the intersection. The speed limit on this roadway is 40 kmph (25 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2006 Nissan Sentra, was stopped westbound, waiting to turn left (south). Vehicle two (V2), a 2006 Harley Davidson motorcycle, was northbound in lane one. The driver of V1 looked left and right and then proceeded into the intersection. The driver of V2 noticed V1 entering the intersection and applied the brakes and steered left. The front of V1 struck the right side of V2. Both vehicles came to rest on the west side of the intersection and were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, a twenty-nine-year-old female, was traveling from her home to a gym to work out. She was healthy, well rested and familiar with her vehicle. She drives this route daily. This driver stated that when she looked left, she did not notice V2, although there were no sight restrictions. She stated that she was looking for southbound traffic from the right as she entered the intersection.
The driver of V2, a twenty-four-year-old male, was traveling from his home to his friend's house. He was healthy, well rested, familiar with his vehicle and had been riding motorcycles since he was ten years old.
