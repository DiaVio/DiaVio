The crash occurred off the right-hand side a 2-lane north/southbound roadway. The north/southbound roadway was bituminous, with a 3% uphill grade, composed of one northbound lane and one southbound lane with a posted speed limit of 64 kmph (40 mph). It was afternoon on a weekday. The road was dry and the sky was cloudy. Traffic flow was light.
V1, a 1997 Lincoln Town Car with one occupant, was traveling north when it departed the roadway on the right and struck a telephone pole. V1 then spun clockwise 90 degrees before coming to rest. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.
V1, driven by a 74-year-old male who reported a complaint of pain to the chest, was incoherent and disoriented on scene and appeared to be under the influence of an unknown substance. Driver's legal counsel later reported that the Driver was under the influence of an unspecified medication and alcohol when he swerved right and struck the pole.
The witness on the PAR stated that V1 was northbound swerving from left to right and nearly striking oncoming traffic. V1 then lost control and struck a utility pole.
