This two -vehicle crash occurred on a two-lane non-divided asphalt constructed roadway. This roadway runs east to west with various curves and grades. This roadway has a bridge on the west side of the crash area. The roadway was wet and a light rain was falling at the time of this crash. The posted speed limit is 89 kmph (55 mph) with posted warning signs of the curves prior to this crash area. This crash occurred during daylight with no obvious road defects. The grade for the westbound lane is -6% with a right curve.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1995 Ford Ranger was westbound negotiating the right hand curve when it began to hydroplane. Vehicle two (V2), a 2004 Chevrolet Avalanche was eastbound and was just at the end of a long bridge. V1 began to rotate counterclockwise and departed the road on the right side. V1 reentered the road and crossed into the eastbound lanes. The front of V2 impacted the rear of V1. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 72 kmph for V1 and 72 kmph for V2.
The driver of V1, a twenty-three year old male, was heading to a job site to see if he was going to be needed. This driver had been driving for about thirty minutes at the time of the crash. This driver was conversing with the passenger about his concerns about working. This driver drives this vehicle daily and over this road on a weekly basis.
The driver of V2, a fifty-six year old male, was out driving around. This driver was on vacation and had been driving for about several hours. This driver drives this vehicle daily and this was the first time on this roadway.
