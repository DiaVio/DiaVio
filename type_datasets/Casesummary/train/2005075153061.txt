This one vehicle crash early morning during daylight hours. On a two lane undivided roadway with a curve to the right, road surface wet, level asphalt. Posted speed limit 40 kmph (25 mph).
Vehicle one (V1), a 2004 Subaru Outback station wagon driven by a 54 year-old female was traveling west in the first lane. The driver fell asleep at the wheel. She drove into the east lane over a double yellow line. She continued and impacted a parked vehicle, a 2001 Chevrolet Cavalier, with the front of her vehicle. The parked vehicle was parked in an implied parking area. V1 drove up the left side of the parked vehicle and started to roll to the right. V1, still driving west, drove off the parked vehicle onto another parked vehicle. This vehicle, a 2000 Honda CRV, was parked behind the first parked vehicle in the implied parking area. The left front wheel of V1 impacted the top of the hood. V1 rolled over two-quarter turn onto its top facing southwest where it came to rest in lane number two. After the impact between V1 and the first parked vehicle, the parked vehicle jumped a curb and continued forwards to rest partially on a sidewalk and grassy yard facing southeast on its wheels. After the impact between V1 and the second parked vehicle, that parked vehicle slid backwards and came to rest on its wheels facing east next to the curbed sidewalk. Police Reported Travel Speed is 40 kmph for V1 and 40 kmph for V2.
This researcher feels the driver was fatigued. Her son had been in town from college for the holidays and with having to get up early caused her to fall asleep at the wheel.
