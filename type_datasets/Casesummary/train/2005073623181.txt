This single vehicle crash occurred in the early morning hours on a two lane undivided roadway. This section of the roadway runs east and west and is divided by a double yellow lane line. The roadway is bituminous asphalt and is straight, level and free of roadway deficiencies. There were no adverse weather conditions and/or sight restrictions at the time of the crash. However, there was sun glare present. The speed limit for this roadway is 48 kmph (30 mph).
Vehicle one (V1) a 1993 Ford F-150 full size pickup truck driven by a 39 year-old male was traveling eastbound and crossed over the centerline to the left. V1 then exited the roadway left and entered a ditch/culvert area. This ditch has a negative 79% grade and is 17.3 meters (57 feet) in length. As V1 traveled the length of the ditch it's front impacted a rock wall at the end of the ditch. V1 then continued northeast moving over the wall and it's front struck a utility wire where it came to final rest facing northeast off of the roadway. V1 was towed from the scene and the driver transported to a local medical facility. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.
The driver stated that he was coming home from work after working 22 hours and fell asleep. He was approximately one block from home at the time of the crash. This driver further stated that he hadn't slept in two days. The last sleep period he remembers was two days ago and that was for a total of four hours. He further stated that he had worked more than 80 hours the previous week.
