This two-vehicle, rear-end crash occurred on a weekday during early morning hours at an intersection with traffic lights. The trafficway is divided, straight and level. The westbound lanes are level and consist of three through lanes and a left turn lane. The speed limit for this trafficway is 56 kph (35 mph). The weather was clear and the roads dry.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2004 Cadillac Deville, was traveling east in the third lane of traffic. Vehicle two (V2), a 2007 Ford Explorer, was traveling in lane three, ahead of V1. V2 stopped for a red traffic signal. The front of V1 struck the back of V2. The vehicles moved forward and the front of V1 again struck the back of V2. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is No PAR obtained (created) for V1 and No PAR obtained (created) for V2.
A 74-year old male drove V1. He was heading from his residence to a coffee shop. He said he looked down (inside his vehicle) for a moment. By the time he looked back up again, he saw V2 stopped in front of him. He said he never saw any brake lights illuminated on V2. He stated that he applied the brakes, but was too close to avoiding a collision. He said he was in fair health although he had been sick with flu symptoms for the five days prior to the day of the crash. He was taking an unspecified flu medication. The driver stated he does not use corrective lenses when driving.
