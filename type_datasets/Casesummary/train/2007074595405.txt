This two-vehicle crash occurred on an undivided, level, two-lane roadway. The speed limit for this roadway is 40 kmph (25 mph). It was snowing at the time of the crash and the road was snow-covered. In the southbound direction, this roadway curves to the right (the radius of curvature is 24 meters.)
Vehicle one (V1), a 1986 Jeep Cherokee, was northbound in lane one. Vehicle two (V2), a 1998 Ford Econoline van, was southbound in lane one. As V2 approached the curve, the driver applied the brakes. V2 slid into the opposing travel lane and the front struck the front of V1. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, a fifty-six-year-old male, was on his way home from work. He was healthy, well rested and familiar with his vehicle. He travels this route daily. This driver takes the medications Lisinopril, Plendil, Furosemide and Viagra. He stated that he saw V2 losing control and came to stop before the crash.
The driver of V2, a thirty-six-year-old male, was on his way back to work from a work site. He was well rested, healthy and familiar with his vehicle. He travels this route daily. This driver stated that he attempted to slow down for the curve, but slid on the snow.
