The crash occurred on a north/south two-lane undivided rural state highway with shoulders. Traffic counts of 2003 indicate the highway carried 12,393 vehicles per day in the area. The road curved to the right at it traveled northward with a radius of curvature of 469m. It is level with a 10 percent superelevation to the east and a speed limit of 80kmph (50mph). Conditions were daylight and dry on a weekday morning.
Vehicle #1 was a 1987 Subaru GL station wagon traveling north on the highway. The vehicle went over into the oncoming lanes and the driver lost control attempting to return to the appropriate lane. Vehicle #1 went into a clockwise yaw and crossed the northbound lane and shoulder, departing the right (east) side of the road. The vehicle struck a tree with its front right and continued to rotate clockwise before rolling over. The Subaru (V1) rolled one-quarter turn to its left side before coming to rest with its top against a second tree facing east. The vehicle was towed due to severe damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.
Vehicle #1 was driven by a 49-year-old female that was belted. She was transported and released from an area hospital with non-incapacitating injuries. In her statement to researcher and police she stated she was unsure what caused her to lose control of the vehicle.
