This two-vehicle collision occurred shortly after noon during daylight hours on a weekday. The crash occurred at the T-intersection of a north-south main road and an east-west side street, which intersects the east curb of the main road. Across from the side street, a private drive intersects the west curb of the north-south roadway. The speed limit is 56 kph (35 mph) on the north/west main road, and 40 kph (25 mph) on the east/west side street. At the time of the crash it was cloudy, and the roadway surface dry level asphalt was free of defects.
Vehicle one (V 1), a 1995 Toyota Corolla sedan, driven by a 19 year-old female with three passengers was traveling westbound in the first lane approaching the intersection with the intention of passing across the main roadway and entering the private drive on the opposite side. Vehicle two (V 2), a 2001 Dodge Intrepid sedan, driven by a 19 year-old female was traveling southbound in the first lane of a five-lane, two-way roadway, approaching the intersection with the intention of passing straight through. As both vehicles crossed the intersection, the front of V 1 impacted the left side of V 2. Both vehicles rotated counter-clockwise from impact. V 2 slid to final rest near the center of the roadway, facing north. V 1 came to rest facing south in the southbound lanes. V 1 was towed from th e scene. V 2 was driven from the scene. The driver of V 1 reported no activity in the vehicle. She reported that she was distracted by a personal problem, and that she was emotionally upset at the time of the crash, having participated in an argument earlier that day. The driver of V 1 reported that she did not see V 2's approach. Investigation of the scene revealed that a concrete wall on the southeast corner partially blocks the view of crossing traffic. The driver of V 1 applied the brakes when she saw V 2, but it was too late. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
