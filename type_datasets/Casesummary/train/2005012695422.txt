V1, a 1994 Buick LeSabre, was traveling eastbound on a two-lane, two-way, dry asphalt roadway with a level grade in dark-unlighted conditions. There was no posted speed limit on the roadway, which by default would be 89 kmph, or 55 mph as reported on the police accident report. V2, a 1995 Volvo WhiteGMC tractor-trailer was turning left out of a private drive onto the same roadway. V2 reported seeing V1 before he pulled out of the driveway but said that V1 was way down the road and thought he had time to pull out. The front of V1 contacted the right side of V2 before they both came to rest off the south road edge.
The driver of V1 was a 22-year old male who later reported that he could not remember anything about the crash. Police reported that he had been traveling 113 kmph (70 mph) prior to the crash because post-crash, V1's speedometer was stuck at 70 mph. The driver of V1 had no health-related problems and had not taken any medication. He does not remember where he was going at the time of the crash. He does not wear corrective lenses. Alcohol presence was reported and may have been a contributing factor. The driver of V1 was transported to a local trauma facility with serious injuries. Police Reported Travel Speed is 113 kmph for V1 and 113 kmph for V2.
The driver of V2 was a 40-year old male who reported that he had been traveling between 2-16 kmph (1-10 mph) prior to the crash. He had no health-related problems, and had taken no medication prior to the crash. He was rested and traveling to work. He does not wear prescribed lenses. He was not injured in the crash.
