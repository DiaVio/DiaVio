This two-vehicle crash occurred mid-afternoon on a four-lane, undivided, dry, bituminous roadway that runs east to west with a moderate curve towards the right. This roadway intersects with a level, dry, two-lane, two-way, bituminous roadway that runs north to south with no curve. The intersections of these two roadways in controlled by a traffic signal. There were no adverse atmospheric conditions at the time of the crash.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1993 Toyota Corolla was traveling in lane one and negotiating a curve to the right at approximately 56 kmph (35 mph). Vehicle two (V2), a 2003 Chevrolet Cavalier was just starting in traffic in an attempt to pass through the intersection at approximately 16 kmph (10 mph). As V1 crossed through the intersection, V2 entered the intersection. V1 braked with lockup and skidded into V2. The front of V1 contacted the left side of V2. Post-impact, V1 continued its westbound path of travel and pulled into a parking lot west of the intersection. V1 was not towed due to disabling damage. Post-impact V2 rotated counterclockwise and came to rest facing east in the intersection. V2 was towed due to disabling damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 56 kmph for V1 and 56 kmph for V2.
