This single vehicle crash occurred on a three-lane, one-way, westbound service road separated from the through lanes by a depressed grass separator. A cable guardrail system is located in the center of this separator. The roadway was straight, level, dry and of concrete construction. The posted speed limit is 56 kph (35 mph). Traffic was moderate at the time of the crash. This crash occurred during daylight hours with clear skies.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2002 Pontiac Grand Prix, was westbound in the second travel lane. The driver merged into the third lane, struck the south curb and departed the south edge of the roadway. The left side of the vehicle struck a wooden post from a cable/guardrail system and then the front left struck the wooden post on the backside of a W-beam guardrail. The right side of the vehicle struck the concrete foundation of an overhead sign support. The vehicle came to rest against the support facing west. The vehicle was towed. The driver refused treatment at the scene but was transported to a treatment center by a friend. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.
The driver of V1, a forty-four year old female, was heading home from work. This driver had been driving for about twenty minutes, drives the route daily and is familiar with the vehicle. The driver stated she was talking on her cell phone and listening to the radio at the time.
