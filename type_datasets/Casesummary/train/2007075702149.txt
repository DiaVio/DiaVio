This single-vehicle crash occurred on a jersey barrier-divided trafficway. The eastbound lanes are straight and level and consist of three through lanes with paved shoulders on both sides. There is a concrete wall next to the right shoulder. The speed limit on this roadway is 105 kmph (65 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2001 Chevrolet C1500 pickup, was eastbound in lane three. A non-contact vehicle was in lane two. The non-contact vehicle began to change lanes to the left. The driver of V1 steered left and the front struck the median jersey barrier. V1 then traveled to the southeast, crossed the travel lanes and the front struck the wall on the right side of the road. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 105 kmph for V1.
The driver of V1, a forty-six-year-old male, was on his way from his home to a job site. He was healthy, well rested and familiar with his vehicle. He travels this route daily. This driver stated that he "over-steered" when he saw the non-contact vehicle begin to change lanes.
