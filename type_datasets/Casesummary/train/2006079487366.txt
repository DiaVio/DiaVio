This three-vehicle collision occurred on a five-lane highway. This road is straight and level with no defects. The speed limit on this road is 104 kmph (65 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2001 Mercedes-Benz C-240 was northbound in the third lane. Vehicle two (V2), a 2005 Honda Civic was northbound in the fifth lane. Vehicle three (V3), an unknown large truck was northbound in the third lane ahead of V1. V1 began to change lanes to the left to pass V3. V2 had just changed lanes to the right. The left of V1 impacted the right of V2. V2 then impacted the left side of V3 with its right plane. V2 then departed the left side of the road and the front impacted the concrete barrier. V2 came back across all lanes of traffic and the front impacted the barrier on the right. V1 was not towed. V2 was towed due to damage. V3 never stopped at the crash. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, a twenty-two year old female, was driving home from school at the time of the crash. This driver had been driving for about twenty minutes at the time of the crash. This driver has two years of driving experience. She said she was having some type of family problem but would not go into detail about it. This driver drives this vehicle daily and over this road on a daily basis.
The driver of V2, a thirty-five year old male, was not interviewed.
The driver of V3 was not listed on the police report, but the presence of V3 was identified from the interview with the driver of V1.
V3 was not involved in first harmful event, and the critical reason was not coded to this vehicle.
