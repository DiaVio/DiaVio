This two-vehicle crash occurred mid-afternoon on a clear day at a four-way intersection controlled with a traffic signal. The roadway is four-lane, undivided, straight, level, and bituminous. This road has a speed limit of 72 kmph (45 mph). There were no adverse atmospheric conditions at the time of the crash.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2002 Chevrolet Prizm, was eastbound attempting to make a left turn on a circular green signal and head north. Vehicle two (V2), a 1998 Dodge Stratus, was westbound in lane one approaching the intersection with a circular green signal. V1 turned left at the intersection and was impacted on the right side by V2 with its front plane. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 8 kmph for V1 and 8 kmph for V2.
The driver of V1, a twenty six year old female, was heading home after leaving a friends house. This driver had four hours of sleep in the last main sleep cycle. This driver was considered fatigued. In her interview, this driver indicated that she saw a large trash truck opposite her that was turning. This driver could not see around the trash truck and turned without adequate surveillance. There were four occupants in V1 at the time of the crash.
The driver of V2, a forty-eight year old male, was heading to a local bank. This driver drove this vehicle daily and over this road daily.
