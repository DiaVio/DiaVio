This two-vehicle crash occurred in a four-legged intersection. The traffic signals for this intersection were under repair and stop signs and cones were posted. The northbound roadway is one-way, straight and level and consists of one through lane, a left turn/through lane and a right turn/through lane. The east/west roadway is undivided and straight. The eastbound travel lanes have a downhill grade and consist of one through lane and a left turn lane. The speed limit for these roadways is 48 kmph (30 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1988 Pontiac Bonneville, was northbound in lane one. Vehicle two (V2), a 2004 GMC Sierra truck, was eastbound in lane one. A non-contact vehicle, a large truck, was stopped eastbound in lane two. In the intersection, the front of V1 struck the right side of V2. V2 traveled to the northeast corner of the intersection and the front struck a pole. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, a seventy-four-year-old male, was on his way from his home to a doctor appointment. He was well rested and familiar with his vehicle. He travels this route monthly. This driver has high blood pressure, high cholesterol and arthritis and takes unknown medications. This driver stated that he entered the intersection on a green light. It is unknown if this driver realized or recognized that the signals were not functioning and had been temporarily replaced by stop signs and cones.
The driver of V2 was a forty-two-year-old male. He and his forty-four-year-old wife were on their way to work from their home. This driver was healthy, well rested and comfortable with his vehicle and passenger. He travels this route daily. This driver stated that the truck stopped in eastbound lane two blocked his view of V1 and that he never saw V1 prior to the impact.
