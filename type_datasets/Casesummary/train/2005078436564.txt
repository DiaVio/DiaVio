This is a single vehicle ran off the left side of the roadway type crash that occurred on a eastbound and westbound dry, bituminous asphalt roadway after dark a few hours before midnight. There is one eastbound lane and two westbound lanes that are separated by a continuous left turn lane. The speed limit on the roadway where the crash occurred is posted 64 kmph (40 mph). The weather was clear and there were no environmental factors that contributed to this crash. There were no line of sight restrictions and no obstructions noted. The area is not lighted and there were no street lights.
Vehicle one (V1) was a 2004 Mazda 3 sedan. V1 was driven by a 20 year-old male who was traveling westbound in the number two lane, when it traveled into the left turn lane in a southwesterly direction. The vehicle continued to travel into the eastbound lane of travel until it traveled off the left (south) side of the roadway striking a power pole located 2.6-meters (8.5 feet) off the south edge of the roadway. The driver was transported to a local medical facility from the scene and later released for lacerations to his right hand and neck and back pain. The driver was also treated for dehydration. V1 was towed from the scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is 80 kmph for V1.
A witness at the scene stated that he saw V1 traveling westbound in the #2 lane at approximately 64-72 kmph (40-45 MPH). The vehicle then traveled off the south side of the road. The driver got out of his vehicle after the crash and told the witness that he was changing a CD when his vehicle ran off the roadway and struck a pole. The witness was driving his vehicle traveling eastbound when he saw V1 travel off the roadway.
