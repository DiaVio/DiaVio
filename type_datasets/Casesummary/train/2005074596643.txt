This two-vehicle crash occurred at the intersection of a six-lane divided roadway and a two-lane one-way roadway. The six lane road has three-lanes in either direction. This road is straight and level with a speed limit of 89 kmph (55 mph). The intersecting road is an entrance ramp from a second road. This road is controlled with a stop sign at the intersection. This road curves left for traffic. This road also has a speed limit of 89 kmph (55 mph). At the time of the crash, it was dusk out with clouds and heavy rain.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1987 GMC Van was westbound in the second lane of the entrance ramp. Vehicle two (V2), a 1995 Nissan Altima was northbound in the first lane. V1 turned left from the intersection and the front of V2 impacted the right of V1. After this impact V1 rolled backwards and impacted the stop sign with its back plane. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, a forty-five year old male was heading home at the time of the crash. This driver had been driving for about twenty-five minutes at the time of the crash. This driver stated that he just rolled through the stop sign. He stated that he looked left and saw a vehicle and then went and when he looked again the vehicle was on top of him. This driver indicated that he was having a bad day. On the way to work his vehicle broke down and took a couple of hours to fix. After getting the vehicle fixed the driver was late for work because he could not find the address of the job. When the driver left to go home, is when the weather changed with heavy rain. This driver is taking Lexapro, Clonidine and an unknown high blood pressure medication. This driver drives this vehicle daily and this was his first time over this roadway.
The driver of V2, a twenty-four year old female who only gave a partial interview. Very little information is known of her pre-crash factors.
