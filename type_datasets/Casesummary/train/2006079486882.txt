This two vehicle crash occurred early morning during daylight hours. The collision occurred at a four-leg intersection. The north/south roadway is a five lane, dry, level, asphalt roadway with left turn pockets. For east/west traffic the roadway is two lanes, dry, level and made of asphalt. The north/south roadway is designated 56 kmph (35 mph). The east/west roadway is designated 40 kmph (25 mph) and is controlled by a stop sign. The weather was cloudy with very low clouds.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1990 Mercedes Benz E 300 sedan driven by a 60 year-old female, was traveling west in the number one lane. Vehicle two (V2,) a 2000 Honda Civic sedan driven by a 25 year old female, was southbound in the number two lane. As V1 entered the intersection attempting to make a left turn, the front impacted the left side of V2. V1 rotated counter clockwise and headed southwest. V1 departed the roadway on the southwest corner and impacted a building with its front. V1 came to rest facing southwest at impact with the building. V2 came to rest facing northwest in the number one and two lanes of the east/west roadway west of the north/south roadway. The driver of V1 failed to stopped for the stop sign. V1 was towed from the scene, V 2 was driven under own power. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
