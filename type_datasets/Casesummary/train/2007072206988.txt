This two-vehicle crash occurred at a four-legged, traffic signal-controlled intersection. The north/south roadway is straight, level, undivided and consists of one lane in each direction, a left turn lane at the intersection and parking lanes on both sides. The speed limit for this roadway is 40 kmph (25 mph). The roadway was wet due to previous rain. This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1998 Toyota Corolla, was northbound in lane one. Vehicle two (V2), a 2002 Mazda Protégé, was northbound in lane one in front of V1. V2 stopped at the intersection. The front of V1 struck the back of V2. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, a twenty-nine-year-old female, stated that she thought that V1 "was going to go through the intersection." She stated that she applied the brakes when she realized that V2 was stopped.
The driver of V2, a twenty-three-year-old female, was on her way to work from her home. She stated that she was familiar with her vehicle and travels this roadway daily. She stated that she did not see V1 prior to the crash.
