This two-vehicle crash occurred in the late evening hours in the middle of a four-way intersection, with three-way traffic signals. The north/south roadway upon which vehicle one (V1) was traveling consisted of two one-way lanes. The east/west roadway upon which vehicle two (V2) was traveling consisted of two lanes, one lane in each direction. The roadway surfaces were bituminous, with level grades for V1 and V2. There was an adverse atmospheric condition at the time of the crash, a heavy rainstorm, and the roadway was wet. The posted speed limit for V1 was 40 kmph (25 mph), and the posted speed limit for V2 was 56 kmph (35 mph).
V1, a 2005 Dodge Caravan, was traveling east approaching the intersection. V1 entered the intersection and impacted the left tire well area of V2, a 2000 Toyota Camry. The impact caused V1 to rotate counterclockwise, coming to an uncontrolled final rest facing north, completely blocking the westbound lane and partially blocking the eastbound lane. The impact caused V2 to also rotate counterclockwise, mounting the northeast curb, striking and knocking down the traffic control signal with its back end. V2 came to an uncontrolled final rest position in the northbound curb lane facing in a southeast direction. Police Reported Travel Speed is Unknown for V1 and Unknown for V2.
