This two crash occurred during early morning daylight hours in the eastbound travel direction of a four-lane, two-way interstate divided by a wide median. There were two eastbound travel lanes that were straight, dry, and paved asphalt. Involving a pick-up truck and a tractor-trailer. There were no environmental conditions that were a factor in this crash. The posted speed limit on the roadway is 121 kmph (75 mph)
Vehicle one (V1), a 2001 GMC Silverado pickup, driven by a 30 year-old male was traveling eastbound in the number one lane on the two-lane roadway with the cruise control set at 129 kmph (80 mph). The driver stated that he last remembers setting the cruise control at 129 kmph (80 mph). The next thing he remembers was waking up after he had struck the rear of Vehicle two (V2). V2 was a 1996 Freightliner tractor pulling one trailer, driven by a 43 year-old male who was traveling eastbound in the number one lane. After this first impact V1 began to rotate counterclockwise and continue across the two eastbound lanes of travel. V1 departed the left side of the roadway into the median. The vehicle traveled about 30 meters (98-feet) into the median and continued to rotate counterclockwise when the right side tires furrowed in the soft dirt causing the vehicle to trip and roll to it's right 2 quarter turns. From the trip point to final rest V1 traveled approximately 38-meters (125 feet) before coming to rest on it's right side facing in a northwesterly direction. The driver and sole occupant of V1 received minor injuries as a result of this crash. V1 was towed from the scene due to damage. V2 was driven to the right shoulder after the initial impact and subsequently from the scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
