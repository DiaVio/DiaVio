The crash occurred in the southbound lane of a two-lane north/southbound roadway. The roadway was bituminous and level; with a posted speed limit of 72 kmph (45 mph). It was morning on a weekday. The roads were dry and the sky was cloudy. Traffic flow was light.
V1, a 2005 Jeep Wrangler with one occupant, was traveling north when the driver experienced a seizure, crossed the center-line into the southbound lane and struck V2, a 2007 Hyundai Sonata with one occupant, on the left side. V2 attempted to avoid V1 by steering right and applying her brakes. V1 made no avoidance maneuver. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
V1, driven by a 32-year-old female who reported a complaint of pain to the back and was transported, stated that she recently began treatment for an unknown seizure disorder and as she was traveling north she experienced a seizure and blacked out. Driver recalls no further details of the crash. V1 drifted over the center-lane into the southbound lane and struck V2 on the right side. V1 made no avoidance maneuver.
V2, driven by a 29-year-old female who reported no injuries, stated that she was traveling south when V1 began to drift over the center-lane into her lane. In an attempt to avoid the collision V2 applied her brakes, steering right onto the shoulder. V2 was unable to avoid V1 and was struck on the left side. V2 spun approximately 160 degrees before coming to rest.
