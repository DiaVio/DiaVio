This two-vehicle crash occurred in a three-legged intersection. The east/west roadway is undivided, straight, has a downhill grade and consists of two lanes in each direction and a center left turn lane. The speed limit for this roadway is 72 kmph (45 mph). The north/south roadway is on the north side of the east/west roadway and is straight, level and consists of one lane in each direction. The speed limit for this roadway is 48 kmph (30 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions. Traffic was congested at the time of the crash.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2000 Jeep Cherokee, was westbound in lane two. Vehicle two (V2), a 2002 Mazda Protégé, was southbound in lane one, stopped at the stop sign. There were non-contact vehicles stopped in westbound lane one, east of the intersection. V2 entered the intersection and the front struck the front of V2. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, a thirty-nine-year-old male, was on his way to lunch from work. He was healthy, well rested and familiar with his vehicle. He travels this route daily. This driver stated that he did not see V2 until the last minute due to the stopped vehicles in lane one. He applied the brakes when he saw V2 in an attempt to avoid the crash.
The driver of V2, a twenty-eight-year-old male, was on his way from his home to a friend's house. He was well rested and had driven this vehicle occasionally in the past three months. He rarely travels this route. This driver stated that there was "a lot of stress at home with my parents." He further stated that he has depression and psychosis and was thinking about his personal problems at the time of the crash. According to this driver and his medical records, this driver takes Lithium, Klonopin, Seroquel, Adderall, Oxycontin and Hydroxyzine.
