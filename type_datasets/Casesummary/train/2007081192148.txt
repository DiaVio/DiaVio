This two-vehicle crash occurred late afternoon during daylight hours. The location was a residential arterial protected by stop signs for north/south street. There were paved shoulders on both sides and no sidewalks. The roadway was a level, bituminous, undivided two lane and two way street. The weather was clear, it was daylight and traffic was moderate. The posted speed limit is 72 kmph (45 mph).
Vehicle one (V1), a 1993 Honda Accord SE sedan driven by a sixty five (65) year old female, was southbound in lane one. A witness standing at the stop sign on the north/east corner observed the driver of V1 drinking coffee and not stopping for the traffic control sign, continuing forward into the intersection. Vehicle two (V2), a 2001 Chevrolet Suburban 4X4 SUV driven by a forty two (42) year old male, was traveling westbound approaching the intersection. V1 pulled in front of V2. The driver of V2 stated V1 was so close he did not have time to apply the brakes. The front of V2 struck the left side of V1. On impact V1 was pushed by V2 toward the southwest corner of the intersection. V1 then rolled forward and came to rest near the curb at the southeast corner of the intersection. V2 regained control and made a controlled stop south of the intersection against the west curb. V1 was towed due to damage. V2 was driven from the scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
