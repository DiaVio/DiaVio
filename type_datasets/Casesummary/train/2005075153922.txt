This two-vehicle crash occurred shortly before noon during daylight hours on a straight, dry, bituminous, physically divided, state highway with a downhill grade. The crash occurred near an intersection with a highway off ramp. The roadway runs north and south at this location. Northbound traffic consists of two through lanes that expand into three through lanes just prior to the intersection. There is a traffic control light with pedestrian crossings and the speed limit on the highway is posted at 64 kmph (40 mph). There were no adverse atmospheric conditions present at the time.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1996 Ford F-150 pickup driven by a 35 year-old male with one passenger, was traveling northbound in lane one. Vehicle two (V2), a 2005 Buick Terraza driven by a 62 year-old female with one passenger, was also traveling northbound in lane one stopped at a red light. The driver of V1 was unable to stop in time and the front of V1 contacted the rear of V2. After the impact, V1 rotated counterclockwise and came to rest in lane one facing northwest. V2 was pushed forward and came to rest in lane one facing north. Both vehicles were towed from the scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
