This single vehicle crash occurred on a three-lane, asphalt roadway. This roadway runs east to west with the eastbound traffic consisting of two lanes. The westbound lane has a downgrade of seven percent. The westbound lane curves left just prior to the crash site and then curves right. There are guardrails on both sides of the roadway in this area. This crash occurred during daylight hours with heavy rain at the time. The posted speed limit is 105 kph (65 mph) with a speed advisory of 80 kph (50 mph).
Vehicle one, a 2002 PT Chrysler, was westbound in lane one. Vehicle one began to rotate counterclockwise as it entered the right curve. Vehicle one traveled across the eastbound lanes and the front struck the left side guardrail. The vehicle continued to rotate and the right side struck the same guardrail. The vehicle then rotated across to the right side of the roadway and the back struck the right side guardrail. Vehicle came to rest in the westbound lane, facing south and was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 72 kmph for V1.
The driver of vehicle one, a sixty-eight-year-old male, was traveling with his sixty-three-year-old wife from their cabin in the mountains to their home. He was well rested and is very familiar with his vehicle as well as his route. He and his wife were discussing the heavy rain and their concern about the water running across the road. He stated that he was traveling at approximately 72 kph (45 mph) and the windshield wipers were on at maximum speed. This driver stated that he applied the brakes and attempted to steer into the rotation, but was unable to regain control.
