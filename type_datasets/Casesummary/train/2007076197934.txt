This single-vehicle crash occurred on a two-lane asphalt roadway in a rural area with various curves and elevations. The eastbound lane curves left and is uphill. In the area of impact, both lanes of travel have dirt / rock embankments bordering them. Prior to the crash is a warning sign: "watch for rocks". The speed limit is 104 kmph (65 mph). The crash occurred at night during a pro-longed rainstorm. The roadway was wet and many large rocks had fallen into the roadway from the embankments. It was dark in the early evening hours.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1998 Saturn SW2 station wagon, driven by a forty eight (48) year old male was eastbound traveling at 89 kmph (55 mph) when it contacted a large rock with its undercarriage. The driver lost control and departed the left road edge rotating counter-clockwise where it contacted the westbound embankment with its right front-end plane. V1 continued to rotate counter-clockwise and came to rest on the roadside facing east. The driver stated to police that he came upon the rock in the road and had no time to avoid it. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 89 kmph for V1.
