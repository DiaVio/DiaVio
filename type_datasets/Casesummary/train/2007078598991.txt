This two-vehicle crash occurred in a three-legged, traffic signal-controlled intersection. The east/west roadway is straight and level and consists of two lanes in each direction and a center left turn lane. The speed limit on this roadway is 56 kmph (35 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions. Traffic was congested at the time of this crash.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1998 Ford Windstar, was eastbound in lane two. Vehicle two (V2), a 2002 Jeep Cherokee, was eastbound in front of V1. Both vehicles were stopped for a red traffic signal. When the signal turned green, both vehicles proceeded into the intersection. V2 stopped due to traffic stopped ahead and the front of V1 struck the back of V2. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 21 kmph for V1 and 21 kmph for V2.
The driver of V1, a thirty-seven-year-old female, was on her way home from work with two male teenage passengers in the back seat. This driver was healthy, well rested and comfortable with her vehicle and passengers. She travels this route daily. This driver stated that she was ten-to-fifteen feet behind V2 and applied the brakes when she realized that V2 had stopped.
The driver of V2, a twenty-four-year-old female, was traveling from her home to a shopping mall. She was well rested, healthy and familiar with her vehicle. She drives this route daily. This driver stated that she realized that V1 was not going to be able to stop, but was unable to move forward due to other traffic.
