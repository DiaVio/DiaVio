This crash took place during the early evening hours of a weekend on a two-lane, undivided, rural roadway. The bituminous roadway was dry and level, with several curves and a posted speed limit of 35mph (56kmph). The traffic flow was normal and there were no adverse conditions at the time of the crash.
V1, a 2000 Plymouth Neon, was northbound. V2, a 2003 Oldsmobile Alero, and V3, 2006 Ford Crown Victoria Police interceptor, were southbound negotiating a curve left. Just after V2 and V3 exited the curve, V1 departed its travel lane over the center lane line and entered the oncoming lane. V1 made contact to V2 and both vehicles began to rotate counter clockwise. As V1 rotated it contacted V3's left side with its front. All vehicles came to final rest in the roadway facing various directions. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, a 78-year-old male, refused to be interviewed. He was transported with incapacitating injuries and his vehicle was towed due to damage.
The driver of V2, a 68 year-old female, was returning home after visiting a relative. She stated that had been cautious of her travel speed because there was a police vehicle traveling behind her. She stated that she saw the oncoming vehicle cross into her lane and she braked but was unable to avoid the crash. She was transported with incapacitating injuries and V2 was towed due to damage.
The driver of V3, a 32 year-old male, was off duty he was en route to get dinner with his son. He stated that he observed V1 cross the centerline and he applied brakes while steering right to avoid the initial contact, however he could not avoid V1 as it spun toward him. Neither him nor his 9-year-old passenger were injured but V3 was towed due to damage.
