The crash occurred on the eastbound lanes of a 10-lane east/westbound interstate highway divided by a positive barrier. There were five lanes in each direction. The asphalt roadway was dry, straight and level with a posted speed limit of 89kph/55mph. The crash happened during early evening on a weekend day under clear sunny skies and with heavy traffic flow.
V1, a 2000 Chrysler Cirrus four door with one occupant was traveling eastbound in lane five. According to the police report V1 veered suddenly to the right for no apparent reason and it drove across the four lanes to its right and office the right edge of the road. V1's front right stuck a 10cm post on the right roadside (Event 1). V1 then continued traveling southeast down a slight embankment with a 17% slope and then up an embankment with a 46% slope where the front of V1 struck the embankment causing it to roll over longitudinally to the left. The vehicle rolled four one-quarter turns and landed on its wheels facing west on the roadside approximately 38m southeast of the first impact. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.
V1, the Chrysler Cirrus, was driven by a 48-year old female who was not injured. The driver would not respond to numerous phone calls or a hand delivered written request from the researcher for an interview. At he scene the driver was observed, by the police officer and the researcher, to be under the influence of intoxicants. She later refused a BAC test. The driver was also very upset and angry about a recent breakup with a boyfriend. The police officer told the researcher that the witness had indicated that prior to departing the roadway the driver appeared to be arguing with someone but she was alone in the car. The researcher was unable to locate the witness at either of two phone numbers listed for her.
