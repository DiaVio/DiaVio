This single vehicle rollover occurred on a divided four-lane road. This road has a concrete Jersey barrier separating the two travel lanes. This road has curves with various grades. The posted speed is 105 kmph (65 mph) with warning signs lowering the speed to 64 kmph (40 mph) just prior to this rollover. This crash occurred during the late afternoon with rain coming down and the road was wet. The driver stated that he had his wipers on in the intermediate speed.
Vehicle one (V1) was a 2004 Honda 4 door sedan driven by a thirty three (33) year old male with one passenger. V1 was southbound in lane one and had just exited a right hand curve when this vehicle began to rotated clockwise. The driver applied left hand steering and braking to compensate for the rotation but the vehicle over rotated and exited the roadway to the right hand side. V1 slid sideways off the road edge where it dug into the wet dirt shoulder and tripped rolling 4 quarters turns coming to rest on its wheels. Both occupants exited under their own power. V1 was towed for damages . Police Reported Travel Speed is 97 kmph for V1.
According to the police report the driver was traveling 97 kmph (60 mph). The driver was having a social conversation with the passenger and they were on a leisure trip from home to a city 158 kilometers (98 miles) from where the crash occurred.. The tires were properly inflated with sufficient tread depth.
