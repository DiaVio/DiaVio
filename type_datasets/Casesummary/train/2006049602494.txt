This two-vehicle crash occurred on a weekday in August during the early afternoon hours involving two compact utility vehicles at a T-intersection. The speed limit for both roadways is 48 kmph (30 mph). The north/south road is a 4-lane, two way, undivided asphalt roadway that has a 3.3 % upgrade to the north. The ease/west road is a two way, two lane undivided level asphalt roadway that is straight and level. The roadway intersects the north/south road from the west. Traffic on the east/west road is controlled by a stop sign. The road surface was dry and there were no detrimental atmospheric conditions at the time of the crash.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1995 Land Rover Discovery with one occupant, was traveling east in the first lane of the westbound lanes. Vehicle two (V2), a 1997 Nissan Pathfinder with four occupants, was traveling north in the second lane of traffic. V1 stopped at the stop sign before proceeding into the intersection with the intention to turn left into the southbound lanes. He waited for a gap in the traffic flow to turn left, letting several vehicles traveling in the southbound lanes pass by. When V1 entered the intersection to attempt its left turn, the front of V1 impacted the front of V2. Both vehicles came to rest within the intersection. The crash was at a low speed and the frontal airbags of V2 did not deploy. Both vehicles were towed from the scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
