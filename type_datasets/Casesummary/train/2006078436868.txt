This two-vehicle rear end crash that occurred mid-morning in the westbound lane of a two-lane, two way roadway separated by a centerline stripe. The crash occurred in a four-way intersection. The intersection has one northbound and one south lane. There is one southbound and one northbound lane. The intersection is controlled by a flashing traffic light and a stop sign for northbound and southbound traffic. The posted speed limit for both roadways is posted 80 kmph (50 mph).The road surface is asphalt, dry and level.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2001 Toyota Tacoma 4X2 pick-up driven by a 19 year-old female was traveling westbound in the number one lane approaching the intersection. Vehicle two (V2), a 1994 Ford Ranger 4X4 pickup driven by a 38 year-old male was stopped in the intersection waiting to make a left turn to travel southbound. The driver of V1 looked to her left prior to the intersection. She then looked ahead and saw V2 stopped in front of her. V1 skidded for about 20 meters (66 feet) before it struck the rear of V2. V1 came to rest in the intersection facing west. V2 went forward and the driver brought the vehicle to a controlled stop west of the intersection off the north side of the westbound lane. V1 was towed due to damage. V2 was driven from the scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is 89 kmph for V1 and 89 kmph for V2.
