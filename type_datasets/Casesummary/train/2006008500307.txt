This crash occurred at a four-way intersection. The north and southbound lanes did not have traffic control devices, the east and west lanes had stop signs. The westbound lane was straight with a slight positive grade and a posted speed limit of 40 kmph (25 mph). The southbound lane had an uphill grade of greater than 3 percent and a posted speed limit of 56 kmph (35 mph). The point of impact was level. It was late afternoon on a weekday and traffic flow was moderate. Conditions were clear.
V1 was a 2002 Buick Le Sabre, driven by a 73-year-old male. V2 was a 1998 Ford Ranger, driven by a 31-year-old male. V3 was a 2001 Chevrolet Silverado, driven by a 33-year-old male. V1 was stopped at a stop sign traveling westbound waiting to cross through the intersection. V1 began to pull through the intersection and was struck on the right side by V2, which was traveling southbound. V1 and V2 contacted each other a second time creating a side-slap hit. V1 was then pushed through the intersection and contacted V3, which was stopped at a stop sign facing eastbound. Police Reported Travel Speed is 32 kmph for V1 and 32 kmph for V2.
V3 was not involved in the first harmful event. Therefore, no Critical Pre-crash Event or Critical Reason was coded to the driver of this vehicle. No associated factors were coded.
