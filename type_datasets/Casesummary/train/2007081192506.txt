This two-vehicle crash occurred during the early evening hours. The location was an interstate freeway in an urban area. The roadway was a five lane concrete surface with the inside lane designated for HOV vehicles. There were paved shoulders on both sides and a center concrete wall median. It was dark, but street lights were on. The weather was cloudy and the roadway was dry. The posted speed limit was 97 kmph (60 mph).
Vehicle one (V1) was a 2004 Toyota Echo four door sedan. The driver of V1 was a twenty four (24) year-old female who was traveling northbound in lane three at a speed greater then the posted speed. V1 struck the left rear corner of Vehicle two (V2), a 2006 Honda CR-V LX hatchback. V2 was driven by a sixty (60) year old female. The driver of V1 attempted to steer left to avoid the impact and lost control of the vehicle. V1 rolled to its right while it traveling toward the left roadside. V1 came to rest on its top in the HOV lane facing west. V2 was traveling at a driver reported speed of approximately 105 kmph (65 mph) in lane three. The driver stated she did not see V1 prior to impact. She stated after the impact she regained control of her vehicle and made a controlled stop on the inside shoulder. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
