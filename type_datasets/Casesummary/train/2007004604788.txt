The crash occurred off the right side of an undivided east/west two-lane roadway. The road was dry and bituminous with a 2.5 percent downhill grade. The posted speed limit was 40 kmph (25 mph). It was during the week in early evening and traffic was very light. It was dark out but the roadway was illuminated by streetlamps.
V1, a 1997 Jeep Grand Cherokee utility vehicle with one occupant, was traveling west when the driver lost consciousness, causing V1 to drift off the road to the right. At this time V1 struck V2, an unoccupied 1993 Yamaha ATV, causing V2 to strike a trailer attached to V3, an unoccupied 1994 Honda Passport utility vehicle. V1 then proceeded to strike V4, an unoccupied 1990 Mazda RX-7 2-door coupe, pushing it 8 meters before coming to rest. V2, V3 and V4 were all unoccupied and parked off the roadway on the right-hand side of the road. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
V1, driven by a 55-year-old male who was transported to the hospital, stated that he was on his way home from the store when his vehicle began to shake and then the next thing he remembers is being helped from the vehicle by emergency personnel. The driver claimed to have applied brakes when the vehicle began to shake. The driver was observed to be incoherent at the scene and emergency personnel reported that the driver was having a diabetic episode.
