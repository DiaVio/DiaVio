This two-vehicle crash occurred mid-afternoon during daylight hours at the intersection of a dry, downhill, two-lane, bituminous roadway that intersected with a dry, uphill, two-lane, bituminous roadway. A yield sign controls the westbound side of the intersection. The Southbound side of the intersection had no traffic control devices. The northbound and eastbound side of the intersection is controlled by stop signs. For southbound traffic there is a curve to the left before the intersection. The roadway is very narrow.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1997 Chevrolet Lumina was traveling westbound. Vehicle two (V2), a 1998 International truck pulling a trailer was southbound. V2 was turned left at the intersection. As V2 made its turn the trailer departed its lane to the left and the front of V1 impacted the left side of the trailer. Both vehicles came to rest at impact. V1 was towed due to damage. V2 was not towed from the scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is 0 kmph for V1 and 0 kmph for V2.
The driver of V1, a forty-year-old female was not interviewed. A witness to the crash was behind vehicle one and witnessed vehicle one just keep pulling into the intersection while V2 was making the turn.
The driver of V2, a forty one-year old male gave a partial interview and indicated that as he was making the turn, V1 just kept moving further and further into the intersection. This driver also indicated that the road is not wide enough to make this turn.
