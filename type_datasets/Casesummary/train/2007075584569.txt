This single-vehicle crash occurred on a rural roadway with no traffic controls. The roadway is undivided with one lane in each direction. The roadway for southbound traffic was on a left curve, bituminous, level and dry, with a posted speed limit of 64 kmph (40 mph). This crash occurred in the late afternoon with light traffic flow.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1989 Oldsmobile Delta 88 with one occupant, was southbound in lane one, negotiating the left curve. The driver was traveling at a police reported speed of 80 kmph (50 mph) and ran off the right side of the road. V1 traveled 45.5 meters (149 feet) and impacted three large rocks and embankment with front end. V1 started to rotate clockwise into a ditch. V1 rolled to its left coming to rest on its top facing south in a ditch. V1 was towed from the scene of the crash, due to severe damage to the top. Police Reported Travel Speed is 80 kmph for V1.
The driver of V1 is a seventeen-year-old female. Little is known about the driver, because her mother and father would not let the researcher talk to her on scene. There was no success in reaching her later.
