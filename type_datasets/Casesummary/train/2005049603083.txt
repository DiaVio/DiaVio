A single vehicle crash happened during early afternoon daylight hours. The location was a two-lane undivided (two-way traffic) roadway. The weather was clear and the road surface concrete and dry. The posted speed limit was 48 kmph (30 mph).
The only vehicle in this crash, a 1999 Chevrolet Pick up truck, was traveling south in the first lane of the roadway. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.
The vehicle drove off the road to its right. Its front impacted a utility pole located west of the roadway. The front right corner initially contacted the pole at a high rate of speed. As the pole was pushed rearward and snapping the wires up top, the pole came lower towards the truck as it was rotating counter clockwise. The wires got low enough to snag on the left side of the vehicles roof rack. The vehicle subsequently rolled over 1/4 turn onto its right side. It came to rest lying on its right side in the southbound lane, located south of the pole it initially impacted. The vehicle was towed from the scene. The driver was transported to a medical facility and released.
The driver a 54 year-old male who fainted and passed out from a medical condition, a severe tooth ache.
