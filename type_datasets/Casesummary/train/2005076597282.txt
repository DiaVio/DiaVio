This single vehicle crash occurred mid-afternoon on a three-lane asphalt constructed roadway. This roadway runs east to west with the east lane having a passing lane. This roadway goes up hill for the eastbound with a left hand curve. The posted speed limit is 105 kmph (65 mph).
Vehicle one (V1), a 2002 Buick Century sedan driven by a 22 year-old male was heading east and had just entered the passing lane area on the roadway which went from a one lane to a two lane roadway. The driver stated that he saw a car coming up from behind at a high rate of speed, so he steered right to get into the right slower lane. As he began to get across the lane the other car went around the left side and he thought it was going to hit his car, so he steered right onto the white fog edge line. As he crossed the fog line his car went over some cinder gravel and the back end started to rotated counter-clockwise crossing all the lanes of traffic in a counter-clockwise yaw. The vehicle went off the left side of the road and down a slope into a drainage ditch. It hit the far side embankment bounced up and rotated right coming to final rest facing south. V1 was towed from the scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is 105 kmph for V1.
The driver stated that he drives this roadway a couple a times a year and that V1 does not belong to him. He drives it occasionally and is somewhat familiar with its handling characteristics. The driver was not familiar with the vehicle and panicked when a non-contact vehicle passed him. He had been driving for over two hours when the crash occurred.
