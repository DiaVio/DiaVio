This two-vehicle collision occurred in a busy intersection. The intersection is controlled by stoplights on colors with pedestrian signals. The pre-crash roadway for vehicle one is a two-way, six-lane roadway with a center median. The pre-crash roadway is a slight downhill grade and curved to the left, with bituminous paving. The posted speed limit for the roadway is 64 kmph (40 mph). The pre-crash roadway for vehicle two is a two-way, three-lane, undivided roadway. Access to this section of roadway is not controlled; the pre-crash roadway has a slight downhill grade, and is straight with bituminous paving. This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1987 Chevy Blazer was northbound in the second lane. Vehicle two (V2), a 1984 Chevy Blazer was turning from west to south at the intersection. V2 was stopped at the stoplight and then entered the roadway on a green light. The front of V1 impacted the left of V2. V1 and V2 then side slapped with the right of V1 impacting the left of V2. V1 was not towed due to damage. V2 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, a twenty-year-old male had just dropped a friend off at a nearby lake and was heading home. This driver had only been driving for a few minutes at the time of the crash. This driver stated that his health is good but is getting over a minor cold. He has been taking Tylenol for headaches with this cold. He has no hearing or vision restrictions, and he does experience work related stress as he works for a temp agency, and it is stressful finding full-time work. He was not in a hurry. This driver stated that he was looking for his cell phone that was in the center console and did not see the light change. This driver drives this vehicle daily and over his road on a daily basis.
