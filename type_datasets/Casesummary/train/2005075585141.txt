This single vehicle crash occurred during daylight hours on a wet, level, physically divided, seven-lane, access-controlled roadway. The roadway runs east and west at the location of the crash and eastbound traffic has three through lanes and one merge lane from the right. The speed limit on the roadway is 105 kph (65 mph) and it was raining at the time of the crash.
Vehicle one, a 2002 Chevrolet Cavalier, was eastbound in lane three on a straight section of the road entering a left curve. The left rear tire blew out and the driver applied the brakes. The vehicle began to rotate counterclockwise and the driver steered right. The vehicle rotated clockwise, traveled across lanes two and one and then off the right shoulder. The back left struck the concrete barrier and the vehicle came to rest facing west on the south shoulder. Police Reported Travel Speed is 89 kmph for V1.
The driver of vehicle one, a twenty-eight-year-old male was driving from work to lunch, a route that he travels frequently. He was well rested and comfortable with the vehicle and the roadway, although he was traveling slower than normal due to the rain. This driver stated that as he was driving, he felt his tire blow out. This driver was told by the officer that he may have hit a piece of debris on the roadway.
