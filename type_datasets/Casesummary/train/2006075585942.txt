This single-vehicle crash occurred on a dry, uphill, physically divided, four-lane, left curve highway. The highway usually runs north and south, but at this location, it runs east and west and has two through lanes in each direction. The speed limit on the highway is 105 kmph (65 mph) and there were no adverse weather conditions at the time.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2005 Ford F-150 was westbound in lane one negotiating a left curve. The vehicle ran off the right side of the road onto a paved shoulder. V1 impacted a guardrail and delineator with it front. The vehicle continued 4.6 meters (15 feet) still intact with the guardrail until final rest. V1 came to rest against the guardrail on its wheels facing west. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 105 kmph for V1.
The driver of V1, a twenty-four year old male, was driving from one supply house to another supply house. This driver rarely drives on this road. He indicated in his interview that he thought he missed his exit. In his description the driver explained that a truck that was in front of him pulled into his lane and then he blanked out. This driver was not transported to a medical facility; as such there are no medical records. This driver sleeps about six hours a night and had been up for thirteen hours at the time of the crash. Fatigue is considered a factor for this driver. This driver drives this vehicle on a daily basis.
