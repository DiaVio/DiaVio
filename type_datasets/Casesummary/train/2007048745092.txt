This single-vehicle crash occurred on an undivided, two-lane, level roadway. The southbound travel lane curves to the right (the radius of curvature is 563 meters and the superelevation is two percent). There are ditches with dirt embankments outside of the grass shoulders on both sides of this roadway. The speed limit on this roadway is 72 kmph (45 mph). It was dark at the time of the crash with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2001 Toyota Camry Solara, was southbound in lane one. V1 traveled off the right side of the roadway. V1 reentered the roadway, crossed the center line and traveled off the left side of the roadway, rotating counterclockwise. The front and then the back struck the embankment. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 105 kmph for V1.
The driver of V1, a sixteen-year-old male, was on his way home from work. He was well rested, healthy and familiar with his vehicle. He travels this route daily. This driver had been driving for one year prior to the crash. He stated that he was looking down at the gear shifter and traveled off the right side of the roadway. He then steered left and applied the brakes. He then lost control of the vehicle.
