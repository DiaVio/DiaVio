This single-vehicle collision occurred in the early afternoon during daylight hours. The collision occurred on a rural two-way, two-lane undivided roadway, in lane one. The roadway is a -19% downhill grade, straight with concrete paving. The weather was clear and the roadway was dry. The roadway has a posted speed limit of 40 kmph (25 mph).
Vehicle one (V1), a 2000 Lincoln Town Car sedan driven by a sixty eight (68) year old male with one passenger, was traveling westbound in lane one. V1 departed the roadway via the right shoulder, and the front of V1 impacted a large rock and tree, The vehicle became wedged between the tree and the roadway. V1 was towed from the scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.
The only pre-existing condition the driver of V1 has, is he had surgery on his knee and has been experiencing numbness in his foot the last few days. The drivers right foot went numb as he was driving and he could no longer feel the pedal. The driver takes the medications: Benadryl, Lovastatin and Atenolol. At the time of the collision the driver was conversing with the passenger. The driver was startled by a car that came up behind him and honked at him as he slowed in the roadway to change positions with his wife. He then intentionally steered into the tree to avoid a worse situation.
