The crash occurred in the eastbound lanes of a two-lane, divided, rural highway with a grass median. The right side of the road had a narrow shoulder and was bordered by a concrete barrier wall. The roadway had a posted speed of 105 KMPH (65 MPH) and a flat grade. Conditions were daylight with cloudy skies. The crash occurred mid-morning hours of a weekday.
Vehicle one was 1996 Toyota Corolla sedan. Vehicle two was a 2001 Dodge Ram 1500 pickup. V1 was traveling in the first lane. V2 was traveling in the second lane. V1 began merging left into V2's lane. V2 moved left towards the shoulder. V1 swerved right, departing the roadway and striking the barrier wall with its right front corner. Both vehicles then reentered the roadway and collided in the second lane, V2's right rear to V1's front left. V2 then departed the roadway to the right, climbing the barrier wall and overturning one complete rotation, coming to rest on its undercarriage. V1 departed the roadway to the right and struck the barrier wall with its left side, coming to rest against the wall facing west. Police Reported Travel Speed is 105 kmph for V1 and 105 kmph for V2.
Vehicle one was driven by a 33-year old female who was transported to a local trauma center. She could not be located for comment regarding the crash. V1 was towed due to damage.
Vehicle two was driven by a 45-year old male who suffered non-incapacitating injuries and refused treatment. He stated that V1 switched lanes towards him and he steered left to avoid the crash, causing his vehicle to lose control. He estimated his speed to be 105 KMPH (65 MPH) during travel and impact. V2 had some cargo in the pickup bed spill during the rollover. V2 was towed due to damage.
