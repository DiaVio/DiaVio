This two-vehicle crash occurred in a four-legged, traffic signal-controlled intersection. Both roadways are straight and divided by a curbed median. The eastbound approach has a downhill grade and consists of two through lanes and a left turn lane. The southbound approach has an uphill grade and consists of one through lane, a right turn lane and two left turn lanes. The speed limit for these roadways is 64 kmph (40 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1997 Buick LeSabre, was eastbound in lane one. Vehicle two (V2), a 1988 Chrysler Lebaron, was southbound in lane two. In the intersection, the front of V1 struck the right side of V2. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, a seventy-four-year-old female, was not interviewed. No further information is available for this driver. This driver applied the brakes (skidding longitudinally) prior to entering the intersection.
The driver of V2, a twenty-year-old female, was traveling from her home to a gas station with her two-month-old son. This driver was healthy, well rested and comfortable with her vehicle and passenger. She travels this route daily. This driver stated that she did not see V1 prior to the crash.
