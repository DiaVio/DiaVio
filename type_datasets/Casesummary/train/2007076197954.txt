This two-vehicle crash occurred on a two-lane asphalt roadway in a rural area with various curves and elevations. The eastbound lane is straight and uphill. The speed limit is 104 kmph (65 mph). The crash occurred during late morning. There was fog and snow present. The roadway had packed snow and a patch of black ice on it in the area of the crash.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2005 Chevrolet C-2500 pickup driven by a thirty seven year old male with one passenger. V1 was eastbound following Vehicle two (V2), a 2001 Chevrolet Cavalier sedan. driven by a seventy three (73) year old female with one passenger. V2 lost control on the slick roadway and the driver of V2 steered right and tapped the brakes. V2 spun completely around in a clockwise direction. The driver of V1 steered left to attempt to avoid V2; however, the front of V2 impacted with the front of V1. Both came to rest in the area of impact. Police Reported Travel Speed is 80 kmph for V1 and 80 kmph for V2.
The driver of V2 was traveling with a friend to visit her children in another town. She drives on this road every 2 or 3 months. She stated she had slowed to 48 kmph (30 mph) while traveling through a foggy area, then hit the icy patch of road and lost control. She saw V1 try to avoid her by steering to his left but could not avoid the impact. V2 was not inspected due to its being towed out of the study area. It is unknown if there were any vehicle condition related factors. The driver was comfortable with the vehicle condition and stated the tires, brakes and other components were in good condition.
