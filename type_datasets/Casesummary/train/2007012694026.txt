This crash occurred in the morning of a weekend on the left roadside of two-lane undivided rural roadway. The north/south roadway consisted of one lane in each direction and flanked by paved shoulders. The bituminous roadway was straight and level with a posted speed limit of 89 kmph (55 mph). The weather was cloudy and damp with freezing temperatures causing the roadway to be covered with a sheet of black ice.
V1, a 2005 Toyota Tacoma pick-up was northbound in lane one. V1 attempted to pass a slower moving vehicle and lost control. V1 began rotating counter-clockwise and departed the roadway to the left. The right side of V1 struck a ditch and than a signpost before rolling four quarter turns. V1 came to final rest on its wheels facing west in a field. V1 was towed from the scene due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.
The driver of V1, a 24-year-old male, claimed to be in good health despite taking medications for high blood pressure and acid refluxes disease. He stated he was on his was to a local lake to go fishing that morning and was trying to pass a slower moving vehicle when he felt his vehicle start to rotate. He stated he counter steered but could not bring his vehicle under control. He left the roadway and struck a signpost. He stated sometime between leaving the roadway and contacting the post his vehicle started to rollover, but he is not sure of how many times he rolled. He suffered non-incapacitating injuries but refused transport from the scene. He stated that his father took him to a local ER for treatment later that morning.
