A three-vehicle crash occurred in the southbound lanes of a divided five-lane roadway. There are two through lanes for southbound and two through lanes and one left turn lane for northbound. This was a straight, level, bituminous roadway with a posted speed limit of 64 kmph (40 mph). It was rush hour traffic on a weekday and the road was dry and the sky was clear. Traffic flow was congested, with stop and go activity.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1999 two-door Honda Civic, with one occupant, was southbound in lane one. Vehicle two (V2), a 1995 Ford Aero star Minivan, with one occupant, was southbound in lane one in front of V1. Vehicle three (V3), a 2001four-door Nissan X-Terra, with one occupant, was southbound in lane one in front of V2. A non-contact vehicle in front of V3 was traveling southbound in lane one. The non-contact vehicle stopped for another non-contact pick-up truck in front of it that was turning into a driveway from lane one. V3 stopped, V2 was slowing down to stop and V1 was going straight. V1's front impacted to V2's back. V2 was pushed forward and its front impacted V3's back. V1 came to rest near impact on its wheels facing south in lane one. After the initial impact V2 was pushed forward and came to rest on its wheels facing southwest partially in lane one and partially on the curb. After its first impact V3 continued off the road and came to a controlled rest on its wheels facing south in a private lot. Police Reported Travel Speed is 64 kmph for V1 and 64 kmph for V2.
