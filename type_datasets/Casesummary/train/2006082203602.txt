This two-vehicle crash occurred in a four-legged, traffic signal-controlled intersection. The east/west roadway is straight, level, undivided and consists of two through lanes in each direction. Lane two is also a left turn lane. The speed limit on this roadway is 48 kmph (30 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2000 Volkswagen Beetle, was stopped eastbound in lane two. Vehicle two (V2), a 1997 GMC Savana G-3500 van, was westbound in lane one. A non-contact vehicle was stopped westbound. V1 turned left in the intersection and the right side was struck by the front of V2. V1 rotated clockwise and the left side struck a curb. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, a sixty-year-old female, was traveling from a store to her home. She was well rested, in good health and familiar with her vehicle. She rarely drives on this roadway. She has high blood pressure and takes Hydrochlorothiazide and an unspecified estrogen medication. This driver stated that she could not see westbound lane one. The driver of the non-contact vehicle indicated that the driver of V1 could safely turn left.
The driver of V2, a fifty-six-year-old female, was traveling was returning to work after a trip downtown to make a delivery. She was healthy, well rested and familiar with her vehicle. She travels this route daily. This driver stated that she has high enzyme levels and takes Plavix and aspirin. She also stated that she did not see V1 until it was too late to attempt to avoid the crash.
