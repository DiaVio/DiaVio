This single vehicle daytime collision occurred during early evening hours. The collision occurred on the northbound lanes of a state highway. The highway is dry, concrete, curves left, has an uphill slope, and consists of four lanes. A concrete shoulder borders the west edge of the highway. West of the western shoulder exists a raised concrete center divider. The center divider separates the northbound lanes from the southbound lanes. A concrete shoulder borders the east edge of the highway. East of the eastern shoulder exists a hillside. North of the hillside exists a metal guardrail that runs along the east edge of the eastern shoulder. The highway is designated a 104 kmph (65 mph) zone.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1998 Toyota Camry four-door sedan driven by a fifty three (53) year old female, was traveling northbound in the number two lane. As V1 was traveling, its right rear tire suffered sidewall separation. The vehicle went out of control and traveled towards the eastern shoulder. The front plane of V1 contacted the hillside as it traveled unto it. V1 then contacted a metal guardrail with its undercarriage as it proceeded to roll left. V1 came to rest facing northbound on the eastern shoulder. V1 suffered rollover damage and was towed from the scene. Driver medication Lipitor. Police Reported Travel Speed is 105 kmph for V1.
