This two-vehicle crash occurred at an intersection with traffic lights. The speed limit for the roadway is 54 kph (35 mph). The roadway is a seven-lane divided (no positive barrier) asphalt downhill for northbound traffic. The roadway is an eight-lane (with one right turn only lane) for southbound traffic. The road surface was dry and there were no detrimental atmospheric conditions at the time of the crash. This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one, a 1986 Chevrolet Monte Carlo was northbound in the fourth lane attempting to turn left. Vehicle two, a 2001 Mercedes C240 was southbound in the fourth lane. The front of vehicle one impacted the front of vehicle two. Vehicle one rotated counter clockwise and vehicle two rotated clockwise before both vehicles came to rest within the intersection. Both vehicles were towed from the scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is No PAR obtained (created) for V1 and No PAR obtained (created) for V2.
The driver of vehicle one, an eight-six year old female was only interviewed through a surrogate and only a partial interview was obtained. She said she entered the intersection after another vehicle. The other vehicle was blocking her line of sight. The light turned red while both vehicles where in a left turn. The other vehicle was able to clear the intersection, the driver of vehicle one wanted to do the same, but did not see vehicle two coming. This driver drives this vehicle daily and over this road on a weekly basis.
The driver of vehicle two, a thirty-four year old female stated that she was coming from a grocery store located in the northwest corner of the intersection. She stated she was stopped at the intersection until her light turned green. She proceeded into the intersection and did not see vehicle one coming until she was hit. This driver drives this vehicle daily and over this road on a daily basis.
