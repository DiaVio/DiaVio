This two-vehicle crash occurred on a busy arterial roadway in an area of commercial business parks. The main road is seven-lanes wide with a two-way center left turn lane. The road surface was bituminous and level. The posted speed limit is 80 kmph (50 mph). The weather was clear, the road was dry and it was daylight.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2003 Nissan Pathfinder was stopped northbound in the center left turn only lane. Vehicle two (V2), a 2006 Saturn Ion was traveling northbound in the first lane. V1 turned right from the center turn lane to enter a driveway. The front of V2 impacted the right side of V1. V1 then rolled over one quarter turn coming to rest on its left side. As V1 rolled over, the left front plane impacted a wooden street sign and the top impacted a tree. V1 was towed due to damage. V2 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, a twenty-two year old female, was on a work related trip and had been driving for approximately fifteen minutes prior to the crash. She was late for the appointment and in a hurry. The driver was looking for an address and had just driven passed it. The driver turned her head and looked out the back window. She did not see any traffic and turned right from the center left turn lane. This driver drives this vehicle daily, and it is unknown how often she drives on this roadway.
The driver of V2, an eighty-one year old male was not interviewed, but the witness stated he was driving northbound in the number one lane at a constant speed.
