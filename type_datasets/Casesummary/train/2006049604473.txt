A single vehicle crash occurred on a two-lane undivided (one-way traffic) level, dry concrete exit road of a highway. The roadway was curved to the left. The weather was clear and the roads dry. This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2005 Toyota Tundra was eastbound in the second lane of the roadway. The vehicle departed the road to its left and its front impacted a light pole located on the north (left) shoulder of the roadway. The light pole broke off its base, and vehicle one reentered the roadway on the left side. It crossed both lanes of traffic to the right and departed the roadway to its right. Vehicle one came back onto the roadway again from the right and crossed all traffic lanes to the left. It then departed the roadway to the left without impact to the concrete median located on the left side of the roadway. Vehicle one re-entered the roadway from the left one more time, crossing the lanes to the right. Vehicle one departed the roadway to the right and its front impacted the concrete pillar of a bridge. Vehicle one came to rest at the pillar. Final rest occurred approximately 630-meters (2,067-feet) from the first impact with the pole. The vehicle was towed from the scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.
The driver of vehicle one was a twenty-nine year old female who was not interviewed. Medical records indicate that this driver had a seizure before the crash. This driver was taking Lamotrigine and Keppra as medications and also tested positive for PCP.
