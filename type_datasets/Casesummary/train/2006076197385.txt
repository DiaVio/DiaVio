This single-vehicle crash occurred on a one-way, two-lane, straight, downhill, asphalt roadway. There are shallow ditches and steep rock/grass upslope embankments on both sides of the roadway. The speed limit for this roadway is 105 kmph (65 mph). The crash occurred during daylight with no adverse weather or road conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2002 Chevrolet Cavalier, was southbound in lane one. The vehicle departed the right road edge into the ditch. The front of V1 struck the embankment and the vehicle rotated clockwise. V1 rolled to the left two quarter-turns, slid across the road and off the left side. The back of the vehicle struck the left side embankment. V1 came to rest on its top facing northwest and was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 113 kmph for V1.
The driver of V1, a twenty-seven-year-old female and her four-year-old son, were driving from the grandmother's home in another town to their own home. They had begun the trip several hours previously, but had just stopped thirty minutes prior to the crash for a restroom and refreshment break. The driver had just purchased the vehicle the day before and had driven it only two times prior to the crash. The driver's son had just had a temper tantrum and the driver admitted to being inattentive.
