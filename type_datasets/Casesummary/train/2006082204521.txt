This three-vehicle collision occurred in the middle lane of a three-lane interstate highway in the southbound direction. The pre-crash roadway for all vehicles is paved with concrete and has no significant slope, but curves to the right. The roadway has a posted speed limit of 96 kmph (60 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1995 Mazda Protégé, was southbound on the roadway in the second lane. Vehicle two (V2), a 1997 Ford Taurus was stopped facing south in the second lane. Vehicle three (V3), a 2003 Ford Focus was stopped in front of V2. The front of V1 impacted the rear of V2. The front of V2 then impacted the rear of V3. V1 was towed due to damage. V2 and V3 were not towed. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, a nineteen-year-old male was heading from home to a friends home. This driver had been driving for about ten minutes at the time of the crash. This driver is diabetic and had checked his sugar level before he left home. This driver stated that he saw that traffic was getting worse and was slowing down but when he came around the curve, the traffic had stopped and he was unable to stop in time. This driver drives this vehicle daily and over this road on a monthly basis.
The driver of V2, a twenty-year-old male, was heading home from church. This driver had been driving for about forty minutes at the time of the crash. This driver was stopped and did not see V1. This driver drives this vehicle daily and over this road on a weekly basis.
The driver of V3, a forty-five year old male, was not interviewed. There is no known pre-crash information for this driver.
