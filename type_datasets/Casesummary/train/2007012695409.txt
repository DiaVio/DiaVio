The crash occurred off the left side of the eastbound side of an eight-lane, two-way highway divided by a median wall. There were four eastbound lanes and four westbound lanes. This was a level, concrete roadway that had curved left with a radius of 2813. The posted speed limit was 112 kmph (70 mph). It was early night on a weekday and the road was icy and it was snowing. Traffic flow was congested.
V1, a 2001 Buick LeSabre four door with two occupants was traveling eastbound in lane four. The driver of V1 stated that she had just come around the curve and noticed that the road was starting to get slippery. She was eating some ice cream and decided to put it in her lap. She was talking with her sister when she hit a patch of ice and started to fishtail to the right. She tried to steer left to correct it but departed the roadway. V1 contacted its front to the barrier wall. It then spun counterclockwise and contacted its right to the wall. V1 rolled left 2-quarter turns before coming to final rest on its roof in the roadway. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.
V1, the Buick LeSabre, was driven by a 22-year old female who reported minor injuries but refused treatment. She advised that she and her sister had left from shopping and were on their way home. She advised that she had been traveling between 82-97 kmph (51-60 mph) prior to loosing control.
