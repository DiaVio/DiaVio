This single-vehicle crash occurred in the eastbound travel lanes of a two-way divided multilevel interstate with no controls. The eastbound roadway has three lanes, is bituminous, dry, downhill and straight, with a posted speed limit of 89 kmph (55 mph). This crash occurred during dusk daylight hours with congested traffic from vehicles coming down from a day in the mountains.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1993 Honda Civic with two occupants, was eastbound in lane two traveling straight. The driver of V1 was traveling at a police reported speed of 24 kmph (15 mph) over the speed limit and lost control of the vehicle while entering a right curve. V1 drove into lane three and off the left side of the roadway while rotating counterclockwise. The vehicle entered a ditch and impacted it with its left front wheel. V1 started to rollover leading with its right side and impacted a delineator pole. V1 continued to roll onto it top and came to rest on its roof facing south on the left side of the road. V1 was towed from the scene due to moderate top damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 113 kmph for V1.
The driver of V1 was a twenty-two year-old male. He and his passenger were driving home from snow boarding that day. He was well rested and is in good health. He drives this roadway weekly and is very familiar with the vehicle.
