This two-vehicle crash occurred on a straight two-lane level asphalt roadway shortly before noon. This roadway has a speed limit of 73 kph (45 mph). There are no defects on this road. The road has no traffic controls. This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one, a 1998 Plymouth Breeze was westbound in lane one. Vehicle two, a 1983 Chevrolet S-10 Blazer was eastbound in the first lane. Vehicle one crossed the centerline and impacted vehicle two on its front plane with its front plane. Both vehicles came to rest in the area of impact. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 89 kmph for V1 and 89 kmph for V2.
The driver of vehicle one, a thirty-two year old male was driving home from work at the time of the crash. Fatigue is considered a factor for this driver. This driver works two jobs for about twelve hours a day. This driver had just gotten off his first job, which runs from 0300 to 1100. This driver slept from 2200 to 0200 the previous night. This driver drives this vehicle daily and on this road on a daily basis.
The driver of vehicle two, a thirty-three year old male gave a partial interview. This driver has no know sleep information. This driver does not work. The driver stated that this vehicle just came over the line.
