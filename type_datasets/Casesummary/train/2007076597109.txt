This two-vehicle crash occurred on a two-lane asphalt constructed rural roadway. This roadway is straight and level with a posted speed of 89 kmph (55 mph). This crash occurred in the early afternoon during daylight hours and no adverse weather. At the area of this crash there was a driveway on the east side. This roadway runs north to south and has improved shoulders on both sides.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2005 Toyota Tundra pick-up, was northbound at a speed higher than posted and impacted the rear of V1 that was slowing to turn right. The uninjured driver, a thirty one (31) year-old female, was cited for extreme DUI and taken to a local jail. This driver refused to be interviewed at the advice of her attorney. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
Vehicle two (V2), a 1986 Oldsmobile Cutlass Supreme sedan, was northbound and slowing to execute a right hand turn. As stated by the driver she looked into the rear view mirror and saw V1 coming up from behind very fast. As she began to turn right this vehicle was impacted on the rear and pushed off the right road edge. V2 left the road level and dropped down to a drainage area some 5 meters (16 feet) lower. The seventy three (73) year-old female driver of V2 was injured and was air transported to a trauma center. Both vehicles were going north when vehicle two (V2) began to slow to execute a right hand turn. As V2 slowed, vehicle one (V1) impacted the rear of V2 sending it off the right road edge. V1 continued down the road then exited the right road edge to final rest. Both vehicles were towed for damage.
