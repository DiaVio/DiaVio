The crash occurred on the south roadside of a two-way, undivided, rural roadway. The road had an eastbound and westbound lane bordered by a narrow, paved shoulder. There was a posted speed limit of 88 kmph (55 mph) and a level grade. Conditions were dry, dark, and clear. The setting was mid-evening hours on a weekend.
Vehicle one was a 1986 Chevrolet S10 pickup truck traveling eastbound in lane one. V1 traveled off the right side of the road and down a grass embankment. V1's front then struck a ditch causing V1 to overturn to the left three quarter turns. During the rollover, V1's top impacted a wooden fence post. V1 came to rest on its right side facing southwest. Police Reported Travel Speed is 97 kmph for V1.
The Chevrolet S10 (V1) was driven by a 41-year old male who was transported to a local trauma center with incapacitating injuries. He stated that he was heading home from a friend's house when he blacked out. The driver said he had recently experienced kidney failure and was taking a couple of prescription pain killers. He could not remember specifics of the crash. The investigating officer estimated his travel and impact speeds to be 96 kmph (60 mph) and 80 kmph (50 mph), respectively. The Chevrolet pickup truck was towed due to damage.
