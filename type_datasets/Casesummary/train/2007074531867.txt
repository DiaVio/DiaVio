This single-vehicle crash occurred near a four-legged intersection. Both roadways are undivided, straight, level and consist of one lane in each direction. The speed limit for these roadways is 48 kmph (30 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1998 Ford Escort, was westbound after turning right out of a southbound driveway. V1 turned left at the intersection and traveled off the right side of the road. The driver of V1 then put V1 into reverse and V1 moved backward into the road and then off the left side of the east/west road. The right side of V1 struck a post and two trees. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.
The driver of V1, an eighty-year-old male, was on his way home from a bank. He was well rested and familiar with his vehicle. He travels this route monthly. This driver has tinnitus, high blood pressure and is paralyzed on the right side due to a stroke three years previously. He takes aspirin and an unspecified blood pressure medication. This driver stated that he did not "know what happened exactly."
