This two-vehicle crash occurred on a five-lane roadway. This road is straight and level with no defects. The speed limit on the road is 97 kmph (60 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1999 Saturn SL2 was traveling east in the second lane. Vehicle two (V2), a 2002 Chevrolet Express van was traveling east in the third lane. V2 changed lanes to the right and the left of V1 impacted the right of V2. V1 spun off the road and slid down a grass slope. V1 then rolled over and came to rest on its roof off of the roadway. V2 continued on and was stopped shortly after the crash and informed that he had been in a crash. V1 was towed due to damage. V2 was not towed. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, a twenty-one year old male who was driving to work. This driver had been driving for about twenty minutes at the time of the crash. This driver stated that he was in a hurry and was thinking about an argument that he had with his fiance earlier that day. This driver drives this vehicle daily and over this road on a daily basis.
The driver of V2, a seventy-two year old male had just purchased this vehicle at auction. This driver had been driving for about ten minutes at the time of the crash. This driver indicated that he does not feel comfortable driving these large vans. This was this drivers first time driving this vehicle and he drives on this road on a weekly basis.
