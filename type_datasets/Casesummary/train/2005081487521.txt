This three-vehicle rear-end type collision occurred during morning daylight hours during rush hour on a weekday. The crash occurred on a straight stretch of a four-lane, dry, level, bituminous, positively divided expressway. In the area of the crash, the speed limit is 97 kmph (60 mph). At the time of the crash, there were no adverse atmospheric conditions, and the roadway was free of defects.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1991 Cadillac DeVille four-door sedan, was traveling northbound in the fourth lane (reserved for high occupancy vehicles), approaching congested traffic ahead. Vehicle two (V2), a 1978 Chevrolet half-ton regular cab pickup, was stopped ahead of V1 in the third lane. Vehicle three (V3), a 2005 Volkswagen Jetta, was stopped ahead of V2 in the third lane. V1 veered right to avoid colliding with the stopped traffic in the fourth lane, and entered the third lane, where its front plane impacted the back of V2. V2 moved ahead and impacted the back of V3. All three vehicles came to rest in the third lane. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
