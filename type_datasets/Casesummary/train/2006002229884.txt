This crash occurred on a weekday night on a two-lane, two-way, undivided rural roadway. The roadway was straight and level with a posted speed limit of 89kmph (55mph). The weather was cloudy and the roadway was wet from a prior rain shower.
V1, a 1997 Ford Explorer, was northbound on the roadway when it suddenly crossed the southbound lane and departed the roadway on the left side where V1 undercarriage impacted an embankment on the roadside. The vehicle continued up the embankment, impacted with a group of 5 to 7 cm trees with its front right. It continued forward to impact with a second group of 5 to 8 cm trees and a 148cm rock with its front and front right. The vehicle rolled over four (4) one quarter turns ejecting both occupants before up righting itself. V1 then rotated 180 degrees counter-clockwise and came to final rest facing an east. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.
V1 was driven by a 40 year-old female and there was a 33-year-old male in the front right seat. This driver stated she had had 4 beers and several “shots” in the past 2 hours. She stated she was arguing with her husband when he suddenly grabbed the steering wheel and “jerked” it to the right. In an effort to regain control she then “jerked” the steering wheel back to the left, which caused the vehicle to depart the roadway to the left. Both occupants were transported to an area trauma center with incapacitating injuries as both occupants were ejected from the vehicle. The driver landed approximately 40 feet south of the vehicle and the passenger landed approximately 25 feet north of the vehicle. Neither occupant was wearing a seat belt. The vehicle was towed due to damage, however it was taken to the impound yard due to alcohol involvement and pending charges.
