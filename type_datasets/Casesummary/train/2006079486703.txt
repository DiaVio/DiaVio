This two-vehicle collision occurred during morning daylight hours on a weekday. The crash occurred at the four-leg intersection of a north-south roadway and an east-west roadway. The intersection is controlled by a tri-light traffic signal with no protected left phase, and the speed limit for east-west traffic is 56 kmph (35 mph). At the time of the crash it was cloudy, road surface dry, level asphalt and the roadway was free of defects.
Vehicle one (V 1), a 1992 Toyota Corolla four-door station wagon, was traveling westbound in the fourth lane of a seven-lane, two-way, undivided, roadway approaching intersection with the intention of turning left into southbound traffic. Vehicle two (V 2), a 2004 Toyota Matrix four-door hatchback, driven by a 35 year-old female was traveling eastbound in the second lane of opposing traffic, approaching the intersection with the intention of passing straight through. As V 1 made its turn, the front of V 1 impacted the front of V 2. Both vehicles deflected southeast to final rest in the eastbound lanes. The driver of V 1 departed the scene, unidentified. No information about the driver could be obtained. Driver of V 2 stated she never saw V 1 and no time for an avoidance maneuver. Both vehicles were towed from the scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
