This two-vehicle crash occurred at an intersection with no control device. It occurred during early morning daylight hours. The roadway is seven lanes, level, asphalt and undivided roadway with a two-way left turn lane. This roadway runs east and west. There is a side street on the north side. No adverse weather conditions existed at the time of the collision. The roadway was wet due to some earlier rain. The posted speed limit is 56 kph (35 mph).
Vehicle one (V1) a 1997 Ford Lincoln Town Car 4-door sedan driven by a 17 year-old male with two occupants. Vehicle two (V2) a 2005 Chevrolet Cavalier 4-door sedan driven by a 25 year-old female with one passenger. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
V1 had just exited a gas station heading east and got into the left turn lane. The traffic was heavy for the westbound side. The cars in the number two and three lanes stopped to let V1 make his left turn. As V1 made his left turn, V2 traveling west in lane one impacted the right side with its front. V1 after impact rotated counterclockwise to face northwest where it came to rest. V2 drove to the east curb of the street coming to final rest facing north. Both vehicles were towed from the scene due to damage.
