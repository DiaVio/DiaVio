This two-vehicle crash occurred in the parking lane of an undivided, straight roadway. The northbound lane has a five percent downhill grade. The speed limit for this roadway is 48 kmph (30 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1983 Ford Ranger, was northbound in lane one. Vehicle two (V2), a 2003 Ford F150 pickup, was parked on the right side of the roadway. V1 traveled to the right and the front struck the back of V2. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, a fifty-year-old female, was familiar with this roadway but does not remember any details of the crash. She takes no medication but had a head injury three weeks prior to this crash. This driver stated that she "had been feeling weird for a few days before the crash." She further stated that she felt fatigued, had been "getting nauseous and disoriented" and could not sleep for more than a couple of hours at a time. She also stated that she "must have passed out behind the wheel."
V2 was occupied by a forty-nine-year-old female driver and a two-year-old male passenger.
