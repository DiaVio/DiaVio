The crash occurred at the intersection of an urban five-lane undivided arterial roadway that runs northeast/southwest and a two-lane undivided residential side street. The intersection was controlled by traffic signals. The arterial road had two lanes in each direction and left turn lanes, the shoulder was labeled as a bicycle lane. The posted speed limit was 56 kmph (35 mph). The two-lane residential side street had a posted speed limit of 48 kmph (30 mph). Conditions were daylight and dry in the mid-afternoon hours on a weekday.
Vehicle #1 was a 1994 Ford Taurus traveling southwest in the second lane of the arterial road. Vehicle #2, a 1995 Chevrolet Lumina, was traveling southeast on the intersecting side street. Vehicle #1 passed through the traffic control and its front struck the left side of Vehicle #2. The Taurus (V1) came to rest facing southeast in the southeast corner of the intersection while the Lumina (V2) came to rest facing south in the middle of the intersection. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
Vehicle #1 was operated by a 60-year old female that was belted and not injured in the crash. The frontal airbags did not deploy in the collision. She stated that she was looking for an address on the left side of the street and took her eyes off the road. When she looked forward again the light had changed to red and she was in the middle of the intersection. Police charged her with failure to yield.
Vehicle #2 was driven by a 24-year old female that was belted with no deployed airbags. She was six months pregnant and she, along with her front right passenger, were transported to a local hospital with minor injuries. She stated that as she approached the intersection the light was green, so she continued and was struck in the left side. She reported seeing the other vehicle only a split second prior to impact.
