This single-vehicle crash occurred on a physically divided, right curve, state highway with two lanes in each direction. The highway runs east and west at the location of the crash and there were no traffic controls. The speed limit is 105 kmph (65 mph) and it was windy at the time.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1997 Toyota Avalon was eastbound on a right curve in lane two. V1 traveled off the left side of the road into a grassy median. The driver started to steer to the right and the vehicle started to rotate clockwise back into lane two. V1 still rotating clockwise entered lane one and ran off the right side of the road onto a paved shoulder. V1 impacted a guardrail with its right side. V1 was still rotating clockwise as it came off the guardrail and impacted it again with its front. V1 to rest in the median facing southeast on its wheels. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.
The driver of V1, a twenty-year-old male was driving home from dropping a friend off. This driver stated that he had started this trip at midnight his time, it is unknown how long this driver had been driving. This driver stated that he slept about eighteen to twenty hours in the last day but this could not be substantiated. Fatigue is considered a factor for this driver. The medical records indicate this driver had a BAC of .323. This driver had damaged his vehicle twice in the past two days. The rear damage is from a hit and run crash two days previous. Some of the front damage is from a crash that this driver had about 1.6 kilometers (1 mile) before this crash. This driver drives this vehicle daily and over this road on a weekly basis. This driver was talking on his hand held cell phone to his girlfriend. This driver also stated that one of his contact lenses popped out while driving and he was driving with one eye closed.
