A two-vehicle crash occurred in the afternoon hours on a seven-lane divided bituminous roadway without positive barrier. This crash occurred at a tri-light controlled intersection. This intersection is also a junction off of a major interstate highway. This crash occurred in the first northbound lane only. The speed limit for this section of the roadway is 56 kph (35 mph). The roadway is straight, level with no defects. This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one, a 1997 Peterbilt conventional tractor pulling one semi dump trailer was northbound in the first lane. Vehicle two, a 2003 Chevrolet Avalanche was stopped in the first lane ahead of vehicle one. The front of vehicle one impacted the rear of vehicle two. Both vehicles came to rest near the area of impact. Vehicle one was not towed due to damage. Vehicle two was towed due to damage (per the PAR). Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of vehicle one, a sixty-three year old male had just dropped off a load and was heading home. This driver had been driving for thirty-five minutes at the time of the crash. This driver stated that he thought vehicle two was going to continue through the light. This driver drives this vehicle daily and over this road several times a month.
The driver of vehicle two, a fifty-six year old male was not interviewed. There is no known pre-crash information for this driver.
