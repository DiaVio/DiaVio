The crash occurred in the northbound lanes of a two-way roadway with one lane in each direction. The roadway was straight and level, and it was clear with no adverse conditions. It was dark and lighted, and the speed limit was 48kmph (30mph). The traffic flow was normal, and this was on a weekday evening.
V1, a 2006 Ford Explorer sport utility vehicle with two occupants, was traveling northbound in lane one. V2, a 1991 Toyota four door with one occupant, was traveling northbound in front of V2, and had stopped for the traffic light at the intersection. V3, a 2006 Jeep Liberty sport utility vehicle, was parked in the southbound parking lane. The front of V1 rear-ended the back of V2, and then went across the intersection where its right side contacted the front of V3. Then V1 went on top of the sidewalk and contacted an iron fence on the northwest corner of the intersection. V1 came to a final rest on the iron fence. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
V1, the Ford Explorer, was driven by a 41 year-old male who reported minor injuries and was transported to a medical facility. A 45 year-old occupant reported no injuries. The driver stated to the police that he fell asleep while driving. Numerous attempts were made to get an interview with the driver of V1; all were unsuccessful.
V2, the Toyota four door, was driven by a 44 year-old male who reported minor injuries and was transported to a medical facility. The driver stated to the police that he was stopped at the red light when he was impacted. This driver had an out-of-town address and could not be located for an interview.
