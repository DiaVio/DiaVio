This crash occurred on a weekday afternoon in the middle of a four-way residential intersection. The intersection consisted of a two-lane north/south roadway intersected by a two-lane east/west roadway that was controlled by a stop sign. Both roadways were bituminous, dry, straight, and level with posted speed limits of 48 kmph (30 mph). The traffic flow was normal and there were no adverse atmospheric conditions at the time of the crash.
V1, a 2002 Ford Explorer with one occupant, was westbound approaching the intersection where it intended to go straight. V2, a 2005 Dodge Caravan with one occupant, was northbound approaching the same intersection where it also intended to go straight. V1 failed to stop for the stop sign and entered the intersection at the same time as V2. The left front corner of V1 struck the right front corner of V2. The two vehicles than side slapped as the continued northwest. Both vehicles came to rest partially off the roadway on the northwest corner of the intersection. Both vehicles were towed from the scene due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, a belted 42-year-old female, was transported for treatment of non-incapacitating injuries. She did not responded to repeated phone messages or mailed requests for an interview. Therefore no additional information could be obtained regarding this driver.
The driver of V2, a belted 57-year-old male, was transported for treatment of non-incapacitating injuries. He was taking his cat to the vet when the crash occurred. He informed this researcher that he rarely travels this road but decided to do so because of lighter traffic volume. He stated that he never saw V2 prior to impact.
