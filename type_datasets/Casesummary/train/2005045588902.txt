The crash occurred on a lightly traveled two lane urban residential roadway. The road curved to the left as it traveled southeast with a radius of curvature of 76m. It did not have any painted lane lines and the width of the road varied between 5.8 and 5.4 meters. The south edge of the road was tree lined and there were curve-warning signs present. The posted speed limit was 48kph (30mph). Conditions were daylight and dry on a weekday morning.
Vehicle #1 was a 2000 Kia Sephia traveling southeast. As the vehicle negotiated the left curve the driver lost control and departed the right (south) side of the road. The car struck a tree and a sign with its right side. The vehicle continued southeast and then struck a large tree with its front. After impact the vehicle rotated ninety degrees clockwise and came to rest facing south partially off the road. The Kia (V1) was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.
Vehicle #1 was driven by a 23-year old male that was not belted. He initially left the scene but later returned and was transported to an area hospital with minor injuries. Although the frontal impact was relatively severe the frontal airbags did not deploy. The driver had just left his house (0.8km away) and was on his way to work when the crash occurred. Police charged the driver with failure to maintain control, no proof of insurance, and violation of state vehicle registration law.
