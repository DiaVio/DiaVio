A two vehicle daytime collision occurred at during late morning hours on a weekday. The collision occurred at the intersection of a two-way five-lane road with a one-way four-lane road that turns into an interstate on-ramp. The intersection is controlled by a three-color traffic signal with pedestrian signals. Both roads are paved with bituminous asphalt. The speed limit for the both roads is 48 kmph (30 mph). Vehicle one (V1), a 1993 Chevrolet Suburban with only a driver, was headed south on a one-way street in lane three. Vehicle 2 (V2), a 2003 GMC Sonoma pickup with only a driver, was stopped at the traffic signal facing west. When the light for V2 turned green, V2 proceeded into the intersection. V1 did not stop for the red light, and the front of V1 struck V2 on the right side towards the front. V1 and V2 both deflected, and side-slapped. The vehicles then rolled together into the southwest corner of the intersection, where they both came to rest. Both vehicles were towed due to disabling damage.
