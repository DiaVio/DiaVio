A crash happened about one hour before noon during daylight hours at an intersection controlled by traffic lights. It was a rear-end collision that involved two vehicles: one flatbed truck, and a passenger car. The speed limit for the primary approach leg to the intersection is 54 kmph (35 mph). The road is a 4-lane divided (no positive barrier) asphalt roadway for both vehicles. The road surface was dry asphalt and there were no detrimental atmospheric conditions at the time of the crash.
Vehicle one (V1) was a 1991 Chevrolet 3500 Flatbed Pick up driven by a 37 year-old male with one passenger. V1 was following vehicle two (V2) in lane 1 at an unknown distance. V2 was a 2004 Buick Century driven by a 83 year-old male who was traveling west in the first lane of traffic. V2 decelerated to make a right turn at the intersection. The driver of V1 tried to avoid a rear end collision and steered to the left while applying his brakes. The right front corner of V1 impacted the rear of V2. It caused V2 to rotated clockwise while moving into the northbound lanes of traffic. V2 came to rest within the northbound lanes, facing east. V1 came to rest within the intersection, facing west. V2 was towed from the scene. No injuries were reported for this crash. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
