This two-vehicle collision occurred at the intersection of a two lane two-way arterial street and a two-way side street. The arterial has a slight right turn of twenty degrees approximately 100 meters (328 feet) before the intersection. The arterial road is straight and level at the site of the crash, and is paved with asphalt. The side street has an uphill grade of +8% approaching the intersection from the east. The speed limit for the both roads is 48 kmph (30 mph). At the time of the crash the road was wet, but the rain had stopped. This crash occurred during evening hours with cloudy conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1991 BMW 525 was southbound in the first lane. Vehicle two (V2), a 1991 Chevrolet Suburban was stopped westbound at the stop sign. As V2 entered the intersection with the intention of going straight across the intersection, the front of V1 impacted the right of V2. V2 came to rest facing south on the right side of the arterial road. V1 veered off to the right went up a curb and traveled south on a sidewalk. All four wheels of V1 impacted the curb. Both vehicles were towed due to disabling damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1 fled the scene. There is no known information about this driver.
The driver of V2, an eighteen-year old male, was returning home after dropping his girlfriend off at home. This driver said that he had been stopped at the stop sign for over ten seconds to allow another car to pass heading north. When he proceeded into the intersection, he heard a speeding engine noise. As he looked to the right to identify the source of the noise, V1 struck his vehicle. The driver of V2 stated that he had looked right and did not see V1 approaching when he entered the intersection. This driver has only driven this vehicle a couple of time in the last month but drives over this road daily.
