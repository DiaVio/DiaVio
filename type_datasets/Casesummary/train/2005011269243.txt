The crash occurred at 4 legged urban intersection controlled by an on-colors traffic signal. Conditions at the time of the weekday, afternoon crash were cloudy and dry. Interviews were obtained with both drivers and both vehicles were inspected.
V1, a 1995 Oldsmobile Achieva, driven by a 22 year-old female, was southbound on a 3 lane, straight and level concrete road traveling in lane one stopped at the intersection on a red traffic signal. Per witnesses, v1 started into the intersection on the red signal and the right side of v1 was struck by v2. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
V2, a 1996 Ford Explorer, driven by a 55 year-old male, was eastbound on a 5-lane straight and level concrete road approaching the same intersection with a green traffic signal. The driver of v2 had no time for avoidance maneuvers. The front of v2 struck the right side of v1. V1 then rotated counterclockwise while v2 rotated clockwise, and the right back of v1 struck the left passenger area of v2. V2 came to rest facing south in lane one of the south leg of the intersection. V2 was towed from the scene due to damage. The airbags in v2 deployed and the fully restrained driver denied injuries.
After the sideslap with v2, v1 continued to the southeast corner of the intersection and left the road striking a small post with the front left. V1 then struck a fire hydrant with the front right corner. V1 was towed from the scene due to damage. The fully restrained driver was transported due to injuries.
