The crash occurred at a three-legged intersection in the southbound lanes of a suburban roadway with a raised concrete median. There were two southbound lanes and four northbound lanes. The intersection was signal-controlled with one pedestrian crosswalk and light. The roadway had a posted speed limit of 72 kmph (45 mph). Southbound lanes were on a 6% downhill grade while the eastbound lanes were on a 5% downhill grade. Conditions were dusk and dry with clear skies. The setting was early evening hours on a weekday.
Vehicle one was a 1998 Chevrolet Blazer sport utility traveling south in lane two. Vehicle two was a 1997 Jeep Grand Cherokee sport utility stopped facing east in lane two waiting to turn left. Both vehicles proceeded through the intersection. The front of V1 impacted the left plane of V2. V1 came to rest southeast of the impact after rotating counter-clockwise 45 degrees. V2 came to rest east of the impact after rotating counter-clockwise 90 degrees. Police Reported Travel Speed is 64 kmph for V1 and 64 kmph for V2.
The Chevrolet Blazer (V1) was driven by a 16-year old female who was treated on scene for possible injuries. She was unable to be located for an interview. However her father stated that she accidentally ran the red light due to being an inexperienced driver. The investigating officer estimated her travel and impact speeds to be 64 kmph (40 mph). The Chevrolet sport utility was towed, but not due to police-reported damage.
The Jeep Grand Cherokee (V2) was driven by a 48-year old female who was treated on scene for possible injuries. She stated that the other driver ran the light and hit her. I was unable to complete the interview on scene and then was unable to locate this driver again. The investigating officer estimated her travel and impact speeds to be 8 kmph (5 mph). The sport utility was towed, but not due to police-reported damage.
