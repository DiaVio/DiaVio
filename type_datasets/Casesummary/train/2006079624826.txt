This three-vehicle collision occurred during daylight hours on a weekday afternoon. The crash occurred at the four-leg intersection of a north-south roadway with a 64 kmph (40 mph) speed limit, and an east-west roadway with a 56 kmph (35 mph) speed limit. Both roadways are five-lane, two-way, straight, level, bituminous roads. The intersection is controlled by a tri-light traffic signal. At the time of the crash, there were no adverse weather conditions, and the roadway was free of defects.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1995 Pontiac Bonneville 4-door sedan, was traveling southbound in the second lane, approaching the intersection with the intention of passing straight through. Vehicle two (V2), a 2002 Lexus ES300 4-door sedan, was stopped at the intersection in the first eastbound lane, waiting to continue straight. Vehicle three (V3), a 2002 Ford Focus 4-door sedan, was stopped next to V2 in the second eastbound lane. As V1 entered the intersection, V2 and V3 began to move forward, with V2 slightly ahead of V3. In the intersection, the front of V1 impacted the left side of V2. Then, the front of V3 impacted the right side of V1. V2 rotated clockwise and the left side of V2 contacted the right side of V3. V1 continued southward and came to final rest several meters south of the intersection. V2 and V3 came to rest in the intersection. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The 49-year-old male driver of V3 was commuting to work. He reported that he was focused on the traffic light, and that he also never saw V1's approach. When the light changed to the green phase, he waited a few seconds, and the proceeded into the intersection. V3 was not involved in the first harmful event.
