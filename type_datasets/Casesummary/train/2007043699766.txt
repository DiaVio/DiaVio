The crash occurred in the westbound lane of a two-lane, undivided rural roadway. The eastbound approach was a right curve with a 4 percent downhill grade. The westbound approach was straight with a 3 percent uphill grade. The roadway had a posted speed limit of 72 kmph (45 mph). Conditions were daylight and dry with clear skies. The setting was late afternoon hours on a holiday weekend.
Vehicle one was a 2001 Honda Accord 4-door sedan negotiating a right curve traveling eastbound in lane one. Vehicle two was a 2003 Lincoln LS 4-door sedan traveling westbound in lane one. V1 completed negotiating the curve and began decelerating to enter a private driveway on the north side of the roadway. V1 proceeded to turn left across the westbound lane. The front of V1 impacted the left front plane of V2. V1 came to rest in the roadway and later moved out of the roadway into the private driveway. V2 continued off the right side of the road, across a ditch, and into a medium sized tree with its front plane. V2 came to rest against the tree. Police Reported Travel Speed is 16 kmph for V1 and 16 kmph for V2.
The Honda Accord (V1) was driven by a 19-year old female who was uninjured. She stated that she was heading to a friend's house from a local restaurant. She decelerated to turn left and then remembers being struck. She estimated her travel and impact speeds to be 16 kmph (10 mph). Her Honda sedan continued in service after the crash.
The Lincoln LS (V2) was driven by a 52-year old male who was uninjured. He stated that was heading to a gas station from home when another vehicle (V1) turned into his vehicle. He attempted to steer right in avoidance but did not have enough time. He estimated his travel and impact speeds to be 72 kmph (45 mph). The Lincoln LS suffered extensive damage to the front end from the tree impact, including a flat restricted right front tire and a broken axle. It was towed from the scene.
