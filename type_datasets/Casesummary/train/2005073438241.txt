This two-vehicle crash occurred at the stop sign controlled intersection of an east/west two lane, two-way roadway with a two lane two-way north/south thru roadway. The crash occurred during daylight hours, the weather was clear; the roadways were straight, level, and dry.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2005 Ford Explorer, occupied by the driver only, was westbound on the two lane, two way roadway approaching the stop sign controlled intersection. Vehicle two (V2), a 2002 Cadillac Seville occupied by the driver only, was northbound in the first lane approaching the intersection. Per witnesses, V1 failed to stop for the stop sign and entered the intersection. The front of V1 struck the right side of V2. V2 was deflected to the west where the vehicle ran off the west road edge and struck a tree where it came to rest. Per the medical report EMS commentary, V1 then fled the scene and proceeded north, where police apprehended the driver a few blocks away. The driver of V2 was transported to a local trauma center for treatment. V2 was towed from the scene; V1 was towed from where it was apprehended. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
