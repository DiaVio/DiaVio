This two-vehicle crash occurred in a four-legged, traffic signal-controlled intersection. The northbound approach roadway was level, curved to the right and consists of one lane in each direction with parking on both sides. The east/west trafficway is level and consists of two lanes in each direction with parking on both sides. There are concrete elevated train support pillars dividing the two lanes in each direction. The speed limit for these roadways is 48 kmph (30 mph). It was raining at the time of the crash. This crash occurred during daylight hours.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1996 Land Rover Range Rover, was northbound in lane one. Vehicle two (V2), a 2000 Lincoln Town Car, was eastbound in the inside lane one. In the intersection, the front of V1 struck the right side of V2. V2 rotated slightly and the left side struck a concrete support pillar. V2 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, a forty-year-old male, was on his way to work from his home. He was well rested, healthy and familiar with his vehicle. He travels this route daily. This driver stated that he tried to stop when he realized that the signal was turning red but did not have time.
The driver of V2, a sixty-seven-year-old male, was traveling with his fifty-eight-year-old wife. He was healthy and comfortable with his vehicle and passenger. This driver stated that he did not see V1 prior to the crash.
