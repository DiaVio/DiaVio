This two-vehicle collision occurred at a T-intersection, where a north-south side street intersects the south side of the east-west main roadway. The main road has five lanes with a two-way center turn lane. The intersecting road has two lanes. Traffic on the side street is controlled by a posted stop sign. The speed limit on the main road is 56 kph (35 mph). The speed limit on the intersecting road is 40 kph (25 mph). At the time of the collision, there were no adverse atmospheric conditions, and the roadways were free of defects.
Vehicle one, a 1994 Cadillac De Ville was stopped at the intersection on the side street. Vehicle two, a 1999 Nissan Altima was eastbound in the first lane. Vehicle one turned left from the intersection and the front of vehicle one impacted the right of vehicle two. Vehicle one came to rest near the point of impact. Vehicle two came to rest in the center turn lane. Vehicle one was not towed. Vehicle two was towed. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The 64-year-old male driver of V1 reported that he did not see the other vehicle, and he attempted no evasive action. This driver was heading to the golf course at the time of the crash. This driver had been driving for about five minutes at the time of the crash. This driver was taking vitamins as a medication. This driver drives this vehicle daily and over this road on a daily basis.
The 14-year-old female driver of V2 reported that she saw V1 come out of the T-intersection into her lane, and she applied the brakes but was unable to stop the vehicle or avoid the collision. This driver was heading to work from a friends house at the time of the crash. She states that she normally sleeps less than six hours per night, but she claims that she was rested and not fatigued at the time of the crash. This driver drives this vehicle daily and over this road on a daily basis.
