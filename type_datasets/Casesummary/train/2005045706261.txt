The crash occurred on a two way, two lane east / west residential arterial road. There was also a crossing two way, two lane residential road that ran north /south. The north/south road was controlled with stop signs in either direction. There were no controls on the residential arterial road. The arterial road had double solid no passing stripes, was straight, level and had a posted speed limit of 48 kmph (30 mph). The crossing residential street also had a posted speed limit of 48 kmph (30 mph). There was a slight downgrade on the westbound approach to the intersection, though the road leveled off in crossing the arterial road. Conditions at the time of the crash were daylight and dry and it was early afternoon on a weekday.
Vehicle 1, a 1993 Ford F700 dump truck was southbound on the residential street approaching the intersection with the arterial road. Vehicle 2, a 1988 GMC Jimmy was eastbound on the arterial road. Vehicle 1 crossed the arterial roadway and Vehicle 2 struck V1 on the right side impacting the dump truck's tandems with it's (V2's) front. Vehicle 1 then completed crossing the intersection and pulled to the right curb and stopped. Vehicle 2 came to rest at point of impact. Vehicle 1 sustained very little damage and was driven from the scene. Vehicle 2 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
Vehicle 1 was driven by a 22 year old, belted male who was uninjured in the crash. The drivers' interview was considered to not be very credible as it was given with the owner of the dump truck (the drivers' boss) standing between the interviewee and the researcher for the duration of the interview.
Vehicle 2 was driven by a 21 year old belted male who was also uninjured in the crash. The other occupant of this vehicle (his mom) was also uninjured and belted.
