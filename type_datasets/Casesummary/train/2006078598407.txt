This two-vehicle crash occurred at the intersection of a two-lane roadway and a four-lane roadway. The two-lane road is straight and level and is controlled by a stop sign. The four-lane road is straight and level with no controls. The two-lane road has a posted speed limit of 40 kmph (25 mph). The four-lane roadway has a posted speed limit of 56 kmph (35 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2006 Dodge Grand Caravan was westbound in the number one lane of the two-lane road. Vehicle two (V2), a 1989 GMC Jimmy was northbound in the second lane. V1 crossed through the intersection and the left front was impacted by the front of V2. Both vehicles came to rest near the intersection. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 16 kmph for V1 and 16 kmph for V2.
The driver of V1, a thirty-five year old male, had just left some work meetings and was heading to a large city several hours away. This driver had been driving for about ten minutes at the time of the crash. This driver is in good general health, was not taking any medications, did not wear glasses and slept for six and one half hours the night before in a motel. He stated that there could have been a parked vehicle to his left that blocked his view of vehicle two. This was the first time driving in this vehicle and over this road.
The driver of V2, a twenty-six year old male, was coming from his home and heading to a friends house. This driver had been driving for about ten minutes at the time of the crash. This driver has no medical conditions. This driver indicated that vehicle one did not stop at the stop sign, but slowed down and then proceeded through the intersection. This driver drives this vehicle daily and over this road on a daily basis.
