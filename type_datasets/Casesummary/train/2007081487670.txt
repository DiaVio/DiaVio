This single-vehicle collision occurred in the early morning hours when it was dark but lighted. The collision occurred on a rural one-way, two-lane undivided roadway in lane one. The pre-crash roadway is a - 7% downhill grade, with a slight curve to the left, and bituminous paving. The weather was clear and the roadway was wet. The roadway has a posted speed limit of 56 kmph (35 mph).
Vehicle one (V1), a 1997 Honda Accord sedan driven by a twenty three (23) year old female, was traveling northbound in lane one. V1 departed the roadway via the right shoulder, and the front of V1 impacted a small tree. V1 continued on in a northwest direction, and then the front of V1 then impacted a light pole, and came to rest with the right rear up against a fence. The vehicle was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.
The driver of V1's pre-crash factors are unknown, as she was unable to be reached for interview. Per the police collision report and conversation with the investigating officer, she was extremely tired and fell asleep at the wheel.
