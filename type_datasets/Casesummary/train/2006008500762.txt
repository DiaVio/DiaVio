This crash occurred on a weekday afternoon on a busy two-lane one-way highway. The asphalt roadway was wet and it was raining at the time of the crash. This was a work zone with a posted speed of 80 kmph (50 mph).
V1 was a 1995 Ford E350 transportation van that was being used to transport a handicapped girl. It was driven by a male (age unknown). V2 was a 2002 Toyota Tacoma pickup, also driven by a male (age unknown). Both drivers related that they were traveling southbound in the first lane of travel. A tractor-trailer that was pulled off of the road on the right side suddenly reentered the roadway in front of V2. The driver of V2 applied his brakes, slowing in time so as to not hit the truck. The driver of V1 also applied his brakes but was unable to stop his vehicle, and V1 hit V2 in the rear. The driver of V1 also stated that the wet roads caused the vehicle to skid slightly. Due to the wet conditions, no skid marks could be seen. Police Reported Travel Speed is No PAR obtained (created) for V1 and No PAR obtained (created) for V2.
The tractor-trailer never stopped.
