This two-vehicle crash occurred in a four-legged, traffic signal-controlled intersection. The north/south trafficway straight, level and is divided by a curbed median. The northbound approach lanes consist of three through lanes and a left turn lane. The southbound approach lanes consist of three through lanes, a left turn lane and a right turn lane. The speed limit for this roadway is 80 kmph (50 mph). It was dark with streetlights on at the time of the crash with no adverse atmospheric conditions. Traffic was congested.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2004 Cadillac CTS, was northbound in lane four. Vehicle two (V2), a 1992 Chevrolet Camaro, was southbound in lane two. Non-contact vehicles were stopped in southbound lanes three and four. V1 turned left in the intersection and the right side was struck by the front of V2. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 8 kmph for V1 and 8 kmph for V2.
The driver of V1, a thirty-two-year-old female, was not interviewed. Her statement to the police officer at the scene indicated that southbound traffic in lanes three and four were stopped to allow her to make her turn.
The driver of V2, a thirty-six-year-old male, was on his way from his home to a store. He was familiar with his vehicle and travels this route daily. This driver confirmed that there were vehicles stopped in lanes three and four restricting his view of V1.
