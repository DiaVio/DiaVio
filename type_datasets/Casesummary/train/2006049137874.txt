This two-vehicle crash occurred on a jersey median-divided roadway. The westbound lanes are curved to the left and consist of four through lanes. The speed limit on this roadway is 97 kmph (60 mph). It was dark with streetlights on at the time of the crash and there were no adverse atmospheric conditions. Traffic was heavy due to rush hour congestion.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1999 Cadillac Deville, was westbound in lane four. Vehicle two (V2), a 2001 Toyota Tacoma, was westbound in lane four in front of V1. V2 stopped for traffic. The front of V1 struck the back of V2. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is No PAR obtained (created) for V1 and No PAR obtained (created) for V2.
The driver of V1, a twenty-three-year-old male, was traveling from his brother's home to his wife's workplace. He was healthy, well rested and familiar with his vehicle. He drives this route daily. When he realized that V2 was stopping, he applied the brakes in an attempt to avoid the crash.
The driver of V2, a twenty-six-year-old female, was traveling from home to work. She was well rested, healthy and comfortable with her vehicle. She travels on this roadway daily.
