This two vehicle daytime collision occurred shortly past noon on the off ramp from a four lane, divided, dry, level, asphalt highway. The highway curves slightly to the left just before the off ramp and is designated 104 kmph (65 mph) speed limit. A concrete center divider borders the left shoulder. A cinder block wall borders the right shoulder. The off ramp has a 4% downhill grade. A dirt embankment separates the highway and the off ramp. The dirt embankment has a 30% uphill grade. No advisory speed limit is posted for the off ramp.
Vehicle one (V1) is a 1999 Ford E-250 full size cargo van, driven by a nineteen (19) year old male who was traveling east in the number one lane. Vehicle two (V2) is a 2003 Chevrolet Suburban Z71, 4-door large sport utility, driven by a thirty five (35) year old male who was traveling east in the number one lane. Traffic was backed up on the off ramp. As V2 slowed for traffic ahead, after it exited the highway, V1's front plane impacted V2's rear plane. V2 then departed the left side of the ramp up the dirt embankment where it came to rest facing northeast. V1 came to rest on the right shoulder of the off ramp. V1 was towed with moderate damage. V2 was driven with moderate damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 97 kmph for V1 and 97 kmph for V2.
An associated factor for both driver's was that there was no reduced advisory speed posted for the off-ramp.
