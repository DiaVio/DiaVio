This two vehicle crash occurred shortly after noon in daylight hours at an intersection without traffic lights. The road is a 3-lane divided concrete roadway from west to east. It is a two lane (two-way traffic) downhill asphalt roadway from west to east. The speed limit for the roadway is 48 kmph (30 mph) The road surface was dry and there were no detrimental atmospheric conditions at the time of the crash.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2001 Ford Focus sedan driven by a 19 year-old female was traveling west in the first lane of this downhill left curved roadway. Vehicle two (V2), a 2004 Mazda Tribute SUV, driven by a 26 year-old male was leaving an apartment complex, driving north in the first lane of the exit road of the complex, attempting a left turn at the intersection of both roadways. Within the intersection of both roadways, the front of V1 impacted the left side of V2. V1 rotated counter clockwise and came to rest within the intersection, facing northeast. V2 rotated counter clockwise after the impact, and came to rest within the intersection, facing northeast. The vehicle was driven back to the driveway of the apartment complex. V1 was towed from the scene, V2 was driven from the scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1 at the time of the crash she was pregnant in the last trimester, but claimed she was not injured. She was on her lunch break on the way to her residence. She had just started the lunch break and was in no hurry to return to work. She stated she did not see the vehicle coming from her right until it was too late. V 2 pulled out in front of her.
An associated factor was a possible sight line obstruction (tree) for V2.
