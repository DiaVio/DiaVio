A single-vehicle ran off roadway collision occurred shortly after noon in daylight hours. The collision occurred on a two-way, three-lane undivided roadway. The roadway is a sharp curve left with a radius of 80 meters (262 feet) with bituminous paving. The weather was clear and the roadway was dry. The roadway has a posted speed limit of 80 kmph (50 mph). Chevron alignment signs are installed on the outside of the curve at right angles to the approaching traffic. The signs are plainly visible to approaching traffic.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2005 Mazda RX-8 with one occupant, was traveling eastbound in lane one. V1 entered the curve and initiated a slight counter clockwise yaw as it departed the roadway via the right shoulder. The front of V1 impacted the ground and some brush. The vehicle continued in a circuitous path as it came to rest facing northeast. As V1 came to rest the rear bumper impacted the ground. V1 was towed due to damage. The driver was not transported. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.
The driver of V1 is a 32 year-old male, whose pre-crash factors are unknown, as he was not interviewed.
