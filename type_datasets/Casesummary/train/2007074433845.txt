This single-vehicle crash occurred on an entrance ramp in an interchange area. This roadway is one-way, curves to the right in the precrash area, has a downhill grade, has a negative superelevation and consists of one lane. The speed limit for this roadway is 97 kmph (60 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours and it was raining.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2005 Jeep Wrangler, was northbound on the ramp. V1 began to rotate clockwise. The driver steered left and V1 rotated counterclockwise. V1 traveled off the left side of the road and the right side struck a delineator post in the separator area. V1 continued to the northwest across the main travel lanes and the front struck the median jersey barrier. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.
The driver of V1, a twenty-four-year-old female, was on her way from a friend's house to another friend's house. She was healthy, well rested and traveled this route daily. She had only been driving a manual transmission vehicle for two weeks and stated that she was distracted by shifting gears prior to the crash.
