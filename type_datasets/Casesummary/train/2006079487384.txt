This two-vehicle collision occurred on a five-lane, undivided, dry, level, asphalt roadway. The speed limit on this road is 56 kmph (35 mph). The road has parking on the right shoulder and traffic was congested. This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2003 Nissan Altima was southbound in the second lane. Vehicle two (V2), a 2000 Ford Windstar was stopped in the first lane. As V1 was traveling it came across a slower moving vehicle in the second lane. V1 then changed lanes to the first lane where V2 was stopped to let a non-contact vehicle parallel park. The front plane of V1 impacted the rear plane of V2. V1 came to rest in the second lane. V2 came to rest in the second lane. Both vehicles were towed with major damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, a sixteen-year-old male, was driving to get this vehicle washed. This driver had been driving for about fifteen minutes at the time of the crash. This driver has a provisional drivers license with one year worth of driving experience. This driver stated that when he came around the vehicle in the second lane, V2 was stopped and he could not avoid the impact. This driver drives this vehicle daily and over this road on a daily basis.
The driver of V2, a forty-seven year old female, was heading to drop the passengers off at a soccer game. This driver indicated that she stopped and was waiting for the vehicle in front of her to park when one of the passengers opened the door to get out. This driver has twenty-two years of driving experience. This driver drives this vehicle daily and over this road on a weekly basis.
