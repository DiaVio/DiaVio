This two-vehicle crash occurred on an urban six-lane highway. This roadway is curved to the left, level and has no defects. The number six-lane is an HOV lane. The north/south highway is designated 104 kmph (65 mph). This crash occurred during the morning rush hour in daylight with no adverse atmospheric conditions. Traffic was congested.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1996 Nissan Altima was southbound in the number five lane. V1 was decelerating in his travel lane due to congested forward traffic. Vehicle two (V2), a 1991 Honda Accord was southbound in the number four lane. Vehicle two changed lanes to the left into the number five lane and the front of vehicle two impacted the rear of vehicle one. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Unknown for V1 and Unknown for V2.
The driver of V1, a thirty-four year old male, refused the interview.
The driver of V2, a sixteen-year-old male, refused to be interviewed. In the police report narrative, this driver indicated that he was looking in his left side mirror as he changed lanes.
