This two-vehicle crash occurred just past dawn during daylight hours in a rural mountainous area on a two-lane roadway with various curves and elevations. The roadway involved was under construction and there were warning signs and flags present for both directions of travel. Warning signs for a bump in the road were also posted. The posted speed limit for the construction area was 56 kmph (35 mph). The westbound roadway curved left and was on a slight uphill elevation prior to the area of impact. The eastbound roadway also curved left with a downhill elevation.
Vehicle one (V1) was a 1999 Ford F-Series van-based motor home with an 2.4 meter long (8 feet) box trailer in tow. V1 driven by a 45 year-old male who was traveling eastbound when the towed trailer became unhitched after traveling over a bump in the road. V1 continued east, unaware of the event. The trailer continued east, departed the eastbound lane and crossed over into the westbound lane where it contacted the left front bumper of Vehicle two (V2), a 1995 Jeep Wrangler driven by a 59 year-old male. V2 had been traveling westbound when it was contacted by the trailer. After the initial impact, V2 rotated counter-clockwise then tripped and flipped to the right 2 quarter turns. V2 came to rest on it's top partially on the guardrail and partially on the paved shoulder. The trailer from V1 came to rest in the eastbound lane facing north. V2 and the trailer were towed from the scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is 56 kmph for V1 and 56 kmph for V2.
