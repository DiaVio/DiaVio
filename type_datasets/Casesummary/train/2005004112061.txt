The crash occurred in the southbound lane of a two-lane highway with a T-intersection. Conditions were daylight and dry with cloudy skies in the mid-afternoon on a weekday. The roadway had a posted speed of 45 MPH and the southbound lane had a negative 2% grade.
Vehicle #1 was a 2003 Ford F350 super duty extended cab pick-up traveling south. Vehicle #2 was a 2004 Honda Civic coupe EX traveling south in front of V1. V2 was braking and drifting to the right side of the road then tried to make an illegal left-handed U-turn. V1 steered left but the front right corner of V1 struck the left side of V2. V1 pushed V2 across the road coming to final rest just south of the impact. V1 was facing south and V2 was facing north. After coming to final rest, V2 drove up and over a median to get out of the way of oncoming traffic. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
Vehicle 1 was driven by a 34-year-old male who sustained minor injuries and did not receive any medical attention. He had stated that he was traveling south when he saw the other vehicle (V2) in front of him braking and drifting towards the right shoulder. V2 then attempted to make a U-turn and the driver of V1 swerved left. The driver of V1 was traveling less than 40 MPH. V1 was driven from the scene.
Vehicle 2 was driven by a 17-year-old male who was treated by EMS at the scene for minor injuries but was not transported. The driver of V2 stated that he was lost so he decided to move to the shoulder then make a U-turn. He did not see the white truck behind him and was struck on the left side then pushed to final rest across the roadway. He stated that he was traveling less than 5 MPH at the time of impact. V2 was towed due to damage.
