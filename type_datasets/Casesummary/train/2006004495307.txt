This crash occurred in the early evening of a weekday in the northeast corner of a T-intersection. The intersection was composed of an undivided four-lane east/west roadway intersected by a divided two-lane north/south roadway. The bituminous east/west roadway was a dry, straight, and level with a posted speed limit of 72kmph (45 mph). The traffic flow was light and there were no adverse atmospheric conditions at the time of the crash.
V1, a 1998 Jeep Cherokee Limited with three occupants, was eastbound in lane two approaching the intersection where it intended to turn left. V1 did not slow down as it attempted its left hand turn, lost traction and started to yaw right. V1 rolled over two quarter-turns as it departed the roadway and struck a tree on the northeast corner of the intersection. V1 made one more quarter turn, for a total of three, before coming to rest on the passenger side. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.
The driver of V1, an18-year-old male, reported bleeding of the elbow/lower arm/hand, and was transported to the emergency room. Passenger 1 was a 16-year-old female, seated in the 1st row left seat, she reported bleeding of the elbow/lower arm/hand, and was transported to the emergency room. Passenger 2 was a 15-year-old female, seated in the 2nd row left seat, she reported contusion/bruising/abrasions to the chest, and was transported to the emergency room. Driver states he was traveling at approximately 72kmph (45 mph), when he attempted a left hand turn. The vehicle then began to slide then rolled over, striking the tree. V1 was towed due to damage.
