This single event, two-vehicle crash occurred at the intersection of two five-lane, two-way, divided busy urban roadways. The intersection is controlled with traffic lights with left turn arrows in all four directions. The roadway is straight and level. This crash occurred on a clear sunny weekday in the early evening hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one, a 1993 Pontiac Grand Prix was eastbound in the third lane (left turn only lane). Vehicle two, a 2004 Chevy Silverado Pickup was westbound in the second lane. Vehicle one turned left at the intersection and the front of vehicle one impacted the front of vehicle two. Both vehicles came to rest in the intersection. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of vehicle one, an eighteen-year-old female was heading home from a social visit at a friend's house, a few blocks from the scene. This driver had just started driving. This driver was taking Imitrex, Ibuprofen and birth control medications. She stated she drives this road a few times per month and this vehicle on a daily basis. A partial interview was conducted and the driver would not answer the question about cell phone use. Both witnesses and the driver of vehicle two remarked that the driver was in a conversation immediately following the crash.
