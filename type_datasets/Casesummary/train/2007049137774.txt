The single-vehicle crash occurred on a divided, east/west roadway just prior to a railroad crossing. There are three travel lanes for westbound traffic in the precrash area. The westbound lanes were dry, are curved to the left curve (the radius of curvature was 225 meters (738 feet) and have an uphill grade. The railroad crossing is appropriately marked with signs and signals. There are no traffic controls for the westbound lanes at the intersection. The posted speed limit is 56 kmph (35 mph). Traffic was light at the time of the crash. This crash occurred at dawn with clear skies.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1991 Honda Accord with one occupant, was westbound in the second travel lane. V1 traveled off the road on the right side and the front of the vehicle struck the metal pole with railroad crossing crossbucks and flashing red signal lights. V1 came to rest off the roadway facing southwest. It was towed due to damage. The driver was not transported. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.
The driver of V1, a forty-one-year-old male, was not interviewed. No further information is available for this driver.
