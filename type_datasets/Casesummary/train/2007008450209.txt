The crash occurred in the eastbound lane of a two lane undivided roadway. There was one eastbound lane and one westbound lane. This was a curved roadway with an 80-meter radius, a 7.5 percent super elevation and a posted speed limit of 56 kmph (35 mph). It was afternoon on a weekday and the skies were cloudy and it was raining off and on. Traffic flow was light and moving.
V1, a 1999 Ford Escort with one occupant, was traveling west. V2, a 2001 GMC Savana large van with one occupant, was traveling east. As V1 negotiated the curve, the vehicle skidded into the eastbound lane of traffic and the left front of V1 sideswiped the left side of V2. V2 was driven off the roadway, and V1 came to final rest within 3 meters of impact, blocking both lanes of traffic. Police Reported Travel Speed is Unknown for V1 and Unknown for V2.
V1, the Ford Escort, was driven by a 23-year-old female who was transported with a minor injury. She advised she was returning from the store and slid on the wet road into oncoming traffic.
V2, the GMC van, was driven by a 41-year-old male who reported no injuries. He advised he was a plumber and was en route to a job and was struck by V1 on V2's left side. He refused an interview.
