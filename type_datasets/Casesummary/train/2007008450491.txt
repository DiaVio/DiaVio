This crash occurred in the southbound lane of a two-lane undivided roadway. There was one southbound and one northbound lane. This was a level left-curve, bituminous roadway with a posted speed limit of 40 kmph (25 mph). It was afternoon on a weekday and the road was dry and skies were clear. Traffic flow was light and moving freely.
V1, a 2005 Chrysler Town and Country minivan with one occupant, was traveling south in lane one. V2, a 2003 Hyundai Sonata sedan with one occupant, was stopped in lane one. V3, a 2000 Chevrolet Cavalier coupe with two occupants was making a right turn. The driver of V1 did not stop in time to avoid striking V2 in the rear with the front of V1. V2 then struck V3 in the rear. All three vehicles came to rest in lane one. Police Reported Travel Speed is Unknown for V1 and Unknown for V2.
V1, the Chrysler Town and Country, was driven by a 27 year old male who reported no injuries. He advised he was running an errand for his parents. He claimed V2 and V3 had backed out on to the road without looking and he struck them. Upon further investigation, it was learned that V2 and V3 were stopped waiting to turn right in lane one.
V2, the Hyundai Sonata, was driven by a 58 year old female who was transported for complaint of pain in the back. The driver of V2 stated she was stopped in the lane behind V3 for approximately 10 sec or more and her daughter was the driver of V3 who had also stopped for 10 seconds or more to turn right.
V3, the 2000 Chevrolet Cavalier, was driven by a 29 year old female who reported no injuries. No interview could be obtained from the driver of V3.
