This two-vehicle crash occurred during early morning hours on a freeway with three lanes north/south with a positive barrier. It was dark but lighted roadway surface asphalt,dry and free of defects. There were no adverse atmospheric conditions at the time. The posted speed limit 96 kmph (60 mph).
Vehicle one (V1), a 1995 Toyota Corolla sedan driven by a 33 year-old male was traveling northbound in the first lane. Vehicle two (V2), a 1971 Chevrolet C10 Pick-up driven by a 21 year-old male was stopped on the shoulder of the same roadway. As V2 attempted to re-enter the roadway its rear was impacted by the front of V1. V2 rotated clock-wise and came to rest northward in an on ramp north of the collision. V1 continued forward and departing the roadway to the right and coming to final rest facing north. V1 and V2 was towed from the scene due to damage sustained in the collision. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V2 was well rested and familiar with the area, he had run out of gas pulled onto the shoulder and to put gas from can in bed into truck, but has no recollection of the crash after returning to his truck.
The driver of V1 was unwilling to speak with the researcher regarding the collision.
