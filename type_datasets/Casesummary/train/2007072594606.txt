This two-vehicle crash occurred on a jersey barrier-divided, straight trafficway. The southbound travel lanes are level and consist of three through lanes with paved shoulders on both sides and a curb on the right side. The speed limit for this roadway is 89 kmph (55 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1992 Honda Civic, was southbound in lane two. Vehicle two (V2), a 2001 Oldsmobile Bravada, was southbound in lane one. V1 attempted to change lanes to the right and the front struck the left side of V2. V2 traveled to the right and the driver steered left. V2 rotated counterclockwise and the right rear wheel struck the curb. V2 then rolled over to the right two quarter-turns. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, a twenty-two-year-old male, was not interviewed. No further information is available for this driver.
The driver of V2, a forty-year-old male, was on his way from work to a friend's house. He was healthy, well rested and familiar with his vehicle. He travels this route weekly. This driver stated that he did not see V1 prior to the first event. He further stated that he was not very comfortable with the steering of his vehicle when it was windy.
