A single-vehicle collision occurred during the mid-morning daytime hours on a Monday. The collision occurred on a two-lane, one-way roadway. The lane is an on-ramp/junction from a North/South Interstate to an East/West State route. Traffic was moderate with the morning commute. The pre-crash roadway is a slight downhill grade, and a slight curve to the right, with bituminous paving. There are curve warning-signs posted in the pre-crash area of the curve. The weather was clear and the roadway was dry. The roadway has a posted speed limit of 72 kmph (45 mph).
Vehicle one (V1), a 1992 Ford Aerostar minivan, was traveling westbound in lane two. The vehicle departed the roadway to the left, then returned and crossed both lanes for approximately 95 meters (311 feet) before departing the roadway via the right shoulder. V1 then returned onto the roadway again crossing both lanes to the left for approximately 24-meters (78-feet), and departing the left shoulder. V1 traveled up a ramp-like embankment and impacted the embankment with its front left corner, and then traveled forward rolling over onto its right side. V1 came to rest facing westbound on the right side in lane two. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.
The driver of V1 was a 39-year-old female. The driver's pre-crash factors are unknown, as she was not interviewed. The police report indicates that the driver's nature of injuries was a "seizure", and further states in the narrative: "...Driver of #1 was unconscious and low pulse. Medical problem with #1 was determined".
