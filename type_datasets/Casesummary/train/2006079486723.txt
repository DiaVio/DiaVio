A two vehicle daytime collision occurred on a cloudy Monday morning. The collision occurred on a four lane, dry, level, asphalt roadway with left turn pockets. The roadway runs east/west and is designated 56 kmph (35 mph). The collision occurred in the crosswalk just before the intersection. The intersection's controlled by a tri-light signal that was working properly at the time.
Vehicle one (V1) is a 1990 Toyota Corolla four-door sedan, traveling west stopped in the number two lane. Vehicle two (V2) is a Peterbilt model 320 straight truck, traveling west in the number two lane. V2 is a city garbage truck. V1 stopped at the intersection on a yellow light. The front of V2 impacted the rear of V1, which caused V1 to travel into the intersection. V1 was then driven to the north curb on the west side of the intersection. V2 continued southwest where it came to rest in the number two and three lanes facing southwest. V2 was driven with minor damage. V1 was towed with moderate damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1 is a 51 year-old female with 20 years of driving experience. The driver of V1 was on her way to work after dropping off her daughter at the babysitter. This driver was on her way to a new job this morning. She was listening to the radio at the time of the crash. She has had no driver's training. She said she saw V2 in her rearview just before impact. This driver drives this vehicle daily and over this road n a weekly basis.
The driver of V2 is a 42 year-old male. He refused to be interviewed, however he did say that he thought the other driver would continue on the yellow light.
