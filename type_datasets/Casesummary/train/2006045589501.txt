The crash occurred at the intersection of a north/south arterial roadway and an east/west side street. The arterial had two through lanes in each direction as well as right and left turn lanes. The side street had one lane in each direction with turn lanes. The large intersection was controlled by traffic signals. Traffic counts from 2003 indicate the north/south artery carried 33,060 vehicles per day in the crash area with a posted speed limit of 64kmph (40mph). Conditions at the time were dark with streetlights illuminated just before dawn on a weekday morning.
Vehicle #1 was a 2000 Nissan Sentra traveling north on the arterial road. Vehicle #2, a 1999 Dodge Neon, was southbound on the arterial in the left through lane. The Sentra (V1) attempted to turn left and go west on the intersecting side street and struck the left side of the Neon (V2) with its front. Based on investigation, both vehicles had solid green lights. After impact, Vehicle #1 continued west and came to rest facing west in a parking lot on the northwest corner of the intersection. Vehicle #2 rotated 180 degrees counterclockwise and came to rest facing north off the southwest corner of the intersection. No one involved required medical attention. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
Vehicle #1 was driven by a 74-year old male who was belted with deployed frontal airbags. He stated that he saw several other southbound vehicles go by but didn't see Vehicle #2. Police charged him with failure to yield.
Vehicle #2 was operated by a 28-year old male who was belted with no deployed airbags. Through a translator he relayed that he was going straight through the intersection with a green light when the other vehicle turned into him. Police charged him with no driver's license and no insurance.
