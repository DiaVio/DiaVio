A four-vehicle collision occurred late afternoon during daylight hours. The collision occurred in the southbound lanes of a divided highway, in lane two of an interchange area. The pre-crash roadway for the vehicles is a straight and level concrete roadway. The roadway has a posted speed limit of 96 kmph (60 mph) and traffic was congested.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2002 Ford ZX2 2 door, driven by a 21 year-old female was traveling southbound on the roadway in lane two. Vehicle two (V2), a 2005 Nissan Maxima sedan driven by a 27 year-old male was traveling southbound in lane two. Vehicle three (V3), a 2003 Mitsubishi Montero SUV driven by a 27 year-old female with two passengers was traveling southbound in lane two ahead of V2. Vehicle four (V4) a 1994 Honda Civic sedan driven by a 22 year-old female was traveling southbound in lane two, ahead of V3. The front of V1 impacted the rear of V2 when traffic ahead came to a fast stop. V2 was pushed forward impacting the rear of V3, which was stopped. The front of V3 impacted the rear of V4, which was stopped. V1, V 2, and V3 were all towed due to damage. V4 was driven from scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is No PAR obtained (created) for V1 and No PAR obtained (created) for V2.
The driver of V1 was a 21 year-old female whose pre-crash factors are unknown. The driver of V2 was a 27 year-old male whose pre-crash factors are also unknown. The driver of V1 is married to the driver of V2 per trooper on scene. The driver of V3, a 27 year-old female, was traveling home from a store with her mother and sister. They were having a conversation about everyday stuff, and she was not distracted. She is reportedly in good health with no hearing or sight restrictions. She was well rested, and familiar with the roadway as she drives it several times a month. V4 was not a case vehicle, and the details and factors for this vehicle are unknown.
