This two-vehicle crash occurred on a two-lane asphalt constructed roadway. This roadway runs north to south and has a posted speed limit of 105 kmph (65 mph). This roadway is straight and level for both directions. This crash occurred during daylight with no adverse weather and no obvious defects.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2003 Chevrolet pickup truck was northbound in the first lane. Vehicle two (V2), a 1999 Ford Windstar was northbound in the first lane ahead of V1. The front of V1 impacted the rear of V2. V2 departed the roadway and rolled over. As V2 rolled over, it impacted a tree with the front plane and a rock with the back plane. V2 came to rest on its right side. V1 was towed due to damage. V2 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 113 kmph for V1 and 113 kmph for V2.
The driver of V1, a twenty-four year old male was driving home after spending a week driving around. This driver had been driving for the last thirteen hours on this trip. This driver's last sleep was almost thirty hours before the crash occurred. This driver stated that he was only about ten kilometers (six miles) from home and was in a hurry to get there. This driver stated that he must have closed his eyes and when he opened his eyes V2 was there. This driver drives this vehicle daily and over this road on a monthly basis.
The driver of V2, a thirty-seven year old female was driving to her mother's house for a visit at the time of the crash. This driver had been driving for almost three hours at the time of the crash. This driver was taking Levothroid as a medication. This driver drives this vehicle daily and over this road only yearly.
