This two-vehicle crash occurred at a controlled intersection during morning daylight hours. There were no adverse conditions at the time of the crash. The east/west roadway is a two way, two lane, undivided, dry asphalt roadway that is straight and level through the crash location. Traffic crossing the intersection is controlled by stop signs. The north/south roadway is also a two way, two lane, undivided, dry asphalt roadway that is straight with a slight incline for traffic approaching the intersection from the north. The speed limit for both roadways is 80 kmph (50 mph).
Vehicle one (V1), a 1996 Chrysler Town and Country van, was traveling east and approaching the intersection. Vehicle two (V2), a 1998 Mazda pick up truck, was traveling south approaching the intersection. V2 was following a large truck that was to turn right, westbound, at the intersection. The driver of V1 stopped at the intersection, saw the large truck making the right turn, but did not detect V2 following the truck. V1 accelerated across the intersection and the front struck the right side of V2. V1, at impact, rotated clockwise as it came to rest in the southeast quadrant of the intersection. V2 also rotated clockwise as it came to rest facing northeast in the northbound lane. V2 was towed from the scene. None of the occupants in either vehicle were injured. Police Reported Travel Speed is Unknown for V1 and Unknown for V2.
