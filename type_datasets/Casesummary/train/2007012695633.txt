The crash occurred at an intersection on a 5-lane undivided roadway. The intersection was controlled by a traffic signal. There were two eastbound lanes and two westbound lanes divided by a left-turn lane. This was a straight, bituminous roadway with a downhill grade and a posted speed of 56 kmph (35 mph). It was early evening on a weekday and the road was dry and the sky was clear. Traffic was flowing.
V1, an unknown year Chevrolet Suburban, was traveling westbound in lane three. V1 was approaching the intersection intending to turn left. V2, a 1997 Chevrolet Blazer utility vehicle with one occupant, was traveling eastbound in lane two. V2 was approaching the intersection intending to go straight. The driver of V2 stated that he saw V1 approaching the intersection about 100 feet back but thought he would yield the right of way. V1 turned at the light and the front of V2 contacted the right of V1. V1 fled the scene. V2 came to final rest in the intersection facing east. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
V1, the Chevrolet Suburban, was driven by an unknown age driver who fled the scene. No further information could be obtained.
V2, the Chevrolet Blazer, was driven by a 44-year old male. He reported minor injuries and was treated on scene. He stated that he had just left the car wash and was heading to the expressway to dry it off. He stated that he had been traveling 50-64 kmph (31-40 mph) prior to attempting a full ABS application. He could not avoid contacting V1.
