This two-vehicle crash occurred during early afternoon hours on a clear day. The crash occurred at a four leg intersection that was controlled with two stop signs on the east and west legs. The east leg is a two-lane, bituminous roadway with a positive slope of 6.5% as it joins the intersection. The north leg has four-lanes and is curved to the west with a radius of curvature of 161-meters (and a super-elevation of 2%. The north leg has a posted speed limit of 56 kmph (35 mph). Adjacent to the north leg is a two-lane two-way access road that joins the east leg of the intersection and is controlled by a yield sign. The Access road has a posted speed limit of 40 kmph (25 mph).
Vehicle one (V1), a 2000 Chevrolet C1500, had driven north on the access road that intersected the east leg of the intersection and stopped at the yield sign. The driver wanted to turn left and cross through the intersection. The driver stated that he looked left from the yield sign and there was a vehicle coming and then he looked right and it was clear. He looked left again and it appeared to be clear so he pull out to make a left turn and impact with the right side of V2 near the right front door & B pillar. After this impact the front plane of V2's trailer impacted with the left front fender of V1. Both vehicles came to rest in the intersection. V2 was driven from the scene & V1 was towed due to disabling damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 8 kmph for V1 and 8 kmph for V2.
