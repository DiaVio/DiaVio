This two-vehicle crash occurred during morning daylight hours on a clear day. Both vehicles were northbound in lane one on a four-lane, median divided, and level bituminous roadway. The roadway is level at pre crash. This side of the road has two lanes.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2005 Nissan Altima, was northbound on a five-lane, median divided bituminous roadway. V1 was traveling in lane one with the cruise control set at 105 kmph (65 mph). Vehicle two (V2), a 2004 Ford F 350, was also northbound in lane one traveling in front of V1 at approximately (40 kmph (25 mph) following a Boom Mower Tractor that was off of the right road edge. The front of V1 impacted the back of V2. V1 went off the left side of the road and impacted a road sign and came to rest in the median. V2 came to rest on the right shoulder. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 105 kmph for V1 and 105 kmph for V2.
The driver of V1 is a healthy 21-year-old female who is very familiar with the roadway. The driver was also familiar with the vehicle. The driver owns a 2003 Nissan Altima, which was being service, and she was driving the 2005 as a loaner. The second row occupant, a two year old had dropped a cup and was asking the driver to get the cup. The driver said in a minute and then the driver turned around to face and talk to the occupant and when she turned back around contact with V2 had been made and the air bags deployed.
V2's driver is a 57-year-old female who was familiar with the roadway and the vehicle. The driver was following a tractor back to the service shop for safety purposes. The tractor was off of the roadway on the right roadside. V2 had strobe lights on for safety.
