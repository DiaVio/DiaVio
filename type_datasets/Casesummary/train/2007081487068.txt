This two-vehicle crash occurred a couple hours past noon during daylight hours. The location was a busy intersection controlled by overhead on-color signals. The roadway is a bituminous five lanes with two lanes in each direction and a center left turn only lane. The crash occurred in the intersection. The weather was clear and dry and visibility was clear. The posted speed limit was 56 kmph (35 mph).
Vehicle one (V1), a 2000 Jeep Cherokee SUV driven by a fifty four (54) year old female, was facing east prior to turning left to head north. Vehicle two (V2) was a 2002 Chevrolet Tahoe SUV driven by a thirty four (34) year old female with two passengers. V2 was traveling westbound through the intersection when V1 turned left in front of it, and the two vehicles collided head-on. The front of right corner of V1 impacted with the front left corner of V2. V1 rotated around and came to rest facing westbound. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Aid was refused by both drivers. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1 stated the three vehicles ahead of her proceeded, and she followed them through the intersection. The left arrow light had disappeared, and the westbound traffic was approaching. This driver did not see the other vehicle until a split-second before impact.
