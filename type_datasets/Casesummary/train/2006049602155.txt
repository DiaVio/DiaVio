This two-vehicle crash occurred in a four-leg intersection during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions. The northbound roadway is one-way with two lanes, has a slight downgrade and a slight curve to the right approaching the intersection. Stop signs and a red flashing signal control this roadway. The eastbound roadway is one-way with two lanes, has a slight downgrade and a slight curve to the left approaching the intersection. This roadway is controlled by a flashing amber signal. The speed limit for both roadways is 64 kmph (40 mph).
Vehicle one (V1), a Dodge Dakota, was northbound in lane one. Vehicle two (V2), a 1995 Ford Contour, was eastbound in lane one. In the intersection, the front of V1 struck the right side of V2. V2 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, an unidentified male, left the scene in his truck. No information is available for this driver or his vehicle. The driver of V1 did not stop for the stop sign,or yield the right-of-way to V2.
The driver of V2, a twenty-year-old male, was traveling, with an eighteen-year-old female, from a store to his home. He was healthy, well rested and familiar with his vehicle. This driver travels on this roadway weekly.
