This two-vehicle crash occurred shortly after noon on a clear day. The crash occurred at a four-leg intersection with all legs controlled with traffic signals on color. The north to south roadway is a six lane divided roadway without a positive barrier. It is dry, bituminous, straight and has a down hill slope. The east to west roadway is straight, level and is a three lane divided roadway without a positive barrier. The speed limit on both roadways is 72 kmph (45 mph).
Vehicle one (V1), a 1985 Ford Tempo driven by a 71 year-old male with one passenger, was southbound in lane two. Vehicle two (V2), a 1999 Ford Explorer SUV driven by a 24 year old male and one passenger, was westbound in lane one. V1 failed to stop for the traffic signal and entered the intersection and was struck on the left side by V2's front. Post impact V1 rotated counter clockwise and came to final rest in lane one of the southbound lanes facing northeast. V2 stopped at impact then the driver drove the vehicle out of the roadway. Both vehicles were towed due to disabling damage. The driver and passenger of V1 were transported to a local medical facility. Police Reported Travel Speed is 72 kmph for V1 and 72 kmph for V2.
