V1, a 2002 Lincoln Continental, was westbound in lane 1 of a straight, level, 2 way, 2 lane, rural, gravel/slag roadway. At the time of the crash it was dark-unlighted and the roadway was dry. V1 exited the roadway to the right. V1, front right, contacted a tree >10cm that caused the vehicle to re-enter the roadway and roll 2/4 turns left onto its roof. V1 came to a stop on its roof facing north. The unrestrained passenger was partially ejected through the sunroof during the rollover and was under the vehicle at final rest. Of the 2 occupants of v1, the driver was injured and transported while the passenger was dead at the scene. V1 was towed due to damage.
A complete driver interview and vehicle inspection was obtained for v1. The driver was found to be familiar with both the vehicle and the roadway and was properly licensed. She was not in a hurry and had been driving for about an hour. The vehicle seemed to be in good mechanical condition. The Pre-event movement of v1 was going straight, the Critical pre-crash event was coded as This vehicle traveling off the edge of the road on the right side and the Critical reason was coded as an Unknown regcognition factor. A major factor contributing to the cause of the crash was the driver's BAC alcohol level. This may have interacted with the medications she was taking to further inhibit her ability to control the vehicle. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.
