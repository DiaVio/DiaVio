This crash occurred in the afternoon in the middle of a four-way intersection controlled by lights in all directions. There were no adverse atmospheric conditions at the time of the crash, and the roadway was dry, straight, and level for both vehicles. V1, a 1995 Buick LeSabre, was traveling east in lane one where the posted speed limit was 64kph (40mph). V2, a 2000 Volvo S40, was stopped at the red light facing south in lane one of a one-way roadway with a posted speed limit of 40kph (25mph).
V1 intended to continue straight through the intersection. V2 was stopped at a red light waiting for the light to change. When the light changed, V2 proceeded into the intersection where its right side was struck by the front of V1. Vehicle two rotated counter-clockwise approximately 90 degrees before coming to final rest. Vehicle one came to final rest shortly after the impact. Police Reported Travel Speed is Unknown for V1 and Unknown for V2.
V1 was driven by a 76 year-old male in good health who was wearing his prescription lenses that corrected a myopic (near-sighted) condition. In an interview with this driver he claimed he had a green light when the collision occurred and never saw V2. The driver did not suffer any injuries as a result of the impact but V1 was towed due to damage.
The driver of V2 was a 45 year-old male who could not be located for an interview. According to statements given by to the police the driver stated he was stopped at a red light and proceeded into the intersection where he collided with V1. V2 was driven into a nearby parking lot and both the driver and passenger with transported to a medical facility with unknown injuries.
