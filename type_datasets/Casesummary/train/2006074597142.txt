This two-vehicle crash occurred at the intersection of a three-lane roadway and a five-lane roadway. The three-lane road is undivided, curved left, with a negative one percent grade, and a speed limit of 40 kmph (25 mph). The three-lane road is controlled by a stop sign. The five-lane roadway is a divided, concrete, straight roadway with a negative five percent grade. The speed limit for the road is 72 kmph (45 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1994 Toyota Camry was westbound in the second lane, and had stopped at the stop sign to wait for traffic to clear before turning left. Vehicle two (V2), a 1996 Buick Le Sabre was northbound in the first lane. V2 turned right at the intersection and departed its lane and the front of V2 impacted the left of V1. Both vehicles were towed from the scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, a sixty-seven year old female had just left the hospital after a monthly cancer check-up. This driver was driving to do some shopping. This driver had been driving for about five minutes at the time of the crash. This driver indicated that they were stopped and waiting. This driver drives this vehicle daily and over this road on a monthly basis.
The driver of V2, a fifty-two year old female had left work about thirty minutes ago. The driver of V2 had gotten a call from the hospital that her father was dying. The driver of V2 had been under a lot of stress all week going back and forth to the hospital looking in on her father and checking his condition. When the driver of V2 received news of her father's health, the driver left work in a hurry to get to the hospital. This driver was looking right as she made the turn and also took the turn too fast. This driver drives this vehicle daily and only rarely over this road, but the frequency had increased with the visits to the hospital for her father.
