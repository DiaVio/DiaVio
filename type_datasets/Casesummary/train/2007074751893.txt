This two vehicle crash occurred mid morning during daylight hours. The asphalt roadway was ice covered since a light rain occurred overnight . This four-lane, curved roadway is divided with a raised median and two lanes in each direction of travel. There were no traffic controls in the crash location and a posted speed limit of 104 kmph (65 mph).
Vehicle one (V1) was a 2000 Ford Ranger pick-up driven by a thirty three (33) year-old male. V1 was traveling south in the right lane on an uphill section of the roadway, following another vehicle negotiating a curve to the right. Vehicle two (V2) a 1999 Ford F-250 pickup driven by a forty seven (47) year-old male was traveling north in the second lane going around the left curve. The driver of V2 noticed V1 start to lose control and cross the median into the northbound lanes. The driver of V2 tried to slow and change lanes to the right but V2's front impacted with front right of V1 This impact directed V1 and V2 into the guardrail off the right side of the northbound roadway. Both vehicles came to rest against the guardrail and both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
