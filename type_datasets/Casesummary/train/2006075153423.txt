This two-car crash occurred in the first northbound lane of a six-lane undivided roadway. There were three lanes in the northbound direction with two through lanes and one left turn lane and three lanes in the southbound direction. This was a straight, slightly downhill, bituminous roadway with a posted speed of 56 kmph (35 mph). It was early afternoon on a weekend and the road was dry and the sky was clear. The traffic flow was steady.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1994 Chevrolet Blazer was northbound in the first lane. Vehicle two (V2), a 1996 Chrysler Cirrus was northbound in the first lane ahead of V1. V2 slowed to turn right at an intersection. The front of V1 impacted the rear of V2. V2 departed the roadway and the front of V2 impacted a stop sign and a bush. V2 came to rest on all four tires on top of the bushes. V1 came to rest on all four tires in the northbound right turn lane. Per the PAR both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 56 kmph for V1 and 56 kmph for V2.
The driver of V1, a sixteen-year-old female, was on her way to her sister's house from home. This driver indicated that she saw V2 slowing to turn right and she stepped on the brakes. This driver stated that the brakes did not stop the vehicle. She stated that she has worked an average of seven hours in the past seven days and normally gets nine hours of sleep per night. This driver had only been driving for four months. This driver drives this vehicle daily and over this road on a weekly basis.
The driver of V2, a twenty-two year old female was not interviewed. There is no pre-crash information for this driver.
