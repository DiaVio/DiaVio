This two-vehicle crash occurred in a traffic signal-controlled intersection. The east/west roadway is level and has two lanes in each direction and a dedicated left-turn lane. The westbound lanes are curved to the left approaching the intersection. The north/south roadway is level, straight and consists of one lane in each direction and a left-turn lane. The speed limit on both of these roadways is 64 kmph (40 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2001 Dodge Intrepid, was northbound in lane one approaching the intersection. V2, a 2006 Dodge Dakota, was westbound in lane two, also approaching the intersection. The driver of V1 did not realize that her traffic signal was red. In the intersection, the front of V1 struck the left side of V2. Both vehicles came to rest west of the intersection, facing west, and were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, a thirty-one-year-old female, was traveling from her home to an appointment. She was healthy, well rested and familiar with her vehicle. She drives on this roadway daily. She stated that she had "personal problems" and was experiencing stress learning a new job. She also stated that she was not thinking about anything specific when she did not notice that the traffic signal was red.
The driver of V2, a seventy-five-year-old male, was traveling with a seventy-four-year-old female, was driving from their home to get his vehicle licensed. He takes Furosemide, Glucophage, an unspecified medication for high blood pressure medication, Aciphex and aspirin. He was well rested and very comfortable with his route, vehicle and passenger.
