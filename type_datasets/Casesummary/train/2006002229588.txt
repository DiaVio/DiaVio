This crash occurred in the evening of weekday in the northbound lanes of a divided high-speed interstate highway. The north/south highway consisted of two lanes in each direction divided by a grassy median. The bituminous highway was dry, straight, and level with a posted speed limit of 105 kmph (65 mph). There were no adverse atmospheric conditions but the roadway was dark and unlit.
Vehicle 1 (V1), a 2005 Freightliner box truck, was northbound in lane one of the highway. Vehicle 2 (V2), a1992 Hyundai Excel, was also northbound in lane one ahead of V1. As V2 continued north, two mattress strapped to it roof began to come lose and slide backwards obstructing its taillights. The driver of V1 braked and came to a stop in the traffic lane shortly before it was rear-ended by the front of V1. V1 came to final rest facing north on the east shoulder. V2 came to final rest in the median next to a guardrail facing north. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, an unbelted 46-year-old male was not injured, but was very shaken up. He claimed this was his very first accident. He stated he was waiting for traffic in lane two to pass so he could pass V2. He did not see any brake or signal lights on V2 and did not realize it had stopped until it was too late. He stated he was traveling about 113 kmph (70 mph) when the crash occurred.
The driver of V2, a belted 54-year-old male was transported to an area trauma center with a head injury. Unsecured cargo in the rear of the vehicle, a shelving board, impacted with the back of the drivers head. This driver could not be reached for an interview. The driver of V2 was issued a summons for obstructed taillights.
