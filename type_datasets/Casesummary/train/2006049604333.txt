A crash involving two vehicles occurred a few hours before midnight at an intersection without traffic lights. The speed limit for the roadway is 56 kmph (35 mph). The road is a 4-lane divided (no positive barrier) concrete roadway with dry travel lanes. It was dark but lighted and there were no detrimental atmospheric conditions at the time of the crash.
Vehicle one (V1) was a 2002 GMC Yukon driven by a 51 year-old male with one passenger. It was traveling north in the fourth lane attempting a left turn onto the on-ramp of a highway. Vehicle two (V2) was a 1992 Volvo 940 driven by a 38 year-old female with one passenger. It was traveling south in lane two of the southbound lanes, attempting to pass through the intersection. At the intersection, the front of V2 impacted with the right side of V1. V2 came to rest within the intersection, facing southwest. V1 continued to drive about 50 meters (164-feet) from the point of impact onto the on-ramp before it stopped. Both vehicles were towed from the scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
