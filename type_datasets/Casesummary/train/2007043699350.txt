The crash occurred in the northbound lanes of a three-lane, divided roadway at an industrial entrance. There were three southbound lanes including a left turn lane on a level grade left curve. There were three northbound lanes including a left turn lane on a 2% uphill grade. The roadway had a posted speed limit of 56 kmph (35 mph). Conditions were daylight and dry with cloudy skies. The setting was midday on a weekday.
Vehicle one was a 2000 Dodge Dakota pickup traveling south in lane three negotiating the left curve and preparing to turn left. Vehicle two was a 2005 Ford Taurus station wagon traveling north in lane two intending to go straight. Vehicle one turned left as vehicle two proceeded straight. The right plane of V1 was impacted by the front of V2. Both vehicles then came together in a sideslap with the right side of V1 striking the left side of V2. V1 then traveled off the northeast corner of the intersection, coming to rest on the roadside. V2 came to rest in the southeast corner of the intersection. Police Reported Travel Speed is 32 kmph for V1 and 32 kmph for V2.
The Dodge Dakota (V1) was driven by a 23-year old male who was transported to a local trauma center with possible injuries. He was unable to be located for an interview. The investigating officer estimated his travel and impact speeds to be 32 kmph (20 mph) and 16 kmph (10 mph), respectively. The Dodge pickup was towed due to damage.
The Ford Taurus was driven by a 57-year old female who was transported to a local trauma center with non-incapacitating injuries. She stated that she was heading home from work when the other vehicle suddenly turned in front of her. She said she didn't have a chance to react. Her estimated travel and impact speeds were 56 kmph (35 mph). The Ford station wagon was towed due to damage.
