The crash occurred in the intersection of two roadways. The north/south roadway was two-way with two lanes in each direction, and the east/west roadway was two-way with one lane in each direction. The roadways were straight and level, and it was dry and clear with no adverse conditions. Both roadways had a posted speed limit of 48 kmph (30 mph). It was during the afternoon hours of a weekday.
V1, a 2002 Ford Explorer sport utility vehicle with one occupant, was traveling northbound in lane one. V2, a 1998 Honda CR-V sport utility vehicle with one occupant, was traveling westbound in lane one. At the intersection, the front of V1 contacted the left side of V2. Both vehicles came to a final rest in the center of the intersection, facing west. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
V1, the Ford Explorer, was driven by a 48 year-old male who reported minor injuries and was transported to a medical facility. He reported that he was traveling through the intersection with a green light, and the driver of V2 ran the red light. V2, the Honda CR-V, was driven by a 48 year-old female who reported incapacitating injuries and was transported to a medical facility. She reported that she was traveling through the intersection with a green light, and the driver of V1 ran the red light.
A witness who was traveling southbound reported that she had stopped for the red light and observed the driver of V1, who was traveling northbound on the same roadway, run the red light. The witness advised that the driver of V1 was traveling too fast to have been able to stop for the light. She advised that V2 had just started up from a stop into the intersection and was struck by V1.
