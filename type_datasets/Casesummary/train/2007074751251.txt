This two-vehicle crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions. The east/west trafficway is straight, divided by a painted median in the crash area and consists of two lanes in each direction. The speed limit for this trafficway is 64 kmph (40 mph). The westbound approach lanes have an uphill grade in the precrash area. The alley on the north side of the east/west trafficway is straight, has a downhill grade in the southbound direction and a speed limit of 24 kmph (15 mph).
Vehicle one (V1), a 2002 Ford F150 pickup, was southbound in the alley. Vehicle two (V2), a 1995 Honda Civic, was westbound in lane two. V1 entered the roadway turning right and began to change lanes to the left. The front of V2 struck the back of V1. V2 traveled off the right side of the roadway and the front struck a concrete wall. V2 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, a sixty-one-year-old male, and his fifty-seven-year-old wife were on their way to work from their home. This driver was healthy, well rested and comfortable with his vehicle and passenger. He travels this route daily. He takes the medication Hyzaar. This driver stated that he did not see V2 prior to the crash.
The driver of V2, a twenty-two-year-old male, was traveling with a twenty-two-year-old male passenger. They were on their way to a fast food restaurant on their way to work. This driver was healthy, well rested and comfortable with his vehicle and passenger. He travels this route daily. According to witnesses, this driver was traveling at a high rate of speed and weaving between lanes prior to the crash. This driver was cited for careless driving. The tires on this vehicle were not properly inflated; the left front tire was overinflated 26 psi and the right rear tire was underinflated by 17 psi.
