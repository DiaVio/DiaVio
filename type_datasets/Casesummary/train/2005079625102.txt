This two-vehicle collision occurred during daylight hours on a weekday. The crash occurred at the intersection of a north-south roadway and an east-west roadway. The intersection is controlled by a tri-light traffic signal, and the speed limit is 56 kmph (35 mph) in all directions. At the time of the crash, there were no adverse atmospheric conditions and the roadway was free of defects.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2001 Nissan Xterra, was traveling northbound in the second lane of a five-lane, two-way, undivided, dry, level, bituminous roadway, approaching the intersection with the intention of passing straight through. Vehicle two (V2), a 2000 Toyota Camry four-door sedan, was traveling westbound in the first lane of a five-lane, two-way, undivided, dry, level, bituminous roadway, approaching the intersection with the intention of passing straight through. As both vehicles entered the intersection, the front of V1 impacted the left side of V2. Both vehicles deflected northwest and came to rest in the intersection. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
