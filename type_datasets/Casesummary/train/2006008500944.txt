This crash occurred in the southbound lanes of a four-lane roadway divided by a grass median. There were two southbound lanes and two northbound lanes. This was a level concrete roadway that curved slightly to the left with a posted speed limit of 89 kmph (55 mph). It was mid afternoon on a weekday, the roadway was dry, and conditions were clear. Traffic flow was moderate with a construction zone merge point in the right-hand southbound lane.
V1, a 2005 Saturn Ion four-door driven by 20-year old female, was traveling southbound in lane two behind V2. V2, a 2006 Jeep Grand Cherokee driven by a 56-year old male, was also traveling southbound in lane two. Two vehicles from lane one quickly changed lanes in front of V2 due to a construction lane merge point in lane one. According to the drivers of V1 and V2, the sign to merge was not placed at a sufficient distance from the merge point to properly react to it. When the other vehicles changed lanes, the driver of V2 applied his brakes hard. The driver of V1 also applied her brakes. The driver of V1 stated that she had been daydreaming and was not able to react fast enough to avoid the collision.
