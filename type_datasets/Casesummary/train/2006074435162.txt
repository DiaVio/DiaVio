This two-vehicle collision occurred in a four-way traffic signal controlled intersection. Both roads have five-lanes of travel, there are two lanes in either direction and one left turn lane. Both roads are straight and level with no defects. The speed limit on both roads is 56 kmph (35 mph). This crash occurred during nighttime hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2000 Pontiac Grand Prix was eastbound in the second lane. Vehicle two (V2), a 1994 Ford Tempo was northbound in the second lane. As V1 approached the intersection the traffic was stopped and V1 changed lanes in to the second westbound lane and entered the intersection. The front of V2 impacted the right rear of V1. V1 rotated clockwise and departed the south road edge and the left side of V1 impacted a utility pole. V1 came to rest against the pole. V2 continued and came to rest north bound in the first travel lane outside of the intersection. V1 was towed due to damage. V2 was towed but not due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, a twenty-year old male had just picked up the two passengers. The passenger in the back seat was having difficult time breathing and the driver was taking him to the hospital. This driver was talking to the passenger trying to get directions. The driver of V1 illegally passed the vehicles stopped at the intersection and entered the intersection on a red light. This driver stated that on this trip that he had only been driving for about four minutes. This driver drives this vehicle daily and only rarely on this roadway.
The driver of V2, a seventeen-year old female was heading to a friends house after leaving school. This driver had been driving for about two minutes at the time of the crash. This driver did not see the car until after it entered the intersection. This driver drives this vehicle daily and over this road on a daily basis.
