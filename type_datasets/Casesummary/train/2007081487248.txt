This two-vehicle crash occurred a few hours after noon on a Monday. The location was in the northbound lanes of a busy interstate highway that was congested. This roadway was a bituminous three-lane divided roadway. The weather was clear, the road surface was dry and visibility was clear. The posted speed limit was 97 kmph (60 mph).
Vehicle two (V2), a 1986 Ford Ranger pickup with one occupant, was stopped in lane two because of congested traffic ahead. Vehicle one (V1), a 1999 Jeep Cherokee 4 door SUV with one occupant, was traveling northbound in lane two. A non-contact vehicle crossed from lane one to lane three, right in front of V1. The driver of V1 swerved to the left into the HOV lane to avoid the non-contact vehicle. Then V1 realized he was in the path of a commercial vehicle and swerved back to the right, when he realized V2 was stopped in traffic. The front of V1 impacted the rear of V2. Both vehicles were driven to the right shoulder where they were towed from the scene. The driver of V2 was transported. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1 is a 66 year-old male who reported he was in good health with no hearing restrictions. The driver stated another vehicle cut across in front of him to change from lane one to lane three. There was no indication of any vehicle related factors for V1 from the vehicle inspection. He was traveling from his office to a business appointment. He was not in a hurry. He was well rested, with 8 hours of sleep. This is a route that he drives daily.
The driver of V2 is a 69 year-old male whose pre-crash factors are unknown as he was unable to be reached for interview.
