The crash occurred ten meters east of a three-leg intersection, in the eastbound traffic lane. The north/south roadway was a two-way, two-lane undivided street. The east/west roadway was a two-way, two-lane undivided street, controlled by a stop sign. The posted speed in all directions was 48 kmph (30 mph). These were straight, level, asphalt roads. Conditions were daylight on a weekday morning with dry roads and clear skies. Traffic flow was normal.
V1, a 2006 Chevrolet Silverado pickup with one occupant was traveling northbound. V2, a 1996 Oldsmobile Cutlass Ciera with one occupant, was stopped facing eastbound, ten meters east of the intersection. V2 made a right turn at the intersection. Immediately following the right turn, the front of V2 made contact with the rear of V1. V2 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 40 kmph for V1 and 40 kmph for V2.
V1, the Cutlass Ciera, was driven by a 29-year old male, who was not injured. He stated that he was doing an errand for a friend. The driver indicated that he was on a cell phone obtaining directions to a house. While the driver was talking, he was also looking for the address. He said that as he made the right turn, his focus was to his left. He said that just as he turned his head forward, he hit the vehicle that was stopped in front of him.
