This two-vehicle crash occurred mid-week shortly after noon. The location was in the five northbound travel lanes of a divided freeway with a guardrail protecting the inside left shoulder. It was raining, the road was wet, level, concrete; it was daylight and there were no view obstructions. The posted speed limit was 97 kmph, or 60 mph.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1986 Toyota MPV mini-van, was traveling northbound in the HOV lane. Vehicle two (V2), a 2003 Pontiac Bonneville SE 4 door, was traveling northbound in the HOV lane ahead of V1. The driver of V2 observed a school bus with it flashers on stopped ahead and begin to slow. The scholol bus had stopped behind a disabled vehicle in front of it. The driver stated he was traveling approximately 56 kmph (35 mph) when his vehicle was struck in the left rear by V1. The driver of V1 was unable to stop in time and struck the rear of V2. V1 was driven to the far right shoulder per the police officers instruction. V1 was then towed due to damage. On impact V2 rotated clockwise across all five lanes and came to rest on the outside shoulder facing east. V2 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Unknown for V1 and Unknown for V2.
