The crash occurred on a northbound entrance ramp for a high volume, high occupancy interstate. The ramp was curved to the left and was level. The ramp consisted of one lane with a right side emergency paved shoulder. The skies were clear and it was early evening on a weekend night. The remains of a previous crash was located on right should of the road.
V2, a 1989 Toyota Corolla, was disabled and left unattended in the middle of the entrance ramp. V1, a 2001 Jeep Cherokee, was just getting on the entrance ramp to the interstate when the turned to look at the previous crash on the right side shoulder. As she slowly refocused her attention back to the roadway she saw disabled vehicle and tried to avoid it by braking tried to hit the brakes, but skidded into the back of the disabled vehicle. Police Reported Travel Speed is Unknown for V1 and Unknown for V2.
V1 was driven by a 25 year-old male in good health who was wearing his glasses that corrected a myopic (near-sighted) condition. Neither the driver nor the passenger of V1 sustained any injuries and was towed due to damage.
