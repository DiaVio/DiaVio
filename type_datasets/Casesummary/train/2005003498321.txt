The crash occurred in the eastbound lanes of an undivided two-way roadway with two lanes in each direction. The roadway was straight and level and it was clear with no adverse conditions. It was dark but lighted and had a speed limit of 48 kmph (30mph). The crash occurred in the evening hours of a weekday.
Vehicle #1 was a 2000 Jeep Grand Cherokee that was traveling eastbound in the right lane and attempting to make a U-turn. Vehicle #2 was a 2001 Nissan X-Terra that was traveling eastbound in the left lane. The front of Vehicle #2 impacted the left side of Vehicle # 1 as Vehicle #1 turned in front of it. This impact spun Vehicle #1 counter-clockwise and its right side impacted Vehicle #3, a parked and unoccupied BMW 325xi (Vehicle #3) on the left side. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The Jeep Grand Cherokee (Vehicle #1) was driven by an 82-year old male who sustained minor injuries and was transported to a local medical facility. The Grand Cherokee was not towed due to damage.
The Nissan X-Terra (Vehicle #2) was driven by a 29-year old male who did not sustain any injuries. He stated that he was going straight when Vehicle #1 darted out in front of him while attempting to make a U-turn. The driver of Vehicle #2 attempted to brake and steer right but could not avoid Vehicle #1. The X-Terra was towed due to damage.
The BMW 325xi (Vehicle #3) was legally parked and unoccupied on the westbound parking lane of the roadway. The BMW was not towed.
