This two-vehicle crash occurred in a three-legged intersection. The north/south roadway is undivided, level and straight and consists of one lane in each direction. The speed limit on this roadway is 89 kmph (55 mph). The intersecting roadway is on the west side of the north/south roadway. This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2006 Dodge Ram pickup, was northbound in lane one. Vehicle two (V2), a 2002 Kubota tractor with a front-end loader, was in front of V1, making a left turn in the intersection. V2 is a construction vehicle with flashing amber caution lights on and a slow-moving vehicle sign on the back. V2 is not equipped with rearview mirrors. V1 moved into the southbound lane to pass V2. The front of V1 struck the left side of V2. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 89 kmph for V1 and 89 kmph for V2.
The driver of V1, a twenty-nine-year-old male, was on his way to work from his home. He was healthy, well rested and familiar with his vehicle. He travels this route daily. This driver stated that he did not realize that V2 was turning left.
The driver of V2, a twenty-year-old male, was on his way from a job site to his workplace. He was healthy, well rested and familiar with his vehicle. He travels this route daily. This driver stated that he signaled his left turn with his arm since his vehicle is not equipped with turn signals. He also stated that he did not see V1 prior to the crash.
