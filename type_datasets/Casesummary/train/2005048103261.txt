This two-vehicle crash occurred at just before midnight at the "T" intersection of a two-lane, two-way dry, level, bituminous north/south roadway and a two-lane, two-way, dry, uphill, bituminous roadway that intersects the north/south roadway from the west. The roadway curves to the right slightly for westbound traffic. The intersection was controlled by a stop sign for westbound traffic. The roadway intersects the northbound roadway at a 50-degree angle. It was dark but lit at the time of the crash and there were no adverse atmospheric conditions. The posted speed limit for the north/south roadway was 89 kmph (55 mph) and the westbound roadway was 56 kmph (35 mph).
Vehicle one (V1), a 2000 Chrysler Cirrus was traveling westbound approaching the intersection. Vehicle two (V2), a 2002 Ford Explorer was traveling northbound and pulling a trailer loaded with musical equipment. V1 entered the intersection attempting to turn left while V2 passed through the intersection going straight. The front of V2 contacted the left side of V1. V1 deflected to the right and came to rest off of the roadway facing southwest. V2 rotated counterclockwise and came to rest on the roadway facing south. While V2 was rotating towards final rest, its trailer contacted the left rear of V2. Police Reported Travel Speed is 64 kmph for V1 and 64 kmph for V2.
