This one-vehicle crash occurred on a weekday at nighttime on a two-way, two-lane north/south roadway. The bituminous roadway was dry, straight, and level, and was not physically divided. There were no traffic control devices associated with this stretch of roadway. The posted speed limit was 56 kmph (35 mph). There were no adverse atmospheric conditions at the time of the crash and traffic flow was normal.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2004 Hyundai Elantra, was traveling north on the roadway intending to continue straight. The vehicle drifted off of the right side of the roadway and struck a utility pole. After the impact, the vehicle rotated clockwise approximately 45 degrees before coming to final rest in the northbound lane. The vehicle had to be towed from the scene due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 48 kmph for V1.
The driver of V1, a 41 year-old male, suffered minor facial abrasions as a result of the crash and was transported to the hospital for treatment. There were no other occupants in the vehicle when the crash occurred. A complete interview was obtained from the driver of this vehicle. He related that he has diabetes and low blood sugar caused him to leave the roadway and strike the utility pole. The driver also relayed that he had not eaten for approximately 7-8 hours prior to the crash. He felt the symptoms of low blood sugar but didn't think he was totally unconscious when he struck the utility pole. The driver stated that he was only driving for a few minutes after making a pizza delivery. He was familiar with the roadway as well as his vehicle. Other than the diabetes, the driver claimed to be in good health. He was taking insulin to help control his blood sugar as well as Zetia and Simvastatin to help control his cholesterol level. The driver stated that his vehicle was in good working condition and that no mechanical problems contributed to the crash.
