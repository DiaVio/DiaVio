This two-vehicle crash occurred in a four-legged intersection. The east/west roadway is straight, level and divided by a curbed median. Both approaches consist of three through lanes and a left turn lane. The speed limit on this roadway is 64 kmph (40 mph). It was dark with streetlights on at the time of the crash with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1989 International moving truck, was westbound in lane four. Vehicle two (V2), a 1998 Ford F150 pickup, was eastbound in lane one. V1 attempted to make a U-turn in the intersection. As V1 turned, the driver realized that there was not enough room to make the U-turn and stopped south of the intersection. V1 backed into the intersection and the back was struck by the front of V2. V2 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, a forty-eight-year-old male, was on his way from his new house to his old house. He was healthy and well rested, although he had been moving household goods all day. He travels this route weekly. This driver stated that he was comfortable driving this vehicle even though he had never driven it until he rented it the morning of the crash to make this move. He further stated that he did not see V2 prior to backing into the intersection.
The driver of V2, a thirty-year-old male, was on his way home from a friend's house. He was well rested, healthy and familiar with his vehicle. He travels this route daily. This driver stated that he applied the brakes and steered left when he noticed V1 re-entering the intersection.
