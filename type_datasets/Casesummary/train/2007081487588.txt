This two-vehicle crash occurred in the evening hours at dusk. The location was at a busy intersection. The roadway was a bituminous five-lane undivided roadway. The impact location is in the intersection. The weather was cloudy and raining. Visibility was fairly clear. Traffic flow was controlled by overhead on-color signals. The posted speed limit was 64 kmph (40 mph).
Vehicle one (V1), a 1999 Volvo S80 sedan driven by a thirty four (34) year old male, was in lane three turning from eastbound to the northbound freeway on-ramp. Vehicle two (V2) was a GMC H3 Hummer SUV driven by a fifty two (52) year old male with two passengers. V2 was traveling westbound in lane two. As V1 was making a left turn, V2 impacted the right side of V1, and pushed it in a northwest direction where it came to rest facing southeast. V1 was towed due to damage. V2 was driven from the scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1's pre-crash factors are unknown, as he was unable to be reached for interview.
