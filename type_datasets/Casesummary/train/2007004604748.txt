The crash occurred in the center of a four-way intersection composed of a two-lane north/southbound roadway and a two-lane east/westbound roadway. All roadways were bituminous, level and dry. The speed limit for the north/southbound traffic was 64 kmph (40 mph) and 40 kmph (25 mph) for the east/west bound traffic. Stop signs controlled the east/westbound traffic. It was night on a weekday. Traffic flow was light.
V1, a 1999 Volkswagen Jetta with one occupant, and V2, a 2004 Lexus IS-300 with one occupant, were traveling south when V1 signaled to turn left. V2 entered the shoulder to pass V1 on the right when V1 abruptly changed his mind and attempted to turn right. The front right side of V1 struck V2, causing V2 to mount the curb and strike a street sign before coming to final rest. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
V1, driven by a 21-year-old male who reported no injuries, stated that he started to turn left when he realized that he needed to turn right, so he immediately changed the direction of his turn and hit V2 as it attempted to pass him on the right. No avoidance maneuver was attempted.
V2, driven by a 21-year-old female who reported a complaint of pain and was transported, stated that she was following behind V1 when he signaled to turn left, so she entered the shoulder in an attempt to pass V1 on the right. As she was passing, when V1 suddenly changed direction and struck her vehicle. No avoidance maneuver was attempted.
