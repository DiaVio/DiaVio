This two-vehicle crash occurred in a four-legged intersection. The north/south roadway was straight and consisted of one lane in each direction. The northbound approach has a three percent downhill grade. This roadway is controlled by a stop sign. The speed limit on this roadway is 40 kmph (25 mph). The east/west roadway is straight, level and divided by a curbed median. The westbound approach consists of two through lanes and a left turn lane. The speed limit on this roadway is 56 kmph (35 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2000 GMC G3500 school bus, was stopped northbound at the stop sign. Vehicle two (V2), a 1996 Subaru Legacy, was westbound in lane two. V1 entered the intersection, going straight, and the right side was struck by the front of V2. V1 rotated clockwise and rolled over onto its left side. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, a seventy-two-year-old male, was traveling from the bus parking yard to the school. His passenger was a fifty-four-year-old female supervisor. This driver had seven hours of sleep the night before the crash, which is two hours less than normal. He has high blood pressure for which he takes an unspecified medication. This driver has been driving this school bus for six years and stated that he was comfortable with it. He travels this route daily. This driver stated that he looked both ways before entering the intersection and did not see V2 prior to the crash.
The driver of V2, a seventeen-year-old male, had just left his school and was on the way to pick up his brother at school. He was healthy, well rested and comfortable with his vehicle. He travels this route daily. This driver's license indicates a vision restriction and this driver was wearing non-prescription sunglasses.
