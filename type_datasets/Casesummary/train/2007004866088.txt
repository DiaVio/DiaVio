The crash occurred in the center of a four-way intersection composed of a four-lane north/southbound roadway, and a four-lane east/westbound roadway. All roadways were bituminous, level and had posted speed limits of 64 kmph (40 mph). The intersection was controlled by traffic lights on each corner. It was afternoon on a weekday. The roads were dry and the sky clear. Traffic flow was moderate.
V1, a 2006 Toyota Corolla with two occupants, was traveling east when it entered the intersection without observing the traffic signal and was struck by V2, a 2005 Toyota sienna with one occupant, as it traveled south. Neither vehicle made an attempt to avoid the collision. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
V1, driven by a 75-year-old female who reported a no injuries but was transported for observation, stated that she was stopped at the intersection when she just decided to go. The driver stated she was not quite sure why she proceeded through the intersection. V1 was also occupied by a 52-year-old male that reported no injuries. He was not transported to the hospital. V1 made no avoidance maneuvers
The Driver of V2, a 31-year-old female, reported no injuries and was not transported to the hospital. Driver stated that she was passing through the intersection with the green light when V2 ran the signal. V2 made no avoidance maneuver.
