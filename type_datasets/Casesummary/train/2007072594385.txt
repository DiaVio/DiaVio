This two-vehicle crash occurred in a four-legged, traffic signal-controlled intersection. Both roadways are straight, level and undivided. All approach lanes consist of two through lanes and a left turn lane. The speed limit for these roadways is 48 kmph (30 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1990 Lincoln Town Car, was eastbound in lane two. Vehicle two (V2), a 2003 Honda Element, was southbound in lane two. In the intersection, the front of V2 struck the left side of V1. V1 continued to the southwest and the front struck a pole on the southwest corner of the intersection. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, a twenty-four-year-old male, was not interviewed. He stated to the police officer that he was avoiding a vehicle behind him that had struck him earlier. The driver of V2 and a witness corroborated this statement, adding that V1 was traveling at a high rate of speed.
The driver of V2, a sixty-one-year-old male, was on his way from work to a store. He was healthy and familiar with his vehicle. He travels this route daily. This driver stated that he saw V1 as he entered the intersection and did not have time to avoid the crash. He also stated that he felt fatigued at the time of the crash.
