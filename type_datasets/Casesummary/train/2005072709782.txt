A two-vehicle crash occurred during the morning daylight rush hour at an urban intersection. The north/south roadway is undivided and comprised of two through lanes and a left-turn lane in each direction. The east/west road is divided by a rumble strip median, and has two lanes in each direction, on the east leg of the intersection. The west leg is undivided and has one lane going westbound and two lanes and a left-turn lane on the eastbound side. Traffic control lights that were working properly at the time of the crash control the intersection, in all directions. The speed limit is 58 kmph (35 mph).
Vehicle one (V1), a 2002 Dodge Durango with two passengers, was traveling northbound in the second lane through the crossover when it was struck on its right side by the front of vehicle two. Vehicle two (V2), a 1999 Chevrolet Caviler with one occupant, was traveling westbound in the second lane. Both vehicles were reported not towed on the police report, but the damage they received during the crash made both vehicles undrivable. None of the occupants were injured or transported due to injuries in the crash. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
