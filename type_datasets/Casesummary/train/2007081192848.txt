This two-vehicle crash occurred shortly after noon during daylight hours. The location was an interstate freeway in an urban area with a grass center median. The roadway was concrete, with five southbound lanes. The far left lane was an HOV lane and there were paved shoulders on both roadsides. The road was dry and it was clear. The posted speed limit was 97 kmph (60) mph.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1992 Chevrolet Suburban SUV, driven by a thirty nine (39) year old male was traveling southbound in lane two. For unknown reasons V1 drove right off onto the shoulder and struck the rear of a semi trailer parked on the shoulder. According to the witness there were no pre-crash avoidance actions taken. V1 under-rode the rear of the semi trailer and stopped. The driver was fatally injured. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
Vehicle two (V2), a 2002 Freightliner CST120 tractor pulling a 2007 Yanc semi trailer, was parked on the right southbound shoulder. The driver stated the tractor had overheated and he stopped to assess the problem. He stated he had been parked for approximately two minutes before his trailer was struck from behind by V1. V2 was not towed due to damage.
According to his wife, the driver of V1 was coming home from work for lunch. He drives the road daily and was familiar with the vehicle. He had a medical history of being diagnosed with an enlarged heart, but had not experienced any problems the past 12 years. The day before the crash the driver saw his doctor because he was experiencing severe pain in his hands. His wife stated he was well rested the night before and did not appear to be having any medical problems when he left for work that morning. He was not taking any medications, according to his wife.
