This two-vehicle crash occurred on the approach to a merger of two limited-access roadways. Each roadway consists of three lanes which merge into five lanes. The eastern southbound lanes are downhill and curved to the left. The speed limit on this roadway is 97 kmph (60 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with heavy rain and limited visibility.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1993 Ford Taurus, was southbound in lane one. Vehicle two (V2), was southbound in lane one in front of V1. V2 decelerated due to upcoming standing water on the roadway. The front of V1 struck the back of V2. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, a nineteen-year-old female, was traveling from work to her home. She was healthy, well rested and familiar with her vehicle. This driver rarely drives on this roadway. She stated that she began to slow down due to the heavy rain, but never saw V2 prior to the impact.
The driver of V2, a twenty-four-year-old female, was traveling from work to her home. She was well rested, in good health and comfortable with her vehicle. This driver travels this route daily. She stated that she was slowing with the rest of the traffic in anticipation of the standing water on the roadway.
