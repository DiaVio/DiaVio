This three-vehicle crash occurred an exit ramp from a jersey barrier-divided trafficway. This roadway is one-way, straight, has an uphill grade and consists of two through lanes. The speed limit for this roadway is 80 kmph (50 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2006 Nissan Sentra, was westbound in lane one of the ramp. Vehicle two (V2), a 1997 Chevrolet Lumina, was westbound in lane one in front of V1. Vehicle three (V3), a 1991 Buick Regal, was westbound in lane two. There had been a crash earlier and there was a truck and police activity on the south side of the separator. V2 decelerated and the front of V1 struck the back of V2. V2 rotated counterclockwise and the front struck the right side of V3. V1 and V2 were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, a thirty-one-year-old female, was on her way from work to lunch. She was healthy, well rested and familiar with her vehicle. She travels this route daily. This driver stated that she was looking left at the police activity. When she returned her attention to the front and realized that V2 was slowing, she steered right and applied the brakes (skidding longitudinally).
The driver of V2, a twenty-three-year-old female, had stopped at a homeless shelter on her way to work. She was well rested, healthy and familiar with her vehicle. She travels this route daily. This driver stated that she was slowing for traffic and did not see V1 prior to the crash.
The driver of V3, a twenty-one-year-old male, He was healthy, well rested and familiar with his vehicle. He travels this route daily. This driver stated that he heard the crash between V1 and V2 and saw V2 rotating and traveling toward him. He steered left and applied the brakes in an attempt to avoid the crash.
