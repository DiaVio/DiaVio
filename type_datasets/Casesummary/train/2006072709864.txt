This two-vehicle crash occurred during daylight hours in the middle of an intersection. The northbound roadway consists of three lanes including a left turn lane south of the intersection and three lanes north the intersection. The southbound roadway consists of three lanes north of the intersection including a left turn lane and two lanes south of the intersection. Both sides roadway also have bicycle lanes along the curbside, and are divided by traffic medians with positive barriers. The east/west roadway is a two lane, not physically divided two-way traffic, with one lane going in each direction. The surface is straight and level, made up of bituminous asphalt, with no visible deficiencies. At the time of the crash there were no adverse weather conditions, and the traffic control lights were functionally properly. The speed limit was 48 kmph (30 mph).
Vehicle one, a 1998 Dodge Caravan was southbound in the first lane. Vehicle two, a 2006 Chevrolet Trailblazer was westbound in the first lane. The first event occurred when vehicle two was struck on its right side by the front of vehicle one. This caused vehicle two to head southwest, onto the curb, sideswiping a pole on its left side; contacting a sign with the front, and coming to a final rest. Vehicle one rotated in a clockwise direction and came to a final rest facing west. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is No PAR obtained (created) for V1 and No PAR obtained (created) for V2.
The driver of vehicle one, an unknown driver was not interviewed. The driver of vehicle two, an unknown driver was not interviewed.
