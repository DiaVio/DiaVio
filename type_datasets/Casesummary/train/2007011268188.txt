The crash occurred on a fully controlled highway with two lanes in each direction divided by a concrete median barrier, at the junction of another highway. The straight, level, bituminous roadway has a posted speed limit of 113 kph (70 mph). Conditions at the time of the weekday afternoon crash were daylight, clear and dry.
V1, a 2005 Mazda 3 four door sedan was driven by a 29 year-old female on her way home from work. V1 was traveling eastbound on the highway in lane one at 70 mph. The driver intended to exit the highway at the interchange to the ramp on the right side. An additional lane for the ramp began 198 meters prior to the impact area. The driver stated that she waited too long to merge over to the right and when she did the front of v1 struck the leading edge of the guardrail. The witness traveling behind v1 stated that he entered the exit lane and at the end of it v1, still traveling in lane one of the highway, activated her right turn signal and attempted to turn right onto the ramp, in front of the witness. Following the impact, v1 rotated counterclockwise and came to rest on the right shoulder of the ramp facing southeast. V1 was towed from the scene due to damage. The fully restrained driver refused medical treatment at the scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.
