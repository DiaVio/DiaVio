This single-vehicle crash occurred on a jersey barrier-divided, straight trafficway. The northbound travel lanes have an uphill grade, paved shoulders on both sides and consist of three through lanes. The speed limit for this roadway is 113 kmph (70 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1999 Honda Civic, was northbound in lane three. V1 traveled off the left side of the roadway and the front struck the jersey barrier. V1 continued northbound and the left side struck the jersey barrier. V1 returned to the roadway rotating clockwise, crossed all three lanes and traveled off the right side of the roadway. V1 then rolled over four quarter-turns. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 113 kmph for V1.
The driver of V1, a nineteen-year-old female, was on her way home from college (to another state). She was healthy, familiar with her vehicle and rarely travels this route. She stated that she was stressed recently due to the move back to her parent's house. This driver had been awake for more than twenty-one hours at the time of the crash. She had spent approximately seven hours prior to the crash packing for this trip. This driver had traveled 257 kilometers (160 miles) in approximately two-and-a-half hours at the time of the crash.
