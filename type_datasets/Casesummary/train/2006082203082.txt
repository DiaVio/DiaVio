This two-vehicle crash occurred on a straight, level, dry, jersey barrier-divided roadway. There are five southbound lanes; the number five lane is a high-occupancy lane. The speed limit for this roadway is 97 kmph (60 mph). This crash took place during daylight hours under cloudy conditions with rush-hour traffic congestion.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2002 Volkswagen Golf 4-door, was southbound in lane three. Vehicle two (V2), a 2004 Hyundai Elantra sedan, was southbound in lane four. V1 moved to the left for an unknown reason and the left side struck the right side of V2. V1 rotated counterclockwise and continued moving to the left. The front of V1 struck the median jersey barrier. V1 came to rest south of this impact facing east and was towed due to damage. Following the impact with V1, V2 moved to the left and the left side struck the median jersey barrier. V2 came to rest on the shoulder facing south and was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is No PAR obtained (created) for V1 and No PAR obtained (created) for V2.
The driver of V1, a twenty-six-year-old male, was driving from work to his home. He was well rested and was familiar with his route and his vehicle. He stated that he was looking for an opening to move two lanes to the right so that he could take the next exit. He denied purposely moving to the left. The driver of V2, a forty-two-year-old male, was driving from his home to a restaurant. He was comfortable with the roadway as well as his vehicle. He was well rested at the time of the crash and was not aware of V1 moving in to his lane.
