The crash occurred in the northbound lanes of an urban highway. Both Vehicles were in lane three approaching a left turn lane. It was evening on a weekday night. The conditions were dry and skies were clear. The roadway was level and straight and the traffic flow was normal.
V1, a 1996 Chevy Cavalier was traveling approximately 72 kmph (45 mph) in lane three when it impacted the back of V2, a 2000 GMC Medium/Heavy utility truck which had been in the area restoring power from a hurricane. The truck was stopped in lane three for about five seconds when he was hit from behind. Police Reported Travel Speed is 40 kmph for V1 and 40 kmph for V2.
V1 was driven by a 79-year-old female who claimed to be in fair despite a history of high blood pressure. She was on her way home from her part-time job and stated in her interview that she did not even see the truck as it was dark in color and the lights were very small on the back of the truck. The driver was not injured but the vehicle was towed due to damages.
V2 was driven by a 31-year-old male in good health who was coming from a work site. He stated that he was stopped in the turn lane for several seconds before he was struck by V1. The driver was uninjured and V2 was driven from the scene.
