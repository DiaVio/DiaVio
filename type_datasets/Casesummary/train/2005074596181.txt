This was a 2-vehicle crash occurred during early morning hours on a 4 lane, undivided, bituminous, level roadway. At the time of the crash, no adverse weather conditions existed and it was daylight. The posted speed limit is 56 kmph (35 mph).
Vehicle one, (V1) a 1998 red Ford Windstar minivan occupied only by the 39 year old female driver. V1 was heading north on a 4 lane undivided roadway in the second lane. The driver of V1 fell asleep at the wheel and crossed the centerline and struck the left side of vehicle two (V2). V2, a 1995 Peterbilt tractor pulling one trailer, was being driven by a 56 year-old male who was traveling southbound in the number two lane. V1 came to final rest approximately 73 meters (240 feet) north of the area of impact, facing north in lane one. V2 stopped just south of impact facing south in lane two. Both vehicles were towed from the scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1 had worked 8 hours the night before the crash. The driver's relief worker was 20 minutes late. The driver only had 10 minutes to get home to get her child to day care. At the time of the crash the driver was in a hurry. The driver of V1 has Asthma and has sporadic sleeping habits. She sleeps in roughly 2 hour intervals all day until she goes to work at night. The driver admitted that she is always tired at home and at work.
