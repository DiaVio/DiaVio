This two-vehicle crash occurred in a four-legged, traffic signal-controlled intersection. The east/west roadway is straight and divided by a curbed median. The westbound approach lanes have a downhill grade and consist of two through lanes and a left turn lane. The speed limit on this roadway is 64 kmph (40 mph). The north/south roadway is undivided. The southbound approach lanes are one-way, undivided, curved to the left (radius of curvature is forty meters), has a downhill grade and consists of a left turn lane, a right turn lane and one through lane. The speed limit on this roadway is 16 kmph (10 mph). It was dusk at the time of the crash and there were no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1985 Lincoln Town Car, was westbound in lane two. Vehicle two (V2), a 2001 Mitsubishi Eclipse, was stopped southbound in lane two. V2 entered the intersection and the left side was struck by the front of V1. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, a seventeen-year-old male, was traveling with an eighty-six-year-old female and a seventy-five-year-old female. He had just left a friend's house and was on his way to work. He was well rested, healthy and comfortable with his vehicle and passengers. He drives this route daily. This driver noticed V2 stopped at the intersection and assumed that it would remain stopped. When V2 entered the intersection, the driver of V1 applied the brakes and skidded (longitudinally) in an attempt to avoid the crash.
The driver of V2, a forty-six-year-old female, had just picked up her fourteen-year-old daughter from school and they were on their way home. This driver was healthy, well rested and comfortable with her vehicle and passenger. She drives this route daily. This driver stated that she was conversing with her passenger when she was stopped at the red traffic signal.
