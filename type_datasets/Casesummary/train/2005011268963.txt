The crash occurred on an undivided two-way, six-lane roadway on a clear, sunny weekend afternoon. The straight, concrete road had a slight downward slope and a posted speed limit of 72 kmph (45 mph). The three vehicles involved in the crash were all traveling southwest in lane three.
V1, a 2002 Ford Taurus driven by a 48-year old female, struck V2 in the back. This impact pushed V2 into V3. The front airbags of V1 deployed, and V1 was towed due to damage. The fully restrained driver was transported to a local trauma center due to a pre-existing heart condition. . Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
V2, a 2000 Buick Park Avenue Ultra driven by an 80 year-old female, was traveling around 40 mph when it was struck in the back by V1, and the front of V2 then struck the back of V3. The driver of V2 observed V1 through her rear-view mirror prior to the crash traveling at a higher rate of speed than V2. V2 had no time for avoidance maneuvers. V2 was towed with minor damage, and the fully restrained driver denied any injuries.
V3, a 1992 Oldsmobile Delta 88 driven by a 56-year old female, was traveling at about 40 mph when it was struck in the back by V2. The driver of V3 was looking straight ahead prior to the crash and took no avoidance maneuvers. V3 was drivable after the crash and the fully restrained driver complained of slight back and left hip pain but was not transported.
No Critical Pre-crash Event and no Critical Reason were coded to V3 because it was not involved in the first harmful event. An associated factor coded to this driver was her use of 10 unidentified prescribed medications for arthritis.
