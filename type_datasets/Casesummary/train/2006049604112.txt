The crash happened mid-afternoon during daylight hours. The location was a five lane divided (positive barrier, ten lanes overall) up hill highway, road surface dry concrete. The weather was clear and the road dry. The posted speed limit 97 kmph (60 mph).
Vehicle one (V 1) a 1995 Toyota Corolla, driven by a 32 year-old male was traveling south in the second lane of the roadway. The driver stated another vehicle cut into his lane. He applied his brakes and steered to the right to prevent a collision. V 1 drove into the first lane of travel and departed the roadway to its right. The driver was able to steer to the left to avoid a frontal impact with the concrete wall located west of the lanes of travel. The right side of V 1 impacted with the concrete wall. It caused the right rear wheel to separated from the vehicle. V 1 continued to move south in a clockwise rotation. It re-entered lane one and departed the lane and the roadway again. Its front impacted the concrete wall while in a clockwise rotation. The vehicle bounced of the wall and came to rest partially on the shoulder and partially in lane one, facing west. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.
The vehicle was towed from the scene. The driver was not injured.
