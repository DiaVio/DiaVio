This three-vehicle crash occurred during daylight hours on a straight, bituminous, level, two-lane, two-way roadway. The posted speed limit is 48 kmph (30 mph) and there were no traffic controls. There were no adverse atmospheric conditions at the time of the crash.
Vehicle one, 1999 Dodge Neon, was eastbound in lane one. Vehicle two, a 1989 Honda Civic, was stopped in front of vehicle one. Vehicle three, a 1989 Chevrolet Silverado pickup, was stopped in front of vehicle two, waiting to turn left into a parking lot. Vehicle three had been stopped for 3 to 4 seconds. Vehicle two had just stopped behind vehicle three. The driver of vehicle one tried to avoid the crash by applying the brakes but the front of vehicle one struck the back of vehicle two. Vehicle two was pushed forward and the front struck the back of vehicle three. Vehicles one and two came to rest facing east in lane one. Vehicle three came to rest in the parking lot facing north. Vehicles one and two were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 56 kmph for V1 and 56 kmph for V2.
The driver of vehicle one, a seventy-nine-year-old female, refused to be interviewed. The police report indicated that she was "distracted, other (i.e. food, objects, pet, etc.)", but the specific distraction is unknown. She did apply the brakes and skidded 3.7 meters (12 feet).
The driver of vehicle two, an eighteen-year-old male, was traveling from his girlfriend's workplace to his home. He was well rested, does not take any medications and was wearing his prescription contact lenses. He is very familiar with the roadway and his vehicle.
The driver of vehicle three, a fifty-three-year-old male, was driving from work to an auto repair shop. He was well rested and comfortable with his vehicle. He rarely drives on this roadway, but had stopped to turn into his destination. He takes aspirin once a day and was wearing his prescription sunglasses.
