This two-vehicle collision occurred a couple of hours after noon during daylight. The collision occurred on a three-lane undivided rural roadway with one lane in each direction and a center turn lane. The pre-crash roadway for eastbound traffic is a level curve to the right with bituminous paving. For westbound traffic the pre-crash roadway is straight and level approaching a curve to the left. The weather was clear and dry. The center lane was covered in old sand from a previous snow a few weeks prior. The posted speed limit for the roadway is 40 kmph (25 mph).
Vehicle one (V1) was a 1997 Acura 2.2 CL 2-door coupe driven by a seventeen (17) year old male with one passenger. V1 was traveling eastbound at a high rate of speed into the easterly curve to the right. Vehicle two (V2) was a 2005 Toyota Corolla sedan driven by a fifty two (52) year old male with one passenger. V2 was traveling in a westerly direction approaching the curve to the left. V1 lost control in the curve and drifted into the center turn lane, when he tried to steer back into his original lane, his tires lost traction in the loose sand and the vehicle continued to travel to the left into the oncoming lane, rotating clockwise. The front of V2 impacted the left side of V1. V1 pushed V2 backwards approximately 18 meters (60 feet). V1 came to rest facing northwest in the center lane. V2 came to rest facing northwest in lane one. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
