This crash occurred on a four lane divided highway that is slush-covered, asphalt with a posted speed limit of 113 Kmph (70 Mph). There were two southbound lanes and two northbound lanes with a grass median. Where the crash occurred there was an overpass that had a guardrail protecting the supports. The crash occurred during daylight hours, and the conditions present at the time of the crash were as follows, light blowing snow.
V1 a 2000 Buick Century was traveling southbound in the left curb lane passing a semi-tractor. V2 a 1999 Freightliner was traveling southbound in the right curb lane being passed by V1. V1 went into a counterclockwise rotation departing the roadway to the left striking a guardrail. V1 then reentered the roadway crossing two lanes of traffic and contacting V2. V1 was towed away from the scene with moderate damage, and the pregnant female passenger was transported to the hospital with minor injuries. V2 was not towed and the driver of V2 was uninjured. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1 a 25 year-old male stated that when he was passing the semi, that the back of his vehicle started to fishtail. He then stated that he hit the guardrail with the back right of his vehicle, which bounced him back across the roadway and into the semi truck.
The driver of V2 a 49 year-old male driver stated that he was traveling south bound in the right curb lane when he felt the trailer move, and when he looked back he saw V1 had hit it. He also stated that he never saw V1 before impact, and that V1 must have been in his blind spot.
