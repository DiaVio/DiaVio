The crash occurred during early morning hours. Three vehicles were involved at an intersection on a two-way, four lane (eight overall), divided, right-curved roadway without a positive barrier. The road was dry, traveled, level concrete. Traffic controlled by traffic lights. The posted speed limit is 56 kmph (35 mph). There were no adverse weather conditions.
Vehicle (V1) is a 1993 Ford Escort Wagon, driven by a 20 year-old female, traveling northbound in the third travel lane. V 2, a 2000 Chevrolet Silverado, driven by a 35 year-old male with two passengers, was stopped northbound in the fourth travel lane. V 3, a 1990 Toyota Corolla, driven by a 43 year-old male, was stopped northbound in the third travel lane. V1 departed its original travel lane to the left and the front struck the back of V2. V1 then returned to the third travel lane and the right side struck the left side of V3. V1 came to rest after striking V3, facing north. V2 rotated counterclockwise slightly and came to rest forward of its pre-impact position. V3 rotated clockwise slightly and came to rest forward of its pre-impact position. V1 was towed from the scene; V2 and V3 were driven from the scene. The driver of V1 was transported to a medical facility. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1 has no memory of the crash events and could not explain her reason for striking the other vehicles.
V3 was not involved in the first event and the critical reason was not coded to this vehicle.
