This two-vehicle crash occurred on a jersey barrier-divided, straight Interstate. The eastbound lanes are level and consist of five through lanes. The speed limit for this trafficway is 97 kmph (60 mph). It was dark with street lights on at the time of the crash, which was a few hours before midnight. There were no adverse atmospheric conditions at this time.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2008 Toyota Sienna, was eastbound in lane three. Vehicle two (V2), a 1995 Plymouth Neon, was eastbound in lane three in front of V1. The left front tire of V2 blew out and V2 came to a stop in lane three. The front of V1 struck the back of V2. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, a twenty-six-year-old male, was on his way from a gas station in another city and was taking his fifteen-year-old female passenger home. He was healthy, well rested and comfortable with his vehicle and passenger. He travels this route weekly. This driver stated that he was "surprised" by a vehicle that passed him in the lane to his right. He further stated that he did not see V2 but also stated that it did not look as if V2 was stopped. Evidence indicates that he steered to the left in an attempt to avoid the crash.
The driver of V2, a twenty-year-old female, was not interviewed. No further information is available for this driver. The police report and physical evidence indicate that the left front tire of V2 blew out prior to the crash and V2 was stopped in the travel lane.
