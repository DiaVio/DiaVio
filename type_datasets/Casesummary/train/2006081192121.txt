This single vehicle crash occurred in the evening hours on a weekend. The location was a rural highway in a residential area. It was dark and there were no street lights. The weather was cloudy with intermittent rain, however the road surface was dry at the time. The roadway was a level bituminous, undivided, two lane, two way with paved shoulders on both sides. The posted speed limit was 72 kmph (45 mph).
Vehicle one (V1), a 1990 BMW 325-i two-door coupe with two occupants, was traveling westbound on a straight level section of the roadway. The driver stated a large animal, possibly a dog, was standing in the road just ahead in the headlights. The driver swerved left, lost control and departed the left side of the roadway. V1 traveled through a wire fence and into a grassy field. V1 continued westbound in the field while the driver was trying to regain control. V1 was in a slight clockwise yaw when the front end dug into a mound of dirt at the fence line. V1 rolled to its left two-quarter turns and came to rest on its top in a private driveway facing north. V1 was towed due to damage. The passenger in V1 was transported. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.
The driver of V1 was a 49 year-old male. The driver was having a conversation with the passenger, his son, at the time the evasive action was taken. They were returning from a shopping trip and had been driving for approximately one hour prior to the crash. The driver has been diagnosed with type 2 diabetes, but was not ill at the time of the crash. He was well rested and familiar with both the vehicle and the roadway.
