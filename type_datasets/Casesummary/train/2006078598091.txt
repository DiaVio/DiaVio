This single vehicle crash struck the rear of a properly parked vehicle at the curb on a two- lane, residential roadway with no centerline markings. The crash occurred in the eastbound, number one traffic lane with no traffic controls present. This was a straight, level, bituminous roadway with a posted speed limit of 40 kmph (25 mph). It was early morning the road was dry but it had been raining very lightly and it was cool.
Vehicle one (V-1), a 1991 Ford Festiva 3-door hatchback, driven by a 31 year-old male was traveling eastbound in the number one lane of a two-lane, unmarked, residential roadway. The vehicle traveled slightly right and struck the rear of a properly parked, not-in-transport vehicle. The entire roadway width at the point of impact was 8.6 meters. V-1 struck the rear of the parked vehicle and stopped on the roadway. The not-in-transport vehicle was pushed forward and right onto a lawn and came to rest against a small, decorative railroad tie riser. V1 was towed from the scene. The driver told police he had the windshield wipers on interval control and was trying to clear the mist and moisture from the inside of the windshield with his right hand while steering with the left hand. Police Reported Travel Speed is 24 kmph for V1 and 24 kmph for V2.
