This two-vehicle crash occurred at the intersection of a five-lane road and a two-lane road. This intersection is controlled by a traffic light. The five-lane road is straight and level with no defects. The speed limit on the road is 56 kmph (35 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1997 Mitsubishi Eclipse was southbound in the number two lane. Vehicle two (V2), a 2000 Toyota Rav 4 was northbound in the third lane, a left turn lane. V2 turned left at the intersection and the right side was impacted by the front of V1. V2 was pushed off to the southwest corner where it rolled over. As V2 was rolling over, it impacted the building on the corner. V1 came to rest near the southwest corner. V2 came to rest on its roof against the building. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 93 kmph for V1 and 93 kmph for V2.
The driver of V1, a seventeen-year-old male, was heading to drop his girlfriend off. This driver had been driving for about five minutes at the time of the crash. This driver had eighteen months of driving experience. He was driving in an aggressive manner by speeding and changing lanes frequently. He was late to drop off his girlfriend at her home. His friend was also traveling at a similar speed behind him. This driver drives this vehicle daily and over this road on a daily basis.
The driver of V2, a nineteen-year-old female, was not interviewed. This driver is not licensed. Per this drivers medical records she was not taking any medications nor had any alcohol or illicit drugs. The PAR indicated that she saw the other vehicle further away and then proceeded into the intersection.
