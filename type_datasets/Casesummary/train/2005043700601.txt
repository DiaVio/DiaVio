The crash occurred in the eastbound lane of a two-way, two-lane, rural roadway. The roadway curved left before straightening out at a driveway entrance on the right. The roadway had a posted speed limit of 72 kmph (45 mph), +4% superelevation, and an uphill grade of 2% in the eastbound direction. Conditions were dry and dark with clear skies. The setting was early evening on a weekday.
Vehicle one was a 1991 White/GMC Volvo 3-axle tractor traveling eastbound. Vehicle two was a 1986 Pontiac Grand Prix coupe also traveling eastbound. V1 negotiated a left curve and then pulled over the left side of the road to prepare to back into a driveway on the right. V1 began backing towards the driveway. At the same time V2 negotiated the left curve and collided with the rear of V1. Both vehicles came to rest in the roadway. Police Reported Travel Speed is 8 kmph for V1 and 8 kmph for V2.
V1 was driven by a 54-year old male who was not transported but suffered possible injuries. He stated that he was finishing up his shift driving the truck for the day and was backing into the company lot. He claims to have had his reverse lights and emergency flashers on. He estimated he was traveling about 8 kmph (5 mph) at impact. The tractor was not towed due to damage, but it was later discovered that the tires had been restricted by impact damage.
V2 was driven by a 20-year old male who was transported to a local trauma center with incapacitating injuries. The driver was accompanied by two passengers. He stated that he was returning home from an errand at the store when he suddenly came upon a backing truck in the roadway. He braked with lockup but didn't have time to avoid the collision. The driver claims to not have been distracted at the time of the crash, and was not talking with his passengers. He estimated his travel and impact speeds to be 64 and 56 kmph (40 and 35 mph). The coupe was towed due to damage.
