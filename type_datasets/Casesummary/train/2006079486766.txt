This two-vehicle collision occurred during evening hours on a weekend. It was dark at the time, and the crash scene was illuminated by street lamps. The crash occurred at the four-leg intersection of a north-south roadway and an east-west roadway. The speed limit is 56 kmph (35 mph) for eastbound and westbound traffic. The intersection is controlled by a tri-light traffic signal. The north-south roadway is a six-lane, two-way, undivided, straight, level, bituminous roadway. The north/south roadway is two lanes and is undivided. At the time of the crash, there were no adverse atmospheric conditions and the roadway was free of defects.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1996 Chevrolet Blazer compact SUV, was traveling eastbound in the second lane, approaching the intersection with the intention of passing straight through. Vehicle two (V2), a 2006 Toyota Tundra four-door pickup, was stopped at the intersection facing westbound, and waiting to turn left into southbound traffic. As V2 made its turn, the front of V1 impacted the right side of V2. V2 rotated clockwise and departed the road over the southeast corner, where the right side of V2 contacted a tree, and its back plane contacted a pedestrian signal pole, bringing V2 to rest. V1 came to rest in the intersection. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
