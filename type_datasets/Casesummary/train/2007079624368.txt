This three-vehicle daytime collision occurred shortly past noon on a five-lane roadway, west of a on-colors controlled intersection. The east/west roadway is undivided, dry, asphalt, and has a 4.9% uphill slope. The roadway is designated a 56 kmph (35 mph) zone.
Vehicle one (V1) is a 2004 Ford Ranger, pickup truck, driven by a forty eight (48) year old male who was traveling eastbound in the number two lane. Vehicle two (V2) is a 2003 Toyota Tundra, pickup truck, driven by a thirty one (31) year old male with one passenger. V2 was traveling eastbound in the number two lane. Vehicle three (V3) is a 1993 Jeep Grand Cherokee, sport utility vehicle, driven by a forty one (41) year old male who was traveling eastbound in the number two lane. As V1 traveled, it encountered a stopped V2 and V3. Both vehicles stopped due to congestion created by the approaching on-colors controlled intersection. The front plane of V1 contacted the rear plane of V2. The front plane of V2 then contacted the rear plane of V3. All three vehicles came to rest facing eastbound in the number two lane. V1 received major damage and was towed. V2 received major damage, but was driven from the scene. V3 also received major damage, but was driven. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
