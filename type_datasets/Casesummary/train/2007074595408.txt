This two-vehicle crash occurred on an undivided, level, straight roadway. This roadway consists of two lanes in the northbound direction, one lane in the southbound direction and a center two-way left turn lane. The speed limit for this roadway is 48 kmph (30 mph). There is a driveway on the east side of this roadway. This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions. Traffic was congested at the time of the crash.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1993 Mazda Protégé, was northbound in lane two. Vehicle two (V2), a 2003 Chevrolet Cavalier, was stopped westbound in the driveway. Non-contact vehicles (NCVs) were stopped in northbound lane one. V2 entered the roadway, turning left. The front of V1 struck the front of V2. The vehicles rotated and the right side of V1 struck the left side of V2. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, a sixty-five-year-old female, was on her way to a store from her home. She was healthy, well rested and familiar with her vehicle. She travels this route daily. She has diabetes and takes the medications Cozaar, Actos, aspirin and Glucophage. This driver stated that she did not see V2 until just before the crash due to the NCVs stopped in lane one. She applied the brakes in an attempt to avoid the crash.
The driver of V2, a twenty-three-year-old male, had just left his girlfriend's workplace and was on his way home. He was healthy and familiar with her vehicle. He travels this route daily. This driver had been awake for two-and-a-half hours after eight hours of sleep but stated that he felt drowsy at the start of this trip and was planning to go back to sleep when he arrived at his home. He further stated that the driver of the lead NCV "waved me into the roadway" and he did not see V1 prior to the crash.
