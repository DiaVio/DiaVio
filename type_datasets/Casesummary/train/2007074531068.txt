This two-vehicle crash occurred on a straight, level, curbed median-divided roadway. The westbound lanes consist of two through lanes. The speed limit on this roadway is 64 kmph (40 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions. Traffic was congested at the time of the crash due to the local high school. This is the normal traffic pattern on this roadway at this time of day.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2007 Honda Accord, was westbound in lane two. V2, a 1993 Honda Civic, was behind V1 in lane two. V1 came to a stop due to traffic conditions. The driver of V2 applied the brakes and skidded longitudinally. The front of V2 struck the back of V1. Both vehicles came to rest just west of the point of impact. V2 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, a sixteen-year-old male, was on his way from school to his home. He was well rested, healthy and familiar with his vehicle and route.
The driver of V2, an eighteen-year-old female, was on her way from school to her home. She was well rested, healthy and familiar with her vehicle and route. Just prior to the crash, she was looking inside her vehicle, but doesn't remember why. She looked back up at the roadway at the last minute, but was unable to avoid the collision.
