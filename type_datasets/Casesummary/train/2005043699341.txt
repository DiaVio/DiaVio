The crash occurred in the eastbound lane of a two-way, undivided roadway. The roadway had a posted speed of 40 kmph (25 mph) and a level grade at the impact location. Conditions were daylight and dry with clear skies. The crash occurred in the early afternoon hours on a weekday.
Vehicle one was a 1996 Chevrolet Blazer sport utility vehicle traveling eastbound. Vehicle two was a 1995 Mazda Millenia sedan traveling westbound negotiating a right curve. V2 was speeding and lost control exiting the curve. V2 began fishtailing and traveled into the eastbound lane. The front of V1 contacted the front of V2. Both vehicles came to rest in the roadway. Police Reported Travel Speed is 56 kmph for V1 and 56 kmph for V2.
Vehicle one was driven by an 18-year old male who was untreated but suffered possible injuries. He stated that the other vehicle came around the curve fishtailing and then collided head on. He estimated his speed to be 30 mph at impact. V1 was towed due to damage.
Vehicle two was driven by a 14-year old male who was transported to a local trauma center with possible injuries. The driver could not be located for an interview, but the investigating officer estimated his speed at impact to be 45 mph. V2 was towed due to damage.
