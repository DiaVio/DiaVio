This two-vehicle crash occurred in the eastbound lanes of a seven lane, divided, straight, level roadway with a speed limit of 56 kmph (35 mph). The roadway intersects a parking lot entrance/exit. The eastbound lanes consist of three through lanes and a left turn lane. This crash occurred during daylight evening rush hour. There were no adverse weather conditions at the time of the crash. The crash location is west of an intersection controlled by traffic signals. Traffic in eastbound lane one was backed up past the entrance/exit.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1993 Ford Mustang with one occupant, was departing the parking lot from the south into the eastbound lanes. Vehicle two (V2), a 2000 Honda CR-V with four occupants, was eastbound in lane three. V1 entered the roadway and the front of V1 struck the right side of V2. Both vehicles came to rest adjacent to the impact location. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, a nineteen year-old female, was traveling from a cosmetics store to home. She was well rested, in good health and comfortable with her vehicle. She drives this route daily. This driver stated that her view of V2 was obscured by traffic in the first lane when she entered the intersection.
The driver of V2 was a thirty-five year-old female who traveling with five, ten and four year-old all female passengers. She was traveling home from picking the children up at school. She was well rested, in good health and comfortable with her vehicle and passengers. She drives this route daily. The driver stated that she was asking the children how their day was at the time of the collision.
