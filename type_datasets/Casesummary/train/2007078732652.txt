This single vehicle crash occurred on a two-way, two lane roadway in a business area. There are two southbound and two northbound lanes separated by a continuous left turn lane. The speed limit on the roadway is posted 56 kmph (35 mph). The roadway was straight, level and constructed of bituminous asphalt. The roadway was dry and the sky was clear at the time of this afternoon daylight crash.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2003 Hummer H-2 with one occupant, was traveling southbound when it departed the left side of the roadway. The left front tires struck the curb. The front left area of the vehicle then struck a metal light pole that was located approximately 1.3 meters (4 feet) east of the edge of the roadway. The impact forced the vehicle to rotate counterclockwise. The vehicle rotated and then tripped on its left tires and rolled onto its right side where it came to rest facing in a southeasterly direction. The driver of V1 was transported from the scene by EMS. V1 was towed from the scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is 48 kmph for V1.
A forty four (44) year old male drove V1. He told police that before the crash. He was talking on the phone, reading his mail and drinking a soft drink. He said that when he looked up, he saw the pole but did not have time to do anything. Witnesses at the scene told the same sequence of events for V1. One of the witnesses at the scene said that she thought she saw the driver of V1 on the phone just prior to the crash. Witness one (W1) and Witness three (W3) said they saw V1 oncoming and had to swerve to avoid being struck by V1. The driver was not interviewed due him expiring 2 weeks after the crash as a result of natural causes.
