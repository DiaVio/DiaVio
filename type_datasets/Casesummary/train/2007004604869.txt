The crash occurred in the center of a four-way intersection composed of an undivided two-lane north/south roadway and an undivided two-lane east/west roadway. All roadways were bituminous. The north/south roadway had a 3.2 downhill grade in the northbound direction and a posted speed limit of 72 kmph (45 mph). The east/west roadway was level and had a posted speed limit of 56 kmph (35mph). It was afternoon on a weekend. The roads were dry and traffic flow was moderate. There was no traffic control device for north/south traffic.
V1, a 2005 Toyota Camry 4-door sedan with one occupant, was traveling south when it entered the intersection and attempted to turn left. V2, a 2002 Dodge Neon 4-door sedan with two occupants, was traveling north on the same road and was traveling straight through the intersection. As V1 turned, the front right of V2 struck the right side of V1. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
V1, driven by a 45-year-old female who reported no injuries and was not transported, stated that she was traveling south and thought she had enough room and time to make the turn. The driver took no avoidance maneuvers.
The driver of V2, a 23-year-old male, reported no injuries while the passenger, a 23-year-old female, reported a complaint of pain. Neither occupant was transported to the hospital. The driver of V2 reported that he was traveling north when V1 suddenly turned in front of him. No avoidance maneuver was attempted.
