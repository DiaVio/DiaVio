A one-vehicle dawn front-end collision occurred on an urban arterial with 6 lanes of travel (three lanes in each direction) and a two-way center turn lane. The roadway is straight, level and paved with bituminous asphalt. The speed limit for the arterial is 56 kmph (35 mph). At the time of the crash the road was wet but there was no precipitation. Rain started after the crash occurred.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1990 Honda Accord sedan, with only a driver, was heading south in lane one approaching a color light controlled intersection. As V1 approached the stopped traffic the driver pressed the brake, and the pedal went all the way to the floor. V1 then steered to the right and departed the roadway to avoid rear-ending the stopped car in front of it. V1 then impacted the corner of a building coming to rest at impact. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.
The driver of V1 was a 37-year-old female who had been transported due to injury. The driver was on her morning commute from home to work when the collision occurred. She had been awake for 3 hours and 24 minutes after 6 hours of sleep and stated that she felt fatigued. As her vehicle approached the forward traffic stopped for the traffic light, she stated that she pressed the brake to stop but the brake went all the way to the floor. Driver stated that she did not want to hurt anyone so she departed the roadway and used the building to stop her vehicle. During the vehicle inspection the researcher did press on the brake pedal and it did go all the way to the floor.
