The single vehicle, weekday, late afternoon crash occurred off the left side of a narrow, rural, 2-lane, 2-way, straight, level, bituminous roadway. At the time of the crash it was daylight, the weather was clear and the roadway was dry. The speed limit was 89 kmh (55 mph).
V1, a 1995 Chevrolet S-10 pickup solely occupied by a 20 year-old male driver, was eastbound in the eastbound lane of the roadway described above. V1 was following a semi/gravel-truck and attempted to pass it. V1 crossed over into the oncoming lane and then the eastbound truck started drifting to the left over the centerline. V1, taking avoidance actions, steered left and braked. V1 exited the roadway to the left, struck the end of a guardrail and went into a 5 foot deep, 6 foot wide ditch running parallel with the roadway. V1 rolled to the left onto it's left side, then it's top, rolled right all the way over onto it's right side and came to rest on it's right side facing east. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.
The driver stated that he was returning home from town after running an errand and was familiar with the road and not in a hurry. He was going about 60mph when he starting passing the truck and was about a half car length behind and to the left of the truck when it started coming over into his lane. The driver swerved left to avoid the truck and went into the ditch and rolled over first left then right.
