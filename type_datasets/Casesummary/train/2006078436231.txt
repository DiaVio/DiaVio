This two-vehicle collision occurred during evening hours on a weeknight. The crash occurred at the intersection of a north-south roadway and an east-west roadway. The intersection is controlled by tri-light traffic signals, and is illuminated by street lamps. The speed limit is 64 kmph (40 mph) in all direction. The east leg of the intersection is a five-lane, two-way, straight, level, bituminous roadway; the west leg has four lanes. At the time of the crash, there were no adverse weather conditions, and the roadway was free of defects.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1999 Ford Taurus 4-door sedan, was traveling westbound and approaching the intersection during the yellow phase of the tri-light, with the intention of turning left into southbound traffic. Vehicle two (V2), a 2001 Ford Mustang 2-door coupe, was traveling eastbound and approaching the intersection with the intention of passing straight through. As V1 made its turn, the front of V1 impacted the left side of V2. V1 came to rest at impact; V2 rotated clockwise and came to rest facing west in the eastbound lanes. Police Reported Travel Speed is 8 kmph for V1 and 8 kmph for V2.
