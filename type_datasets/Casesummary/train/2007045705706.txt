The crash occurred on a residential street that ran north/south, was straight and level and had one lane in each direction divided by a double yellow painted stripe. The posted speed limit was 48 kmph/30 mph. Conditions at the time of the crash were daylight and dry at late morning on a weekday.
V1 was a 1992 Oldsmobile Cutlass 4-door traveling north on the residential road when it struck the back end of V2, a 2000 International garbage truck that was stopped in the northbound lane waiting to be loaded. Final rest for both vehicles was at the point of impact. V2 was driven away and V1 was towed from the scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
V1 was driven by an unrestrained eighteen-year-old female who was transported from the scene with non-incapacitating injuries to an area hospital. Her passenger was also unrestrained and transported. She told police that she had dropped a drink and was reaching for it at the time of the crash but no evidence of a drink was found in V1.
V2 was driven by an unrestrained forty-three-year old male who was uninjured as was his passenger. The driver had stopped as was usual for him on his trash route when he was rear-ended.
