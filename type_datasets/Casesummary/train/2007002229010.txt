This crash occurred on a two-lane undivided straight roadway with a downhill grade of 3.2 percent and a posted speed limit of 72 KMPH (45 MPH). The weather was clear and the roadway was dry. This crash occurred on a weekday morning.
Vehicle #1, a 2001 Toyota Corolla 4-door, was traveling east on the undivided roadway when it drove off the roadway, to the right, impacting a utility pole with its right front. The vehicle continued on up a short embankment and came to final rest on a slope facing south. This vehicle was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.
The Toyota Corolla (Vehicle #1) was driven by a belted 60-year old female who was transported to an area hospital, treated and released. The driver stated she was looking at a bird that flew at her car. She was not sure it was a bird. An ambulance driver at the scene stated the problem might have been sun glare as he had the same problem as he drove up to the scene. It should be noted here that the driver is on medication for high blood pressure, a thyroid condition and has COPD (Chronic Obstructive Pulmonary Disease) for which she uses a tank of oxygen with a mask when needed. She also reported that she suffers from sleep apnea, but does not receive treatment for it.
