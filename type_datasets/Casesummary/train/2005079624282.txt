This two vehicle daytime collision occurred just past noon at an intersection formed by a east/west roadway and a north/south driveway. The roadway is undivided, dry, level, asphalt, and consists of five lanes. The driveway consists of two undivided, level, dry, asphalt lanes. The driveway has a mirror located on its western side so that drivers can see vehicles approaching from the east/west roadway. The east/west roadway is designated a 56 kph (35 mph) zone.
Vehicle one (V1) a 1996 Dodge Caravan driven by a 43 year-old female was exiting the north/south driveway. Vehicle two (V2) a 1994 Kia Sephia sedan driven by a 20 year-old male was traveling westbound in the number one lane of the east/west roadway. As V1 attempted to make a left-turn unto the east/west roadway, its left plane was contacted by the front plane of V2. The impact caused V1 to roll unto its right plane. V1 came to rest on its roof facing southeast blocking the number three lane. V2 came to rest facing southwest blocking the number two lane. V1 was towed with rollover damage. V2 was towed with major damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
