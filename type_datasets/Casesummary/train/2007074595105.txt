This two-vehicle crash occurred in a four-legged, traffic signal-controlled intersection. All approaches are straight, have a downhill grade and consist of two through lanes and a left turn lane. The speed limit for these roadways is 64 kmph (40 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1995 Mercedes Benz S420, was eastbound in lane three. Vehicle two (V2), a 2000 Pontiac Sunfire, was southbound in lane two. V1 turned left in the intersection and the left side was struck by the front of V2. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, a fifty-five-year-old female, was driving her employer's vehicle to a gas station and to get a car wash. She was healthy, well rested and familiar with her vehicle. She travels this route weekly. This driver stated that the signal was green when she approached the intersection and she proceeded with her turn without looking for cross traffic.
The driver of V2, a twenty-year-old male, had just picked up his twenty-year-old girlfriend from school and they were on their way home. He was well rested, healthy and comfortable with his vehicle and passenger. He travels this route daily. This driver stated that he was talking to and looking at his passenger and did not notice that the signal was red.
