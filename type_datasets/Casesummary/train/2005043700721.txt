The crash occurred at an intersection in the southbound lane of a two-way, three-lane roadway. The southbound lanes were sloped 5% downhill and the westbound lanes were on a level grade. The posted speed limit was 56kmph (35 mph). Conditions were daylight and dry with clear skies. The setting was mid-afternoon hours on a weekday.
Vehicle one was a 1992 Buick Skylark coupe, traveling west in lane three approaching the intersection intending to turn left. Vehicle two was a 1999 Dodge Durango sport utility traveling southbound in lane one. Both vehicles proceeded into the intersection. The front of V1 impacted the left plane of V2. Both vehicles came to rest in the southwest corner of the intersection. Police Reported Travel Speed is 40 kmph for V1 and 40 kmph for V2.
V1 was driven by a 25-year old female who was transported to a local hospital with possible injuries. She was unable to be located for an interview. The investigating officer estimated her speed at impact to be 40kmph (25 mph). Her vehicle was towed due to damage.
V2 was driven by a 35-year old female who was listed with possible injuries but was not transported. She was wearing her prescription lenses to correct a myopic (near-sighted) condition. She stated that she was heading to a doctor's appointment and was going straight through the intersection when the other vehicle crashed into her. She estimated her travel and impact speeds to be 56kmph (35 mph). Her sport utility was towed due to damage.
