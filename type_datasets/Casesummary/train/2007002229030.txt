This crash occurred on a two lane undivided roadway curving to the right with a grade of 3.1% and a super elevation of 5.8%. The radius of curvature was 18 meters. The posted speed limit was 89 kmph (55 mph). The weather was blowing snow, sleet and freezing rain and the roadway was wet and icy. This crash occurred on a weekday morning.
Vehicle 1 (V1), a 1996 Ford Series E350 Van, modified as a private school bus, was westbound on the undivided roadway when it started skidding laterally in a clock-wise direction. The driver steered hard right and the vehicle departed the roadway to the right. The vehicle rolled left one quarter turn landing on it's left side facing east. The vehicle was towed, due to damage, to the driver's destination in another county. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.
The driver of V1, a belted 65-year-old male, stated he started to drift into the on-coming lane and he negotiated the curve. He saw other cars approaching from the east in that lane so he pulled the wheel to the right and accelerated slightly. He stated he supposed he accelerated too much and turned the wheel too much to the right. The driver had a cut forehead from a first aid kit that came loose from it clips and hit him in the head. He was treated at the scene.
