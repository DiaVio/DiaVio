The two-vehicle crash occurred on a three-lane, eastbound exit ramp off of a five lane highway. Both roadways were straight, dry and of concrete construction. The highway was level and the exit ramp has a positive grade. The posted speed limit is 97 kmph (60 mph). Traffic was moderate at the time of the crash. This crash occurred during daylight hours with clear skies.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1996 GMC cutaway van, was eastbound in the third travel lane. Vehicle two (V2), a 1994 Lincoln Town Car, was eastbound in the third travel lane and then moved onto the ramp. V1 passed the exit lanes and crossed over the gore area to the exit lanes in front of V2. The front of V2 struck the back of V1. The driver of V1 drove the vehicle to the right shoulder. V2 rotated clockwise and came to rest facing west on the left shoulder. V2 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, a twenty-two year old male, was heading to a warehouse from work. This driver had been driving for about three minutes, drives the route daily and is familiar with the vehicle. The driver stated that he had taken the exit and was hit from behind. He did not detect V2 until impact and did not attempt any evasive maneuvers.
The driver of V2, a twenty-six year old male, was heading from his mother's home to work. This driver had been driving for about five minutes, drives the route daily and is familiar with the vehicle. The driver stated that V1 cut across from the highway lanes into his exit lane at the last second. He stated that he applied the brakes in an attempt to avoid the collision.
