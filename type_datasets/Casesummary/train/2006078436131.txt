This two-vehicle crash occurred just past noon at a T-intersection in a residential area. The intersection has a north/south roadway. There is no centerline stripe to divide the roadway. There is an east/west roadway that also has no centerline stripe to divide the roadway. The speed limit on the roadway is posted 40 kph (25 mph). The roads were straight, dry asphalt and the sky was clear. This crash occurred during daylight hours.
Vehicle one(V-1), a 1991 Buick Century sedan driven by a 21 year-old female was traveling westbound approaching the intersection and made a left turn at the intersection. Vehicle two(V-2), a 1965 Chevrolet Nova Station wagon driven by a 50 year-old male was traveling northbound at approximately 40 kph (25 mph). V-1 made a left turn to avoid striking a house across the through street. The brakes were apparently not operating properly. V-1 entered the intersection and struck V-2 near the right front tire. V-1 rotated clockwise and came to rest in the intersection facing in a southwesterly direction. The driver of V-2 tried to avoid the crash by steering to the left and braking . The impact forced V-2 to rotate counterclockwise and travel in a northwesterly direction until it came to rest in the intersection facing in a northwesterly direction. The vehicles came to rest within 2 meters of each other. V-1 was disabled and was pushed to the residence of the driver which was about 50 feet from the crash site. V-2 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 40 kmph for V1 and 40 kmph for V2.
Note: The brake pedal went to the floor during the vehicle inspection. This indicated that the braking capability was degraded.
