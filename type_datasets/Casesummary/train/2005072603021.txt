This two-vehicle crash occurred mid-afternoon during daylight hours at an intersection. It involved a passenger car that turned left in front of an oncoming passenger car. The roadway is a four-lane, two-way, undivided asphalt roadway that runs East/West. There are no traffic control devices at the intersection in the direction the two vehicles were traveling. The cross street is a one way, one lane roadway. Traffic on this roadway leads away from the four lane roadway. The speed limit is 58 kmph (35 mph). There were no adverse weather conditions or roadway restrictions.
Vehicle one, a 1991 Dodge Shadow carrying two occupants, was traveling eastbound in lane two. Vehicle two, a 1998 Ford Taurus SE carrying four occupants, was traveling westbound on the same roadway in lane two. Vehicle one turned left onto the cross street and was struck on the right front by the front of vehicle two. Both vehicles rotated and the right rear corner of vehicle one struck the left rear corner of vehicle two. After impact vehicle two ran off the north side of the roadway and the front struck a luminary where it came to rest. After initial impact, vehicle one continued to rotate 180 degrees as it ran off the north side backwards where it came to a stop. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
Both vehicles were towed from the scene.
No interviews were obtained so very little is know about the drivers.
