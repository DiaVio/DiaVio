This crash occurred on a Sunday in December mid-afternoon involving a passenger car and a pickup truck on an interstate highway. The speed limit for the roadway is 105 kmph (65 mph). The road is a 4-lane, straight, level, divided (positive barrier) concrete roadway. The road surface was dry and there were no detrimental atmospheric conditions at the time of the crash.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2002 Saturn SL 1 was traveling west in the second lane. Vehicle two, a 2002 Chevrolet 2500 Pick up, was traveling west in the third lane ahead of vehicle one. The driver of V1 suffered a diabetic related seizure and he blacked out. His vehicle departed into the third lane and into the fourth lane. From there it re-entered lane three where the front right corner struck the left side of V2. Both vehicles disengaged and the driver of V2 was able to drive to the north side of the roadway where the driver stopped the vehicle on the right shoulder. V1 crossed lane four and entered the south shoulder before the front left struck the concrete median barrier. The vehicle continued to slide along the median before it came to rest. V1 was towed from the scene. The driver was treated at the scene. The other three occupants were transported to a local hospital with minor injuries. V2 was driven from the scene and the two occupants were not injured. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
