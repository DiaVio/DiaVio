This crash occurred in an interchange area during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions. The trafficway is straight and divided by a jersey barrier. The northbound roadway is level and consists of four through lanes and a right exit lane. The speed limit for this roadway is 97 kmph (60 mph). The exit ramp is straight, level, consists of one lane and the advisory speed limit is 64 kmph (40 mph).
Vehicle one (V1), a 2002 Honda Civic, was northbound in lane two. Vehicle two (V2), a 2006 Ford Ranger, was northbound in lane one, the exit lane. The driver of V1 steered to the right and traveled into the gore. The front of V1 struck the impact attenuator. V1 rotated counterclockwise and traveled onto the exit ramp. The left side of V1 was struck by the front of V2. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, a twenty-six-year-old male, was fatal from the collision. No surrogate was located for an interview. A witness to this crash stated that it appeared that V1 missed the exit and was attempting to exit the freeway across the gore. Physical evidence indicates that the driver of V1 applied the brakes (skidding longitudinally) prior to striking the attenuator. No further information is available for this driver.
The driver of V2, a fifty-three-year-old male, was on his way to work from his home. He was healthy, well rested and familiar with his vehicle. He travels this route daily. This driver stated that he did not see V1 until it "hit the barrier and spun around."
