This two-vehicle crash occurred at a four-legged, traffic signal-controlled intersection. The east/west roadway is level and straight and consists of two lanes in each direction and a center left turn lane. The speed limit on this roadway is 56 kmph (35 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions. Traffic was congested at the time of the crash.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1990 Chevrolet C2500 pickup, was westbound in lane two. Vehicle two (V2), a 2002 Jeep Cherokee, was westbound in lane two in front of V1. V2 stopped due to traffic. The front of V1 struck the back of V2. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 0 kmph for V1 and 0 kmph for V2.
The driver of V1, a thirty-five-year-old female, was on her way home from work with her three-year-old daughter. She was comfortable with her vehicle and passenger. She travels this route daily. This driver stated that she had a migraine and felt "out of it." She takes ibuprofen and vitamins. She also stated that she had just moved and was worried about how she was "going to make it." She further stated that, just prior to the crash, she looked left and then right, and applied the brakes and steered left when she realized that V2 had stopped.
The driver of V2, a thirty-year-old female, was on her way home from a library with her children. She was well rested, in fairly good health and comfortable with her vehicle and passengers. She travels this route several times each month.
