This single vehicle crash occurred about an hour and a half before midnight on a divided, highway. The roadway consists of 2 lanes that run north that are separated from 2 southbound lanes by a depressed dirt and grass median. The road is straight, level, bituminous, and there are no traffic controls present. At the time of the crash it was cloudy, dark, the road surface was wet and there were no street lights to illuminate the roadway, which has a posted speed limit of 105 kmph (65 mph).
Vehicle one (V1) was a 2003 Toyota Tacoma, driven by a 24 year-old female with one passenger. They were on their way home from camping. V1 was traveling southbound in the #2 travel lane when the driver stated that she saw a dark shadow coming from her left. Shortly after noticing the shadow, a large elk entered the roadway and was struck by the front of V1. The elk tumbled over the hood and windshield. V1 driver braked and attempted to steer straight but V1 exited the roadway to the left into the median and came to rest facing generally south. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 97 kmph for V1.
