The crash occurred on a two-way, two-lane, straight, level roadway with a posted speed limit of 80 kph (50 mph). Conditions at the time of the weekday morning crash were daylight, cloudy and the bituminous roadway was icy.
V1, a 1997 Ford E 150 Econoline large van driven by a 55 year-old female was westbound on the roadway traveling 72 kph (45 mph). The driver stated that she was aware that the roadways were icy in spots and was proceeding below the posted speed limit. The driver stated that the van began to slide sideways on ice and she braked (full ABS application) and steered left and right attempting to remain on the roadway. V1 crossed the eastbound lane in a counterclockwise yaw and left the road on the left down a 74% slope. V1 struck a small tree with the right side and then rolled 3 quarter turns with the right side leading. The front of the van struck another tree and came to rest on the left side facing south. V1 was towed from the scene due to damage. The fully restrained driver denied injuries at the scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.
