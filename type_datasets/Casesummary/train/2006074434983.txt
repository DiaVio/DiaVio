This two-vehicle driveway related collision occurred on a two-way, five-lane, straight, level, and wet bituminous roadway. No adverse weather conditions present at the time of collision, but roadway was wet due to melting snow. There were no traffic control devices present and posted speed limit is 64 kmph (40 mph) for north and southbound traffic.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2004 Jeep Liberty was traveling south in lane two. Vehicle two (V2), a 1999 Subaru Forester was stopped, facing east, in a driveway on west side of roadway in order to turn left. V2 turned left from the driveway and was impacted on the left side by the front of V1. V1 came to rest in the center left turn lane. V2 came to rest in the first lane. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, a thirty-five year old male had just left an appointment and was heading home for lunch. This driver had only been driving for a few minutes at the time of the crash. This driver was talking to the passenger as he was driving. This driver drives this vehicle daily and over this road on a weekly basis.
The driver of V2, a fifty-nine year old male had just left a store in the shopping center. This driver was heading to another store and had been driving for just a few minutes. This driver stated that he was stopped for a few minutes and then saw a car turning right into the driveway. This driver said he got impatient from waiting and did not look and see the other vehicle. This driver drives this vehicle daily and over this road on a monthly basis.
