This two-vehicle crash occurred in a three-legged, traffic signal-controlled intersection. The north/south trafficway is level and divided by a curbed median. The northbound approach consists of three through lanes and two left turn lanes. The southbound approach consists of five through lanes. The speed limit for these roadways is 64 kmph (40 mph). The intersecting roadway is on the west side of the north/south roadway. This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1999 Toyota Corolla, was stopped northbound in lane five. Vehicle two (V2), a 2006 Chrysler Town and Country, was southbound in lane five. V1 entered the intersection, turning left, and the front struck the front of V2. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, a thirty-two-year-old female, was traveling, with two passengers, from her home in another city to go sightseeing downtown. This driver was healthy, well rested and claimed to be comfortable with her vehicle. She had been driving for six months prior to this crash. This driver stated that she was frustrated because her passengers were arguing with her about finding a place to park. She further stated that she did not see V2 prior to the crash.
The driver of V2, a forty-three-year-old male, was traveling, with his wife and children, from a friend's house to their home. He was healthy, well rested and comfortable with his vehicle and passengers. He rarely travels this route. This driver stated that he was talking to his wife and noticed V1 just prior to the crash.
