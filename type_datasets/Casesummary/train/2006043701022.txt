This crash occurred on a weekend evening in the eastbound lane of a two-lane, two-way, suburban roadway at the entrance to an apartment complex. The bituminous roadway featured an 8% uphill grade in the eastbound direction, a right curve and a posted speed limit was 56kmph (35 mph). There were no adverse weather conditions and the roads were dark and dry at the time of the crash.
V1, a 2003 Nissan Xterra sport utility, was stopped facing east in lane one waiting to turn left into the apartment complex. V2, a 2005 Toyota Prius, hatchback was eastbound negotiating the right curve approaching apartment complex entrance. V2 failed to stop behind V1 and the front of V2 impacted the rear of V1. Both vehicles came to final rest in the roadway. Police Reported Travel Speed is 0 kmph for V1 and 0 kmph for V2.
V1 was driven by a 24-year old female who was uninjured. She stated that she was going home from work and waiting to turn left into her apartment complex. She noticed a vehicle approaching in her rear view mirror, but did not have a chance to execute an avoidance maneuver. She was stopped at the time of impact. Her sport utility continued in service after the crash.
V2 was driven by an 80-year old male who was transported to a local hospital with non-incapacitating injuries. He was unable to be located for an interview by phone or in-person. The investigating officer estimated his travel and impact speeds to be 56kmph (35 mph). His hatchback was towed due to damage.
