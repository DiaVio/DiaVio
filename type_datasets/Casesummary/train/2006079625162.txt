This single vehicle crash occurred on a highway off-ramp. The off-ramp exits a north/south two-lane concrete highway. The off-ramp is one-way, with 2% downgrade and curves right with a 45-meter radius. The right side of the off-ramp contains a painted shoulder. The off-ramp is designated a 40 kmph (25 mph) zone. A warning sign that informs drivers of the approaching circular curve controls the off-ramp. This crash occurred during daylight hours with wet roads and light rain.
Vehicle one, a 1996 Toyota Camry was on the off-ramp in the first lane. As vehicle one was traveling, it began to rotate clockwise. The left front wheel of vehicle one contacted the north curb. The left plane of vehicle one then contacted a light pole with its left plane. Vehicle one came to rest partially blocking the north shoulder. Vehicle one received moderate damage and was towed from the scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.
The driver of vehicle one, a thirty-six year old female was traveling to her brother's home for a visit. The driver had driven approximately fifteen minutes. The driver last slept from 0100 hours to 1000 hours at her residence. The driver has been driving for four and a half years. She has an expired license, but a valid international license from Syria. The driver claimed that she felt that her brakes were not responding and decided to exit the highway. It was at that point that she lost control at the bottom of the off-ramp. This driver drives this vehicle daily and over this road on a monthly basis.
