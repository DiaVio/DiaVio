This single vehicle crash occurred on a six-lane roadway divided by a continuous two way left turn lane. There are right turn lanes for both north and southbound traffic at the intersection that is related to this crash. This was a straight level, bituminous roadway with a posted speed limit of 56 KHP (35 MPH). The roadway was wet and there was a slight rain. It was early morning and the sky was cloudy. Traffic at the intersection is controlled by traffic control signals on colors that were functioning at the time. .
Vehicle one (V1), a 1998 Ford Expedition with one occupant, was traveling eastbound in the left turn lane. The driver then made a left turn to travel northbound. The vehicle then began to fish tail and departed the right side of the roadway. The vehicle rotated clockwise and the left front struck a metal power pole approximately 1.2 meters east of the roadway. The impact caused V1 to continue to rotate clockwise coming to rest across the northbound lanes facing in a southeasterly direction. The driver was transported from the scene by EMS for non-incapacitating injuries. The vehicle was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 48 kmph for V1.
A 19-year old male was driving V1. He told EMS that he does remember hitting the pole. A witness at the scene said that she was traveling southbound approaching the intersection. V1 was making a left turn to travel northbound when the car began to fish tail and went off the roadway and struck a pole. The witness stated the driver was trying to make the light just prior to the crash.
