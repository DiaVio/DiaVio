This two-vehicle crash occurred near an interchange exit ramp in a construction zone on a jersey barrier-divided, level trafficway. The northbound lanes consist of two through lanes and curve to the right (the radius of curvature is 55 meters). There were construction barrels in the gore area. The speed limit for this roadway is 72 kmph (45 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1993 Ford Escort, was northbound in lane one. Vehicle two (V2), a 2003 Freightliner tractor/trailer, was northbound in lane two. V1 moved to the left and the left side struck the right front tire of V2. V1 then traveled to the right and the right side struck a construction barrel, the driver steered left and the right side struck the right jersey barrier. V1 then reentered the roadway and the front struck the right side of V2's trailer. V1 continued forward and the left side was struck by the right rear trailer tires. V1 then moved back to the right and the front struck the right jersey barrier. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, a thirty-three-year-old female, was on her way home from work. She stated that she fell asleep prior to the first impact. This driver's medical record indicates a positive test for opiates.
The driver of V2, a fifty-five-year-old male, had picked up a load and was on his way to drop it off. He was healthy, well rested and comfortable with his vehicle and cargo. He travels this route several times each month. He had twenty-five years of experience driving a truck. This driver stated that he did not see V1 prior to the first impact.
