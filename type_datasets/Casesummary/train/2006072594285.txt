This three-vehicle crash occurred in the third lane of a four-lane eastbound expressway during the early evening rush hour. A concrete barrier separates the westbound lanes and the westbound lanes are not involved in this crash. This roadway is straight and made up of concrete and has no visible deficiencies. The speed limit for the roadway is 89 kmph (55 mph). There was traffic congestion at the time of the crash and the weather conditions were clear.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1991 Chevrolet Caprice with one occupant, was traveling eastbound in lane three of the expressway at an unknown speed. Vehicle two (V2), a Toyota Corolla with one occupant, was traveling eastbound in lane three of the expressway ahead of V1. Vehicle three (V3), a 1996 Nissan Sentra with one occupant, was traveling ahead of both V2 and V1 in the third lane of the expressway. Traffic slowed and V3 and V2 slowed with it. V1 did not respond in time and rear-ended V2, who in turn rear-ended V1. The driver of V2 was transported with an unknown injury and the vehicle was towed due to significant front and rear-end damage. The drivers of V1 and V3 were not cooperative and pre-crash information could not be obtained in later attempts as well. The drivers of V1 and V3 were not transported and their vehicles were not towed, though V1 had severe front-end damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
V3 was not involved in the first harmful event and the Critical pre-crash reason for the critical pre-crash event was not coded to this vehicle.
