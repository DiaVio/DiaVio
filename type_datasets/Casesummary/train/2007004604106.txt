The crash occurred in the southbound lane of a two-lane undivided north/south roadway. The roadway was bituminous and level with a posted speed limit of 80 kmph (50 mph). It was night on a weekday. The roads were dry and the sky was clear. Traffic flow was light.
V1, a 1989 Saab 900 coupe with one occupant, was traveling north when it suddenly turned left in front of V2, a 1998 Lincoln Navigator SUV that was traveling south. The driver of V2 applied his brakes and turned left in an attempt to avoid V1 but struck V1 on the right side. The collision sent V1 into a clockwise rotation and V1 was pushed V1 sideways onto the west curb and then into a mailbox. The driver of V1 was ejected and pinned under the driver's door when V1 came to rest. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
V1 was driven by a 37-year-old male who died as a result of his injuries. For unknown reasons, the driver made an unexpected left turn with insufficient clearance in front of V2. The medical examiner's report revealed a BAC of .157. The driver of V1 was unrestrained and was ejected from the vehicle as a result of the crash.
V2 was driven by a 25-year-old male who was transported to the hospital with a minor head injury. He stated that he was traveling south when V1 suddenly turned left in front of him without signaling. V2 attempted to avoid the collision by applying his brakes and steering left; however, V2 struck V1 on the right side.
