This two-vehicle collision occurred at the intersection of two two-way city streets. The intersection has a stop sign for the north/south roadway. The roadway for northbound travel has a downhill grade of 4% and for eastbound traffic a downhill grade of 9% both are straight and paved with concrete. At the time of the crash the sky was cloudy and the roads were wet.
Vehicle one, a 2002 Ford Explorer SUV was northbound in the first lane. Vehicle two, a 1994 GMC Van was eastbound in the first lane. Both vehicles entered in the intersection at the same time. The front of vehicle two impacted the left of vehicle one. Vehicle one then departed the roadway and the front of vehicle one impacted a street sign and a fence. Vehicle one was towed due to damage. Vehicle two was driven from scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of vehicle one, a sixty-six year old female was not interviewed. Based on statements from the driver of V2, V1 entered the intersection without stopping for the stop sign, an illegal maneuver. V1 failed to yield the right of way to V2 who had no traffic controls.
The driver of vehicle two, a forty-five year old male was coming from one job and heading to a second job. It is unknown how long this driver had been driving on this trip. He stated he was coming down the hill and saw vehicle one approaching the intersection and noticed she was not slowing for the stop sign, so he braked with no lockup.
