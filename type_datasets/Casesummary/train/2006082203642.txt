This single-vehicle crash occurred on a four-lane, undivided roadway. This roadway consists of two lanes in each direction and parking lanes on both sides. The southbound lanes curve to the left and superelevation is negative one percent. The speed limit on this roadway is 48 kmph (30 mph). It was raining and dark with streetlights on at the time of the crash.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1995 Saturn SL1, was southbound in lane one. V1 traveled off the right side of the roadway and the front struck a utility pole. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.
The driver of V1, a fourteen-year-old male, was traveling with two adult male passengers. This driver was not licensed but was comfortable with this vehicle, having driven it several times in the past year. He travels on this roadway monthly. This driver stated that he was awakened after six hours of sleep (less than his normal eight hours) to drive the owner of the car and his friend home. He stated that he blacked out before the crash; this is confirmed by medical reports. This driver further stated that he has had an untreated blackout incident previously.
