This two-vehicle crash occurred in the middle of a four-way intersection, which was controlled by traffic signals in all directions. The crash took place during early evening hours, and there were no streetlights present. There were no adverse atmospheric conditions and the roadway was dry. The gradient for vehicle one (V1) was level, however, vehicle two (V2) had a slight positive grade. The posted speed limit for both vehicles was 56 kmph (35 mph).
Vehicle one, a 2003 Dodge Neon, was traveling south, intending to make a left at the intersection. Vehicle two, a 1999 Cadillac Deville, was traveling north on the same roadway, intending to continue straight through the intersection. The front of V2 made contact with the right front of V1. The impact caused V1 to rotate counter clockwise approximately 45 degrees before coming to final rest. Vehicle two continued forward slightly before coming to final rest. Both vehicles had to be removed from the scene due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Unknown for V1 and Unknown for V2.
The driver of vehicle one, a 22 year-old male, sustained moderate injuries including but not limited to, open facial wounds and neck pain. There were no other occupants present in the vehicle at the time of the crash. After numerous attempts, an interview from the driver could not be obtained. Therefore, it is uncertain whether any other factors contributed to this crash.
The driver of vehicle two, a 37 year-old male, sustained minor injuries including but not limited to neck soreness. There were no other occupants present in the vehicle when the crash occurred. An interview with the driver could not be obtained, therefore, it is uncertain if any other factors played a part in this crash.
