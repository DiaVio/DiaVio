This crash occurred on a 4-lane undivided roadway with two lanes in each direction. This was a straight roadway with a posted speed limit of 48 kmph (30 mph) and an uphill grade greater than 2 percent. It was mid-afternoon on a weekday and the roads were dry. Traffic flow was at a minimum. There were no adverse weather conditions.
Vehicle 1 was a 2000 Ford 350 light truck driven by a 55-year old male. V1 was traveling westbound on the highway in lane two going at a high rate of speed. Vehicle 2 was a 1999 Mazda 626 driven by a 33-year old female. V2 was stopped in lane two, waiting to turn left into a daycare facility. Vehicle 1 skidded approximately 4.7 meters before its front struck the rear of Vehicle 2. After impact, Vehicle 2 spun about 45 degrees counterclockwise and came to a rest facing south in the first westbound lane. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of Vehicle 2 stated that she saw V1 about one second before impact and there wasn't anything she could do. The driver of Vehicle 1 spoke no English. From hand gestures, he basically described what had happened.
*The PAR indicated that neither driver was transported; however, the driver of V2 was transported to a medical facility.
