This two-vehicle crash occurred on a jersey barrier-divided, straight, level trafficway. The westbound lanes consist of four through lanes and a right lane for the approaching exit ramp. The speed limit on this roadway is 105 kmph (65 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions. Traffic was congested at the time of the crash.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1996 Ford Windstar, was westbound in lane four. Vehicle two (V2), a 2005 Sterling flatbed single-unit truck, was westbound in lane four in front of V1. V2 was slowing due to traffic. The front of V1 struck the back of V2. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 48 kmph for V1 and 48 kmph for V2.
The driver of V1, a fifty-four-year-old male, was traveling from his home to an auction. He had already driven 274 km (170 miles) of his trip. He was healthy, well rested and familiar with his vehicle. He rarely travels this route. This driver stated that he was thinking about the auction and was concerned that he was going to lose money on his truck consignments. He further stated that he was going to pass V2 and started to move into lane five. When he realized that a non-contact vehicle was in that lane, he moved back into lane four and applied the brakes but was unable to avoid the crash.
The driver of V2, a -year-old male, was driving between job sites. He was in good health, well rested and comfortable with his vehicle and cargo load. He takes Albuterol for his asthma. This driver travels this route weekly.
