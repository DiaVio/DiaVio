This two-vehicle crash occurred on a curbed median-divided, straight, level roadway. The westbound lanes consist of two through lanes. The speed limit on this roadway is 64 kmph (40 mph). It was dark with street lights on at the time of the crash and there were no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1994 Flexible bus, in use as a city transit bus, was westbound in lane one. Vehicle two (V2), a 1992 Ford Escort, was eastbound in lane one behind V1. V1 stopped to pick up passengers at the bus stop. The front of V2 struck the back of V1. V2 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 0 kmph for V1 and 0 kmph for V2.
The driver of V1, a sixty-three-year-old female, was traveling between bus stops on her normal daily route. She was healthy, well rested and comfortable with her vehicle and passengers. She travels this route daily. This driver stated that she was watching the passengers load when the crash occurred.
The driver of V2, a twenty-eight-year-old male, was traveling from his home to work. This driver refused to be interviewed, but his spouse stated that he has asthma and takes Primatine for it. She also stated that he was healthy, familiar with his vehicle and travels this route several times each month. This driver's medical records include a blood test indicating that Marijuana, Amphetamines and Opiates were in his system one hour after the crash.
