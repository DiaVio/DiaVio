This two-vehicle crash occurred mid-afternoon at a four-way intersection that is controlled by a stop sign for westbound and eastbound traffic. There is one eastbound and one westbound lane that are separated by a centerline stripe. The speed limit on this roadway is posted 64 kmph (40 mph). There is one northbound and one southbound lane that are separated by a painted centerline stripe. The speed limit on this roadway is posted 80 kmph (50 mph). The roadways were straight, level and constructed of bituminous asphalt. The roadways were dry and the sky was cloudy at the time of the crash. There were no sight line restrictions noted at the scene.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1995 Chevrolet Lumina Mini Van driven by a 26 year-old female with two passengers was traveling eastbound and stopped for the posted stop sign and began to make left turn to travel north. Vehicle two (V2), a 2000 Chevrolet Cavalier sedan driven by a 23 year-old female with two passengers was traveling southbound approaching the intersection at about 80 kmph (50 mph). V1 made a left turn and was struck on the left side by the front of V2. V1 rotated slightly clockwise and backwards and came to rest in the intersection facing in a northeasterly direction. V2 rotated counterclockwise and began to travel in a northwesterly direction until it came to rest on the north shoulder to the west of the intersection facing in a westerly direction. V1 was removed from the scene on it's own power. V2 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 8 kmph for V1 and 8 kmph for V2.
The driver of V1 stated that she stopped for the stop sign for more than 10 seconds. She said that she saw V2 when it was about 46 meters (150 feet) before the intersection. She said that she then decided to make a left turn because she thought she had enough room.
