This two-vehicle collision occurred during morning daylight hours on a weekend. The crash occurred on a straight, level stretch of a seven-lane, two-way, undivided, bituminous roadway. In the area of the crash, private business drives intersect the roadway on both sides. The speed limit on this roadway was 56 kph (35 mph). At the time of the crash, there were no adverse atmospheric conditions, and the roadway was free of defects.
Vehicle one (V 1), a 2004 Nissan Sentra four-door sedan, driven by a 56 year-old female was traveling southbound in a private drive, approaching the roadway with the intention of turning left into eastbound traffic. Vehicle two (V 2), a 2002 Toyota Highlander, driven by a 58 year-old female was traveling westbound in the second lane of the main roadway, approaching the private driveway on its right. As V 1 made its turn, the front of V 2 impacted the left side of V 1. Both vehicles moved southwest to final rest in the westbound lanes,driver of V 1 reported that she was on her way home from shopping at the supermarket. At the time of the crash, she was rested and in good health. She reported that as she approached the roadway, she looked right, then looked left and did not see V 2's approach. She said that her view was blocked, and investigation of the scene revealed that some newspaper vendors positioned along the north curb partially blocked the view of crossing traffic. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
