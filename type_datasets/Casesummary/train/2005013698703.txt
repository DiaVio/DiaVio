This crash occurred during the hours of daylight on a dry, asphalt road. The east-west roadway is a five-lane non-divided two-way city street. The north-south road is a two-lane non-divided city street. The north-south roadway is controlled by a stop sign.
V1, a 2002 Chevy Cavalier, was stopped in the northbound lane, preparing to turn left. As V1 proceeded to pull out into the roadway it was struck on the left side by V2, a 1989 Oldsmobile Delta 88 that was traveling east bound in lane two. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
V1 was driven by a 50 year-old female in good health on an aspirin regime. The driver of V1 stated that she looked both ways, and did not see any vehicles coming. She assumed it was clear to proceed. The driver suffered minor injuries and was transported for treatment and V1 was towed due to damage.
V2 was driven by a 58 year-old female in good health and wearing his prescription lenses that corrected a myopic (near-sighted) condition. The driver of V2 stated that the driver of V1 pulled out in front of her, she then stated that there were vehicles that were stopped in the right lane and that she did not see V1 until it was almost to late. The driver of V2 stated that she locked up the brakes but could not avoid hitting V1.
