The crash occurred at an intersection in the northbound lanes of a four-leg intersection. The roadway had a posted speed limit of 72 kmph (45 mph) and the southbound lanes had a 2% downhill grade. The northbound lanes had a 4% uphill grade. Conditions were daylight and dry with clear skies. The setting was mid-morning hours on a weekday.
Vehicle one was a 2006 Toyota RAV4 compact utility traveling southbound in lane three. Vehicle two was a 2003 Honda Accord sedan traveling northbound in lane one. V1 proceeded to turn left as V2 continued straight through the intersection. The front of V1 impacted the left side of V2. V1 came to rest facing north in the northbound lane one. V2 traveled off the northeast corner of the intersection and came to rest just off the roadway. Police Reported Travel Speed is 16 kmph for V1 and 16 kmph for V2.
The Toyota RAV4 was driven by a 36-year old female with a 4-year old passenger who were both transported by family to a local medical facility. She stated that she was heading to a friend's house and wasn't sure what happened when she turned left at the intersection. She estimated her travel and impact speeds to be 16 kmph (10 mph). The compact utility was towed due to damage.
The Honda Accord was driven by a 23-year old female who was reported with possible injuries. She stated that she was heading to work when an oncoming vehicle turned in front of her at an intersection. She braked and swerved right but was unable to avoid the collision. Her estimated travel and impact speed was 80 kmph (50 mph). The sedan was towed due to damage.
