This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions. The roadway is undivided and consists of one lane in each direction. The northbound travel lane curves to the left and has a downhill grade. The southbound travel lane curves to the right and has an uphill grade. There were warning signs for the curved roadway in both directions. This roadway was covered in ice at the time of the crash. The speed limit for this roadway is 40 kmph (25 mph).
Vehicle one (V1), a 1998 Dodge Neon, was northbound in lane one. Vehicle two (V2), a 2004 Kia Optima, was southbound in lane one. V1 slid off the right side of the roadway and the front struck a wooden utility pole. V1 rotated clockwise, returning to the roadway and the left side was struck by the front of V2. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, a twenty-two-year-old female, was on her way home from dropping her boyfriend off at work. She was healthy, well rested and familiar with her vehicle. She travels this route monthly. This driver stated that she had just had a crash in a parking lot and was fleeing because she was afraid. She further stated that she slid off the roadway when she attempted to slow down for the curve.
The driver of V2, a thirty-one-year-old female, had just left her home to take her ten-year-old daughter to school. This driver was well rested, healthy and comfortable with her vehicle and passenger. She stated that she was in a hurry because they were late for school. This driver applied the brakes (skidding longitudinally) when she saw V1 rotating into her lane.
