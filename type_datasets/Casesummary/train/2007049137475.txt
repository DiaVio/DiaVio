This two-vehicle crash occurred during daylight hours in an intersection. The east/west roadway is straight, level, has one lane in each direction and is controlled by a stop sign. The speed limit on this roadway is 48 kmph (30 mph). There were no adverse atmospheric conditions at the time of the crash.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1996 Toyota Avalon, was eastbound in lane one, approaching the intersection. Vehicle two (V2), a 2002 GMC Envoy, was stopped at the stop sign, intending to turn left at the intersection. V2 entered the intersection and stopped for the northbound stop sign. V1 did not stop at the stop sign, continued eastbound and the front of V1 struck the back of V2. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is No PAR obtained (created) for V1 and No PAR obtained (created) for V2.
The driver of V1, a thirty-five-year-old female, was traveling from a hospital visit with a family member to her home. She was healthy, well rested and familiar with her vehicle. She travels on this roadway monthly. This driver stated that she did not realize that V2 was going to stop again and did not have time to make any avoidance maneuvers.
The driver of V2, a twenty-seven-year-old female, was traveling from work to her home. She was well rested, healthy and comfortable with her vehicle. This driver drives this route daily.
