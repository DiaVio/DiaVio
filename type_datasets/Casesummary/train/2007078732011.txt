This two-vehicle crash occurred in a four-legged, traffic signal-controlled intersection. The north/south roadway is straight, level and consists of two lanes in each direction and a center left turn lane. The speed limit on this roadway is 72 kmph (45 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2001 Toyota Echo, was northbound in lane three. Vehicle two (V2), a 1996 Dodge Caravan, was southbound in lane one. In the intersection, V1 turned left and the front struck the left side of V2. Both vehicles towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 13 kmph for V1 and 13 kmph for V2.
The driver of V1, an eighteen-year-old female, was not interviewed. This driver stated to the police officer at the scene that she saw V2, but thought that she had time to make her turn.
The driver of V2, a forty-two-year-old male, was on his way home from work. He was in good health at the time of the crash. This driver stated that he was fatigued from having worked ten hours in the fields just prior to the crash.
