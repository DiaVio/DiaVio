The crash occurred in the northbound lanes of an interstate highway, that was high volume, high speed. There were five lanes running northbound with lane five being a high, occupancy lane. There were paved emergency shoulders on both the right and left hand side. There was also a concrete barrier wall on both the right and left sides. The conditions were dry and clear. It was afternoon on a weekday. The posted speed was 105 KPH (65MPH).
Vehicle 1 was a 2005 Chevy Monte Carlo that was driven by a 21-year-old female. She was driving the speed limit in lane five when she saw a white Cadillac cutting people off in front of her and driving erratically. She saw a truck ahead of her start spinning out of control in front of her and hit another vehicle. She then did not want to be involved in the crash in front of her so she steered to the left and hit her brakes. She left the roadway and hit the left concrete barrier wall with the front left side of her vehicle and then the car rotated clockwise and the back left of her car hit the concrete barrier. She was not injured but her vehicle was towed due to damages. Police Reported Travel Speed is No PAR obtained (created) for V1.
V1, the Monte Carlo, was in lane five and was keeping up with the holiday traffic when she saw a crash happen in front of her. She stated in the interview that the cause of that crash was a white Cadillac who was driving out of control and cutting people off. She stated that she saw a truck spin across the highway and hit 2 other cars. She stated her natural reaction was to steer away from the crash. She hit her brakes and steered to the left to avoid being involved in the other crash. She steered to the left and hit the left concrete wall with her left front end. She was successful in avoiding the crash but was unable to not hit the concrete wall. She was not cited for her crash.
