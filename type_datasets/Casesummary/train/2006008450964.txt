This crash occurred in the early evening of a weekday on the roadside of a three-lane undivided roadway. The east/west roadway consisted of two westbound lanes and one eastbound lane with a speed limit was 40kmph (25 mph). The bituminous roadway was dry and straight and featured a downhill grade of 4/61. The traffic flow was normal and there were no adverse atmospheric conditions although the sun was setting which caused glare for vehicles traveling westbound.
V1, a 1994 Chevrolet Lumina sedan, was westbound in lane one traveling down the hill. As Vehicle 1 continued downhill the driver noticed he lost his brakes. V1 swerved to the left to avoid striking a slower moving vehicle directly in front of it and the swerved back to the right. The driver of V1 drove off the roadway to the right and blew out his left front tire when the vehicle entered a used car parking lot. V1 then impacted a 2001 Chevrolet 1500 van that was parked in the lot on the left rear bumper area. Upon impact the van was pushed off the parking lot out into traffic. V1 then impacted a second van, a 2004 Ford E350 van that was also parked in the lot. V1 struck the second van on the left side and both vehicles came to rest in the parking lot. Police Reported Travel Speed is 64 kmph for V1 and 64 kmph for V2.
V1 was driven by a 46-year-old male who reported no injuries. He had come from making a pizza delivery and was on his way back to the shop when he realized he had no brakes. He stated he successfully avoided hitting the vehicle in his path after he realized his brakes were out and than ran off the road.
