This single-vehicle crash occurred on an undivided dirt roadway. In the precrash area, this roadway is curved to the right (radius of curvature is 26 meters and superelevation is 7%) and uphill (13% grade). On the inside of the curve, erosion has caused a "washboard" effect. There is a steep downhill embankment on the outside of the curve. The speed limit on this roadway is 40 kmph (25 mph). Prior to the curve, there is a 26 kmph (15 mph) curve advisory speed sign. There are arrows indicating the curvature on the outside of the curve. This crash occurred during the hours of darkness with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2004 GMC C1500 pickup, was northbound in lane one at approximately 40 kmph (25 mph). V1 began to rotate in the curve. The driver applied the brakes and steered right in an attempt to regain control. V1 slid off the left side of the roadway and rolled over four quarter-turns down the embankment. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 40 kmph for V1.
The driver of V1, a twenty-four-year-old male, was traveling with a twenty-five-year-old male. They had stopped at a store and were "just driving around" before going home. This driver was healthy, well rested and comfortable with his vehicle and passenger. He drives this route monthly. This driver stated that he was focused on his conversation with his passenger who was discussing serious personal problems.
