This two-vehicle crash occurred on a jersey barrier-divided, straight, level trafficway. The westbound lanes consist of four through lanes. The speed limit for this roadway is 97 kmph (60 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2003 Kia Rio, was westbound in lane two. Vehicle two (V2), a 2005 Chevrolet Impala, was westbound in lane two in front of V1. V2 stopped due to an object on the roadway. The front of V1 struck the back of V2. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, a twenty-three-year-old female, was traveling with a twenty-one-year-old male passenger. They had left home and were on their way to work. This driver was healthy, well rested and comfortable with her vehicle and passenger. She travels this route daily. This driver was talking to her passenger prior to the crash. When she realized that V2 stopped, this driver applied the brakes, skidding longitudinally for thirty meters.
The driver of V2, a forty-five-year-old female, was on her way to a store from her home. She was well rested, in good health and familiar with her vehicle. She has diabetes and takes the medications Insulin, Glucophage, Lantus, Crestor, Tramadol and Ibuprofen. This driver stated that she stopped when she saw a vehicle in front of her drop a shelf unit. She noticed V1 in her rearview mirror, but was unable to attempt any avoidance maneuvers.
