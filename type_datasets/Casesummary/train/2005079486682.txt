A two-vehicle collision occurred during the morning daylight hours on a weekday. The crash occurred at the intersection of a north-south roadway and an east-west roadway. The intersection is controlled by a tri-light traffic signal, and both roadways are designated 56 kmph (35 mph) zones. At the time of the crash, there were no adverse atmospheric conditions, and the roadway was free of defects.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2003 Hyundai Santa Fe four-door SUV, was traveling eastbound in the fourth lane of a seven-lane, two-way, undivided, dry, level, bituminous roadway. V1 was stopped in the intersection and awaiting an opportunity to turn left into northbound traffic. Vehicle two (V2), a 1992 Toyota Camry four-door sedan, was traveling westbound in the first lane of opposing traffic, approaching the same intersection with the intention of passing straight through. As V1 made its turn, the front of V1 impacted the left side of V2. V2 deflected right and departed the road over the northwest corner, where it came to rest. V1 spun counter-clockwise from impact and came to rest in the intersection, facing southwest. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
