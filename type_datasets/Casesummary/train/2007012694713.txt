This crash occurred on a straight, level, undivided two-lane roadway. The roadway was snow-covered asphalt. It was cloudy. The speed was posted at 40 kmph (25mph). The crash occurred at midday at the end of the week.
V1, a 1992 Oldsmobile Ninety Eight 4-door sedan, turned left onto the roadway, accelerated for a short distance and then traveled off the roadway to the right. The right front of V1 struck a sign and then the front of V1 struck a tree. V1 came to final rest on the grass area next to the tree. The driver was transported to a local trauma unit because of injuries. The vehicle was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.
A 57-year-old male drove V1. He reported being in fair health and being treated for a lung condition. He wore corrective lenses for an unknown condition. He stated he had left his home to go to the laundry mat to find it had been closed due to a power outage. He then stated he traveled to the store to buy laundry products that he needed for his laundry. He stated he then traveled to where he got into the crash but did not remember any of the details of the crash.
