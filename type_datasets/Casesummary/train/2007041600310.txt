This crash occurred during the morning rush hour of a weekday in the eastbound lanes of a four-lane undivided roadway. The east/west roadway consisted of two lanes in each direction. The bituminous roadway was dry, straight and level with a posted speed limit of 40 kmph (25 mph). At the time of the crash the roadway was under construction and lane two was blocked off with construction barrels. There were no adverse atmospheric conditions at the time of the crash.
V1, a 1993 Toyota Corolla with one occupant, was eastbound in lane one. V2, a 1990 International armored truck with two occupants, was stopped in lane in the rush hour congestion ahead of V1. V1 failed to stop and its front struck the rear of V2. Both vehicles came to final rest at the point of impact. V1 was towed due to damage while V2 was driven from the scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is 56 kmph for V1 and 56 kmph for V2.
The driver of V1, a 25-year-old male, was transported for treatment of minor injuries. He informed this researcher that he was on his way to school in lane one when the traffic came to a quick stop in front of him. He tried to avoid the crash by steering to the left and slamming on his brakes but was unsuccessful. The front of his vehicle came to rest under the back of the armored truck. He was cited for the crash for failure to use due care.
The driver of V2, a 39-year-old male, was not injured in the crash. He stated that he and his co-worker were proceeding along their normal work route when they came to a stop in lane two. As they were stopped when they were hit from behind. Since they were driving an armored vehicle filled with money they were nervous that this might have been a robbery attempt. The driver denied access to the vehicle to perform a vehicle inspection.
