This two-vehicle crash occurred in a four-legged intersection. The north/south roadway is straight, level and consists of two lanes in each direction and a center left turn lane. The speed limit for this roadway is 64 kmph (40 mph). The roadway on the west side of the north/south roadway is undivided, straight, level and consists of one lane in each direction. The eastbound lane is controlled by a stop sign at the intersection. The speed limit for this roadway is 40 kmph (25 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2007 F450 medium truck, was eastbound in lane one, stopped at the stop sign. Vehicle two (V2), a 1997 Chevrolet Malibu, was southbound in lane one. V1 entered the intersection, turning left. The front of V2 struck the left side of V1. V2 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, a thirty-six-year-old male, was on his way to work from his home. He was well rested, in good health and comfortable with his vehicle and cargo. He travels this route monthly. He has diabetes and takes the medications Avandia and Glipizide. This driver stated that he looked to the left and thought that V2 had a right turn signal on. He further stated that he assumed that V2 was turning right at the intersection and that it was clear for him to enter the intersection.
The driver of V2, a thirty-three-year-old male, was on his way to work from his home. He was healthy, well rested and familiar with his vehicle. He travels this route daily. This driver stated that he saw V1 and assumed that V1's driver would wait until he (V2) had passed through the intersection. He further stated that his right turn signal was not on.
