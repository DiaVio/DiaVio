This single-vehicle crash occurred on a 4-lane divided asphalt roadway with a raised median. The westbound lanes are straight then curve left and are level. The speed limit is 64 kmph (40 mph). The crash occurred during early morning at approximately 0700 hours with no adverse weather or road conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2001 Kia Sephia 4-door sedan, was westbound in the #1 lane when it departed the #1 lane to the right and contacted the curb edge with its right front tire. V1 traveled over the curb and across the concrete sidewalk as it continued forward where it impacted a short cement wall with its front-end. V1 continued forward and contacted the same short wall 2 more times before coming to rest partially up on the top edge of the wall facing north. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 64 kmph for V1.
The driver of V1, a 47-year-old female, was on her way home from her workplace where she assists with developmentally disabled adults in a group home. She had switched work shifts from the swing shift to graveyard earlier in the week. She had driven 11 kilometers (7 miles) and was almost to her residence when she fell asleep and drifted off the roadway. She is in good health and her vehicle was in good working condition.
