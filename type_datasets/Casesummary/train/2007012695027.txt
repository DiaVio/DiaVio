This crash occurred in the late evening of a weekend off the east side of a two-lane, two-way roadway. The roadway consisted of one lane in each direction with wide shoulders on each side. The dirt roadway was a straight and level with a speed limit of 89 kmph (55 mph). The roadway was icy and it was sleeting at the time of the crash.
V1, a 1999 GMC Safari minivan with one occupant, was southbound in lane one. V1 started to fishtail and the driver lost control. The driver steered to the left and to the right, but could not regain control of the vehicle. V1 departed the roadway on the left side and went into a ditch. V1 rolled right 2 quarter turns before coming to final rest in the ditch on it's roof. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.
The driver of V1, a 46-year-old female, suffered minor injuries and was transported for treatment. She advised that she had just come from her sister's house and was on her way home. She was traveling between 50-64 kmph (31-40 mph) prior to loosing control.
