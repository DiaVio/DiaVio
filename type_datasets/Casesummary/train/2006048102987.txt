This single vehicle crash occurred early morning during daylight hours on a two-lane, undivided, dry bituminous, rural roadway. The roadway curves to the left with a radius of 375 m and a super elevation of 0%. The speed limit is 56 kph (35 MPH) and there is a curve warning sign.
Vehicle one(V 1), a 1997 Ford Thunderbird coupe driven by a 28 year-old female, was traveling northbound. V 1 was attempting to negotiate the left hand curve at a high rate of speed. V 1 departed the right road edge and then stuck a ditch. V 1 then went airborne, cleared an intersecting roadway, and landed approximately 13.5 m away. V 1 then traveled another 22 m to final rest. The driver was admittedly intoxicated. The driver first stated that someone else was driving her vehicle. Then she stated that she went into the curve too fast. The driver had been in a bar drinking beer all night and was attempting to go to her brother-in-law's to sleep. V 1 was towed from the scene, the driver was transported to a local medical facility. Police Reported Travel Speed is 80 kmph for V1.
Associated factors- The driver's intoxication, fatigue, aggressive driving, and poor direction control are all contributing factors to this crash.
