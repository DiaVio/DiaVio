This accident occurred on a two-way, four-lane roadway. It is physically divided with a median strip, and has a traffic-control device at this intersection. The conditions were as follows: late evening, cloudy skies, the roadway was wet and was experiencing snowfall for the atmospheric conditions. The gradient for vehicle 1 was -2 percent and vehicle 2 was +2 percent. The posted speed limit for vehicle one (V1) and vehicle two (V2) was (72 kph) 45 mph, V1 was stopped in the left hand turn lane prior to the crash.
V1 was a 1993 Black Ford Thunderbird, which had a green round light, and did not have a left turn arrow, in the traffic control device. V1 struck V2 in the left front, near the front door and down the side until it reached the left rear tire and started to push V2. The impact caused V2 to be pushed off the right side of the road, coming to final rest on the curb on the southbound side. V2 needed to be towed from the scene. V1 drove to a nearby parking lot and parked. Police Reported Travel Speed is 8 kmph for V1 and 8 kmph for V2.
The driver of V1 was not treated, because he did not suffer any injuries. After obtaining the interview from the driver of V1, he thought he had enough space to proceed.
V2 was a 2005 Black Subaru Legacy was traveling southbound and had a green light at the intersection. Upon realization of the impending crash, the driver of V2 attempted to avoid the collision by steering his vehicle to the right. With this maneuver, the driver unfortunately could not prevent this crash. This vehicle also needed to be towed from the scene.
