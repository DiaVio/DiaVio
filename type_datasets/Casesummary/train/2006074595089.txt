This two-vehicle crash occurred on a straight, level, access-controlled, jersey barrier-divided roadway. The eastbound lanes consist of four through lanes and were covered with snow and ice at the time of the crash. The speed limit on this roadway is 97 kmph (60 mph). It was dusk and snowing and traffic was congested at the time of the crash.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2003 Mazda 6, was eastbound in lane three. Vehicle two (V2), a 2001 Dodge Intrepid, was eastbound in lane two. V2 began to rotate counterclockwise and traveled into lane three. The front of V1 struck the front of V2. V1 then traveled off the left side of the roadway and the front struck the median jersey barrier. V2 continued rotating and traveled off the left side of the roadway and the back struck the median jersey barrier, causing a small fire to erupt. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, an eighteen-year-old female, was traveling from her home to another state. She was in fairly good health, well rested and familiar with her vehicle. She travels this route monthly. This driver has asthma and takes Albuterol, Methylphenidate, Tylenol and vitamins. She stated that she noticed V2 lose control and move into lane three. This driver steered left and applied the brakes in an attempt to avoid a crash.
The driver of V2, a forty-four-year-old female, was traveling with a sixty-four-year-old female and a twelve-year-old female. They were on their way from their home to an airport hotel and had been traveling for more than four hours. This driver was healthy, well rested and comfortable with her vehicle and passengers. She rarely travels on this roadway. She stated that she was thinking about a family problem and also was uncomfortable with the weather at the time of the crash.
