This two-vehicle crash occurred on a two-lane asphalt constructed roadway. This roadway runs east to west is straight and level. The posted speed limit is 105 kph (65 mph) with a passing painted centerline. This crash occurred during daylight hours with good visibility and no adverse weather.
Vehicle one, a 1992 Pontiac Grand Prix was entering this roadway from the south side exiting a private driveway. Vehicle two, a 2000 Chevy Cavalier was westbound in the first lane. Vehicle one turned left from the driveway and the front of vehicle two impacted the left rear of vehicle two. Vehicle one rotated counterclockwise and came to rest in the eastbound shoulder. Vehicle two came to rest in the westbound lane. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 16 kmph for V1 and 16 kmph for V2.
The driver of vehicle one, a seventeen-year-old male was on his way to the post office. This driver was leaving his driveway and had just started driving. This driver stated that he had waved to a friend who was on a motorcycle off to the west side of his vehicle. This driver indicated that he was going to go over and talk to his friend. This driver drives this vehicle daily and over this road on a daily basis. This driver had only been driving for less than two years.
The driver of vehicle two, a twenty-year-old male was heading home from the hospital. He was traveling with his girlfriend and his newborn daughter. This driver had been driving for about forty minutes. This driver stated that he also was looking at the driver on the motorcycle. This driver drives this vehicle daily and over this road on a weekly basis.
