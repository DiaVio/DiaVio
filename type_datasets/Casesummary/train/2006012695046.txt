The crash occurred on a two lane, one way, dry concrete roadway in daylight conditions.
V1, a 2003 Chevrolet Malibu was traveling north in lane two. There was a crash up ahead on the left side and it was a construction zone. Traffic had slowed down. V2, a 1993 Chevrolet Suburban was also in lane two, traveling northbound on the same roadway in front of V1. V2 had stopped because of another crash up ahead, V1's front contacted V2's back. They both came to final rest in the roadway. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1 was a 20 year-old male who stated that he was slowing down for traffic because there was another crash. He didn't realize the traffic slowed down that fast and he couldn't avoid hitting V2. The driver reported that he had been traveling between 34-48kmph (21-30mph) prior to the crash. The EDR reported a speed of 121kmph (75mph) prior to the crash. He was rested and traveling home. He was uninjured in the crash and refused treatment.
The driver of V2 was a 47-year old male who had reported that he had been stopped for approximately 5 seconds because the traffic was backed up from another crash. Then V1 just ran in the back of him. He was rested and traveling to a job. He was uninjured in the crash and refused treatment.
