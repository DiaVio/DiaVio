This single vehicle collision occurred heading north on a straight section of a two-way arterial road with four lanes of travel. The pre-crash roadway has a -4% grade and is paved with asphalt. At the time of the crash, the road was thoroughly wet, and there was a constant drizzle of rain. The posted speed limit for the roadway is 48 kmph (30 mph).
Vehicle one (V1), a 1991 Infiniti M30 was southbound in the first lane. A southbound vehicle turned left in front of V1, initiating a critical crash envelope. To avoid a collision with the non-contact vehicle, the driver of V1 braked and steered right in front of the other vehicle. V1 departed the road and the front plane hit a pole and the right front tire impacted the curb. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.
The driver of V1, a twenty-five year old male, was heading to an appointment with the passenger. This driver had been driving for about twenty minutes at the time of the crash. This driver was certain that the car that caused the crash was a small green car, but the plate given to the police came up as a red car. There was no contact between the cars that left any visible mark. V1 had very little tread on three of its tires, and the left rear tire was a completely bald temporary spare tire. This driver drives this vehicle on a daily basis and over this road on a daily basis.
