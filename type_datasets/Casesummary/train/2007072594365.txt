This two-vehicle crash occurred in a four-legged, traffic signal-controlled intersection. Both roadways are straight, level, undivided and consist of one lane in each direction with parking on both sides. The speed limit on these roadways is 48 kmph (30 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1994 G20 van, was westbound in lane one. Vehicle two (V2), a 1991 Oldsmobile Ninety-Eight, was southbound in lane one. In the intersection, the front of V1 struck the left side of V2. Both vehicles traveled off the southwest corner of the intersection. The front of V2 struck a steel post. The right side of V1 struck a traffic signal pole. The right side of V2 then struck another steel post. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, a male, left the scene and was not interviewed. No information is available for this driver.
The driver of V2, a thirty-three-year-old male, was on his way home from dropping his niece and nephew off at school. He was healthy, well rested and familiar with his vehicle. He travels this route several times each month. This driver stated that he did not notice V1 prior to the crash.
