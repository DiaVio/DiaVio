The crash occurred in the early afternoon at an intersection of two one-way 4-lane asphalt roadways. The intersection was controlled by traffic signals. Both roadways were dry, straight, and level. The posted speed limit on both roadways was 56 kmph (35 mph).
V1, a 1997 Ford Taurus, was traveling southbound approaching the intersection intending to go straight. V2, a 2000 Buick LeSabre, was traveling eastbound approaching the same intersection, also intending to go straight through the intersection. The driver of V1 was unfamiliar with the area and it was her first time on this road. She was looking for the expressway and did not see her traffic light turn red. The front of V1 contacted the left of V2. V2's undercarriage then struck the curb on the southeast corner of the intersection and its front then struck a fence before coming to final rest off the south side of the roadway. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1 was a 26-year old female who reported that she had been traveling between 67-80kmph (41-50mph) prior to the crash. She had no health-related problems and had taken no medications prior to the crash. She was rested and traveling to a restaurant. She was wearing her prescribed lenses that corrected a myopic (near-sighted) condition. She sustained minor injuries but refused treatment.
The driver of V2 was a 59-year old female who reported that she had been traveling between 34-48kmph (21-30mph) prior to the crash. She had a history of high blood pressure and had taken medication for it. She was rested and traveling to work. She was wearing her prescribed lenses that corrected a myopic (near-sighted) condition. She sustained minor injuries from the crash and was transported to a local trauma facility.
