This two-vehicle crash occurred in the intersection of two two-lane roadways. The north-south road is stop sign controlled. This roadway is constructed of asphalt with an upgrade of 8 percent for the southbound lanes and a downgrade of 6 percent for the westbound traffic. This crash occurred during daylight with no adverse weather and good visibility. The stop sign on the north entrance is obstructed by trees, which are on private property. The posted speed limit is 40 kph (25 mph).
Vehicle one, a 1983 Mercedes 240D was southbound stopped at the stop sign. Vehicle two, a 2006 Chevrolet Cobalt was westbound in the first lane approaching the intersection. Vehicle one inched out into the intersection and the front plane of vehicle one impacted the right side of vehicle two. Vehicle one rotated clockwise and came to rest on the westbound lane heading west about 15 meters from this intersection. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 0 kmph for V1 and 0 kmph for V2.
The driver of vehicle one, a fifty-one year old female was on her way home after her first day at a new job. This driver had been driving for about fifteen minutes at the time of the crash. This driver was not familiar with the roadway and was looking for a cross street just prior to the crash. She stated that she had stopped for the stop sign and that she was creeping forward looking right (west) for the cross street. This driver drives this vehicle daily but this was her first time on this roadway.
The driver of vehicle two, a forty-five year old female stated that she was coming from her work site and is very familiar with this roadway and that she saw vehicle one stopped at the intersection. Then as she got closer to this intersection vehicle one began to move forward. This driver drives this vehicle daily and over this road on a daily basis.
