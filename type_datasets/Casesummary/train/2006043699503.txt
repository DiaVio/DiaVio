The crash occurred in the eastbound lanes of a four-lane, divided highway with a median wall. There were four southbound lanes on a down-sloping, right curve. The roadway had a posted speed limit of 96 kmph (60 mph). Conditions were dusk and dry with cloudy skies. The setting was late afternoon hours on a weekday.
Vehicle one (V1) was a 2004 Honda Civic sedan with one occupant. Vehicle two (V2) was a 2001 Toyota MR2 convertible, also with one occupant. V1 was traveling east in the second lane, negotiating the right curve. V2 was traveling east in the fourth lane negotiating the same curve. V1 was slightly ahead of V2. Both vehicles merged in lane three simultaneously. The left plane of V1 impacted the right plane of V2. Both vehicles then began rotating counterclockwise and traveled into the median wall. The front of V1 impacted the wall. The rear of V2 impacted the wall. The right of plane of V1 then impacted the right plane of V2. Both vehicles came to rest on the left shoulder. Police Reported Travel Speed is 97 kmph for V1 and 97 kmph for V2.
The driver of V1, a 27-year old female was uninjured. She stated that she was going home from work when she merged into the third lane. The other vehicle merged at the same time, causing the crash. She did not have a chance to react in avoidance. She estimated her travel and impact speeds to be 101 kmph (63 mph). The Honda sedan was towed due to damage.
The driver of V2, a 42-year old female, was transported to a local trauma center with minor injuries. She was unable to be located for an interview. The investigating officer estimated her travel and impact speeds to be 96 kmph (60 mph). The Toyota convertible was towed from the scene, but not due to damage.
