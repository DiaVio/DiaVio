This incident occurred on a two-lane undivided winding roadway that had no traffic control devices. The crash occurred in the early afternoon hours while it was raining out. The posted speed limit was 48 kmph (30 mph).
V1 was a 1993 Ford Explorer driven by a 24-year old female. The driver of V1 was heading north on the roadway when she lost control of the vehicle and skidded off the roadway to the left. V1 struck a residential mailbox and overturned. Upon arrival, the driver of the vehicle was waiting in another car with a friend that had been traveling behind her. The driver and her friend told police that she wasn't speeding and that she just lost control of the car for no reason. She stated that the car started to slide and she lost control. She was uninjured. Police on the scene stated that accidents on this stretch of roadway were plentiful and one officer stated that there was mud on the roadway that comes from a house that is being worked on about 50 yards from where the accident occurred. Numerous attempts to get in contact with the driver were unsuccessful. In listening to the conversation between the driver and the officer, it was learned that the driver had no driver's license and was not supposed to be driving. She was issued a citation and released. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.
