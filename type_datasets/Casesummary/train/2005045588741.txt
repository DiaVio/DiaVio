Case #2005-45-128
The crash occurred on an east/west urban four-lane roadway that was not divided with two-way center left turn lane. It was intersected from the north by a private drive. Fifty meters east of the private drive the four lane road intersects a separate side street that is controlled by traffic signals. The posted speed was 48kph (30mph). Conditions were daylight and dry on a weekday afternoon. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
Vehicle #1 was a 1981 Ford Escort traveling east on the four-lane road attempting to turn left into the private drive. Vehicle #2, a 2003 Nissan Altima, was westbound in the right lane. Vehicle #1 attempted the left turn and was struck in the right side by the front of Vehicle #2. After impact the Escort (V1) rotated clockwise and came to rest facing southeast in the private drive. The Altima (V2) came to rest near impact facing northwest. Both vehicles were towed due to damage.
Vehicle #1 was driven by an 81-year old female that was belted. She and an 80-year old belted passenger were both transported to an area hospital with non-incapacitating injuries. The driver stated that she saw the other vehicle turn right on the four lane road from the side street but thought she had time to make it across to the private drive. Police charged her with failure to yield.
Vehicle #2 was operated by a 19-year old male that was belted and not injured in the collision. He was accompanied by an 18-year old female that was also belted with no injuries. The frontal airbags did not deploy in the crash. Vehicle #2 left 7m of pre impact skids prior to impact.
