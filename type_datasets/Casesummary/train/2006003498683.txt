The crash occurred in a signalized T-intersection. The north-south roadway had two lanes in each direction and the east-west roadway had one lane in each direction. The roadway was straight and level, and it was clear with no adverse conditions. It was dark but lighted, and the speed limit was 48kmph (30mph).
Vehicle # 1 was a 2003 Toyota Corolla that was traveling northbound. Vehicle # 2 was a 1997 Ford Escort Wagon that was traveling southbound and attempting to make a left turn. At the intersection, the front of V1 impacted the front of V2. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
A 54 year-old female who did not sustain any injuries drove the Toyota Corolla. An 86-year-old female in the right-front seating position also did not sustain any injuries. The driver and passenger both declined to participate in the study. The Toyota was towed due to damage.
A 55-year old male who did not sustain any injuries drove the Ford Escort. The driver stated that he was at the intersection waiting to make a left turn after the traffic light turned red, and the driver of V2 ran the red light prior to impact. The Ford was towed due to damage.
