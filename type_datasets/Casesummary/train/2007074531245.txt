This two-vehicle crash occurred in a four-legged, traffic signal-controlled intersection in an interchange area. The southbound roadway is one-way, straight, has a four percent uphill grade and consists of three through lanes. The speed limit for this roadway is 48 kmph (30 mph). The westbound roadway is one-way, straight, level and consists of four through lanes. The speed limit for this roadway is 56 kmph (35 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours, the roadways were wet and there were no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2001 Ford Taurus, was southbound in lane two. Vehicle two (V2), a 003 Ford Taurus, was westbound in lane four. There were non-contact vehicles (witnesses) in westbound lanes two and three. In the intersection, the front of V2 struck the left side of V1. V1 rotated counterclockwise and the right side struck a pole in the southwest corner of the intersection. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, a thirty-two-year-old female, was on her way to work from her home. She was healthy, well rested and familiar with her vehicle. She travels this route daily. She takes an unspecified medication for high blood pressure. This driver stated that she is stressed at work due to learning a new position due to a recent promotion. She further stated that she did not see V2 prior to the crash.
The driver of V2, a -year-old female, was on her way to a doctor appointment from her home. She was healthy, well rested and familiar with her vehicle. She rarely travels this route. This driver stated that she did not see V1 prior to the crash.
