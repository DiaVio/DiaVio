The crash involved two vehicles a few hours before midnight. The location was a six lane, undivided (two-way traffic), straight, downhill roadway. Posted speed limit 56 kmph (35 mph). The weather was clear and the road surface dry concrete. It was dark but lighted.
Vehicle one (V1) was a 1990 Lincoln Town Car driven by a 27 year-old male. He was traveling west in the second lane of traffic. Vehicle two (V2) was a 1997 Pontiac Trans-sport minivan driven by a 33 year-old female with one passenger. They were also traveling west in second travel lane in front of V1. V1 was at a higher rate of speed and it's front impacted the back of V2, V2 rotated clockwise coming to final rest facing east in the travel lane. V1 continued west hitting the curb on the north side of the roadway continuing west contacting the bridge with it's right front. Driver then attempted to flee the scene continued to drive west entering a circled downhill exit ramp. V1 continued to drive on the exit ramp until it came to rest at the east curb of the exit ramp. Both vehicles were towed from the scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The pre-event movement for V 2 coded going straight, category other motor vehicle in lane traveling in same direction with higher speed. Critical reason not coded to this vehicle.
