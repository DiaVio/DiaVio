This single-vehicle crash occurred on a curbed median-divided trafficway. The westbound lanes are level, curve to the left and consist of three through lanes. The speed limit for this roadway is 56 kmph (35 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1991 Acura Legend, was westbound in lane two. V1 changed lanes to the left and traveled off the left side of the road. The left front wheel struck the median curb. V1 returned to the westbound lanes and rotating clockwise, traveled to the northwest. The back left wheel of V1 struck the right curb. V1 then traveled off the road and the left side struck a utility pole guy wire. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.
The driver of V1, a forty-five-year-old male, was on his way from work to lunch with two male passengers. He was healthy, well rested and comfortable with his vehicle and passengers. They were conversing and he was smoking. This driver stated that he takes Vietnamese tree root on a daily basis. He further stated that he was "cut off" by another vehicle causing him to abruptly change lanes to the left. There is no evidence to support this claim and the witness indicated that there was no other vehicle. This driver's BAC was 0.015 according to the police report.
