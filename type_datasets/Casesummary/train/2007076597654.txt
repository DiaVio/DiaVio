This single-vehicle crash occurred on an undivided roadway during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions. The westbound lanes are curved to the left, have an uphill grade and consist of two through lanes. The speed limit on this roadway is 89 kmph (55 mph) with a warning sign for the curve and a speed advisory sign for 80 kmph (50 mph).
Vehicle one (V1), a 2002 GMC K1500 pickup, was westbound in lane one. V1 traveled off the right side of the road. The front of V1 struck several large boulders. V1 then rotated counterclockwise and rolled over to the right two quarter-turns. V1 came to rest on its top and was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 89 kmph for V1.
The driver of V1, a fifty-two-year-old female, was traveling from her home to a store in another city. She was well rested and familiar with her vehicle. She travels this route weekly. This driver has fibromyalgia and lower back pain and takes Oxycontin and Xanax. She stated that she did not remember anything about the crash. A witness stated that V1 just "drifted off the right side of the road."
