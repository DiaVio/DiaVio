This two-vehicle crash occurred in a four-leg intersection. The east/west roadway is straight, level, undivided and consists of one lane in each direction with parking on both sides. The speed limit for this roadway is 56 kmph (30 mph). The intersecting roadway is straight, level, and one-way in the southbound direction with parking on both sides. This roadway is controlled by a stop sign at the intersection. The speed limit for this roadway is 40 kmph (25 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2003 Toyota Camry, was eastbound in lane one. Vehicle two (V2), a 2003 Chevrolet Suburban, was stopped southbound in lane one. V2 entered the intersection and the right side was struck by the front of V1. The vehicles rotated and the left side of V1 struck the right side of V2. V2 continued to the southeast and off the roadway. The right side of V2 struck a fence and a tree. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, a fifty-five-year-old male, was traveling with a forty-six-year-old male passenger. This driver was not interviewed. No further information is available for this driver.
The driver of V2, a forty-two-year-old male, was traveling with his thirty-six-year-old wife. They were on their way home from dropping off their children at school. This driver was healthy, well rested and comfortable with his vehicle and passenger. He travels this route daily. This driver stated that he saw V2 "about fifty feet away" as he crossed the intersection. He further stated that other traffic on the intersecting street had stopped to allow him to proceed.
