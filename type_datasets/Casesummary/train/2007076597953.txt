This single vehicle crash occurred on a two-lane asphalt constructed road. This road runs north to south with guard railing on both sides. At the area of this crash the road is straight and runs uphill with a +6 % grade for southbound traffic. The posted speed limit is 89 kmph (55 mph). This crash occurred the late morning daylight hours with no adverse weather and good visibility. This road is in a rural area with light traffic.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2001 Pontiac Montana APV, driven by a forty (40) year old female was southbound going around 105 kmph (65 mph) when the driver reached down for a water bottle and steered right off the road edge. Once off the road edge V1 contacted the end of a guard railing with its front bumper plane. V1 rotated counter-clockwise and side slapped the railing with the right rear quarter panel. V1 then crossed the centerline and impacted the east side railing with its front plane. It bounced right and impacted the railing a second time. V1 rotated clockwise again and impacted the railing with left front corner coming to rest against the railing. This driver was injured and air transported to a trauma center where she was treated and released. Vehicle was towed for disabling damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 105 kmph for V1.
The driver stated that she was on her way to a friend's house and that when she placed a water bottle on the center console holder that it fell to the floor. She looked down and reached for the bottle and this is when the impact occurred. After the initial impact the driver was tossed sideways in the seat and was unable to control the vehicle.
