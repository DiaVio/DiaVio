This two-vehicle crash occurred in a driveway area on the north side of a straight, level east/west roadway. This roadway consists of two lanes in each direction and a center left-turn lane. The speed limit for this roadway is 56 kmph (35 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions but with traffic congestion present.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1995 Land Rover Range Rover, was stopped eastbound in the center left-turn lane, waiting to turn into the driveway. Vehicle two (V2), a 2005 Dodge Neon, was westbound in lane one. A driver in westbound lane two indicated that V1 was clear to make his left turn into the driveway. The driver proceeded with the left turn. The driver of V2 noticed V1 at the last minute and applied the brakes, skidding longitudinally. The front of V1 struck the front of V2. Both vehicles came to rest near the point of impact. V2 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, a sixty-one-year-old male, had just left a store and was "just driving around." He was well rested, healthy and comfortable with his vehicle. He takes Lipitor for high cholesterol. This driver stated that he didn't see V2 in lane one until just before the crash. This was the first time he had driven on this roadway.
The driver of V2, a twenty-one-year-old female, was traveling from work to her home. She was healthy, well rested and familiar with her vehicle. This driver travels on this road daily.
Associated factors for both vehicles were the traffic congestion and the sight line restriction resulting from the stopped traffic in westbound lane two.
