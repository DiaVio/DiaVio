The crash occurred on a rural undivided two-lane east/west road at an intersecting residential northbound road (T-type intersection). The rural road was curved to the left (north) if eastbound. The curve radius was measured as 150m with a super-elevation measuring 2 percent. The rural road had a positive 4.3 percent grade and the posted speed limit was 48 kmph (30 mph). Conditions at the time of the crash were daylight and dry at mid afternoon on a weekday. There was a utility pole and some small trees on the southeast corner of the intersection.
V1 was a 1995 Dodge Ram 1500 pickup truck that was traveling east on the rural roadway when it departed the right side of the road at the intersection and then went over the residential road's east curb. The front left of V1 struck a small tree and then V1 hit a utility pole with its front center. V1 came to rest with its front centered on the utility pole facing southeast. V1 was towed from the scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.
V1 was driven by an unrestrained eighteen-year-old male who was transported to an area hospital with police reported non-incapacitating injuries. His airbags did deploy in the crash. He was unable to be contacted for an interview.
