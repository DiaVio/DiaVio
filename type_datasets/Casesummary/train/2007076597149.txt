This two-vehicle rear-end crash with a rollover occurred on a 5-lane non-divided asphalt constructed roadway. This roadway is straight with a slight down grade when going south and has business driveways along both sides of this road. The posted speed limit is 72 kmph (45 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with heavy traffic conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1989 Toyota 4 door Camry sedan, was southbound following Vehicle two (V2), a 1996 Toyota Tacoma pickup, when it impacted the rear plane of V2. V1 continued forward a short distance then turned off the roadway and into the same area as V2. V1's driver, a twenty seven (27) year-old female, stated that she had been on the road for a couple of hours and that the traffic was heavy at the time of the crash. She stated that she had glanced into the left door mirror checking traffic to change lanes and when she looked forward that V1 had slowed almost to a stop and was turning right off the roadway. She stated that she slammed on the brakes and steered slightly left trying to avoid hitting V2 but still hit V2 with her front bumper. She stated that she was in good health but had recently had gastric by-pass surgery and was doing very well. She stated that she had seen her surgeon earlier this day and was on her way home. V1 was driven from the scene Police Reported Travel Speed is 32 kmph for V1 and 32 kmph for V2.
V2 was southbound in the #1 lane decelerating to execute a right hand turn to enter a business when it was contacted on the rear plane by V1. V2 was pushed in a clockwise rotation where it tripped with the left side tires and rolled 2 quarters coming to rest on its roof. V2 did impact a standing pole with its right side bed railing area. A twenty seven (27) year-old male driver was slightly injured and transported to a local hospital where he was treated and released. V2 was towed from the scene.
