Case Summary 2006-11-033
The crash occurred on a two-way, two-lane, straight, level, bituminous, urban road with a posted speed limit of 72 kmh (45 mph). Conditions at the time of the weekday evening crash were daylight, clear and dry. A vehicle inspection was obtained on-scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.
V1, a 1999 Mercury Cougar, driven by a 17 year-old male, was traveling eastbound on the road after accelerating quickly from a side street 2 tenths of a mile prior to the crash. V1 was observed by a witness to be swerving back and forth across both lanes and then rotated counterclockwise before leaving the road on the right side. V1 struck a 4” x 4” wooden mailbox post with the front left corner, and then dug into a grassy embankment with the right side tires. V1 rolled right, became airborne, and continued rolling 8 quarter turns. V1 came to rest 40 meters from the trip point, which was 2-4 meters past the mailbox.
The Mercury was towed from the scene due to damage. The unrestrained driver was ejected from the vehicle 31 meters past the trip point. The driver was transported to a local trauma center from the scene.
