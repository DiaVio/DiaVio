This two-vehicle crash occurred on a straight, curbed median-divided roadway, on the approach to a four-legged, traffic signal-controlled intersection. The northbound lanes have a 6.5% uphill grade and consist of two through lanes, a left turn lane and a right turn lane. The speed limit on this roadway is 56 kmph (30 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1997 BMW 318IC, was northbound in lane three. Vehicle two (V2), a 2003 Ford Taurus, was northbound in lane three, stopped for the red traffic signal. The front of V1 struck the back of V2. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, a fifty-one-year-old male, was traveling from lunch to a medical appointment. He was well rested, in fairly good health and familiar with his vehicle. He travels this route monthly. This driver takes Melformin for his diabetes. This driver stated that "something" unspecified on the right caught his eye and he was looking to the right when his vehicle struck V2.
The driver of V2, a seventy-eight-year-old male, was traveling with his seventy-eight -year-old wife. They had just dropped their daughter off at work and were on their way to lunch. This driver takes several medications for his heart condition and high blood pressure. He was well rested and comfortable with his vehicle and his passenger. This was the first time this driver had driven on this roadway.
