The crash occurred near a small intersection in the southbound lane of a two-lane roadway with wide paved shoulders. There was a breakdown lane off the southbound lane of the road and parking lot entrances to the east and west. No traffic controls were present. The roadway had a posted speed limit of 72 kmph (45 mph) and was on level ground. Conditions were daylight and dry with clear skies. The setting was late afternoon hours on a weekday.
Vehicle one was a 2000 Nissan Maxima sedan stopped facing east at the entrance to a parking lot waiting to turn left. Vehicle two was a 2000 Chevrolet Venture minivan traveling south in lane one. V1 proceeded to turn left into v2's lane. The left front corner of v1 was impacted by the front of v2. V1 rotated 180 degrees clockwise and came to rest in lane one facing south. V2 traveled across the oncoming lane of travel and came to rest on the partially off the east edge of the road. Police Reported Travel Speed is 24 kmph for V1 and 24 kmph for V2.
The Nissan Maxima (v1) was driven by a 16-year old male who was treated on scene with unknown injuries. He stated that he was heading home when he came to this intersection. He claims the driver of a parked school bus waved him out into the roadway. He turned left and was struck by v2 before ever seeing it coming. He estimated his travel and impact speeds to be 16 kmph (10 mph). His Nissan sedan was towed due to damage.
The Chevrolet Venture (v2) was driven by a 36-year old female who was transported to a local trauma center along with her two passengers for non-incapacitating injuries. She stated that she was heading to a doctor's appointment when she noticed v1 traveling through a parking lot preparing to enter the road. He turned into her lane coming towards her. She claims to not have had a chance to react. She estimated her travel and impact speeds to be 72 kmph (45 mph). Her Chevrolet minivan was towed due to damage.
