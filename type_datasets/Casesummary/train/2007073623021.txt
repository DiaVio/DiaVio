This two-vehicle crash occurred in a four-legged intersection. Both roadways are straight and level. The north/south roadway consists of two lanes in each direction and a center left turn lane. The trafficway west of the north/south roadway is divided, consists of one lane in each direction and is controlled by a stop sign at the intersection. The speed limit for this roadway is 56 kmph (35 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1993 Pontiac Grand Am, was eastbound in lane one. Vehicle two (V2), a 2003 Pontiac Grand Am, was southbound in lane two. A non-contact vehicle (a school bus) was southbound in lane one, turning right. V1 turned left and the front struck the front of V2. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, a seventeen-year-old female, was on her way to school from her home. She was healthy, well rested and familiar with her vehicle. She travels this route daily. She had one year of driving experience at the time of the crash. This driver stated that she did not see V2 due to the bus in the southbound lane.
The driver of V2, a fifty-six-year-old female, was on her way to work from her home. She was healthy, well rested and familiar with her vehicle. She travels this route daily. She takes an unspecified high blood pressure medication. This driver stated that she saw V1 traveling eastbound but did not realize that V1 was going to enter the intersection in front of her.
