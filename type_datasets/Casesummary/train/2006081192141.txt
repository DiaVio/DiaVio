This two-vehicle crash occurred in the late morning hours on a Wednesday. The location was a busy four-way intersection in a residential area. The weather was mild, it was daylight and it was raining. The roadway was wet, but visibility was clear. The roadway for vehicle one (V1) was a bituminous four lane, undivided, two way arterial with a slight positive grade approaching the intersection. The roadway for vehicle two (V2) was a bituminous three lane, undivided, two way with a negative grade approaching the intersection. The posted speed limit for both roadways was 56 kmph (35 mph). Traffic at the intersection is controlled by traffic signals that were operating properly.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2004 Toyota Prius four door hatchback with one occupant, was traveling westbound in the outside curb lane approaching the intersection. Vehicle two (V2), a 2001 Dodge Neon four door sedan with one occupant, was stopped southbound in a left turn only lane waiting for the traffic signal to change. As the signal changed to green the driver pulled forward into the intersection with the intent to turn left. The driver saw V1 enter the intersection from his left and stated he immediately stopped his vehicle. V1 entered the intersection on a red traffic light and the front of V1 struck the left side of V2. On impact V1 stopped. The driver exited his vehicle to assess the damage. The driver got back into his vehicle and drove to the northwest curb of the intersection and parked his vehicle. V1 was towed due to damage. At impact V2 rotated clockwise 180 degrees and come to rest in the north crosswalk facing north. V2 was towed due to damage. Neither occupant was transported. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
