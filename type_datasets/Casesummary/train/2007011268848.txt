The crash occurred on a two-way, five lane, straight, level, bituminous road with a posted speed limit of 72 kph (45 mph). Conditions at the time of the weekend, morning crash were daylight, clear and dry.
V1, a 2005 Chevrolet Silverado 1500 4-door pick up truck, was driven by a 48 year-old male on his way from the credit union to a store. V1 was traveling westbound in lane two when the driver looked to the right and saw that his cousin's shop was opened. The driver stated that he slowed to 15 mph and decided to turned right into the driveway, without activating his right turn signal. The driver saw v1 in lane one at the last second and tried to steer left to avoid the crash. The right side of v1 struck the left side of v2. V1 pulled into the next driveway and came to a stop. V1 was drivable following the crash. The fully restrained driver denied injuries at the scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
V2, a 2003 Jeep Wrangler SE 2-door, driven by a 35 year-old male on his commute to work, was traveling 55 mph in lane one. The driver stated that he saw v2 in the next lane ahead of him, and when he saw v1 begin his turn he braked (fully locked up) and steered right. After the impact, v2 left the roadway on the right and went up a curb, flattening both front tires. V2 came to rest off of the road, facing west. The fully restrained driver denied injuries at the scene.
