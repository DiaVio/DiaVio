The crash occurred in the eastbound lane of a straight two lane undivided roadway with a posted speed limit of 64 KPH (40 MPH). The roadway is a narrow country road with numerous small hills and no lane delineations. The weather was clear, the roadway dry and it was daylight at the time of this early afternoon weekday crash.
Vehicle #1, a 2004 Honda Civic, was traveling east on the roadway and approaching a hillcrest with limited sight distance. As Vehicle #1 crested the hill another non-contact vehicle was slowing or stopped in her lane. The Driver of Vehicle #1 took evasive maneuvers by steering to the left and braking. The Driver of Vehicle #1 then observed an oncoming vehicle and braked and steered to the right. Vehicle #1 continued off the right side of the roadway, up a small embankment striking it with its front. Vehicle #1 then rolled over to the left two Â¼ turns and came to rest on its roof facing west. Vehicle #1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.
The Honda Civic (Vehicle #1) was driven by a belted 16 year old female who sustained an abrasion on her hand, but refused any medical transport. It should be noted that she had only had her license for 6 months. She was familiar and comfortable with the vehicle. She was not fatigued and not taking any type of medications. She was wearing contacts to correct a myopic condition.
