This three-vehicle crash occurred on a median-divided, straight trafficway near an intersection. The westbound lanes have an uphill grade and consist of three through lanes and a left turn lane. At the time of the crash, traffic was directed into the right through lane only by flares on the roadway, due to a previous crash in the intersection. The speed limit on this roadway is 56 kmph (35 mph). It was dark at the time of the crash with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1996 Mercury Cougar, was westbound in lane one. Vehicle two (V2), a 2001 Ford Taurus, was westbound in lane one in front of V1 and vehicle three (V3), a 1999 Volkswagen Passat, was in front of V2. The driver of V3, an off-duty emergency medical technician, stopped and got out of his vehicle to assist with the previous crash. The driver of V2 decelerated to avoid V3. The front of V1 struck the back of V2. V2 moved forward and the front struck the back of V3. V2 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 56 kmph for V1 and 56 kmph for V2.
The driver of V1, a seventeen-year-old male, was traveling with three teenage passengers from a store to his home. He was healthy, well rested and comfortable with his vehicle and passengers. He rarely drives this route. This driver stated that he was looking to the left at the previous crash and did not realize that V2 was slowing for traffic.
The driver of V2, a seventy-seven-year-old male, was traveling with his sister from a store to a pharmacy. He was well rested and comfortable with his vehicle and passenger. This driver has diabetes, high blood pressure and asthma and takes the medications Furosemide, Lisinopril, Glipizide, Digoxin, Diltiazem, Allopurinol, Protonix, Albuterol and Plavix.
V3 was not occupied at the time of the crash.
