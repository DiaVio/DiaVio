V1, a mid 90's Ford Taurus, was traveling southbound on a five-lane, two way, icy, asphalt roadway in dark-lighted conditions. V1 was approaching a 4-way intersection intending to go straight. V2, a 1997 Dodge Caravan, was traveling eastbound on a similar roadway approaching the same intersection. The posted speed limit on both roadways was 56 kmph (35 mph). V2 was also intending to go straight through the intersection. V1 failed to stop for the traffic control device and the front of V1 contacted the left side of V2. V2 came to final rest in the roadway. V1 did not stop.
The driver of V1 was an unidentified male in his mid-40's. It is unknown if he was injured or if he had any health-related problems. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V2 was a 31-year old male who reported that he had been traveling between 67-80kmph (41-50mph) prior to the crash. He had a history of depression and was taking medication for it. He was rested and traveling to the passenger's house. He was wearing his prescribed lenses that corrected a myopic (near-sighted) condition. He was uninjured and refused treatment.
