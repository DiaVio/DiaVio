This two-vehicle crash occurred on a jersey barrier-divided trafficway. The eastbound lanes are curved to the right in the precrash area. These lanes are level and consist of four through lanes with shoulders on both sides. The speed limit for this roadway is 89 kmph (55 mph). It was raining at the time of this crash that occurred during daylight hours.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1997 International school bus, was eastbound in lane one. Vehicle two (V2), a 2006 Chevrolet Malibu, was eastbound in lane four. V1 traveled across the lanes and the front struck the right side of V2. V1 continued to the northeast and the front struck the jersey barrier. V2 moved to the left, the driver steered right and the left side of V2 struck the jersey barrier. V1 continued on and the left side struck the jersey barrier. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, a thirty-eight-year-old female, was traveling with two male passengers. This driver was not interviewed. A motor carrier safety inspector determined that V1 had a defective steering system prior to the crash.
The driver of V2, a forty-nine-year-old male, was on his way from a cousin's house to a friend's house. He was healthy, well rested and familiar with his vehicle He travels this route daily. This driver stated that he did not see V1 prior to the crash.
