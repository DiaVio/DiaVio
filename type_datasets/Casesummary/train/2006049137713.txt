The single vehicle crash occurred on a three-lane roadway divided by a concrete barrier. This was a straight, uphill, concrete roadway with a posted speed of 97 kph (60 mph). Just beyond where the critical pre-crash event began, an on-ramp comes into the highway. This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one, a 1993 Ford Econoline van was eastbound in the second lane. The driver of vehicle one heard a "pop" and lost directional control of the vehicle. Vehicle one departed the original travel lane on the right side, crossed through the shoulder. Vehicle one began to roll to the right after contacting the ground in the grass separating the highway from the service road. Vehicle one rolled onto it's top and slid across the service road, coming to rest in a private business entrance facing northeast. Vehicle one was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.
The driver of vehicle one, a forty-nine year old female was heading home from a friends house at the time of the crash. This driver had been driving for about twenty minutes at the time of the crash. This driver indicated that she was just driving along and then heard a "pop". This driver was unable to maintain control after the tread separation. The left rear tire experienced a tread separation. The remaining tires had sufficient tire inflation and adequate tread depth.
