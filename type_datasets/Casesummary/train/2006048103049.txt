This two-vehicle crash occurred on a controlled-access roadway. This roadway is divided by a median, straight, downhill and dry. There are two westbound lanes with paved shoulders on both sides. The posted speed limit for this roadway is 113 kmph (70 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2001 Ford Ranger pickup, was westbound in lane one. V2, a 2006 Buick Lucerne, was parked on the right shoulder due to a previous crash. V1 departed the roadway to the right and the front struck the left side of V2. V1 continued westbound and the front struck a guardrail off the right side of the roadway. The right front wheel of V1 broke off and caused undercarriage damage to V1. V1 then reentered the roadway rotating counterclockwise, crossed both lanes and departed the roadway to the left. V1 came to rest facing west in the median. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 113 kmph for V1 and 113 kmph for V2.
The driver of V1, a seventy-seven-year-old male, was not interviewed. The surrogate interviewed stated that this driver indicated that he had "looked down for a second and hit the other car."
