This single-vehicle crash occurred in the northbound lanes of a divided four-lane roadway. There were two through lanes in each direction. The roadway is divided by a curbed depressed sod median. The roadway is curved to the west. It was on a dark, icy, bituminous, downhill roadway with a posted speed of 64 kmph (40 mph). It was early evening after rush hour and traffic flow was moderate.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2002 Chevrolet Blazer with two occupants, was traveling in lane two negotiating a left curve. The driver allowed the vehicle to travel onto the sod median. The vehicle was tracking on the median until the driver steered right and the vehicle initiated a clockwise rotation. As the vehicle re-entered the northbound lanes the vehicle rolled right for two quarter turns. V1 came to rest on its top facing west in lane one and curbed roadside. V1 was towed from the scene due to extreme damage to the top. Both occupants were transported. Police Reported Travel Speed is 64 kmph for V1.
A 59 year-old female drove V1, the Chevrolet Blazer. She was leaving a fast food restaurant and going home when the crash occurred. She is near-sighted and was wearing her glasses. She takes multi-vitamins on a regular basis and stated she was well rested. She also stated she is from a warmer climate. She has lived in this area for two years and doesn't like the vehicle that she was driving. She stated she is going to get a bigger vehicle next time, as she wanted one when she got this one.
