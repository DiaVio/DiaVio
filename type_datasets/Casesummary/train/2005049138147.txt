This three-vehicle collision occurred during early evening daylight hours. This occurred in an interchange area on an urban four lane lane, ramp section of the freeway that was under construction, which had two lanes in each direction separated by a positive jersey barrier.. The northbound pre-crash roadway was dry, traveled, concrete that curved very slightly to the right and was relatively level. The posted speed limit was 89 kmph (55 mph). There was heavy rush hour traffic at the time of the collision. There were no adverse weather conditions.
Vehicle one (V1) is a 1999 Freightliner tractor with an attached trailer traveling northbound in the second lane driven by a 33 year-old male in good health. Vehicle two (V2) is a 2000 Toyota 4 Runner traveling northbound in the second lane driven by a 35 year-old female in good health. She was not in a hurry drives this same route daily. Vehicle three (V3) was a 1995 Chevrolet Silverado pick-up traveling northbound in the second lane driven by a 31 year-old male in good health. He also drives this route daily. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
V3 slowed to a stop because of forward traffic congestion. V2 slowed to a stop behind V3. V1 was unable to stop and it's front impacted the rear of V2. V2 was forced forward and it's front impacted the rear of V 3. All of the vehicles came to rest in the second lane, facing north. V2 was towed from the scene. V1 and V3 were driven from the scene.
