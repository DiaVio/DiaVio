Case Summary 2006-11-032
The crash occurred in a four legged urban intersection controlled by an on-colors traffic signal with a left turn green arrow following the green cycle. The roads are two-way, five lanes, straight, <2% downhill slope, and concrete. The posted speed limit on the east leg is 72 kmh (40mph), and the posted speed limit on the west leg is 64 kmh (40 mph). Conditions at the time of the weekday nighttime crash were dark and dry. Interviews were obtained from both drivers on-scene, and the vehicles were inspected the following day. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
V1, a 2002 Dodge Durango, driven by a 31 year-old male on his way to the airport to pick up his relatives, was traveling eastbound in the left turn lane. V1 stopped on the green signal to wait for traffic to clear. The driver stated that the light turned yellow, he thought it was clear, so he proceeded into the intersection and began to turn left. The driver stated that he looked but did not see the other car and had no time for avoidance maneuvers. The right front of v1 struck the front of v2.
V2, a 1995 Ford Probe driven by a 38 year-old female running an errand, was traveling westbound in lane two at about 45 mph. V2 proceeded into the intersection on the green light. V1 turned left in front of v2, and the driver of v2 had no time for avoidance maneuvers.
Both vehicles were towed from the scene due to damage, with the front airbags in v2 deployed. The fully restrained driver of v1 denied injuries. The fully restrained driver of v2 was treated at the scene. The adult female front right passenger of v2 was transported to a local trauma center due to injuries.
