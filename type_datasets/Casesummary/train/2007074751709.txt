This single-vehicle crash occurred on straight, undivided, gravel roadway. This roadway has a four percent downhill grade in the eastbound direction. The speed limit for this roadway is 80 kmph (50 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2002 Suzuki Grand Vitara, was eastbound in lane one. A non-contact vehicle was westbound, driving in the middle of the roadway. The driver of V1 steered to the right and off the right side of the road. V1 then returned to the roadway and continued (out of control) in the eastbound direction. V1 left the right side of the roadway and rolled over to the left two quarter-turns. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.
The driver of V1, a forty-eight-year-old male, was on his way from his home to work. He stated that he was in fair health. He rarely travels this route. He feels comfortable with his vehicle although he has owned it for only one month. This driver has high blood pressure, acid reflux and sleep apnea and takes the medications Hydrochlorothiazide, Toprol - XL, Prevacid and aspirin. He also uses CPAP treatment for his sleep apnea. This driver had slept for eight hours and then was awake for six hours prior to this trip. He stated that he awoke feeling drowsy and felt fatigued when he started this trip. He further stated that he feels stressed by his job due to working extended shifts. This driver also stated that he was in a hurry to get to work and then get back home again.
