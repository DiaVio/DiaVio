This single-vehicle crash occurred on a six lane divided roadway. In the northbound direction the roadway consisted of three through lanes. This roadway is straight and uphill with a posted speed limit of 105 kmph (65 mph). The crash occurred during the evening daylight hours with poor road conditions due to rain and ice. The driver lost control on a bridge that had patches of ice.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1998 Toyota 4-Runner with two occupants, was northbound in lane two. V1 changed to the number one lane to pass a non-contact vehicle on the right side and was re-entering lane two when the driver lost control and the vehicle started to rotate counterclockwise. V1 continued to rotate counterclockwise while departing the roadway on the left side. The front of V1 contacted the cement barrier on the bridge before the right side contacted the barrier. The vehicle continued to rotate counterclockwise as the vehicle rolled over two quarter times before coming to rest between the median and lane three on its top facing northeast. V1 was towed due to damage. The two occupants were transported. Police Reported Travel Speed is 72 kmph for V1.
The driver of V1, a sixteen-year-old-male, was driving from home to a ski resort when the crash occurred. He stated being rested, drives this route weekly and is very comfortable with this vehicle. This driver is in good health and does not take any medications. He has had three months of driving experience and had the basic driver education course. He estimated he had driven 30 miles (48 kilometers) and had been driving for approximately an hour. The driver lost control of the vehicle on the bridge as he was returning to lane two after passing a non-contact vehicle on the right.
