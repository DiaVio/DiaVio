This is a three-vehicle rear end crash occurred just past noon on a straight, two-way, two-lane, dry bituminous asphalt roadway. There is one westbound and one eastbound lane where the crash occurred. The crash occurred near an entrance to an unimproved roadway. The speed limit on the roadway where the crash occurred is posted 72 kph (45 mph). The weather at the time was slightly windy form the south and there was dust blowing across the roadway that was not a factor in this crash. There were no sight line restrictions and no obstructions noted.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1994 Jeep Wrangler, driven by a 25 year-old male with one passenger was traveling eastbound on the roadway and began to slow down in an attempt to make a right turn onto an unimproved dirt roadway. V-1 was struck from the rear by the front of Vehicle two (V-2) a 2003 Chevrolet Trail Blazer driven by a 79 year-old female. The impact caused V-1 to rotate clockwise. The vehicle then traveled off the right side of the roadway and began to travel in a soft dirt area in a southeasterly direction. The driver of V1 was ejected from the vehicle when it exited the roadway. The vehicle continued to travel in a southeasterly direction on the soft dirt until it came to rest facing in a southeasterly direction. V2 was struck in the rear by the front of Vehicle three a 1994 Suzuki Tracker driven by a 30 year-old female with three passenger. After impact V2 was moved to the shoulder along with V3. V2 was towed from the scene due to damage, V1& V3 were driven from the scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is 8 kmph for V1 and 8 kmph for V2.
The pre event movement for V-3 was coded "going straight". This vehicle was not involved in the first harmful event and the critical reason was not coded to this vehicle.
