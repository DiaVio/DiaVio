This two-vehicle crash occurred in a three-legged intersection. The east/west roadway is straight and level and consists of two lanes in each direction with a center left-turn lane. The speed limit for this roadway is 64 kmph (40 mph). The north/south roadway is on the south side of the east/west roadway. This roadway is straight, level and consists of one lane in each direction. The northbound traffic is controlled by a stop sign at the intersection. The speed limit for this roadway is 40 kmph (25 mph). It was dark with streetlights on at the time of the crash with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1997 Chevrolet Tahoe, was eastbound in lane two. Vehicle two (V2), a 1996 Ford Probe, was stopped northbound in lane one. V2 entered the intersection, turning left, and the left side was struck by the front of V1. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, a nineteen-year-old male, was on his way home from work. He was healthy, well rested and familiar with his vehicle. He travels this route daily. This driver stated that he noticed V2 as it entered the intersection and he steered to the left in an attempt to avoid the crash.
The driver of V2, a twenty-one-year-old male, had just left his home and was on his way to a friend's house. He was well rested, healthy and familiar with his vehicle. He travels this route daily. This driver stated that he stopped and waited for westbound traffic to clear. He was looking straight ahead when he entered the intersection and did not see V1 prior to the crash.
