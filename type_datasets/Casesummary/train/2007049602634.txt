The single vehicle crash occurred on a weekday during early morning hours. The location was a two way, two lane, undivided, straight, asphalt roadway. The weather was clear and the roads dry. The temperature was below freezing. The posted speed limit for the roadway was 48 kmph (30 mph). It was dark with no street lighting.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1990 Toyota 4-Runner, with one occupant, was traveling south in lane one. V1 departed the roadway to the right and the front struck a large tree. The vehicle came to rest at the tree. The vehicle was towed from the scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is No PAR obtained (created) for V1.
The driver was a 46-year old male. The driver does not take any medication and is in good health. He drives the vehicle on this roadway on a daily basis. The driver said he fell asleep at the wheel. He was was on his way home after he worked his usual eight-hour night shift. He said he usually sleeps for 8 hours during the daytime but the day before the crash, he only slept for only 3 hours.
