This two-vehicle crash occurred in a four-legged, traffic signal-controlled intersection. Both roadways are straight, level and undivided and were wet at the time of the crash. The north/south roadway consists of one lane in each direction and the east/west roadway consists of two lanes in each direction. The speed limit for these roadways is 40 kmph (25 mph). It was dawn with streetlights on at the time of the crash with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2005 GMC Sierra C1500 pickup, was westbound in lane two. Vehicle two (V2), a 1997 Chevrolet S10 pickup, was southbound in lane one. In the intersection, the front of V1 struck the left side of V2. V2 rotated counterclockwise and traveled to the southwest corner of the intersection. V2 rolled over to the right one quarter-turn and then back up on its wheels. V2 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, a thirty-five-year-old male, was on his way to work from his home. He was in good healthy, well rested and familiar with his vehicle. He travels this route daily. He takes an unspecified medication daily for his bipolar disorder. This driver stated that he was traveling at 64 kmph (40 mph) when he approached the intersection. He thought the signal was green and when he saw V2 at the last minute in front of him, he steered to the left and applied the brakes in an attempt to avoid the crash.
The driver of V2, a seventy-three-year-old male, was not interviewed. No further information is available for this driver.
