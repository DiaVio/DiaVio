The crash occurred in the eastbound lanes of a two-way roadway with one lane in each direction. The roadway was wet, asphalt, straight and level. It was daylight and raining and the speed limit was 48kmph (30mph). The crash occurred in the morning hours of a weekend, and the traffic flow was normal.
V1, a 1999 Lexus ES300 four door with three occupants, was traveling eastbound in lane one. V2, a 1992 Honda Accord was traveling eastbound in the parking lane and attempting to make a U-turn to go westbound, when the front of V1 contacted the left side of V2. Then V2 impacted a non-breakaway pole located seven meters north/east of the point of impact. V1 came to a final rest shortly after impact, and V2 came to a final rest at the pole. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
V1, the Lexus ES300 was driven by a 34 year-old female who reported minor injuries but refused treatment. The 34 year-old female occupant also reported minor injuries, but refused treatment. The 32 year-old male occupant reported no injuries. The driver advised that she was traveling straight in lane one when V2 was making a U-turn from the parking lane. She tried to stop and steer away to the left, but there was no place to go except hitting the left side of his vehicle.
V2, the Honda Accord was driven by a 66 year-old male who reported minor injuries and was transported to a medical facility. The 63 year-old female occupant also reported minor injuries and was also transported to a medical facility. The driver declined to participate in the study.
