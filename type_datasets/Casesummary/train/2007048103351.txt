This two-vehicle crash occurred in a four-legged, traffic signal-controlled intersection. The east/west trafficway is straight and divided by a curbed median. The westbound approach lanes are level and consist of three through lanes and a left turn lane. The eastbound approach lanes have an uphill grade and consist of three through lanes and a left turn lane. The speed limit for this roadway is 72 kmph (45 mph). It was dark with streetlights on at the time of the crash with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1988 Chevrolet S10 Blazer, was westbound in lane four. Vehicle two (V2), a 1991 Mercury Grand Marquis, was eastbound in lane two. V1 entered the intersection turning left and the front struck the front of V2. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Unknown for V1 and Unknown for V2.
The driver of V1, a twenty-four-year-old male, was not interviewed; no further information is available for this driver.
The driver of V2, a twenty-three-year-old male, was on his way from his home to the library. He was healthy, well rested and familiar with his vehicle. He travels this route daily. This driver stated that he thought that V1 would yield the right-of-way.
