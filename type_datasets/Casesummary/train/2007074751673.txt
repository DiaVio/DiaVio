This two-vehicle crash occurred in a four-legged, traffic signal-controlled intersection. Both trafficways are straight and divided by curbed medians. The westbound and southbound approach roadways are straight, level and consist of two through lanes, two left turn lanes and a right turn lane. The speed limit for these roadways is 72 kmph (45 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2003 Ford Windstar, was westbound in lane three. Vehicle two (V2), a 1997 Chevrolet S10 pickup, was southbound in lane two. Both vehicles were traveling in straight paths. In the intersection, the front of V2 struck the right side of V1. V1 then rolled over to the left two quarter-turns. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, a forty-three-year-old male, was traveling with three of his young children. They had been at a store and were on their way to pick up another daughter from an overnight trip. This driver was healthy, well rested and comfortable with his vehicle and passengers. He travels this route daily. This driver stated that he did not see V2 until the last second and did not make any avoidance maneuvers.
The driver of V2, a forty-seven-year-old male, was traveling from one bank to another. He was well rested, healthy and familiar with his vehicle and this roadway. He has allergies and stated that he had taken ibuprofen within the past twenty-four hours. This driver stated that he saw V1 "one second" before the crash and applied the brakes, skidding longitudinally. This driver's medical record indicates a positive test for opiates.
