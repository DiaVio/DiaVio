The crash occurred in the northbound lanes of an undivided roadway with three lanes of travel in each direction. This was a straight, level, bituminous roadway with a posted speed limit of 48kmph (30mph). It was the early evening hours of a weekday and the road was dry and the sky was clear. Traffic flow was congested with stop-and-go activity.
V1, a 1999 Hyundai Accent four door sedan with two occupants, was traveling northbound in lane three. V2, a 2007 Pontiac G6 four door sedan with one occupant, was traveling northbound in lane three in front of V1. V3, a 1999 Toyota Camry four door sedan with one occupant, was traveling northbound in lane three in front of V2. The front of V1 impacted the back of V2, pushing the front of V2 into the back of V3. All vehicles came to final rest near impact. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
V1, the Hyundai Accent, was driven by a 25 year-old female who reported no injuries. A 23 year-old male occupant also reported no injuries. The driver stated to the police that while traveling northbound on the roadway, she struck V2 in the rear.
V2, the Pontiac G6, was driven by a 31 year-old male who reported no injuries. He advised that he was driving in traffic at a slow pace when all of a sudden the car behind slammed into him, and pushed him into the back of V3.
V3, the Toyota Camry, was driven by a 39 year-old male who reported no injuries. He advised that he was commuting from work to home, and while moving very slow in traffic he was struck by V2 very lightly.
