This two-vehicle collision occurred during early morning hours on a four-way traffic device signal controlled intersection. No adverse weather conditions at the time of collision, but sun glare was present due to rising morning sun. The posted speed for the east/west roadway is 56 kmph (35 mph). The posted speed for north/south roadway was 40 kmph (25 mph).
Vehicle two (V2), a 1994 Mercury Tracer sedan with the 36-year-old male driver and two occupants, was traveling east in lane 1 of a bituminous, seven lane, divided, and +2% slope roadway. As V2 approached the intersection the traffic signal turned to yellow/red and the driver entered the intersection on a red light. Vehicle one (V1), a 1996 Plymouth Voyager minivan occupied only by the 45-year-old female driver was traveling north in lane 1 of a bituminous, two-way, two-lane, and undivided roadway. V1 driver was stopped at the traffic signal and proceeded forward/north upon traffic signal turning green. V1 driver never looked to the sides before proceeding into intersection. The driver of V2 applied the brakes and steered left to avoid the crash. The front of V2 contacted the left front of V1. Both vehicles came to rest in the intersection. The driver of V1 and the right rear passenger in V2 were both slightly injured; neither was transported. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
