The crash occurred on the west (right) roadside of an undeveloped, suburban cul-de-sac. The road had a 1% uphill grade and a posted speed limit of 40 kmph (25 mph). Conditions were daylight and dry with clear skies. The setting was mid-afternoon hours on a weekday.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1992 Chevrolet 1500 pickup with one occupant, was stopped on the west shoulder facing south. V1 entered the roadway and then accelerated rapidly. The driver lost control and locked-up the brakes. V1 traveled off the right side of the road, struck an embankment with its undercarriage, struck a small tree with its front, and then struck a medium tree with its front. V1 came to rest against the medium-sized tree. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 97 kmph for V1.
The driver of V1, an unlicensed 14-year-old male, was transported to a local trauma center with possible injuries. He stated that he was working for his grandfather over a spring break from school. He attempted to move the pickup down the road. The driver doesn't remember the specifics of the crash before hitting the second tree. He states that after the crash, he was unconscious for a brief period.
