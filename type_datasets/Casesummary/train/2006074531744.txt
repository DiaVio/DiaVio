This single-vehicle crash occurred on a two-lane, undivided, straight roadway with a 5% uphill grade. The speed limit on this roadway is 97 kmph (60 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2002 Dodge Dakota pickup, was westbound in lane one. V1 traveled over the center lane line. The driver of V1 steered right and V1 rotated clockwise, traveling off the right side of the roadway. V1 traveled across the grass and rolled over to the left, one quarter-turn. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.
The driver of V1, a fifteen-year-old male, was traveling from school to his home. He was healthy and comfortable with his vehicle, even though he had been driving for only two months. He travels this route daily. This driver stated that he was fatigued (he had slept seven hours the previous night and had been awake for eight-and-a-half hours) and fell asleep prior to the vehicle crossing the center lane line. When he realized that he was out of his lane, he abruptly steered to the right.
