V1, a 1999 Chevrolet Lumina, was traveling northbound on a five-lane, two-way, dry, asphalt roadway with a level slope in daylight conditions. V1 was in lane three and was attempting to make a left-hand turn. V2, a 2003 Pontiac Vibe, was traveling southbound on the same roadway. V2 was in lane two intending to go straight. The traffic signal turned yellow and the driver of V1 assumed that V2 was going to stop and the driver of V2 assumed that V1 would stop. The front of V2 contacted the right side of V1 and came to final rest in the intersection.
The driver of V1 was a 53-year old male who reported that he had been traveling between 2 and 17kmph (1-10 mph) prior to the crash. He had a history of muscular disease but does not take medication for the condition. He was rested and traveling to a family member's home. He was wearing his prescribed lenses that corrected a Myopic (near-sighted) condition. He did not sustain any injuries in the crash. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V2 was a 32-year old female who reported that she had been traveling 50-64kmph (31-40mph) prior to the crash. She had no health related problems and had taken no medication prior to the crash. She was rested and traveling home. She does not wear corrective lenses and sustained minor in juries in the crash.
