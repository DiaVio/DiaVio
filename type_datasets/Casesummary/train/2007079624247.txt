This two vehicle daytime collision occurred just past noon on a five lane, undivided, dry, level, roadway made of asphalt. The east/west roadway is designated 56 kmph (35 mph).
Vehicle one (V1) is a 1993 Ford Probe 2-door hatchback, driven by a forty four (44) year old female. V1 was exiting a parking space to traveling east, from the south curb to the number one lane. Vehicle two (V2) is a 2002 Chevrolet Trailblazer 4-door compact sport utility, driven by a thirty (30) year old female who was traveling east in the number one lane. V2's front plane impacted V1's left plane as V1 attempted to enter the roadway. V1 continued southeast to impact a guy wire, a block wall, and a small brick planter with its front plane. Simultaneously, V1 contacted a power pole with its left plane. V2 continued northeast and came to rest facing east in the number two lane. V1 was towed with moderate damage. V2 was driven with moderate damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
