This three-vehicle crash occurred on a divided, straight, roadway. The westbound lanes have a slight downgrade and consist of three through lanes and a left turn lane. There is a driveway on the right side of the westbound lanes. The speed limit on this roadway is 72 kmph (45 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1999 Cadillac Deville, was southbound in the driveway. Vehicle two (V2), a 2003 Ford E-series ambulance was westbound in lane two, with emergency lights activated. Vehicle three (V3), a 1989 Toyota Camry, was westbound in lane one. A non-contact vehicle stopped in lane one. The driver of V3 saw the ambulance and turned right into a parking lot. V1 entered the westbound lanes, intending to turn right into lane four. The front of V1 struck the right side of V2. V2 continued westbound, out of control. V2 entered the parking lot and the front struck the back of V3. Police Reported Travel Speed is 8 kmph for V1 and 8 kmph for V2.
The driver of V1, a twenty-one-year-old male, was traveling from his home to a store. He was healthy, well rested and familiar with his vehicle. He drives this route daily. This driver stated that he thought that the non-contact vehicle was stopped to allow him to enter the roadway. He also stated that he did not see the ambulance.
The driver of V2, a twenty-seven-year-old male, was traveling with a male passenger, from their office to an emergency call. This driver was healthy, well rested and very familiar with his vehicle. He drives on this roadway daily.
The driver of V3, a sixty-two-year-old female, was traveling from a store to her home. She was well rested, healthy, familiar with her vehicle and drives this route daily.
