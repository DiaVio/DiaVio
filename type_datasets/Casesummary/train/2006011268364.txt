V1, a 2000 Volkswagen Jetta driven by a 16-year-old female, was eastbound on in lane 1 of a wet, 2 lane, 2 way, straight, level, gravel/slag roadway. At the time of the crash the un-posted speed limit for the road was 89kmph/55mph, it was dusk and it was raining. There were 3-16 year old passengers in v1. As v1 was proceeding eastbound, one of the occupants started to make a phone call. The driver reached down to adjust the radio and then v1 struck a large puddle covering most of the right lane pulling v1 to the right. The driver steered left, v1 went into a counterclockwise yaw, exited the roadway to the left and struck a tree >10cm with its right side. V1 came to rest facing northeast against the tree. The 3 passengers were injured and transported. V1 was towed.
A complete driver interview and vehicle inspection was obtained for v1. The Pre-event movement of v1 was going straight, the Critical pre-crash event was this vehicle traveling off the edge of the road on the left side and the Critical reason for the critical pre-crash event was the performance error of poor directional control. Some factors leading to the crash included the wet condition of, and standing water in, the roadway, the driver's inexperience, the driver's inadequate/incorrect evasive actions, the driver's failure to look far enough ahead, her unfamiliarity with the road and her taking her eyes off the road to adjust the radio. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.
