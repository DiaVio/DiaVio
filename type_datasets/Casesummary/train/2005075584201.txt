This single vehicle crash occurred early evening hours on a dark, five-lane urban roadway with double yellow lines for controls. Westbound lanes consisted of two through lanes and one left turn lane. The road was a dry, bituminous "S" curve. The speed limit is 72 kmph (45 mph).
Vehicle one (V1), a 1992 Eagle Talon coupe driven by a 20 year-old male was traveling westbound on an "S" curve in lane one. V1 was coming out of a left curve onto a right curve and entered lane number two. V1 then entered the left turn lane started to loose control rotating clockwise into lane number four and lane number five lanes, the eastbound lanes. V1 rotated three quarter times and impacted a curb with its right front wheel. The wheel broke off as the vehicle rode up the curb continued to rotate another quarter turn and impacted a telephone box with its front continued forward still rotating clockwise and impacted a wooden fence with its left front bumper. The vehicle continued forward and impacted a tree with it front. After the last impact, the vehicle bounced backwards and came to rest near the tree facing west. V1 was towed from the scene due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 137 kmph for V1.
The criticial event for V1 was coded - this vehicle traveling off the edge of the road on the left side. Critical reason for the critical event coded driver related factor "too fast for curve/turn".
Additional factor, speeding.
