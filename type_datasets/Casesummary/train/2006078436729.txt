This two-vehicle crash occurred on a divided roadway. There are two westbound lanes and two eastbound lanes that are divided by depressed dirt/vegetation median. This bituminous roadway was straight, level and dry with a posted speed limit of 121 kmph (75 mph). It was mid-afternoon and there were no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1994 GMC Jimmy, was westbound in the number one lane. V2, a 2003 Cadillac Deville, was eastbound in the number one lane. The tread separated from the right rear tire of V1 and the driver steered left, causing the vehicle to rotate counterclockwise. V1 departed the left side of the roadway into the median. V1 rolled to the right eight quarter-turns across the median. V1 then entered the eastbound lanes. The driver of V2 steered right to avoid V1, but the right side of V1 struck the left side of V2. Debris from this collision struck the right A-pillar area of V2. V1 came to final rest on its wheels facing east on the south shoulder of the eastbound lanes. V2 came to a controlled stop on the south shoulder east of V1. Police Reported Travel Speed is Unknown for V1 and Unknown for V2.
V1 driver, a thirty-year-old male, was traveling with his twelve-year-old son. This driver later died due to his injuries in this crash. No information was obtained on this driver. Medical laboratory report results were negative for alcohol & all other illegal drugs screened. It is unknown if the driver was taking any medications.
V2 driver, a seventy-two-year-old male, was traveling with his seventy-year-old wife. They were traveling from their home to their daughter's home in another state. This driver was well rested and comfortable with his vehicle. He rarely drives this route, but was comfortable with the road and the conditions at the time of the crash.
