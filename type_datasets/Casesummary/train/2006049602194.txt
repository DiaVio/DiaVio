This single vehicle crash occurred late afternoon during daylight hours at an intersection with tri-color traffic lights. The road is a 5-lane divided asphalt roadway from west to east. It is a two-lane asphalt roadway from south to north. The speed limit is 48 kph (30 mph) for both roadways. The road surface was dry and there were no detrimental atmospheric conditions at the time of the crash.
Vehicle one (V 1) a 1996 Infinity sedan driven by a 33 year-old male was traveling east in the fourth lane of traffic, a left turn lane only, attempting to turn left into the northbound lanes of traffic. V 1 made the left turn and partially departed the roadway to the right. V 1 traveled north with its right side wheels on the pedestrian walkway, located east of the northbound roadway. The front of V 1 impacted a wooden utility pole located east of the northbound lanes. V # 1 slightly rotated clockwise around the pole where it came to rest. V 1 was towed from the scene. The driver was transported to a local medical facility. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.
The driver stated the right front tire blew up during the left turn. The researcher could not determine or located any pre-crash damage on the right front tire during the vehicle inspection. The right front wheel was pushed backward during the impact.
Associated factor - physical evidence at the scene and the extend of crush indicate the vehicle was traveling at a higher rate of speed then necessary for the left turn at this location.
