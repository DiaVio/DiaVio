This single-vehicle crash occurred in an interchange area of a jersey barrier-divided, straight trafficway. The southbound lanes have an uphill grade and consist of two through lanes and a right exit lane. The speed limit for this roadway is 89 kmph (55 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions. Traffic was congested at the time of the crash.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1992 Oldsmobile Ninety-Eight, was southbound in lane two. V1 changed lanes to the right twice and then traveled off the road into the gore area. The front of V1 struck a delineator post. V1 then rolled over to the left eight quarter-turns and was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.
The driver of V1, a twenty-seven-year-old female, was traveling with her two small children. She stated that she was angry with the children's father due to a recent argument. She also stated that she was chasing him southbound and followed him when it appeared that he would take the exit. He abruptly drove back onto the through lanes and the driver of V1 was unable to follow, at which time she traveled into the gore area. According to a witness, this driver was throwing soup and soda cans at the vehicle in front of her. This driver's medical record indicated a positive result for opiates.
