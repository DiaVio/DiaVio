This two-vehicle rear end crash occurred on a 5-lane undivided asphalt road. This road runs east to west and has a center turn lane. At the crash area there is a driveway entrance on the north side. The east/west roadway is straight and level with a posted speed limit of 64 kmph (40 mph). This crash occurred in the early morning hours at dawn with no street lighting.
Vehicle two (V2), a 2005 Dodge Neon driven by a twenty (20) year old male, was westbound going over the speed limit at a driver reported speed of 84 kmph (52 mph). V2 impacted the rear plane of Vehicle one (V1), a 1986 Chevrolet S-10 pickup, with is front plane. V1 was driven by a fifty nine (59) year old male and was decelerating to turn right off into a driveway when it was impacted on the rear plane. V2 under rode V1 and damage extended over the bumper and into the hood and radiator support. After the first impact, V1 spun counterclockwise and was pushed left across the #2 lane and came to rest in the center turn lane heading in a northeasterly direction. V2 came to rest along the right shoulder. Both V1 and V2 were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 64 kmph for V1 and 64 kmph for V2.
The driver of V2, stated that he was on his way to work, running late and was going a little over the speed limit. He stated that he saw V1 ahead and looked down to the floorboard for a CD and when he was looking up the airbags deployed. The driver indicated that he was in a hurry due to a work related delivery schedule.
The driver of V1 stated that he was on his way to work and was about to turn into the driveway when the crash occurred. He was not in use of the lap and shoulder belt and was slightly injured. EMS was dispatched to this crash, however, this driver refused treatment.
