This single-vehicle crash occurred on a divided, east/west roadway near an intersection and a north/south railroad track. There are four straight travel lanes for westbound traffic. The roadway was dry and of concrete construction. The westbound lanes had a 2.5% uphill grade. Overhead traffic control lights control traffic at the intersection. The posted speed limit is 56 kph (35 mph). Traffic was light at the time of the crash. This crash occurred during daylight hours with clear skies.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2003 Ford F-150, with two occupants, was westbound in the third travel lane. V1 departed the lane on the right side, crossed through lanes two and one and departed the roadway on the right side. The front of V1 struck a wooden road work sign, a 21 cm diameter pole and the guy wire attached to the pole. V1 came to rest between the pole and the guy wire facing northwest. The vehicle was towed from the scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.
The driver of V1 left the scene, was not identified and could not be interviewed.
