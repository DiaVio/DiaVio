This crash occurred in the southbound lanes of a divided roadway with a grass median. There were 2 sourthbound lanes that divided into five lanes at the signal. Two left turn lanes, two straight lanes and one right turn lane. The crash occurred just before the road divided on the two lane width. This road had a left curved, level, bituminous with a posted speed limit of 89 kmph (55 mph). It was early morning on a weekday and the roads were dry and the skies were a little cloudy. Traffic flow was minimal.
V1, a 2004 Chevy Malibu 4-door with one occupant, was traveling southbound in lane 1. V2 was a 1986 GMC Truck with two occupants, stopped in lane 1 directly in front of V1. V3 was a 1984 Ford L9000 dump truck and was stopped for a traffic light directly in front of V2. V1 was traveling at a constant rate of speed. V1 struck V2 in the rear, then V2 struck V3 in the rear. All vehicles came to a rest in lane 1. All drivers and occupants were transported due to injuries. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
V3 was not involved in the first harmful event. Therefore, no Critical Precrash Event or Critical Reason was coded to the driver.
