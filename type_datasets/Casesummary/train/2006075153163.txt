This single-vehicle crash occurred on a two-lane, bituminous residential roadway. The roadway curved left with a downhill slope and a posted speed limit of 32 kmph (20 mph). The road was dry and the sky was clear. This crash occurred in the early morning hours with minimal traffic flow.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2006 Chrysler Pacifica, was northwest bound in lane one. V1 was negotiating the left hand curve when it lost control and proceeded off the roadway. The driver applied the brakes and steered left but was unable to regain control. V1 contacted a mailbox with its front end, continued on to sideswipe a tree with its right side, and then contacted a dirt embankment with its undercarriage and front end before coming to rest on all four tires facing northwest. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 48 kmph for V1.
The driver of V1, a sixteen-year-old male, was on his way home from a friend's house. He was rested and drives this route daily. This was the first time he had driven this vehicle. The driver stated he was traveling 48 kmph (30 mph) and listening to his IPOD before the crash occurred. Alcohol was also a contributing factor in the crash. The driver stated that he was in good health.
