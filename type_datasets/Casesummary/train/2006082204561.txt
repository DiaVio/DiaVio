This two vehicle daytime collision occurred during the morning hours after rush hour. The collision occurred in an intersection with a stop sign for the east/west lanes only the roadway is straight with a downhill grade of 6%. The north/south roadway is straight with an uphill grade of 8%. Both roadways are dry paved with concrete. There is a posted speed limit of 40 kmph (25 mph) for both vehicles.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1993 Toyota Camry, driven by a 91 year-old male was traveling eastbound on the roadway in lane one intending to take a left at the intersection. Vehicle two (V2), a 1997 Nissan Quest Van, driven by a 46 year-old male with one passenger was traveling northbound in lane one. V1 was trying to stop but his pant leg had gotten stuck around the accelerator. V1 ran the stop sign and entered the intersection. The right plane of V1 impacted the front plane of V2 causing them to deflect north/east. V1 continued up the sidewalk, contacting two big yard rocks, and came to rest against a tree and another big yard rock. For an unknown reason, the driver of V1 then put his car in reverse, backed up and came to rest on the north/west corner. V2 came to rest in lane one in the eastbound lane. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
