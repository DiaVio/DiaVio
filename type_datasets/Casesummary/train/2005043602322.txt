Case Summary-2005-43-091
This single vehicle crash occurred on a 2-lane undivided rural roadway. The roadway runs north and south and has a curve to the left at the location of this crash. There is also an intersecting east/west roadway near this crash site. The conditions were clear, dry, daylight and it was a weekend afternoon. Police Reported Travel Speed is 40 kmph for V1.
V1, a 2002 Chevrolet Cavalier with one occupant, was traveling north on this 2-lane road negotiating a curve to the left. As V1 approached an intersecting roadway, a vehicle on that intersecting road failed to yield for a posted stop sign and turned in front of V1. V1's driver steered to the left to avoid striking the unknown vehicle and drove into a ditch on the west side of the intersection. The driver was transported to a hospital for minor injuries and the car was towed from the scene.
V1 was driven by a retired 72-year-old female that sleeps eight hours per night and takes several different prescriptions and over the counter medication. For this trip today, she was driving north to the market when an unknown vehicle turned in front of her, causing her to drive off the road and into a ditch. She could not recall if the vehicle was a truck, car, or van; nor could she remember the color of the vehicle. She said she was looking straight ahead when this unknown vehicle suddenly crossed in front of her.
This driver has a corrective lens and a blood/alcohol restriction on her driving license. She reported that she took a multi vitamin, Claritin and Sinex but her medical record showed that she was also taking Prilosec, ASA, Calcium, and Benacar.
