This two-vehicle crash occurred late in the afternoon on a four-lane, level, dry asphalt, median divided, straight roadway with no positive barrier. The speed limit for this roadway is 113 kmph (70 mph). There were construction zone warning signs present at the location of this daytime crash. There were no adverse atmospheric conditions present at the time of the crash.
Vehicle one (V 1), a 1994 Jeep Cherokee towing a vehicle, driven by a 20 year-old male was traveling southbound in lane two. Vehicle two (V 2), a 1993 Dodge Caravan towing another vehicle driven by a 45 year-old male was traveling southbound in lane two. V 2 was traveling directly behind V 1. A large truck attempted to change lanes from lane one to lane two. V 1 steered left off of the roadway into the median in an attempt to avoid striking the large truck. As V 1 made its avoidance maneuver, V 2 steered left into the median to avoid striking the large truck. V 1's trailer began to shift and V 1 lost control rotating 360 degrees and stopping in the median facing north. As V 1 came to a stop, the front of V 2 struck the front of V 1. At impact, V 2's cargo came unattached and V 2 came to rest in the median facing north. Neither of the drivers was injured in the crash. Both vehicle's were towed due to disabling damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 113 kmph for V1 and 113 kmph for V2.
