The crash occurred off the east side of a two-lane two-way dirt roadway. It had one northbound lane and one southbound lane. This was a straight, dirt roadway with a negative slope and a posted speed of 40 kmph (25 mph). It was evening on a weekday and the road was dry and the sky was clear. Traffic was sparse.
V1, a 2000 Jeep Cherokee compact utility vehicle with two passengers was traveling northbound in lane one. The driver of V1 stated that he was driving the passenger home when the passenger asked if he could take him to the store. On the way to the store the passenger decided they did not need to go to the store and told the driver to turn around. The driver of V1 stated that he turned around and stopped at a stop sign. He stated that he started to accelerate and lost control. A witness that was walking down the road stated that V1 was driving aggressively by swerving and speeding up and down the road at about 72 kmph (45 mph). He stated that V1 did not stop for the stop sign. V1 lost control and contacted its front to a fence and then rolled left one-quarter turn. V1 came to final rest on its left off the east side of the road. Police Reported Travel Speed is No PAR obtained (created) for V1.
The driver of V1 was an unlicensed 14-year old male. He reported no injuries and refused treatment. He stated that he had left his house and was driving the passenger home with his mother's vehicle. He stated that he was traveling between 18-32 kmph (11-20 mph) when he lost control. He tried to apply the brakes but could not correct the vehicle.
