This two-vehicle crash occurred on a straight, level roadway. This roadway consists of two lanes in each direction and a center left turn lane. The speed limit on this roadway is 64 kmph (40 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1997 Chrysler LHS, was westbound in lane one. V1 traveled off the right side of the roadway and the front and then the right side struck a guardrail. V1 continued westbound, crossing the roadway. The front of V1 struck the jersey barrier on the left (south) side of the road. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.
The driver of V1, a seventy-two-year-old male, was traveling from his home to get an oil change for his vehicle. He was well rested, in fairly good health and familiar with his vehicle. He has arthritis but takes not medication regularly. This driver travels this route daily. This driver stated that he reached over to put an object in the glove compartment and then attempted to close the door. He leaned to the right again to again attempt to close the glove compartment door and traveled off the road.
