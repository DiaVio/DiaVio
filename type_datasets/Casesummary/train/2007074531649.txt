This two-vehicle crash occurred in a four-legged, traffic signal-controlled intersection. The east/west roadway is undivided, straight and level. Both approaches consist of a left turn lane and a through/right turn lane. The speed limit for the eastbound approach is 40 kmph (25 mph) and the speed limit for the westbound approach is 64 kmph (40 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1994 Toyota Camry, was eastbound in lane one. Vehicle two (V2), a 1997 Chevrolet Cavalier, was westbound in lane two. V2 turned left in the intersection and the front struck the front of V1. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, a forty-seven-year-old male, was on his way to work. He was healthy, well rested and familiar with his vehicle. He travels this route daily although he was on vacation the previous week. He has high blood pressure and takes the medications Micardis and Divan. This driver stated that he saw V2 but assumed that it would stop to allow him to pass through the intersection.
The driver of V2, a twenty-three-year-old female, was on her way to work. She was healthy, well rested and familiar with her vehicle. She travels this route daily. This driver stated that she was watching the car in front of her turning left and did not see V1 prior to the crash. She also stated that she was not feeling rushed, but was scheduled to be at work within a few minutes of the time of the crash.
