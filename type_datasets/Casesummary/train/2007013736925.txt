This crash occurred in the first lane of a five-lane undivided, level, straight asphalt roadway with a posted speed limit of 56 kmph (35 mph) and an advisory speed of 40 kmph (25 mph). The crash occurred during the afternoon hours and there were no adverse conditions present at the time of the crash.
V1, a 2005 Chrysler PT Cruiser, was traveling westbound in the first lane. V2, a 1998 Oldsmobile Bravada utility vehicle, was also traveling westbound in the first lane, in front of V1. V2 was preparing to make a right turn into a restaurant parking lot. The front right of V1 struck the left rear of V2. V2 spun approximately 180 degrees clockwise and came to rest in the entranceway of the restaurant, facing east. V1 drove to the next parking lot entrance where it was parked for final rest. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, a 60-year-old female, stated she was on her way to the restaurant when she saw V2 slam on his brakes to turn into the restaurant. She stated V2 did not have its blinker on. She stated that she braked a little and swerved left to avoid V2 but struck V2 with the right front of her vehicle. When questioned, the driver of V1 stated that immediately before she saw V2 brake, she glanced ahead and to the right at a construction site.
The driver of V2, a 24-year-old male, stated he was going to the restaurant to pick his sister up from work. He stated he noticed the other vehicle approaching in the rearview mirror and attempted to accelerate and turn but was struck in the rear on the left side.
