A single vehicle collision occurred on a two-lane urban street with a parking lane on the right. The crash occurred in the evening during daylight hours. The roadway has a 2% downhill grade. The speed limit is 48 kmph (30 mph). At the time of the crash the sky was clear.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1998 Honda Civic with only a driver, was traveling north in lane one. V1 departed the roadway to the right, corrected her path of travel and returned to the number one travel lane. V1 then departed the number one travel lane to the right again and entered the parking lane and impacted a parked flatbed trailer. V1 then continued to travel north and came to rest just south of an intersection. The trailer was forced to rotate clockwise about 35 degrees coming to rest on the sidewalk. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1 was a 79-year old female with reported possible injuries due to the crash. She was transported from the scene to a medical facility. The driver refused to take part in the study. The police report indicated that the driver of V1 stated she had consumed alcohol prior to the collision. Medical records indicate the driver tested positive for alcohol with a BAC level of 0.28. Medical records indicate the driver takes numerous medications: Plavix, Toprol, Benicar, Lipitor, Elmiron, Levothroid, Norvasc and Etodolac. She suffers from arthritis, increasing insomnia and depression.
