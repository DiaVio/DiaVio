This two-vehicle morning crash occurred at the 4-way intersection of two urban arterial roads. The intersection is controlled by tri-light traffic signals and pedestrian signals. Both roadways are paved with bituminous asphalt and have five lanes of travel, two through lanes in each direction divided by a two-way turn lane. Before the intersection, the westbound traffic has a downhill grade of -8%, but the intersection is level in all directions. The speed limit is 48 kmph (30 mph) for both roads. At the time of the crash it was clear and there were no adverse weather conditions, sight restrictions or road defects.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1988 Chrysler New Yorker 4 door, was westbound in lane two approaching the intersection intending to go straight. Vehicle two (V2), a 1990 Toyota Camry 4 door, was northbound in lane one, stopped at the threshold of the intersection intending to go straight. V2 entered the intersection after the traffic light turned green, and V1 entered against the red light and the front of V1 struck the right side of V2 at the passenger door. The V1 left front bumper corner snagged on the right rear wheel of V2 and V1 was deflected to the right, and V2 spun over 90 degrees coming to rest in the intersection facing southeast. V2 was towed due to damage, but V1 was driven from the scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
