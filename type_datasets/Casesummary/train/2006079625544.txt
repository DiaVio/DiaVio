This two-vehicle collision occurred during morning daylight hours on a weekday. The crash occurred at the four-leg intersection of a north-south roadway and an east-west roadway. The intersection is controlled by a tri-light traffic signal with no protected left phase, and the speed limit is 56 kmph (35 mph) in all directions. At the time of the crash, there were no adverse atmospheric conditions and the roadway was free of defects.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1993 Toyota Previa, was traveling northbound in the third lane of a six-lane, two-way, undivided, dry, level, bituminous roadway, approaching the intersection with the intention of turning left into westbound traffic. Vehicle two (V2), a 1994 Chevrolet 1500 single-cab pickup, was traveling southbound in the first lane of opposing traffic, approaching the same intersection with the intention of passing straight through. As V1 made its turn, the front of V2 impacted the right side of V1. V1 rotated clockwise and came to rest in the eastbound lanes. V2 continued forward and came to rest in its original lane. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
