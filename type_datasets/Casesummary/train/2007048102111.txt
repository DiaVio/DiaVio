This two-vehicle crash occurred in a four-legged, traffic signal-controlled intersection in an interchange area. The north/south trafficway is divided, level and straight. The northbound lanes consist of three through lanes and a left turn lane. The westbound roadway (exit from freeway) is straight, level and consists of two left turn lanes and a right turn channelized lane. The speed limit for this roadway is 80 kmph (50 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2004 Chevrolet Cavalier, was northbound in lane one. Vehicle two (V2), a 2004 Toyota Tundra, was stopped westbound in lane one. V2 entered the intersection turning left and the left side was struck by the front of V1. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Unknown for V1 and Unknown for V2.
The driver of V1, a thirty-seven-year-old female, was not interviewed. This driver told the police officer on-scene that she tried to stop for the red traffic signal but her shoe was stuck under the brake pedal. There is no further information for this driver.
The driver of V2, a sixty-year-old male, was on his way from his home to a restaurant. He was healthy, well rested and familiar with his vehicle. He travels this route daily. This driver stated that his signal turned green and he waited for several vehicles to pass traveling northbound. He did not see V1 and entered the intersection.
