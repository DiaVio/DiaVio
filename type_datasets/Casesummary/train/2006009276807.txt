This crash occurred in the southbound lane of a two-lane one-way highway on the entrance ramp. There are two southbound lanes with a small merge lane from another highway that crosses it. This was a bituminous roadway with a slightly elevated entrance ramp with a very slight curve to the right. The speed limit was 89 kmph (55 mph). It was the mid afternoon on a weekday and the roads were dry and the sky was clear. Traffic flow was minimal.
V1, a 1998 Chevy Lumina Police vehicle with one occupant, was traveling eastbound on an entrance ramp into the southbound lane of the highway. V2 was a 1999 Honda CRV with one occupant, was stationary in lane 1 of the entrance ramp waiting to merge into the two-lane highway. V1 was following behind V2, when V2 stopped, V1's front contacted the back of V2. Both vehicles came to a rest in the shoulder of the highway. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
V1, the 98 Chevy Lumina police cruiser was driven by a 25 year old female who was transported from the scene for possible injuries. Other police officers on the scene did not want to say much about the incident since it involved a police officer, but one did say that the officer involved in the incident stated that she was probably not paying attention and looking at the monitor but then another officer said that the driver said that she was following to closely and she didn't think that V2 would stop in lane.
V2, the 1999 Honda CRV was driven by a 51 year old male who was not injured. Drivers attorney has denied interviews for his client.
