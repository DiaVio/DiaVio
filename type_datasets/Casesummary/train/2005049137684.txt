A two-vehicle collision occurred at 1048 hours in the morning on a Monday in May. The roadway was dry and free of defects. The speed limit for the roadway is 35 mph (56 kph).
V1, a 2001 Toyota Tacoma, was stopped facing southbound in the third lane of a three-lane, two-way, divided, asphalt roadway without a positive barrier. V2, a 2000 Ford Escort, was traveling northbound in the second lane of a three-lane, two-way, divided, asphalt roadway without a positive barrier. There were no traffic controls for either vehicle at this intersection. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1 attempted to make a left turn across the path of V2. As V1 crossed the second northbound lane, the front of V2 struck the right rear of V1. V1 rotated clockwise approximately 90 degrees and came to rest in the intersection. V2 rotated clockwise approximately 20 degrees and came to rest in the intersection. V1 and V2 were towed from the scene due to damage sustained in the collision.
The 46-year-old male driver of V1 was well rested but unfamiliar with area. The driver indicated that there was a large SUV in the third northbound lane waiting to turn left that obstructed his view of V2. He took no evasive action prior to impact as he made his left turn. The 17-year-old male driver of V2 was well rested and familiar with area. The driver stated that there was a black SUV in the third lane obstructing his view of V1. He applied his brakes to avoid the collision, but was unable to stop prior to impact.
