The crash occurred on a six-lane, two-way roadway. There were three eastbound lanes and three westbound lanes. This was a straight, bituminous roadway with a downhill grade and a posted speed of 56 kmph (35 mph). It was late evening on a weekday and the roadway was dry and it had just started snowing. Traffic was flowing.
V1, a 1998 Pontiac Grand Am two door with one occupant was traveling eastbound in lane two. V2, a 2001 GMC pickup truck with one occupant was traveling also traveling eastbound in lane two. The driver of V2 stated that she had stopped for a red light at an intersection. She saw the light turn green and was getting ready to accelerate when she was contacted by V1. She did not see V1 prior to impact. The front of V1 contacted the back of V2. This pushed V2 past the intersection where it came to final rest facing east in lane one. V1 came to final rest by the southeast corner in lane one facing northeast Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1 was a 31-year old male. He reported no injuries and refused treatment. He refused to answer any questions so I talked with a relative who told me what he had said about the crash. She stated that she did not know where he was coming from or going. And he traveled at an unknown speed.
The driver of V2 was a 56-year old female. She reported no injuries and refused treatment. She stated that she was coming from home on her way to work. She stated that she was stopped at the stop light for more than ten seconds when she was contacted.
