This single vehicle crash occurred during the early afternoon during daylight hours. The location was a residential street with multiple driveways and sidewalks with mountable curbs. The roadway was a bituminous two way, undivided level street with a right curve approaching the location of the crash. The weather was clear and dry. The posted speed limit was 40 kph (25 mph).
Vehicle one (V1), a 1988 GMC conventional cab pickup, driven by a sixty six (66) year old male was traveling northbound negotiating the right curve. The driver stated he started a coughing spell and had his head turned downward and was not able to see the roadway. His vehicle drifted across to the west road side and struck the front of a 1997 Ford Explorer 4 door SUV, which was legally parked next to the curb and unoccupied. On impact V1 stopped. The parked vehicle was knocked backwards approximately 6 meters (20 feet). Both vehicles were disabled. V1 was towed due to damage and the parked vehicle remained at the scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver stated that he was ill with a bad cold and he had spent most of the prior day and night in bed. He had a coughing spell that caused him to lower his head and not see the road. He was taking the following medications: Lipitor, unspecified type of insulin, Nyquil and Coumadin.
