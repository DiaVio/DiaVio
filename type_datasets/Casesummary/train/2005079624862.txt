This two-vehicle collision occurred during daylight hours on a weekend. The crash occurred at the four-leg intersection of a north-south roadway and an east-west roadway. The intersection was controlled by a tri-light traffic signal. At the time of the crash, there were no adverse atmospheric conditions, and the roadway was free of defects.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1987 Ford Ranger pickup, was traveling southbound in a 64 kmph (40 mph) zone, in the second lane of a five-lane, two-way, undivided, dry, level, bituminous roadway, approaching the intersection with the intention of passing straight through. Vehicle two (V2), a 2002 Honda Accord four-door sedan, was traveling eastbound in the first lane of a three-lane, divided (five lanes overall, divided by a raised median), dry, level, bituminous roadway, approaching the same intersection with the intention of passing straight through. As both vehicles entered the intersection, the front of V1 impacted the left side of V2. Both vehicles rotated counter-clockwise and came to rest in the intersection. V1 then drove away from the scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
