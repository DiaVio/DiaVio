This two-vehicle crash occurred on a two-lane roadway during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions. The northbound lane is straight and has a 5% upgrade. The southbound lane is curved right (radius of curvature is 375 meters) and level. This crash occurred in a no-passing zone, indicated by double yellow center lines. The speed limit on this roadway is 56 kmph (35 mph).
Vehicle one (V1), a 1990 Ford Ranger, was southbound and vehicle two (V2), a 1991 Honda Accord, was northbound. V1 traveled into the opposing traffic lane to pass a vehicle. The driver of V2 noticed V1 and applied the brakes and steered right. The front of V1 struck the left side of V2. V2 traveled off the right side of the roadway and the front struck a tree. Police Reported Travel Speed is 97 kmph for V1 and 97 kmph for V2.
The driver of V1, a twenty-eight-year-old male, left the scene of the crash and could not be located for an interview.
The driver of V2, a twenty-four-year-old female, was traveling from her home to another town. She was healthy and familiar with her vehicle. This driver travels this route daily.
