This single-vehicle crash occurred during daylight hours. The roadway is straight, level, undivided and consists of one lane in each direction. The speed limit for this roadway is 105 kmph (65 mph). It had been raining and hailing causing slush on the roadway.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2001 Nissan Pathfinder, was southbound in lane one. The driver of V1 tried to slow down and V1 slid off the roadway on the left side. The left front tire/wheel struck a ditch and then the right front tire/wheel struck a culvert. V1 then rolled over to the right two quarter-turns. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 89 kmph for V1.
The driver of V1, a sixty-five-year-old male, was traveling with his sixty-four-year-old wife. They had left their home and were on their way to a golf resort for a vacation. They had traveled approximately 185 km (115 miles) in 1.58 hours. This driver was healthy and comfortable with his vehicle and passenger. He stated that he felt rested although he had worked ten hours each day for the previous five days. He travels this route several times each year. This driver stated that he and his wife were discussing the weather and he "let off the gas pedal to slow down and my vehicle lost traction".
