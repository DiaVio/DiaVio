The crash occurred in the eastbound lane of a four-lane roadway, divided by a grass median. There were two westbound and two eastbound lanes. The eastbound lanes had a curve to the right. This was a curved to the right, level, bituminous roadway with a posted speed of 113kmph (70mph). It was late evening on a weekend and the road was dry and they sky was clear on this dark/unlighted roadway.
V1, a 2003 Chevrolet Blazer 2 door was traveling eastbound. The driver fell asleep as he entered the curve, he awoke as he was drifting into lane 2 and “cranked” the steering wheel to the right. V1 rolled left ¼ turn onto it's left side, slid off the edge of road, rolled another quarter turn left onto its roof into the median where it slid to a stop on its roof facing east. The driver and his passenger refused treatment at the scene and V1 was towed. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.
V1, a 2003 Chevrolet Blazer was driven by a 40 year old male who stated that he had gotten up early and drove to an outdoor music festival 150 miles away and spent the day there in 95 degree heat. He and his girl friend had left early in the event and stopped to eat about a half hour later. They had a heavy meal and drank 2 beers each. He was fatigued when he started the trip. His girl friend was asleep in the back seat at the time of the crash. He was weaving as he fell asleep at the wheel and woke up when the car overturned.
