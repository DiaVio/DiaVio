This two-vehicle crash occurred in a four-legged, traffic signal-controlled intersection. The east/west roadway is undivided, straight, level and consists of two lanes in the westbound direction and one lane in the eastbound direction. The intersecting roadway is straight, level and consists of four lanes in the southbound direction. The speed limit for these roadways is 48 kmph (30 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1997 Dodge Stratus, was westbound in lane two. Vehicle two (V2), a 2003 Ford F150 pickup, was southbound in lane three. In the intersection, the front of V1 struck the left side of V2. V2 rotated counterclockwise and rolled over onto the right side. V2 continued to rotate and rolled back over onto its wheels. Bothe vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, a sixteen-year-old male, was not interviewed. No further information is available for this driver. Police report indicates that this vehicle was traveling at a high rate of speed.
The driver of V2, a fifty-three-year-old male, was on his way from his workplace to a different branch office. He was well rested, in good health and familiar with his vehicle. He travels this route weekly. This driver has high blood pressure and takes the medications Allegra, Zaick and Zantac. This driver stated that he did not see V1 prior to the crash.
