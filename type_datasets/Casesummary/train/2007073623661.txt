This two-vehicle crash occurred in a four-legged intersection. The north/south roadway is straight, level, undivided, consists of one lane in each direction and is controlled by stop signs. The east/west roadway is straight, level, undivided and consists of one lane in the eastbound direction and two lanes in the westbound direction. The speed limit for these roadways is 48 kmph (30 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions. Traffic was congested at the time of the crash.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2000 Honda Civic, was stopped at the stop sign southbound in lane one. Vehicle two (V2), a 1993 Dodge Intrepid, was eastbound in lane one. Non-contact vehicles were stopped in the westbound lanes. V1 entered the intersection and the right side was struck by the front of V2. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, a sixteen-year-old female, was on her way home from shopping with her fifty-year-old mother. They were talking at the time of the crash. This driver was healthy, well rested and comfortable with her vehicle and passenger. She rarely travels this route. She had one year of driving experience at the time of the crash. This driver stated that she looked for traffic and did not see V2 prior to the crash.
The driver of V2, a thirty-seven-year-old male, was on his way from lunch to school. He was in fairly good health, well rested and familiar with his vehicle. He travels this route weekly. This driver is diabetic and takes insulin and vitamins. He stated that he did not see V1 due to the stopped traffic in the westbound lanes. When he saw V1 at the "last second, " he applied the brakes in an attempt to avoid the crash.
