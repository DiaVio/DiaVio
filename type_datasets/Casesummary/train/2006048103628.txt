This single-vehicle crash occurred on a two-lane, dry, bituminous, uphill, roadway that curves slightly to the left. The radius of this curve is 167 meters (548 feet) and the super elevation is 5% (6cm/122cm). The posted speed limit is 56kmph (35mph). There were no traffic control devices present at the location of the crash. This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
The vehicle, a 2006 Mitsubishi Eclipse, was traveling southbound in lane one. As the vehicle negotiated the curve, it departed the roadway to the right and then re-entered the roadway. On the right side of the roadway where the initial road departure occurred there is no shoulder and the dirt surface drops about 5 to 8 cm (2 to 3 inches) lower than the paved asphalt travel lane. The vehicle then traveled off the left side of the roadway, rotating slightly clockwise. The left side of the vehicle struck a shrub and then the front struck a shrub and a hedge. The vehicle crossed a driveway and the left front wheel struck a landscape timber. The vehicle continued on and the left side struck a wooden post, the vehicle rotated counterclockwise and the right side struck a hedge. The vehicle came to final rest facing east-northeast. Police Reported Travel Speed is 105 kmph for V1.
The driver of the vehicle, a twenty-year-old male, was well rested and was traveling from his brother's house to his father's house. He drives this roadway and vehicle daily and has never received any kind of driver training.
