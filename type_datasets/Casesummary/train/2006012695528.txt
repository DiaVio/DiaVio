The crash occurred in the eastbound lanes of a five-lane roadway. There were two westbound lanes and two eastbound lanes with a left-turn lane. This was a straight, level, bituminous roadway with a posted speed limit of 72 kmph (45 mph). It was late morning on a weekday and the road was dry and the sky was clear.
V1, a 2006 Buick Lacrosse four-door with one occupant was traveling northbound in lane one. V2, a 1980 Ford 9000 dump truck with one occupant was traveling eastbound in lane one. V1 was attempting to make a right-hand turn at the intersection. V2 was passing through the intersection. V1 had a red light, but thought it was green so he proceeded to turn in front of V2. The driver of V2 saw V1 turning and attempted to avoid him by steering left and locking up the brakes. He could not avoid him and the front of V2 contacted the back of V1 before they both came to final rest in the roadway. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
V1, the Buick Lacrosse, was driven by a 65-year old male who reported minor injuries and was transported to a local trauma facility. He advised that he had just left home and was on his way to the bank. He advised that he had been traveling between 50-64 kmph (31-40 mph) prior to the crash. He stated that he thought the light was green and proceeded to make his right turn.
V2, the Ford dump truck, was driven by a 35-year old male who reported no injuries. He advised that he had just come from dumping a load and was on his way back to the work site. He advised that he was traveling between 50-64 kmph (31-40 mph) prior to the crash.
