A single vehicle crash occurred in the early evening hours after dusk on a weekday. The roadway was a two-lane, two-way, undivided bituminous arterial with paved shoulders. This portion of the roadway was straight and level with dry travel lanes and no adverse weather conditions. It was dark with street lights and the posted speed limit is 64 kmph, or 40 mph.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2002 Honda Accord EX four door sedan, was traveling northbound. The driver states the headlights of an on-coming vehicle blinded her with its high beams. The vehicle drifted to the right, departed the roadway and onto an asphalt and then gravel shoulder. The vehicle then struck a mail box post, traveled up and over a driveway, struck a warning sign post and continued into the ditch and rode up the embankment coming to rest on the embankment with the left side of the vehicle against the ditch. Vehicle one was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.
The driver of vehicle one, a 53 year-old female, was wearing a lap & shoulder belt and was not injured. The driver does not have any health problems, takes no medications and was well rested. She had left her home approximately 25 minutes before the crash occurred and was on her way to work a 12-hour shift to end her 60-hour workweek. At the time of the crash she still had 35 minutes before she started work and was not in a hurry. She was very familiar with the roadway and drives it frequently.
