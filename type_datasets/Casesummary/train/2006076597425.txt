This three-vehicle crash occurred on a level, straight, dry roadway. This roadway consists of three westbound lanes, a center left-turn lane and two eastbound lanes. The speed limit on this roadway is 72 kmph (45 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1975 Cadillac Calais, was westbound in lane three. Vehicle two (V2), a 2005 Pontiac Grand Am, was in front of V1 and vehicle three (V3), a 2005 Ford Expedition, was in front of V2. V3 came to a stop for a stopped non-contact vehicle. The driver of V2 applied the brakes and was decelerating. The front of V1 struck the back of V2. V2 moved forward and the front struck the back of V2. V2 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 37 kmph for V1 and 37 kmph for V2.
The driver of V1, a ninety-three-year-old male, was traveling from his home to a friend's home. This driver was well rested and familiar with his vehicle and his route. He takes aspirin and Prevacid. This driver stated that he has a heart condition, acid reflux and "old age." He also stated that he didn't realize that V2 was slowing soon enough to make any avoidance maneuvers.
The driver of V2, a forty-three-year-old male, was traveling with a fifty-five-year-old female, from his home to a store. He was healthy, well rested and comfortable with his vehicle and passenger. This driver travels this route daily.
The driver of V3, a thirty-one-year-old female, was not interviewed. No further information is available for this driver.
