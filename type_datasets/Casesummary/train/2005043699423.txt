The crash occurred in the northbound lanes of a four-lane urban highway with a grass median. There were three northbound lanes and a left turn lane. The roadway had a posted speed limit of 55 mph and the northbound lanes had a downhill grade of 5%. Conditions were dark and dry. The crash occurred in the mid-evening hours of a weekday.
Vehicle one was a 1990 Oldsmobile Cutlass Cruiser SL wagon. Vehicle two was a 2000 Chevrolet 1500 Silverado. V1 was traveling south in the left turn lane approaching an intersection. V2 was traveling north in lane two approaching the same intersection. V1 came to a stop in the median area of the intersection. Another vehicle was also in the median area turning in the opposite direction and was partially obscuring V1's view of traffic. V1 inched out into the intersection and then proceeded to turn left. The right side of V1 was impacted by the front of V2. V1 traveled north back into the median and came to final rest. V2 traveled northeast into the east roadside and came to final rest off the roadway. V1 then experienced an engine compartment fire. Police Reported Travel Speed is 8 kmph for V1 and 8 kmph for V2.
V1 was driven by a 24-year old female who was transported to a local trauma center with non-incapacitating injuries. She was unable to be located for an interview. The investigating officer estimated her travel speed to be 5 mph. V1 was towed due to damage.
V2 was driven by a 28-year old male who was uninjured. He stated that he saw V1 inching out behind a van from the median intersection. V2 then cut across the roadway in front of him. He braked with lockup and steered right to no avail. His vehicle caught fire in the engine compartment after impact. He estimated his speed to be 50 mph at impact. V2 was towed due to damage.
