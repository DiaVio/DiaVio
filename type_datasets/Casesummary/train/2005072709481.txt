This two-vehicle crash occurred before noon during daylight hours in the middle of a four-way intersection. The east/west roadway is comprised of two through lanes and a left-turn lane in each direction. The north leg of the intersection runs in both directions; the two northbound lanes are separated by a triangular shaped median island with lane one heading straight and lane two on a slight angle that straightens further down the road, the southbound lanes run parallel with lane two of the northbound lanes and are comprised of two lanes with a additional left-turn lane. The south leg of the intersection has one southbound lane, and three travel lanes, including a left turn lane that run northbound. The posted speed limit is 48 kmph (30 mph).
Vehicle one (V1), a 2000 Chevrolet Trailblazer with one occupant, was traveling westbound through the intersection in lane two. Vehicle two (V2), a 1992 Geo Tracker with two occupants was traveling east bound through the intersection in lane three (the left turn lane). V2 was stopped in the intersection, yielding to oncoming traffic, attempting to turn left. When the light turned yellow the driver of V2 waited for the traffic to stop to proceed out of the intersection. As V2 proceeded to turn, V1 was speeding to surpass the traffic control light. As both vehicles proceeded through the intersection V2 impacted the left side of V1 with its front. V1 then deflected off V2 and traveled off the right side of the road and with its front plane crashed into a light pole that was located on the northwest corner of the intersection.
