The crash occurred on a two-way, two-lane, straight, slag rural road with a statutory speed limit of 89 kmph (55 mph). Conditions at the time of the weekday early morning crash were snowing, dark, unlighted and the slag/dirt snow covered road was icy.
V1, a 1999 Ford Taurus SE 4 door sedan driven by a 17 year-old male on his way from home was westbound on the road. The driver stated that he was running late to pick up his girlfriend on his way to school and was traveling 50 mph. V1 went up a >2% slope, crested the hill and the driver stated that as he came over the top of the hill he felt the vehicle begin to slide down the >2% slope on the icy road. V1 rotated clockwise and the driver applied the (ABS) brakes and steered left. The vehicle began to rotate counterclockwise and the driver over-corrected to the right and V1 again rotated clockwise and slid with the left side leading off the edge of the road on the right side. The left front of V1 struck two >10cm diameter trees and rotated counterclockwise. The front of V1 struck shrubbery and V1came to rest on the road facing southwest. V1 was towed from the scene due to damage. The fully restrained driver refused treatment at the scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.
