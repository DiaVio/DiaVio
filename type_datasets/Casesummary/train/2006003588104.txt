The crash occurred in the afternoon hours of a weekend at the intersection of a one-way roadway and a two-way roadway. The intersection consisted of a one-way northbound roadway and a two-way east/west roadway. The east/west roadway was controlled by a stop sign. Both roadways were dry, straight and level, and had a speed limit of 48kmph (30mph).
V1, a 1996 Chevrolet Caprice four door with a single occupant, was traveling northbound. V2, a 2001 Ford Focus four door with a single occupant was traveling westbound. At the intersection, the frontal plane of V1 impacted the left side plane of V2. As V2 was continuing straight V1 as it rotated counterclockwise hit V2 again in the back. V1 came to a final rest with its right/rear wheel on the sidewalk 14 meters west of the north/west corner. V2 came to a final rest on the sidewalk 16.2 meters west of the north/west corner. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
V1, the Chevrolet Caprice was driven by a 25 year-old male who reported no injuries. The driver refused to participate in the study (would not be interviewed or allow his vehicle to be inspected).
V2, the Ford Focus was driven by a 36 year-old male who reported shoulder/upper arm pain, and was transported to a hospital. He stated that he stopped at the intersection and when proceeding forward he was impacted by V1. He also stated that he was watching a vehicle proceed westbound through the intersection in front of him.
