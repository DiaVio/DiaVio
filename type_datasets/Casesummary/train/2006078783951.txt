This single vehicle ran off the right side of the roadway crash occurred late afternoon during daylight hours on a five lane, two-way roadway. There are three northbound lanes two southbound lanes that are separated by a raised concrete median. There is a northbound left turn lane at the area where the crash occurred. This was a straight level, bituminous roadway with a posted speed limit of 72 kmph (45 mph) the road was dry and the sky was clear.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1991 Honda Civic DX 3 door Hatchback, was traveling northbound in the #2 lane at about 72-80 kmph (45-50 mph). The vehicle departed its original lane in a northwesterly direction. The vehicle began to rotate clockwise and eventually exited the roadway and struck a curb. The vehicle continued to travel off the roadway and rotating clockwise. The right front area struck a signpost. The left front area also struck a signpost. The vehicle came to rest approximately 4 meters (13 feet) north of the signpost. The vehicle was racing another non-contact vehicle that was traveling northbound in the #1 lane. V1 was towed from the scene. The driver and passengers fled the scene in the vehicle they were racing. Police Reported Travel Speed is 72 kmph for V1.
The driver of V1 was located for an interview and described the crash circumstances. The driver was an 18-year-old male, and there were two additional male passengers in the vehicle ages 17 and 13. They were racing a non-contact vehicle and before the situation became critical the driver was observing another vehicle rearward of his vehicle by using the side mirror. He was being overtaken by the vehicle he was racing when he lost control of his vehicle. He is in good health, takes no medications, and was not fatigued. He has been driving for two years with no training. He reported no problems with the vehicle.
