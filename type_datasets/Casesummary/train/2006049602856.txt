This two-vehicle crash occurred on a level, one-way, one-lane roadway, controlled by a yield sign. This roadway is the right turn lane for an intersection and is separated from the main roadway by a curbed triangular island. This roadway curves to the right and the speed limit is 72 kmph (45 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours and it was raining.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2006 Chrysler 300, was southbound in lane one. Vehicle two (V2), a 1989 GMC 1500 pickup, was in front of V1. V2 stopped for traffic. The front of V1 struck the back of V2. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, a forty-eight-year-old male, was traveling from his home to work. This driver was well rested and familiar with his vehicle and route. He takes an unspecified medication for an unspecified heart condition. This driver stated that he was aware of V2, but he was looking to the left for traffic and did not realize that V2 stopped. He applied the brakes, but slid on the wet roadway.
The driver of V2, a twenty-four-year-old male, was traveling with a thirty-three-year-old male, from his home to work. He was healthy, well rested and familiar with his vehicle. He travels this route daily.
