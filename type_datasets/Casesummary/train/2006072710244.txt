This two-vehicle crash occurred in a four-way intersection. The north/south section is comprised of two through lanes and a left-turn lane in each direction. The west leg of the intersection is a non-divided two-way road, and the east leg is a one-way, westbound, roadway. The intersection is controlled by stoplights at all four legs. The weather condition at the time of a crash was a light rain and the roads were wet. The speed limit on the roadway was 48 kph (30 mph).
Vehicle one, a 2000 Oldsmobile Alero was traveling southbound in the second lane. Vehicle two, a 2001 Nissan Xterra was traveling northbound in the left turn lane. Vehicle two turned left at the intersection. The front of vehicle one contacted the right side of vehicle two. Vehicle one was not towed due to damage. Vehicle two was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of vehicle one, a twenty-five year old male was heading home after shopping. This driver had been driving for only a few minutes at the time of the crash. This driver stated that he thinks that as vehicle two was turning it lost control on the wet roads but there is no evidence of this. The driver of vehicle one drives this vehicle daily and over this road on a daily basis.
The driver of vehicle two, a fifty-nine year old male was not interviewed. There is no known pre-crash information for this driver.
