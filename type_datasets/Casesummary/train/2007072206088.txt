This three-vehicle crash occurred in a four-legged intersection. The east/west roadway is straight, level, undivided and consists of two through lanes in each direction with parking on both sides. The north/south roadway is straight, level, undivided, consists of one lane in each direction with parking on both sides and is controlled by stop signs. The speed limit for these roadways is 48 kmph (30 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions. Traffic was congested at the time of the crash.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2005 Nissan Altima, was northbound in lane one. Vehicle two (V2), a 2003 Suzuki motorcycle, was westbound in lane one. Vehicle three (V3), a 1993 Nissan Altima, was parked northbound on the right side north of the intersection. In the intersection, the front of V1 struck the left side of V2. V1 continued to the north and the front struck the left side of V3. V1 and V2 were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, a twenty-five-year-old female, was on her way home from work. She was healthy, well rested and familiar with her vehicle. She travels this route daily. This driver stated that eastbound traffic stopped to allow her to cross the intersection. She further stated that she looked left and right and did not see V2 prior to the crash.
The driver of V2, a thirty-seven-year-old male, was taking his "bike out for a ride." He was healthy and had two years of experience riding motorcycles. This was the first time he had been on this roadway. This driver stated that he did not see V1 prior to the crash.
