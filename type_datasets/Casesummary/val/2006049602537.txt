This crash occurred at an intersection with traffic lights. The road is a 5-lane divided concrete roadway from west to east. It is a six-lane undivided (two-way traffic) concrete roadway from south to north. The speed limit is 54 kmph (35 mph) for the roadway. The road surface was dry and there were no detrimental atmospheric conditions at the time of the crash. The crash happened during the early morning daylight hours during a weekday and there was medium traffic on the roadways.
The only vehicle, Vehicle one (V1), a 2002 Chevrolet Silverado extended cab pick up with one occupant, was traveling east in the third lane of traffic at a speed of 64 kmph (40 mph). V1 traveled thru the intersection, then departed the roadway to the left and entered the curbed, raised median. The front of V1 impacted two small trees and a breakaway pole supporting a streetlight. Both trees were uprooted and dragged along with the vehicle. The pole was separated from its base and fell and contacted the roof and bed of V1. V1 crossed the median and came to rest partially on the median and partially within the westbound lanes of traffic. V1 traveled 25 meters (75 feet) from the road departure to final rest. The vehicle was towed from the scene. The driver was transported to a local hospital. According to the investigating police officer on scene, the driver suffered a broken arm but left the emergency room without being released. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.
A 38-year old female drove V1, the 2002 Chevrolet truck. The police report lists her as 43 years old, with a date of birth. The driver was not licensed. The source of the age on the police report is not known. The driver herself advised her age as 38 years. She was only willing to provide basic information and wanted to keep the interview brief. She stated she traveling at a speed of 34-48 kmph (21-30 mph) and was approaching the intersection when she fell asleep. According to her she woke up when the vehicle was at final rest, realizing she had hit a light pole. She did not provide any further information. The truck was used as a work truck with insignificant cargo in the bed. Information downloaded from the data recorder, scene evidence and damage to the vehicle verified the driver's description of falling to sleep prior to the vehicle running off the roadway onto the median.
