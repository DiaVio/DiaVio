This two-vehicle crash occurred during early afternoon daylight hours on a dry, bituminous, level, four-lane, median divided roadway with no positive barrier. The roadway runs north to south and intersects with a two-lane, dry, bituminous, level, uncontrolled roadway that runs east to west. There were no adverse atmospheric conditions at the time of the crash.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1999 Pontiac Bonneville was traveling westbound in lane one. V1 entered the intersection in an attempt to cross the northbound lanes and go south. Vehicle two (V2), a 1998 Ford Escort ZX2 was traveling northbound in lane three. As V1 entered the intersection, V2 skidded for 17 meters (56 feet)and the front of V2 contacted the front of V1. Post-impact, V1 rotated counterclockwise and came to rest facing northwest in lane two of the northbound roadway. Post-impact, V2 rotated counterclockwise and came to rest facing east in lane three of the northbound roadway. V1 contained two occupants. The driver was transported to a local trauma center for incapacitating injuries. V2 contained one occupant, she was transported to a local trauma center for incapacitating injuries. Both vehicles were towed due to disabling damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 16 kmph for V1 and 16 kmph for V2.
The driver of V1, a 58-year-old female was familiar and comfortable with the vehicle and passenger load. She drives the roadway several times weekly and has 40 years of driving experience. The driver of V2 was a 19-year-old female who has 4 years of driving experience and was familiar/comfortable with the vehicle and passenger load. She drives this roadway daily.
The driver of V1 stated that she waited for at least 20 seconds and then looked both ways before pulling into the intersection. She said that she did not see V2. The driver of V2 stated that she was traveling between 88 - 96 kmph (55 - 60 mph). However, scene evidence indicates that V2 was traveling at a higher rate of speed. The speed of V2 was taken into consideration as a factor in this case.
