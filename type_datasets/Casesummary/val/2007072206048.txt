This two-vehicle crash occurred on an undivided and straight roadway. The eastbound lanes have a downhill grade and consist of two through lanes and a left turn lane. The speed limit on this roadway is 48 kmph (30 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions. Traffic was congested at the time of the crash.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2007 Buick LaCrosse, was eastbound in lane one. Vehicle two (V2), a 2000 Ford F750 straight truck, was eastbound in lane two. V1 attempted to change lanes to the left and the front of the vehicle struck the right side of V2. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is No PAR obtained (created) for V1 and No PAR obtained (created) for V2.
The driver of V1, a twenty-year-old male, was on his way downtown with four passengers. This driver was from another state and had never driven on this road. He stated that he saw the truck in lane two and activated his left turn signal. This driver further stated that he thought that the driver of V2 would slow down and allow him to make his lane change.
The driver of V2, a thirty-eight-year-old male, was transporting a load of asphalt from the asphalt yard to a job site. He was familiar with his vehicle and travels this route weekly. This driver stated that he did not see V1 until after the crash.
