This two-vehicle crash occurred in a four-legged, traffic signal-controlled intersection. The north/south roadway is undivided, straight and consists of two lanes in each direction. The southbound travel lanes have an uphill grade and the northbound travel lanes have a downhill grade. The speed limit for this roadway is 56 kmph (35 mph). It was dark with street lights on at the time of the crash with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1972 Chevrolet Malibu, was southbound in lane two. Vehicle two (V2), a 1997 Mercury Tracer, was northbound in lane two. V1 turned left in the intersection and the right side was struck by the front of V2. V2 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, a sixteen-year-old male, was on his way home from a gas station with a twenty-year-old male friend. This driver was well rested, healthy and comfortable with his vehicle and passenger. He travels this route daily. He had one year of driving experience at the time of the crash. This driver stated that he was talking to his passenger. He also reached for his cell phone when it rang and was talking on the phone. He further stated that he applied the brakes when he looked up and saw V2.
The driver of V2, a twenty-year-old female, was on her way home from her boyfriend's house. She was well rested, healthy and familiar with her vehicle. She travels this route daily. This driver stated that she saw V1 and applied the brakes and steered to the right when she realized that it was turning in front of her.
