A two-vehicle daytime collision occurred at an uncontrolled intersection of two two-way two-lane roadways. Parking on both sides of the roadway on both streets is allowed. Both roadways have an asphalt surface and the intersection is level. Both roadways are straight approaching the intersection with traffic approaching from the south have a 9% up grade. The speed limit for both vehicles was 40 kmph (25 mph). The sky was clear and the roadway was dry. Vehicles legally parked obscured the line of sight of both drivers.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1994 Honda Civic with only a driver, was heading east in lane one. Vehicle two (V2), a 2003 Toyota Highlander with two occupants, was headed north in lane one. V2 entered the intersection and was struck on the right side by the front of V1. V1 proceeded to push V2 into a signpost to its final rest. The driver of V1, in an attempt to avoid charges backed his vehicle away from V2 and proceeded to drive away from the scene. V1 was only able to travel about 45 meters (148 feet) before his vehicle failed. Both vehicles were towed from the scene. The driver of V1 and the passenger in V2 were transported to a treatment center. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1 was an 18 year-old unlicensed male. He was on his way to the store from his residence to pick up medication for his cold/flu like symptoms. He had travelled two blocks before the crash occurred. He drives the route weekly and has owned the vehicle one month. The driver stated he could not see V2 because of a white van parked at the intersection.
The driver of V2 was a 31 year-old female. The driver who had not been on this roadway before stated she never saw the other vehicle. She also noted that the driver of V1 was making an attempt to flee the scene by backing away from her vehicle and proceeding down the roadway. The driver and passenger were returning to work after a lunch break. The two were conversing at the time of the collision.
