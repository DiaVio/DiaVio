This three-vehicle crash occurred in a rural mountainous area with various curves and elevations on a 2-lane asphalt roadway The northbound lane curves left before straightening out and is downhill. The speed limit is 89 kmph (55 mph). The crash occurred during late morning daylight hours with no adverse weather or road conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2001 Dodge Ram pickup was northbound negotiating the left curve when it came upon vehicle two (V2), a 2004 Chevrolet Silverado pickup. V2 was stopped in the lane behind vehicle three (V3), a 2007 Honda CRV that had stopped in the lane behind a non-contact vehicle that was attempting to make a U-turn. With its front end-plane, V1 impacted with the rear plane of V2 pushing V2 forward into the rear plane of V3. All vehicles came to rest in the area of impact. V1 and V2 were towed due to damage. V3 was driven from the scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, a thirty seven (37) year-old male, was returning to his residence in another town after a weekend fishing trip at a lake recreation area 97 kilometers (60 miles) away. He stated he came around a blind turn and saw V2 stopped in the lane. He applied his brakes and skidded into the back of V2. He travels the roadway occasionally and stated he was going just under the speed limit.
The driver of V2, a fifty two (52) year-old female and the passenger, were unable to be interviewed.
The driver of V3, a fifty seven (57) year-old male, was traveling to a local lookout area from his residence 80 kilometers (50 miles) away. He stated that a vehicle with out-of-state license plates had stopped in the northbound lane signaling to turn left and waiting for oncoming traffic to pass although there is only a dirt pullout area to the left. He assumed that the out-of-state driver was lost and was attempting to make a U-turn. He saw through his rear view mirror that V2 had stopped behind him. He had been stopped several seconds when the impact occurred. He is familiar with the roadway.
