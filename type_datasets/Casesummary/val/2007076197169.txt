This single-vehicle crash occurred in a rural area on a two-lane asphalt roadway. The roadway is straight and level. It is bordered on both sides by an irrigation ditch and farmland. The speed limit for the area is 72 kmph (45 mph). The crash occurred mid-afternoon during daylight hours with no adverse weather or road conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2002 Chevrolet Cavalier coupe, was southbound when it steered right to avoid something in the road. It steered left back onto the roadway then overcompensated right where it rotated clockwise as it departed the dirt shoulder and impacted the west side of the ditch with its front plane and came to rest facing west. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 80 kmph for V1.
The driver of V1, a fourteen (14) year-old male and three of his friends, were driving from the friend's house back to the residence of the driver. He stated that something appeared in the roadway. He wasn't sure what it was, maybe a big brown rock or an animal. He steered right to avoid it and went off the right side of the road then he steered left. He then panicked and overcompensated right where the vehicle rotated clockwise and impacted the ditch. The driver is not licensed but has been driving for 6 months.
