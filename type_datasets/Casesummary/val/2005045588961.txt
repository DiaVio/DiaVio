The crash occurred on a north/south four-lane median divided urban highway with shoulders. There was a median crossover that provided access for an intersecting two-lane road and store that were located on the west side of the road. The highway curved slightly to the right (west) as it traveled southward with a radius of curvature of 804m. The posted speed limit was 72kph (45mph). Conditions were dark with streetlights and dry on a weekend night.
Vehicle #1 was a 2005 Chevrolet 3500 series pickup traveling south on the highway. Ahead of the pickup (V1) four other southbound vehicles were forced to stop due to vehicles turning left into and waiting in the crossover. Directly ahead of the pickup was Vehicle #2, a 2002 Mazda Millenia and in front of it was Vehicle #3, a 1996 Chrysler Town and Country minivan. Vehicle #1 was unable to slow in time and rear-ended Vehicle #2. The Mazda (V2) was pushed forward and rear-ended the minivan (V3). Vehicle #3 then struck the rear of another vehicle. All vehicles came to rest near impact in their original lane. Vehicle's #1 and #2 were towed; Vehicle #3 was driven away. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
Vehicle #1 was driven by a 28-year old male that was belted and not injured. The frontal airbags did not deploy. He stated that traffic stopped abruptly and he was unable to stop in time. Police charged him with following too closely.
Vehicle #2 was operated by a 29-year old female that was belted with no deployed airbags. She was transported to an area hospital with minor injuries.
Vehicle #3 was driven by a 29-year old female that was belted with no deployed airbags. She was not injured in the collision. She stated that she'd been stopped for approximately 20-30 seconds and that the vehicle behind her (V2) had been stopped about 10 seconds when the crash occurred.
