This single-vehicle crash occurred on an exit ramp from a controlled-access trafficway. This ramp is straight, one-way and has a downhill grade. The speed limit for this roadway is 89 kmph (55 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2006 Ford Crown Victoria, a police vehicle, was southbound on the exit ramp. The police officer was responding to a call and had the emergency lights and sirens activated. A non-contact vehicle (NCV) was in front of V1. This NCV moved off the roadway to the left. As V1 was attempting to pass the location of the NCV, the NCV abruptly returned to the roadway. The driver of V1 steered to the left and and traveled off the left side of the roadway. The driver of V1 steered abruptly right. V1 then rotated clockwise and the left side struck a utility pole in the gore area. V1 continued southbound, then rolled over to the left two quarter-turns. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.
The driver of V1, a thirty-six-year-old male, was not interviewed. No further information is available for this driver.
