This two-vehicle crash occurred at the intersection of a seven-lane roadway and a two-lane roadway. The roadways were illuminated by streetlights. The north/south roadway is undivided, dry, level, asphalt, and consists of seven lanes (including a left-turn lane). The east/west roadway is undivided dry, level, asphalt, and consists of two lanes. The north/south roadway is designated a 35 mph (56 kph) zone. The east/west roadway is designated a 25 mph (40 kph) zone. The intersection is controlled by a tri-light signal that was functioning properly at the time of the collision. This crash occurred during evening hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one, a 2000 Chrysler 300M was westbound in the third lane. Vehicle two, a 2003 Mercedes-Benz E500 was westbound in the third lane. Vehicle two was stopped approximately three to four vehicles east of the intersection. As vehicle two was waiting for the green light, the front plane of vehicle one contacted its rear plane. Vehicle one came to rest facing southwest blocking the number three and number four lanes. Vehicle two came to rest facing westbound in the number three lane. Vehicle one received moderate damage and was towed from the scene. Vehicle two received moderate damage and was not towed from the scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of vehicle one, a fifty-two year old male was traveling to a music shop from a restaurant. The driver informed police that he had consumed two 12-ounce beers at the restaurant. The driver was given alcohol tests at 2041 hours and 2045 hours. Both tests yielded a result of 0.13. The driver has depression and takes unspecified medications. The driver is currently experiencing stress at work because he is not doing as well as he should. He stated that he works 24 hours a day, seven days a week since his clients can call him at any time. The driver had Lasik eye surgery five years ago to correct a myopic condition. Since the surgery he sees "halos" around lights at night.
The driver of vehicle two, a fifty-one year old female was traveling home from a shopping mall. The driver had driven approximately 15 minutes prior to the collision. The driver works for her husband at his business when he needs her assistance. The driver stated she was stopped behind three or four cars waiting for the green light when she felt an impact to her rear plane.
