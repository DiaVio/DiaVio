This three-vehicle crash occurred at an intersection. The north/south road is a 4-lane divided (no positive barrier) concrete roadway. It is a two lane (two-way traffic) undivided roadway from east to west. The speed limit for the east/west roadway is 48 kmph (30 mph). The speed limit for the north/south is 64 kmph (40 mph). The road surface was dry and there were no detrimental atmospheric conditions at the time of the crash. It was late morning hours on a weekday and there was little to medium traffic on the roadways. The east/west traffic had a stop sign prior to entering the intersection.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1989 Chevrolet Cavalier with one occupant, was traveling east in the first lane of traffic, crossing through the intersection. Vehicle two (V2), a 2005 Dodge Ram Pick up with two occupants was traveling north in lane two. Vehicle three (V3), a 2005 Volvo S 40 with one occupant, was traveling north in the first lane adjacent to V2. V2 and V3 were approaching the intersection of both roadways. At the intersection of both roadways the front of V2 impacted the right side of V1. V1 rotated clockwise and the front of V1 struck the left side of V3. V2 rotated clockwise after the impact, and moved to the northeast corner of the intersection. V2 came to rest within lane one and two, facing north. V3 continued to drive north in lane one until the driver stopped the vehicle. V1 continued to rotate as the vehicle ran off the northeast corner of the intersection moving backward. The back of the vehicle struck a stop sign and several shrubs as the vehicle came to a stop. The vehicle came to rest facing southwest. V1 was towed from the scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
A 92-year old male drove V1. He could not be interviewed in person. His wife and son advised the researcher the driver is living at an assisted living home since his release from the hospital. Medical records states he sustained a syncopal episode prior to the crash. He takes unspecified medication for high blood pressure. He was involved in a minor crash with the same vehicle earlier that day. At the time of the crash he was on his way to his insurance agent to report this previous crash. He is familiar with the vehicle and drives the route several times a month.
A 37-year old male drove V2. He stated he was in the second lane, traveling at a normal rate of speed. He was heading from one work assignment to another. He does not take any medication and is in good heath. He drives on the roadway weekly. He said he glanced at his right front occupant with his right eye but was not conversing with his occupant. He insisted he still focused on the roadway. He saw V1 pre-crash, but not in time to avoid the crash. He failed to look to his left earlier.
A 35-year-old female was the driver of V3. She was heading from a work presentation to her office. She is in good health, was well rested. She takes birth control and pain medication. She rarely drives on the roadway. She said she saw V1 crossing the intersection and realized a collision with V2 was imminent due to the high speed of V1. She accelerated her vehicle in an attempt to avoid involvement.
V3 was not involved in first harmful event. No critical reason for the critical event was coded to this vehicle.
