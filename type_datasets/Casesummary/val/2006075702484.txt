This two-vehicle crash occurred in a three-legged, traffic signal-controlled intersection. The north/south roadway is straight and divided by a curbed median. The southbound approach has a 2.5 percent downhill grade and consists of a two through lanes and a right turn lane. The eastbound approach is undivided, has a 3.3 percent uphill grade and curves to the right (radius of curvature of 103 meters and superelevation of two percent). The eastbound approach consists of a left turn lane and a right turn lane. The speed limit on these roadways is 72 kmph (45mph). These roadways were wet and covered in slush at the time of the crash. This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1999 Ford F250 pickup, was southbound in lane three. Vehicle two (V2), a 2000 Ford Excursion, was stopped eastbound in lane two. V2 entered the intersection, turning left and the left side was struck by the front of V1. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 72 kmph for V1 and 72 kmph for V2.
The driver of V1, a twenty-three-year-old male, was traveling from work to his parents' home. He was healthy, well rested and familiar with his vehicle. He travels this route weekly. This driver stated that the sun glare prevented him from realizing that his traffic signal was red.
The driver of V2, a twenty-eight-year-old male, was traveling with a six-year-old female. He had left his home and was on his way to pick up his daughter. He was healthy, well rested and comfortable with his vehicle and passenger. He travels this route weekly. This driver stated that he entered the intersection after the traffic signal turned green.
