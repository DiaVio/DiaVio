This crash occurred on an eastbound on-ramp that merged onto a two-lane highway. The approach to the merge point was a curve to the right with a slight downhill grade, which then straightened out and was level. A yield sign was located at the end of the entrance ramp. The speed limit was 80 kmph (50 mph). The crash occurred in the evening on a weekday, traffic was congested, and it had just stopped raining. The roadway was illuminated by intermittent streetlamps.
V1 was a 1998 Nissan Sentra with 3 occupants. The driver was an 18-year old female. V1 was traveling eastbound on the off-ramp when it collided with V2, which was stopped in the lane waiting to merge into traffic. V2 was then pushed into the rear of V3, which was also waiting to merge into traffic. V2 was a 1996 Chevrolet Cavalier that was driven by a 21-year old female and V3 was a 2000 Chrysler Town & Country minivan that was driven by a 19-year old female. The driver of V1 could not be located for an interview and only a surrogate interview was obtained for V3. A full interview was obtained from the driver of V2. Police Reported Travel Speed is 64 kmph for V1 and 64 kmph for V2.
V3 was not involved in the first harmful event. Therefore, no Critical Pre-crash Event or Critical Reason was coded to this vehicle. Heavy traffic congestion was the only associated factor for this vehicle.
