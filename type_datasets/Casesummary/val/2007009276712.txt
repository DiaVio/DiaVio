The crash occurred on a 4-lane roadway divided by a painted median at the entrance/exit to a nursing home/rehabilitation center. The north/south roadway was level, bituminous and straight with two lanes in each direction. There was no traffic control device. The posted speed limit was 56 kmph (35 mph). It was the late afternoon shortly before rush hour. Traffic flow was moderate.
V1 was a 2000 Ford Expedition utility vehicle driven by a 32-year-old male who was traveling alone. V1 was exiting out of the nursing home driveway attempting a left onto the southbound roadway. V2 was a 1997 Ford Taurus 4-door sedan that was traveling north in the second lane of travel at a constant rate of speed. When V1 entered the roadway, its front struck the right front of V2. V1 rotated slightly in a counterclockwise direction and the front of V1 struck the mid-to-rear right side of V2. V2 came to a rest in its original travel lane. Upon arrival, V2 was being lifted onto tow truck and driver of V2 was already transported to hospital. The driver of V1 stated that he had just visited a family member at the nursing home and was leaving to go home. He said he had looked left before he pulled out but then he looked right and pulled out. He stated when he turned left, he saw V2 appear out of nowhere and that was when he struck it. He said his mind must have been somewhere else since he had just visited a loved one at the nursing home and was a little saddened by their physical and mental state. The driver of V1 was not injured from the crash. The driver was cooperative about the interview but did not want pictures of the interior of his vehicle taken for security and personal protection reasons. Police Reported Travel Speed is No PAR obtained (created) for V1 and No PAR obtained (created) for V2.
V2, the Ford Taurus, was driven by an unknown age male who was transported due to possible injuries. A police report could not be located. With no police report, the driver of V2 could not be identified or interviewed.
