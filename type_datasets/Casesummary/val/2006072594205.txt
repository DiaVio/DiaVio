This two-vehicle crash occurred shortly before noon on a five-lane northbound/southbound roadway intersecting with a westbound roadway. The conditions at the time of the crash were wet roadways with light rain. The northbound roadway consists of three lanes including a left turn lane at the intersecting road while the southbound roadway consists of two lanes before and after the intersection. The speed limit for this roadway is 48 kmph (30 mph). The westbound roadway is a one-way two-lane roadway, with a painted median separating the turn entrances for northbound and southbound vehicles. The northbound roadway curves to the left as it approaches the intersection. The southbound roadway curves to the right & then left near the intersection. The super elevation in the curves is unknown. Both are level and made of bituminous asphalt.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2002 Chrysler PT Cruiser carrying two occupants, was traveling southbound in an unknown lane at an unknown rate of speed. No pre-crash information was obtained directly from the driver after numerous failed attempts to get an interview. Vehicle two (V2), a 1985 Mercedes-Benz 190E carrying one occupant, was traveling northbound in lane three of the roadway, which is the left turn lane. The driver of V2 conveyed that she was clear to turn after noticing an oncoming bus making a stop to pick up passengers. She proceeded to make the left turn when the front of V1 struck the right side of V2. Both vehicles came to rest in the intersection. Both vehicles were towed from the scene. The driver of V2 and the passenger in V1 were transported to a local trauma center. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
