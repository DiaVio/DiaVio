This single vehicle crash occurred on a four-lane, divided, north/south roadway. There are two travel lanes for southbound traffic. The roadway was straight, level, dry and of asphalt construction. Southbound lane one is bordered by a 3.6-meter (12 feet) wide gravel shoulder followed by a steep down slope. The posted speed limit is 64 kmph (40 mph). Traffic was light at the time of the crash. This crash occurred during daylight hours with clear skies on a weekend.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1986 Chevrolet Silverado pickup, was southbound in the first travel lane. V1 began to rotated clockwise and entered the west side gravel shoulder. The vehicle was sliding with the left side leading as it entered the downhill embankment and the vehicle rolled two-quarter turns. It came to rest on its top facing north. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.
The driver of V1, a sixteen-year-old male, was heading to his cousin's house from his grandmother's residence. This driver had been driving for about ten minutes, drives the route rarely and is comfortable with the vehicle despite only driving the vehicle three times. The driver is currently attending a driver's education course, but is not licensed to drive.
