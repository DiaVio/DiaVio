This two-vehicle crash occurred in a traffic signal-controlled intersection. The north/south roadway is divided by a curbed median. The northbound approach lanes have a 4.1% uphill grade and consist of three through lanes and a left turn lane. The southbound approach lanes have a 4.1% downhill grade and consist of three through lanes and a left turn lane. The speed limit on this roadway is 48 kmph (30 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2005 Ford Taurus, was stopped southbound in lane four waiting to turn left. Vehicle two (V2), 2006 Scion TC, was northbound in lane one. There were stopped vehicles in northbound lanes two, three and four. V1 turned left and the right side was struck by the front of V2. V2 continued to the northeast and the front struck a tree and the left side struck the concrete base of a utility pole. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, a fifty-four-year-old female, was traveling from her home to work. She was healthy, well rested and familiar with her vehicle. She drives this route daily. This driver stated that the northbound stopped vehicle drivers "waved me through."
The driver of V2, a forty-seven-year-old female, was traveling from her friend's home to work. She was well rested, in good health and comfortable with her vehicle. This driver travels on this roadway several times per month.
