This two-vehicle crash occurred in the morning during daylight hours. The location was a busy interstate highway. The roadway was a bituminous two-lane divided roadway. The impact location is just south of an overhead bridge for the light rail. The weather was clear and dry, and visibility was clear. Traffic was congested with the normal morning commute, and a possibly moving a little slower with the distraction of a previous crash on the opposite side of the highway. The posted speed limit was 97 kmph (60 mph).
Vehicle one (V1), a 2005 Ford Explorer 4 door SUV driven by a sixty two (62) year old male, was traveling southbound in lane two, approaching much slower traffic. Vehicle two (V2), a 1998 Ford Escort sedan driven by a twenty five (25) year old male, was southbound ahead of V1, in lane two. As V1 came around the slight curve to the right, the driver noticed a previous crash on the other side of the road, and was looking left outside his driver's side window. When he looked forward again V2 had stopped for traffic, V1 braked but was unable to stop and ran into the rear of V2. Both vehicles were towed due to damage.
