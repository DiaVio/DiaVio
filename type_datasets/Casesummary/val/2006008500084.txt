This crash occurred in an intersection of two 2-lane undivided roads. The eastbound roadway was an uphill grade of 5 percent, while the northbound roadway was a downhill grade of 14 percent. The intersection itself was level. The north/south roadway was controlled by stop signs; the east/west roadway had no traffic control device. All roadways were dry and bituminous with a posted speed limit of 40 kmph (25 mph). It was mid-morning on a weekday and conditions were clear.
V1 was a 2003 Mitsubishi Gallant, driven by a 27-year-old male, traveling northbound in lane one. V2 was a 2000 Ford Ranger, driven by a 33-year-old male, that was traveling west. The driver of V1 stopped at the stop sign and then proceeded into the intersection as V2 was traveling westbound through the intersection. The front of V1 struck the left rear of V2, which sent V2 into a counterclockwise spin and its right rear struck a telephone pole on the side of the road. Police Reported Travel Speed is 8 kmph for V1 and 8 kmph for V2.
