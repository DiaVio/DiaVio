This is a single vehicle crash that occurred in the morning during darkness on a rural two-lane, two-way, bituminous roadway. The roadway at this point has a long curve left with a warning sign, and becomes a straight and level section. The weather was clear and not a factor. There were no roadway deficiencies and the posted speed limit is 56 kmph (35 mph).
Vehicle one (V1) a 2004 Nissan Pathfinder Armada 4 door SUV driven by a 38 year-old female that was traversing a curve right while traveling east in the number one lane. The driver failed to negotiate the curve, departed the roadway on the right road edge, struck a warning sign with the front plane and went up a slight embankment and into a dry concrete canal 2.5 meters (8.2 feet) wide. V1 traveled east in the canal, struck a metal walkway and continued more than 100 meters (328 feet) in the canal causing damage to the tires, wheels, and undercarriage. The driver exited the vehicle and was found lying on the ground in a fetal position near the crash scene. The driver was transported to a local medical facility where she was interviewed later in the day. V1 was towed from the scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is 64 kmph for V1.
The police report indicated the driver tested positive with a 0.245 BAC test result. The driver is having family & personal problems and had a disagreement within the 12 hours preceding the crash. She admitted to feeling fatigued prior to the crash but could not recall her hours of recent sleep. The driver is unemployed and did not remember anything about the crash.
