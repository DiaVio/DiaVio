This two-vehicle collision occurred during dawn hours on a weekday. The crash occurred at the intersection of a north-south roadway and an east-west roadway. The two roadways are not at right angles to each other, and the intersection is controlled by a tri-light traffic signal. Both roadways are designated 56 kmph (35 mph) zones. At the time of the crash, there were no adverse atmospheric conditions, and the roadway was free of defects.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1999 Mercedes E320 four-door sedan, was traveling westbound in the fourth lane of an eight-lane, two-way, undivided, dry, level, bituminous roadway, approaching an intersection with the intention of passing straight through. Vehicle two (V2), a 1992 Mercedes 300SE four-door sedan, was traveling southbound in the fourth lane of a six-lane, two-way, undivided, dry, level, bituminous roadway, approaching the same intersection with the intention of turning left into eastbound traffic. As both vehicles entered the intersection, the front of V1 impacted the left side of V2. Both vehicles deflected northwest, and V1 departed the road over the northwest corner, where the front of V1 impacted a steel pole, bringing the vehicle to rest. V2 came to rest facing northeast in the intersection. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
