This two-vehicle crash occurred in a three-legged, traffic signal-controlled intersection. The north/south roadway is level, undivided and consists of two through lanes in each direction. The southbound approach is straight and the northbound approach is curved to the left (radius of curvature is 379 meters and the superelevation is four percent). The speed limit on this roadway is 56 kmph (35mph). The intersecting roadway is east of the north/south roadway. This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1996 Honda Accord, was southbound in lane two. Vehicle two (V2), a 2002 Jeep Liberty, was northbound in lane two. V1 turned left in the intersection and the right side was struck by the front of V2. V1 rotated clockwise and the left rear wheel struck the curb. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, a twenty-four-year-old male, was traveling, with a twenty-year-old male passenger, from his home to his office. He was well rested, healthy and comfortable with his vehicle and passenger. He travels this route daily. This driver stated that he did not see V2 until just prior to the crash and accelerated in an attempt to avoid the crash.
The driver of V2, a thirty-eight-year-old female, was on her way home from work. She was well rested, healthy and familiar with her vehicle. She drives this route daily. This driver has allergies and takes Zyrtec and vitamins. She stated that she realized that V1 was going to turn and applied the brakes (full ABS application) in an attempt to avoid the crash.
