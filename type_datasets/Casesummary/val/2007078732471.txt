This is a single rollover that occurred in the two westbound lanes of a two-way, four-lane interstate roadway. There are two westbound and two eastbound lanes that are separated by a depressed earth median. The speed limit on the roadway is posted 105 kmph (65 mph). It was an afternoon crash (daylight hours) during a weekday. The crash occurred on a U.S. highway inside the city limits. The roadway was dry with blowing dust and severe cross winds. The winds were in excess of 80 kmph (50 mph) at the time of the crash.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2007 Chrysler Sebring 4-door sedan with 5 occupants, was traveling westbound in the #2 lane traveling on a curved road to the right. The vehicle departed the left side of the roadway and traveled on the left shoulder for 35 meters (116 feet). The driver then steered to the right and began to rotate clockwise as it skids laterally. While on the left shoulder the left rear plane of V1 near the tire struck a small metal pole. The vehicle traveled back onto the roadway in a counterclockwise yaw and tripped on its left wheels near the center of the westbound lanes. The vehicle rolled for approximately 35 meters (115 feet) before it came to rest on its top facing in a southerly direction. All 5 occupants were transported from the scene by EMS. The vehicle was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.
A healthy 25-year old male drove V1, a rental vehicle. The driver resided in an adjacent State and could not be located for an interview. All driver information obtained from the police report and medical records. The driver told police that he was traveling westbound and a gust of wind pushed his vehicle into the median. He said that he tried to steer the vehicle back onto the roadway but the vehicle began to roll after returning to the roadway. Five occupants occupied the vehicle and the total weight of the occupants was approximately 411 kilograms (1100 pounds). There were also clothes inside the vehicle that weighed approximately 75 kilograms (200 pounds). There was a high wind advisory in the area of the crash for high winds in excess of 80 kmph (50 mph). There also heavy dust in the area due to the location. The driver takes no prescribed medications and had no medical conditions. He tested negative for alcohol consumption but positive for marijuana. His hours of sleep and work prior to the crash are unknown, however, the EMS report stated that the driver told them he possibly dozed off prior to losing control of the vehicle. The medical report indicated the driver's last meal was 4 hours before the collision occurred.
