This two-vehicle crash occurred during mid-afternoon daylight hours on a cloudy day in a, traffic signal controlled, four-way intersection. The southbound roadway is bituminous, straight, and level and consists of two through lanes and three left turn lanes that are divided from northbound lanes by a raised concrete curbed median. The eastbound roadway is bituminous, straight, level, one-way and consists of one left turn lane, one right turn lane and a middle lane from which one may proceed straight or turn in either direction. The posted speed limit for the southbound road is 56 kmph (35 mph) and the eastbound road has an advisory speed limit of 72 kmph (45 mph). At the time of the crash the road surfaces were dry, and there were no adverse weather conditions, road defects or sight restrictions.
Vehicle one (V1), a maroon 2001 Chrysler PT Cruiser 4-door, was southbound in lane two, approaching the intersection intending to go straight. As V1 entered the intersection, the V1 driver, a 48-year-old male, noticed V2 enter the intersection and applied his brakes, but was unable to avoid the collision and the front of V1 and left side of V2 collided in the intersection. The impact redirected V1 to the southeast and V1 came to rest near the middle of the intersection facing generally southeast. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
Vehicle two (V2), a black 1998 Nissan Sentra 4-door, was eastbound in lane two, was stopped for a red light waiting to turn left. The V2 driver, a 21-year-old female entered the intersection when the light turned green and was struck on the driver side by V1. After the impact with V1, V2 departed on an east/southeast trajectory and came to rest in the middle of the intersection facing east/southeast.
V1 was towed due to moderate frontal damage. V2 was towed from the crash scene due to moderate left side damage.
