This crash occurred just prior to a 4-way controlled intersection. The east/west approach of the intersection are controlled by a stop sign and in a two lane undivided roadway with one lane going in each direction. The north/south approach is a 2-lane uncontrolled undivided roadway with one lane going in each direction. All the roadways involved are straight, level, bituminous with a posted speed limit of 56 kmph (35 mph). This crash occurred in the mid-afternoon of a weekday. The skies were clear and roads were wet. The roads were slightly congested with some stop-and-go traffic activity.
V1, a 1997 Mazda MPV minivan with one occupant was traveling south in lane one directly behind V2. V2, a 2000 Honda CRV sport utility vehicle with two occupants was traveling south in lane one behind a landscaping vehicle. All three vehicles are traveling south in lane one. All of a sudden the landscaping vehicle (non-contact vehicle) came to a sudden stop for ducks crossing the roads and V2 consequently decelerated and came to a stop. V1 was unable to stop in time and struck the rear of V2. Both vehicles came to final rest at approximately the point of impact. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
V1, the Mazda MPV was driven by a 28-year-old female who reported no injuries. The driver was unable to be successfully contacted for an interview at the time of case release. V1 was traveling south in lane one directly behind V2. When V2 came to a sudden stop, V1 was able to stop in time and struck the rear of V2.
V2, the Honda Cr-V was driven by a 51-year-old female with who reported "C" injuries but refused medical treatment. The driver stated that she was on her way from her home to the store to run some errands. She was driving behind a non-contact landscaping vehicle and in front of V1. All of a sudden the landscaping vehicle suddenly stopped and V2 consequently stopped. After the driver came to a stop she was immediately struck from behind by V1.
