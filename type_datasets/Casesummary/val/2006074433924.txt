This two-vehicle collision occurred at the intersection of two two-lane roadways. The east/west road is controlled by a stop sign. For westbound traffic, there is an 8% grade leading into the intersection. The east/west road has a posted speed limit of 40 kmph (25 mph). The north/south road is downhill at a -5% grade and has a posted speed limit of 48 kmph (30 mph). This collision occurred during evening hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1991 Oldsmobile Cutlass Cierra is westbound stopped at the stop sign. Vehicle two (V2), a 2005 International flatbed tow truck is northbound in the first lane. V1 stopped at the stop sign and pulled forward to see. V1 turned left and the front of V2 impacted the left of V1. V1 rotated and the back left of V1 impacted the right side of V2. Both vehicles came to rest north of the intersection. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, a forty-five year old male was heading home after visiting with his boss. This driver had just begun this trip. This driver does not take any medications but had consumed alcohol before the trip. On-scene it was over heard that this driver had a BAC of 0.11 from a breath test. This driver stated that he stopped at the stop sign and then pulled forward to see traffic. There is a large tree to this drivers left. After the driver pulled forward, he was able to see around the tree. This driver stated that he saw the other vehicles and thought he had time to turn. This driver drives this vehicle daily and over this road on a daily basis.
The driver of V2, a forty-five year old female was on her way to a restaurant to meet her boyfriend. This driver had just left the gym. This driver takes Claritin and Nexium as medications. This driver stated that she felt hot and hungry at the time of the crash. This driver drives this vehicle daily and over this road on a daily basis.
