This crash occurred in the southbound lane of a two-lane undivided roadway at an intersection with a two-lane east/west roadway. The southbound lane curved to the left and was level. There was no traffic control device and the posted speed limit was 56 kmph (35 mph). The east/west roadway was controlled by a stop sign. The crash occurred in the early morning of a weekday. The roads were wet and skies were dark and cloudy. The roads were congested with a lot of stop-and-go traffic activity.
V1, a 2007 Ford F-150 pick-up truck, was traveling south in lane one following V2. V2, a 1994 Lincoln Continental 4-door sedan, was traveling south in lane one following V3, a 2005 Ford E-series van. V3 was traveling south in lane one intending on turning left at the intersection. All the vehicles were following each other when V3 decelerated and stopped to make a left turn at the intersection. V2 decelerated as well and stopped behind V3. V1 was not able to stop in time and struck the rear of V2. This impact pushed V2's front into the rear of V3. All the vehicles came to final rest at approximately the point of impact. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
V1, the Ford F-150, was driven by a 60-year-old male who reported no injuries. The driver stated that he was on his way to work. The roads were wet and congested and when V2 stopped suddenly he attempted to stop but was unsuccessful due to the wet roads.
V2, the Lincoln Town car, was driven by a 68-year-old male who reported no injuries. Two passengers of V2, a 59-year-old female and a 44-year-old female, were transported to a medical facility with complaints of pain. A 24-year-old male passenger reported no injuries. The driver of V2 was the driver of a medical transport vehicle and was traveling south in lane one behind V3. When V3 stopped to make a left, V2 slowed down as well and stopped. Then V2 was struck from behind by V1.
V3, the 2005 Ford E-series van, was driven by a 37-year-old male who reported no injuries. The driver was running errands and returning home. As he approached his block, he slowed down and came to a stop. He was stopped and waiting for traffic to clear to turn left when he was struck from behind by V2.
No Critical Pre-crash Event and no Critical Reason were coded for V3 because V3 was not involved in the first harmful event. The only associated factors that were coded to this vehicle were wet roads, rain, and traffic congestion.
