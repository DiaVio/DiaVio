This two-vehicle crash occurred on a straight, level, undivided roadway. This roadway consists of one lane in each direction and paved shoulders on both sides. The speed limit on this roadway is 89 kmph (55 mph). It was dark at the time of the crash with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1991 Ford F150 pickup towing a travel trailer, was northbound in lane one. Vehicle two (V2), a 2002 Oldsmobile Alero, was northbound in lane one behind V1. V1 drove off the right side of the roadway and stopped. V1 then entered the roadway, making a u-turn. V2 traveled into the southbound lane and the front struck the left side of V1. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, a forty-year-old male, was traveling with a thirty-year-old female. This driver was not interviewed, but according to the police report, he had been working on the trailer brake lights and was making a u-turn to travel southbound. No further information is available for this driver.
The driver of V2, a twenty-eight-year-old female, was not interviewed; no further information is available for this driver. The physical evidence at the scene indicates that this driver steered left in an attempt to avoid the crash.
