This single-vehicle crash occurred near a four-legged intersection. The north/south roadway is straight, and consists of one lane in each direction. The northbound approach has a five percent uphill grade. The speed limit on this roadway is 56 kmph (35 mph). It was dark with street lights on at the time of the crash and there were no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1997 Achieva, was northbound in lane one. The driver of V1 attempted to make a left turn at the intersection. V1 skidded, traveled to the right and the right front tire/wheel struck the northwest corner curb. V1 continue in a northwestern direction into a yard and the front struck landscape timbers that were twenty centimeters high. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.
The driver of V1, a thirty-two-year-old female, was traveling with a thirty-five-year-old male. They had just left a gas station and were on their way home. They were in a hurry at the time of the crash and when this driver realized that they were on the wrong road, she made an abrupt left turn. This driver was distracted by the very loud music in the vehicle. She was well rested, in good health and comfortable with her passenger. This driver rarely drives this route. This driver stated that her vehicle had recently been stolen and recovered; she was not comfortable driving this vehicle due to damage from this incident.
