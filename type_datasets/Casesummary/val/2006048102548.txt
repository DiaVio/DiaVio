This single-vehicle crash occurred at dawn with no adverse atmospheric conditions. The roadway is divided, level and straight, and was wet at the time of the crash. The southbound lanes consist of two through lanes and a left turn lane. The speed limit on this roadway is 72 kmph (45 mph).
Vehicle one (V1), a 2004 Dodge Ram pickup, was southbound in lane one. V1 traveled off the right side of the roadway and the front struck a gas main pipe and a building. V1 continued into the building and the left side struck the building. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 72 kmph for V1.
The driver of V1, a twenty-seven-year-old male, was not interviewed at the scene and could not be located later. At the scene, this driver told a police officer that his vehicle was home in his garage. An EMS technician on the scene stated that the driver had a hypodermic needle in his arm, injecting a substance believed to be methamphetamines, at the time of the crash.
