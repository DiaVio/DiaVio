This three-vehicle crash occurred in a four-leg intersection. The east/west roadway is undivided, level and straight and is controlled by stop signs. The speed limit on this roadway is 48 kmph (30 mph). The southbound approach roadway is level and straight. This roadway consists of one lane in each direction and a left turn lane. A raised island separates this roadway from a right turn lane at the intersection. The speed limit on this roadway is 56 kmph (35 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1988 Chevrolet S10 pickup, approached the intersection and stopped westbound in lane one. Vehicle two (V2), a 1998 Chevrolet 1500 pickup was southbound in lane one. Vehicle three (V3), 2006 Ford Explorer, was stopped eastbound in lane one. V1 entered the intersection and the right side was struck by the front of V2. V2 continued in a southwestern direction and the front struck the left side of V3. V1 and V2 were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 16 kmph for V1 and 16 kmph for V2.
The driver of V1, a nineteen-year-old male, was traveling from his home to his employer's house. He was healthy, well rested and familiar with his vehicle. He drives this route daily. This driver stated that he was plugging his cell phone charger into the cigarette lighter port while he looked to see if the intersection was clear. He stated that he did not see V2 until just prior to impact.
The driver of V2, a twenty-seven-year-old male, was traveling, with a twenty-eight-year-old female and three young children, from a bank to his home. This driver was well rested, in good health and comfortable with his vehicle and passengers. He travels on this roadway several times each month. This driver stated that he saw V1 but assumed that he would yield the right-of-way. When he saw V1 entering the intersection, he applied the brakes in an unsuccessful avoidance maneuver.
The driver of V3, a forty-two-year-old female, was traveling with a thirty-nine-year-old female from a store to her home. She was healthy, well rested and familiar with her vehicle. This driver travels this route weekly.
