This four-vehicle crash occurred on a weekday on a wet, physically divided, bituminous, nine-lane, right curve highway. Westbound traffic consists of four through lanes and one exit lane while eastbound traffic consists of four through lanes. It was late rush hour on a weekday and it was raining at the time. Traffic flow was congested with vehicles slowing down for the wet roads.
Vehicle one (V1), a Hit and Run silver hatchback was westbound, negotiating a right curve in lane three. Vehicle two (V2), a four-door 1999 Dodge Durango with one occupant, was westbound, negotiating a right curve in lane four. Vehicle three (V3), a four-door 2002 Kia Rio with one occupant, was westbound, negotiating a right curve in lane four in front of V2. Vehicle four (V4), a four-door 2005 Acura MDX was westbound, negotiating a right curve in lane four in front of V3. V1 either tried to change lanes or hydroplaned into lane four and impacted V2 on the right side. V1 then cut in front of V2 and entered lane five and left the scene. V2 continued in lane four and impacted V3 in the back with its front. V3 was pushed forward and impacted V4 in the back with its front. Vehicles two, three and four came to rest in lane five on their wheels facing west. V1 and V3 were driven from the scene, V3 and V4 was towed. The driver of V3 was transported to a local trauma center; there were no other injuries. Police Reported Travel Speed is 97 kmph for V1 and 97 kmph for V2.
