This two-vehicle crash occurred in an interchange area on a jersey barrier-divided trafficway. The roadway approaching the crash area curves to the right and then to the left, has a downhill grade and consists of three through lanes. This roadway then divides into a two-lane upper level ramp and a two-lane lower level ramp. The speed limit for this roadway is 64 kmph (40 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2003 Buick LeSabre, was westbound in lane three. Vehicle two (V2), a 2000 Ford Focus, was westbound in lane two. V2 moved to the right and the right side struck the left side of V2. V2 then continued westbound and the front struck the impact attenuator in the gore area between the ramps. V2 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, a thirty-seven-year-old male, was on his way home from work. He was healthy, well rested and familiar with his vehicle. He travels this route daily. This driver stated that he had to wait for a truck in lane two to pass before he could make his lane change. He further stated that he did not see V2 prior to the crash.
The driver of V2, a thirty-five-year-old female, was on her way from her home to work. She was well rested, healthy and familiar with her vehicle. She travels this route daily. This driver stated that she did not see V1 prior to the crash.
