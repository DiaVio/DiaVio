This single vehicle crash occurred on a straight two-lane roadway. This road has a speed limit of 105 kph (65 mph). At the time of the crash, this road was snow and slush covered. This crash occurred during daylight hours with heavy snow falling.
Vehicle one, a 2001 Ford Excursion was eastbound in the first lane. Vehicle one lost control and departed the right side of the roadway. Once off the roadway vehicle one slid across an extended dirt shoulder and across a ditch. After the ditch, vehicle one continued forward impacting two trees with the right side of the vehicle. Once vehicle one impacted the trees it came to rest against these trees. Vehicle one was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 72 kmph for V1.
The driver of vehicle one, a sixty-five year-old female was driving up to see her daughter at the time of the crash. This driver had been driving for about three hours at the time of the crash. This driver is taking quite a few medications for a heart condition. This driver stated that as she was driving the snow was getting worse and she lost traction. She had just let up on the accelerator and applied the brakes gently but still slid off the roadway. This driver drives this vehicle daily but only rarely on this road.
