This four-vehicle crash occurred at the intersection of seven-lane roadway and a five-lane roadway. Both roads are straight and level with no defects noted. The intersection is controlled by a tri-light signal that was working properly at the time. All four legs are comprised of asphalt. Both roadways have a speed limit of 56 kmph (35 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2006 Toyota Camry was westbound in the second lane. Vehicle two (V2), a 2006 Chrysler Sebring was southbound in the second lane. Vehicle three (V3), a 1997 Dodge Caravan was southbound in the second lane. Vehicle four (V4), a 1993 Toyota Tercel was stopped in the second eastbound lane. As V1 passed through the intersection its right plane was impacted by V2's front plane. V1 rotated counter clockwise and began to rollover and headed southwest where its undercarriage was impacted by V3's front. V1 rolled on to its left side and continued southwest. V1's undercarriage impacted V4's front pushing it northwest. V1 rolled back onto its wheels and continued southwest where it impacted a curb with its front left tire. V3, after its first impact, continued southwest up onto the southwest sidewalk and lawn area where its right side impacted a tree. V1 came to rest facing southwest at the south curb. V2 came to rest facing southeast in the number one lane just south of the south curb. V3 came to rest on the southwest corner facing south. V4 came to rest facing southeast in the number three westbound lane east of the intersection. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, a twenty-eight year old female, was heading home from a store. This driver had been driving for about two minutes at the time of the crash. This driver had five years of driving experience. She has only had the car for a little over a week but has driven it 6-10 times. The driver was listening to the radio at the time of the crash. She had 7.5 hours of sleep the night before. She normally gets only 6 hours. She works 47 hours a week and sometimes works a 12-hour shift. She said her light was green. She was coming from a store to buy Valentines Day gifts for her husband at the time of the crash.
The driver of V2 was a thirty-nine-year-old female with 23 years of driving experience. Her vehicle is a rental car that she has been driving for a few months now. She has an ear infection and is taking an antibiotic (augumentin).
The driver of V3, a fifty-eight year-old female, was on her way from one work location to another work location. She wears "blended bi-focals" and is both myopic and hyperopic. She has arthritis in her knees.
V3 was not involved in first harmful event, and the critical reason was not coded to this vehicle.
