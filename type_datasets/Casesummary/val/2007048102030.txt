This three-vehicle crash occurred on a grass median-divided, straight trafficway. The southbound travel lanes have a downhill grade and consist of three through lanes. The speed limit for this roadway is 105 kmph (65 mph). It was dark with streetlights on at the time of the crash. Traffic was congested and there were no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2006 Toyota Sienna, was southbound in lane three. Vehicle two (V2), a 1998 Ford Expedition, was southbound in lane three in front of V1. Vehicle three (V3), a 1997 Volvo 960, was southbound in lane two. V2 slowed down for traffic and the front of V1 struck the back of V2. V1 traveled into lane two and the back was struck by the front of V3. V1 and V2 were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 72 kmph for V1 and 72 kmph for V2.
The driver of V1, a forty-two-year-old male, was traveling with three passengers. He was not interviewed; no further information is available for this driver. The police report indicated that this vehicle was following too close for the congested traffic and that the driver applied the brakes when V2 decelerated, but was unable to avoid the crash.
The driver of V2, a sixty-seven-year-old male, was on his way home from his brother's house. He was well rested, in fairly good health and familiar with his vehicle. He travels this route daily. This driver has high blood pressure and diabetes and takes Amaryl, Toprol and Lantus.
The driver of V3, a seventeen-year-old female, was traveling from her home to a friend's house in another city. She was healthy, well rested and familiar with her vehicle. She travels on this roadway daily.
