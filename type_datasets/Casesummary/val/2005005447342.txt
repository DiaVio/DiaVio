This accident occurred on a two-way, two-lane roadway. The roadway is not physically divided, and has traffic lights for each lane at this intersection. The conditions were as follows: early morning, dawn, clear skies, the roadway was dry and there were no atmospheric conditions. The gradient for vehicle one (V1) was 4%. The gradient for vehicle two (V2) was -4%. The gradient for vehicle three (V3) was level. The posted speed limit for V1 and V2 was 72 Kmph (45 mph). The posted speed limit for V3 was 64 Kmph (40 mph).
V1 was a 2005 International Harvester 2 axle straight truck, traveling northbound, and he had a light that was changing to red at the intersection, but proceeded through. V3 was a 1990 Mercury Cougar traveling eastbound and was coming up to the intersection with a green light. V2 was a 1993 Ford Escort wagon that was stopped in the opposing traffic from V1. V1's left rear wheel struck V2 over the right front. V1 then struck the traffic light, for his lane. Then V1 went over the double yellow lines and struck the left side and rear of V2. V1 ended up on its left side across the roadway, down about 22 meters from the edge of the intersection. Police Reported Travel Speed is 72 kmph for V1 and 72 kmph for V2.
The driver of V3 was taken to a local Hospital via ambulance for possible broken bones. The Driver of V1, and V2 did not need treatment, because he did not suffer any injuries.
