This single vehicle daytime collision occurred mid-morning on the northbound lanes of a U.S. highway. The highway is dry, level, curves right, five lanes, and comprised of concrete. A raised concrete divider separates the northbound and southbound lanes. An asphalt shoulder borders the east edge of the highway. East of the eastern shoulder exists a sound wall. An asphalt median borders the west edge of the highway. The highway is designated a 104 kmph (65 mph) zone.
Vehicle one (V1) is a 2005 Chevrolet Malibu, four-door sedan, driven by a twenty (20) year old male was traveling northbound in the number four lane. As V1 attempted to change lanes to the left, it encountered an approaching vehicle. V1 partially returned to the number four lane, but encountered a motorcycle. V1 veered into the number five lane and proceeded across the lane towards the concrete center divider. The front plane of V1 contacted the concrete center divider. V1 came to rest facing northwest in the number five lane. V1 received moderate damage and was towed from the scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is 113 kmph for V1.
