The crash occurred in the westbound lanes of a three-lane roadway with a T-intersection. Conditions were dark with lights, dry with cloudy skies in the evening on a weekday. The westbound lanes had a posted speed of 80 kmph (50 mph), the southbound lane had a speed limit of 56 kmph (35 mph).
V1 was a 1998 Ford Escort sedan traveling southbound in the right lane approaching a T-intersection with a stop sign. V2, a 1993 Subaru Legacy sedan, was traveling westbound in the right lane approaching the T-intersection. V1 made a left turn at the stop sign and the front of V2 struck the left side of V1 in the westbound lane. Both vehicles came to final rest facing westbound. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
V1 was driven by a 19-year-old male who was not injured. He stated that he was lost and trying to find a church that was close by. He approached the stop sign and did not see V2 until it was too late. He turned left directly into V2 traveling between 21-30 MPH. V1 was towed due to damage.
V2 was driven by a 63-year-old female who was transported to a local hospital with minor injuries. The driver refused to be interviewed at the hospital and refused us when we visited her home. V2 remained at the scene.
