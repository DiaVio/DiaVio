This single-vehicle crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions. The roadway was divided by a jersey barrier. The northbound lanes are level with a left curve in the pre-crash area and consist of three through lanes. The speed limit on this roadway is 64 kmph (40 mph). The roadway was wet at the time of the crash.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1993 Toyota Corolla, was northbound in lane two. The driver of V1 lost control of the vehicle as he negotiated the left curve. He attempted to gain control, but the vehicle began to rotate clockwise and departed the right side of the roadway. The left side of V1 struck a tree. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.
The driver of V1, a twenty-one-year-old male, was traveling with two male passengers. This driver could not be interviewed due to the severity of his injuries. His BAC was .073 one hour after the crash. According to witnesses, this driver was speeding and weaving in traffic before the crash.
