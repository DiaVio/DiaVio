This two-vehicle rear-end collision occurred on an interstate highway. This crash occurred on an off ramp. There were three lanes of travel all in the same direction. This crash occurred during nighttime hours with additional lighting. The speed limit is posted as 97 kph (60 mph) with an advisory limit of 80 kph (50 mph). No adverse weather conditions present at the time of collision.
Vehicle one, a 1986 Chrysler New Yorker was traveling west in the first lane. Vehicle two, a 1997 Honda Passport was westbound in the first lane. Vehicle one stalled and came to a controlled stop in the first lane. The driver of vehicle one tried to get vehicle one started and in the process turned off the lights to get more power from the battery. Vehicle one was impacted on the rear plane by vehicle two. Both vehicle moved forward and came to rest in the gore of the off-ramp. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of vehicle one, a sixty-two year old female was heading to work at the time of the crash. This driver had been driving for about thirty minutes at the time of the crash. This driver stated that the vehicle died on her and stopped. The driver was attempting to start the vehicle when it was impacted. This driver was taking Seroquel as a medication. This driver drives this vehicle daily and over this road on a daily basis.
The driver of vehicle two, a thirty-year old male was driving to work at the time of the crash. This driver had been driving for about twenty minutes at the time of the crash. This driver stated that there was a car that was in front of him that swerved and missed vehicle one. This driver applied the brakes but was unable to avoid the crash. This driver drives this vehicle on a daily basis and over this road on a daily basis.
