This single vehicle-crash happened on a weekend during early morning hours on a divided trafficway. The southbound lanes are elevated, have an uphill grade, curve to the left and consist of five lanes with shoulders and concrete barriers (bridge rails) on both sides. The weather was clear and the roads dry. The posted speed limit for this roadway was 97 kmph (60 mph).
Vehicle one (V1), a 2005 Dodge Stratus, was traveling south in an unknown lane of the roadway. V1 traveled off the left side of the road and the front and then the left side struck the bridge rail. V1 re-entered the southbound travel lanes and came to rest in lanes two and three, facing west. V1 was towed from the scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.
A 30-year old male was the driver of V1. This driver was on his way home from his new part time job at night. He stated he worked 8 hours until 1 hour and 45 minutes before the crash and left his job site at about 90 minutes prior to the crash. In addition to the part time job, the driver works a regular 40-hour per week job. On the day before the crash, he slept for eleven hours and then was awake for twenty-one hours prior to the crash. According to the police report, this driver had been drinking alcohol but no BAC test was done.
