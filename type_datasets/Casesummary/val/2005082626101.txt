This two vehicle daytime collision occurred mid-afternoon. The collision occurred at an intersection comprised of a west/east roadway and a north/south roadway. Both roadways are two-way, two lane and bituminous. The east /west roadway is controlled by a stop sign and has a +10% grade. Both roadways were dry, free of congestion, poor weather conditions, and/or road deficiencies at the time of the collision. Both roadways are designated as 40 kph (25 mph) zones.
Vehicle two (V2), a 1997 Ford Escort wagon, driven by a 52 year-old female was traveling southbound in lane one. Vehicle one (V1), a 1993 Nissan Quest Van, driven by a 19 year-old male was stopped at the stop sign heading east, then proceeded to enter the intersection The right front quarter panel of V2 struck the front left corner of V1. V1 was spun clockwise approximately 50 degrees before coming to rest. V1's bumper was ripped off and landed approximately 15 meters south of the crash. V2 was deflected to the left and came to rest south of the intersection in the northbound lane pointing south. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The pre-event movement for V1 coded stopped in traffic lane, category this vehicle traveling crossing over passing through intersection. Critical reason environment related factor "View obstructed by other vehicles".
The pre-event movement for V2 coded going straight, category other vehicle encroachment from crossing street across path. Critical reason not coded to this vehicle.
.
NOTE; At the time of the crash it was noted that a vehicle was parked at the northwest corner of the intersection. There is a "No parking within 30 ft" sign. However this vehicle was parked within 30 ft. It is determined the vehicle did block V1 driver's view, and quite likely V2's view as well.
