The crash occurred on an access ramp that ran from west to south, from a side road to a primary arterial. There was only one lane to the ramp with a speed limit of 48kph (30mph). The ramp was curved to the right with a measured radius of 148m. It was super-elevated to 4.4%. There were no traffic controls. Conditions at the time were daylight and dry on a weekday around mid afternoon. Traffic was relatively light.
V1 was a 1999 Acura 3.5RL traveling on the ramp when it s front hit the back of V2, a 1995 Toyota 4-Runner that had stopped on the ramp due to a non contact vehicle in front of it stopping to apparently merge. Both vehicles came to rest within a meter of impact. Both vehicles were towed from the scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
V1 was driven by a twenty-year old belted male who was not injured in the crash. He was on his way from school to pickup another vehicle of his at a repair shop.
V2 was driven by a belted thirty-seven year old male who was transported due to possible injury to an area hospital. His right front female passenger was restrained and not injured.
