The crash occurred in the middle of a four-way intersection. The southbound roadway was curved to the right and the east/west roadways were controlled with a stop sign. The north/south roadway had no traffic device. The roadway was in a residential neighborhood and traffic flow was slow. The conditions were dry and clear on a weekday morning. The posted speed was 56 kmph (35 mph).
V1 was a 2006 Mercury Milan that was traveling westbound in lane one. The driver did not stop for a stop sign and hit the left rear side of V2, a 2006 Hyundai Elantra, that was traveling southbound in lane one. V2 rotated counterclockwise and hit a tree in the median making final rest facing a southwesterly direction. The driver of V2 was transported to a local hospital with injuries and V2 was towed due to damages. The driver of V1 was not injured and V1 had minor bumper damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Unknown for V1 and Unknown for V2.
V1 was driven by an 89-year-old woman who was on her way to meet her friend for their weekly lunch date. The driver stated that she was driving and did not see the stop sign. She also stated that since the roadway was curved she never saw V2 prior to hitting her. The driver stated that she had never been in a crash in all her driving years. The driver was cited for the crash for failure to stop at a traffic control device.
V2 was driven by a 60-year-old woman who was traveling southbound in lane one. The driver was traveling along at the speed limit when V1 just cut across the intersection and hit the left side. The driver of V2 tried to accelerate to the right to avoid V1 but was unable to avoid the crash. V2 rotated counterclockwise and yawed till it made contact with a tree in the median. V2 hit the tree with the right front and also the right rear tire. The vehicle made final rest facing southwest. The vehicle was towed and the driver was injured.
