This two-vehicle collision occurred on a five-lane two-way arterial street. There are three lanes headed south, and the crash occurred in the second of these three lanes. The lanes are paved with bituminous asphalt and are straight and level in the area of the crash. At the time of the crash there was a light drizzle, enough to wet the roadway but not enough to hamper vision. The speed limit for the road is 56 kmph (35 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1991 Toyota Corolla was southbound in the second lane. Vehicle two (V2), a 2006 Mazda pickup truck was southbound in the second lane ahead of V1. V2 was stopped. The front of V1 impacted the rear of V2. V1 was towed due to disabling damage, but V2 was driven from the scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, a twenty-two year old female, stated that she was heading from work to a friends home. This driver had been driving for almost one hour at the time of the crash. This driver indicated that she was looking at the road signs, trying to figure out where to go. She has never driven through this part of town before. When she looked back to the traffic in front of her , it had stopped. She slammed on her brakes and skidded into the back of V2. This driver is taking an unknown medication for migraines and an unknown medication for seizures.
The driver of V2, a twenty-two year old female, was unable to be reached for an interview.
