This two-vehicle crash occurred during early morning daylight hours on a two way, two lane, dry bituminous asphalt roadway. The crash occurred at an intersection that is controlled by stop signs. The through roadway has one northbound and one southbound lane. The lanes are separated by a painted centerline stripe. The cross street is constructed of dry, hard pack dirt and has no centerline to divide the roadway. The speed limit on the through street is posted 80 kmph (50 mph). There were no environmental factors that contributed to this crash and there were no obstructions or sight line restrictions noted. There is no posted speed limit on the cross street.
Vehicle one (V1,) a 1997 Ford Expedition SUV driven by a 29 year-old female, was traveling northbound on the roadway to the rear of vehicle two (V2), a 1997 Freight Liner tractor pulling one flat bed trailer. V2 moved over into the southbound lane to make a right turn, V1 thought V2 was turning left and continued to pass when V2 turned right. The front of V1 collided with the right side of V2. Both vehicles came to final rest at the at point of impact. V1 was towed from the scene, V2 was driven from the scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is 89 kmph for V1 and 89 kmph for V2.
