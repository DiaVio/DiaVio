This crash occurred just after noon on a weekday in the middle of a four-way suburban intersection. The intersection consisted of two, two-way, three lane roadways controlled by a properly function traffic signal. The east/west roadway was dry, straight and level. The north/south roadway was dry and straight with a grade greater than 2%. The posted speed limit for both roadways was 72kmph (45 mph). The roadway was dry and there were no adverse atmospheric conditions at the time of the crash.
V1, a 1992 Chevrolet Lumina, was eastbound approaching the intersection the intersection where it intended to go straight. V2, a 2002 Chevrolet Malibu, was southbound approaching the same intersection where it also intended to go straight. V3, a 1987 Toyota Camry, was northbound approaching the intersection as well where it intended to pass through. V1 failed to stop for the red light and struck the right side of V2 as it crossed over the intersection. The impact then caused V2 to veer into V3. V3 came to final rest in its original lane of travel facing north. V2 came to final rest facing south in the northbound lane. V1 came to final rest facing east in the eastbound lanes. V1 and V2 were towed from the scene due to damage. V3 was driven from the scene shortly after the researcher's arrival. Police Reported Travel Speed is Unknown for V1 and Unknown for V2.
The driver of V1, an 87-year-old female, sustained unknown injuries and was airlifted to a medical facility for treatment. There were no other occupants in the vehicle when the crash occurred. Numerous attempts to contact the driver could not produce an interview.
The driver of V2, a 20-year-old female, suffered unknown injuries, and was transported to the hospital for observation. Police informed the researcher that she was pregnant. There were no other occupants in the vehicle when the crash occurred. An interview could not be obtained from this driver.
The driver of V3, a 41-year-old female was not injured as a result of the crash. There were three other occupants present in the vehicle when the crash occurred; none of which suffered any injuries. The driver of this vehicle could not be contacted for an interview.
