V1, a 2002 Chevrolet Trailblazer, was westbound in lane 3 of three westbound lanes of a dry, bituminous, level, interstate roadway that curved gradually left. At the time of the crash it was dark unlighted. A non-contact vehicle ahead of v1 was traveling just ahead of v1 in lane 2. The non-contact vehicle veered left abruptly and v1 exited the roadway to the left. V1, front, struck the median guardrail, rotated counterclockwise 360 degrees and came to rest facing the guardrail. The single occupant of v1 was injured and transported. V1 was towed due to damage.
A complete driver interview and vehicle inspection was obtained for v1. The driver was properly licensed for a familiar with the vehicle but not familiar with the roadway. She was exceeding the posted speed limit by 5 to 8 miles per hour. The Pre-event movement of v1 was negotiating a curve, the Critical pre-crash event was This Vehicle Traveling off the edge of the road on the left side and the Critical reason for the critical pre-crash event was an incorrect evasive action. Some factors contributing to the crash include unfamiliarity with the roadway, choosing inappropriate/unsuccessful evasive action and overcompensation. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.
