The two-vehicle crash occurred in an interchange area of a jersey barrier-divided, access-controlled expressway. The northbound lanes in this area are downhill and curve slightly to the left. At the crash location, there are three through lanes, a lane that can be used as a through lane or an exit lane and one exit lane. The speed limit on this roadway is 97 kmph (60 mph). There is an advisory speed limit of 76 kmph (45 mph) for the exit lanes. This crash took place during daylight hours and there were no adverse atmospheric conditions at the time.
Vehicle one, a 1984 Oldsmobile Cutlass, was northbound in lane three, and the driver was intending to move to lane one (right exit lane). Vehicle two, a 1996 Mack truck with a full load of cement, was northbound in lane three behind vehicle one. The driver of vehicle two also intended to move to lane one to exit the freeway. Both vehicles moved in to the number two lane and vehicle one decelerated in an attempt to move in to the number one lane. The driver of vehicle two noticed vehicle one slowing and applied the brakes, skidding longitudinally. The front of vehicle two struck the back of vehicle one. Vehicle one came to rest facing north in lane two and was towed due to damage. Vehicle two came to rest on the left shoulder. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of vehicle one, a twenty-four-year-old male, had stopped at his friend's house after work and was on his way home at the time of the crash. He was familiar with his route, but was not comfortable with the traffic flow or the speed of the traffic at this interchange. He was well rested and comfortable with his vehicle. He was concentrating on changing lanes to exit the freeway and did not notice vehicle two behind him.
The driver of vehicle two, a fifty-year-old male, was on his way from loading his cement truck at the plant, intending to deliver the cement to a job site. He was well rested and very familiar with his truck. He also stated that although he was familiar with this roadway, he was uncomfortable with the speed, volume and general traffic flow in this area. He stated that vehicle one "came out of nowhere" but due to his speed and his load, he was unable to avoid the collision.
