This two-vehicle crash occurred on an eight-lane, median divided, bituminous roadway with no barrier. Just prior to the crash the road has five-lanes in the eastbound direction and at the point of impact there are four-lanes. The crash occurred approximately 120 m from a traffic device controlled intersection. The roadway is straight, level with a speed limit of 80 kph (50 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1997 Chevrolet Caviler was eastbound in the third lane. Vehicle two (V2), a 1993 Ford Aerostar minivan was eastbound in the third lane ahead of V1. V2 decelerated due to traffic slowing. The front of V1 impacted the rear of V2. V1 was towed due to damage. V2 was not towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 72 kmph for V1 and 72 kmph for V2.
The driver of V1, a twenty-four year old male was not interviewed. There is very little pre-crash information known for this driver. The driver was tested for alcohol and had a BAC of .206. A witness saw the crash and stated that the driver was looking straight ahead. V1 had low tread depth on all four tires.
The driver of V2, a fifty-two year old male was coming from a repair shop just down the street on the way to the bank. This driver indicated that he had just started driving a few minutes before the crash. This driver indicated that he was slowing due to the traffic signal and stopped traffic ahead of him. This driver drives this vehicle daily and over this road on a daily basis.
