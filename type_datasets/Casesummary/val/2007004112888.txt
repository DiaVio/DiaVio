This crash occurred in the northbound lane of a two lane undivided roadway. There was one southbound lane and one northbound lane. This was a straight, level, and bituminous roadway with a posted speed limit of 56 kmph (35 mph). The crash occurred in the evening on a weekend. The roads were dry and skies were clear and the roads were well lit. Traffic flow was steady.
V1, a 2002 Chevrolet Trailblazer Sport Utility Vehicle, was traveling south in lane one intending on continuing south. V2, a 2003 Infiniti Q45 4-door, was traveling north in lane one intending on continuing north. As V1 was traveling south, it suddenly swerved over the left lane line into oncoming traffic just as V2 was traveling north. Both vehicles were unable to avoid a collision and the front of V1 struck the front of V2. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
V1, the Chevy Trailblazer, was driven by a 65-year-old male who reported no injuries. According to the surrogate interview, provided by the investigating police officer, the driver stated that he was driving south in lane one and does remember exactly how the crash occurred. He felt that he began to cough and thus swerved into the oncoming traffic lane. The officer also stated that the driver has cancer and has been undergoing treatment and chemotherapy and that might have played a role in the crash as well as his inability to remember exactly what happened.
V2, the Infiniti Q45, was driven by a 57-year-old male who reported moderate injury and was transported to a medical facility. His passenger, a 59-year-old female reported possible injuries and was also transported to a medical facility. The driver was traveling north in lane one when V1 swerved into his lane in front of him.
