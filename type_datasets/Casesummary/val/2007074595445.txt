This two-vehicle crash occurred on a wet, five-lane, level, straight roadway. This roadway consists of two lanes in each direction with a center left-turn lane. The speed limit for this roadway is 56 kmph (35 mph). It was dark with streetlights on with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1999 Ford Explorer, was northbound in lane one. Vehicle two (V2), a 1997 GMC Jimmy, was southbound in lane two. V1 lost control due to a bump on the wet road and traveled into lane two. The driver steered right and accelerated, traveling into lane one. The driver of V1 then steered left and accelerated, traveling to the left into southbound lane two. The left side of V1 was struck by the front of V2. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, a fifty-four-year-old male, was on his way home. He travels this route daily. This driver stated that when he drove over a bump in the road, the back of his vehicle slid to the left. He accelerated and steered left and then right in an attempt to regain control.
The driver of V2, a nineteen-year-old male, was traveling with a nineteen-year-old female. They had left home and were on their way to a store. This driver was well rested, healthy and comfortable with his vehicle and passenger. He stated that he applied the brakes and steered to the left in an attempt to avoid the crash.
