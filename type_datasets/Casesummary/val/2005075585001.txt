This two-vehicle crash occurred during daylight hours on a dry, straight, level, undivided, eight-lane, state highway near a traffic light-controlled four-leg intersection. The roadway runs north and south at the location of the crash and has one right turn lane, two through lanes and two left turn lanes. The speed limit on this roadway is 64 kmph (40 mph) and there were no adverse weather conditions at the time.
Vehicle one (V1), a hit-and-run Ford F-150, was northbound in lane three, the inside through lane. Vehicle two (V2), a 2002 Chevrolet Cavalier, was northbound in lane two, the outside through lane. The vehicles passed through the intersection and V1 changed lanes to the right due to an emergency vehicle approaching from the rear. The front of V2 struck the back of V1. V1 drove away from the scene. V2 came to rest facing northeast at the curb and was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 8 kmph for V1 and 8 kmph for V2.
No details are known about the driver of V1.
