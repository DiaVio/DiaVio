This two-vehicle crash occurred in a four-legged, traffic signal-controlled intersection. Both roadways are level and straight and consist of one lane in each direction with a left turn lane at the intersection. The speed limit on the east/west roadway east of the intersection is 56 kmph (35 mph). The speed limit on this roadway west of the intersection is 40 kmph (25 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1999 Dodge Dakota, was westbound in lane one. Vehicle two (V2), a 2001 Pontiac Sunfire, was eastbound in lane two. In the intersection, V2 turned left and the front struck the front of V1. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, a forty-five-year-old male, was traveling from his home to his brother's workplace. He was healthy, well rested and comfortable with his vehicle. He travels this route weekly. This driver was not wearing corrective lenses at the time of the crash. He stated that he had broken his glasses the day before the crash and was planning to replace them later that day. This driver stated that he saw V2 and applied the brakes when he realized that V1 was going to turn.
The driver of V2, a twenty-nine-year-old female, was not interviewed. No further information is available for this driver.
