This two-vehicle crash occurred on an expressway with congested traffic. The time was on a weekday during early afternoon hours. The location was a five-lane divided (with positive barrier) straight level concrete roadway. The speed limit is 97 kmph (60 mph). The weather was clear and the roads dry. Traffic was backed up from another crash located several miles north of the crash site.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2007 Toyota Tacoma pick-up with one occupant was traveling north in the second lane of traffic. Vehicle two (V2), a 2000 Ford F-150 pick up with one occupant was traveling ahead of V1. V2 slowed down with the intention to stop when the front of V1 struck the rear of V2. Both vehicles came to rest in lane two adjacent to the impact location. V1 was towed from the scene. V2 was driven from the scene. No one was transported. Police Reported Travel Speed is No PAR obtained (created) for V1 and No PAR obtained (created) for V2.
A 23-year old male drove V1, the 2007 Toyota pick up. He was heading to a store from work. He left his work, which is located 3.2 kilometers (3 miles) from the crash site, within 10-15 minutes prior to the crash. He does not take medication, is in general good health and was well rested. He stated he dropped something inside his vehicle and was reaching down on the floor to pick this unknown object up. When he looked up he noticed traffic ahead of him slowing down to a stop. He braced on the steering wheel with both hands and applied the brakes. He could not estimate how close he was to V2 when he noticed the vehicle to stop. The researcher saw a variety of personal objects spread out inside the vehicle. The driver stated he was traveling at a speed of 40 kmph (25 mph) prior to the critical event.
A 28-year old male drove V2, the 2000 Ford F-150 truck. He was heading home from work. He intended to use the next exit after the upcoming exit. He does not take medication, is in general good health and was well rested. He said he briefly checked his rearview mirror before he slowed down for a stop. He said he was still moving when V1 struck him. The driver of V2 stated he was traveling between 16 and 32 kmph (10-20 mph) due to the slower traffic.
