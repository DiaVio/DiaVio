This two-vehicle crash occurred on a north/south, seven lane, divided, straight roadway with a -3.3% grade for southbound traffic. The speed limit is 56 kph (35 mph). The roadway intersects an east/west, two lane, undivided, level roadway. The southbound lanes consist of three through lanes and a left turn lane. Both roadways are of concrete construction. East/west traffic is controlled by stop signs prior to entering the intersection. This crash occurred during daylight hours with light traffic. There were no adverse weather conditions at the time of the crash.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1999 Mercury Villager with one occupant, was westbound, had stopped at the stop sign with the intention of turning left to proceed south. Vehicle two (V2), a 2001 GMC Yukon XL with four occupants, was southbound in lane three. V1 entered the intersection attempting the left turn when the right side of V1 struck the left side of V2. V1 was not disabled; V2 was towed due to damage. None of the occupants was transported. Police Reported Travel Speed is No PAR obtained (created) for V1 and No PAR obtained (created) for V2.
The driver of V1, a forty-two year-old male, was traveling from home to work. He was well rested, in good health and comfortable with his vehicle. He drives this route daily. This driver stated that he did not see V2 when he entered the intersection.
The driver of V2, a thirty-four year-old female with three passengers, was traveling from home to a grocery store. She was well rested, in good health and comfortable with her vehicle. She drives this route daily. The driver stated that she assumed V1 would yield the right-of-way. Just prior to impact she braked and steered right. The driver was three months pregnant. She does not possess a valid driver's license.
