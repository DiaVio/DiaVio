This crash occurred on a two lane undivided roadway, curving to the right, with and grade of -9.8%, and a posted speed limit of 72KMPH (45MPH). It was raining heavily and the roadway was wet. This crash occurred on a weekend evening on a dark unlighted roadway.
Vehicle #1, a 1993 Toyota Paseo, was traveling westbound on the two lane undivided roadway, negotiating the curve to the right when it skidded over the left lane line into the eastbound lane and impacted the front right of Vehicle #2, a 2000 Honda Odessy, with its front left. Vehicle #1 continued off the roadway and came to rest on the south side, against a embankment, facing west. Vehicle # 2 came to rest off the roadway on the south side, facing east. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The Toyota Paseo (Vehicle #1) was driven by a belted 20 year old male who was not injured. A belted 20 year old male passenger in the front right seat was also not injured.
The driver stated that he did not think he was traveling too fast for the weather conditions and then stated he probably was.
The Honda Odessy (Vehicle #2) was driven by a 60 year old belted male who was not injured. A 60 year old belted female passenger in the front right seat was transported to an area hospital with an incapacitating injury. An 11 year old belted female passenger in the second row center seat was not injured. No interview could be obtain from the drdiver of Vehicle #2 who could not be contacted and lives in another state.
The police did not consider illegal drugs or alcohol to be involved and ordered no test for either driver.
