This single vehicle crash occurred during the mid-morning daylight hours on a Tuesday. The location was an interstate highway within the city limits of a major city. The roadway is a divided freeway with five northbound lanes. The surface was wet concrete with a negative grade greater than 2%. It was raining with limited visibility and there was standing water on the roadway. The crash occurred in a left hand curve with an unknown curvature or super elevation. The posted speed limit is 97 kmph (60 mph).
Vehicle one (V1), a 2002 Jeep Sport Utility four door SUV with one occupant, was traveling northbound in lane three in a left curve. The driver stated she was traveling slightly under the speed limit when her vehicle hit a patch of standing water. The vehicle began to fishtail and the she lost control. V1 rotated clockwise across three lanes toward the right shoulder and struck a Jersey barrier with the left plane. The vehicle came to rest on the shoulder facing west against the barrier. V1 was towed due to damage. The driver was not injured. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.
The driver of V1, a 33 year-old female, was on her morning commute from home to work and had been driving for approximately 45 minutes prior to the crash. She was familiar with both the road and the vehicle. She was well rested and has no health problems.
