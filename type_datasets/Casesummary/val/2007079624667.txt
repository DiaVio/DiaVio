This single vehicle daytime collision occurred mid-morning at a four-leg intersection comprised of a north/south roadway and an east/west roadway. The north/south roadway is undivided, dry, asphalt, curves left, has a -4% downgrade slope as it travels north, and consists of two lanes. The east/west roadway is undivided, dry, asphalt, curves left, and consists of two lanes. The east/west roadway has an upgrade slope of 5.6% as it travels east. The Both roadways are designated 40 kmph (25 mph) zones. Traffic on the east/west roadway is regulated through the intersection by stop signs.
Vehicle one (V1) is a 2004 Infinity G-35 four-door sedan, driven by a sixty nine (69) year old female who was traveling northbound in the number one lane. As V1 negotiated the curve, the vehicle went to the right side of the travel lane and its front plane contacted the east curb. V1 then traveled left across all lanes towards the west curb. The front plane of V1 contacted the west curb as it traveled unto a private driveway. The front plane of V1 then contacted a wood fence as it continued unto a private front lawn. The front plane of V1 contacted a tree and then contacted a brick base. V1 continued forward and the front plane then contacted a light pole encompassed by the brick base. V1 continued travel across the private front lawn until contacting a second tree with its front plane. V1 then contacted a second wood fence with its front plane. V1 traveled unto a second private property and contacted a tree with its front plane. V1 came to rest facing northwest against the tree. V1 was towed from the scene due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.
The driver could not remember where she was looking before departing the road, but stated she was not doing any activities prior. She was coming from home going to pick her husband up at the hospital, for whom she had concerns. Normally, the driver usually avoids that street because she feels uncomfortable with the narrowness of the roadway. This driver doesn't drive very often, her husband usually does all the driving. She has been driving a lot more since he fell ill with cancer. The driver has many medical conditions including myocarditis, an enlarged heart. She also eats while sleep walking and has severe acid reflux. The driver takes the following medications: Cozaar, Wellbutrin, Crestor (generic Lipitor), Ambien, Premarin, Effexor, Coreg, L-Thyroxine, Vitamin D, and Tums antacids. She takes at least ten extra strength Tums antacids per day. The driver had pyrothyroid surgery approximately 20 years ago. The driver has no history of black outs or seizures.
