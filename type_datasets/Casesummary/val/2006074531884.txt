This single-vehicle crash occurred on a jersey barrier-divided, straight, level, access-controlled roadway. The speed limit on this roadway is 97 kmph (60 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours and it was cloudy.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2001 Oldsmobile Alero, was westbound in lane four of five. A non-contact vehicle was in lane three and began to change lanes to the left. The driver of V1 steered left and V1 rotated slightly counterclockwise, departing the roadway on the left side. The front of V1 struck the jersey barrier. V1 continue westbound and rolled over to the right two quarter-turns, coming to final rest on its top. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.
The driver of V1, a twenty-two-year-old female, was on her way home from visiting a friend in another state and had been driving for two hours. She was healthy and familiar with her vehicle. She travels this route monthly. This driver had returned the previous week from a six-week Olympic volleyball training trip in Japan, which caused a change in her sleep habits due to jet lag. She also exercises for two hours every day. This driver stated that she felt fatigued when she started this trip.
