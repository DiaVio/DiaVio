This single-vehicle crash occurred on a jersey barrier-divided, straight, level trafficway. The eastbound lanes consist of four through lanes. On the right side of this roadway, there is a paved shoulder, an asphalt curb, a dirt shoulder, a downhill embankment and a chain link fence. The speed limit on this roadway is 105 kmph (65 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2001 Toyota Corolla, was eastbound in lane two. The driver of V1 was looking to the right to check for clearance to change lanes to the right. When this driver looked forward, there was a tire tread in the lane. The driver of V1 abruptly steered right. V1 rotated clockwise, traveling to the southeast. The left front wheel and then the left rear wheel struck the curb. The left side of V1 then struck a reflector post. V1 rolled over to the left and the top struck some bushes. V1 rolled onto the top and the right side struck the chain link fence. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 113 kmph for V1.
The driver of V1, a nineteen-year-old female, was on the way from her home to church with two passengers. She was healthy, well rested and comfortable with her vehicle and passengers. She rarely drives this route. This driver was in a hurry to get to church on time. This driver stated that while she was looking to the right, one of her passengers shouted, "look out" and the driver looked forward again. She then avoided the tire tread by steering right.
