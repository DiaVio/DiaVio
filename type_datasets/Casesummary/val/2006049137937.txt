This two-vehicle crash occurred in a four-legged, traffic signal-controlled intersection. The east/west roadway is divided by a curbed median and the approach lanes consist of three through lanes and a left turn lane. The eastbound lanes have a 2.5% upgrade and curve slightly to the left. The westbound lanes are level and curve slightly to the right. The speed limit on this roadway is 64 kmph (40 mph). It was dark with streetlights on at the time of the crash and there were no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1999 Mercury Marquis, was stopped eastbound in lane four. Vehicle two (V2), a 1995 Dodge Ram pickup, was westbound in lane three. V1 entered the intersection, turning left. The front of V2 struck the right side of V1. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, a seventy-eight-year-old male, was traveling, with a sixty-eight-year-old female, from a restaurant to his home. He was well rested, comfortable with his vehicle and passenger, and takes Digoxin for his heart problems. He travels this route daily. This driver stated that he saw V2 but thought he had enough time to make his turn.
The driver of V2, a forty-six-year-old male, was traveling from a relative's home to his home. He was healthy, well rested and familiar with his vehicle. He travels on this roadway weekly. This driver stated that he applied the brakes when he realized that V1 was turning in front of him.
