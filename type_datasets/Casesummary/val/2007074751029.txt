This two-vehicle crash occurred in a four-legged, traffic signal-controlled intersection. The east/west trafficway is straight, level and divided by a curbed median. The westbound approach consists of two through lanes and a left turn lane. The speed limit for this trafficway is 56 kmph (35 mph). The roadway south of the intersection is undivided. The northbound approach curves to the right, has an uphill grade and consists of one through lane and a left turn lane. The speed limit for this roadway is 40 kmph (25 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2000 Chevrolet S10 Blazer, was westbound in lane one. Vehicle two (V2), a 1998 Chevrolet Lumina, was northbound in lane one. V2 stopped and then proceeded at the intersection. In the intersection, the front of V1 struck the right side of V2. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, a fifty-five-year-old female, was on her way from a store to another store. She was healthy, well rested and familiar with her vehicle. This driver takes the medications Benicar, Premarin and vitamins. She travels this route weekly. This driver stated that her signal was green and she proceeded through the intersection without looking right or left. She applied the brakes when she saw V2 in front of her.
The driver of V2, an eighty-seven-year-old female, had left her home and was going shopping. She was in fair health, well rested and familiar with her vehicle. This driver takes the medications Vytorin, Ziac, Advil and aspirin. She travels this route daily. This driver stated that she stopped at the red signal, looked both ways and "no cars were coming so I went across the road." She further stated, "I drive like this all the time."
