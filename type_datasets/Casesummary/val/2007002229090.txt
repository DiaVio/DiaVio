This crash occurred on a two lane undivided roadway. The roadway was straight, after a curve to the right, with a grade of 4.2%. The speed limit was 64KMPH (40MPH). The weather was clear and the roadway was dry. This crash occurred on a weekday afternoon.
Vehicle #1, a 1990 Acura Integra, was traveling south on the two lane roadway when it suddenly went off the roadway to the right. The vehicle ran over a cement grate down an embankment, hit a metal culvert, with its undercarriage, before impacting with a utility pole and the poles guide wire. The Acura Integra (Vehicle #1) was driven by a belted 16-year-old male who was not injured. There was a belted 16-year-old male passenger in the right front seat. The passenger was transported to an area hospital, with minor injuries, treated and released. When finally reached by phone, this driver refused to be interviewed. He did not give a reason, just hung up.
A witness, driving in back of Vehicle #1, stated it looked like there was a mechanical problem with the front right wheel as the vehicle veered off the roadway suddenly. He stated that Vehicle #1 was not speeding.
Police did not consider alcohol or illegal drugs to be a factor in this crash and ordered no test for the driver.
