An intersection related collision occurred during the morning daylight hours on a two-way, four lane, undivided roadway. The road was dry, traveled asphalt. Southbound travel had a +1.6% grade. Northbound travel had a -1.6% grade. The posted speed limit was 56 kmph (35 mph). There was rush hour traffic at the time of the collision, which involved two vehicles. There were no adverse weather conditions.
Vehicle one (V1) was a 1999 Toyota Camry driven by a 68 year-old male who was not fatigued with one passenger. V1 was stopped southbound in the second lane waiting to turn into a business parking lot. Vehicle two (V2), a 2005 Honda Pilot driven by a 28 year-old male who was not fatigued, was traveling northbound in the first lane. V1 proceeded across the path of V2 without the right of way and was impacted on the right side by the front of V2. V1 rotated clockwise and came to rest in the roadway facing southeast. V2 rotated clockwise and came to rest in the roadway facing northeast. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1 was having a social conversation with the passenger. His view of on-coming traffic in lane one was blocked by traffic backed up in on-coming lane number two. He was otherwise attentive to the driving task. The stopped traffic waved the driver of V1 to make the turn.
