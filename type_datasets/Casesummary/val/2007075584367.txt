This three-vehicle crash occurred on an undivided, two-lane, driveway related roadway. The roadway was a dry, level and straight roadway with one lane in each direction and a posted speed limit of 56 kmph (35 mph). The crash occurred during nighttime hours with moderate traffic. The roadway is illuminated at night.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1994 Toyota Pickup with one occupant, was westbound in lane one traveling straight. Vehicle two (V2), a 2004 Jeep Cherokee with three occupants, was westbound in lane one stopped behind vehicle three. Vehicle three (V3), a 1995 Dodge Ram with one occupant, was westbound, stopped in lane one in front of V2. The front of V1 struck the back of V2. V2 moved forward and the front of V2 struck the rear of V3. All three vehicles came to rest in lane one facing west adjacent to the impacts. V1 was towed from the scene to the driver's house. V2 and V3 were driven. A passenger in V2 was transported. Police Reported Travel Speed is Unknown for V1 and Unknown for V2.
The driver of V1, a fifty-two year-old male was not injured. The driver was not available for an interview. Pre-crash skid marks were noted at the scene to be from V1.
The driver of V2, a forty year-old female was not injured. She was stopped for a few seconds and was talking to the occupants in the vehicle. She stated she heard driver of V1 slam on his brakes from far away before hitting them. She was driving from home to get something to eat with her children when the crash happened. She drives this roadway weekly, was well rested, in good health and takes vitamins on a regular basis.
The driver of V3, a forty-nine year-old male was not injured. He was stopped for 25-30 seconds waiting for eastbound traffic to clear so he could turn left into his driveway. He stated he also heard the squealing of tires and started to accelerate a little to get out of the way before he was impacted. He had just left a friend's house and was heading home when the crash occurred. He is in business with his father and worked sixty hours the week before without a day off in sixty days. He does not, however, feel that he was fatigued. He drives this road daily, was well rested and was wearing his glasses because he is near sighted.
