This two-vehicle crash occurred during early morning hours on a dry, left curve, five-lane, divided, bituminous roadway at a on-colors controlled intersection with pedestrian signals. The road generally runs east/west; however, at this point it runs northeast/southwest. Westbound traffic consists of a right turn lane, a painted median and two through lanes. Southbound traffic consists of a right turn lane and two left turn lanes. The speed limit for east/west traffic is 89 kmph (55 mph) and there were no adverse atmospheric conditions present.
Vehicle one, a 1993 Ford Probe GT coupe, driven by a 18 year-old male was traveling westbound in lane three, the outside through lane. Vehicle two, a 1998 Chevrolet Cavalier sedan, driven by a 62 year-old female was traveling southbound in lane two, the outside turn lane. Both vehicles entered the intersection, vehicle one intending to go straight and vehicle two intending to turn left. The front of vehicle one contacted the left side of vehicle two. Vehicle one rotated counterclockwise and vehicle two rotated clockwise. The two vehicles side slapped, the right side of vehicle one contacting the left side of vehicle two. Vehicle one continued counterclockwise coming to rest facing northeast in lane two of eastbound traffic. Vehicle two continued rotating clockwise. It came to rest facing south partially in lane one and the paved shoulder of eastbound traffic. Both vehicles were towed from the scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
