This single-vehicle crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions except wind gusts in excess of 64 kmph (40 mph). The gravel roadway is undivided, straight and consists of one lane in each direction. This roadway has a seven percent downhill grade in the westbound direction. The speed limit for this roadway is 72 kmph (45 mph).
Vehicle one (V1), a 1999 Mazda 626, was westbound in lane one. V1 traveled off the right side of the road, returned to the road and traveled off the left side of the road. V1 rotated clockwise and rolled over six quarter-turns. Physical evidence at the scene indicates that this vehicle was traveling at a high rate of speed. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.
The driver of V1, an eighteen-year-old female, was on her way to school from her home. She was not interviewed. Her mother provided all information. According to the mother, this driver was upset because she had broken up with her boyfriend (who had run over her dog the previous week) and had argued with him within the previous twelve hours. This driver was reportedly well rested and familiar with the vehicle and roadway. Also according to her mother, this driver initially steered to the right to avoid a small animal in the roadway. This driver's medical record indicates a negative blood alcohol test. No further information is available for this driver.
