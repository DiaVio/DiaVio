A two-vehicle midday collision occurred on a Thursday. The collision occurred at a four-leg intersection comprised by a north/south roadway and an east/west roadway. The north/south roadway is undivided, dry, level, asphalt, and consists of two lanes. The east/west roadway is undivided, dry, level, asphalt, and consists of two lanes. Both roadways have a posted speed limit of 40 kmph (25 mph). The north/south roadway is controlled by stop signs.
Vehicle one (V1) is a four-door 1999 Dodge Stratus, which was traveling southbound in the number one lane. Vehicle two (V2) is a 2003 Peterbilt 320 cab-over-engine straight trash truck that was illegally parked along the south curb on the east leg of the intersection. V2's right third axle was adjacent to a red no-parking curb. As V1 made its left turn, the front plane of V1 contacted the rear plane of V2. V1 rotated clockwise coming to rest facing northeast blocking the eastbound number one lane. V2 came to rest at its point of impact facing eastbound along the south curb. V1 received moderate damage and was towed from the scene. V2 received minor damage and was driven from the scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1 is a 23-year-old female traveling with a 27-year-old male, 38-year-old female, and a 4-year-old male. The driver was en route to drop off her passengers at their home. She indicated that she was in a hurry because she was dropping them off and then returning to work. She rarely drives through the collision's intersection. She was using residential side streets to avoid traffic. The driver was listening to the radio and talking with her passengers about social matters. As the driver attempted her left turn, she turned her head to look at the four-year-old passenger sat in the rear seat. The 38-year-old female passenger in the rear seat was attempting to appease the child who was being restless. The driver has two full time jobs and stated that she is typically fatigued, and felt fatigued while driving. She indicated that she had worked 90 hours in the past seven days and works an average day of 15 hours. The driver wears eyeglasses for a myopic condition; however, she was not wearing her eyeglasses at the time of the collision. The driver has five years of driving experience, but does not have a license. She bought the vehicle three weeks ago.
The driver of V2 is a 47-year-old male. The driver was en route, in a company-owned trash truck, to his employer's headquarters. The driver rarely drives through the collision's area. He was in the area assisting a fellow co-worker. The driver was parked along the south curb of the intersection's east leg, filling out paperwork. His vehicle was about 60% full carrying approximately 6 tons of trash. He did not see V1 prior to the collision because he was busy filling out his paperwork.
