This two-car crash occurred on a weekend day at an intersection with traffic lights. The road is a six-lane undivided concrete roadway from south to north. It is a three lane (one-way traffic) concrete roadway from east to west. The speed limit for the roadways is 48 kmph (30 mph) for both roadways. The road surface was dry and there were no detrimental atmospheric conditions at the time of the crash.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1997 Mercury Villager with four doors and seven occupants was traveling west in the second lane of traffic approaching the intersection of both roadways. Vehicle two (V2), a 2006 Cadillac DTS, was traveling north in the second lane. At the intersection of both roadways, the front of V1 impacted the right side of V2. V1 rotated clockwise and came to rest within the intersection, facing northwest. V2 rotated clockwise after the impact, and moved northwest into the southbound lanes of traffic. V2 kept rotating clockwise and departed the roadway entering the raised median of the roadway. The left rear wheel impacted with the curb of the median, causing the left rear wheel to separate from the rear axle. The vehicle came to rest partially on the median, partially in the southbound lanes of traffic, facing southwest. V2 was towed from the scene. V1 was able to drive from the scene. It had already been driven onto a nearby parking lot upon the researchers arrival. No injuries were reported.
