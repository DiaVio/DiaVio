This two-vehicle crash occurred on a concrete, level, and straight section of a two-way north/south divided roadway. The northbound lanes are comprised of a single right merge lane with left curvature that is alongside two additional northbound lanes and the single lane merges into the two northbound lanes. The single lane is controlled by a yield sign and divided from the northbound lanes by a raised curb divider that changes to solid white lines in the merge area. No adverse weather conditions, crash occurred mid-afternoon during daylight hours. The posted speed limit 56 kmph (35 mph).
Vehicle one (V1), a 2000 Dodge Intrepid sedan, was traveling north in the third lane. The driver, a 32 year old female, stated that she observed the other vehicle traveling to the right of her vehicle. Suddenly the other vehicle turned in front of V1. V1's right front corner was contacted by vehicle two's (V2's) left center side. V1 came to rest in original travel lane approximately 5 meters (16 feet) from impact location. Vehicle two (V2) was a 1998 Buick Regal driven by a 54 year old female. V2 was initially traveling west on another roadway and exited onto the northbound ramp merge lane. After completing the exit, V2's driver started to change lanes into the northbound lanes in order to pull off to the side of the road due to feeling ill. While changing lanes, V2 driver was feeling hot, nauseated, and sweaty. Driver does not recall what travel lane she was in when the collision occurred. After the collision, V2 came to final rest approximately 20 meters (66 feet) from the impact location, facing northwest. Both vehicles were towed from the scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
