This two vehicle crash occurred at an urban four-leg intersection early in the afternoon during daylight hours. Traffic approaching the intersection traveling west/east bound are controlled by posted stop signs. The north/south travel directions have no traffic controls for this intersection. The two-way north/south through street was comprised of one travel lane in each direction and a center two-way left turn lane and a posted speed limit of 56 kmph (35 mph). The two-way two-lane undivided cross street has a posted speed limit of 40 kmph (25 mph). It was a clear day with no adverse weather and roadways were dry, level asphalt.
Vehicle one (V1) was a 1997 Dodge Grand Caravan SE minivan that was traveling westbound on the two-lane two-way roadway that has no centerline stripe to separate the lanes approaching the intersection. . Vehicle two (V2) was a 1998 Chevrolet Cavalier 4 door sedan that was traveling southbound on the through street. V2 was traveling at a police reported speed of 77 kmph (48 mph). The driver of V1 stopped for the stop sign for approximately 30 seconds, began to accelerate through the intersection and was struck on the right side of the vehicle by the front of V2. The impact caused V1 to rotate counterclockwise while traveling in a westerly direction coming to rest on the west side of the cross street facing in an easterly direction. V2 rotated clockwise while traveling in a southwest direction across the intersection coming to rest on the west side of the cross street facing west. Both vehicles were towed from the scene. The driver of V1 was not transported for his injuries. The driver of V1, an 81 year old male, was cited by the investigating officer for Failure to Yield from a Stop Sign. The driver of V2, a 22 year old male traveling with a 19 year old female passenger, was cited for Speed Greater than Reasonable and Prudent. Police Reported Travel Speed is 16 kmph for V1 and 16 kmph for V2.
