This two-vehicle crash occurred at a T-intersection of a seven-lane roadway divided by a raised median for east and westbound traffic. To the south there is a three-lane roadway that is controlled by a stop sign prior to entering onto the east/west roadway. There were no traffic controls on the four-lane roadways. The posted speed limit for the seven-lane roadway is 72 kmph (45 mph). The posted speed limit for the three-lane roadway is 40 kmph (25 mph). This crash occurred during hours of daylight, the weather was clear with no adverse atmospheric conditions. The roadways are constructed of bituminous asphalt.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1990 Chevrolet 1500 4x2 pickup truck with one occupant, was northbound in the number two, left turn lane, of the three-lane roadway with a stop sign posted. The driver stopped, looked both right and left, saw approaching traffic but thought he had enough time to negotiate a left turn. The driver of V1 accelerated and was turning left when it impacted the right side of eastbound vehicle two (V2) in the number three lane of eastbound traffic. V1 backed to final rest position against the east curb in the number one northbound lane, facing north. V1 was not towed and the driver was not injured. Police Reported Travel Speed is 24 kmph for V1 and 24 kmph for V2.
Vehicle two (V2), a 2000 Ford Taurus SE with two occupants, was eastbound in the number three lane. V2 was traveling about 72 kmph (45 mph) approaching the intersection with no traffic controls. The driver of V2 saw the pickup just as it pulled out turning left. She did not have time enough to avoid the crash and was struck in the right side by V1. V2 rotated clockwise and struck a raised concrete divider with the right side tires, went over the divider and came to rest in the number two lane of eastbound traffic, facing west. The driver and passenger of V2 were transported with possible injuries. V2 was towed due to damage.
The driver of V1, a sixty-four (64) year old male, stated he had just left an auto dealership less than a block from the crash and was going home. He said he had traveled less than five minutes at the time of the crash. He said he was stopped at the stop sign with the intention of making a left turn. He said he looked left and saw two or three vehicles approaching from the left. He then looked right and entered the intersection turning left. He then saw vehicle two just as they collided and did not have time to attempt any avoidance effort. The driver then backed his vehicle to the curb and stopped. This driver was wearing prescription sunglasses for near-sightedness. He had slept eight hours the previous night, was in good health, had taken no medications and had been driving since the age of twelve. He also said he was working forty hours per week at a second job.
The driver of V2, a seventy seven (77) year-old female, stated she and her forty-six (46) year-old passenger were going to a residence where she was employed. She said was traveling about 72 kmph (45 mph) in the number three lane for eastbound traffic and saw V1 pull out and attempt to turn left. She said she tried to steer left but was struck in the right side. She did not have time to brake. This driver had slept ten hours the previous night, was in good health, did not require glasses and had taken Tylenol earlier. She stated she had been driving about twenty minutes at the time of the crash. She was not talking to the passenger and began driving at the age of eighteen.
