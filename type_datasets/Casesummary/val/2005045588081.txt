The crash occurred on an east/west two-lane undivided residential roadway. The road was straight with a 5 percent downgrade in the eastern direction. There were driveways to residences on the north and south sides of the road. Conditions were daylight and cloudy on a weekday afternoon. The posted speed limit was 56 kmph (35mph).
Vehicle #1 was a 2000 Chevrolet Cavalier. It backed out of a private drive on the south side of the road and began to travel eastbound. The driver intentionally ran off the right (south) side of the road and struck a utility pole that was 1.7m off the side of the road with its front. The vehicle was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.
Vehicle #1 was driven by a 19-year-old female that was not belted with deployed frontal airbags. She was transported to an area hospital for minor injuries and psychological evaluation. She had been in an argument with a friend that lived at the residence in front of the crash site and threatened suicide. She admitted to police that she intentionally ran into the pole; there were no skids or apparent avoidance actions.
