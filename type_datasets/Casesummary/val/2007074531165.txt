This two-vehicle crash occurred in a four-legged, traffic signal-controlled intersection. The north/south roadway is straight and divided by a curbed median. The southbound lanes have a three percent uphill grade and consist of two through lanes and a left turn lane. The northbound lanes have a three percent downhill grade and consist of two through lanes and a left turn lane. The speed limit on this roadway is 64 kmph (40 mph). It was dark with streetlights on at the time of the crash with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1990 Chrysler Lebaron, was southbound in lane two. Vehicle two (V2), a 1993 Chevrolet Caprice, was stopped northbound in lane three, waiting to turn left. V2 turned left and the front struck the front of V1. The vehicles rotated and the left side of V1 struck the right side of V2. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, a thirty-three-year-old male, was on his way to work from his home. He was well rested, healthy and familiar with his vehicle. He travels this route daily. This driver stated that he saw V2, but assumed that the driver of V2 would wait to make the turn until V1 cleared the intersection.
The driver of V2, a twenty-four-year-old male, had just dropped off his son at a day care facility on his way to work. He was healthy, well rested and familiar with his vehicle. This roadway is not his usual route but he was detoured due to construction on the day of the crash. This driver stated that he saw V1 approximately fifty feet from the intersection, but assumed that the driver would stop for the yellow traffic signal.
