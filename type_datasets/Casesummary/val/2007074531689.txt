This two-vehicle crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions. The roadway is an interchange exit ramp that is level, curves to the right and consists of two lanes with a paved shoulder on each side. The speed limit for this roadway is 80 kmph (50 mph).
Vehicle one (V1), a 2000 Ford Explorer, was southbound in lane two. The left rear tire blew out and V1 departed the roadway to the left. The driver of V1 steered to the right and V1 returned to the roadway. The driver then steered abruptly to the left and V1 traveled off the roadway on the left side. The front of V1 struck a delineator post. V1 then returned to the roadway, rotating clockwise, traveling to the southwest. V1 departed the right side of the roadway and rolled over one quarter-turn to the left. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.
The driver of V1, a seventy-eight-year-old male, was on his way from his home to pick up a friend from work. He was in good health, well rested and familiar with his vehicle. He travels this route monthly. This driver takes unspecified medications for his high blood pressure and gout. He stated that his vehicle experienced a tire blowout causing him to lose control of it. He further stated that he was not aware of any tire defects prior to this trip.
