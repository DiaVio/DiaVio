This crash occurred on a divided four-lane roadway without a positive barrier. The posted speed limit was 48KMPH (30MPH). The weather was clear and the roadway was dry. This crash occurred on a weekday afternoon.
Vehicle 3 and Vehicle 2 were stopped in traffic heading south. Vehicle 1, a 1992 Cadillac Brougham, was traveling south on the roadway when it impacted with the rear of Vehicle 2, a 2000 Pontiac Sunfire, pushing Vehicle 2 into the rear of Vehicle 3, a 2006 GMC Envoy. Vehicle 1 then backed up and impacted with the front of Vehicle 4, a 2001 Mercedes E430, with its rear. Vehicle 1 then proceeded forward impacting, again, the rear of Vehicle 2 with its front. All vehicles came to final rest in their original lane facing south. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The 1992 Cadillac Brougham, (Vehicle 1) was driven by a belted 86-year-old male who was transported to an area hospital. This driver, at the scene, was very upset and he stated he wanted to die, he asked the police for a gun to shoot himself. He, at first, refused to exit the vehicle when requested by police. This driver also takes Coumadin and Lasix for a heart related condition. These medications have possible side effects of drowsiness and blurred vision. Coumadin can also cause Purple Toe Syndrome, which causes pain in the toes. This driver was not coherent for an interview and a surrogate was interviewed in his place. The surrogate said the driver was kind of “out of it”. The driver was confused and he remembers going back and forth but not hitting anything. This vehicle was towed due to the driver going to the hospital.
The 2000 Pontiac Sunfire (Vehicle 2) was driven by a belted 22-year-old male who was not injured. This driver stated he was stopped in traffic and saw Vehicle 1 only 5 seconds before impact while looking in his rear-view mirror. He stated he already had his foot on his brake pedal and performed no other evasive actions due to lack of time.
The 2006 GMC Envoy, (Vehicle 3) was driven by a belted 30-year-old female who was not injured. A belted 60-year-old female in the front right seat and 2-month-old child in a child seat situated in the second row center, were not injured. This driver could not be reached for an interview after numerous calls. This vehicle was not towed.
