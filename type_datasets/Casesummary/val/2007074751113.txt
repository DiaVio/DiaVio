This single vehicle crash occurred mid-afternoon during daylight hours with no adverse weather conditions present in a residential neighborhood. The crash location occurred on a straight, level, two-lane, two-way, concrete, dry roadway. There were no traffic controls and a posted speed limit of 40 kmph (25 mph).
Vehicle one (V1) was a 1985 GMC Jimmy blazer SUV driven by a an unknown aged male, who abandoned the vehicle at the scene. The driver was in the number one travel lane going straight when V1 departed the right side of the roadway. V1 then entered the sidewalk on the east side of the street, contacted a wooden fence and then a tree with the front. V1 came to final rest facing northwest on the sidewalk. V1 was towed from the scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.
A witness observed V1 depart the right side of the roadway from the number one travel lane and contact the fence & tree. He did not know the driver's intent or if the driver made any avoidance actions. The travel lanes were dry, but once the driver departed the roadway the sidewalk was covered with packed snow and ice. Tire marks leading up to the impacts suggest V1 was tracking up to the initial point of impact with the fence with no avoidance actions taken. The tires on V1 had sufficient tread depth & inflation.
