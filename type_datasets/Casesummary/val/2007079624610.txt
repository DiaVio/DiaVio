This single vehicle collision occurred at midday on a two-lane by-pass interchange. The interchange is designed for medium/heavy trucks to circumvent a tunnel on an interstate highway. The interchange re-joins the interstate about a mile north of the collision area. The interchange is dry, asphalt, and has a 0.8% downhill slope. East of the east edge exists an asphalt shoulder. West of the west edge exists an asphalt median and a raised asphalt curb. A downgrade embankment exists west of the curb. At the bottom of the embankment are the northbound lanes of an interstate highway. The interstate highway is dry, level, asphalt, and consists of four lanes. Asphalt shoulders border the west and east edges of the interstate. Both the highway and the interchange are designated 104 kmph (65 mph) zones.
Vehicle one (V1) is a 1993 Jeep Grand Cherokee, sport utility vehicle, driven by a forty (40) year old male with one passenger. V1 was traveling northbound in the interchange's number one lane. As V1 traveled, it headed towards the west edge of the interchange and departed the left side of the roadway. The front plane of V1 contacted the raised asphalt curb. V1 traveled down the embankment as it rolled unto its right plane. The top of V1 contacted a warning sign. V1 came to rest on its roof, (vehicle was rolled over by the passenger onto it's wheels) blocking the highway's number one and two lanes. V1 was towed from the scene due to rollover damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 113 kmph for V1.
The driver did not give a statement. The passenger stated, to the police, that the driver had borrowed the vehicle from a friend. He stated that they had both been drinking alcohol for the past six hours. He also stated that they were both drinking while en route to the collision location. According to medical records the driver had a BAC of 0.194.
