This crash occurred in the late evening of a weekend in the northbound lanes of a four-lane divided highway. The north/south highway consisted of two lanes in each direction divided by a positive median divider. The bituminous roadway was dark (unlit), straight, and level with a posted speed limit of 89kmph (55 mph). Traffic flow was normal but the roadway was soaked from a rain that was falling at the time of the crash.
V1, a 1995 BMW 325i, was northbound at a high rate of speed when at least one deer, possibly two, entered the roadway. V1 impacted a deer and the vehicle to veered left. V1 departed the roadway and impacted a concrete culvert. It then rotated counter-clockwise before coming to final rest facing southbound. After V1 had come to final rest its engine compartment caught fire and quickly engulfed most of the front of the vehicle. Police Reported Travel Speed is No PAR obtained (created) for V1.
The driver of V1, a 33-year-old male was taken from the scene shortly after this researcher's arrival and could not be subsequently located for an interview.
