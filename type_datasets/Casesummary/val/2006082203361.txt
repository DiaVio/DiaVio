A two-vehicle daytime rear-end type collision occurred heading south on an interstate highway. In the area of the crash, the highway has five lanes of travel. The road is paved with concrete and is straight and level in the immediate area of the crash. The speed limit for the road is 97 kmph (60 mph). At the time of the crash the sky was clear and the road was dry.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2001 BMW 325 sedan with only a driver, was heading south in lane one. V1 crossed over four lanes of travel and established stabilization in lane five. Vehicle two (V2), a 2006 Toyota Sienna van with only a driver, was traveling south in lane five. V1 was traveling faster than V2 and rear-ended V2 at approximately 64 kmph (40 mph) per the officer on scene. V2 then hit the center median barrier causing damage to the left front corner. Both vehicles came to rest in lane five. Just prior to the crash, V1 also had minor collisions with two other vehicles and drove away from those incidents. An interview with one of those drivers who witnessed this crash to stated that V1 clipped his wife's vehicle then his vehicle and kept going before changing across all four lanes to the left and impacting the rear of V2. This driver estimated V2 was traveling about 80 kmph (50 mph) and V1 was "flying" at an estimated speed over 129 kmph (80 mph). He said it was like the driver of V1 was trying to get away after these first two previous incidents. Both V1 and V2 were towed due to disabling damage, but neither driver was transported to the hospital. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1 was a 37-year-old female who reported no injuries from the crash. For this trip she stated she was on her way home from a mall. She indicated she had an argument in the previous 6-hour period and was concerned about her personal relationships. There were no health concerns & she was not taking any medications, nor was consumption of alcohol a factor for her. At the time of the crash she had been awake for 13 hours, but could not remember how many hours of sleep she had. This driver is unemployed and felt irritated & upset when this trip started. She stated that a car to the right of her vehicle was driving radically towards her vehicle and she had to change lanes to feel safe. She kept going across lanes & didn't see traffic in lane five was slowing to a stop.
