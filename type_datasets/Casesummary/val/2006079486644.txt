This two-vehicle collision occurred during morning daylight hours on a weekend. The crash occurred at the four-leg intersection of a north-south roadway and an east-west roadway. The speed limit for north-south traffic is 56 kmph (35 mph), and there are no traffic control devices at the intersection for north-south traffic. At the time of the crash, it was cloudy, and the roadway was free of defects.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1985 Chevrolet three-quarter-ton single-cab pickup, was traveling southbound in the second lane of a four-lane, two-way, undivided, dry, level, bituminous roadway. V1 was stopped at the intersection and awaiting an opportunity to turn left into eastbound traffic. Vehicle two (V2), a 1992 Honda Accord two-door coupe, was traveling northbound in the second lane of opposing traffic. As V1 made its turn, the front of V1 impacted the front of V2. The vehicles rotated towards each other and the right side of V1 impacted the left side of V2 in a side-slap configuration. The vehicles separated and moved northeast to final rest in the intersection. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
