This single-vehicle crash occurred on a dry, undivided, straight, two-lane, four-leg, state highway. The main road has two lanes and is not controlled by traffic controls. The intersecting road has stop signs controlling them. The speed limit on the highway is 72 kmph (45 mph) and there were no adverse weather conditions at the time.
Vehicle one, a 1999 Toyota 4Runner was traveling northbound in lane one. A non-contact vehicle was stopped eastbound at a stop sign and pulled into the southbound lane. The driver of vehicle one applied the brakes with ABS activation and steered to the right. Vehicle one avoided the non-contact vehicle. Vehicle one ran down an embankment and sideswiped a tree with its front. Vehicle one continued northeast and impacted a small embankment with its left front wheel. Vehicle one bounced over the small embankment and came to rest on its wheels facing east on a dirt road. Vehicle one was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 72 kmph for V1.
The non-contact vehicle, also the witness who was interviewed, was stopped at a stop sign next to another vehicle that was stopped at the same stop sign to turn right. There were two southbound vehicles wanting to turn westbound, but the non-contact vehicle was in the way. The two southbound vehicles stopped. The non-contact vehicle pulled out into the southbound lane to let them proceed with their turn.
The driver of vehicle one, a forty-three year old female was heading home from her property. This driver had been driving for about eight minutes at the time of the crash. This driver has two children and two dogs in the vehicle with her. This driver stated that as she was northbound, there were some cars at the intersection and the red car pulled out into her lane. This driver drives this vehicle daily and over this road on a daily basis.
