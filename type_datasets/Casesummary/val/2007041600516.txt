The crash occurred in the northbound lanes of a high occupancy, high volume, and high-speed interstate highway. There were five lanes northbound with both right and left side paved emergency shoulders. There were concrete wall barriers separating the northbound lanes from southbound traffic. The road widths were a standard 3.7 meters and the emergency-paved shoulders were 3.5 meters. The road was straight and level. The conditions were dry and clear on a weekday afternoon. The posted speed was 105 KMPH (65 MPH).
V1 was a 1992 Honda Civic 4-door sedan that was traveling northbound in lane one. The driver was attempting to make a lane change into lane two. The driver stated that he was looking in his left side mirror and then felt a smash when he veered off the roadway to the right into the right emergency paved shoulder where he hit V2, a disabled 1999 Mazda Millenia 4-door sedan. V2 was parked on the right shoulder and was unoccupied. V1 hit the back left of V2, causing right front damage to V1. V1 and V2 were both towed due to damage. No one was injured. Police Reported Travel Speed is No PAR obtained (created) for V1 and No PAR obtained (created) for V2.
V1 was driven by a 35-year-old male who was on his way to a friend's house after having just gotten kicked out of his mother's house. He stated that he was going through some rough times and was also unemployed. The driver stated that he was traveling north in lane one and wanted to change lanes into lane two. He stated that he was looking in his left side mirror to make a lane change and then looked up and hit the left rear of V2 that was disabled on the right side shoulder. The driver reacted and steered to the left after he made contact with V2. The driver of V2 was out of the vehicle getting into a cab when he heard a smash and saw V1 hit the back of his broken down vehicle. V1 left a large tire mark from the point of impact to final rest on the right side shoulder.
V2 was parked legally on the right side emergency roadside with a broken oil pan. The driver stated that he was waiting for AAA to pick up his disabled vehicle, as it was not drivable. The driver stated that he was loading his baby into a cab about 50 feet ahead of his disabled vehicle when he heard a crash, looked up and saw V1 hit the back of his car.
