This five-vehicle crash occurred early afternoon during daylight hours. The location is a busy four-way intersection of an arterial in a commercial/industrial area. The roadway was a five lane, two-way, bituminous, level surface street with a c-curb center divider. Traffic flow controlled by on-colors traffic signals. The weather was dry, cloudy and it was daylight. The posted speed limit for all five vehicles was 64 kmph (40 mph).
Vehicle one (V1), a 2003 Freightliner FLD132 bobtail tractor, driven by a thirty (30) year old male was traveling northbound in the inside lane (lane 2) approaching the intersection. As V1 passed through and entered the north side of the intersection the left front wheel ran over a manhole cover. The manhole cover gave way and split in half causing the driver to lose control of the vehicle. V1 jumped the c-curb and entered the southbound lanes. The driver attempted to redirect and gain control, but sideswiped the left side near the rear of Vehicle two (V2). V2 was a 2001 Chevrolet Astro van driven by a forty eight (48) year old male. V1 continued in a northwesterly direction and struck the front end of Vehicle three (V3). a 2000 Dodge Caravan driven by a thirty nine (39) year old female. At impact V3 rotated clockwise striking Vehicle five (V5), a 1987 Chevrolet Astro van. V1 after impact with V3 pushed V3 into V4 that stopped to the rear of V3. The driver of V1 backed his vehicle a few feet and stopped facing north in the southbound left turn only lane. V1 and V3 were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, stated he had just left his place of work approximately 18 minutes prior to the crash and traveled approximately 4.8 kilometers (3 miles). He was well rested and familiar with both the vehicle and the roadway. He stated he was in good health and was taking medication for high blood pressure. All other drivers were stopped in traffic and had no reported health problems.
