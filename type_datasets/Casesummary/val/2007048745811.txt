It was dark with no adverse atmospheric conditions at the time of this single-vehicle crash. The roadway is undivided and consists of one lane in each direction. In the eastbound direction, the travel lane has a seven percent downhill grade and curves to the right (the radius of curvature is 125 meters and the superelevation is three percent). The speed limit for this roadway is 72 kmph (45 mph).
Vehicle one (V1), a 2005 Honda Civic, was eastbound in lane one. V1 traveled to the left, rotating counterclockwise and off the left side of the road. V1 rolled over to the right two quarter-turns into a ditch and was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 89 kmph for V1.
The driver of V1, a twenty-three-year-old female, was on her way home from work. She was healthy, well rested and familiar with her vehicle. She travels this route daily. This driver stated that a deer approached the right side of the road and she steered left to avoid it. The police report and physical evidence indicate that this driver was traveling too fast for the curves in this roadway.
