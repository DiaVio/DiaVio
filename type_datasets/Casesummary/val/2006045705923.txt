The crash occurred on an east/west secondary arterial roadway that was a two-way, two-lane road with no shoulders. The arterial roadway was straight with a positive 3% grade if traveling east but leveled off at the crash location. There was an intersecting road from a school campus that intersected the arterial from the south. It was a two-way, two lane road that was divided by a raised median with landscaping present. This roadway was straight and level. Conditions at the time of the crash were overcast with wet roads and some loose gravel due to a road-widening project although there was no construction equipment present at that time. The posted speed limit on the arterial road was 48kph (30mph) and the speed limit on the intersecting road was 24kph (15mph). The crash occurred in the early evening hours on a weekday.
Vehicle #1 was a 2002 Toyota RAV-4 traveling east on the arterial road when Vehicle #2, a 2003 Honda CRV turned left (west) from the intersecting road. Vehicle #2 on realizing Vehicle #1's proximity attempted to accelerate out of the way but was struck on it's left side by the front of Vehicle #1. Vehicle #1 rotated counterclockwise and came to rest facing north at impact. Vehicle #2 also rotated counterclockwise and came to rest facing southwest in the westbound travel lane of the arterial road with its back end off the roads edge. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
Vehicle #1, was driven by a sixteen year old belted female who was driven by her mom to a local hospital for treatment of possible injuries. Her airbags did deploy. No interview was obtained for this vehicle due to the parent of the minor aged driver refusing.
Vehicle #2, was driven by a fifteen year old belted female who was on her way home from school with her mom in the right front seat at the time of the crash. Neither occupant was injured in the crash.
