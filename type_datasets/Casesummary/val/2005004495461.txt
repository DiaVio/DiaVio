Case Note: Both drivers are bus drivers for the municipality where the crash occurred. At the time both drivers were on their way to work for the afternoon shift (school dismissal) in their personal vehicles. The drivers' supervisor responded to the scene and advised both of his employees not to speak with the researchers.
The crash occurred in the westbound lane of a five-lane intersection with a traffic control signal. Conditions were daylight and dry in the afternoon on a weekday. Both roads had a posted speed limit of 64 kmph (40 MPH). Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
V1 was a 1998 Pontiac Bonneville sedan traveling southbound in the right lane intending on going straight. V2, a 2004 Dodge Durango SUV, was traveling westbound in the right lane intending on continuing straight when an unknown vehicle supposedly cut in front of V2, which made V2 run the red light. The front of V1 struck the right side of V2 in the westbound lane. V1 came to final rest facing north and V2 came to final rest facing northwest.
V1 was driven by a 44-year old female who was not injured. The driver of V1 was contacted several times to be interviewed but did not respond back. V1 was towed due to damage.
V2 was driven by a 43-year old female who refused to be interviewed at the scene. V2 was towed due to damage.
