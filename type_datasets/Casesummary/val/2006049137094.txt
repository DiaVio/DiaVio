This two vehicle crash occurred a few hours before mid-night during dark hours at the intersection of a three-lane, north-south, asphalt, undivided roadway with a -6.6% grade and a four-lane (seven overall), east-west, concrete, divided, level roadway without a positive barrier. The intersection was controlled with tri-color traffic signals. The posted speed was 56 kph (35 mph). The roads were straight and dry and the sky was clear. Traffic flow was moderate.
Vehicle one (V 1), a 1999 Ford F-150 pick-up driven by a 27 year-old male was traveling northbound in lane one. Vehicle two (V 2), a 1994 Ford Crown Victoria sedan driven by a 21 year-old male with one passenger was traveling eastbound in lane two. V 1 entered the intersection without the right of way and was struck on the left side by the front of V 2. V 1 rotated counterclockwise and came to rest in intersection facing south. V 2 rotated counterclockwise came to final rest in the intersection facing north. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V 1 advised that he was on his way home from work. He advised that he was traveling at between 48-64 kph (30-40 mph) when he entered the intersection. He did not see V 2 prior to the crash and made no attempt to avoid the collision.
The driver of V 2 stated that he was traveling from one friend's house to another's. He advised that he was traveling at between 64-80 kph (40-50 mph) when he entered the intersection. He did not see V 1 prior to the crash and made no attempt to avoid the collision. Both vehicles were towed from the scene.
Associated factor could be inadequate surveillance- the driver of V 2 did not report seeing V 1 prior to the collision even though he had a clear view of V 1. The driver also advised that he was talking to the passenger at the time of the accident.
