The crash occurred on a two-way, two-lane, straight, level, bituminous road with a posted speed limit of 89 kmh (55 mph). Conditions at the time of the weekday morning crash were dark, unlighted, cloudy and dry. Ambient conditions included sporadic wind gusts.
V1, a 1987 Chevrolet 10 full size van driven by a 25 year-old male on his way home from work, was southbound on the road. The driver stated that he was traveling about 45 mph when the van drifted to the right onto the soft dirt shoulder, possibly due to a wind gust. The driver steered the van to the left but it stayed off the road on the right. The front of v1 struck a road sign and continued into a ditch. The front of v1 struck the ditch and v1 rolled onto it's left side to final rest. V1 was towed from the scene due to damage. The fully restrained driver denied injuries at the scene.
