This crash occurred during morning rush hour in the northbound lanes of a high occupancy, high volume, high-speed interstate highway. The north/south roadway consisted of five lanes in each direction flanked by paved emergency shoulders and divided by a concrete barrier wall. The road widths were a standard 3.7 meters and the paved shoulders were 3.5 meters. The bituminous roadway was dry, straight, and level with a posted speed limit of 105 kmph (65 mph). The conditions were dry and clear at the time of the crash.
V1, a 1993 Toyota Camry with one occupant, was northbound in lane two. The driver decided to change lanes into lane three. As the driver decided to change lanes she noticed a large truck in the lane. The driver jerked the wheel to the right and than to the left which caused her to lose control and cross into lane three. The right front corner of V1 struck the left side of V2, a 1996 Lexus LX450 (with one occupant) that was traveling in lane three behind the non-contact vehicle. V1 and V2 than side slapped. After this impact V1 spun out of control into lane four where it was struck by V3, a 2001 Lexus RX300. V1 came to final rest facing east in lane four and was later moved onto the shoulder. V2 and V3 came to final rest on the shoulder of the highway as well. V1 and V3 were towed due to damages while V2 was driven from the scene. There were no reported injuries related to this crash. Police Reported Travel Speed is 105 kmph for V1 and 105 kmph for V2.
The driver of V1, a 27-year-old female, was on her way to work when she was involved in the crash. She stated that she was driving her aunts vehicle and was nervous driving on the highway because of all the crashes that happen. She sated that she was in lane two when she wanted to change lanes to her left (lane three) as she proceeded to make the lane change she saw a large tractor trailer in the intended lane and steered hard right to get back into her lane. She realized she oversteered and tried to correct it by steering hard left which caused her to lose control. She swerved into lane three and hit V2. After the impact with V2 her car started to spin out of control until she impacted V3 in lane four. The driver was cited for the crash for careless driving.
The driver of V2, a 45-year-old male was on his morning commute at the time of the crash. He was in lane three when he was hit by V1 who had lost control and crossed into his lane. He was unable to avoid the crash, as he did not see V2 until impact. He was not injured and his vehicle was not towed due to damages.
The driver of V3, a 41-year-old female, was on her way to work in lane four when she saw V1 hit V2. She watched V1 continue into her lane directly in her path. She stated in the interview she steered left but could not avoid V1. She managed to pull off the highway onto the left paved emergency shoulder after the impact. She was not injured but her vehicle was leaking fluids and was towed due to damages.
