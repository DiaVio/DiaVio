The crash occurred on a straight, two-way, two-lane road that ran north/south and had a posted speed limit of 48kph/30mph. There were no shoulders and the road edges were starting to give way. A drainage ditch bordered either side of the road and on the east side of the east drain-ditch was a fencerow. The road had a precrash grade of negative 3.6%. The roadway was rural and conditions at the time were daylight and dry on a weekend afternoon.
V1 was a 2002 Isuzu Rodeo 4x2 that was traveling south on the road when the right side tires departed the right roadside with the undercarriage striking the road. V1 then steered left crossing the road and departing the left roadside striking the fence and coming to rest facing south, parallel to the road. V1 was towed from the scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.
V1 was driven by a sixty-two year old belted female who was driven by Privately Operated Vehicle (POV) to an area hospital along with her right front passenger who were both coded by police as sustaining possible injuries. They were on their way back from an introductory meeting at one of her relatives' homes (business).
