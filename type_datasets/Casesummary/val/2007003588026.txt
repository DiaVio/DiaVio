The crash occurred in the westbound lanes of a physically divided two-way roadway with three lanes in each direction. This was a straight, level, and bituminous roadway with a posted speed limit of 48 kmph (30 mph). It was the morning hours of a weekday and the road was dry and the sky was cloudy.
V1, a 1997 Nissan Pathfinder sport utility vehicle with one occupant, was traveling westbound in lane one. V2, an unknown M/H fuel delivery truck, was traveling westbound in lane two. At the intersection, V2 attempted to make a right turn while next to V1, and the front of V2 impacted the left side of V1. V1 went on top of the sidewalk and the front of V1 impacted the staircase located on the northwest corner of the intersection, and caused V1 to rollover a quarter turn. The top of V1 impacted the wood fence then rolled over another quarter turn. V1 came to a final rest on its roof approximately 15 meters from the point of impact. V2 left the scene of the crash. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
V1, the Nissan Pathfinder, was driven by a 36 year-old male who reported minor injuries and was transported to a hospital. The driver advised that he was commuting from home to work, and while traveling straight in lane one, V2 attempted to make a right turn from lane two. The driver stated that he attempted to brake and turn right.
V2, the unknown M/H fuel delivery truck, left the scene of crash, without stopping.
