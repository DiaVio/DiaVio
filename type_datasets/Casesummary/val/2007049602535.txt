This two-vehicle crash occurred on a north/south, undivided, two-lane, straight and level roadway. There is a driveway on the east side of the roadway in the area of the crash. The speed limit on this roadway is 48 kmph (30 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), 1995 Lincoln Town Car, was southbound in lane one. Vehicle two (V2), 1996 Ford F450 flatbed delivery truck, was southbound in front of V1. V2 traveled into the opposing lane, back into lane one and stopped in lane one at a forty-five degree angle, intending to back into the driveway. V1 was traveling above the speed limit and the driver steered right when he noticed V2 stopped. V1 traveled onto the gravel shoulder and the left side struck the right front side of V2. V1 rotated counterclockwise, traveled southbound and came to rest partially off the east side of the roadway, facing northwest. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, a forty-seven-year-old male, was traveling from his home to a store. He was healthy, well rested and familiar with his vehicle. He rarely drives on this roadway. This driver stated that he was driving above the speed limit and steered right in an attempt to avoid V2, but he did not apply the brakes.
The driver of V2, a sixty-eight-year-old male, was traveling from his office to make a delivery at the crash location. He was well rested, healthy and had been driving this class of vehicle for more than fifty years. This driver stated that he stops and backs into this driveway daily. He further stated that he was focused on his intended path into the driveway and did not see V1 prior to the crash.
