A crash occurred just before noon involving two passenger cars at an intersection with traffic lights. The speed limit for the roadway is 64 kmph (40 mph). The road is a 3-lane divided (no positive barrier) concrete roadway for both vehicles. The road surface was dry and there were no detrimental atmospheric conditions at the time of the crash.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1998 Ford Mustang driven by a 16 year-old male with one passenger, was traveling south attempting a left turn from the third lane of traffic at the intersection. Vehicle two (V2), a 2003 Oldsmobile Alero driven by a 17 year-old female, was traveling north in the first lane. At the intersection, the front of V1 impacted with the front of V2. V2 rotated clockwise and moved to the northeast corner of the intersection. It came to rest in the northeast corner, facing northeast. V1 rotated counter clockwise after the impact, and traveled north. The driver pulled the vehicle into the entry of a business parking lot, where it became disabled. At the final rest the vehicle was facing east. Both vehicles were towed from the scene. Both drivers and the right front occupant of V2 were transported to local medical facility. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
