This two-vehicle crash occurred in a four-legged, traffic signal-controlled intersection. The north/south roadway is straight, level, undivided, consists of one lane in each direction and the speed limit is 48 kmph (30 mph). The east/west trafficway is straight, level and divided by a curbed median. The westbound lanes consist of two through lanes and a left turn lane. The speed limit for this roadway is 64 kmph (40 mph). The roadways were wet at the time of the crash. It was dark with streetlights on at the time of the crash with no adverse atmospheric conditions. There was a snow bank (six feet tall) in the northeast corner of the intersection.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2001 Ford Taurus, was southbound in lane one. Vehicle two (V2), a 1993 Dodge Spirit, was westbound in lane two. V1 turned left in the intersection and the left side was struck by the front of V2. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, a forty-one-year-old female, was not interviewed. No further information is available for this driver.
The driver of V2, a forty-six-year-old female, was on her way to work from her home. She was healthy, well rested and familiar with her vehicle. She travels this route daily. She takes Norvasc for her high blood pressure. This driver stated that she did not see V1 until it was in the intersection.
