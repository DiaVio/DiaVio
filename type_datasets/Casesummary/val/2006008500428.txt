This crash occurred in the westbound lanes of a four-lane highway divided by a grass median. There were two westbound lanes and two eastbound lanes. The roadway is level, curves to the left, and is bituminous with a posted speed limit of 89 kmph (55 mph). It was late afternoon on a weekday, traffic flow was moderate, and conditions were clear.
V1 was a 2004 Lincoln Navigator, driven by a 37 year-old male. V1 was traveling westbound in lane one when the driver's cell phone, which was located on the passenger floor, began to ring. The driver reached to pick up his phone and the vehicle drifted off of the right side of the road. Upon departing the roadway V1 continued up an embankment striking a total of five trees. The first three trees did minor damage to the vehicle, mostly causing scratches from overhanging branches. The last two trees caused severe damage to the vehicle. Tree four contacted the front left of the vehicle. Tree five contacted the front right of the vehicle. Contact with the last tree caused the vehicle to rotate around the tree in a clockwise direction. As it rotated the wheels caught on the ground causing the vehicle to rollover one quarter turn. Police Reported Travel Speed is 105 kmph for V1.
