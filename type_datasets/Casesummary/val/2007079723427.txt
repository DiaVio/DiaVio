This single vehicle crash occurred on a cloudy dark but lighted four lane divided, dry, concrete divided highway in the westbound travel lanes. The roadway is designated 105 kmph (65 mph.) and comprised of four westbound travel lanes. The right side is bordered with an asphalt shoulder and a cinder block wall. The left side is bordered with a concrete center divider. There is an unknown pre-crash down hill grade that appears to be grater than -2%. There is an uphill grade of 4% at the first point of impact through final rest.
Vehicle one (V1) is a 1965 Ford Thunderbird 2-door coupe, driven by a twenty (20) year old male with one passenger was traveling west in the number four lane. The driver of V1 suffered a seizure and started to veer to the right. V1 departed the roadway on the right and it's front impacted the cinder block wall adjacent to the right shoulder. V1 continued west to impact the same wall again with its front plane. V1 came to rest facing west up against the wall and was towed from the scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is 105 kmph for V1.
