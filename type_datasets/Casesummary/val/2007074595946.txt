This two-vehicle crash occurred in a four-legged, traffic signal-controlled intersection. The east/west roadway is undivided, straight and consists of two lanes in each direction with a left turn lane at the intersection. Both directions have a slight uphill grade. The speed limit for this roadway is 56 kmph (35 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1999 Mercedes Benz ML320, was stopped westbound in lane three, waiting to turn left. Vehicle two (V2), a 1998 Buell 1200cc motorcycle, was eastbound in lane one. V1 entered the intersection turning left and the right side was struck by the front of V2. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, a seventeen-year-old female, was traveling with her eighteen-year-old female friend. They were on their way to another friend's house. This driver was well rested, healthy and familiar with her vehicle. She travels this route daily. This driver had twelve months of driving experience at the time of the crash. She stated that she was talking to her passenger prior to the crash. She also stated that she waited while a motorcycle crossed through the intersection, but did not see V2 behind the other motorcycle.
The driver of V2, a twenty-one-year-old male, was riding around with his friend (on the other motorcycle) for several hours and they were on their way home at the time of the crash. This driver was well rested, healthy and travels this route weekly. He had been riding motorcycles for five years and was very familiar with his vehicle. This driver stated that he was following his friend as they passed through the intersection and applied his brakes (skidding longitudinally) when he saw V1 at the last minute.
