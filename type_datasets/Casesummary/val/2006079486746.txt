This single vehicle crash occurred on a five-lane, undivided, dry, level, asphalt roadway. This roadway consists of two lanes in each direction, a center two-way left-turn lane and parking lanes on both sides. The speed limit on this roadway is 35 mph (56 kmph.). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1979 Dodge Sportsman Van converted into an RV, was southbound in lane one. V1 veered to the left, rotating counterclockwise, and traveled across all four travel lanes. The front of V1 struck a parked 1995 Chevrolet pickup. V1 continued rotating and the right rear wheel struck the curb and the back right corner struck a parked 1993 Honda Accord. V1 continued rotating and traveling southeast until the right side struck the corner of a brick wall. V1 came to rest facing northeast on the east curb and was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, a fifty-four-year-old male, was pronounced dead at the scene and no surrogate could be located.
Two witnesses were interviewed. One witness said that it appeared that V1 was making a U-turn and the other said that the driver of V1 turned left. Both indicated that the V1 was speeding and that the driver appeared to be accelerating at the time of the crash.
