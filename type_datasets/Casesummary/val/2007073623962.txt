This two-vehicle crash occurred in a four-legged intersection. The east/west trafficway is straight, level and divided by a grass median. The westbound lanes consist of two through lanes, a left turn lane and a right turn/through lane. The southbound approach is straight, level, undivided and consists of one lane in each direction. There are yield signs in the median for the north/south traffic. The speed limit for these roadways is 56 kmph (35 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1984 Chevrolet Cavalier, was westbound in lane three. Vehicle two (V2), a 2004 Chevrolet Trailblazer, was southbound, stopped in the median, waiting to turn left (south). V1 traveled to the left and the front struck the left side of V2. V2 rotated counterclockwise and the left side struck the yield sign. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, a twenty-year-old male, was on his way to work from his home. He was healthy, well rested and familiar with his vehicle. He travels this route daily. This driver stated that he was traveling at 66-80 kmph (41-50 mph) because he was going to be late for work. He further stated that he did not see V2 until just before the crash. He did not provide any information on the reason that he traveled to the left.
The driver of V2, a seventy-four-year-old male, was taking his seventy-four-year-old wife from a restaurant to a medical appointment at a hospital. He was well rested, healthy and comfortable with his vehicle and passenger. He travels this route daily. This driver takes an unspecified medication for high blood pressure.
