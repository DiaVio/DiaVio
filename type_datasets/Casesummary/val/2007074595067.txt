This two-vehicle crash occurred on a straight, level, median-divided roadway, at a three-legged, traffic signal-controlled intersection. The eastbound approach consists of two through lanes and a right turn lane. The speed limit for this roadway is 89 kmph (55 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2005 Chevrolet Trailblazer, was stopped in eastbound lane two. Vehicle two (V2), a 1995 Ford Contour, was eastbound in lane two. The front of V2 struck the back of V1. V2 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 0 kmph for V1 and 0 kmph for V2.
The driver of V1, a thirty-two-year-old female, was on her way to lunch from her home. She was well rested, healthy and familiar with her vehicle. She travels this route daily. This driver had stopped and was applying makeup just prior to the crash. She stated that she did not see V2 prior to the crash.
The driver of V2, a fifty-three-year-old female, was on her way to lunch from work. She was well rested and familiar with her vehicle and the route. She works ten hours a day and had worked fifty hours in the past seven days. This driver stated that she has family and financial problems. She also stated that she had taken Alavert and Tylenol Sinus in the previous twenty-four hours. This driver further stated that she was hungry when she started this trip and then had a pain in her side. She was looking for a specific restaurant at the time of the crash. She also stated that she had a blackout and did not see V1 prior to the crash.
