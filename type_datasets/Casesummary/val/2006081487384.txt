This three-vehicle crash occurred on a level, straight and dry roadway. This roadway consists of one lane in each direction and a center left-turn lane. The speed limit on this roadway is 48 kmph (30 mph). This crash occurred during the dark with streetlights on with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1999 Dodge Durango, was southbound in lane one. Vehicle two (V2), a 2000 Ford Windstar and vehicle three (V3), a 1994 Honda Accord, were southbound in front of V1. V2 and V3 decelerated due to traffic conditions. The driver of V1 attempted to avoid the crash by applying the brakes and steering right. The front of V1 struck the back of V2. The front of V2 struck the back of V3. V1 and V2 were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, a thirty-six-year-old male, was traveling from his home to a customer's house. He stated that he hadn't slept well lately due to a cold as well as working long hours and felt "exhausted" when he began this trip. He is familiar with his vehicle and drives this route daily. This driver stated that he was looking at a building on the left side of the road and didn't notice V2 and V3 slowing until the last minute.
The driver of V2, a forty-year-old female, was traveling with her three young children, from a hotel to their home. She was well rested, healthy and comfortable with her vehicle. She drives this route weekly. This driver takes Zyrtec, Lamictal, Klonopin and Tegretol.
The driver of V3, a twenty-four-year-old female, was not interviewed. No information is available for this driver.
