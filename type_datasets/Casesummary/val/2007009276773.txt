The crash occurred in the eastbound lanes of a seven-lane divided roadway. There were four eastbound lanes and three westbound lanes. The roadway is straight, level, bituminous with a speed limit of 89 kmph (55 mph). It happened in the early morning on a weekday during rush hour. The skies were cloudy, the roads were wet from rain earlier on in the morning. Traffic flow was moderately heavy.
V1, a 2000 Toyota Camry 4-door sedan was traveling eastbound in lane four behind V2. V2, a 1989 Nissan Maxima 4-door sedan was stopped at the traffic signal in lane four heading eastbound behind V3. V3, a 1999 Isuzu Box Truck T-series 2-door was stopped at the traffic light in lane four in front of V2. V1's front contacted the back of V2 and then the front of V2 contacted the back of V3. All vehicles came to a rest in their original lane. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
V1, a 2000 Toyota Camry 4-door sedan was driven by a 23 year old female who was traveling with her 1 year old infant child. The driver of V1 stated that she had looked in the rear view mirror at her baby who was coughing and when she looked back towards the road, she noticed that V2 was stopped. She said she slammed on the brakes but it was to late. Her vehicle slid into the back of V2. She stated she only looked away for a split second. She and her infant child were not injured. V1 did not want interior shots of her vehicle taken since her infant was in there. Numerous attempt to get to interior shots where unsuccessful.
V2, a 1989 Nissan Maxima 4-door sedan was driven by a 36 year old male who was traveling alone. He was following his friend to a job site before the crash. He stated that he was waiting for the traffic light to change when he heard V1 sliding behind him and then feeling the strike of the vehicle. He said he lunged forward and then struck V3 who was stopped in front of him. The driver of V2 was also not injured from the crash.
V3, a 1999 Isuzu Box Truck T-series 2-door was driven by a 34 year old male who was traveling with one other occupant who was 22 years old. He stated that he did not see the crash because it was in his blind spot but he did feel the impact. When he got out and looked he stated he saw his friends vehicle practically under his truck.
V3 was not involved in the first harmful event.
