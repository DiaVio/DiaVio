This single vehicle crash occurred on a two-lane asphalt constructed rural roadway. This road has various curves and grades with drop offs on both sides. The posted speed is 89 kmph (55 mph) with a speed reduction to 80 kmph (50 mph) just prior to this crash area. This crash occurred during daylight with a light snow falling and no road defects.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1997 Chevrolet Tahoe was southbound in the first lane. Per witnesses V1 had crossed the centerline and then come back to the right before departing the roadway. Once off the roadway it dropped down off the road grade and landed on the extended right-of-way on its wheels. V1 then impacted two trees with its front plane. V1 subsequently caught on fire at final rest. V1 was towed from the scene due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 113 kmph for V1.
The driver of V1, a seventy-nine year old male was fatal at the scene from the fire. Per a surrogate interview with the driver's wife, this driver had just left home and was heading into town to pick up a gift for a friend. This driver drives this vehicle daily and over this road on a weekly basis. This driver got seven hours of sleep the previous night and takes the medication Allopurinol. The second witness to this crash indicated that as he passed V1, the driver looked "scared or surprised". It is due to this statement that the critical reason was assigned. The investigating officer listed the driver's speed at 24 kmph (15 mph) above the posted speed limit.
