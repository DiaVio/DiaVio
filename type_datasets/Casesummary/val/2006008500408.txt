This crash occurred in a four-way intersection. The intersection had one northbound lane, one southbound lane, two eastbound lanes, and one westbound lane. The eastbound lanes were straight with a slight downhill grade, and were bituminous with a posted speed limit of 56 kmph (35 mph). The northbound lane was straight with an uphill grade of greater than 3%, and was bituminous with a posted speed limit of 40 kmph (25mph). It was late afternoon on weekday, traffic flow was heavy, and conditions were clear.
V1 was a 1987 Oldsmobile Calais, driven by a 49 year-old female was traveling north. V2 was a 1998 Nissan Altima, driven by 27 year-old male was traveling east. V2 had a 23 year-old female passenger in position 13. V3 was a 2000 Dodge Ram 1500 that was legally parked off of the roadway in a parking lot. There were no occupants in V3. V1 was stopped at a stop sign in the northbound lane. The driver had been stopped at the sign for close to a minute waiting for traffic to clear so that she could proceed across both eastbound lanes and turn left into the westbound lane. When the driver believed the way was clear she proceeded into the intersection where V2 impacted her left side drivers door. V1 was thrown across the roadway and into an adjacent parking lot on the north side of the roadway. When V1 was forced into the parking lot it contacted V3, which was legally parked in the lot. V1 and V3 came to final rest in the parking lot and V2 came to final rest in lane two of the eastbound roadway. Police Reported Travel Speed is Unknown for V1 and Unknown for V2.
V3 was not involved in the first harmful event. Therefore, no Critical Precrash Event or Critical Reason was coded to this vehicle.
