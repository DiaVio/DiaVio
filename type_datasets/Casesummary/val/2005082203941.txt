This two vehicle daytime collision occurred at 1713 hours. The collision occurred heading southbound on a divided two-way roadway with on-street parking on both sides. The pre-crash roadway for both vehicles is straight and level dry with bituminous paving. The roadway has a posted speed limit of 48 Kmph (30 mph).
Vehicle 1 (V 1), a 1999 Ford Escort sedan, driven by a 80 year-old female with one passenger was traveling southbound on the roadway. Vehicle 2 (V 2), a 2000 BMW 323 station wagon, was parked against the west curb facing south. V 1 accelerated down the street, drifted to the right and side struck the left rear of V 2, continued south then came to rest about three car lengths in front of V 2 on the west curb. The driver of V 1 was transported to a hospital for a bump on the head and dizziness. V 1 was towed from the scene. During the interview with driver of V 1, the driver stated that she didn't understand how she could see her car when she was inside it . She also said she didn't know she hit anything. After talking to her further she said her vision went blurry and she could no longer see. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
