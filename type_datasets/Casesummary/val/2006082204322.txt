This single vehicle crash occurred on a four-lane, divided roadway separated from southbound traffic by a positive barrier. The pre-crash roadway for vehicle one is straight with an uphill grade of +3%. The roadway is paved with asphalt. There is a posted speed limit of 64 kph (40 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle 1, a 1990 Buick Century was northbound in the second lane. Vehicle one drifted right, crossed lane one, and departed the roadway to the right. The front of vehicle one struck a wood utility pole. Vehicle one rebounded slightly from the head-on impact, and the vehicle came to rest approximately one meter south of the utility pole, with the front off the roadway and the left rear in lane one northbound. Vehicle one was towed from the scene due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.
The driver of vehicle one, a forty-two year old male was heading home at the time of the crash. This driver had been driving for almost one hour at the time of the crash. This driver had to get this vehicle home so his mother could take the kids somewhere. This driver thought he could get home without falling asleep since he had been working so many hours at work. This driver was not taking any medications. A blood test came back negative but this driver did test positive for cocaine. This driver does not remember the crash and fell asleep before the crash. This driver drives this vehicle daily and over this road on a daily basis.
