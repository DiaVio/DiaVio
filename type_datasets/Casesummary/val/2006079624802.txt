This two vehicle crash occurred shortly past noon during daylight hours in the rain. The collision occurred on the eastbound lanes of an east/west state highway. The highway is level, wet, concrete, and consist of five lanes. The number five lane is a high occupancy vehicle (HOV) lane separated by double yellow lines. The south edge of the eastbound lanes is bordered by an asphalt shoulder. South of the south shoulder exists a raised asphalt curb. South of the raised curb is a flat dirt embankment with trees and shrubs. North of the north edge exists a concrete embankment. The highway is designated a 104 kmph (65 mph) zone.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2001 Ford Ranger pickup driven by a 21 year-old male with one passenger, was traveling eastbound in the number one lane. Vehicle two (V2), a 1998 Dodge Caravan mini-van driven by a 32 year-old female with two passengers, was traveling eastbound in the number five (car pool) lane. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1 lost control on the wet roadway and began to rotate counter-clockwise and traveled across all of the eastbound lanes. The right rear plane contacted the right plane of V2. V2 rotated clockwise as it traveled towards the south shoulder. The left plane of V2 contacted the raised asphalt curb located south of the highway's south edge. V2 rolled unto its left plane contacted the bushes on the dirt embankment coming to rest on its roof facing westbound on the dirt embankment. V1 contacted the concrete center divider, with its front plane then came to rest facing eastbound against the concrete center divider.
The driver of V1 stated that he felt uncomfortable traveling at a high speed in the heavy rain. He decided to change lanes from the number two to the number one lane to drive at a slower rate than the traffic. He was in the number one lane for approximately ten to fifteen seconds when he began to hydroplane across the eastbound lanes. V2 was towed from the scene and V1 was not towed from the scene.
