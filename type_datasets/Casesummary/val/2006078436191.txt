This two-vehicle crash occurred in a three-legged intersection. The north/south roadway is straight, level and consists of two lanes in each direction and a center left turn lane. The speed limit on this roadway is 72 kmph (45 mph). The east/west roadway (on the west side of the north/south roadway) is straight, level and consists of one lane in each direction. The eastbound traffic is controlled by a stop sign. The speed limit on this roadway is 40 kmph (25 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1997 Ford Escort, was stopped eastbound in lane one. Vehicle two (V2), a 2004 Mitsubishi Lancer, was southbound in lane two. A non-contact vehicle was southbound in lane one. V1 entered the intersection turning left, and the front struck the front of V2. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 16 kmph for V1 and 16 kmph for V2.
The driver of V1, an eighteen-year-old male, was traveling with a four-year-old male passenger, from his home to a pharmacy. This driver was well rested, healthy and comfortable with his vehicle and passenger. He travels this route daily. This driver stated that he looked left before entering the intersection and was looking right for northbound traffic when he entered the intersection. He stated that he saw V2 just before the crash and applied the brakes in an attempt to avoid the crash.
The driver of V2, a twenty-year-old male, was traveling from a park to his home to pick up equipment he had forgotten for a soccer game. He was healthy, well rested and familiar with his vehicle. He drives on this roadway daily. This driver noticed V1 stopped at the stop sign and assumed that the driver of V1 would wait until he and the non-contact vehicle had passed before entering the intersection. This driver applied the brakes and steered left, skidding longitudinally.
