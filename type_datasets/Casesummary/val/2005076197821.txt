This single-vehicle crash occurred in a rural area on a four-lane asphalt roadway with an extended grass median. The two southbound lanes curve right and have a downhill elevation of 6%. The speed limit for the area is 104 kmph (65 mph). The crash occurred during daylight with no adverse weather or road conditions. The officer in charge confirmed no wind was present while he investigated the crash.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2004 Chevrolet Avalanche pickup towing a 4.9-meter long (16-feet) travel trailer, was southbound in the #2 lane. The driver indicated the trailer swung to the right, which the driver stated he was able to straighten out by steering towards the #1 lane, but then it swung to the left. This swing of the trailer caused the power unit to rotate in a clockwise direction. V1 departed the #1 lane in a yaw. V1's trailer began to rollover and its centrifugal forces caused the power unit if V1 to turnover. The power unit rolled left four quarter turns with the trailer remaining attached by the safety chains. V1 came to rest on the dirt shoulder on its wheels facing northwest. The trailer rolled onto its left side and came to rest on the dirt shoulder facing north. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.
The driver, a 59-year-old male, and his right front passenger, a 58-year-old female were transported due to injury. They were on their way home from a weekend camping trip and had spent the previous night in the trailer and were well rested. The driver indicated he had driven for about 80-kilometers (50-miles) when the crash occurred and he and the passenger were carrying on a conversation at this time. The driver takes Allegra & Adalat medications and wears corrective lenses.
