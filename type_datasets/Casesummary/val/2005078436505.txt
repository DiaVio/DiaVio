This two vehicle crash occurred during morning daylight hours on an urban two lane dry asphalt roadway that was under construction at the time of the crash. The crash involved two vehicles one passenger car and a pick-up truck. The posted speed limit on the roadway is 56 kmph (35 mph) The posted advisory speed limit is posted 40 kmph (25 mph) due to the construction in the area. There were"Keep Right" lane-use regulatory signs and temporary orange and white striped markers with flashing lights attached, posted at intervals to act as lane-use control while driving through the construction area. There was one travel lane available in each direction. The weather at the time was cloudy .
Vehicle one (V1) a 1989 Nissan Maxima driven by a 26 year-old male with five passengers was traveling westbound The vehicle was traveling in the only lane designated for that direction of travel. Vehicle two (V2), a 1991 Ford Ranger pickup driven by a 37 year-old male was traveling eastbound on the same roadway in the only lane designated for that direction of travel. The driver of V1 was attempting to elude immigration officers that were attempting to conduct a traffic stop on the vehicle he was driving. V1 traveled across the left lane line and its front impacted the left side of V2 The impact caused V1, to rotate counterclockwise coming to rest in the eastbound lane facing in an southeasterly direction. The impact caused V2 to rotate clockwise coming to rest in the hard pack dirt to the south facing in a southerly direction. Both vehicles were towed from the scene. The driver of V1 was located and placed under arrest for leaving the scene of a non-injury crash, endangerment and reckless driving. Police Reported Travel Speed is 48 kmph for V1 and 48 kmph for V2.
