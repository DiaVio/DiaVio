This three-vehicle collision occurred on a five-lane, dry, asphalt roadway with a two-way left turn lane. The roadway runs east/west and has a speed limit of 56 kph (35 mph). The collision occurred just before a intersection with a tri-light signal that was working properly at the time. This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one, a 2006 Nissan Altima was eastbound in the second lane. Vehicle two, a 1997 Toyota Camry was eastbound stopped in the second lane. Vehicle three, a 1995 Ford Thunderbird was stopped eastbound in the second lane ahead of vehicle two. The front of vehicle one impacted the rear of vehicle two, which caused vehicle two to travel east where its front impacted the rear of vehicle three. Vehicle one continued southeast where it came to rest in the first lane. Vehicle two came to rest facing northeast in the second lane. Vehicle three came to rest facing east in the second lane. All three vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of vehicle one, a twenty-nine year old female refused to be interviewed, however she did say that she fell asleep and woke up just before impact to hit her brakes and swerve the left.
The driver of vehicle two, a forty-year-old female was on her way to work after dropping her children off at school. This driver had only been driving for about two minutes at the time of the crash. This driver had eighteen years of driving experience. She wears glasses for a hyperopic condition, which is a restriction on her license. She has had no drivers training. This driver stated that she saw vehicle one coming in her rearview mirror. This driver drives this vehicle daily and over this road on a daily basis.
The driver of vehicle three, a thirty-year-old male was taking his wife to work at the time of the crash. This driver had been driving for about five minutes at the time of the crash. This driver is unlicensed but has three years of driving experience. This vehicle belongs to his brother and it was his first time driving it. This driver states that he does drive this route daily.
