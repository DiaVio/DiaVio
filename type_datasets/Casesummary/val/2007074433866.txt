This two-vehicle crash occurred in a four-legged, traffic signal-controlled intersection. Both roadways are straight, level, undivided, one-way and consist of three through lanes. The eastbound roadway has a five-percent downhill grade and the northbound roadway is level. The speed limit for these roadways is 48 kmph (30 mph). There is a building on the southwest corner of the intersection. This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2006 Dodge Charger, was eastbound in lane three. Vehicle two (V2), a 1996 Geo Prizm, was northbound in lane three. In the intersection, the front of V1 struck the left side of V2. V2 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, a twenty-one-year-old female, was on her way to an event at the civic auditorium from her home. She was talking with her thirty-four-year-old male passenger. She was well rested, healthy and familiar with her vehicle. She travels this route daily. This driver stated that she saw V2 when they were in the intersection and it was too late to avoid the crash.
The driver of V2, an eighteen-year-old female, was traveling with a thirty-year-old female and a four-year-old female. They were on their way home from dropping off the kids at the YMCA. This driver was healthy (pregnant and takes pre-natal vitamins), well rested and comfortable with her vehicle and passengers. This driver stated that she saw V1 at the "last second" and made no attempt to avoid the crash.
