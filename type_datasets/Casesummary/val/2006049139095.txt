The crash occurred mid-afternoon on a two-way, ten-lane, divided freeway divided by a positive barrier and a right curve in the northbound direction of travel. There were five travel lanes in the northbound direction of travel. The road was dry, traveled, level asphalt. The posted speed limit is 96 kmph (60 mph). Traffic was moderate at the time of the collision. There were no adverse weather conditions.
Vehicle one (V 1) was a 1998 Ford Explorer driven by a 23 year-old male with one passenger. It was traveling northbound in the first travel lane. A non-contact recreational vehicle attempted to merge from the second lane of travel into the first lane of travel. The driver of V 1 attempted to avoid a collision by accelerating and steering to the right; departing the roadway to the right shoulder. The driver avoided the recreational vehicle and re-entered the original traffic lane. The driver then lost directional control of the vehicle by steering right. V 1 rotated clockwise and rolled onto its left side, completing one full turn. V 1 departed the roadway to the right and came to rest off the roadway, upright and facing southbound. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.
