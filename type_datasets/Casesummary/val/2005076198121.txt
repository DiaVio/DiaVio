This single-vehicle crash occurred during morning daylight hours in a rural canyon area with various curves and elevations on a two-lane asphalt roadway. There is an emergency shoulder lane for westbound traffic. In the area of the crash, the eastbound roadway curves left, straightens out for a short length then curves right and is on a downgrade. The speed limit is 56 kmph (35 mph) and there is a warning sign posted for the downgrade and curves. The crash occurred during daylight with no adverse weather or road conditions present.
Vehicle #1, a 1997 Ford Contour 4-door sedan, driven by a 47 year-old female was traveling eastbound negotiating the left curve when, for unknown reasons, crossed over the westbound lane and onto the emergency shoulder. The V1 driver steered right entered a clockwise rotation, contacted the westbound guardrail with its left rear corner, continued to rotate clockwise, crossed both lanes of travel, departed the right road edge, and contacted a dirt embankment with the front right corner. V1 came to rest in the area of the second point of impact. V1 was towed due to damage. The driver was transported due to injury. Several attempts were made to contact the V1 driver, all were not successful. In the medical report, the driver stated simply that she lost control of her vehicle. Police Reported Travel Speed is 56 kmph for V1.
