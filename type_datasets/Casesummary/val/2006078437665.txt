This is a two-vehicle intersection crash that occurred at a four-way intersection that is controlled by a stop sign for south bound and northbound traffic at the intersection. There are two westbound and two eastbound lanes that are separated by a left turn lane. There is one northbound and one southbound lane at the south side of the intersection. There is also a left turn lane and a dedicated right turn lane. There is one southbound and one northbound lane at the north side of the intersection. The roadways are straight, level and constructed of bituminous asphalt. The speed limit for the roadways is posted 89 kmph (55 mph). It was a late afternoon crash, the roadway surface asphalt, level and dry and the sky was clear. Traffic was light at the time of the crash.
Vehicle one (V1) was a 2005 Freightliner truck tractor pulling a 16.2 meter long (53 feet) Great Dane trailer loaded with 33,113 kilograms (73,000 pounds) of furniture equipment. V1 was driven by a 35 year-old male with one passenger and was making a left turn to travel eastbound. Vehicle two (V2), a 1998 Ford F-150 4X2 pickup driven by a 31 year-old female, was traveling westbound in the #2 lane. V1 made a left turn to travel eastbound. V2 apparently applied the brakes (no lockup). The front of V2 struck the left side of the trailer of V1. The impact caused V2 to rotate counterclockwise. V2 came to a rest in the intersection across the #2 eastbound lane facing in a southeasterly direction. V1 came to a controlled stop in the intersection completely blocking the eastbound lanes. The vehicles came to rest within 2 meters (6.7 feet) of each other. V2 was towed from the scene the driver was transported to a local medical facility. V1 was driven from scene under own power. Police Reported Travel Speed is 16 kmph for V1 and 16 kmph for V2.
