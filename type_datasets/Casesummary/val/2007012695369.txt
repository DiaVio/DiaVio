The crash occurred in the third lane of the eastbound lanes of a six-lane, two-way, roadway. There were three eastbound lanes and two westbound lanes divided by a two-way left-turn lane. This was a dry, asphalt roadway that curved right and had an uphill grade. The posted speed limit was 72 kmph (45 mph). It was early evening and the sky was cloudy and traffic was congested.
V1, a 1993 Buick Lesabre was facing north pulling out of a commercial drive, planning to go straight across all lanes of traffic to a road on the other side. V2, a 2000 Ford Ranger was traveling eastbound in lane three intending to go straight. The driver of V1 stated that she had waited for more than ten seconds until it was clear to proceed. She stated that she did not see V2 because the curve of the road obstructed her view. The driver of V2 stated that he saw V1 but could not avoid her. The front of V2 contacted the left of V1 before they both came to final rest in the roadway. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1 was a 19-year old female who reported no injuries and refused treatment. She stated that she did not see V2 prior to impact. She stated that she had just left her work and was on her way to get some fast food. She stated that she was traveling between 2-16 kmph (1-10 mph) prior to the impact. She stated that she did not attempt any avoidance actions.
The driver of V2 was a 16-year old male who reported no injuries and refused treatment. He advised that he had just left from getting his hair cut and was on his way back to school. He stated that he was traveling between 67-80 kmph (41-50 mph) prior to making the evasive action to avoid contacting V1. He was unable to stop in time.
