This two-vehicle crash occurred at a three-legged intersection on a straight, level, undivided, two-lane roadway. The speed limit on this roadway is 89 kmph (55 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1993 Dodge Caravan, was westbound in lane one. Vehicle two (V2), a 2007 GMC K1500 pickup, was stopped westbound in lane one, waiting to turn left at the intersection. The front of V1 struck the back of V2. V2 then traveled to the southwest and off the roadway. The front of V2 struck a concrete pillar in the yard of a home. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 89 kmph for V1 and 89 kmph for V2.
The driver of V1, a thirty-nine-year-old male, was on his way home from work with a twenty-three-year-old male passenger. He was healthy and comfortable with his vehicle and passenger. He travels this route daily. This driver stated that he had just finished his third twelve-hour night shift and previously had been working day shifts. He also stated that he had slept only five-and-a-half hours the day before and was awake for about 17 hours when the trip started. This driver stated that he was asleep when the crash occurred.
The driver of V2, a thirty-year-old male, was on his way to work from his home. He was healthy, well rested and familiar with his vehicle. He drives on this roadway daily. This driver stated that he had been stopped, with his left turn signal on, for at least fifteen seconds prior to the crash.
