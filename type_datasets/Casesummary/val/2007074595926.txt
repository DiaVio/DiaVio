This two-vehicle crash occurred on an undivided, straight roadway. The westbound travel lane has an uphill grade and is controlled by a stop sign at the upcoming intersection. The speed limit for this roadway is 56 kmph (35 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2005 Dodge Stratus, was westbound in lane one. Vehicle two (V2), a 2007 Chevrolet Suburban, was westbound in front of V1. Vehicle three (V3), a 2002 Toyota Tundra, was westbound behind V1. V2 decelerated and the front of V1 struck the back of V2. V3 came to a stop. V1 then rolled backward and the back struck the front of V3. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, a twenty-one-year-old male, was on his way from a friend's house to school. He was well rested and familiar with his vehicle and route. This driver stated that he had been smoking marijuana and does not remember anything about the crash. He further stated that he had "blacked out" previously but that was "a long time ago." He also said that he had concerns with his family, job (adversarial relationships with coworkers) and school.
The driver of V2, a thirty-year-old male, was traveling from his home to a gym to work out. He was healthy, well rested and familiar with his vehicle. He travels this route daily. This driver stated that he was decelerating for the upcoming stop sign and did not see V1 prior to the crash.
The driver of V3, a fifty-nine-year-old male, was on his way home from work. He was well rested, healthy and familiar with his vehicle and route. This driver stated that he saw V1 and stopped when it struck V2. He was unable to avoid the crash due to traffic behind him.
