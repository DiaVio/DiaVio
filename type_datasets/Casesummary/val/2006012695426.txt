This crash occurred on a weekday morning off the right side of the southbound lanes of a divided interstate highway. The north/south highway consisted of two lanes in each direction flanked by paved shoulders and divided by a concrete barrier wall. The concrete highway was dry, straight, and level, with a posted speed limit of 112kmph (70 mph). The traffic flow was normal and there were no adverse atmospheric conditions at the time of the crash.
V1, a 1998 Buick LeSabre four door with one occupant, was southbound in lane one. As V1 continued south it began drifting to the right, across the lane line and off the paved shoulder. V1 departed the roadway and traveled 154 meters off-road. It contacted a tree to the front, a fence to the front, and the pole of the fence contacted his top. It then contacted several trees to the undercarriage, a tree down the left side, a tree down the right side and another fence to the front. V1 came to final rest with the last tree stuck into his undercarriage. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.
The driver of V1, a 47-year old male, suffered minor injuries and was transported for treatment. The driver of V1 stated that he was a diabetic and had not eaten prior to the crash. He also stated that he had only had 3.5 hours of sleep the night before. He had left work and was on his way to a job interview when he felt a diabetic episode coming on and blacked-out. He advised that he had been traveling 98-112kmph (61-70 mph) prior to him blacking out.
