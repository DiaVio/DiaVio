This two-vehicle crash occurred at a traffic signal-controlled intersection. The east/west trafficway is divided by a curbed median. The eastbound roadway is level, curves to the right, consists of three through lanes and a left turn lane and the speed limit is 56 kmph (35 mph). The north/south roadway is undivided and consists of two lanes in each direction. The northbound lanes are level, curve to the right and the speed limit is 48 kmph (30 mph). There were no adverse atmospheric conditions at the time of the crash. It was early afternoon hours on a weekend and there was little traffic on the roadways.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2007 Chevrolet Silverado pickup with two occupants, was traveling east in lane two approaching the intersection. Vehicle two (V2), a 2002 GMC Sierra pickup with one occupant, was traveling north in the second lane of traffic. The front of V1 struck the left side of V2. V2 rotated counterclockwise, entered the westbound lanes of traffic and came to rest in lane two and three of the westbound lanes, facing northeast. V1 continued to move east while rotating counterclockwise, moved off the road to the left and the front struck a traffic light pole located on the east median. V1 came to rest partially on the east median, facing west. Both vehicles were towed from the scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
An 18-year old male drove V1. According to his father, he and the right front occupant were heading to their school graduation ceremony. A further interview was refused. This driver told police he was not paying attention and did not notice his light was red. No further information is available for this driver.
A 22-year old male drove V2. This driver was not interviewed. No further information is available for this driver.
