This three-vehicle rear-end crash occurred in the early afternoon hours on a weekend. It took place on a two-way, two-lane roadway that was not physically divided. The roadway lead to a four-way intersection, which was controlled by traffic lights in all directions. The bituminous roadway was dry, straight, and level. The posted speed limit was 64kmph (40mph). There were no traffic flow problems and there were no adverse atmospheric conditions at the time of the crash.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2006 Mercury Mariner, was heading north on the roadway in lane one approaching the intersection. Vehicle two (V2), a 1997 Toyota Rav 4, was stopped in the northbound lane directly in front of V1. Vehicle three (V3), a 2000 BMW 525 was also stopped in the northbound lane in front of V2. The front of V1 contacted the back of V2. The impact then caused vehicle two to be pushed into the back of V3. All three vehicles came to final rest facing north shortly after impact. Vehicle one was the only vehicle towed from the scene due to vehicle damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Unknown for V1 and Unknown for V2.
The driver of V1, a 61 year-old male, suffered possible injuries with symptoms including stiffness, headache, and knee pain as a result of the crash. He was taken to a medical facility for observation. There were no other occupants in the vehicle when the crash occurred. After numerous attempts an interview could not be obtained with this driver. For this reason it is therefore unknown if any fatigue or health related issues contributed to this crash.
The driver of V2, a 64 year-old female was not taken to the hospital from the scene of the crash, however, she relayed in the interview that she did suffer non-incapacitating injuries. She was evaluated by a doctor because of radiating pain in her left wrist. She was also diagnosed with a cardiac contusion as a direct result of the crash. There were no other occupants in the vehicle when the crash occurred. The driver relayed that she was at a complete stop before the crash. She had noticed in her rearview mirror the other vehicle approaching. She thought to herself that this vehicle was not going to stop in time. She braced herself in her seat for the impending impact. The driver also claimed that she was fairly familiar with the roadways. She was in good physical health at the time of the crash, and no fatigue or other concerns were contributing factors. She related that her vehicle was in proper working condition when the crash took place.
The driver of V3, a 43 year-old male, was not injured as a result of the crash and did not require any medical treatment. There was one other occupant in the vehicle located in the front right seat that also did not suffer any injuries. A partial interview was conducted with this driver. He claimed that he was stopped in front of vehicle two, when the rear-end collision between the first two vehicles pushed V2 into the back of his car. No fatigue or health related factors contributed to this crash.
There was no Critical Precrash Event coded to this vehicle because it was not involved in the first harmful event. Therefore, no Critical Reason could be coded to this vehicle. Finally, no other associated factors were coded to this vehicle.
