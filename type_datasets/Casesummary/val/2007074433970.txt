This two-vehicle crash occurred in a four-legged, traffic signal-controlled intersection. The east/west trafficway is straight and divided by a curbed median. Both approach roadways consist of two through lanes, a left turn lane and a right turn lane. The eastbound approach is level and the westbound approach has an uphill grade. The speed limit for this trafficway is 64 kmph (40 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2001 Hyundai, Sonata, was stopped eastbound in lane four, waiting to turn left. A non-contact vehicle (NCV) was stopped in front of V1 waiting to turn left. Vehicle two (V2), a 2002 Ford Escape, was westbound in lane two. V1 and the NCV turned left and the front of V2 struck the right side of V1. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, a forty-seven-year-old male, was on his way to work from his home. He was healthy, well rested and familiar with his vehicle. He travels this route daily. He has asthma and takes the medications Combivent and Nexium. This driver stated that he was focused on the NCV and the traffic signal and did not see V2 prior to the crash.
The driver of V2, a forty-three-year-old female, was on her way home from a library with a three-year-old female passenger. This driver was healthy, well rested and comfortable with her vehicle and passenger. She travels this route daily. This driver stated that she saw V1 but thought that it would wait until she cleared the intersection.
