This two-vehicle crash occurred in a four-legged, traffic signal-controlled intersection. The north/south roadway is level, straight and consists of two lanes in each direction and a center left turn lane. The speed limit for this roadway is 64 kmph (40 mph). The east/west roadway is level, straight, consists of one lane in each direction and is controlled by stop signs. The speed limit for this roadway is 40 kmph (25 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2004 Ford Mustang, was northbound in lane two. Vehicle two (V2), a 1996 Toyota Camry, was stopped westbound in lane one. V2 entered the intersection and stopped. The front of V1 struck the left side of V2. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, a seventeen-year-old female, was on her way from her home to a hairdresser appointment. She was healthy, well rested and familiar with her vehicle. She travels this route daily. She has depression and takes the medication Zoloft. This driver stated that she was thinking about the homecoming dance that night and did not see V2 prior to the crash.
The driver of V2, a thirty-six-year-old male, was on his way home from a company picnic. He was well rested, healthy and familiar with his vehicle. He travels this route daily. This driver stated that he saw V1 when it was one-half block from the intersection. When he realized that he would not be able to clear the intersection before V1 got there, he stopped with the intention of backing up. However, he was not able to back up prior to the crash.
