The crash occurred during early morning hours involving one passenger car driving on an exit ramp of a freeway. The speed limit for the roadway is 64 kmph (40 mph). The road is a 2-lane undivided one-way exit ramp. The road surface is blacktop and dry and there were no detrimental atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1) is a 2005 Toyota Corolla, was traveling south in the second lane, coming from the highway. The vehicle drove off the road to it's left. The front of V1 impacted with a guardrail located east of the roadway. The vehicle pushed the guardrail backwards and drove down a 9 % downhill embankment. The vehicle drove directly into a creek where it came to rest. V1 was towed from the scene, and the driver was not injured. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.
The driver of V1 was a 36-year old female who claimed to be well rested and in good health, despite unusual short sleep. The police at the scene noted the driver was fatigued and drowsy. She possesses a valid drivers license with no restrictions or endorsements. Despite going through a separation procedure and spending the previous night at a friends' residence, she claimed to have had no previous arguments. She claimed to be not in a hurry, not conversing and attentive to the driving task. She does not take any medication. There was no indication of alcohol or drug involvement. She slept 4.5 hours the night before the crash. It was an unusual short sleep for her. She usually has an average of 8 hours sleep during a 40-hour work week. She drives the route daily and is familiar with the vehicle. She had just exited the highway and was going on the exit ramp about the posted speed limit.
