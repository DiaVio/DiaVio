This single-vehicle crash occurred on a two-lane, straight, level, undivided roadway. The speed limit on this roadway is 48 kmph (30 mph). This roadway consists of one lane in each direction and dirt/grass shoulders. This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1996 Honda Civic, was traveling westbound in lane one. V1 traveled off the right side of the roadway and the front struck a short concrete pole, a fire hydrant, another concrete pole and a fence. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.
The driver of V1, an eighty-four-year-old female, was traveling from her home on her way to the park to drop off her daughter's dog. She was in fairly good health, well rested and familiar with her vehicle. She travels this route daily. She has hypertension and takes the medication Lopressor. This driver stated that as she was traveling, she was thinking about whether her daughter would be on time. This driver stated that she saw a small animal in the roadway and applied the brakes, steering right. She traveled on the shoulder for approximately twenty meters before her vehicle struck the first pole. This driver is near-sighted and was not wearing corrective lenses at the time of the crash.
