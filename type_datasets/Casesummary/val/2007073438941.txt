This three-vehicle crash occurred at a four-legged, traffic signal-controlled intersection. The north/south roadway is undivided, level and straight and consists of two lanes in each direction. The speed limit on this roadway is 48 kmph (30 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2004 Chrysler Sebring, was northbound in lane two, approaching the intersection. Vehicle two (V2), a 2006 Dodge Charger, was stopped southbound in lane two on the north side of the intersection. Vehicle three (V3), a 2003 Ford Expedition, was stopped behind V2. V1 traveled over the center lane line, through the intersection and the front struck the front of V2. V2 moved backward and the back struck the front of V3. V1 and V2 were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, a thirty-three-year-old female, was traveling with a twenty-eight-year-old female. This driver was not interviewed but her statement to the police officer indicated that she was reaching for her ringing cell phone just prior to the crash.
The driver of V2, a forty-three-year-old female, was returning to work from lunch at home. She was healthy, well rested and familiar with her vehicle. This driver travels this route daily. She stated that she noticed V1 traveling over the lane line but was unable to attempt any avoidance maneuvers due to surrounding traffic.
The driver of V3, a fifty-eight-year-old female, was returning to work after lunch. She was in good health, well rested and familiar with her vehicle. This driver travels this route daily.
