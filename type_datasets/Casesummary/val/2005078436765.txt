This is a two-vehicle rear end configuration crash that occurred on a two-way, two lane, divided, bituminous interstate highway. The roadway consists of two westbound and two eastbound lanes separated by a depressed dirt median. To the left of the travel lanes is a 1.2 meter shoulder with rumble strip and to the right of the travel lanes is a 3.0 meter shoulder with rumble strip. The crash occurred during daylight hours on a clear morning. The road surfaces were dry, and there were no road defects or sight line restrictions and no adverse weather conditions. The roadway has a posted speed limit of 121 kph (75 mph).
Vehicle one (V1), a red 1999 Freightliner tractor, was towing a 48-foot refrigerated semi-trailer loaded with approximately 15,566 kilograms (41,706 pounds) of produce and 700 kilograms (1,875 pounds) of pallets. V1, was traveling eastbound in the number one lane at the posted speed limit when the driver apparently fell asleep. V1 continued to travel east until it struck the rear of a U-Haul trailer being towed by V2. Witness statements in the police report indicate that the driver of V1 did not take any avoidance actions before this impact. After impact with V2, V1 departed the roadway to the left on a northeast trajectory, traveled into the median, and the tractor and trailer rolled right one quarter turn and came to rest onto its right side in the median facing in a northeasterly direction. V1 was towed due to damage. The restrained V1 driver, a 32-year old male, was treated at the scene by EMS for lacerations and abrasions to his arms. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
Vehicle two (V2), a light gray 2003 Chrysler Voyager minivan, was towing a 4.9 meter long (16-foot) U-Haul enclosed 2-axle trailer that was loaded with numerous household items. V2 was traveling eastbound in the number one lane when it was struck from the rear by V1. The impact pushed the tongue of the trailer forward and also caused V2 and the trailer to jackknife. V2 departed the right side of the roadway on a southeast trajectory and came to rest facing generally with the trailer attached and facing generally south. V2 was towed due to damage. The V2 driver, a 30-year old female, sustained no injuries as a result of this crash. The first row right passenger, a 30-year old male, and the third row right passenger, a 9-year old female, were transported from the scene by EMS for minor injuries according to the police accident report. V2 was also occupied by the second left passenger, a 3-year old male, the second row middle passenger, a 5-year old male, and the third row left passenger, a 6-year old female, all of whom were not injured.
