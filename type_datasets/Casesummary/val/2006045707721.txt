This crash occurred in the early evening (dusk) of a weekday in the intersection of the northbound lanes of a suburban primary arterial roadway and a secondary side road that is an access road to a shopping center. The bituminous arterial roadway featured two northbound lanes with paved shoulders and a posted speed limit of 80kmph (50 mph). The level roadway contained a slight curve right (east) with the radius taken about 60meters south of impact measuring 450meters. The east/west bituminous side road was straight and level with a posted speed limit of 56kmph (30mph) and stop signs controlling both east and westbound traffic at the intersection. There were no adverse atmospheric conditions as it dry at the time of the crash.
V1, a 1993 Acura Vigor, had just pulled onto the arterial roadway traveling north as it accelerated in lane one. V2, a 2001 Toyota Tacoma, was stopped at the stop sign heading eastbound intending to cross over the intersection. V3, a 1986 Ford F-150 pickup, was stopped at the stop sign westbound intending to cross over the intersection. As V2 was crossing over the intersection it was struck on its right side by V1's front. The impact caused V2 to yaw to its left striking V3's front with its left. V2 then rolled to its left over the front of V3 before coming to final rest upside down on the bed of V3. V1 came to final rest on the north side of the side road facing northeast adjacent to V3, which never left its stopped position. All three vehicles were towed from the scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
V1 was driven by a belted 17 year-old male who along with the passenger in the right front seat was the beneficiary of deployed airbags. He was on his way home after leaving a nearby restaurant. He was wearing his prescription lenses that corrected a myopic (near-sighted) condition. Neither occupant was injured in the crash.
V2 was driven by a belted 26 year-old male whose traveling to his office on a work related trip. He stated to be in good health despite suffering from seasonal allergies for which he was taking an over the counter medication. He was wearing his prescription lenses that corrected a myopic (near-sighted) condition. Both the driver and his right front occupant were uninjured in the crash.
V3 was driven by a belted 31 year-old female who was on her way home after running an errand. She stated there was nothing she could do to avoid the crash as she was already stopped in the lane. The right front passenger was transported from the scene to a local hospital for treatment of possible injuries.
V3 was not involved in the first harmful event and the Critical Reason was not assigned to this vehicle. There are no known associated factors and the driver was not thought to have contributed to the crash.
