This single-vehicle crash occurred during daylight hours on a dry, divided, access-controlled roadway. This roadway is divided by a jersey barrier and consists of two lanes in each direction. The northbound lanes are curved to the right and downhill in the area of the crash. The speed limit on this roadway is 72 kmph (45 mph) and there were no adverse weather conditions at the time of the crash
Vehicle one, a 1998 Ford F-150 pickup with a camper shell, was northbound in lane two negotiating a right curve. The vehicle exited the left side of the road, the driver steered right and the vehicle returned to lane two rotating slightly clockwise. The vehicle entered lane one, the driver steered left and the vehicle rotated counterclockwise. The vehicle departed the left side of the road again and the front struck the jersey barrier. The vehicle continued rotating counterclockwise and rolled over to the right side. The top of vehicle one struck the barrier, and then the back struck the barrier as vehicle one rolled back on its wheels. The vehicle came to rest facing southwest in lane one and was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.
The driver of vehicle one, a thirty-seven-year-old female, was driving from her home to work. She was well rested at the time of the crash, exercises for one hour four times a week and takes no medication. She drives this vehicle on this roadway on a daily basis and is comfortable with both. She had a cup of coffee in the cup-holder mounted on the center instrument panel. As she was driving, the coffee began to tip. She reached for the cup and inadvertently traveled off the left side of the roadway.
