The crash occurred in the northbound lanes of a high occupancy, high volume, and high-speed interstate highway. There were five lanes northbound with both right and left side paved emergency shoulders. There were concrete wall barriers separating the northbound traffic from the southbound traffic. The road widths were a standard 3.7 meters and the emergency-paved shoulders were 3.5 meters. The road was straight and level. The conditions were dry and clear on a weekday afternoon during rush hour. The posted speed was 105 KMPH (65 MPH).
V1 was an unknown vehicle that was traveling northbound in lane four. The driver decided to change lanes into lane five and hit the right front of V2, a 2005 Toyota Matrix utility vehicle, which was traveling northbound in lane five, the HOV lane. When the driver of V2 saw V1 encroach into her lane, she braked and steered to the left. This action, combined with the impact, caused V2 to begin to rotate in a counterclockwise direction. While rotating across lane 5 and perpendicular to the lane, V2 was hit on the left side by V3, a 2001 GMC 3500 straight truck that was traveling northbound in lane five. V1 then fled the scene without stopping. V2 was towed due to damages. No one was injured. Police Reported Travel Speed is 97 kmph for V1 and 97 kmph for V2.
V1 was described as a white sedan that was traveling northbound in lane four. The driver of V2 decided to change lanes to the left into lane five and struck the front left corner of V2. A witness had stopped after the crash and stated that V1 was an American model sedan. No other information was collected on V1 as it fled the scene.
A 41-year-old woman who was a full time graduate student on her way from school to a doctor's appointment drove V2. The driver stated in her interview that she was traveling in lane five trying to pass a white vehicle that was to her right in lane four when that vehicle decided to change lanes into her lane, hitting her right front bumper. The driver stated that she hit her brakes and tried to steer away from the crash, and then her car lost control and started to fishtail sideways across lanes four and five. As she was spinning across lane five, she was hit on the left side by V3, which was traveling in lane five. The driver stated she was trying her best to straighten out and regain control of her vehicle. V2 was towed due to damages.
V3 was driven by a 22-year-old male who was traveling in lane five on his way back to his office from a job site. He stated that he and his passenger were just talking and listening to the radio when he saw a white car in lane four cut into lane five. He stated in his interview that he was not certain that the white vehicle actually made contact with V2. The driver also stated that in his opinion the driver of V2 panicked and slammed on the brakes and then lost control. The driver stated that when he saw V2 sideways in his lane he tried to slow his vehicle as quickly as possible but was not able to avoid the crash as he was in a large box truck. V3 had a buckled hood and minor damage to the hood and left bumper. It was not towed due to damages. No one was injured.
No Critical Pre-crash Event and no Critical Reason were coded to this vehicle, as it was not involved in the first harmful event. Associated factors coded to this driver include conversation with the passenger and traffic congestion.
