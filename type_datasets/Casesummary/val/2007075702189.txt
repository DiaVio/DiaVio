This two-vehicle crash occurred on a straight, level roadway that was divided by a painted median. This roadway consists of two lanes in each direction. The speed limit on this roadway is 89 kmph (55 mph). Traffic was congested and backed up from the intersection 200 meters north of the crash. This crash occurred during daylight hours and it was raining.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2004 Dodge Durango, was northbound in lane two. Vehicle two (V2), a 1996 Honda Accord, was northbound in lane two in front of V1. Vehicle three (V3), a 2002 Acura 3.2TL, was northbound in lane two behind V1. V2 stopped due to traffic. The front of V1 struck the back of V2. The front of V3 then struck the back of V1. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 0 kmph for V1 and 0 kmph for V2.
The driver of V1, a forty-nine-year-old male, was on his way home from work. He was healthy, well rested and familiar with his vehicle. He travels this route daily. This driver stated that when V2 stopped abruptly, he steered left and applied the brakes. His vehicle traveled partially into the median area, but he was unable to avoid the crash. According to the police officer at the scene, this driver was distracted by "food, objects, pet, etc."
The driver of V2, a nineteen-year-old female, was on her way from her home to a meeting. She was well rested, in good health and familiar with her vehicle. She travels this road monthly, but more often when school is in session.
The driver of V3, a fifty-six-year-old male, was on his way home from work. He was healthy, well rested and familiar with his vehicle. He travels this route daily. This driver applied the brakes and steered left when he realized that V1 was stopping, but was unable to avoid the crash.
The rain and wet roadway were associated factors in this crash.
