This two-vehicle crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions. The roadway has four-lanes and supports two-way traffic. The roadway is straight, level, undivided and bituminous. This roadway is intersected with a three-lane entrance into a mall. This intersection is controlled with traffic signals (on color). The speed limit is 56 kmph (35 mph).
Vehicle one (V1), a 2001 Honda Accord with one occupant, was westbound in lane two approaching the intersection with intentions of making a left turn to head south into the mall. Vehicle two (V2), a 1998 Honda Accord with three occupants, was eastbound in lane one approaching the intersection. V1 made the left turn as V2 was attempting to pass through the intersection. The front of V1 struck the left side of V2. V1 pulled forward and stopped facing south. V2's driver steered right in an attempt to avoid the crash. V2 was knocked right and departed the east road edge in the SE corner and struck a concrete base of a sign. V2 came to final rest facing east. V1 was towed due to disabling damage. None of the occupants in either vehicle was transported. Police Reported Travel Speed is Unknown for V1 and Unknown for V2.
The driver of V1, a sixty year-old female, was out running errands. She stated that she was in town getting her daughter settled in to her new apartment for school. She is very familiar with her vehicle but rarely drives the roadway. She had been painting her daughter's apartment all morning. She was fatigued. She was looking at her intended destination and did not see V2 prior to impact.
The driver of V2, a thirty year-old healthy male, was traveling with his two children to a nearby gas station. He is very familiar with his vehicle and the roadway. He stated that he noticed V1 attempting her turn and tried to steer right and avoid the collision.
