This three-vehicle collision occurred at at mid-afternoon during daylight hours on a weekday. The crash occurred on a curved stretch of a five-lane, two-way, undivided, dry, east-west asphalt roadway. The roadway has a slight uphill grade for westbound traffic, and the speed limit is 56 kmph (35 mph) in both directions. A two-way left turn lane separates eastbound and westbound traffic. At the time of the crash, there were no adverse atmospheric conditions, and the road surface asphalt and free of defects.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1992 Mitsubishi Mirage four-door sedan, driven by a 24 year-old male was traveling westbound in the second lane, approaching the rightward curvature. Vehicle two (V2), a 2000 Ford Ranger pickup, driven by a 50 year-old male was traveling eastbound in the second lane of opposing traffic, curving left. Vehicle three (V3), a 2005 Chrysler Town and Country minivan, driven by a 39 year-old male was traveling behind V2 in the first eastbound lane. V1 departed its lane to the left as the road curved, crossed the center lane, and entered the second eastbound lane, where the front of V2 impacted the right side of V1 in a sideswipe configuration. V1 continued forward, and the front of V1 impacted the left side of V3 in a sideswipe configuration. All three vehicles came to rest in the roadway. All three vehicles were towed from the scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
