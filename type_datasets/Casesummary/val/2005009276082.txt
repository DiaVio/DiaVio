This accident happened in the southbound lanes of a four-lane roadway divided by a grass median. The posted speed limit was 89 kmph (55 mph) and the roadway was dry, straight, and level. The weather conditions were normal on a regular sunny day.
V1, a 1988 Pontiac Fiero, was traveling northbound on the roadway and pulled into a turning lane to head west onto a residential area. V2, a 1992 Ford Ranger, was traveling southbound in the opposite direction at a constant rate of speed. V1 was driven by a 19-year old male who turned left at the intersection. V2 was driven by a 25-year old male whose vehicle struck V1 while it was turning. The front of V2 struck the right side of V1. V1 spun out of control at impact and skidded onto a small median on the west side of the intersection. V2's final rest was in lane 2 of the roadway facing north. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
On arrival of the scene, both occupants had already been taken to the hospital for treatment. Upon speaking to the driver of V1, he stated that he didn't remember anything from the accident. The last thing he said he recalls is leaving home and then waking up in the hospital the later part of the evening of the accident. An attempt to talk to the driver of V2 was unsuccessful. He left a message referring the researcher to his lawyer. Upon speaking to his lawyer, the lawyer stated that her client needs not talk to anyone other than herself.
The medical report obtained for the driver of V1 reported that he arrived via air ambulance awake and moving all extremities but confused. The diagnosis was a closed head injury.
