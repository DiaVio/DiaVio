This is a two-vehicle crash that occurred at an intersection where the east/west roadway has three westbound lanes and a left turn lane, and three eastbound lanes and a left turn lane The north/south street is a 2-way, 2-lane roadway. Both roadways are straight and level with an asphalt surface. The speed limit for both roadways is 45 MPH (73 KMPH). There are posted stop signs for the north/south roadway that were completely visible at the time of the crash. The crash occurred on a Friday morning in daylight hours on a clear day. There were no environmental factors that contributed to this crash.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1978 Chevrolet Â½ ton pickup with one occupant, was stopped, facing northbound, in the number one lane, at a stop sign. The driver of V1 looked right and left after waiting for traffic to clear, and then drove forward across the eastbound lanes. He attempted to make a left turn into the westbound lanes. Vehicle two (V2), a 2004 Infinity 4 door with one occupant, was westbound in the number three lane approaching the collision intersection. As V2 entered the intersection, the front of V2 struck the right front of V1. The vehicles side slapped and the right side of V1 struck the left side of V2. V1 rotated counterclockwise, crossed two eastbound lanes and came to rest in the number one and number two lanes facing southwest. V2 rotated clockwise, crossed the westbound lanes, crossed a curb, went over a small bush, onto the grass and came to rest against a concrete curb facing northwest. Both vehicles were towed from the scene. The driver of V2 was transported. Police Reported Travel Speed is 32 kmph for V1 and 32 kmph for V2.
V1 was driven by a 16-year-old male with only 6 months driving experience and no driver education. V1 was northbound and stopped for the traffic control device for more than 10 seconds. The driver looked to his right, then to his left, did not detect V2 approaching from his right and traveled forward across the eastbound lanes while attempting to make a left turn. The driver stated that he looked and did not see V2. The driver slept 7 hours the night before, was in good general health, and had not taken any medications. He was not wearing his prescription contact lenses. He had just left his place of employment, located at the collision intersection and was headed to his girlfriend's residence. He was employed part time and had attended a meeting. He drives the route daily and is familiar with the vehicle.
A 36-year-old female who stated she had 19 years driving experience with driver education more than five years ago drove V2. She stated she was traveling westbound in the number three lane about 45 MPH (73 KMPH). She did not see V1 until a moment before it was in front of her. The driver stated she was eating and listening to the radio just prior to the collision. She was on her way to work, drives the route daily and is familiar with the vehicle.
