This single vehicle crash occurred on the southwest side of a three-leg intersection with no controls for the primary roadway. On the east side of the intersection, the roadway is dry, level, bituminous and straight with one lane in each direction. The posted speed limit is 56 kmph (35 mph). This crash occurred at mid-afternoon, during daylight hours with moderate traffic.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1994 Saturn SL2 with one occupant, was westbound in lane one going straight approaching a three-leg intersection. The driver departed the travel lane to the left and then departed the left side of the roadway at the three-leg intersection. V1 then impacted a large tree with its front. After the impact, the vehicle had bounced backward slightly and rotated counterclockwise. V1 came to final rest on its wheels facing southwest close to the tree. V1 was towed from the scene with severe frontal damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 48 kmph for V1.
The driver of V1, a twenty year-old male suffered a seizure while driving. According to the driver's father, the driver has seizures only when he sleeps. The driver was driving the vehicle from an auto shop, where he works, to the customer's house. He stated he worked 45 hours in the past week. He was well rested, this was his first time on this roadway and first time driving this vehicle. He stated he is in good health and does not take any medications.
