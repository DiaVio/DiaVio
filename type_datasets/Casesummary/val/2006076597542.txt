This two-vehicle crash occurred on a four-lane divided asphalt-constructed roadway. This roadway runs east to west and has a railroad crossing at the area of this event. This roadway is slightly curved to the left in the eastbound direction of travel. The roadway has a posted speed of 64 kmph (40 mph). At the time of this crash the railroad crossing arms were coming down with flashing lights stopping traffic. This crash occurred during daylight with no adverse weather and good visibility.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1997 Ford pickup truck was eastbound in the first lane. Vehicle two (V2), a 1998 Peterbilt tractor with an empty materials trailer was eastbound in the first lane ahead of V1. V2 came to a stop for the railroad crossing. The front of V1 impacted the rear of V2. V1 was towed due to damage. V2 was driven from the scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is 64 kmph for V1 and 64 kmph for V2.
The driver of V1, a twenty-one year old female was on the way to the hospital for some blood tests for her pregnancy. This driver had been driving for almost twenty minutes at the time of the crash. This driver was taking pre-natal vitamins as a medication. This driver indicated that as she was approaching she looked to the right to see the train coming. When she looked back, V2 was stopped. This driver drives this vehicle daily and over this road on a weekly basis.
The driver of V2, a sixty-eight year old male had just picked up this trailer and was heading to a job site to get a load for the trailer. This driver had been driving for about twenty minutes at the time of the crash. This driver drives this vehicle daily and over this road on a daily basis.
