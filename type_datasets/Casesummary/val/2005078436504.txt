This is a single vehicle ran off the right side roadway crash that occurred during morning daylight hours on a straight, two lane westbound, two way divided asphalt roadway. there were no roadway deficiencies and no sight restrictions noted. Road surface dry asphalt there is a depressed dirt median separating the east and west bound roadways. There were no adverse weather conditions and the weather was clear at the time of the crash. The speed limit on the roadway where the crash occurred is posted 121 kmph (75 mph).
Vehicle one (V1) was a 2005 Chevrolet Avalanche SUV driven by a 17 year-old male with two passengers. V1 was traveling westbound in the number one lane at approximately 121 kmph (75 MPH) when the vehicle departed the right side of the roadway and struck a mile marker delineator post. The vehicle continued to travel westbound and rotated counterclockwise and then traveled back onto north shoulder of the westbound lanes and then rotated clockwise. The vehicle then rotated counterclockwise and traveled back into the number one westbound lane while rotating. The vehicle slid laterally in the number two lane where it tripped and began to roll to it's right. The vehicle rolled to it's right for approximately 7 quarter turns before coming to rest in the center of the roadway on it right side facing in a southwesterly direction. The vehicle was towed due to damage. The driver and one passenger were transported to a local medical facility. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.
