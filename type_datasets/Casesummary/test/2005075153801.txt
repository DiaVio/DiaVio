This three-vehicle crash occurred shortly past noon on a divided, straight, bituminous, five-lane state highway at a on-colors controlled intersection. The highway runs north and south. Northbound traffic consists of two through lanes, which have a 3.2% uphill grade. The posted speed limit is 64 kmph (40 mph). At the time there were no adverse atmospheric conditions present; however, just to the west, there were several large storm clouds.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1993 Chrysler New Yorker sedan driven by a 49 year-old female, was traveling north in lane two. Vehicle two (V2), a 1998 Chrysler Cirrus sedan driven by a 79 year-old female, was traveling north in lane two and was stopped behind traffic that was stopped for the traffic light ahead. Vehicle three (V3), a 2004 Ford Crown Victoria sedan driven by a 30 year-old female, was also north in lane two stopped in front of V2. V1 was unable to stop and its front contacted the rear of V2. V2 was pushed forward and its front contacted the rear of V3. All three vehicles came to rest at impact. V1 was towed from the scene due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 40 kmph for V1 and 40 kmph for V2.
