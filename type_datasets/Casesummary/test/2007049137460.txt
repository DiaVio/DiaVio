This two-vehicle crash occurred on an east/west, eight lane, divided, straight, level roadway with a speed limit of 64 kmph (40 mph). There are three eastbound travel lanes east of the impact and an additional left turn lane west. The roadway is intersected from the south by a pair of entrance/exits from a gas station. This crash occurred during daylight hours with light traffic. There were no adverse weather conditions at the time of the crash.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2003 Mercury Sable with one occupant, was entering the roadway from the gas station. Vehicle two (V2), a 1993 Nissan Maxima with two occupants, was eastbound in lane two changing lanes to lane three. V1 entered the roadway to turn right, crossed through lanes one and two and the front of V1 struck the right side of V2. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. None of the occupants was transported. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, a fifty-two year-old male, was traveling from the gas station to the grocery store. He was well rested, in good health and comfortable with his vehicle. He drives this route weekly. This driver stated that he was stopped for 1-2 seconds prior to entering the intersection, looked to his left and did not see V2 prior to impact.
The driver of V2, a nineteen year-old male, and a nineteen year-old male passenger were traveling from the passenger's home to school. He was well rested, in good health and comfortable with his vehicle. He drives this route daily. The driver stated that he did not see V1 prior to the collision. The driver stated that he and the passenger were talking at the time of the collision. The occupants were late for a social appointment. The driver has had one year of driving experience.
