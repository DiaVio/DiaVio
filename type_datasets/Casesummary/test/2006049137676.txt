The single vehicle crash occurred on a four-lane divided roadway with a curbed, sod median. For eastbound traffic there are two travel lanes. Eastbound traffic has a curve to the right before the location of the crash. The radius of curvature is 750 meters (2460 feet). The road surface was dry and traffic polished. The roadway is level through the location. The posted speed limit is 56 kph (35 mph). Traffic was light at the time. This crash occurred in the afternoon hours on a weekend with clear skies. There is an exit to a private parking lot located on the south side of the eastbound lanes at the location where the curve ends and the roadway is straight.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1993 Lexus ES300 with a single occupant, was eastbound in the first travel lane. A non-contact vehicle entered the eastbound lanes from a private parking area. In an effort to avoid a collision, the driver of V1 applied the brakes and steered to the left. The driver lost directional control of the vehicle as the vehicle initiated a counter clockwise yaw as it departed the travel lane on the left side, departed the roadway on the left side and struck a bridge structure located in the median separating the east and westbound travel lanes. After impact, V1 rotated counterclockwise and came to rest facing northwest, partially on the median. The vehicle was towed due to damage. EMS personnel treated the driver at the scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.
The driver of V1, a forty-five year old male was heading home from work at the time of the crash. This driver had been driving for about four minutes at the time of the crash. This driver stated that he saw the non-contact vehicle enter the roadway and believed there was no other way to avoid a collision. This driver drives this vehicle daily and over this road on a daily basis.
