This single vehicle crash occurred on a two-lane, asphalt, right-curve, rural roadway with dirt shoulders. This roadway has a posted speed limit of 56 kmph (35 mph) and has a 6% downgrade at the location of the crash. There is a curve warning sign with an advisory speed limit of 40 kmph (25 mph) just before the northbound travel lane curves to the right. This crash occurred during the late afternoon with good visibility and no adverse weather conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1996 Chevrolet Blazer, was northbound and traveled off the right side of the roadway. The driver steered left, the vehicle returned to the roadway and crosses into the opposing travel lane. The driver then steered right and the vehicle rotated clockwise. V1 then rolled over four quarter-turns as it traveled back into the right lane and then off the right side of the roadway. During the rollover event the driver was ejected from the vehicle and was fatally injured. The vehicle came to rest on the right shoulder facing southwest and was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 56 kmph for V1.
The parents of the driver were interviewed. The driver, a twenty-four-year-old female, was driving from her friend's house to her own home. She was very familiar with this route, but had been driving only six months and had little experience driving this vehicle. This driver suffered from several illnesses, including attention deficit disorder, asthma and a sleeping disorder, and was taking multiple prescription medications. According to the autopsy, this driver had a BAC of 0.102.
