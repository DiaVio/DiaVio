This two-vehicle crash occurred on a four-lane roadway. This road is straight and level with no defects noted. The road has a speed limit of 56 kmph (35 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one, a 1996 Ford Contour was southbound in the second lane. Vehicle two, a 1991 Buick Park Avenue was northbound in the second lane. Vehicle two turned right into a convenience store parking lot and the front of vehicle two impacted the left of vehicle one. Vehicle one departed the road and the undercarriage impacted a raised concrete block. Vehicle one came to rest in the parking lot. Vehicle two came to rest in the parking lot. Vehicle one was towed due to damage. Vehicle two was not towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of vehicle one, an eighteen-year-old male was driving to lunch from home. This driver had been driving for less than ten minutes at the time of the crash. This driver saw vehicle two and steered right to avoid but just barely got hit. This driver drives this vehicle daily and over this road on a daily basis.
The driver of vehicle two, a fifty-three year old male had just left home to got to the store to purchase cigarettes. This driver had been driving for about twenty minutes at the time of the crash. This driver indicated that he has very bad vision. He barely passed his drivers license vision test. This driver said that he saw vehicle one approaching and thought that it was further away. This driver says he always tries to get ahead instead of waiting for traffic. This driver was taking aspirin, Spironolactone, Altace, an Asthma inhaler and Coreg as medications. This driver drives this vehicle daily and over this road on a weekly basis.
