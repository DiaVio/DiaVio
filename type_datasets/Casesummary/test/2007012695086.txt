The crash occurred in the eastbound lanes of a six-lane, two-way highway divided by a positive barrier. There were three eastbound lanes and three westbound lanes. This was a straight, level, concrete highway with a posted speed of 112 kmph (70 mph). It was late afternoon on a weekend and it was snowing and the road was icy. Traffic was flowing steady.
V1, a 2003 Ford Crown Victoria four-door with two occupants, was traveling eastbound in lane one. The driver of V1 stated that he was attempting to merge into lane two, when he hit some ice. He stated that he did not realize how icy the road was. V1, departed the road on the left side and contacted its front to the barrier wall. V1 then spun counterclockwise and contacted its right to the median wall before coming to final rest off the north side of the highway. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.
V1, the Ford Crown Victoria was driven by a 53-year old male who reported no injuries. He advised that he had left his friend's house and was on his way to his brother-in-law's house. He advised that he had been traveling 82-97 kmph (51-60 mph) prior to loosing control. He attempted braking and steering left and right but could not regain control.
