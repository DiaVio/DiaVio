This single-vehicle collision occurred before midnight on a northbound undivided street with two way travel and two parking lanes, with a posted speed limit of 40 kmph (25 mph). It was overcast and the blacktop roadway was wet. The roadway is straight and level.
Vehicle one (V1), a Oldsmobile Cutlass Supreme coupe, driven by a 57 year-old male was traveling northbound in lane one. The driver traveled into the parking lane and collided with vehicle two (V2), a Ford F150 truck. Pushing V2 into a third vehicle, a Ford Escape 4-door. Both V2 and and the additional parked vehicle were legally parked. The right front of V1 hit the right front of V2. V2 then was pushed forward into the other parked vehicle. V1 was towed due to damage, V2 was left at the scene per owners request. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
During the interview the driver of V1 stated he had a lot on his mind, his mother having multiple strokes, friends dying from the agent orange poisoning, and his general heath. In addition, the driver very rarely sleeps more then 5 hours a night because of reoccurring nightmares from Vietnam. An associative factor assigned to this driver is driver inattention due to the family and personal concerns he had on his mind. He indicated that he blacked out while driving due to the medications he was taking.
