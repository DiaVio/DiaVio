This two-vehicle crash occurred about three and a half hours before midnight on a dry, two-lane, uphill, bituminous, roadway that runs north to south. This roadway intersects with a dry, downhill, seven-lane, bituminous roadway that runs east to west. This intersection was controlled by a traffic light. The roadway was dark but lit at the time of the crash and there were no adverse atmospheric conditions at the time of the crash.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1996 Jeep Cherokee was traveling northbound in lane one. V1 turned left in the intersection to head westbound. Vehicle two (V2), a 1996 Mercury Villager was stopped at the traffic light in lane two. V1 began its left turn just as V2 was starting from the green light. As they both entered the intersection, the front of V1 contacted the front of V2. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, a 16-year-old female had just left a church function and was headed home. In her interview, this driver stated that she wasn't paying attention for a second. She had only been driving unsupervised for 2 months.
The driver of V2 a twenty-nine year old female was not interviewed and has no known pre-crash factors.
