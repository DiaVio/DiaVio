This two vehicle crash occurred during morning daylight hours in an urban 4-way intersection controlled by traffic signals in all directions. The east/west roadway consists of 5 lanes, is straight, level and bituminous. The north/south roadway consists of 2 lanes in each direction which are divided by a raised concrete median and is straight, level and bituminous. At the time of this mid-morning crash it was clear and the road surfaces were dry, however, strong winds were present.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2001 silver Chrysler Sebring 4-door, was driven by a 19 year-old female who was traveling eastbound in lane two, stopped at the intersection intending to go straight. Vehicle two (V2) was a 1996 Saturn SL2 sedan driven by a 26 year-old male who was traveling north in lane #2. V2 entered the intersection and the front collided with the right side of V1. Both vehicles departed the initial impact on a northeast trajectory and also caused V1 to rotate counterclockwise and V2 to rotate clockwise and a side slap impact between the right side of V1 and left side of V1. V1 came to rest in the northeast corner of the intersection facing generally south. The driver of V1 stated that she had been stopped at a red light for about 2 minutes before the light turned green. V2 departed the northeast corner of the intersection and impacted a guardrail with the front plane, then coming to final rest. V1 and V2 were towed due to damage. both drivers was transported to a local medical facility. Police Reported Travel Speed is Unknown for V1 and Unknown for V2.
