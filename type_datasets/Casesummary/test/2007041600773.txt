The crash occurred in the northbound lanes of a high occupancy, high volume, and high-speed interstate highway. There were six lanes running northbound with both right and left side paved emergency shoulders. There were concrete wall barriers separating the southbound traffic. The road widths were a standard 3.7 meters and the emergency-paved shoulders were 3.5 meters. The road was straight and level. The conditions were wet and cloudy on a weekday evening during rush-hour traffic. The posted speed was a 105 KPH (65 MPH).
V1 was a 2005 Ford F-650 Flatbed Tow Truck that was traveling northbound on a call. The driver was transporting a disabled vehicle to the intended destination. The driver was traveling in lane five during rush-hour traffic and needed to change lanes to the left in to lane six, the HOV lane. As the driver changed lanes into lane six he sideswiped V2, a 2007 Pontiac G-6 that was traveling in lane six. V2 swerved into the left concrete median wall and then went across five lanes of traffic into the right side concrete wall making final rest in the right emergency paved shoulder facing northbound. V1 continued on after hitting V2 as, he did not realize he had hit another vehicle. No one was injured but V2 was towed due to damages.
The driver of V1 was a 36-year-old male who was driving the tow truck. The driver was transporting a disabled vehicle and stated tin his interview that he was changing lanes and did not realize he had hit anyone. The driver stated that due to his heavy load and the loudness he assumed he had driven over some debris in the roadway. The driver then got a call from his boss stating that the police were looking for the tow truck that matched his truck. At that point the driver turned around and went back to the crash scene. The driver stated that he never intended to hurt anyone and didn't realize he hit anything. There was minor damage to his truck with the damage confined to a piece of sheet metal bent out of place. The driver received a ticket for improper lane change and was cited for the crash.
