The crash occurred in the eastbound lanes of a 4-lane roadway divided by a concrete barrier (two eastbound lanes and two westbound lanes). This was a straight, level bituminous roadway with a posted speed limit of 64 KPH (40 MPH). There was a heavy rain falling, the roadway wet and it was dark but lighted at the time of this weekday late afternoon crash. The traffic flow was normal for that time of day.
V1, a 1997 Chevrolet Monte Carlo, was traveling east in the passing lane of the roadway approaching the tollbooths. V2, a 2004 Subaru Legacy, was traveling behind V1 in the same lane. V1 approached a crossover on the left between the east and westbound lanes. V1 attempted a left turn into the crossover. V2 attempted to avoid V1 by steering left, but was unable to stop in time and struck V1 in the left side with its front. V1 was rotated counterclockwise and came to rest facing in a northerly direction. V2 came to rest facing in a northeasterly direction. V1 was towed due to damage. Police did not consider either alcohol or illegal drugs to be involved and ordered no tests. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
V1, the Chevrolet Monte Carlo, was driven by a belted 40-year-old female who complained of pain, but refused treatment. The right rear passenger, a 4-year-old male secured in a child safety seat was not injured. Driver of V1 stated to police that she was making a left turn into the rest area on the westbound side of the roadway. No additional information was obtained as the driver could not be contacted or located.
V2, the Subaru Legacy, was driven by a belted 28-year-old female who was not injured. Driver of V2 stated to police that it appeared that V1 was signaling to use the left hand E-Z pass lane when V1 slowed/stopped to make a left hand turn prior to the toll booths. V2 stated she was unable to stop in time and struck V1 in the driver's side. No additional information was obtained as the driver could not be contacted or located.
