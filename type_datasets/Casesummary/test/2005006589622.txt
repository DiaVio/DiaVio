Case Summary #2005-06-041
The single-vehicle crash occurred off-road on the wet grass beyond the asphalt, dry, and level roadway. The posted speed limit was 64 kmph (40 mph). There weren't any traffic control devices. The crash occurred in the early morning hours on a weekday. The traffic flow was normal and the weather was clear. Police Reported Travel Speed is 89 kmph for V1.
Vehicle one, a 2003 Subaru Forester, was traveling northbound on a wide three lane roadway when it drifted off-road and contacted a tree and the guardrail located on the grass/ dirt west of the northbound road. After contacting the tree with its front left, vehicle one traveled several feet steered left rotated counter clockwise 180 degrees and contacted the metal guardrail with its right side rear where it came to final rest facing south. Vehicle one was towed due to the damages.
The driver of V1, a 34-year old male, was traveling north at approximately 89 kmph (55 mph) when the driver fell asleep and his vehicle drifted off road to the left and contacted the tree and the guardrail. The driver said that he was on his way to work and was tired from studying. Although the crash occurred at approximately 8:00 hours in the morning, he'd been awake for seven hour. The driver travels on the road daily and said he was aware of the speed limit, as well as, is familiar with the roadway. He figured he accelerated while he was asleep.
The driver said he'd awaken suddenly and realized he was off the roadway and steered to the right but it was too late he'd already contacted the tree. He then, believes he steered to the left and rotated counter clockwise while sliding on the wet grass and contacted the guardrail.
