The crash occurred on an east/west two-lane suburban residential roadway. The road curved to the north just east of impact with a radius of curvature of 489 meters, but was straight west of impact. The grade was <2% in the collision area. The posted speed limit was 64kph (40mph) and conditions were daylight and dry on a weekday morning.
Vehicle #1 was a 2000 Mazda Millenia traveling west on the two-lane road. Vehicle #2, a 2003 Chevrolet Impala, was eastbound. The Mazda (V1) crossed over into the oncoming lane as it exited the curve and struck the Impala (V2) head-on. After impact, Vehicle #1 came to rest facing north in the east lane. Vehicle #2 departed the noth side of the road, rotated clockwise and came to rest facing northeast near impact. Both vehicles were towed due to severe damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
Vehicle #1 was driven by a 29-year old male that was belted with deployed frontal airbags. He was transported to an area hospital with minor injuries. He stated that he looked down to change a CD momentarily and when he looked forward again he was in the oncoming lane. He attempted to return to his original lane but was unable to avoid a collision. Police charged him with driving on the wrong side of the road.
Vehicle #2 was operated by a 34-year old male that was belted with deployed frontal airbags. He was transported to a local hospital with moderate injuries. He said that the other vehicle came around the curve in his lane and he steered left in an attempt to avoid the crash. However, Vehicle #1 attempted to return to its initial lane and impact took place just north of the lane line. The Impala's (V2) speedometer stuck at impact at 64kph (40mph). A witness substantiated the drivers' statements that Vehicle #1 crossed the centerline.
