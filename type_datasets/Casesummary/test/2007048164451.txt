This two-vehicle crash occurred in a traffic signal-controlled intersection. The east/west roadway is straight and divided. The eastbound approach lanes are uphill and consist of three through lanes, a left turn lane and a right turn lane. The intersecting roadway north of the intersection is undivided, level and straight, with one lane in each direction. The speed limit for both of these roadways is 72 kmph (45 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1996 Honda Accord, was eastbound in lane three, approaching the intersection. Vehicle two (V2), a 1998 Nissan Altima, was stopped southbound in lane one waiting for a green signal at the intersection. Both vehicles entered the intersection and the front of V2 struck the left side of V1. After rotating, the left side of V1 struck the right side of V2. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Unknown for V1 and Unknown for V2.
The driver of V1, a sixty-five-year-old female, was traveling from a bank to a store. She was well rested and familiar with her vehicle. She drives this route weekly. This driver was not comfortable with the density of the traffic on this route and was looking ahead for a location to turn around to take an alternate route. She stated that she did not notice the traffic signal or V1.
The driver of V2, a fifty-eight-year-old female, was traveling from work to a store. She was well rested and comfortable with her vehicle. She drives this route monthly. This driver stated that she proceeded into the intersection after her signal turned green.
