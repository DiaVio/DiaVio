The crash occurred in the eastbound lanes of a five-lane residential roadway with a two-way center turn lane. There were two eastbound and two westbound lanes. The roadway had a posted speed limit of 56 kmph (35 mph) and an uphill grade of 2 percent. Conditions were daylight with clear skies. The crash occurred in the early afternoon hours of a weekday.
Vehicle one was a 2002 Chevrolet Malibu sedan. Vehicle two was a 2000 Ford Utilimaster postal van. V1 was traveling eastbound in the first lane. V2 was delivering mail to mailboxes along the roadway in the first lane ahead of V1. V2 had just completed a mailbox delivery and had started forward towards the next mailbox. The driver of V1 looked down for a moment or two and when she looked up, she realized a rear-end crash was imminent and steered left. The front right corner of V1 struck the rear left corner of V2. V1 came to rest in the roadway. V2 was pushed partially onto the curb. Police Reported Travel Speed is 56 kmph for V1 and 56 kmph for V2.
V1 was driven by a 50-year old female who was transported to a local hospital for non-incapacitating injuries. She stated to the researcher that sun glare had blinded her prior to impact. She estimated her speed to be 35-40 mph at impact. V1 was towed due to damage.
V2 was driven by a 33-year old female who was transported to a local hospital for non-incapacitating injuries. She stated the she was delivering mail on her route and had just begun to initiate her move to the next mailbox. She was struck on the rear left and pushed towards the right onto the curb. She estimated her speed to be about 2 mph. V2 was not towed due to damage.
