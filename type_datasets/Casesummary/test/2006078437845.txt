This is a two-vehicle rear end type crash that occurred on a rural concrete two-way bridge. There is no centerline stripe to divide the bridge. The bridge is used to travel over a north/south canal. There is a canal bank that borders both the west and east sides of the canal. A levee road that is divided by a rock area borders the east canal bank. The roadways are straight, level and the levee road is constructed of loose dirt. There is no posted speed limit on the roadways involved in this crash. It was an early morning crash. The roadway was dry and the sky was clear.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2005 Ford F-150 was traveling northbound on the levee road with approximately 485 kilograms (1,300 pounds) of cargo inside the vehicle. Vehicle two (V2), a 2005 Chevrolet K-2500 four-door extended cab pickup truck, was stopped on the bridge facing in a southwesterly direction. V1 approached the bridge and began to make a left turn to cross the bridge. The front of V1 impacted the rear of V2. V1 came to rest further down the road. V2 came to rest near the point of impact. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is No PAR obtained (created) for V1 and No PAR obtained (created) for V2.
The driver of V1, a twenty-three year old male, was not interviewed. This driver was charged by the investigating officer with aggravated assault on the driver of V2.
The driver of V2, a thirty-four year old male was stopped on the bride facing in a southwesterly direction when he saw V1 approaching and then making the left urn to cross the bridge. Seven occupants in an enclosed area occupied this vehicle at the time of the crash. The vehicle was used to transport undocumented immigrants and the driver, who is a border patrol agent, was not interviewed.
