This two-vehicle crash occurred in a three-legged, traffic signal-controlled intersection. The east/west roadway is level, straight and consists of one lane in each direction. The north/south roadway intersects this roadway on the north side. The north/south roadway is level, straight and consists of one lane in the northbound direction and two lanes in the southbound direction. The speed limit on these roadways is 48 kmph (30 mph). It was dark with streetlights on at the time of the crash and there were no adverse atmospheric conditions although the roadways were wet due to earlier rain.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2000 Freightliner ambulance, was southbound in lane two with the emergency lights and siren on. Vehicle two (V2), a 2001 Dodge Intrepid, was westbound in lane one. V1 attempted to turn left (eastbound) at the intersection and the left side was struck by the front of V2. V2 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, a forty-two-year-old male, was traveling with a male passenger, responding to an emergency call. This driver is familiar with his vehicle and drives this route daily. No further information is available for this driver due to supervisor's refusal to allow a complete interview.
The driver of V2, a twenty-six-year-old female, had left her home and stopped at a convenience store. She stated that she was frightened when "some homeless-looking guys" approached her vehicle. She quickly left and was traveling to a different convenience store. This driver stated that she was listening to music (loud) and watching behind her to see if she was being followed. She also stated that she did not have the vehicle's headlights on. When she realized that V1 was in her path, she applied the brakes but slid on the wet pavement. This driver was healthy, well rested and familiar with her vehicle. She travels this route daily.
