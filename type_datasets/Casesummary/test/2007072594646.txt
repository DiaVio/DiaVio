This two-vehicle crash occurred in a four-legged, traffic signal-controlled intersection. The east/west roadway is undivided, straight and level. The eastbound lanes consist of one through lane and a left turn lane. The northbound roadway is one-way, straight, level and consists of three through lanes. The speed limit for this roadway is 40 kmph (25 mph). It was dark at the time of the crash with no adverse atmospheric conditions. The area of the crash was a road construction zone.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2001 Land Rover Discovery, was eastbound in lane one. Vehicle two (V2), a 2003 Ford Crown Victoria taxi, was northbound in lane three. In the intersection, the front of V2 struck the right side of V1. V1 then rolled over one quarter-turn onto its left side. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, a forty-five-year-old male, was a valet and had just picked up V1 from the owner to take it to the parking lot. He was healthy, well rested and familiar with the roadway. This was the first time he had driven this vehicle.
The driver of V2, a forty-year-old male, was a taxi driver who had just picked up a fare (his twenty-eight-year-old male passenger). This driver was healthy, well rested and comfortable with his vehicle and passenger. He stated that he saw V1 just prior to the crash and applied the brakes in an attempt to avoid the crash.
