This single vehicle crash occurred on an urban interstate freeway. The roadway has five-lanes in the southbound direction. This road is level and made of concrete with paved shoulder on both sides and a wide center grass median. The weather was clear and dry. It was daylight with no visibility problems. The posted speed limit was 97 kmph (60 mph).
Vehicle one (V1), a 1993 Mercury Marquis was southbound in the first lane. V1 departed the roadway via the right shoulder, traveled over an embankment and became airborne for a short distance. The vehicle traveled down the embankment traveling southbound and struck a small bush. V1 traveling approximately 55 meters 180 feet) from the point of departure to final rest approximately 15 meters (49 feet) west of the road edge. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.
The driver of V1, a thirty-two year-old male was heading to work at the time of the crash. This driver was driving for about fifteen minutes at the time of the crash. This driver takes Tegretol-XR for seizure control. This driver indicated and the PAR confirmed that this driver had a seizure before the crash. This driver drives this vehicle daily and over this road on a daily basis.
