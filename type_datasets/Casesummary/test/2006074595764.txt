This two-vehicle crash occurred in a four-legged, traffic signal-controlled intersection. The north/south roadway is straight, level and consists of two lanes in each direction and a left turn lane at the intersection. The east/west roadway consists of one lane in each direction and a left turn lane at the intersection. The westbound approach is curved to the left and has a 2% downhill grade. The speed limit on these roadways is 56 kmph (35 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1999 Toyota Camry, was northbound in lane one. Vehicle two (V2), a 2006 Chevrolet Impala, was westbound in lane two, turning left (south) at the intersection. In the intersection, the front of V1 struck the left side of V2. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, an eighteen-year-old female, was traveling from her home to work. She was well rested and familiar with her vehicle and the roadway. This driver stated that she was "overwhelmed" by school and work. Additionally, she had a cold at the time of the crash and was taking Minocycline and Tylenol. This driver stated that she was thinking about work at the time of the crash.
The driver of V2, a twenty-five-year-old male, was traveling with a twenty-six-year-old female, from his home to a relative's home to watch a football game. He was well rested, healthy and comfortable with his vehicle and passenger. This driver travels this route daily. He realized that V1 was not stopping for the traffic signal and accelerated in a futile attempt to avoid the crash.
