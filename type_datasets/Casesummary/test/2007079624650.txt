This two vehicle daytime collision occurred early morning at a four-leg intersection comprised by a north/south roadway and an east/west roadway. The north/south roadway is undivided, dry, level, asphalt, curves southeast. The north leg consists of six lanes. The south leg consists of five lanes. The east/west roadway is dry, level, asphalt, and consists of seven lanes. The east leg is divided by a raised concrete island. The west leg is physically undivided. Both roadways are designated a 56 kmph (35 mph) zone. The intersection is controlled by on-color lights that were functioning at the time of the collision.
Vehicle one (V1) is a 2007 Nissan Altima, four-door sedan, driven by a twenty (20) year old male. V1 was traveling eastbound in the number one lane. Vehicle two (V2) is a 2001 Honda CR-V, sport utility vehicle,driven by a fifty six (56) year old male with one passenger. V2 was traveling southbound in the number two lane. As V2 traveled through the intersection, the front plane of V1 contacted its right plane. V1 rotated clockwise as V2 rotated counter-clockwise. The right plane of V2 then contacted the left plane of V1 in a side slap. V1 came to rest facing southeast, partially blocking the northbound number two lane. V2 came to rest against the southeast corner. V1 was towed due to major damage. V2 was towed due to moderate damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
