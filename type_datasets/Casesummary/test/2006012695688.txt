The crash occurred in the intersection of two three-lane, two-way roads. Both were straight, level, bituminous roadways with a posted speed of 72 kmph (45mph). The intersection was a four-way stop. It was evening on a weekday and the road was wet and it was raining lightly.
V1, a 2005 Ford Taurus four-door with one occupant, was traveling southbound in lane two. V2, a 2005 Hyundai Accent four-door with one occupant, was traveling westbound in lane two. The driver of V1 stated that she approached the intersection and stopped at the stop sign. She waited for two vehicles to cross and then she proceeded. She was in the middle of the intersection when she looked left and saw V2 coming at her. The driver of V2 also stated that she thought she had stopped at the stop sign. Witnesses later agreed that she did not. The front of V2 contacted the left of V1; V1 rotated counterclockwise and came to final rest by the southwest corner of the intersection. V2 continued forward and V2's front contacted V1's front. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1 was a 50-year old female who reported no injuries. She advised that she had left work and was on her way home. She advised that she had been stopped at the stop sign more than 10 seconds before proceeding, and that she was traveling between 2-16 kmph (1-10 mph) prior to being hit. She stated that she did not have time to attempt an avoidance action.
The driver of V2 was a 40-year old female who was transported to a local trauma facility. She advised that she had left home and was on her way to the grocery store. She advised that she was traveling an unknown speed when she made the evasive action to avoid contacting V1. She could not stop in time. She stated that she did not remember that much about what had happened.
