The crash occurred in the southbound lanes of a high occupancy, high volume, and high-speed interstate highway. There were five lanes southbound with both right and left side paved emergency shoulders. There were concrete wall barriers separating the southbound lanes from northbound traffic. The road widths were a standard 3.7 meters and the emergency-paved shoulders were 3.5 meters. The road was straight and level. The conditions were wet and rainy on a weekday afternoon. The posted speed was 105 KMPH (65 MPH).
V1 was a 2003 Nissan 350Z coupe that was traveling southbound in lane two when the driver lost control on the wet roads and skidded off the right side of the roadway down the grassy embankment into a chain linked fence. Before striking the fence, the undercarriage of V1 struck a raised sewer cover and the left side of V1 struck a pole. The driver was not injured but V1 was towed due to damage to the oil pan and undercarriage. Police Reported Travel Speed is No PAR obtained (created) for V1.
V1 was driven by a 42-year-old male who was on his way to an appointment with his wife. He stated that he left his home early, as he knew traffic was slow due to the low visibility and heavy rain. The driver stated he was going under the speed limit when he must have hit some oil or puddle of water as he lost control and started to hydroplane sideways off the right side of the roadway. The driver hit a chain link fence and also went over a concrete plate causing undercarriage damage.
