This single vehicle crash occurred on a five-lane, divided two-way roadway. The roadway is bituminous, straight, level and dry at the time of the crash. The speed limit on the roadway is 72 kph (45 mph). The crash occurred in the morning hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one, a 1999 Dodge Caravan was eastbound in the first lane. Vehicle one departed the roadway right at a very shallow departure angle. Vehicle one struck and sheared off a utility pole with it's front end, continued east where it clipped a second utility pole with it's left front tire, then veered right into a 1.8 M (6 Ft.) tall chain link fence. Vehicle one struck the fence initially with it's front end, then rotated right and continued east 13.3 M through the fence, taking the impact to it's left side. Vehicle one came to final rest off the roadway facing southwest against the fence. Vehicle one was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.
The driver of vehicle one, a twenty-year old male was had just got off work and was driving home. This driver had been driving for about twenty-five minutes at the time of the crash. This driver works a steady midnight shift, and gets approximately four and a half hours of sleep per day. Fatigue is considered a factor for this driver. Driver stated that he fell asleep and doesn't remember anything until the first pole impact, when he awoke with the airbag in his face, and couldn't see out of the vehicle in order to try to avoid the second pole and fence. This driver drives this vehicle daily and over this road on a daily basis. Vehicle one was equipped with a wheelchair lift. The lift was locked in place at the time of the crash.
