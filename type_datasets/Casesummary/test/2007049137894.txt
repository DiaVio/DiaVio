The two-vehicle crash occurred on a divided, east/west roadway. There are four travel lanes for eastbound traffic. This roadway is straight and has a 3.3 percent uphill grade. The posted speed limit is 72 kmph (45 mph). Traffic was heavy at the time of the crash. This crash occurred during daylight hours with clear skies.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1997 Buick LeSabre, was eastbound in the fourth travel lane. Vehicle two (V2), a 1995 Plymouth Voyager, was eastbound in the fourth travel lane ahead of V1. V2 stopped behind a line of traffic prior to an intersection. The front of V1 struck the back of V2. Both vehicles came to rest near the point of impact in the fourth travel lane. V1 was towed due to damage; V2 was driven. Police Reported Travel Speed is No PAR obtained (created) for V1 and No PAR obtained (created) for V2.
The driver of V1, an eighty-two year old male, was heading home from the mall. This driver had been driving for about two minutes, drives the route daily and is familiar with the vehicle. The driver stated he looked over his right shoulder to see if the third lane was available so that he could change lanes. When he turned back forward, V2 had stopped and he did not have time to attempt any avoidance maneuvers. The driver also stated his wife had passed away within the past week.
The driver of V2, a forty-two year old male, was heading home from lunch with his family. This driver had been driving for about ten minutes, drives the route daily and is familiar with the vehicle. The driver stated he did not notice V1 until impact and did not attempt any evasive maneuvers.
