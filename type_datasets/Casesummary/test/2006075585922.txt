This two-vehicle crash occurred on a dry, physically divided, eight-lane, four leg, light controlled intersection with pedestrian signals. The road runs east and west at the location of the crash. Eastbound traffic lanes consist of one right turn lane, three through lanes and one left turn lane. Westbound traffic is on a left curve and the lanes consist of one right turn lane, three through lanes and one left turn lane. The speed limit on the road is 72 kmph (45 mph) and it was cloudy at the time. It was cloudy at the time of the crash.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1987 Honda Accord was eastbound in lane five, the left turn lane. Vehicle two (V2), a 2002 Pontiac Grand Am was westbound in lane two. V1 waited at a red light for over 10 seconds and the light turned green. The driver started his turn and almost completed a U-turn into lane one. V2 was going through the intersection on the green light and impacted with its front to vehicle one's right side. V1 rotated counterclockwise and V2 rotated clockwise and V1 impacted with its right side to the left side of V2. V1 came to a controlled stop in lane one of the westbound traffic facing northwest on the west side of the intersection. After the second impact, V2 came to a controlled stop in the left turn lane of southbound traffic next to a curbed median facing northeast. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 48 kmph for V1 and 48 kmph for V2.
The driver of V1, a twenty-eight year old male was driving to work from home. This driver had been driving for about twenty-five minutes at the time of the crash. This driver had just moved into the area about two weeks earlier. This driver did not have a valid drivers license for this state but had a foreign drivers license. This driver stated that he typically gets about thirteen hours of sleep a night. The tires on the vehicle all had low tread depths in addition to there being three different tire sizes on this vehicle.
The driver of V2, a seventy-five year old female was driving home from dropping off a friend. This driver had been driving for about fifteen minutes at the time of the crash. This driver stated, "that all of a sudden this vehicle turned all the way". This driver drives this vehicle daily and on this road on a daily basis. This driver was taking a Calcium, Atenolol, Lisinopril and Evista as prescription medications.
