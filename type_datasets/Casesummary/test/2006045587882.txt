Case #2006-45-050
The crash occurred on an east/west two-lane rural residential roadway. The road was straight and level with private driveways located intermittently along both sides of the road. The posted speed limit was 64kph (40mph). Conditions were daylight and dry on a weekday afternoon. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.
Vehicle #1 was a 1993 Mazda Protégé traveling eastbound. The vehicle edged off the right (south) side of the road and struck a stone mailbox (90cm x 90cm) that was one meter off the road with its front right corner. After impact the vehicle rotated clockwise and continued east 21m before contacting a utility pole with its left front. It came to rest near the pole facing west just off the south edge of the road. The Mazda (V1) was towed due to damage.
Vehicle #1 was driven by a 22-year old male that was belted and not injured in the crash. He stated that he was unsure what happened or why he went off the road. He initially stated that he hit something slick causing him to lose control, but later said that he suspects something malfunctioned on the vehicle.
