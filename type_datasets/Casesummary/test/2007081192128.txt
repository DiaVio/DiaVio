This two-vehicle crash occurred mid-afternoon during daylight hours. The location was a divided interstate highway in an interchange area. The roadway was a three lane divided by a positive barrier. The left lane (lane 3) was an HOV lane and there is an on-ramp for eastbound traffic. The weather was clear and dry. The traffic was heavy due to merging traffic. The posted speed limit was 97 kmph (60 mph).
Vehicle one (V1), a 1994 Toyota Corolla sedan driven by a twenty six (26) year old female, was traveling eastbound in lane two approaching the area of the merging traffic. Vehicle two (V2), a 2006 Acura TL sedan driven by a sixty three (63) year old male, was eastbound in front of V1 in lane two. V2 was stopped in the travel lane due to a prior rear-end crash . There was also a police vehicle stopped on the right shoulder with its flashing emergency lights on. The driver of V1 took her eyes off the traffic ahead to look at the police vehicle. When she looked back V2 was stopped after it had struck the rear of another vehicle and was stopped in lane two. The driver of V1 applied the brakes and tried to steer left, but struck the left rear of V2 with the right front of V1. V1 stopped on impact and was towed due to damage. V2 was driven away from the scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
