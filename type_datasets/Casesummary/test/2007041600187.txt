This crash occurred in the intersection of a residential neighborhood. The roadway was a four-way intersection with the east/westbound lanes being controlled by stop signs. The roadway was straight and level. The conditions were dry and clear on a weekend night. The posted speed was 48 kmph (30 mph).
V1, a 1993 Jeep Cherokee with one occupant, was southbound in lane one approaching the intersection where it intended to turn left. V2, a 1991 Ford F-250 with one occupant, was northbound in lane one approaching the same intersection. V1 failed to yield the right of way and turned in front of V2. The front right of V1 struck the front left of V2. After impact V1 spun counter-clockwise and came to final rest facing north in lane two. V2 came to final rest along side V1 in the northbound traffic lane. Both vehicles were towed due to damages. Police Reported Travel Speed is 48 kmph for V1 and 48 kmph for V2.
The driver of V1, a 52-year-old male, was uninjured in the crash. He was on vacation and was on his way to pick up his niece. He stated that he rarely drove the roadway and was not that familiar with the area. He claimed to be traveling at the speed limit in lane one when he stated that he misjudged the speed and distance of V2. He was cited for the crash for failure to yield right of way.
The driver of V2, a 37-year-old male, was transported for treatment of non-incapacitating injuries. He was on his way home from fishing at the local marina and towing his boat by a trailer hitch attached to the rear of the truck. He stated in the interview that he was traveling in lane one going northbound on his way home when V1 just turned left right in front of him. He tried to steer to the left and hit the brakes to avoid the crash but was not successful.
