This three-vehicle crash occurred on a two-lane asphalt constructed roadway. This roadway was in a construction zone with proper signs and a flagman controlling traffic at the crash area. This roadway has a posted speed of 89 kmph (55 mph) with warning signs bringing the speed down at the area of this crash to 40 kmph (25 mph). This roadway is straight and level at the crash area. This crash occurred during daylight with no adverse weather and clear visibility.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1999 Chevrolet Cavalier was eastbound in the first lane. Vehicle two (V2), a 2004 Saturn Ion was stopped eastbound ahead of V1. Vehicle three (V3), a 2006 GMC pickup was stopped in the first lane ahead of V2. The front of V1 impacted the rear of V2. The front of V2 then impacted the rear of V3. V1 and V2 were towed due to damage, V3 was driven from the scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is 72 kmph for V1 and 72 kmph for V2.
The driver of V1, a twenty-two year old male, was not interviewed. The PAR indicates that this driver had been drinking and was tested with an unknown result.
The driver of V2, a fifty-year-old female was heading home from a local town. This driver had only been driving for about fifteen minutes at the time of the crash. This driver indicated that she was talking to the two passengers. They were talking and laughing. This driver indicated that she was stopped for at least ten seconds at the time of the crash. This driver was taking Femera as a medication. This driver drives this vehicle daily and over this road on a monthly basis.
The driver of V3, a forty-eight year-old driver was heading to a summer cabin from home. This driver had been driving for about two hours and fifteen minutes at the time of the crash. The driver stated that he had been stopped for about 15 seconds and had put the transmission into park waiting for a lead car to return to conduct traffic thru the construction zone. This driver had been up for about fourteen hours at the time of the crash. This driver drives this vehicle daily and over this road several times a month.
V3 was not involved in first harmful event and the critical reason was not coded to this vehicle.
