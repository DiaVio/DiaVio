This single vehicle, rollover crash, occurred just past noon on the two eastbound lanes of a rural interstate roadway. There were two lanes westbound and two lanes eastbound divided by a depressed earth median. The roadways are straight and level asphalt with no traffic controls. The posted speed limit is 121 kmph (75 mph). The surrounding terrain is open desert.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1998 Nissan Pathfinder 4x2 SUV, driven by a 46 year-old female with one passenger, was eastbound in the number one lane. The driver stated she was traveling at 113 kmph (70 mph) in the number one eastbound lane. She stated the vehicle felt strange as though she were on a bumpy road. She changed lanes into the number two lane and began to decelerate to 97 kmph (60 mph). She stated she heard the left rear tire blow out and the vehicle swerved right. She stated that she did not apply the brakes, attempted to steer hard left and the vehicle began to overturn on the roadway. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.
