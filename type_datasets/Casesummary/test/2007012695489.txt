The crash occurred off the southbound entrance ramp to a highway. This was a one-lane, one-way ramp that led onto a six-lane, two-way highway divided by a positive barrier. This ramp curved right with a radius of curvature of 86m and a super elevation of 10/122. This was a concrete ramp and a posted advisory speed of 56 kmph (35 mph). It was afternoon on a weekend and the sky was clear and the road was dry. Traffic was flowing.
V1, a 1995 Toyota Corolla four-door with one occupant was traveling eastbound around to the southbound highway. The driver started to loose control and tried to correct it by steering left and right. He departed the roadway on the left-hand side and crossed over three lanes before contacting his front to the concrete barrier wall, where he came to final rest facing east. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.
V1, the Toyota Corolla, was driven by a 21-year old male who reported minor injuries and was transported to a local trauma facility. He advised that he had left his house two hours ago and was on his way to his sister's. He advised that he was traveling between 50-64 kmph (31-40 mph) prior to loosing control. He overcorrected when he tried to avoid departing the roadway.
