This two-vehicle crash occurred in a four-legged, traffic signal-controlled intersection. Both roadways are straight, undivided and consist of one lane in each direction. The north/south roadway is level. The eastbound approach lane has a ten percent uphill grade. There are trees and bushes in the northwest corner of this intersection inside the sidewalk line. The speed limit on these roadways is 48 kmph (30 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1993 Mercury Tracer, was southbound in lane one. Vehicle two (V2), a 1988 Cadillac DeVille, was eastbound in lane two. Vehicle three (V3), a 2003 Mercury Sable, was stopped northbound in lane one. In the intersection, the front of V1 struck the left side of V2. V2 rotated clockwise, moved south and the left side struck the left front corner of V3. V1 rotated slightly counterclockwise, moved south and the left side struck the right front corner of V3. V1 and V2 were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Unknown for V1 and Unknown for V2.
The driver of V1, a nineteen-year-old male, was traveling from a relative's home to run an errand. He was healthy and well rested. He has asthma and takes Albuterol. This driver stated that he was driving above the speed limit and failed to realize that the traffic signal was red. He also stated that he was not comfortable with the vehicle's brakes or engine. This driver travels this route daily.
The driver of V2, a twenty-seven-year-old female, was traveling from her home to a store. She was healthy and familiar with her vehicle and her route. This driver stated that she works two jobs, up to seventeen hours each day, sleeps only five hours each night and was tired at the time of the crash. She also stated that she has only seven months of driving experience.
The driver of V3, a forty-two-year-old female, was traveling from her home to the post office. She was healthy, well rested and comfortable with her vehicle. This driver travels this route daily.
