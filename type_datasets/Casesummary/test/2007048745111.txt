This two-vehicle crash occurred at a three-legged intersection on an undivided, two-lane, level roadway. In the eastbound direction, this roadway curves to the right (the radius of curvature is 252 meters and the superelevation is three percent). The speed limit on this roadway is 72 kmph (45 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2000 Pontiac Montana, was eastbound in lane one. Vehicle two (V2), a 1990 Lincoln Town Car, was stopped eastbound in lane one, waiting to turn left at the intersection. The front of V1 struck the back of V2. V2 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 32 kmph for V1 and 32 kmph for V2.
The driver of V1, a forty-eight-year-old female, was not interviewed. At the scene, she told the police officer that her foot slipped off the brake as she was approaching V2. No further information is available for this driver.
The driver of V2, a twenty-five-year-old female, was on the way home from a store with four passengers. She was healthy, well rested and comfortable with her passengers. This driver travels this route daily and has been driving for ten years. This was her first time driving this vehicle. This driver stated that she had stopped and saw V1 when she looked in her rearview mirror. She turned the wheels to the right but was unable to attempt any avoidance maneuvers.
