This single vehicle crash occurred on a six lane divided roadway. This road is divided with a concrete barrier with four lanes on either side. This road is straight and level. The speed limit on this road is 105 kmph (65 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with heavy snow and blowing snow. The road was snow covered.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1993 Pontiac Grand Am was traveling south in the third lane of the roadway. As V1 was southbound, it lost control on the snow and the front impacted the median barrier. V1 rotated counterclockwise and the rear impacted the median barrier. V1 came to rest in shoulder and was towed due to damage later. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.
The driver of V1, an eighteen-year-old female, had left church and was heading home at the time of the crash. This driver had been driving for about fifteen minutes. This driver indicated that she was nervous about driving on the roadway due to the conditions. This driver indicated that during church she felt sleepy, fatigue is considered a factor for this driver. This driver drives this vehicle daily and over this road on a weekly basis.
