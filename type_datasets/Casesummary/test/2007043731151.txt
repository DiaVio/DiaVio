Case 2007-43-159
This two-vehicle crash occurred in late afternoon of a weekday in a neighborhood subdivision. The crash happened at a t-bone type intersection that consists of two city streets. The main thoroughfare is a two-lane undivided road that runs north - south, while the intersecting road runs east - west with a posted stop sign at the intersection. The bituminous roads are straight and have a posted speed limit of 56 kmph (35 mph). The conditions were clear, dry and daylight. Police Reported Travel Speed is 48 kmph for V1 and 48 kmph for V2.
V1, a 2001 Ford F150 4 x 4 with two occupants, was stopped at the intersection facing west intending to turn left. V2, a 2006 Chrysler 300C with one occupant, was northbound approaching this intersection intending to go straight. V1 turned in front of V2 and was struck in the left side (event #1). V2 continued to depart the road on the left side and struck shrubbery (event #2) that was growing in front of a home. Both drivers were transported from the scene and tow trucks removed both vehicles.
