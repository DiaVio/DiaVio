A two-vehicle collision occurred at a T intersection at dusk in the evening hours, on a Tuesday. The collision occurred in a residential intersection. The east/west leg intersects the through north/south roadway from the west. Vehicles entering the intersection from the west are controlled by a stop sign. Both roadways are two-way, two lane undivided roadways that are straight and level. The asphalt road surface was dry at the time. The posted speed limit is 40 kmph (25 mph). There were no detrimental environmental or traffic factors at the time.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1995 Volkswagen Golf 4 door, was originally headed east with the intention of turning left at the intersection to travel north. It is unknown if V1 stopped for the stop sign. Vehicle 2 (V2) a 1999 Chevrolet Lumina was traveling south in lane one, As V1 entered the intersection the front of V1 impacted the right side of V2. V2 was deflected to the east, and came to rest in the oncoming lane against the curb. V1 rotated clockwise where it came to rest. Both vehicles were left at the scene. The officer on scene said V1 was going to be towed, however the officer left as the police department was shorthanded, and it is unknown if V1 was towed or not. V1 was repossessed from the scene by the dealership. The driver of V1 was transported due to injuries. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1 was 22 year-old male. Pre-crash factors are unknown as he was not interviewed.
The driver of V2 was a 17 year-old female who is reportedly in good health with no hearing restrictions. Her near-sighted vision is corrected by contact lenses that she was wearing at the time of the collision, and the only medications she takes is Ortho-Evra, the birth control patch. She was headed home from driving to the convenience store. She had driven for less than 10 minutes, and is familiar with the roadway and the vehicle.
