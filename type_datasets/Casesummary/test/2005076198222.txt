This single-vehicle crash occurred in a rural area on a two-lane asphalt roadway. The northbound lane curves left and slightly downhill, then curves right before straightening out and becoming level in the area of impact. The speed limit for the area is 56 kph (35 mph). The crash occurred during darkness with no artificial lighting during a light rain.
Vehicle one, a 1998 Ford Contour was northbound in the first lane. The vehicle's right side tires departed the northbound road edge. The driver over-steered left, returned to the roadway and crossed over into the southbound lane. The driver then steered right and began to rotate clockwise as it departed the southbound road edge backwards. Vehicle one impacted a wooden fence with its right side plane and then a large tree with its rear end plane where it came to final rest facing south. Vehicle one was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.
The driver of vehicle one, a sixteen-year-old male had just left home to go to a convinance store. This driver had been driving for about three minutes at the time of the crash. This driver stated that he was looking down to adjust the radio controls. This driver had only been driving for about one year. This driver drives this vehicle daily and over this road on a daily basis.
