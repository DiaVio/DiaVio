This two-vehicle crash occurred on a divided, straight, level trafficway. The westbound lanes consist of two through lanes with paved shoulders on both sides. The speed limit for this roadway is 97 kmph (60 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1998 Freightliner tractor towing a tanker trailer, was westbound in lane two. Vehicle two (V2), a 2004 Dodge Stratus, was westbound in lane one. V1 began to change lanes to the right and the front struck the left side of V2. V2 rotated counterclockwise and the left side was struck by the front of V1 and then the left side of V1 struck the left side of V2. V2 then traveled off the right side of the road and the front struck a sign and a tree. V2 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 80 kmph for V1 and 80 kmph for V2.
The driver of V1, a forty-three-year-old male, was on his way from another state to a nearby town. He was healthy, well rested and travels this route several times each month. He had been driving trucks for four years and was comfortable with his vehicle and trailer. This driver stated that he did not see V2 prior to the crash; that it must have been in his blind spot.
The driver of V2, a thirty-five-year-old female, was on her way home from work. She was well rested, healthy and familiar with her vehicle. She travels this route daily. This driver stated that she did not realize that V1 was changing lanes.
