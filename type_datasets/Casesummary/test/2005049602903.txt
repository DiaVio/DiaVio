This two vehicle crash occurred during mid-morning hours on a 5-lane undivided asphalt two-way traffic roadway from east to west. The speed limit for the roadway is 48 kmph (30 mph). The third lane in east-west direction is a left turn only lane. There is an intersection preceding the west-east road. The road surface is asphalt dry and there were no detrimental atmospheric conditions at the time of the crash.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1999 Honda Accord sedan driven by a 54 year-old male was traveling eastbound in the third lane. Vehicle two (V2), a 2004 Mazda MPV driven by a 44 year-old female with one passenger was traveling west in the second lane. V1 went over the left lane line in an attempt to turning left. The front of V1 impacted the left side of V 2. V2 rotated counter clockwise and started to roll over. V1 rotated counter clockwise after the impact, and came to rest in the third lane, facing north. V2 rolled over its right side onto its roof. V2 came to rest in the second lane of traffic, facing north. V2 was towed from the scene. V1 drove away from the scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1 stated he was going straight in the third lane prior to the crash. He could not really tell why he went over the lane line. The driver of V2 said she did not see where V1 came from.
There was a fast food restaurant located at the previous intersection. This business had an observation camera recording events on its parking lot. The manager of the restaurant showed the researcher and police the recording of the crash as captured by the observation camera. On this recording (viewed in real time and slow motion) the researcher clearly saw V1 leaving a driver through/parking lot of a restaurant prior to the crash. No witnesses were found at the restaurant itself.
