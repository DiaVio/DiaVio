This single-vehicle crash occurred in a rural area on a 2-lane one-way asphalt roadway. The southbound lanes curve right and are downhill. The posted speed limit for the area is 88 kmph (55 mph) with an advisory speed of 72 kmph (45 mph) posted for the curve. At the pre-crash location, there are rumble strips across the lanes to warn of the curve. The crash occurred during late afternoon with no adverse weather or road conditions present. The crash occurred in a curve where there are numerous tire skid and yaw marks. The physical data from this crash was not documented because of the hazardous condition.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2001 Ford F-150 propane fueled crew cab pickup, was southbound in the first lane traveling at a high rate of speed, when it crossed both lanes to the left before steering right. The truck then crossed both lanes to the right while rotating clockwise and departed the right road edge. V1 contacted a dirt embankment with its front plane, and then its left back plane struck the embankment before rolling right one-quarter turn. It slid then up righted itself back onto its wheels where it came to rest on the paved shoulder facing north. V1 was towed from the scene. The driver refused medical treatment. Police Reported Travel Speed is 105 kmph for V1.
The driver of V1, a 38-year-old male, was arrested for Aggravated DUI / Alcohol and was unable to be contacted for an interview. The police report narrative states the driver was sleepy and fatigued as well as under the influence of alcohol.
