This single-vehicle crash occurred near a three-legged. The east/west roadway is straight, level, undivided and consists of one lane in each direction. The north/south roadway (on the north side of the east/west roadway) is also straight, level, undivided and consists of one lane in each direction. The southbound approach is controlled by a stop sign. The speed limit for this roadway is 72 kmph (45 mph). It was dawn at the time of this crash with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2004 Chevrolet Colorado, was southbound in lane one. V1 continued southbound across the intersection and traveled off the south side of the east/west roadway. The left side of V1 struck a sign, the front of V1 struck a ditch, a steel cable traffic barrier and a chain-link fence. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.
The driver of V1, a fifty-one-year-old male, was on his way to work from his home. He was comfortable with his vehicle and travels on this roadway daily. This driver has diabetes, depression and had a liver transplant one year prior to the crash. He takes the medications Prograf, Prednisone, Amitriptyline, Glipizide, Bactrim and Hydroxyzine. This driver was not interviewed. His wife stated that this driver told her that he did not recall the details of this crash, but did remember realizing that he "missed his turn." She also stated that he remembered traveling above the speed limit because he was late for work. Also, she stated that he was stressed due to recently starting a new job. Additionally, this driver's wife stated that he slept for ten hours the night before the crash but that this driver was "fatigued."
