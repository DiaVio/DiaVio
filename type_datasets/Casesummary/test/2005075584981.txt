This three-vehicle crash occurred during daylight hours on a dry, divided, five-lane, straight, level roadway. The speed limit on the roadway is 64 kmph (40 mph) and there were no adverse weather conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1997 Ford F-250 pickup truck was westbound in lane one of three lanes. Vehicle two (V2), a 1998 Toyota Sienna was westbound in lane one in front of V1. Vehicle three (V3), a 2002 Ford Focus was westbound in lane one in front of V2. V3 stopped when the vehicle in front of him slowed to make a turn. V2 stopped to avoid a collision with V3. The front of V1 impacted the rear of V2. The front of V2 then impacted the rear of V3. V1 and V3 were not towed. V2 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 48 kmph for V1 and 48 kmph for V2.
The driver of V1, a thirty-two-year-old male was driving from his home to work after a two-week vacation. This driver was thinking about his vacation that just ended as he was driving. This driver drives this vehicle daily and over this road on a weekly basis.
The driver of V2, a sixty-five-year-old female was driving from her home to her fitness center. This driver had been driving for about ten minutes at the time of the crash. This driver is drives this vehicle daily and over this road on a daily basis.
The driver of V3, a twenty-nine-year-old female, was traveling from her home to work. This driver had been driving for about two minutes at the time of the crash. This driver drives this vehicle daily and over this road on a daily basis.
