The two-vehicle crash occurred on an undivided, east/west roadway at the intersection with a two-lane, divided, north/south roadway. This roadway was straight, level, dry and of asphalt construction. There was road construction on the east/west roadway and there was one lane of traffic for each direction of travel. Overhead traffic control lights control traffic at the intersection. The posted speed limit is 48 kmph (30 mph). Traffic was heavy at the time of the crash. This crash occurred during daylight hours with clear skies.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1996 Chevrolet Silverado, was westbound in lane one. Vehicle two (V2), a 1998 Mitsubishi Eclipse, was eastbound in the lane one. V1 entered the intersection, turning left, and the front struck the front of V2. V1 rotated counterclockwise and came to rest in the intersection facing southwest. V2 rotated clockwise and came to rest facing southeast in the intersection. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, a twenty-four year old male, was heading home from work. This driver had been driving for about fifteen minutes, drives the route daily and is familiar with the vehicle. The driver stated the light was green and he believed that eastbound traffic had a red light.
The driver of V2, a thirty-six year old male, was heading home from work. This driver had been driving for about ten minutes, drives the route daily and is familiar with the vehicle. The driver stated the light was green. He did not realize that V1 was not going to yield the right-of-way until impact and did not attempt any evasive maneuvers.
