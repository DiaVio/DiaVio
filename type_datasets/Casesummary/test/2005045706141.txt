The crash occurred on an east/west residential arterial road at an intersection with a crossing side street. Both roads were two-way, two-lane roads with double yellow stripes leading to the intersection. North/south traffic was controlled by stop signs, while east/west traffic had no traffic control device. The arterial road was straight and level in the area involved in the crash. The southbound approach to the intersection on the crossing side street had a measured grade of +2 percent. The crash occurred in the early evening on a weekday during rush hour. Conditions were daylight and dry. The posted speed limit on the arterial road (Vehicle 2) was 64 KMPH/40 MPH and the posted speed limit on the crossing side street (Vehicles 1 and 3) was 48 KMPH/30 MPH. There was some tall grass on the northwest corner of the property bordering the intersection.
Vehicle 1, a 1995 Dodge Intrepid, had stopped at the stop sign on the southbound approach to the intersection on the crossing side street. It then accelerated across the intersection and was struck in the right side by the front of Vehicle 2, an eastbound 2004 Cadillac CTS. Vehicle 1 then rotated clockwise about 45 degrees and its left side contacted the front left corner of Vehicle 3, a 1998 Thomas school bus that was stopped northbound on the side street. Vehicle 1 came to rest facing west against the front corner of the school bus. Vehicle 2 came to rest about 8 meters east of POI still in its travel lane facing east. The school bus never moved. Vehicles 1 and 2 were towed due to damage. Vehicle 3 was driven from the scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
Vehicle 1 was driven by a 56-year old male who was belted and whose frontal airbags did not deploy. There was also a 47-year old female who was belted in the right front seating position. Both occupants were transported to a local hospital for treatment of non-incapacitating injuries. The driver of Vehicle 1 claimed to be under some stress at this point in his life and believes that he suffers from undiagnosed sleep apnea.
.
Vehicle 3 was driven by a 47-year old belted male whose vehicle was not equipped with airbags. He was uninjured in the crash. Neither the Critical Pre-crash Event nor the Critical Reason was coded to Vehicle 3 because it was not involved in the first harmful event. Associated factors coded to the driver of Vehicle 3 included prescription medications for high blood pressure, an on-going conversation with a passenger, and unfamiliarity with the roadway.
