This single-vehicle crash occurred on a jersey barrier-divided, straight trafficway. The eastbound lanes are level and consist of three through lanes with paved shoulders on both sides. In the area of the crash, there is a guardrail on the shoulder separating the roadway from a ditch. The speed limit for this roadway is 113 kmph (70 mph). It was dark at the time of the crash with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1999 Isuzu Rodeo, was eastbound in lane three. V1 traveled off the left side of the roadway. The driver steered right and V1 traveled back onto the roadway and off the right side of the roadway. The driver then steered left and the front of V1 struck the end of the guardrail. V1 rotated counterclockwise and rolled over four quarter-turns. V1 then caught fire. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 153 kmph for V1.
The driver of V1, a thirty-four-year-old male, was not interviewed. Medical records for this driver indicate that a blood alcohol test was performed with a result of 0.19. The police report indicated that this driver was speeding at the time of the crash. A witness provided some of the precrash and crash details.
