This two-vehicle crash occurred in a four-leg intersection with stop signs for both north and southbound traffic. On the north side of the intersection, there are two lanes, one lane in each direction with implied parking. On the west side of the intersection, there are two lanes, one lane in each direction with bike lanes. Southbound traffic is bituminous, straight and uphill, with a posted speed limit of 40 kmph (25 mph). Eastbound traffic is bituminous, straight and slightly uphill, with a posted speed limit of 48 kmph (30 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with moderate morning rush hour traffic.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1992 Ford Ranger with one occupant, was southbound in lane one. Vehicle two (V2), a 2003 Land Rover Discovery with one occupant, was eastbound in lane one. V1 traveled through a stop sign into the intersection where the front of V2 struck the right plane of V1. V1 at impact rotated counterclockwise and came tor rest facing northeast. The post crash travel was documented by a tire yaw mark. The driver and V1 failed to remain at the scene. V2, after the impact, came to rest on its wheels facing southeast in the southeast bike lane. V2 was towed from the scene due to moderate damage to the front. No one was transported for injuries. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, a 30 year-old male was not injured. Little is known about the driver since the researcher was unable to contact him by phone or in person.
The driver of V2, a 31 year-old female was not injured. She had just left home for work and drives this roadway daily. She is in good health, well rested, takes no medications and wears contacts for her near-sightedness. This driver failed to look ahead to the side to detect V1 earlier.
