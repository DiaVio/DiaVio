This three-vehicle/single pedestrian crash occurred near a four-legged intersection. The east/west roadway is undivided, straight, level and consists of two lanes in each direction with a center left turn lane. The north/south roadway is undivided, straight, level and consists of one lane in each direction. The southbound approach is controlled by a stop sign. The speed limit for this roadway is 56 kmph (35 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1991 Cadillac Deville, was eastbound in lane two. Vehicle two (V2), a 2004 Kia Spectra, was stopped southbound, waiting to turn left. Vehicle three (V3), a 2007 Ford F150 pickup, was parked (unoccupied) facing east, in a parking lot (bordered by a chain link fence) in the northeast corner of the intersection. A pedestrian was standing in the roadway next to the right side of V2. V1 traveled off the left side of the roadway and then entered the right side of the north/south roadway. The front of V1 struck the pedestrian and then the right side of V2. V1 continued to the east, off the left side of the north/south roadway and the front struck the fence and the back of V3. V1 and V2 were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, a ninety-four-year-old female, was on her way from a store to her part-time job. After the crash, she was transported to a hospital and died the next day. Her relatives provided some information. This driver had had a stroke in the past and had been diagnosed with a large abdominal aortic aneurysm. According to her medical reports, her spleen and the aneurysm ruptured prior to the crash.
The driver of V2, a thirty-nine-year-old male, was on his way to lunch from work with a thirty-eight-year-old male coworker. This driver was healthy, well rested and familiar with his vehicle and route. This driver stated that he was looking right at his passenger who was talking to the pedestrian. He stated that he did not see V1 prior to the crash.
The pedestrian died and no surrogate was interviewed. The driver of V2 provided the information regarding this non-motorist.
