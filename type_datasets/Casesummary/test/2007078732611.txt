This single vehicle rollover occurred on a two-way, two lane roadway. There is one southbound and one northbound lane bordered by a hard packed dirt shoulder and a wheat field. There are dirt berms that separate the wheat field and the shoulder. The lanes are separated by a painted centerline tripe. The speed limit on the roadway is posted 80 kmph (50 mph). The roadway was straight, level and constructed of bituminous asphalt. The roadway was dry and the sky was clear at the time of this afternoon crash.
Vehicle one (V1) was a 1992 Nissan 4 door Sentra 4 door sedan, occupied an unknown age or sex driver with an unknown number of passengers. V1 was traveling southbound when the vehicle departed the right side of the roadway. The vehicle then traveled back onto the roadway and the left side tires crossed the centerline. The driver then steered the vehicle to the right and the vehicle rotated clockwise and traveled across the southbound lane and eventually departed the right side of the roadway in a southwesterly direction. The vehicle continued onto the shoulder and the left side wheels struck a dirt berm and tripped. The vehicle rolled to it's left and slide on its top. V1 came to rest in a dirt field on its right side facing in a northeasterly direction. The driver of V1 and any passengers that may have been in the vehicle left the scene and could not be located by police or EMS. V1 was towed due to damage. There were no known witnesses to the collision. Police Reported Travel Speed is 80 kmph for V1.
