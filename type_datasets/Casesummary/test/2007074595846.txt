This two-vehicle crash occurred in a four-legged intersection. The north/south roadway is undivided, straight, level and consists of one lane in each direction. The speed limit for this roadway is 40 kmph (25 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1992 Geo Prizm, was southbound in lane one. Vehicle two (V2) was northbound in lane one. V2 turned left in the intersection and the front struck the front of V1. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, a nineteen-year-old female, was on her way to work from her home. She was well rested and familiar with her vehicle. She travels this route daily. This driver has bipolar disorder and takes the medications Adderall, Seroquel, Albuterol aerosol and Tylenol. She stated that she applied the brakes (skidding longitudinally) when she realized that V2 was turning.
The driver of V2 left the scene and was not interviewed. No further information is available for this driver.
