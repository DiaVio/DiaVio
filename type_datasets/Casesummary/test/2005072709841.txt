This two-vehicle crash occurred at a few hours before midnight on a westbound expressway consisting of 4 travel lanes with shoulders on both sides and a concrete barrier on the left side which separates the westbound lanes from eastbound traffic. The roadway is straight, level, concrete, and was dry at the time of the crash. It was a dark, but clear evening and the expressway was illuminated by street lights and had a posted speed limit of 89 kmph (55 mph).
Vehicle one (V1), a 1988 Buick LeSabre 4-door, was traveling in the third lane. Vehicle two (V2), a 2002 Chevrolet Blazer S-10 2-door, driven by a 50 year-old male was traveling in the #4 lane. V1, driver information unknown, departed the #3 lane into the #4 lane and impacted with the right side of V2 and the front of V1 collided. V2 lost control then entered a counterclockwise rotation and rolled right 2 quarter-turns onto its top and slid to final rest in the #4 lane facing generally west. After impact with V2, V1 departed the left side of the roadway and contacted the concrete barrier with it's left side, then came to final rest on the south shoulder, facing generally west. The V1 driver left the scene on foot and has not been located or identified. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
