This crash occurred on a two-lane undivided roadway, early in the morning on a weekend. The roadway curved to the left, was level, bituminous, and had a posted speed limit of 40 kmph (25 mph). Conditions were clear.
V1 was a 1994 Saturn SL 4-door sedan driven by a 26 year-old male. V1 was traveling northbound negotiating a curve to the left. As the vehicle negotiated the curve it went off of the roadway on the right side, reentered the roadway, crossed the double yellow lines into the southbound lane, reentered the northbound lane, and again departed the roadway on the right side. While off the roadway, the front right of V1 struck a railing attached to a building. The vehicle began to reenter the roadway for a second time, and as it did, it impacted the back of V2, which was a legally parked vehicle. V1 came to final rest in the northbound lane facing south. V2 was a 1995 Ford Windstar minivan. Police Reported Travel Speed is 80 kmph for V1 and 80 kmph for V2.
