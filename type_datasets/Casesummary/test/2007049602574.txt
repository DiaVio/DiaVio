This single vehicle crash occurred on a weekend day during early morning hours. It was dark and the roadway was not lighted. The trafficway was divided by a jersey barrier. The southbound lanes are straight and level and consist of four through lanes. The weather was clear and the roads dry. The posted speed limit is 97 kmph (60 mph).
Vehicle one (V1) in this crash was a white 2004 Nissan Titan pickup. V1 was traveling south in lane two of the roadway. V1 departed the roadway to the right and traveled down a grass embankment. V1 started to rotate counterclockwise and moved back towards the roadway. Before V1 reentered the highway, the right side of V1 impacted the leading edge of the guardrail. V1 continued to rotate counter clockwise and the front struck a guardrail wooden post. The vehicle came to rest partially on top of the guardrail. The guardrail deformed and extended into the southbound lanes of traffic. V1 was towed from the scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.
The driver of V1 was a 28-year old male. The driver stated he fell asleep at the wheel. The previous night, he worked until midnight. For the last four weeks he was working extended hours, seven days a week. His last day off was 28 days prior to the crash. His average sleep periods were about 6 hours per night. On the morning of the crash, he left work at midnight and visited a friend. He originally planned to stay at his friend's house, but he later decided to drive home instead.
