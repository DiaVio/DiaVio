This three-vehicle dawn rear-end collision occurred on a four-lane access controlled highway heading south. The roadway is straight, level and paved with concrete in the area of the crash. The roadway surface is paved with a round-stone aggregate concrete that is well worn and becomes slick during rain. The speed limit for the road is 97 kmph (60 mph). At the time of the crash it was raining lightly and the roadway was wet, but there was no standing water on the road.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1997 Ford Tractor pulling two empty semi-trailers driven by a forty eight (48) year old male, was traveling south in lane two. Vehicle two (V2), a 1993 Acura Integra coupe, driven by a twenty three (23) year old female; and, Vehicle three (V3) was a 2004 Subaru Forester SUV driven by a fifty three (53) year old female. Both V2 and V3 were also heading south in lane two. V2 & V3 decelerated for traffic congestion, but V1 was unable to stop in time and skidded into the rear of V2. This pushed V2 into V3, but the majority of damage was to the rear of V2. All three vehicles came to final rest facing south in lane two, V2 was towed from the scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
