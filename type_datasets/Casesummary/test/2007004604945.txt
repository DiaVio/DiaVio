This crash occurred in the late morning of a weekday in the center of a “T” intersection between a two-lane east/west roadway, and a one-way, single lane, northbound exit ramp. Both roadways were bituminous, dry, and straight. The east/west roadway had a 2.5% downhill grade and a posted speed limit of 56 kmph (35 mph). The northbound exit ramp had a 0.5% downhill grade and a posted speed limit of 40 kmph (25 mph). A Yield Sign controlled the northbound ramp. The traffic flow was normal and the weather was cloudy and overcast.
V1, a 2006 Chevrolet Trailblazer with one occupant, was stopped at the end of the northbound exit preparing to turn left. V2, a 2006 Honda Accord with one occupant, was eastbound in lane one. As V1 began its left turn it was struck on the left front corner by the front of V2. As V2 continued forward its left side was struck by the front of V1. Both vehicles came to final rest past the intersection facing northwest. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, a 63-year-old female, suffered minor injuries but was not transported. She stated that she stopped briefly at the intersection and looked left then right before making her turn. The driver stated that because she did not see V2 until just before the impact and made no avoidance maneuvers.
The Driver of V2, a 64-year-old female, suffered minor injuries but was not transported. She stated that she was traveling at approximately 56 kmph (35 mph) as she entered the intersection, when V1 turned across her path. She did not attempt any avoidance maneuvers.
