The crash occurred in the intersection of two roadways. One had two northbound lanes and two southbound lanes. The other roadway had two eastbound lanes and two westbound lanes. Both were bituminous roadways with a level grade and a posted speed of 72 kmph (45mph) and a traffic control signal on colors. It was evening on a weekend and the road was dry and the sky was clear. Traffic was flowing.
V1, a 2001 Pontiac Sunfire four-door with one occupant was traveling northbound in lane two. V2, a 1986 Buick Park Avenue four-door with one occupant was traveling westbound in lane one. Both vehicles were approaching the intersection intending to go straight. The driver of V1 stated that she looked at the light and it was green. She could not remember if had looked left or right but when she looked back up as she was going through the intersection the light had turned red. She applied the brakes but could not avoid V2. The driver of V2 stated that she was stopped at the light and was putting her cigarette lighter into the console. She stated that the car behind her beeped and her light was green so she stated to accelerate into the intersection. She did not see V1 prior to impact and so made no avoidance actions. The front of V1 contacted the right of V2. They then rotated and the left of V1 contacted the right of V2. V1 then departed the roadway off the northeast corner and came to final rest. V2 came to final rest in the intersection facing west. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
V1, the Pontiac Sunfire was driven by a 23-year old female. She reported minor injuries and refused treatment. She stated that she had just left her mother's house and was on her way to take her son's father a basin. She stated that she had been traveling between 67-80 kmph (41-50 mph) prior to applying the brakes.
V2, the Buick Park Avenue was driven by a 53-year old female. She reported injuries and was transported to a local trauma facility. She stated that she had just come from dropping off her son off and was on her way home. She stated that she had been stopped at the light for more than ten seconds before proceeding at 2-16 kmph (1-10 mph). She did not see V1 prior to impact.
