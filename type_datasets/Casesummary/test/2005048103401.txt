This single vehicle crash occurred during morning daylight hours on a four-lane, median divided, level, dry, bituminous, interstate roadway with a positive barrier. The roadway is fully controlled and runs north to south with no traffic control devices present at the location of the crash. There is a paved shoulder on the right side of the roadway and there were no rumble strips present.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1993 Lexus LS 400 was traveling northbound in lane two at 113 kmph (70 mph). V1 departed lane two, entered lane one, then departed the right side of the roadway and the right side of V1 impacted the guardrail. V1 began to rotate clockwise and the front plane impacted the guardrail and the vehicle began to climb up the guardrail. As the guardrail was yielding to the vehicle, the guardrail sheared a pole on the outside of the guardrail. As the pole fell it impacted V1 on the left side. V1 then contacted the guardrail again and came to rest partially in lane one and partially on the shoulder facing west. Police Reported Travel Speed is 113 kmph for V1.
The driver of V1, 23-year-old male stated that he was reaching down to pick up cigarettes and a lighter from the floorboard when he departed the roadway and struck the guardrail. Medical records for this driver indicate that less than forty five minutes before the crash, this driver had ingested Methadone. No interview was obtained for this driver so there is no sleep or work information.
