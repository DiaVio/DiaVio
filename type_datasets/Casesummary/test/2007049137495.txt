This four-vehicle crash occurred on a divided trafficway. The westbound roadway is straight, level, dry and of concrete construction. The posted speed limit is 97 kmph (60 mph). Traffic was moderate at the time of the crash. This crash occurred before sunrise with clear skies. The road was lighted.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1986 Chevrolet Silverado with one occupant, was westbound in the fifth travel lane, a high-occupancy vehicle (HOV) lane. Vehicle two (V2), a 1996 Ford F-150 with two occupants, was westbound in the fourth travel lane. Vehicle three (V3), a 2003 Ford Ranger with one occupant, was westbound in the third travel lane. Vehicle four (V4), a 2005 Dodge Ram with two occupants, was westbound in the third travel lane. V1 changed from the fifth lane into the fourth lane. The right side of V1 struck the left side of V2. V2 was redirected to the right where the front of V2 struck the back of V3. V1 continued westbound and the front of V1 struck the back left corner of V4. V4 began to rotate counterclockwise and the front of V4 struck the median concrete traffic barrier. Meanwhile, V3 began to rotate counterclockwise, over-corrected, began to rotate clockwise, crossed through lanes two and one, entered the grass roadside and rolled over to the left four quarter-turns. V2 also crossed through lanes two and one and departed the north edge of the roadway. V1 and V4 came to rest in the fourth lanes facing northwest. V2 came to rest on the two-lane service road facing west. V3 came to rest upright, facing northeast on the two-lane service road. V1 and V3 were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, a forty-six year old male, was heading to work from home. This driver had been driving for about twenty minutes, drives the route daily and is familiar with the vehicle. The driver stated he was in the fourth lane, was struck from behind and did not see the vehicle prior to impact. The driver stated that after being struck, the front of his vehicle struck the concrete traffic barrier on the south edge of the roadway. However, according to witnesses, this driver was in lane five and attempted to change lanes to the right.
The driver of V2, a twenty-three year old female, was heading to work from her sister-in-law's home. This driver had been driving for about twenty minutes, drives the route daily and is familiar with the vehicle. The driver stated that she was initially struck on the left side of the vehicle and does not recall seeing the other vehicle prior to impact. The driver stated that the steering wheel was "jerked" out of her hand and she lost control of the vehicle.
The driver of V3, a thirty-two year old male, was heading to work from home. This driver had been driving for about fifteen minutes, drives the route daily and is familiar with the vehicle. The driver stated that he heard a tire squeal to his left and remembers seeing a white vehicle prior to impact. The driver stated that he may have steered to the right just before impact.
The driver of V4, a fifty-four year old male was heading from home to a horse auction out of state. This driver had been driving for about ninety minutes, drives the route weekly and is familiar with the vehicle. The driver stated that he did not see any vehicles prior to impact and was struck from behind. The driver stated that after impact the vehicle began to rotate counterclockwise and struck the concrete traffic barrier on the south edge of the road.
V4 was not involved in the first harmful event. A critical reason was not coded to this vehicle.
