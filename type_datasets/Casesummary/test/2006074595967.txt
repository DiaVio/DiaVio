This two-vehicle crash occurred during daylight hours on a two-lane, two-way, straight, downhill roadway. The speed limit on this roadway is 56 kmph (35 mph). There were no adverse atmospheric conditions at the time of the crash.
Vehicle one, a 1996 Chevrolet Blazer, was southbound on this roadway, approaching an intersection. Vehicle two, a 1985 Chevrolet Monte Carlo, was stopped at the intersection, waiting to turn left. The driver of vehicle one applied the ABS, but the front of vehicle one struck the back of vehicle two. Both vehicles continued south and came to rest facing generally south. Vehicle two was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of vehicle one, a sixty-three-year-old male, has Parkinson's disease and is taking Lexapro, Sinemet and Lipitor. This driver only gets about six hours of sleep each night. On the day of the crash, the driver had been at the gym, as he does daily for a half-hour cardiac workout. After the workout, the driver was heading home and claimed to feel rested. The driver stated that his Parkinson's disease had not affected his driving in the past.
The driver of vehicle two, a sixty-six-year-old female, had stopped at the intersection to turn left. She was well rested and had just picked up her grandsons from their home to bring them back to her residence. At the time of the crash the driver had been stopped at the intersection for 30 to 60 seconds waiting for northbound traffic to clear when her vehicle was struck.
