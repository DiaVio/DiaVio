This two-vehicle crash occurred on the westbound lanes of a four-lane expressway. This westbound section is separated from the eastbound section by a median wall. The eastbound section did not play a role in the crash. The speed limit on the expressway was 89 kmph (55 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2004 Subaru Legacy was traveling westbound in the fourth lane. Vehicle two (V2), a 1996 Honda Odyssey was traveling westbound in the third lane. Both vehicles were traveling at a constant speed when V1 crossed over the right travel lane and with its right side coming into contact with the back left corner of V2. V1 then swerved to the left, striking the left front side against the median wall. Then V1 rolled over one quarter roll left onto its right side, and coming to a final rest facing south. V1 was towed due to damage. V2 was not towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, a forty-eight year old female, was heading to a hair salon from home at the time of the crash. This driver had been driving for about twenty minutes at the time of the crash. This driver stated that she was driving and this lane was to end and she needed to merge. This driver merged without looking to her right. This driver drives this vehicle daily and over this road on a weekly basis.
The driver of V2, a fifty-year-old female, was driving from one school to another school at the time of the crash. This driver had been driving for about fifteen minutes at the time of the crash. This driver stated that she was talking to the passenger before the crash. This driver drives this vehicle daily and over this road on a weekly basis.
