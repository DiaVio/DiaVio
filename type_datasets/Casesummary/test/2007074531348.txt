This two-vehicle crash occurred in a four-legged, traffic signal-controlled intersection. The east/west roadway is undivided, level and straight and consists of one lane in each direction and a left turn lane at the intersection. The north/south roadway is undivided and straight. The northbound approach has a downhill grade and consists of a through lane and a left turn lane. The speed limit for these roadways is 56 kmph (35 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1995 Dodge Ram van, was eastbound in lane one. Vehicle two (V2), a 1999 Chevrolet G2500 van, was northbound in lane one. In the intersection, the front of V2 struck the right side of V1. The vehicles rotated and the right side of V1 struck the left side of V2. V1 continued off the northwest corner of the intersection and the front struck a fence. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, a twenty-two-year-old male, was traveling from his home to pick up his wife at work. He was healthy, well rested and familiar with his vehicle. He travels this route weekly. This driver stated that he never saw V2 prior to the crash.
The driver of V2, an eighteen-year-old male, was on his way from work to make a delivery. He was well rested, healthy and familiar with his vehicle. This was the first time he had driven on this road. This driver stated that he saw V1 enter the intersection and applied the brakes and steered to the right in an attempt to avoid the crash.
