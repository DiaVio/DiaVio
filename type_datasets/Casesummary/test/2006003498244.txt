The crash occurred in the intersection of two roadways. The two-way north/south roadway has one lane in each direction. The one-way westbound roadway has one lane of travel. It was daylight and clear with no adverse conditions. The roadway was dry, straight, level and had a speed limit of 48kmph (30mph).
V1, a 1999 Hyundai Elantra four door with three occupants, was traveling westbound. V2, a 1995 Nissan Maxima four door with two occupants, was traveling northbound. At the intersection, the front of V1 contacted the right side of V2. Both vehicles came to a final rest shortly. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
V1, the Hyundai Elantra, was driven by a 46 year-old female who reported no injuries. The two occupants, a 43 year-old male and a 40 year-old female also reported no injuries. She advised that they were coming from home to go watch the marathon when V2 ran the steady red light. She advised that she did not attempt any avoidance maneuvers because she did not see V2.
V2, the Nissan Maxima, was driven by a 33 year-old male that reported minor injuries and was transported to a medical facility. A 40 year-old female occupant also reported minor injuries and was transported to a medical facility. The driver stated on the Police Accident Report that he went through the steady red light.
