This two-vehicle crash occurred in a four-legged, traffic signal-controlled intersection. Both roadways are straight, level and undivided. The east/west roadway consists of two lanes in each direction and the speed limit is 40 kmph (25 mph). The north/south roadway consists of one lane in each direction and the speed limit is 32 kmph (20 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2000 Pontiac Sunfire, was westbound in lane one. Vehicle two (V2), a 1999 Dodge Ram pickup, was northbound in lane one. In the intersection, the front of V1 struck the right side of V2. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, a twenty-six-year-old male, travels this route weekly and is familiar with his vehicle. He stated that he was traveling too fast (50-64 kmph/31-40 mph) to stop when he saw V2 crossing the intersection. He did apply the brakes in an attempt to avoid the crash.
The driver of V2, a seventy-five-year-old male, was on his way home from a store with two male passengers. He was healthy, well rested and comfortable with his vehicle and passengers. This driver stated that he was talking to his passengers and did not see V1 until the last second and did not have time to make any avoidance maneuvers.
