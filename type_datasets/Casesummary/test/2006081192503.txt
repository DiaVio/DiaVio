This single vehicle crash occurred mid-afternoon during daylight hours. The location was a four-way intersection at the bottom of a freeway off-ramp, which curved to the right as it approached the intersection. The off-ramp was a two lane, one way, undivided bituminous roadway, with a negative grade. There were overhead tri-color traffic signals with crosswalks and traffic was moderate. The weather was clear, the road was dry. The posted speed limit for the off-ramp 96 kmph (60 mph) and the intersection was 48 kmph (30 mph).
Vehicle one (V1), a 1996 Dodge Extended Wagon minivan, driven by a 26 year-old female was traveling southbound in the right lane of the off-ramp approaching the intersection. The vehicle entered the intersection on a green light, crossed the intersection, jumped the curb and struck a sign. The driver of V1 stated her brakes failed as she approached the intersection. The police officer at the scene examined the brakes and found them to be inoperative. On impact with the large wooden sign, V1 bounced backwards and stopped. V1 was towed due to damage and defective brakes. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.
