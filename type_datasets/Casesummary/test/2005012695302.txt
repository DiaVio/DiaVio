This crash occurred on a four-lane, two-way, dry, level, asphalt roadway on a weekday afternoon. The posted speed was 56 kmph (35 mph).
V2, a 1991 Buick Park Avenue, was eastbound as it slowed to a stop intending to left turn onto anther roadway. V1, a 1996 Pontiac Grand Prix, was also eastbound and had just changed lanes when the driver realized V2 was stopped. V1 applied his brakes but contacted the rear of V2. V1 came to final rest at the point of impact. V2 rolled down the roadway crossing the double yellow line and climbed the curb before coming to final rest over the sidewalk on the north side of the roadway nearly 49 meters from the point of impact. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
V1 was driven by a 20 year-old male in good health drove vehicle. He had just left the Secretary of States office where he had just taken his CDL test and was on his way to apply for a driver's job with the Salvation Army. This driver states that he did not see a turn signal or brake lights, he also claimed that when he applied his brakes they felt soft and his foot went to the floor. He does not remember hearing his tires locking-up.
V2 was driven by a 75 year-old man. He did not speak very good English so his daughter, an occupant of the vehicle, gave the interview. She stated that the driver is in good health but does have diabetes and a pre-existing heart condition. This driver does wear glasses for a hyperopic (far-sightedness) condition. They stated that they were on there way home from the dentist office so she could get ready for work.
