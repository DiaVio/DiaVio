This crash occurred on a weekday afternoon on a two-lane undivided roadway with a posted speed limit of 48 kmph (30 MPH). The bituminous roadway was dry, straight, and level. Traffic flow was normal and there were no adverse atmospheric conditions at the time of the crash.
V1, a 1998 Toyota Camry, was northbound when it departed the roadway to the right and struck a 50 cm diameter tree with its front. V1 came to rest facing in a northerly direction at the point of impact. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Unknown for V1.
V1 was driven by a belted 58-year old male who was transported, treated and released after sustaining minor injuries. He stated that he had been hiking for 4 hours and was on his way home at the time of the crash. He stated that he woke up that day feeling drowsy and had a very irregular sleep schedule. He did not remember how long he had slept the night before. He also advised that his doctor had given him a new prescription for his depression and it made him very sleepy. He had been "slapping" himself in the face to stay awake as he was heading home. He has poor vision and wears bifocals for a myopic (near-sighted) condition. He also takes medication for heart arrhythmia.
