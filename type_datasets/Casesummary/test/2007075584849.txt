This two-vehicle crash occurred in a four-leg, light controlled intersection. On the south side of the intersection, the roadway is undivided and the northbound lanes consist of two through lanes and one left turn lane. On the north side of the intersection, the roadway is divided by a painted median and the southbound lanes consist of two through lanes and one left turn lane. This roadway is straight, with both north and southbound on a downhill, with a posted speed limit of 64 kph (40 mph). This crash occurred during early evening hours on a lighted roadway with moderate traffic flow.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2002 Toyota 4-Runner with two occupants, was northbound in lane three, turning eastbound. Vehicle two (V2), a 2001 Saturn SL1 with one occupant, was southbound in lane two traveling straight. Both vehicles entered the intersection at the same time and V1's right side impacted with V2's front. After the impact, V1 rotated clockwise and came to rest on its wheels facing south on the southwest corner of the intersection. After the impact, V2 rotated clockwise and came to rest on its wheels facing northeast in lane one, on the south side of the intersection. Both vehicles were towed from the scene with moderate damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 24 kmph for V1 and 24 kmph for V2.
The driver of V1, a seventy-two year-old male, was driving from a restaurant to his son's house to deliver shoes to him. He was well rested, in good health and takes Vitamins and Folic Acid on a regular basis. He as an astigmatism and was wearing his glasses at the time. He is very familiar with the vehicle and drives this roadway almost every day. He stated he looked up the hill and saw vehicles in the curb lanes that were far away and no one was in the other lane. He stated as he started his turn, V2 was traveling in lane two, fast. He accelerated to avoid the collision and was hit on the right backside.
The driver of V2 is a thirty-eight year-old male. Little is known about this driver as he was taken from the scene to jail; the police smelled alcohol on his breath. After a couple of attempts by the researcher for an interview, the girlfriend of the driver stated he stated he would not call her back.
