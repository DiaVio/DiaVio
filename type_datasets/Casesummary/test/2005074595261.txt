This two vehicle crash occurred shortly before noon during daylight hours on a five lane divided concrete roadway. There were two southbound lanes and three northbound lanes with a raised curbed median that separated the two directions of travel. The road surface was dry and free from defects. The posted speed limit 64 kmph (40 mph).
Vehicle one (V1) a 1991 Oldsmobile 88 sedan driven by a 55 year-old female, was southbound in the number two travel lane. Vehicle two (V2), a 1997 Dodge Dakota 4x4 white pickup driven by a 38 year-old male, was traveling north in the second lane. V1 departed the roadway to the left and impacted a breakaway pole with her front plane. The pole then impacted the roof of V1. V2 was traveling about 48-56 kmph (30-35 mph) when he noticed V1 going the wrong way in front of him and abruptly change lanes to the left into his travel lane. The driver of V2 observed V1 heading straight at him in the second lane. With vehicles on both sides of V2, the driver had no choice but to stop and wait for the impact. V2 was struck on the right front bumper corner, sustaining minor damage to the bumper and right fender. V1 impacted with V2 about 42 meters (132 feet) after the impact with the sign. V1 came to final rest facing south in-between lanes 1 and 2 of the northbound lanes. V1 was towed from the scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
