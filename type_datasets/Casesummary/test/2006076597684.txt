This single vehicle rollover occurred on a three-lane asphalt constructed roadway. This roadway runs north to south and is just at the top of a -7 percent downgrade. This roadway has various curves with a right hand curve in the south bound direction. The posted speed limit is 89 kmph (55 mph) with the southbound lane having only one travel lane. This crash occurred during daylight with no adverse weather conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2003 Dodge Durango with a 7.3 meter long (24 feet) trailer was southbound in the first lane. After V1 topped a hill and began to descend a long grade, the driver changed gears from second to fourth. V1 began to descend the slope. The driver of V1 noticed that the trailer began to sway. He accelerated in an effort to stop the trailer swing. This action increased his speed and the trailer continued to swing side to side. As the driver continued down the road he realized the danger and jammed on the brakes hoping to control the trailer. This action caused the trailer and SUV to jackknife with the SUV rotating clockwise. After the jackknife the SUV was pushed off the roadway to the right. Once off the roadway the SUV furrowed through a drainage ditch and up an embankment. After sliding up the embankment the left wheels tripped and the SUV and trailer rolled left two complete quarters coming to rest on their tops. The SUV and trailer came to rest heading in a northwesterly direction. V1 and its trailer were towed for damages. Police Reported Travel Speed is 89 kmph for V1.
The driver of V1, a forty-three year old male was heading home from a camping trip. This driver had been driving for about two hours at the time of the crash. This driver stated that he had limited experience with a trailer this large and this was his first trip with it. He stated that he was uncomfortable towing it. He said that he should have used the trailer brakes when he first noticed the trailer was swinging but instead he chose to accelerate thinking this would straighten it out. The driver claims to be in good health with no physical problems and had rested well the night before. He stated that he was not tired or fatigued at the time of this crash. This driver drives this vehicle daily and only rarely over this roadway.
