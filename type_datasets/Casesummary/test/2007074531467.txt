This two-vehicle crash occurred in a four-legged, traffic signal-controlled intersection. The east/west trafficway is straight and divided by a curbed median. The eastbound approach is level and consists of a right turn lane, two through lanes and two left turn lanes. The westbound approach has a ten percent downhill grade. This roadway normally consists of four lanes. At the time of the crash, the two left lanes were closed (with large construction truck and traffic cones) for resurfacing. The remaining lanes were a right turn lane and a through/left turn lane. The speed limit for this trafficway is 64 kmph (40 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1997 Buick LeSabre, was westbound in lane two. Vehicle two (V2), a 1997 Ford F150 pickup, was eastbound in lane three. Several non-contact vehicles (NCVs) were stopped eastbound in lanes four and five. V1 entered the intersection, turning left, and the front struck the left side of V2. V2 traveled off the southeast corner of the intersection and the front struck the base of a utility pole. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, a seventy-two-year-old female, was traveling with a seventy-four-year-old female and a seventy-five-year-old female. They had been swimming at the YMCA and were on their way to take the passengers home. This driver was in fair health, well rested and comfortable with her vehicle and passengers. She travels this route weekly. This driver has high blood pressure and diabetes and takes the medications Metoprolol Tartrate, Glipizide and vitamins. This driver stated that she never saw V2 due to the NCVs in the westbound left turn lanes.
The driver of V2, a sixty-three-year-old male, was traveling from work to a store on a work-related errand. He was well rested, healthy and familiar with his vehicle. He travels this route daily. This driver stated that he did not see V1 prior to the crash.
