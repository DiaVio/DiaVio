This is a three-vehicle rear end type crash that occurred at 1243 hours in the #2 eastbound lane of a two-way, seven lane, dry asphalt roadway. The roadway has three eastbound lanes and three westbound lanes that are separated by a raised median. There are two dedicated through lanes and a dedicated left turn lane at the intersection. There is also an additional left turn lane that is used also for through traffic traveling eastbound through the intersection. The intersection is controlled by a traffic light on colors that was functioning properly at the time of the crash. There were no environmental factors that contributed to this crash.
V-1, a 1991 Ford F-150 4X2 pickup,driven by a 43 year-old male with one passenger was traveling eastbound in the #2 lane approaching the intersection. The driver stated that he was intending to change lanes to the left into the left turn lane to turn left. Just as he was going to change lanes, the right front passenger, said watch out . The driver then steered to the right and applied his brakes (no lock up). The front of V-1 struck the rear of V-2 that was stopped. The impact caused V-1 to rotate clockwise coming to rest in the #2 lane facing in a southeasterly direction. The vehicle was towed due to damage. V-2, a 2003 Ford F-150 4X4 pickup, driven by a 50 year-old female with one passenger was traveling eastbound in the #2 lane and was stopped for about 5 seconds at the intersection about 1Â½ -2 car lengths to the rear of V-3. The driver stated that she did not see V-1 prior to the crash. The rear of V-2 was struck by the front of V-1 and pushed forward into the back cargo area of V-3. The vehicle came to rest in the #2 lane facing east. The right front passenger, a 43-year old male, complained of pain to his back. EMS personnel transported him from the scene to a local medical facility. The vehicle was towed due to damage. V-3, a 1995 International Truck Tractor pulling a 28 foot Strick trailer loaded with about 1000 pounds of furniture/office equipment, driven by a 31 year-old male was stopped at the intersection. The driver stated that he was stopped for about 1-2 seconds when he heard tires screeching. He looked in his left side mirror and saw the brown truck (V-1) hit the rear of the white truck (V-2). The front of the white truck then hit the back of his trailer. After the impact, he eventually drove his truck around into the parking lot and waited for police. The driver stated that the light just turned red when he stopped at the intersection. The driver also saw another non-contact vehicle stopped in the left turn lane next to him. The vehicle was driven from the scene on it's own power. Police Reported Travel Speed is 40 kmph for V1 and 40 kmph for V2.
