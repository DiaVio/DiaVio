A single-vehicle collision occurred early morning during daylight hours. The collision occurred on rural two-lane, two-way roadway. The pre-crash roadway is level and straight, with bituminous paving. The weather was clear and the roadway was dry. The roadway has a posted speed limit of 72 kmph (45 mph).
Vehicle one (V1), a 1994 Subaru SVX coupe driven by a 17 year-old male was traveling eastbound in lane one. The vehicle departed the roadway via the right shoulder in a clockwise yaw, and the left driver's door area impacted a large power pole and rotated counterclockwise, and the front of the vehicle impacted a nearby fence. The driver told the police officer that the batteries in his ipod were low, and the music was cutting in and out. He had looked down too long, when he looked up he was on the shoulder. He tried to correct, and ended up heading out of control into the pole. V1 was towed from the scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.
