A two-vehicle collision occurred in the mid-afternoon during daylight hours, on a Friday. The collision occurred on a five-lane roadway when one of the vehicles attempted a left turn in front of an oncoming vehicle. The roadway runs north/south is level with bituminous paving. For traffic approaching from the south, there is a right hand curve. The roadway is level with bituminous paving. It was clear, and dry. The posted speed limit for the roadway is 64 kmph (40 mph). Access to the roadway is not controlled. Traffic at the intersection is controlled by traffic signals. There was rush hour congestion at the time.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2007 Toyota Camry 4-door with one occupant, was headed south in lane 3, was stopped and preparing to turn left and head east when oncoming traffic cleared the intersection. Vehicle two (V2), a 2000 Land Rover Discovery SUV with one occupant was northbound in lane two. As V1 entered the intersection the driver noticed the traffic light turn yellow, and proceeded when she thought it was clear. She never saw the other vehicle. The front of V1 struck the left side of V2. V1 rotated counter clockwise and came to rest adjacent to the impact point. At impact V2 rotated counter clockwise as it continued in a northeasterly direction before it initiated a roll over onto its right side and rolled six quarter turns, coming to rest on it roof on the east curb line north of the impact. Both vehicles were towed from the scene. The driver of V2 was transported. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1 was a 58 year-old female who is reportedly in good health, with weak muscles. She is familiar with the roadway and vehicle as she drives both daily. She had just left her home and was going to a friend's house to pick her up and take her to see her husband in the hospital. She is retired, and stated she was well rested. This driver did not see V2 prior to impact and attempted no evasive maneuvers. She was listening to the radio at the time.
The driver of V2 was a 46 year-old male who is reportedly in good health, with no hearing or sight restrictions. He is familiar with the vehicle and roadway as he drives both daily. He was on his way from home to the park to walk his dogs. He is unemployed at the time, and was well rested. This driver failed to detect V1 attempting a left turn in front of him and did not attempt any evasive maneuvers.
