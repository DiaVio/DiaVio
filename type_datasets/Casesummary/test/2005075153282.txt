This two-vehicle crash occurred at dusk. The crash occurred on a dry, five-lane, divided, bituminous, state highway approaching a light controlled intersection. The highway runs north and south at the location of the crash with two through lanes for southbound traffic. At this location there is a seven percent uphill grade. The speed limit on the highway is 64 kmph (45 mph) and there were no adverse atmospheric conditions present.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1999 Chrysler Concorde Lxi sedan driven by a 67 year-old male was traveling southbound in lane two. Vehicle two (V2), a 1998 Mercedes-Benz ML-320 SUV driven by a 49 year-old female was traveling southbound in lane two, just in front of the Chrysler. Vehicle two began slowing for traffic at an approaching intersection. The front of the Chrysler impacted the back of the Mercedes-Benz. The Chrysler under rode the Mercedes-Benz. Both vehicles came to rest facing southbound in lane two, with the front end of the Chrysler underneath the back of the Mercedes-Benz. Vehicle one was towed due to moderate frontal damage. Vehicle two was not towed. Police Reported Travel Speed is 48 kmph for V1 and 48 kmph for V2.
