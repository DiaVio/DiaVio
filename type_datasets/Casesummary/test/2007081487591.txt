This two-vehicle crash occurred in the evening hours on a dark but lighted section of a divided interstate, The location was just north of a junction between a busy interstate highway, and a state route highway. The road surface is concrete, with five lanes in the southbound direction. The weather was clear and dry with good visibility. The posted speed limit was 97 kmph (60 mph).
Vehicle one (V1) was a 1993 Freightliner Tractor pulling one trailer driven by a fifty nine (59) year old male. V1 was traveling southbound in lane three. Vehicle two (V2), a 1995 Chevy Geo Prism sedan driven by a fifty six (56) year old female, was traveling southbound in lane two. V1 signaled to change lanes to the right, when V2 saw this, she realized she was in the blind spot of V1, and accelerated to get out of his way. The front right corner of V1 impacted the left rear corner of V2. V2 rotated counter clockwise while moving toward the right jersey wall. V2 then impacted the right side wall with the right rear of the vehicle, and the bounced back out into the lanes. The front of V2 then impacted the right front tire area of V1, as V1 was slowing down. V2 then rotated counter-clockwise off the front right of V1, and across all other lanes in an eastbound direction and came to rest facing southwest. V1 came to a controlled stop on the right shoulder. V2 was towed due to damage. V1 was driven from the scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
