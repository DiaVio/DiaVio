This two-vehicle crash occurred on a physically divided, straight, bituminous, level, four-leg intersection with a posted speed limit of 64 kmph (40 mph). There are three through lanes and two left turn lanes for both north and southbound traffic. It was mid-morning on a weekday, the traffic flow was moderate and the sky was clear.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2003 Ford Crown Victoria was southbound in the fourth lane (left turn lane). Vehicle two (V2), a 1995 Toyota Corolla was northbound in the second lane. V1 turned left at the intersection. The front of V2 impacted the right side of V1. After the impact, V1 came to rest on its wheels facing east in the middle of the intersection in lane one of northbound traffic near the impact. V2 came to rest on its wheels facing east in the middle of the intersection in lane two of northbound traffic near the impact. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 24 kmph for V1 and 24 kmph for V2.
The driver of V1, an eighty-one year old male his friend and he went to a restaurant for breakfast and he was heading for home. He is retired and lives about 161 kilometers (100 miles) from the crash scene. He has artery problems and takes Aspirin, Altace and Metrolpolol. He is near sighted and was wearing his glasses at the time of the crash. He spent the night at a friend's house and went to bed at 2330 hours and woke up at 0630 hours. This was his first time on this roadway. He wanted to turn left but couldn't because of the curbed median. He turned right out of the restaurant parking lot and crossed the three through lanes of traffic and got into the outside left turn lane. He stated the green arrow was on has he made the turn.
