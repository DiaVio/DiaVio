This three-vehicle crash occurred at an intersection controlled by traffic control signals. There are right hand turn lanes in all directions of travel. For westbound traffic there are four travel lanes including a left turn lane on approach to the intersection. For southbound traffic, there are three travel lanes including a left turn lane. The westbound direction has a downgrade of 6 percent with a posted speed on 64 kmph (40 mph). The north/south roadway is level with a posted speed of 40 kmph (25 mph). All control lights were working properly with good visibility and no obvious road defects. The crash occurred in mid morning with moderate traffic flow.
Vehicle three (V3), a 2001 Chevrolet Tahoe with one occupant, was southbound and stopped in the left turn lane waiting for traffic to clear to turn left in the intersection. Vehicle two (V2), 1982 Chevrolet pickup with two occupants, was northbound crossing thru the intersection. Vehicle one (V1), a 2005 Ford Ranger pickup with one occupant, was westbound in lane three and entered the intersection as V2 was crossing the intersection. The front of V1 struck the right side of V2. Both vehicles moved together in a northwesterly direction where the left side of V2 struck the front of V3. The three vehicles came to rest at the impact point. V1 and V2 were towed from the scene; V3 was driven. None of the occupants were transported. Police Reported Travel Speed is 48 kmph for V1 and 48 kmph for V2.
A 48 year-old male drove V3. The driver was waiting for oncoming traffic to clear the intersection to turn left. The vehicle was stopped at impact. The driver was enroute to a vehicle maintenance shop after being on patrol. He drives the route weekly and is familiar with the vehicle. The vehicle was not involved in the first harmful event and a critical reason was not assigned to this vehicle.
