This two-vehicle rear end crash occurred on an interstate highway. A concrete traffic barrier divides the roadway, and the westbound traffic lanes consist of four through lanes that are joined by an entrance ramp on the right. This roadway is straight and level with a posted speed limit of 105 kmph (65 mph). This crash occurred in the late afternoon on a weekday with heavy rush hour congestion.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1997 Volkswagen Jetta with one occupant, was traveling westbound in lane one. Vehicle two (V2), a 2007 Honda Civic with one occupant, was stopped behind traffic in lane one, in front of V1. V1 failed to stop and the front of V1 impacted the back of V2. Both vehicles pulled to the shoulder to get out of traffic. The driver of V2 thought the driver of V1 was getting insurance information from his car when he got in it, started it up, and left the scene before the arrival of police. He was later observed in his vehicle several kilometers/miles away in another jurisdiction. The vehicle had severe front end damage and not driveable. The investigating officer for this crash was notified of the whereabouts of the driver of V1 some two hours after the crash occurred. V2 was towed due to damage. V1 was towed when the driver was apprehended. The driver of V1 was transported with a fractured arm Police Reported Travel Speed is 48 kmph for V1 and 48 kmph for V2.
The driver of V1, a fifty-year-old male, refused to give a description of the crash events or answer any questions. Little is known about this driver except that he appeared to be very intoxicated at the time of the researcher's meeting with him at the hospital emergency room. The driver of V2's account of the crash events and supplemental police statements back up this observation.
The driver of V2, a twenty-nine-year-old female, was driving from work and going home. She was well rested, very comfortable with her vehicle, and drives this route daily. She is in good health and takes allergy shots twice a month.
