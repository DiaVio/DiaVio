This three-vehicle crash occurred on an undivided, straight and level roadway. This roadway consists of one lane in each direction and was ice-covered at the time of the crash. There is a driveway on the east side of this north/south roadway. The speed limit for this roadway is 48 kmph (30 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2002 Ford Focus, was southbound in lane one. Vehicle two (V2), a 1996 Plymouth Neon, was northbound in lane one. Vehicle three (V3), a 2002 Chevrolet Monte Carlo, was southbound in lane one behind V1. V1 began to turn left into the driveway and the front struck the front of V2. The front of V3 then struck the back of V1. V1 and V2 were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, a thirty-five-year-old male, was on his way to work from his home. He was healthy, well rested and familiar with his vehicle. He travels this route daily. This driver stated that he was in a hurry, which is normal for him. He further stated that he was looking at V3 in the rear view mirror and did not see V2 prior to the crash.
The driver of V2, a nineteen-year-old female, was driving her rental car from her home to a bank. She was healthy at the time of the crash but had had only four hours of sleep rather than her usual eight hours. She travels this route daily. This driver stated that she saw V1 when she was about one-half block away and applied the brakes in an attempt to avoid the crash.
The driver of V3, a twenty-year-old female, was on her way home from driving her boyfriend to work. She was in good health, well rested and travels this route daily. She had driven this vehicle occasionally in the past three months. This driver stated that she was aware of V1and steered to the right and applied the brakes when she realized that V1 was turning.
