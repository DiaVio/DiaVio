This single-vehicle crash occurred near an entrance ramp on a jersey barrier-divided, access-controlled roadway. The eastbound lanes are level, straight and consist of four through lanes with paved shoulders on both sides. The speed limit on this roadway is 97 kmph (60 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1997 Jeep Cherokee, entered the roadway from the entrance ramp to travel eastbound. The driver of V1 suffered a seizure and V1 traveled off the left side of the roadway. The front of V1 struck the median jersey barrier. V1 came to rest facing east on the shoulder and was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.
The driver of V1, a thirty-year-old male, had just dropped off his wife at work and was on his way home. He was familiar with his vehicle and the route. He has a history of epileptic seizures and is taking Dilantin, Lexapro, Aleve and multivitamins. He slept poorly the night before this crash due to an argument with his wife and felt tired when he awoke one hour prior to the crash. This driver also stated that he is stressed due to his unemployment.
