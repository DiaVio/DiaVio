This two-vehicle crash occurred at the intersection of two three-lane one-way roadways. This intersection is controlled by a traffic signal. Both roadways have a speed limit of 56 kph (35mph). For southbound traffic the road curved to the left. During the investigation of this crash, it was noted that many vehicles tried to turn left at the intersection 1 block east of the accident site and never made it to the intersection in time to have the green light. This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse weather conditions present.
Vehicle one, a 1997 Dodge Neon was westbound in the second lane. Vehicle two, a 1996 Chrysler Sebring was southbound in the second lane. Both vehicles entered the intersection and the front of vehicle two impacted the right side of vehicle one. Both vehicles came to rest in the west end of the intersection. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of vehicle one, a thirty-five year old male was heading home to prepare to go to work after having lunch with friends. This driver had been driving for about ten minutes at the time of the crash. This driver takes no medications. The driver had nine and one half hours sleep the previous night and appeared well rested. This driver drives this vehicle daily and over this road on a weekly basis.
The driver of vehicle two, a forty-four year old female was heading home after eight hours of work. The driver had eight and one half hours of sleep the night before and was well rested. This driver stated that she did not see vehicle one until it was too late. This driver drives this vehicle daily and over this road on a weekly basis.
