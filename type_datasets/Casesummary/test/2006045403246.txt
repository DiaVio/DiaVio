The crash occurred in the southbound lanes of a four-lane undivided roadway. There were two southbound lanes, and one northbound lane with a left-turn only lane between them. The roadway was asphalt, straight, level and dry with a posted speed limit of 64kph (40mph). The crash occurred on a weekday during the evening rush hour in heavy stop and go traffic which was exacerbated by a high school sporting event near by. The weather was clear.
V1, a 2001 Audi Quattro four-door with one occupant was traveling southbound in lane two. V2, a 2003 Honda Element four-door SUV with one occupant was southbound in lane two, ahead of V1. V3, a 2005 Toyota Scion XB four-door SUV with one occupant was southbound in lane two ahead of V2. V4, a 2007 Jeep Compass four-door SUV with one occupant was southbound in lane two ahead of V3. V5, a 2005 Ford F250 pickup with one occupant was southbound in lane two ahead of V4. Vehicles 2 through 5 were stopped in a line of traffic stopped for the overhanging stop and go signals at the intersection approximately 70m ahead. V1 tried to stop to avoid a collision but it struck the rear of V2 from behind (Event 1) pushing it into the rear of V3 (Event 2), which was in turn pushed into the rear of V4 (Event 3), which was in turn pushed into the rear of V5 (Event 4). All the vehicles came to rest still southbound in lane two within a meter or two of each impact. When the researcher arrived, only V1 had not been moved from its final resting place by the towing company. All the vehicles were still at the scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
V1, the Audi Quattro, was driven by a 19-year old female who was not injured. The driver reported that she was traveling about 32kph (20mph) while on her way from home to a relative's home. She added that she did not see the traffic signal ahead or any brake lights until just before impact when her vehicle's front end struck the rear of V2 (Event 1), which stopped suddenly in front of V1.
V2, the Honda Element, was driven by a 59-year old female who was not injured. The driver said she was stopped for more than 10 seconds with her foot on the brakes. She added that she was on her way home from work and was not aware that her vehicle was going to be struck. She stated that there was a long line of traffic stopped ahead of her due to the traffic signal at the next intersection. The driver reported that she suddenly felt a jolt from behind when V1 struck hers and then a second jolt when her vehicle was pushed forward into the rear end of V3 (Event 2). She said she had stopped only about three feet behind V3 and added that she was not certain if the first impact caused her foot to slip off the brakes and possibly onto the accelerator.
V3, the Toyota Scion, was driven by a 42-year old female who has received possible injuries and was transported to a hospital. The driver of V3 reported that she had been stopped, with her foot on the brakes, for several minutes in the line of traffic stopped for the red light ahead of them. She said she was on her way home from an errand and had no indication that she was going to be struck when V2 hit her from behind knocking her vehicle forward into the rear of V4 (Event 3). She said there was about five feet between her vehicle and V4 prior to impact.
V4, the Jeep Compass, was driven by a 20-year old male reported no injury but was transported to a hospital. The driver reported that he had been stopped, with his foot on the brakes, for about two minutes behind other traffic stopped for the traffic signal ahead. He said he was on his way home from work and added that he did not know how close he was to the vehicle ahead of his. He reported suddenly being struck from behind by V3 which pushed him forward into the rear of V5 (Event 4).
V5, the Ford F250, was driven by a 35-year old male who was not injured. The driver said he was on his way home from a friend's home. The driver advised that he had been stopped for over ten seconds behind two other cars that had also stopped for the traffic signal ahead of them. He added that his foot was on the brake when he was struck from behind by V4.
