V1, a 1987 Honda Accord 4 door driven by a 22 year old male, was westbound in lane 1 of a 2 lane, 2 way, straight, bituminous roadway with a speed limit of 80kmph/50mph. At the time of the early morning crash it was daylight, dry and clear. The lone occupant of v1 fell asleep at the wheel and drifted off the roadway to the right. He awoke while off-road, struck a sign with the front of v1 and was heading for a tree. He steered left but still struck the tree with the right side of v1, veered left, crossed both lanes of the roadway, exited the roadway to the left and struck another tree >10cm with v1's front right. V1 rotated clockwise and came to rest next to the tree facing north-northwest. The driver was injured and transported. V1 was towed.
A partial driver interview and complete vehicle inspection was obtained for v1. The Pre-event movement of v1 was going straight, the critical pre-crash event was This vehicle traveling off the edge of the road on the right side and the Critical reason for the critical pre-crash event was the driver related factor of sleeping/asleep at the wheel. Some factors which may have contributed to the crash include the driver's drowsiness/fatigue at the beginning of his trip (he had attended a party the night before, consumed alcohol 9 hours prior to the crash and not slept at home) and his unfamiliarity with the roadway. It is unknown how far or how long he had driven prior to the crash or whether he was in a hurry. It is not believed that speed was a factor in the crash. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.
A complete interview was obtained for a witness (obtained at the scene) who was traveling in the opposite direction. The witness interview supports the above description of the crash.
