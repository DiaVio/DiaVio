This two-vehicle collision occurred during daylight hours on a two-lane, two-way, undivided, straight, uphill, residential roadway. Parking is allowed on the roadway on both sides. The roadway has a posted speed limit of 48 kmph (30 mph). There were no adverse atmospheric conditions at the time of the crash.
Vehicle one, a 1996 Chevrolet Cavalier, was traveling northbound on the roadway. Vehicle two, an unoccupied 1990 Honda Accord sedan, was legally parked against the east curb facing north. Vehicle one traveled to the right and the front right corner struck the back of vehicle two. Vehicle one rotated clockwise and rolled over to the left two quarter-turns before coming to rest facing east. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of vehicle one, a twenty-four-year-old female, was driving from the mall to her home, a route she traveled often. She was well rested and comfortable with her vehicle. She stated that she looked away from the road “for a couple of seconds.” She was focused on something in her vehicle; she stated she might have been adjusting the radio.
