The crash occurred on a two lane undivided roadway with a posted speed limit of 48 KPH (30 MPH) and a -2.2% grade. There was an intersecting driveway to the right with a -13.3% grade. The weather was clear, the roadway dry and it was daylight at the time of this early morning weekday crash.
Vehicle 1, a 1996 Chevrolet Blazer, was traveling north on the two lane undivided roadway approaching the driveway. It should be noted that there was a large sign, small hill and telephone pole to this driver's right, which may have obscured the approach of Vehicle 2. Vehicle 2, a 2000 Toyota Corolla, was traveling west on the driveway approaching the main roadway. Vehicle 2 entered the main roadway and was struck in the left side by the front of Vehicle 1. Vehicle 2 was rotated counterclockwise off the left side of the roadway, over a small curb, onto a private lawn and came to rest facing east. Vehicle 1 continued and came to rest in the southbound lane facing north. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The Chevrolet Blazer (Vehicle 1) was driven by a belted 50-year-old male who was transported, treated and released for a complaint of pain. Vehicle 1 was traveling north on the roadway and saw Vehicle 2 coming down the driveway off to the right. He put on his brakes and steered right but hit Vehicle 2 on the left side. Vehicle 1 was towed due to damage.
The Toyota Corolla (Vehicle 2) was driven by a belted 23-year-old female who was transported, treated and hospitalized for 1 day due to a head injury. The driver of the Corolla stated that she had "blacked out" and did not recall the accident or the events preceding the accident. Vehicle 2 was towed due to damage.
