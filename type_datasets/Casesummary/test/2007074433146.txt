This two-vehicle crash occurred in a four-legged, traffic signal-controlled intersection. Both roadways are level and divided by a curbed median. The westbound approach is level and consists of two through lanes, a left turn lane and a right turn lane. The southbound approach is level and consists of one through/right turn lane and a left turn lane. The speed limit for these roadways is 64 kmph (40 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1992 Honda Accord, was westbound in lane two. Vehicle two (V2), a 1996 Chrysler LHS, was stopped southbound in lane two. V2 entered the intersection turning left and the left side was struck by the front of V1. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, a thirty-three-year-old male, was on his way home from a restaurant/bar. He had had only four hours of sleep in the past twenty-four hours and stated that he felt "fatigued." He was healthy and familiar with his vehicle. This driver stated that he was upset, that he has employment problems and his stepfather is dying. He further stated that he did not see V2 prior to the crash. This driver's BAC was .440 according to the police report.
The driver of V2, a seventy-six-year-old male, was traveling from his home to a chiropractor appointment. He was healthy, well rested and familiar with his vehicle. He travels this route daily. This driver stated that he was watching a vehicle traveling northbound and saw V1 as a "flash of white" just before the crash but did not have time to attempt any avoidance maneuvers.
