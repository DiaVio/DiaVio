The crash occurred in the intersection of two roadways. One had two northbound lanes and two southbound lanes. This was a straight, level, bituminous roadway with a posted speed of 48 kmph (30 mph). The other road was one-way and had three eastbound lanes. This was a level, bituminous roadway that curved right with a radius of 161 meters and a superelevation of 2 percent. The posted speed was also 48 kmph (30 mph). The weather was clear and the roadways were dry.
V1, a 2005 GMC Sierra pick-up truck with one occupant, was traveling northbound in lane two. V2, a 1997 Chevrolet Venture minivan with three occupants, was traveling eastbound in lane one. The driver of V1 stated that she had never traveled this roadway before and was looking for a particular road to turn onto. She stated that she did not see the light turn red and did not see V2 until she was in the intersection. She attempted braking but could not avoid V2. V2 stated that she had a green light and she saw V1 right before she entered the intersection but did not think she would go through. V2 attempted to steer left and accelerate but could not avoid V1. The front of V1 contacted the right side of V2. V2 then spun clockwise and came to rest on the eastbound roadway facing northwest. V1 came to final rest facing northbound in its original lane of travel. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1 was a 46-year old female who reported no injuries and refused treatment. She stated that she had just come from work and was on her way to the hospital. She advised that she had been traveling between 50-64 kmph (31-40 mph) when she made the evasive action.
The driver of V2 was a 38-year old female who reported no injuries and refused treatment. She stated that she had just left the repair shop and was on her way to pick up her niece. She stated that she had been traveling between 50-64 kmph (31-40 mph) when she made the evasive action.
