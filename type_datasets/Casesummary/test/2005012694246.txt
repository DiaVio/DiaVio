This crash occurred at a two-way two-lane intersection. There was a flashing control signal (red to the north and south and yellow to the east and west). The roadways were both posted at a speed of 72 kmph (45mph). The north side of the intersection has less than a 2% negative slope and the westbound roadway had a positive grade of 2.5%. It was daylight and the roadway was wet and a light snow had fallen.
Vehicle 1 was a 1995 Ford Wind Star, which was traveling south decelerating to the stop. As the driver applied the brakes the wheels locked up on the slick roadway. Trying to avoid the crash, the driver steered right. This maneuver was not successful and Vehicle 1 was contacted in the left drivers side door by the right front of Vehicle 2. Vehicle 1 spun off in a clockwise rotation and came to final rest off road in the grass on the north side of the east, west roadway. Vehicle 2 came to final rest on the south shoulder of the east, west roadway. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
A 27-year-old female who drove Vehicle 1 was transported to a local trauma center with minor injuries. She stated that she was on her way to work one block away. She also stated the road seemed clear but must have had black ice. Her vehicle was towed due to damage.
Vehicle 2 was a 2004 Trail Blazer and was driven by a 30-year old female who was not injured, but her vehicle was towed due to damage. She stated that she was on her way home from taking her child to school when the crash had occurred. She also stated that she tried to stop when she saw the other vehicle, could not stop, and contacted the left side of Vehicle 1.
