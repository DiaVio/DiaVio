A two-vehicle intersection collision occurred a few hours before dark during evening hours, on a Wednesday. The collision occurred in a residential intersection controlled by control signals, with pedestrian signals. The east/west roadway is a two-way, three-lane roadway that has a slight uphill grade approaching the intersection from the east. It is straight with bituminous paving. The posted speed limit for both roadways is 48 kmph (30 mph). The north/south roadway is a two-way, three-lane, undivided roadway. The roadway is level, and is straight with bituminous paving. Westerly sun glare was present at the time.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1997 Toyota Camry 4 door with one occupant, was traveling west in lane one approaching the collision intersection. Vehicle two (V2), a 2005 Jeep Wrangler with one occupant, was stopped facing southbound in lane two (left turn lane) waiting for oncoming traffic to clear to make a left turn. The left rear of V2 was impacted by the front of V1. V2 rotated counterclockwise and came to rest facing northwest in the southeast corner of the intersection. V1 came to rest forward of the impact point. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. The driver of V2 was transported due to injuries. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1 was a 21 year-old male who is reportedly in good health with no hearing restrictions, his vision restrictions are corrected with glasses, which he was wearing at the time of collision, he was on his way from his friends house to pick up his friend from work and was listening to the radio. He was well rested, and claims that the sun-glare was in his eyes and played a major factor in the cause of this crash. He had looked at the light and saw it was green, and that was when he made the decision to pass through the intersection. It was difficult to see the light due to the sun glare. He wears corrective lenses but was not wearing sunglasses at the time of the crash. He drives the route weekly and is familiar with the vehicle.
The driver of V2 was a 37 year-old female who is reportedly in good health with no hearing or sight restrictions. The only medication she takes is Zyrtec for allergies. She was headed to pick her son up from soccer, and she was not in a hurry. She rarely drives this section of roadway and is familiar with the vehicle. She was well rested, and does not work outside the home, there were no other notable factors for this driver. The driver had been stopped over 10 seconds, waiting for oncoming traffic to clear so she could complete a left turn.
