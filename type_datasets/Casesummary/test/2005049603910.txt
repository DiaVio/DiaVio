A crash happened mid-afternoon. Involving one passenger car,The location is a three lane divided (no positive barrier, six lanes overall) downhill roadway. The roadway was curved to the right. The weather was clear but the road was iced. The posted speed limit 56 kmph (35 mph).The entire study area experienced a hard freeze the previous night. The roadways started to thaw around noon that day. This downhill roadway was running from west to east. There were tall pine trees located south of the south curb of the roadway. These pine trees covered the roadway with shadow at this time of the day. The ice on the roadway did not melt at this part.
V 1 a 1993 Subaru Legacy driven by a 30 year-old male with three passengers was traveling east in the first lane of the roadway. The vehicle hit a patch of ice on the bottom of the hill. It caused the vehicle to depart the road to its right. Its right side impacted a tree located south of the roadway. The vehicle rotated clockwise before it came to rest between the tree and a utility pole located east of the tree. V 1 was towed from the scene, the three passengers were transported to a local medical facility. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.
