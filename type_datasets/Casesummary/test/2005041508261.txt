The crash occurred at the intersection of two roadways. The east/west street and the north/south street were two-way, two-lane, undivided roadways. The north/south street leading into the intersection was controlled by a stop sign. These were straight, level, bituminous roads. Conditions were dark with streetlights on. The roadway was dry and skies were clear. Traffic flow was normal.
V1, a 2002 Ford Crown Victoria Interceptor with two occupants, was traveling westbound in lane one. V2, a 2001 BMW 330i with two occupants, was traveling northbound in lane one. Both vehicles were approaching the same intersection. The driver of V1 was driving slowly, circulating the area looking for a culprit. The driver saw the suspect riding a bike. As the suspect began to flee from the police car, the driver speed up, knocking the driver off of the bicycle, and coming to a halt. The suspect then fled on foot, in the direction of the intersection. At this point, V1 accelerated toward the intersection in pursuit of the suspect. As the two vehicles entered the intersection, the front left corner of V1 made contact with the front right corner of V2. This impact was immediately followed by a sideslap impact of the left side of V1 and the right side of V2. V1 continued to travel northwest, making contact with a tree. V1 came to final rest facing west, leaning against the tree at a forty-five degree angle. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 48 kmph for V1 and 48 kmph for V2.
