This single-vehicle crash occurred while approaching a four-leg suburban intersection. The west side of the intersection is undivided and has one lane in each direction. There is a warning sign on the right side of the road for eastbound traffic stating a railroad crossing is ahead. The roadway is bituminous, straight and level with a posted speed limit of 56 kmph (35 mph). This crash occurred during late evening hours in darkness with light traffic flow. The eastbound approach to the intersection is unlit until you arrive at the intersection, where there is one overhead globe light over the southeast corner of the intersection.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1998 Pontiac Sunfire convertible with one occupant, was eastbound in lane one traveling straight. There was a large tent on the northwest corner of the intersection. A non-contact vehicle was southbound and entered the intersection from a stop sign. The driver if the non-contact vehicle stopped in the middle of the intersection, after seeing V1 approaching the intersection. The driver of V1 steered to the right and applied the brakes, and avoided impact with the non-contact vehicle. V1 traveled in a southeast direction and ran off the east side of the roadway south of the intersection. V1 traveled 7.2 meters (23.6 feet) and impacted a railroad track with its front frame and undercarriage, which deployed the airbags. V1 traveled over the railroad track and began to rotate clockwise, traveling southwest. V1 impacted a railroad sign with its right side. V1 came to rest on its wheels facing southwest at the sign. V1 was towed from the scene with moderate right side damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 72 kmph for V1.
The driver of V1, a twenty-four year-old female, had just left work and was on her way to a bar to socialize. She was well rested and is in good health. She takes vitamins on a regular basis. She drives this roadway twice a week and is very familiar with the vehicle. She also stated she just bought this vehicle a month ago because she was involved in another crash just after moving here.
