This three-vehicle rear end type crash occurred on a four lane divided asphalt road. This road runs north to south with a Jersey barrier separating the lanes. This crash occurred during early evening hours with no street lighting and no adverse weather conditions. This roadway has a posted speed limit of 89 kmph (55 mph) with warning signs for curves and grades and speed reduction to 64 kmph (40 mph). Just prior to this crash a single vehicle towing a trailer lost control and rolled the trailer in the northbound travel lanes and was blocking the #1 northbound lane. Lane 2 was open for traffic to go around the rolled trailer and a person was directing traffic slowing traffic around this crash area.
Vehicle one (V1) was a 2001 Chevrolet K-2500 pickup, driven by a nineteen year old female with two passengers. V1 was northbound when it changed lanes from the #1 to the #2 lane due to traffic stopping in the #1 lane. As V1 approached this crash area it impacted the rear of vehicle two (V2) that was slowing for the previous crash that had occurred. V1 impacted the rear plane of V2 and came to rest in the northbound lane heading in a northerly direction. A female driver and two male occupants were not injured. V1 was towed from the scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is Unknown for V1 and Unknown for V2.
Vehicle two (V2) was a 2001 Oldsmobile Alero sedan, driven by a thirty four year old male. V2 was northbound in the #2 lane and had slowed for a previous crash when it was impacted on the rear plane by V1. V2 was pushed forward and into the rear of a small cargo trailer, being pulled by vehicle three (V3). V2 came to rest in the northbound #2 lane heading in a northeasterly direction. A male driver was injured and air transported to a trauma center. There were no other occupants in this vehicle. V2 was disabled and towed
Vehicle three (V3), a 2007 Chevrolet four door extended cab pickup pulling an 2.4 meter long (8 foot) cargo trailer with an ATV on board. V3 was driven by a fifty six year old female with five passengers and was stopped for a previous crash as it was impacted on the rear of the towed trailer by V2. This impact damaged the trailer's coupler and both units were towed from the scene because the trailer could not be separated from the the pickup. A female driver and five occupants were treated on scene with only occupant #2 being transported to a local hospital. V3 was not disabled and was driven from the tow yard the next day, after the trailer was separated from V3..
