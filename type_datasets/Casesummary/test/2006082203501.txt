This two-vehicle crash occurred on a controlled-access, jersey barrier-divided, straight roadway. There are four through lanes in the northbound direction with an uphill grade in the area of the crash. The speed limit on this roadway is 97 kmph (60 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1997 Acura Integra, was northbound in lane three, reportedly at a high rate of speed. Vehicle two (V2), a 1990 Chevrolet K2500 pickup, was northbound in lane four. V1 began to rotate counterclockwise and traveled into lane four. The front of V1 then struck the median jersey barrier. V1 continued rotating, continuing northbound and the right back corner struck the barrier. V1 continued rotating and the back was struck by the front of V2. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, a twenty-eight-year-old female, was not interviewed; no further information is available for this driver.
The driver of V2, a twenty-nine-year-old male, was traveling from a job site back to his workplace. He was healthy, well rested and familiar with his vehicle. He travels this route monthly. This driver stated that he braked after V1 crossed in front of him, but skidded and was not able to stop before his vehicle struck V1.
