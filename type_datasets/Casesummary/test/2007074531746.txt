This two-vehicle crash occurred in a four-legged, traffic signal-controlled intersection. The north/south roadway is undivided, straight and consists of one lane in each direction with left turn lanes at the intersection. The southbound approach has an eight percent uphill grade and the northbound approach has a five percent uphill grade. The speed limit for this roadway is 72 kmph (45 mph). It was dusk at the time of this crash with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2005 Dodge Caravan, was stopped southbound in lane two, waiting to turn left. Vehicle two (V2), a 2006 Hyundai Sonata, was northbound in lane one. V1 entered the intersection, turning left. The front of V1 struck the left side of V2. The vehicles rotated and the left side of V2 sideswiped the front of V1. V2 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, a fifty-year-old female, was on the way home from a store with her fifty-three-year-old husband. This driver was well rested and comfortable with her vehicle and passenger. She travels this route daily. This driver was talking to her passenger and looking left when she entered the intersection. She stated that she saw V2 but "it was quite a ways back so I though I had time to make my turn." This driver has various medical conditions and takes the following medications: saline nasal spray, estrogen, aspirin, vitamins, Cipro, Furosemide, Celexa, Docusate, Flunisolide, Gemfibrozil, Synthroid, Prilosec, Zocor, Ambien, Alprazolam, Plavix, Zetia, Isosorbide Mononitrate, Metoprolol, Tartrate and Potassium Chloride.
The driver of V2, a forty-year-old male, was traveling from his home to a store. He was well rested and familiar with his vehicle. He travels this route daily. He takes high blood pressure medication as well as Cipro and Atenolol. This driver stated that he saw V1 but thought that they would wait for him to clear the intersection. When he realized that V1 was turning, he applied the brakes and steered right in an attempt to avoid the crash.
