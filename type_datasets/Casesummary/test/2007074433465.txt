This two-vehicle crash occurred in a four-legged, traffic signal-controlled intersection. The north/south trafficway is straight, level and divided by a curbed median. The approach roadways consist of two through lanes and a left turn lane. The speed limit for this roadway is 64 kmph (40 mph). It was dark with streetlights on at the time of the crash with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1998 Chrysler Concorde, was northbound in lane three. Vehicle two (V2), a 1998 Dodge Intrepid, was southbound in lane two. V1 turned left in the intersection and the front struck the front of V2. The vehicles rotated and the right side of V1 struck the left side of V2. V1 traveled to the southwest corner and the front struck the base of a utility pole. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, a twenty-three-year-old female, was traveling from visiting a relative at the hospital to her son's apartment. She was well rested and familiar with her vehicle. She rarely travels this route. She had five months of driving experience at the time of the crash. This driver had taken Thera-Flu within the past twenty-four hours. She stated that she was following her husband who had turned at the light. This driver further stated that she saw V2 but "thought the other car was farther away. "
The driver of V2, a seventeen-year-old female, was on her way from a wedding at a church to a friend's house. She was well rested, healthy and familiar with her vehicle. She rarely travels this route. She had one year of driving experience at the time of the crash. This driver was on her cell phone talking to her mother at the time of the crash. She stated that she saw V1 just as it turned in front of her and she steered to the right and applied the brakes (skidding longitudinally) in an attempt to avoid the crash.
