This two-vehicle crash occurred in a three-legged intersection. The north/south roadway is undivided, level and straight and consists of two lanes in each direction. The east/west roadway is on the west side of the north/south roadway. This roadway is undivided, level and straight with one lane in each direction and a left turn lane. This roadway is controlled by a stop sign at the intersection. The speed limit for these roadways is 72 kmph (45 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1993 Nissan Sentra, was stopped eastbound in lane two. Vehicle two (V2), a 1973 Ford F100 pickup pulling a utility trailer, was southbound in lane one. V1 entered the intersection, turning left. The front of V2 struck the left side of V1. V2 rotated counterclockwise and the trailer jackknifed. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 5 kmph for V1 and 5 kmph for V2.
The driver of V1, a twenty-seven-year-old female, was not interviewed. No further information is available for this driver.
The driver of V2, a fifty-seven-year-old male, had just left a construction supply house and was on his way to another city. He was healthy, well rested and familiar with his vehicle and trailer. This driver stated that he saw V1 stopped at the intersection and applied the brakes (skidding counterclockwise) when he realized that it was entering the intersection.
