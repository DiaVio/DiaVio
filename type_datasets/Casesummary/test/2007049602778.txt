This two-vehicle crash occurred on a weekend during morning daylight hours at an intersection without traffic signals. The speed limit for the roadway is 56 kmph (35 mph). The road is a 4-lane divided (no positive barrier) asphalt roadway for both vehicles. Prior to the intersection for southbound traffic, the road has a left curve with a curvature of 375 meters (1231 feet). The road surface was dry and there were no detrimental atmospheric conditions at the time of the crash.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2005 Chevrolet Avalanche with two occupants was traveling north in lane four of the northbound lanes attempting a left turn at the intersection. Vehicle two (V2), a 2004 Chrysler Sebring with two occupants, was traveling south in the first lane, attempting to pass through the intersection. V1 let three cars pass before attempting to turn. At the intersection, the front of V2 impacted with the right side of V1. V2 rotated clockwise and came to rest within the intersection, facing southeast. V1 continued to move through the intersection and the front struck a stop sign located in the southeast corner of the intersection. V1 came to rest at the stop sign. Both vehicles were towed from the scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
A 30-year old female drove V1. She and the right front occupant were heading from a nearby fast food restaurant to the residence of occupant two. She had looked to the north and did see V2 approaching the intersection. Her occupant was interviewed at the scene. He stated the driver misjudged V2's speed and the gap in the traffic. The driver is in good health but receives medical treatment for an emotional anxiety condition. Her passenger used this term. He stated it was not affecting her driving. According to medical records, medication includes Norco, Ativan, Clonazepam and Trazodone.
A 52-year old female drove V2. The researcher was not able to obtain an interview from her. According to medical records, medication includes Atenolol and Maxzide. Medical history includes hypertension and cancer.
