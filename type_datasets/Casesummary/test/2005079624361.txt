A two vehicle collision occurred at 1810 hours on a Wednesday. The collision occurred at a four-leg intersection comprised by an east/west roadway and a north/south roadway. The east/west roadway is undivided, dry, level, asphalt and consists of five lanes including a left turn lane. The north/south roadway is undivided, dry, level, asphalt and consists of two lanes on the north side and three lanes on the south side. The south side is an entrance to a shopping mall. The east/west roadway is designated 35 mph (56 kmph). The intersection is controlled by a tri-light signal that was working properly at the time of the collision. There are no protective arrows for traffic turning left at the intersection.
Vehicle one (V1) is a 1993 Pontiac Grand Am 4-door sedan traveling east in the number one lane. Vehicle two (V2) is a 1993 Toyota Camry 4-door station wagon traveling west in the left turn lane. V2 was stopped for 5-10 seconds prior to proceeding to make the intended left turn. The traffic signal turned amber, and then red for east/west bound traffic when V2 initiated the left turn. As V2 made its left turn its right plane was impacted by the front of V1. V1 came to rest at impact in the intersection. V2 came to rest facing southwest near the southwest corner in the intersection. Both vehicles were towed from the scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
