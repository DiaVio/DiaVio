This crash occurred on a two lane undivided roadway with a curve to the left. The curve had a radius of 322 meters and a super elevation of 6%. The weather was clear and the roadway was dry. This crash occurred on a weekend night.
Vehicle #1, a 1996 Chevrolet Blazer, was traveling east on this roadway, negotiating the curve when it went off the roadway to the right, went up on and over the guardrail, impacted with ground with its front then continued on to impact with several trees. The vehicle then rolled over 2 one-quarter turns onto its roof, slid down the embankment and impacted with 2 more trees with its front. The driver of this vehicle left the scene and walked home. She returned to the scene the following morning and was observed by police taking items from the vehicle. The police were concerned with her condition and called EMS. The police encouraged the driver to go to the hospital in the ambulance, however, no records of arrival in the hospital could be found.
