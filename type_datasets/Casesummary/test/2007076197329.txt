This single-vehicle crash occurred in a rural area on a 2-lane one-way asphalt roadway that runs south. The lanes curve right and are downhill. The posted speed limit is 89 kmph (55 mph) with an advisory speed of 72 kmph (45 mph) for the curve. Rumble strips are present on both sides of the roadway as well as across both lanes prior to the curve to warn of the curve and the downhill elevation. This crash occurred mid-afternoon during daylight hours. There were no adverse weather or road conditions present.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2007 Toyota Camry 4-door sedan, was southbound in the lane two negotiating the right curve when it departed the left road edge and contacted the guardrail with its rear left side plane. V1 rotated clockwise as it crossed both lanes to the right and impacted the dirt and rock embankment with its front-end plane. V1 rotated clock-wise and came to rest on the shoulder facing north. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Unknown for V1.
The driver, a 54-year-old male, was arrested for extreme DUI and treated at the hospital. No interview was obtained with this driver. A witness stated the driver passed him on the left traveling at 112 kmph (70 mph) as they were negotiating the right curve. He stated V1 contacted the guardrail with its left side then crossed the lanes and impacted the embankment. According to the medical records the driver stated that he had drank approximately a fifth of vodka recently. The medical records indicate the driver had a BAC test result for alcohol of 0.264.
