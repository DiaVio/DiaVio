This single vehicle crash occurred on an uphill, two-lane, bituminous roadway that curves moderately to the left and has two-way traffic. The posted speed limit for this roadway was 72 kmph (45 mph). The road was dry and there were no adverse atmospheric conditions present at the time of the crash.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1973 Chevrolet C10 Deluxe pickup truck was traveling eastbound in lane one at 97 kmph (60 mph). V1 departed the right side of the road and began to roll over. V1 rolled upon is longitudinal axis towards its right 4-quarter turns and the right side of V1 struck a pole (>10cm in diameter). Post impact with the pole, V1 rotated counterclockwise and came to rest on its wheels. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 97 kmph for V1.
The driver of V1, a twenty-five year old male stated that he was running late for work at the time of the crash. He was also using a telephone/two-way radio prior to the crash. The nature of the conversation was an argument. He had to turn around and go back home to deliver something to his wife. This was making him angry and increasingly late for work. At the time of the crash, this driver was on the phone. This driver drives this vehicle daily and over this road on a daily basis.
