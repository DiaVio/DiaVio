This two-vehicle crash occurred at approximately one hour before noon on a weekend. The location was a five-lane, two-way, undivided arterial city street with multiple business and apartments in the area. The roadway was level bituminous with curbs and sidewalks on both sides. The weather was clear and dry and it was daylight. The posted speed limit for both vehicles was 56 kmph, or 35 mph.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1986 Pontiac Sunbird 4 door sedan, was traveling westbound in the outside curb lane. V1 slowed for traffic ahead, which was stopping at the intersection. Vehicle two (V2), an unknown year/make/model hit & run pickup truck traveling eastbound, suddenly turned left in front of V1 to enter an apartment building driveway. The driver of V1 was unable to stop and impacted with the right side of V2. V1 stopped near impact and V2 spun counter-clockwise and struck a phone box located off the roadway just west of the driveway. V2 then drove away westbound, and, V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
