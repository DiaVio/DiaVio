The crash occurred on a level two lane undivided roadway with a posted speed limit of 64kmph (40 mph). The bituminous roadway featured a very slight curve to the left with a radius of curvature of 11,250 meters and 0% superelevation. The weather was clear, the roadway dry and it was daylight at the time of this early morning weekend crash.
V1, a 2001 Ford Focus, was traveling west on the roadway negotiating the curve to the left. V1 crossed into the eastbound lane, corrected to the right across the westbound lane, began to rotate clockwise and off the north shoulder of the roadway. V1 continued off the roadway, down a small embankment and struck a tree stump with its front. V1 began to rollover to the left as it became airborne. V1 continued to roll over three quarter turns and came to rest on its right side facing north. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Unknown for V1.
V1 was driven by an unbelted 21 year-old female who was transported, treated and released at a local hospital. The driver refused an interview. Statements made by the driver to police indicated that she was a waitress and had worked until midnight. She had gone home, changed clothes and gone out with friends to a local bar. She stated she had one drink and a few beers before she stopped drinking at 3 a.m. She then helped close and clean up the bar. She was on her way home at the time of the crash and stated she must have dozed off. She was found walking on the roadway at the time of the officer's arrival.
