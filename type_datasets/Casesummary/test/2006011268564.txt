The crash occurred on a fully controlled limited access highway with two lanes in each direction divided by a grassy median with a metal guardrail. The straight, level bituminous road has a posted speed limit of 113kmh (70 mph). Conditions at the time of the weekday afternoon crash were daylight, clear and dry, and traffic was moving at the posted limit.
V1, a 2002 Ford Windstar minivan driven by a 37 year-old male on his way home from work, was northbound in lane one traveling 105-113 kmh (65-70 mph). The driver stated that he felt someone 'clip' his vehicle and he did not know what happened. A witness behind v2 stated that v1 suddenly left lane one on the left side. The left side of v1 struck the right side of v2 in lane two. The driver of v1 never saw v2 and took no avoidance actions. The driver of v1 steered back right and braked and came to rest on the right shoulder facing northeast. V1 was towed from the scene due to damage. The fully restrained driver did not seek medical treatment. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
V2, a 1996 Ford Windstar minivan driven by a 38 year-old male was northbound in lane two on his way from home to work. The driver of v2 stated that he was traveling 105-113 kmh (65-70 mph) next to and a little behind v1. He stated that v1 slowed a bit and suddenly came into his lane and the left side of v1 struck the right side of v2 in lane two. The driver of v2 braked and steered left. V2 left the roadway on the left, crossed the paved (with a rumble strip) shoulder and entered the grassy median. The left front and side of v2 contacted the metal guardrail, bounced off and came to rest in the grass facing north. V2 was towed from the scene due to damage. The fully restrained driver refused medical treatment.
