This crash occurred in the late evening of a weekday in the westbound lanes of a fully controlled urban highway. The east/west highway consisted of two lanes in each direction divided by a concrete barrier wall. The concrete highway was dry and level with a sharp curve to left and a posted speed limit of 80kmph (50 mph). The traffic flow was normal for this time of night and there were no adverse atmospheric conditions.
V1, a 1999 Chrysler Town & Country, was westbound in lane one negotiating the left curve. A non-contact vehicle traveling alongside V1 began drifting in lane one. V1 accelerated and lost control, crossing over into lane two and striking the concrete barrier wall with its left front corner. V1 then crossed back over the roadway and struck the cement barrier wall located just beyond the shoulder on the right side of the roadway. V1 came to final rest facing westward with its right side the barrier wall. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is No PAR obtained (created) for V1.
The driver of vehicle one, a 48-year old male, said he was traveling westbound in lane one negotiating a leftward curve when a vehicle attempted to cross in front of his vehicle into his lane, he realized what the vehicle was attempting and he panicked and steered left. He lost control of his vehicle and contacted the cement barrier all located on the left side of the roadway. He steered right and contacted the guardrail on the right side beyond the shoulder area. After the vehicle made contact with the right side guardrail it traveled against it for several feet blowing out the right front tire where it came to final rest against the right guardrail. The driver said he was traveling approximately 105kmph (65 mph). The posted speed limit for the curved section of the highway was 80kmph (50 mph). When asked if he new the posted speed limit, the driver said, he thought it was 65 mph. Neither he nor any of his passengers were injured.
