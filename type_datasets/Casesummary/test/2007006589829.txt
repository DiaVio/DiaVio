This three-vehicle crash occurred in the middle of the block several hundred feet beyond the intersection in the westbound lane of travel. There were no dividing lines separating the two-way roadway. The roadway consisted of two lanes: one westbound and one eastbound non-physically divided. Curbside parking was permitted on both sides of the east/westbound roadway. There were no signalized traffic control devices at this middle of the block location. There were two sets of metal trolley tracts planted in cement in the center of the road. The road surfaces were bituminous dry and straight and there were no adverse weather conditions. The posted speed limit was 56 kmph (35 mph). There was a school speed limit sign for 24 kmph (15 mph). The crash occurred on a weekday during the early afternoon daylight hours. The roadway was free of any defects and the traffic flow was normal for this roadway.
Vehicle one, 1999 Dodge Ram 1500 series Large Truck, was traveling westbound when it veered right and contacted with its front the rear of vehicle two, a 2002 Lexus RX300, legally parked against the north-side curb edge. Vehicle two, after the initial impact with vehicle one, rotated clockwise while contacting with its left side the rear of vehicle three, a 1998 Ford Aerostar Van, parked legally in front of vehicle two. Vehicle two, came to final rest facing northward with its front tires straddling the north-side curb. Vehicle three was pushed several feet forward rotating slightly counter clockwise. Vehicles one and two damages were disabling and were later towed from the scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is Unknown for V1 and Unknown for V2.
The driver of vehicle one, a 45-year old male, fled from the scene and was not present at the time of arrival. Several attempts were made to contact the driver, however, to no avail.
Vehicle two was legally parked and the driver was not present at the scene. The police PAR list the driver/owner as being a 43-year old male.
Vehicle three was also legally parked and the driver was not present at the scene.
