This two-vehicle crash occurred on an undivided, four-lane, straight, level, wet roadway. The speed limit for this roadway is 64 kmph (40 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2000 Mitsubishi Eclipse, was eastbound in lane two. Vehicle two (V2), a 1995 Chevrolet Monte Carlo, was in front of V1. V2 stopped to make a left turn into a driveway on the north side of the roadway. The driver of V1 noticed that V2 stopped and applied the brakes. V1 slid on the wet road and the front struck the back of V2. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 48 kmph for V1 and 48 kmph for V2.
The driver of V1, an eighteen-year-old female, was traveling from a restaurant to her home. She was healthy, well rested and familiar with her vehicle. She drives this route daily. This driver stated that she was aware that her vehicle did not stop well on wet roads.
The driver of V2, a twenty-six-year-old male, was traveling with a female passenger, from a friend's house to his home. He was well rested and healthy at the time of the crash. This was the first time that this driver drove this vehicle, but he felt comfortable with it. He drives this route weekly.
