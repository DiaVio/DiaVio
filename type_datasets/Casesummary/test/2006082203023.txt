A two vehicle daytime T type collision occurred during a weekday morning at the intersection of an urban arterial road and a two-way neighborhood street. The intersection has stop signs controlling the east-west neighborhood traffic and both roads are straight, level and paved with bituminous asphalt. The speed limit for both roads is 48 kmph (30 mph). At the time of the crash the sky was cloudy but the road was dry.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2001 Volvo S60 sedan with only a driver, was heading west into the intersection. Vehicle two (V2), a 1998 Honda Passport SUV with a driver and toddler in a child seat, was headed south on the arterial road. The front of V2 struck the right side of V1 at the rear passenger door and V1 spun clockwise out of the intersection. V1 then rolled backwards into the south curb on the west side of the intersection. V2 came to rest at the edge of the arterial road in the intersection, still facing south. V2 was driven to a nearby residence to be towed later. V1 was parked on the south curb to be towed later. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
