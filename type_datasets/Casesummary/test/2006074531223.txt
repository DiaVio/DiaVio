This two-vehicle crash occurred in a four-legged, traffic signal-controlled intersection. The east/west roadway is divided by a curbed median. This roadway curves to the right in the westbound direction. The eastbound and westbound approach lanes consist of three through lanes and a left turn lane. The speed limit on this roadway is 56 kmph (35 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1994 Lincoln Town Car, was westbound in lane two. Vehicle two (V2), a 2004 Saturn Vue, was eastbound in lane four, intending to turn left at the intersection. As V2 turned left, the front struck the front of V1. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, a fifty-three-year-old male, along with his forty-nine-year-old sister, was driving from his mother's home to his sister's home. He was well rested at the time of the crash. This driver stated that he takes the medication Mavik, which is prescribed for high blood pressure. He also stated that he was comfortable with this vehicle even though he had just purchased it earlier in the day. This driver travels this route monthly.
The driver of V2, a twenty-eight-year-old female, along with her twelve-month-old son, was driving from their home to drop her son off at her parent's home on the way to the driver's workplace. She was healthy, well rested and comfortable with her vehicle and passenger. She drives this route daily. This driver stated that a large truck in westbound lane three was in her line of sight and she did not see V1 prior to the crash.
