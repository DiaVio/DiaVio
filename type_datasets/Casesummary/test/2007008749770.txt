This crash occurred in the westbound lane of a two-lane undivided roadway. Traveling westbound the roadway had a sharp right curve, had a downhill grade of 14 percent, was bituminous, and had a posted speed limit of 40 kmph (25 mph). There were chevrons on the roadside to warn of the sharp curve. In the eastbound direction, there was an uphill grade of 10 percent. An advisory speed limit of 24 kmph (15 mph) was posted on the curve. The curve had a radius of curvature of 47 meters and a superelevation of 6 percent. The curve met the AASHTO standard for an advisory speed of 15 mph. It was morning on a weekday and conditions were clear.
V1 was a 1996 Honda Accord 4-door sedan driven by a 41 year-old female. V2 was a 2007 Honda Accord 4-door sedan driven by a 36 year-old female. V1 was traveling westbound and beginning to negotiate a sharp right curve when the vehicle lost traction on black ice. The vehicle skidded into the oncoming lane as it traveled downhill and around the curve. V1 then struck V2 in the front, which was traveling eastbound, as it was just about to enter the curve. Police Reported Travel Speed is 0 kmph for V1 and 0 kmph for V2.
