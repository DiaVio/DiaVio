V1, a 2006 Honda Civic 4dr, was northbound in lane 2 of 2 northbound lanes of a straight, level, bituminous, interstate roadway. A non-contact vehicle was northbound in lane 1 of the same roadway just ahead of v1. At the time of the crash it was daylight and the roadway was dry. The non-contact vehicle started to change lanes to the left into lane 2. The driver of v1 took evasive action by steering left, ran onto the inside shoulder, steered right and then v1 slid into the grassy median, left side leading. V1 then rolled 2/4 turns to it's left and came to rest on its top facing north. Both occupants were transported and the vehicle was towed due to damage.
A complete driver interview and vehicle inspection was obtained for v1. The driver was properly licensed for her vehicle and familiar with the roadway. The vehicle was new and in good mechanical condition. The Pre-event movement of v1 was going straight, the Critical pre-crash event was this vehicle traveling off the edge of the road on the left side and the Critical reason for the critical pre-crash event was incorrect evasive action. Factors that may have contributed to the cause of the crash were the driver's conversing with a passenger, a non-contact vehicle entering her lane (exterior factor) and choosing an inappropriate evasive action. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.
The driver was not cited.
