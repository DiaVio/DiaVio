This two-vehicle crash occurred on a jersey barrier-divided trafficway. The westbound lanes are level, curve to the left and consist of four through lanes. The speed limit for this trafficway is 97 kmph (60 mph). It was dark in the late afternoon with street lights on at the time of the crash with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2003 Mitsubishi Galant, was westbound in lane one. Vehicle two (V2), a 2006 Ford F150 pickup, was westbound in lane two. V1 traveled off the right side of the roadway. The driver of V1 abruptly steered left and V1 returned to the roadway and into lane two. The front of V2 struck the left side of V1. V1 rotated and the left side struck the left side of V2. V1 continued to the southwest and the back struck the median jersey barrier. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, a fifty-nine-year-old male, was on his way home from a store. He was well rested, in good health and familiar with his vehicle. He travels this route daily. He has diabetes and takes the medications Lantus, Lasix, Potassium Chloride, Zoloft and Metformin. This driver stated that he did not know why he traveled off the right side of the road.
The driver of V2, a thirty-four-year-old male, was on his way home from work. He was healthy, well rested and familiar with his vehicle. He travels this route daily. This driver stated that he applied the brakes when he saw V1 entering the roadway but could not steer to the left or right due to vehicles in those lanes.
