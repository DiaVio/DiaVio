This two vehicle nighttime collision occurred shortly after the evening rush hour. The collision occurred on a four lane, two-way street near a complex intersection. The intersection is where the arterial roadway curves to the east, intersecting an east west street and a north south street. The collision occurred on the arterial road where northbound traffic can make an uncontrolled left turn west. The southbound traffic has a curve to the left. There is no measurable super elevation (bank) for the southbound traffic. All roads are paved with asphalt, and the streets were dry and well lit at the time of the crash. The speed limit for arterial streets is 48 kmph (30 mph).
Vehicle one (V1), a 2004 Nissan Quest minivan, driven by a 56 year-old female with one passenger was headed south in lane two was negotiating the curve. Vehicle two (V2), a 1993 Chrysler New Yorker sedan, driven by a 36 year-old male with one passenger was headed north in lane two. V2 began to turn left onto the westbound street where there is no light or stop sign, and stopped after encroaching in the southbound lanes. V1 did not stop and the front of V1 struck the front of V2. V2 was pushed back approximately one car length, Both vehicles were towed due to disabling damage and the driver and passenger in V1 were transported to the hospital for whiplash injuries. The driver and passenger of V2 had no injuries. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
