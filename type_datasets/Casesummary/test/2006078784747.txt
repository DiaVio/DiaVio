This single vehicle roll-over crash occurred during morning daylight hours on a four-lane, divided two-way interstate with two travel,lanes in each direction and a depressed median. There are two westbound and two eastbound travel lanes. The roadway surface was bituminous, level, and dry with a slight curve to the left. The posted speed limit 120 kmph (75 mph). There were no adverse weather conditions present.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2001 Infinity QX4 SUV driven by a 29 year-old female with four passengers, was traveling westbound in the number one lane with the cruise control set for 120 kmph (75 mph) when a bird flew into her path. She swerved to avoid hitting the bird by steering right and departed the roadway to the right shoulder. The driver then counter steered to the left, re-entered the roadway and began rotating counterclockwise causing V1 to roll over on the roadway. V1 continued to travel across the left lane line and depart the left side of the roadway going off into the median to final rest. V1 was towed from the scene and one passenger was transported to a local medical facility. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.
The driver only had four hours of sleep in the 24 hours preceding the crash, so fatigue was a contributing factor for her. She and her friends were on spring break traveling from her home to an unspecified destination. They had driver 258 kilometers (160 miles) when the crash occurred. Alcohol tests after the crash were negative for this driver; she was in good health & takes no medications.
