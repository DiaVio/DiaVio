This two-vehicle crash occurred in a four-way, traffic signal-controlled intersection. On the west side of the intersection, the roadway is divided by a curbed median and the eastbound traffic lanes consist of a left turn lane and three through lanes. On the south side of the intersection, the roadway is undivided with two lanes and the northbound lane is used for left and right turning as well as through traffic. The roadway is straight in the eastbound direction and curving left in the northbound approach to the intersection. The intersection is level and bituminous with a posted speed limit of 72 kmph (45 mph). The crash occurred during daylight hours on a weekend shortly after noon.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1995 Buick Century with only the driver present, was eastbound in lane one, approaching the intersection. Vehicle two (V2), a 2004 Jeep Liberty with only the driver present was stopped northbound in lane one, in preparation to turn left. V1 entered the intersection as V2 was turning left. The front of V1 contacted the left side of V2. V1 rotated counterclockwise and continued on until coming to rest on all four tires facing northeast against the curbed median on the east side of the intersection. After impact, V2 rotated clockwise and came to rest facing northeast on all four tires in the first lane of eastbound traffic. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 56 kmph for V1 and 56 kmph for V2.
The driver of V1 was an eighty-seven-year-old female. Little is known about this driver due the researcher being unable to contact her for an interview. She had been transported from the scene for medical treatment. According to the investigating officer, the driver of V1 failed to observe and disregarded the red light. Medical records indicate that the driver was retired and has a valid drivers license with restrictions for vision. The driver takes an unspecified cholesterol medication in addition to a hypertensive medication and vitamins. Alcohol consumption was not a factor for this driver.
The driver of V2, a forty-four-year-old female, had been shopping and was on her way to pick up her daughter. She was rested, drives this route weekly and is comfortable with this vehicle. She stated being in good health and taking no medications. She stated that prior to pulling out into the intersection she looked but did not see anyone coming. She observed V1's presence just prior to impact.
