This two vehicle daytime collision occurred during morning daylight hours. The collision occurred heading westbound on a two lane undivided roadway. The pre-crash roadway for all vehicles is straight and level, dry concrete paving. The roadway has a posted speed limit of 56 kmph (35 mph).
Vehicle one (V1), a 2001 Kia Rio sedan driven by a 21 year-old male, was traveling westbound on the roadway in lane 1. Vehicle two (V2), a 1999 Volvo 940 station wagon driven by a 42 year-old male, was also traveling westbound in lane 1. V2 braked for a cat that was crossing the roadway. V1 did not stop in time and the front of V1 impacted the rear of V2, Neither driver was transported. V2 was driven from scene and V1 arranged for a private tow. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1 stated he was tired and should have not been driving. But due to a friend needing a ride on short notice he took her to the airport. On his way back home and he was following V2 but didn't remember doing that or why the vehicle slowed down, he remembered seeing him at the last second and slammed on the brakes then he felt the burn from the airbags.
