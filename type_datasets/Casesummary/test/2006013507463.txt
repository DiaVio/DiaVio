This crash occurred in the intersection of a five-lane, two-way roadway and a two-lane, two-way roadway. The five-lane roadway has a posted speed limit of 48 kmph (30 mph) and is oriented east and west with a center turn lane dividing traffic. The two-lane roadway has a posted speed limit of 40 kmph (25 mph) with north and southbound traffic. This intersection is controlled by normal tricolor traffic signals that at the time were functioning properly. This crash occurred in the early afternoon of a weekend day, that was clear and sunny, the roadway was clear and dry at the time of the crash.
V1, a 1995 Chevrolet Beretta was traveling southbound with two occupants approaching the intersection. V2, a 1996 Eagle Vision was traveling eastbound in the second lane also approaching the intersection. V1 entered the intersection under a green signal, at about the same time as V2 entered the intersection. The front of V1 contacted the left side of V2, the two vehicles then side-slapped, the right side of V1 contacted the left side of V2, before coming to rest in the south east corner of the intersection. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The 30 year-old female driver of V1 stated that she and the 23 year-old female occupant were headed to her home via the intersection, she stated that there was not enough time to see V2 approaching and that she was unable to complete any evasive maneuvers before the crash.
The pre-event movement for V1 is coded going straight; the Critical Pre-crash Event for V1 is "other vehicle encroachment, from crossing street, across path". No critical reason could be coded for this vehicle. The driver refused to answer any questions she didn't feel were relevant to the crash, so no associative factors could be coded for this driver, or vehicle.
The 21 year-old male driver of V2 stated that he was heading back to his mother's home after dropping his sister off at work. He stated that he had never driven this vehicle before and that he rarely travels through this intersection. He stated that he thought the traffic signal was green even though he was unable to say for sure because of the glare from the sun off the lights.
