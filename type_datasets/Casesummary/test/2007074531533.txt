This single-vehicle crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions. The east/west roadway is straight, level, undivided and consists of one lane in each direction with parking on both sides. The speed limit for this roadway is 40 kmph (25 mph).
Vehicle one (V1), a 1995 Mercury Grand Marquis, was westbound in lane one. A non-contact vehicle (NCV) was eastbound on the same roadway. V1 traveled off the right side of the roadway and the front struck a metal utility pole. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.
The driver of V1, a twenty-three-year-old male, had dropped a friend off and was on his way home. He was healthy, well rested and familiar with his vehicle and the roadway. This driver stated that he had been talking on his cell phone and was hanging it up. He looked up and saw that the NCV was driving down the middle of the road. He abruptly steered to the right to avoid it. This driver further stated that he was stressed at work due to working extended shifts and that the sun glare affected his view of the NCV. According to the police report, the result of this driver's BAC was 0.
