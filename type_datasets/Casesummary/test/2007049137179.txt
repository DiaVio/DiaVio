This two-vehicle rear end crash occurred on a westbound, five lane, straight, level roadway with a positive barrier. The speed limit on the roadways is 97 kmph (60 mph). This crash occurred after sunset with moderate traffic. The roadway was lighted. There were no adverse weather conditions at the time of the crash. The fifth travel lane was closed for construction.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2001 Chrysler Grand Voyager with one occupant, was westbound in lane four. Vehicle two (V2), a 2000 Chevrolet Astro Van with one occupant, was westbound in lane four ahead of V1. V2 began to slow for traffic ahead and the front of V1 struck the back of V2. Both vehicles came to rest in lane four. V1 was towed due to damage. Neither of the occupants was transported. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, a twenty-nine year-old male, was traveling home from the hospital. He was rested, in good health and had bought the vehicle two weeks ago. He drives this route daily. This driver stated that he had missed work the previous week while attending to his young son who was in the hospital. The driver had erratic sleep patterns because of his family situation. The driver had been at the hospital the entire day prior to the crash. The driver was also concerned since he has missed so much work due to his son's illness. He had been following V2 for some distance. He did not know what the gap distance was between his vehicle and V2. The driver stated that he braked, but was unable to avoid the collision.
The driver of V2, a twenty-five year-old male, was traveling home from work. He was well rested, in good health and comfortable with his vehicle. He drives this route weekly. The driver stated that he slowed down for the traffic ahead of him. He did not detect V1 prior to the collision and therefore did not attempt any evasive action.
