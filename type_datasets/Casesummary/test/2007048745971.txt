This two-vehicle crash occurred on a straight and level trafficway on the approach to a four-legged, traffic signal-controlled intersection. This trafficway is divided by a raised median with a guardrail. The southbound lanes consist of three through lanes. The speed limit on this roadway is 80 kmph (50 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1999 Ford Escort, was southbound in lane three. Vehicle two (V2), a 2001 Toyota Sequoia, was southbound in lane three in front of V1. V2 stopped and the front of V1 struck the back of V2. V2 moved to the southeast and the front struck the guardrail in the median. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 80 kmph for V1 and 80 kmph for V2.
The driver of V1, a twenty-two-year-old female, was traveling with one passenger. According to the police report, this driver "did not see that traffic had stopped until it was too late." The vehicle damage indicates that this driver steered right in an attempt to avoid a crash. This driver was not interviewed; no further information is available for this driver.
The driver of V2, a thirty-five-year-old male, was on his way to lunch from work. He was healthy, well rested and familiar with his vehicle. He travels this route daily. This driver stated that he saw V1 in his rearview mirror but was unable to avoid the crash due to traffic in front of him.
