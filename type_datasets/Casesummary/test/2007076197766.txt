This single-vehicle crash occurred in a rural area on a four-lane asphalt roadway with an extended depressed median. The two westbound travel lanes curve left and are downhill in the area of the crash. The speed limit is 89 kmph (55 mph). There are warning signs present indicating the #1 lane ends and to merge left, and chevron signs warning of the continuing left curve. The crash occurred late afternoon during a heavy rainstorm.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2005 Chevrolet Silverado K1500 crew-cab pickup, was westbound traveling at a police reported speed of 89 kmph (55 mph) in the #1 lane. V1 was negotiating the left curve when, as stated on the police report, it began to fishtail on the wet roadway. V1 then rotated counter-clockwise as it crossed both travel lane and departed the left road edge into the muddy median where it tripped and slowly rolled right one-quarter turn coming to rest on its right side against a metal post of a warning sign. The driver and two passengers were not injured. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 89 kmph for V1.
