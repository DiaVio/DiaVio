This two-vehicle crash occurred on an urban roadway with no controls. Westbound travel the roadway is undivided, curves right, and is uphill for single through lane. For Eastbound travel, the roadway is undivided, straight, and level for the single through lane. This roadway has a posted speed limit of 48 kmph (30 mph). This crash occurred in the early morning daylight hours on a weekday with moderate traffic flow.
Vehicle one (V1), a 2001 Saturn SL with one occupant, was traveling westbound in lane one, negotiating the right curve. Vehicle two (V2), a 1996 GMC Suburban with two occupants, was traveling eastbound in lane one going straight. The driver of V1 looked down to get her drink and to adjust the radio and V1 crossed into the opposing traffic lane. The driver of V2 steered right and applied the brakes when he saw V1 enter his traffic lane, but the front of V1 impacted the left side of V2. V1 crossed the eastbound traffic lane and impacted the curb on the south side of the road with its left front wheel. V1 departed the roadway and came to final rest facing southwest on the south roadside. V2 rotated counterclockwise in lane one of eastbound traffic and impacted the curb on the south side of the road with its right rear wheel. V2 came to final rest facing north-northeast in lane one. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, a thirty-nine-year-old female, had just dropped her daughter off at school and was going back home. She was well rested, very comfortable with her vehicle, and drives this route daily. She is in good health and takes no medications.
The driver of V2, a forty-seven-yea-old male, was driving from his home and taking his daughter to school. He was rested, very comfortable with his vehicle, and drives this route weekly.
