This two-vehicle crash occurred on a westbound three-lane roadway. The roadway is separated by a raised median with a wide concrete garden planter separating the eastbound and westbound travel lanes. There are three eastbound lanes and a left turn lane that goes into a parking lot. However, this side of the roadway did not play a role in the crash. It was dark, but the roadway was lighted and the speed limit was 58 kph (35 mph). This road was at a hillcrest at the location of the crash.
Vehicle one, a 1996 Jeep Cherokee was traveling westbound in the third lane. Vehicle two, a 1999 Chrysler Concorde was traveling westbound in the second lane. Vehicle one departed all the westbound lanes and impacted a pole with the front plane. Vehicle one then rotated clockwise and rolled over. While vehicle one was rolling over, the right of vehicle two impacted the undercarriage of vehicle one. Vehicle one came to rest on its roof. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of vehicle one, a fifty-year old male was not interviewed. There is no known pre-crash information on this driver. Medical records indicate that this driver had a BAC of 0.435.
The driver of vehicle two, a twenty-five year old male was heading out to dinner at the time of the crash. This driver had been driving for about ten minutes at the time of the crash. This driver stated that he saw vehicle swerve, hit the post and roll over but was unable to avoid it. This driver drives this vehicle daily and over this road on a daily basis.
