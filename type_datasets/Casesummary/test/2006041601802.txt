This crash occurred on a weekday night in the southbound lanes of a high-occupancy, high volume interstate. There were five southbound lanes with paved emergency shoulders on both sides of the roadway. There were concrete barriers on both sides of the roadway. The bituminous roadway was dry, straight, and level with a posted speed limit of 105kmph (65mph). The traffic flow was normal and there were no adverse atmospheric conditions at the time of the crash.
V1, an unidentified vehicle, was southbound in lane one traveling at an unknown speed. V2, a 1997 Toyota corolla, was southbound in lane two ahead of V1 traveling at 113kmph (70 mph). For unknown reasons V1 began to change lanes to the left into lane two. As V1 entered lane two his front left front corner made contact with the right rear of V2. The impact caused V2 to lose control of his vehicle and depart the roadway where he struck the concrete barrier with his right side causing his vehicle to roll left on quarter turn. V1 fled the scene without stopping. Police Reported Travel Speed is Unknown for V1 and Unknown for V2.
V1 fled the scene and could not be located. No subsequent information could be obtained regarding the either the vehicle or the driver.
V2 was driven by a 23 year-old on his commute to work. He stated in the interview that he was driving to work with the cruise control on when all of the sudden was hit from behind by an unidentified vehicle. The impact caused him to lose control of the vehicle. He tried to correct the loss of control by steering left and hitting his brakes but was unable to regain control of the vehicle. The driver was treated on scene for non-incapacitating injuries and V2 was towed due to damage.
