This two-vehicle crash occurred in a four-legged intersection. All approaches to this intersection are controlled by stop signs. Both roadways are straight, undivided and consist of one lane in each direction. The northbound approach is level and the speed limit is 56 kmph (35 mph). The eastbound approach has a ten percent downhill grade and the speed limit is 72 kmph (45 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1996 GMC K1500 pickup, was northbound in lane one, stopped at the stop sign. Vehicle two (V2), a 2001 Lincoln Town Car, was eastbound in lane one. V1 entered the intersection and the left side was struck by the front of V2. The vehicles rotated and the left side of V1 struck the right side of V2. Both vehicles moved off the road in the northeast corner of the intersection. The front of V1 struck a stop sign and the front of V2 struck a ditch. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, a forty-five-year-old male, was on his way home from work. He was healthy, well rested and familiar with his vehicle. He travels this route daily. This driver stated that he saw V2 but assumed that it would stop at the stop sign.
The driver of V2, a fifty-five-year-old female, was on her way from her office to show a house to a client. She was well rested and familiar with her vehicle. She rarely travels this route. She has a thyroid disease and high blood pressure and she takes Lisinopril and Synthroid. This driver stated that she was looking for street signs since she is not familiar with the area and did not see the stop sign or V1 prior to the crash.
