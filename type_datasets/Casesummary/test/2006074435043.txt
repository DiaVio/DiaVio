This two-vehicle collision occurred at a four-way intersection. Both roads are straight and have two travel lanes. The northbound road has a six and one half percent slope. North and southbound traffic is controlled by a stop sign and has a posted speed limit of 40 kmph (25 mph). East and westbound is controlled by a pedestrian activated (school zone) crosswalk traffic signal and has a posted speed limit of 48 kmph (30 mph). No adverse weather conditions present at the time of collision.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1994 Ford Probe was stopped southbound. Vehicle two (V2), a 2002 Land Rover Discovery was westbound in the first lane. V1 began to cross through the intersection and impacted V2 on its right plane with V1's front plane. V1 rotated and came to rest facing west in the first lane. V2 departed the road way and came to rest off the south shoulder. V1 was not towed due to damage. V2 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, a sixteen-year old female had just left her sisters house and was driving to her grandfather's house. This driver had been driving for about three minutes at the time of the crash. This driver indicated that she had stopped and let a few cars pass. This driver also indicated that she saw the other car and it was a long way off. This driver had only been driving for about one year at the time of the crash. This driver drives this vehicle daily and over this road on a daily basis.
The driver of V2, a twenty-three year old female had just left a sports club and was heading to a friends house. This driver had been driving for about fifteen minutes at the time of the crash. This driver indicated that she saw the other car start to pull out but was unable to stop. This driver drives this vehicle on a daily basis and over this road on a weekly basis.
