This two-vehicle collision occurred during daylight hours on a weekday. The crash occurred at the four-leg intersection of a north-south roadway and an east-west roadway. The intersection is controlled by a tri-light traffic signal. The speed limit for north-south traffic is 56 kmph (35 mph), and the limit for east-west traffic is 64 kmph (40 mph). At the time of the crash, there were no adverse atmospheric conditions, and the roadway was free of defects.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1991 Chevrolet Astro Van, was traveling southbound in the first lane of a five-lane, two-way, undivided, dry, level, bituminous roadway, approaching the intersection with the intention of passing straight through. Vehicle two (V2), a 1996 Chevrolet Lumina four-door sedan, was traveling eastbound in the third lane of a seven-lane, two-way, undivided roadway, approaching the same intersection with the intention of passing straight through. As both vehicles entered the intersection, the front of V2 impacted the right side of V1. Both vehicles rotated clockwise and came to rest in the intersection. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
