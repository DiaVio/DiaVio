This two-vehicle crash occurred on the approach to a four-legged, traffic signal-controlled intersection. The east/west roadway is straight and consists of two lanes in each direction and a center left turn lane. The westbound approach has a four percent downhill grade. The speed limit for this roadway is 64 kmph (40 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours and it was raining.
Vehicle one (V1), a 1999 Toyota Corolla, was westbound in lane one, changing lanes into lane two. Vehicle two (V2), a 2002 Dodge Ram pickup, was stopped westbound in lane two. The front of V1 struck the back of V2. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V1, an eighteen-year-old female, was on her way from breakfast to a store. She was well rested, healthy and familiar with her vehicle. She travels this route monthly. This driver stated that she was looking left to ensure that it was clear to make her lane change. When she returned her attention forward, V2 was stopped and she applied the brakes (skidding longitudinally) in an attempt to avoid the crash.
The driver of V2, a twenty-nine-year-old male, was on his way to a store from his home. He was healthy, well rested and familiar with his vehicle. He travels this route daily. This driver stated that he did not see V1 prior to the crash.
