V1, a 2005 Chevrolet Trailblazer was traveling northbound on a 5 lane, two-way dry asphalt roadway, intending to go straight. V2, a 2001 Pontiac Montana was also traveling north on the same road. V2 was stopped at the light and had just started to proceed when the front of V1 contacted the back of V2. V1 came to final rest in the roadway. V2 departed the roadway on the right side after impact and came to final rest off the roadway. .
The driver of V1 was a 59-year old male who had reported that he had been traveling between 50-64Kmph (31-40Mph) prior to the crash. He had reported a history of Parkinson's and had taken several medications prior to the crash. He was wearing his prescribed lenses that corrected astigmatism and a myopic (near-sighted) condition. He stated that he was rested and running an errand. He sustained minor injuries and was transported to a local trauma facility. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
The driver of V2 was a 68-year old male who had reported that he had been traveling between stopped prior to the impact. He had a history of Diabetes and had taken Insulin prior to the crash. He was rested and traveling to the mall. He was wearing his prescribed lenses that corrected a hyperopic (near-sighted) condition. He sustained minor injuries and was transported to a local trauma facility. V2 was towed due to damage.
