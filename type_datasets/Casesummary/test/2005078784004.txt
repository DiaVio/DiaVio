This is a two vehicle crash that occurred during early afternoon daylight hours. The crash occurred at a rural intersection of two roadways. The roadways were bituminous, dry, straight and level. There were no roadway deficiencies and the weather was sunny, clear and dry. The posted speed limit for both roadways 80 kmph (50 mph).
Vehicle one (V1) was a white 1999 Chevrolet Suburban 4x4 utility vehicle driven by a 19 year-old male with one passenger was southbound. Vehicle two (V2) was a yellow 1978 Chevrolet 1500 4x4 pickup towing an empty three-horse trailer driven by a 35 year-old male with one passenger. V 2 slowed at the intersection, signaled to make a left turn and proceeded to make the turn headed east. V1 was behind V2 and attempted to pass on the left when V2 turned left, The left side V2 impacted with V1 on the right side. The impact caused a control loss to V1. V1 departed the roadway on the left, returned to the roadway and the left side tires and wheels dug into the asphalt causing V 1 to overturn on the roadway two quarter turns. V1 came to final rest on the roof facing generally southeast in the #1 southbound lane. The driver of V2 brought the vehicle to a controlled stop,facing east, on the right shoulder of the eastbound roadway. V1 was towed due to damage. V2 was driven from the scene. The driver of V1 was transported to a local medical facility. Police Reported Travel Speed is 80 kmph for V1 and 80 kmph for V2.
