The one-vehicle crash occurred near the intersection of a three-lane, one-way road and a two-lane, undivided roadway. For southbound traffic there is three travel lanes. Southbound traffic has a curve to the right approximately 100-meters (328-feet)prior to the intersection. The road was dry, traveled asphalt. The posted speed limit is 56 kmph (35 mph). Traffic was light at the time of the accident. This crash occurred during daylight hours with clear skies.

Vehicle one, a 1997 Mercury Grand Marquis was southbound in the first travel lane. The driver of vehicle one lost consciousness and departed the roadway on the right side. The front of vehicle one impacted a concrete wall off the roadway on the southwest corner. Vehicle one rotated counterclockwise and came to rest facing south. The vehicle was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

The driver of vehicle one, a thirty-seven year old male was heading to a fast food restaurant from his parent's house at the time of the crash. This driver had been driving for about twenty minutes at the time of the crash. The driver stated that he had a vasectomy earlier that morning and had been told not to eat prior to the procedure. The medical procedure involved the use of anesthesia. The driver recalled exiting the freeway nearby, but has no memory of events until after the collision.

The critical pre-crash event for vehicle one was coded as "this vehicle traveling: off the edge of the road on the right side". The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event was attributed to vehicle one and was coded as "driver related factor-heart attack or other physical impairment of the ability to act." The driver passed out prior to the impact.
