This case occupant was a 35 year old female who was the front right passenger in a severe frontal impact crash. She was wearing the manual lap/shoulder belt and the belt pretensioners actuated and a frontal airbag deployed. She sustained serious injuries and was transported to the trauma center. This crash occurred at night in a dark area during clear and dry weather conditions. Vehicle one (V1), a 2007 Ford F150 crew cab truck was northwest bound on a two lane, two way roadway with paved shoulders. V1 had just negotiated around a curve to the right and was heading north when the driver saw a downed tree that was laying laterally over both lanes and shoulders. The driver of V1 began to brake and steer right but ran over the tree trunk/limb. V1 then continued to the right and departed the roadway and struck a large tree head on. V1 was towed due to damage. The case occupant is a 35 year old female who was the front right passenger. She was restrained by the manual lap/shoulder belt and her belt buckle pretensioner actuated on impact. A front instrument panel airbag also deployed in her position. She sustained serious injuries and was transported to the trauma center. The driver, a 29 year old male who was wearing the manual lap/shoulder belt, was the only other occupant in the vehicle. His seat belt buckle pretensioner actuated and the steering column mounted air bag deployed in his position. He sustained minor injuries and was treated on scene.
