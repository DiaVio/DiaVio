This single car crash occurred off the right side of a wet, rural, 2-lane, 2-way, gravel/slag, level, and straight roadway. At the time of this weekday early afternoon crash, it was snowing and the roadway was wet and snow covered. The unposted speed limit was 89 kmph (55mph).

V1, a 2006 Chrysler Pacifica solely occupied by the 33-year-old male driver, was northbound in lane 1 of the roadway described above. V1 drifted off the roadway to the right and struck a tree on the edge of the road. V1 struck the tree with its front. The driver was injured and transported. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

The driver of V1 stated that he had left work and was on his way home. He was traveling about 30 mph for the first time on that roadway thinking about his wife who was 9 months pregnant. He was not in a hurry but was anxious to get home, as she was due anytime. The last thing he remembered was going straight down the road then waking up with strangers asking him if he was all right after striking the tree. He said that he passed out prior to leaving the road and passed out again while sitting in the car following the crash. There was no evidence of any avoidance actions by the driver at the scene.

The Critical Pre-crash Event for V1 was "This vehicle traveling off the edge of the road on the right side." The Critical Reason for the Critical Pre-crash Event was "Heart attack or other physical impairment of the ability to act" because the driver blacked out. Associated factors coded to the driver of V1 include the driver's losing consciousness prior to running off the road, the driver's pre-existing medical problems (he is bipolar), several prescription medications, his fatigue from sleeping problems, the wet/snow covered condition of the road, and his lack of knowledge of the roadway.
