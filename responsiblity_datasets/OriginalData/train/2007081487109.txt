This single-vehicle crash occurred a couple hours past noon during daylight hours. The location was a rural section of a state route highway. The roadway was a bituminous three lane divided highway. The roadway was straight and level. The weather was clear and dry. The posted speed limit was 97 kmph (60 mph).

Vehicle one (V1), a 2003 Chevy Silverado 4X4 Extended cab driven by a thirty six (36) year old male with one passenger, was traveling northbound in lane one, when it departed the roadway via the right shoulder. It impacted a narrow pole, and continued in a north-east direction, impacting then climbing up over a gravel hill / embankment and came to rest in the bushes. The vehicle was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

The driver of V1, reported to the officer on-scene that he passed out after having a coughing attack. The driver was using chew (smokeless tobacco) and had some go down his throat causing coughing fit & then found it hard to breath. All other factors for this driver are not available, as he was not interviewed

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: this vehicle traveling, off the edge of the road on the right side. The critical reason was coded as a driver related factor: heart attack or other impairment of the ability to act. The driver passed out while driving after excessive coughing from swallowing chewing tobacco.
