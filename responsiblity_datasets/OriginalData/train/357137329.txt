This case involves an impact of a passenger car with the ground that resulted in an arrested roll into a utility pole. The 19-year-old male right-front passenger is the case occupant. He was restrained by a three-point belt. The top-mounted instrument-panel air bag deployed. The case vehicle a 2010, two-door Chevrolet Cobalt was eastbound on a two-lane, dry asphalt road with a 6% downgrade. It was clear and dark. The case vehicle crossed the center-line, the westbound travel lane, departed off the road from the north side, entered a counterclockwise yaw, traversed a downgrade into a shallow ditch, struck a sign and engaged the opposite side of the ditch with the undercarriage in front of a group of small trees and brush. The undercarriage impact and yaw initiated a right-side leading roll. At the same time, the front end swiped a number of the small trees and brush. The vehicle rolled one quarter-turn and struck a utility pole with the roof at the back-light header. The vehicle rotated clockwise around the pole, reversed the direction of the roll and came to rest on its wheels facing east on a private drive. The 19-year-old male driver was reportedly not restrained by a three point belt, but the steering-wheel air bag deployed. The 19-year-old male right-front passenger was restrained by a three-point belt, and the top-mounted instrument-panel air bag deployed. The 20-year-old male right-rear passenger was reportedly not restrained by a three point belt. The driver and the right-rear passenger sustained police reported possible injuries. The 19-year-old male right-front passenger is the case occupant. He sustained multiple moderate injuries. He reportedly exited the vehicle on his own. He was transported to a level-one trauma center and enrolled as a case occupant.
