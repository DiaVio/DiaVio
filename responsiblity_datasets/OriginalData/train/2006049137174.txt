This two-vehicle crash occurred in a three-leg intersection. The east/west roadway is divided by a curbed median and level. The westbound approach lanes are straight and consist of three through lanes and a left turn lane. The eastbound approach lanes curve left and consist of three through lanes. The speed limit on this roadway is 56 kmph (35 mph). The intersecting roadway is on the south side of the east/west roadway and is undivided. This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions. There was rush hour congestion at the time of the crash.

Vehicle one (V1), a 2000 Hyundai Elantra, was westbound in lane four. Vehicle two (V2), a 1995 Chevrolet 1500 pickup, was eastbound in lane one. The traffic in eastbound lanes two and three was stopped. V1 turned left (south) at the intersection and the right side was struck by the front of V2. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The driver of V1, a forty-four-year-old female, did not speak English and was not interviewed.

The driver of V2, a fifty-six-year-old female, was traveling, with a forty-seven-year-old female, from work to her home. She was well rested, healthy and familiar with her vehicle. This driver travels this route daily.

The pre-event movement for V1 was coded as "changing lanes." The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "this vehicle traveling; turning left at intersection." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "driver related factor; type of driver error unknown."

The pre-event movement for V2 was coded as "negotiating a curve." The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded as "other vehicle encroachment; from opposite direction - over left lane line." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V2 was "not coded to this vehicle."
