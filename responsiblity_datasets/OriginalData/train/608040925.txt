This case involves a single vehicle impacting a large light-standard with its front end and a subsequent two-quarter turn roll over event. The case vehicle a 2000 Dodge Grand Caravan minivan was traveling in the center northbound lane of six-lane, dry, level asphalt divided highway. It was dark, but lighted and there was no adverse weather. The driver fell asleep and the vehicle drifted across the roadway and departed the road off the west shoulder. The case vehicle went down an embankment, traversed a shallow ditch, went up the opposite embankment and the left side of the front end stuck a 60-cm light standard. The impact caused the vehicle to rotate counterclockwise. As it rotated the wheels dug into the ground causing the vehicle to trip and rollover two-quarter turns. The vehicle was towed due to damage. The sole occupant, the 44-year-old male driver was restrained by a three-point belt and the steering-wheel air bag deployed. He sustained multiple moderate injuries and was transported to level-one trauma center and enrolled as a case occupant.
