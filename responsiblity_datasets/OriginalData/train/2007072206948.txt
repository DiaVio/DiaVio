This two-vehicle crash occurred at a three-legged, traffic signal-controlled intersection. The east/west trafficway is straight, level and divided by a curbed median. There are two travel lanes in each direction with parking lanes on both sides. The speed limit for this roadway is 48 kmph (30 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.

Vehicle one (V1), a 1998 Ford Contour, was westbound in lane two. Vehicle two (V2), a 2000 Ford Explorer, was westbound in lane two in front of V1. V2 stopped at the intersection. The front of V1 struck the back of V2. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The driver of V1, a twenty-six-year-old male, was not interviewed. According to the police report, this driver was arrested for drug impairment.

The driver of V2, a twenty-year-old female, was on the way home from running an errand. She was healthy, well rested and familiar with her vehicle. She travels this route daily. This driver stated that she did not see V1 prior to the crash.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "other motor vehicle in lane; other vehicle stopped." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "driver related factor; type of driver error unknown."

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded as "other motor vehicle in lane; traveling in same direction with higher speed." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V2 was "not coded to this vehicle."
