This single-vehicle crash occurred on a one-way, two-lane, straight roadway with a downhill grade. There is a steep downhill embankment on the left side of the roadway. The speed limit on this roadway is 105 kmph (65 mph). It was dark at the time of the crash with no adverse atmospheric conditions.

Vehicle one (V1), a 1995 Nissan Sentra, was southbound in lane one. V1 rotated counterclockwise and departed the left side of the roadway. V1 rolled over to the right seven quarter-turns. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Unknown for V1.

The driver of V1, a fifty-five-year-old female, was traveling with two passengers, a fifty-seven-year-old female and a two-year-old male. This driver was killed in this crash; no surrogate was available for an interview. According to the police report, the female passenger reported that this driver had an argument on a cell phone just prior to the crash. One witness to the crash reported to the police that he saw V1 lose control and that it looked like the driver overcorrected and rolled into the ditch. Another witness reported to the police that she saw the collision in her rearview mirror and that it appeared that several vehicles in the vicinity were swerving. A brother of this driver informed the police that this driver was going to a store to buy groceries and pick up her prescription medications. The autopsy on this driver indicated "acute hydrocodone intoxication."

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: this vehicle control loss due to; unknown cause of control loss. The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as a driver related factor: type of driver error unknown. Acute hydrocodone intoxication was an associative factor and may have been related to a reduction of the driver's ability to control V1. A witness observed the driver had appeared to overcompensate initiating the control loss.
