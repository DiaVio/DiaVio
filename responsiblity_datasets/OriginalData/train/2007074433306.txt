This single-vehicle crash occurred on a one-way, straight roadway that has an eight percent uphill grade. In the precrash area, this roadway consists of three through lanes. In the crash area, the right lane is a parking lane and there are two through lanes. The speed limit for this roadway is 48 kmph (30 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.

Vehicle one (V1), a 2007 Chevrolet Malibu, was eastbound in lane two. Vehicle two (V2), a 1995 Dodge Caravan, was parked in the parking lane. V1 traveled to the right and the front struck the back of V2. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The driver of V1, a seventy-five-year-old female, was traveling from a cardiac rehabilitation center to work. She was well rested, comfortable with her vehicle and travels this route daily. This driver has diabetes and heart problems and takes the medications Glucophage, Indapamide, Cymbalta, Levothroid and Metoprolol Tartrate. This driver stated that she was concerned about her health at the time of the crash. She further stated that she "needed a cigarette real bad" and was attempting to find a pack of cigarettes in her purse just prior to the crash.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "this vehicle traveling; over the lane line on right side of travel lane." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "driver related factor; internal distraction.
