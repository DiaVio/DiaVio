It was dusk with heavy rain at the time of this single-vehicle crash. The roadway is undivided and consists of two lanes in the westbound direction and one lane in the eastbound direction. The westbound lanes have an uphill grade and curved to the right (the superelevation is seven percent). On the right side of the westbound lanes, the grass embankment has a 33 percent downhill grade. The speed limit for this roadway is 97 kmph (60 mph).

Vehicle one (V1), a 2006 Ford F250 pickup, was westbound in lane two. A non-contact vehicle (NCV) was in front of V1. V1 moved to lane one to pass the NCV. V1 then moved back into lane two and the driver lost control. V1 rotated clockwise and traveled off the right side of the roadway. V1 rolled to the left four quarter-turns and the left side struck a tree. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

The driver of V1, a thirty-three-year-old male, was on his way home from work. He was well rested, healthy and comfortable with his vehicle. He travels this route daily. This driver stated that he did not remember the details of the crash. This driver has obsessive-compulsive disorder and takes the medications Aciphex and Zoloft. The witness (driver of the NCV) and the researcher noted the smell of alcohol on this driver.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "this vehicle control loss due to traveling too fast for conditions." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "driver related factor; too fast for conditions - heavy rain and curved road." This driver's poor directional control was an associated factor in this crash. Additionally, his alcohol use may have been an associated factor in this crash.
