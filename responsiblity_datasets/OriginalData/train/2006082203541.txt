This two-vehicle crash occurred on a straight, level, undivided roadway at dawn. This roadway consists of two lanes in each direction and parking lanes on both sides. The speed limit on this roadway is 56 kmph (35 mph). There were no adverse atmospheric conditions at the time of the crash.

Vehicle one (V1), a 1989 Honda Civic CRX, was northbound in lane one. Vehicle two (V2), a 1986 Toyota pickup, was parked and unoccupied on the right side of the northbound lanes. A non-contact vehicle that was northbound in lane two attempted to change lanes to the right. The driver of V1 applied the brakes and steered right, skidding off the right side of the roadway. The front of V1 struck the back of V2. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The driver of V1, an eighteen-year-old male, was not interviewed; no information is available for this driver.

The pre-event movement for V1 was coded as "going straight." The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "other vehicle encroachment; from adjacent lane (same direction) - over left lane line." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "driver related factor; too fast to be able to respond to unexpected actions of others."
