This one-vehicle crash occurred during early evening hours just east of a four-way intersection. The north/south roadway is comprised of two asphalt through lanes in each direction and a left-turn lane. The east/west roadway is structured the same, except large concrete planters separate the opposite direction traffic. The speed limit was 48 kmph (30 mph). Light rain had fallen just prior to the crash, causing the roads to be wet and slick.

Vehicle one (V1) was a 1998 Ford Expedition XLT SUV driven by a 40 year-old female who was traveling southbound in the third (left-turn) lane. The driver turned left in the intersection and lost control for unknown reasons. The vehicle likely began to rotate counter-clockwise and the driver overcompensated back to the right, putting the vehicle into a clockwise spin. V1 departed the roadway to the south and the left side contacted a tree. The vehicle continued southwest and its front contacted a chain-link fence. The vehicle was towed due to damage and the driver was transported to a medical facility, The police report indicated the driver said that her steering malfunctioned. This was not confirmed by researcher. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: this vehicle loss due to, traveling too fast for conditions. The critical reason for the critical event was coded as a driver related factor: poor directional control.

Note: The roadway had been dry for a long period and the rainfall likely mixed with the oil buildup, causing slick conditions.
