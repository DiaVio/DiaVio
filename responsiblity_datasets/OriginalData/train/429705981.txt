This investigation focused on the injury mechanisms of a 46-year-old female belted driver with no deployed airbags. She sustained serious injuries in a small overlap frontal impact to a large tree. The 2006 Cheverolet Trailblazer compact utility case vehicle sustained moderate damage. The crash occurred during midafternoon hours on a dry road under clear skies. A 2006 Chevrolet Trailblazer compact utility vehicle (Vehicle 1) was traveling north on a two-way, two-lane suburban road negotiating a right curve. The bituminous road was on a 7% downhill grade with 10% superelevation in the curve. The driver of V1 was reported by police to have had a loss of consciousness prior to the crash. V1 departed the left side of the lane and continued off the left side of the road. V1 traversed a short downwards embankment and then struck a large tree (25 cm x 50 cm) with its front left corner (Event 1). V1 came to rest at the point of impact and was towed due to damage. The 46-year-old female driver was belted at the time of the crash. The available retractor pretensioner was not actuated. The vehicle was not equipped with buckle or anchor pretensioners. The available steering wheel hub and mid right instrument panel airbags were not deployed in the crash. No other airbags were installed. This occupant was transported to a local trauma center for treatment of serious injuries. There were no other occupants in the vehicle.
