The case vehicle, a 2005 Jeep Liberty 4x4 SUV was traveling south on a dry, level asphalt, three-lane roadway approaching a three-leg intersection on a curve. Vehicle two (V2) a 1979 Buick Le Sabre 4-door sedan was northbound on the same roadway approaching the same intersection on the right sweeping curve. It was daylight and there was no adverse weather. The case vehicle entered the right turn lane intending to enter a traffic channel leading to the westbound lane of the intersecting roadway. V2 crossed the center line and the front of the case vehicle in an off-set, oblique frontal impact. The impact caused the case vehicle to rotate clockwise before to came to a rest in the right-turn lane facing southwest. V2 rotated counterclockwise before it came to a rest in the southbound traffic lane straddling the center line, facing northwest. The 85-year-old male driver was wearing a 3-point restraint and was the only occupant of the case vehicle. The driver frontal-impact air bag deployed. The driver was transported to a level-one trauma center with serious injuries and was enrolled as a case occupant.
