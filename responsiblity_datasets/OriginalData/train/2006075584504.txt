This single-vehicle crash occurred on a divided roadway. The southbound lanes curve to the left in the precrash area, have an uphill grade and consist of two through lanes. The roadway was ice-covered at the time of the crash. There is a shoulder on the right side with a snow bank. The speed limit on this roadway is 89 kmph (55 mph). It was dark at the time of the crash with blowing snow.

Vehicle one (V1), a 1986 Jeep CJ-7, was southbound in lane one. V1 began to "fishtail." V1 rotated (counterclockwise) off the right side of the road. V1 then rolled over to the right two quarter-turns and came to rest on its top. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 64 kmph for V1.

The driver of V1, a thirty-six-year-old male, was traveling from his friend's house to his home. This driver was healthy, well rested and comfortable with his vehicle. He travels this route daily. This driver stated that he was traveling at 64 kmph (40 mph) and he knew that the roadway was icy. He steered left and right in an attempt to regain control of V1.

The pre-event movement for V1 was coded as "negotiating a curve." The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "this vehicle control loss due to traveling too fast for conditions." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "driver related factor; traveling too fast for conditions - icy roads." Poor directional control (e.g. failing to control vehicle with skill ordinarily expected) was an associated factor.
