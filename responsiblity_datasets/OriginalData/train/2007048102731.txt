This single-vehicle crash occurred mid-morning on a clear day with no adverse atmospheric conditions. This crash occurred at a "T" type intersection. The west to east roadway is straight, level, bituminous and has four lanes. A two-lane roadway that runs north to south intersects the eastbound lanes. This roadway is controlled with a stop sign. The speed limit is 40kmph (25mph).

Vehicle one (V1), a 1994 Cadillac Deville with three occupants, was eastbound in lane one at a high rate of speed. The driver was attempting to elude the police. The driver lost control as he attempted to make a right turn onto the intersecting street. The left front wheel of V1 struck the east curb of the north to south roadway. V1 then departed the roadway and the front of the vehicle struck a tree. V1 came to final rest off the roadway facing SE. The vehicle was towed due to disabling damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 64 kmph for V1.

Neither the driver nor passengers could be located for an interview. The police on scene stated that narcotic detectives initiated the police chase. The driver was going too fast for the turn and focused on the pursuing police officers than the roadway ahead. According to the police report, the driver was unemployed.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as " this vehicle control loss due to traveling too fast for conditions." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "driver related factor; decision error too fast for turn."
