This near-side impact crash case involves a 47 year old female driver, wearing the available manual lap/shoulder belt with a deployed side impact air bag. She sustained serious injuries. This crash occurred during daylight hours in a four legged intersection under clear weather and dry roadway conditions. The intersection is comprised of one north and one southbound asphalt travel lane, with no traffic controls present. The intersecting east/west asphalt roadway is comprised of one east and one westbound lane, with stop signs present. The case vehicle one (V1), a 2005 Honda Pilot compact utility, was traveling east in lane one and Vehicle 2 (V2), a 1995 Ford F250 super cab large pickup, was traveling south on the intersecting street. Both vehicles entered the intersection and the front of V2 impacted the left side of V1, resulting in the deployment of V1's left side impact air bag. V1 rotated counterclockwise as it moved laterally and came to final rest near the southeast corner, facing northeast. After the initial impact, V2 continued traveling forward and came to final rest near V1, facing southeast. V1 was towed due to damage. V2 was driven from the scene. The case occupant is the 47 year old female driver of V1 who was wearing the available manual lap/shoulder belt. This case occupant's outboard seat back mounted side air bag deployed during the crash. She sustained serious injuries and was transported to the trauma center. There were no other occupants in the vehicle.
