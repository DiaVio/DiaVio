This single-vehicle crash occurred on a two-lane asphalt roadway in a rural area with thick forest bordering both directions of travel. In the area of the crash, the westbound lane was straight and level gradually going uphill in the area of impact. The crash occurred at dusk with no adverse weather or road conditions present. The speed limit for the area is 88 kph (55 mph).

Vehicle one (V1), a 1998 Dodge Caravan with two occupants, was westbound traveling at approximately 80 kph (50 mph) when a large elk suddenly approached from the right into the roadway. The driver stated she had no time to avoid the animal and struck it with the front plane. V1 then pulled off to the right side of the road and came to rest. V1 was towed from the scene. Neither occupant was injured. Police Reported Travel Speed is 89 kmph for V1.

The driver of V1, a 59-year-old female and her husband were coming from a campsite 10 miles away in their daughter's vehicle. They were traveling to a neighboring town to pick up supplies at a drugstore. The driver stated she watched another elk cross the road prior to contacting the second elk. Oncoming traffic struck the first elk as it crossed the roadway. The driver was engaged in a conversation with the passenger at the time of the crash.
