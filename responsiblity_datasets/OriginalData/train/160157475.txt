The focus of this case is on a 44-year old, male driver (case occupant), of a 2006 Toyota Prius (V1), involved in an angled frontal impact with a 1999 Ford Escort (V2). As a result of the first impact, V1 was displaced rearward into two vehicles parked along the edge of the roadway, a 2002 Dodge Ram pickup truck (V3) and a 1994 Nissan Maxima (V4). This multiple vehicle collision occurred during the morning hours (daylight), of an autumn weekend day (Saturday), on a curved and sloped section of a two-lane roadway. This bituminous roadway runs east and west. The weather was rainy and the roadway surface was wet. The speed limit for the east/west roadway is 48 km/h (30 mph). The 44-year old male driver (case occupant) was operating V1 in the westbound travel lane. He was wearing the available 3-point lap/shoulder belt and had the benefit of an advanced frontal impact air bag. For unknown reasons, V2 crossed the centerline and impacted V1 V1's driver frontal impact air bag deployed. V2 rotated clockwise approximately 15 degrees and came to rest facing southeasterly in the easterly travel lane. V1 was displaced rearward into V3 and V4 legally parked along the north edge of the westbound travel lane. V1 contacted V3 with V1s' right side plane, then contacted V4 with its right rear bumper, coming to rest pointing in a southwesterly direction. The driver of V1 (case occupant) was removed from the vehicle and transported, by ground rescue, to a trauma center and hospitalized with serious injuries. V1 was towed due to damage sustained in the crash. The driver V2 was transported to a local medical facility. The extent of his injuries and/or treatments is unknown. V1 and V2 were towed due to damage sustained in the crash.
