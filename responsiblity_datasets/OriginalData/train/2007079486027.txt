This single vehicle crash occurred during early evening hours. It was dark with overhead street lights at the time of the crash. The crash occurred on a one lane undivided, dry, one way, roadway comprised of asphalt. The roadway curves to the right as it approaches the main street and is an access road for a mail drop-off of a post office. The roadway is controlled by a stop sign.

Vehicle one (V1) is a 1997 Buick LeSabre sedan. driven by a eighty nine (89) year old female with one passenger was traveling west when it departed the roadway to the left impacting the wall of a building. V1 came to rest facing northwest at impact. V1 was towed with major damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: this vehicle traveling, off the edge of the road on the left side. The critical reason for the critical event was coded as a driver related factor: poor directional control (e.g., failing to control vehicle with skill ordinarily expected). The driver had inadvertently put her foot on the accelerator instead of the brake while dropping off mail at the post office.
