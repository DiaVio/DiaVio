The crash occurred on a fully controlled highway with three lanes in each direction divided by a concrete median barrier. The concrete roadway has a posted speed limit of 89 kmph (55 mph) and has a downhill grade of 14%. Conditions at the time of the weekday afternoon crash were daylight, cloudy and wet.

V1, a 1997 Ford Explorer XLT 4 door sports utility vehicle was driven by a 33 year-old male with a 31year-old male front right passenger. The men were on their way home from work. V1 entered the eastbound highway into lane one from an entrance ramp on the right. V1 changed lanes to lane two. The driver stated that he changed lanes again to lane three traveling about 40-45 mph. The passenger stated that the driver's cell phone rang and he answered it and the front left wheel of v1 left the roadway to the snow covered paved shoulder on the left. The front left corner of v1 struck the concrete median wall. V1 rolled to the right 2-quarter turns and came to rest in lane two facing northeast. V1 was towed from the scene due to damage. The fully restrained driver and occupant denied injuries at the scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

The Critical Precrash Event coded to v1 was 'this vehicle traveling off the edge of the roadway on the left side'. The Critical Reason coded to this driver was 'internal distraction-using cell phone'.
