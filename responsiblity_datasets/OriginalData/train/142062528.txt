Vehicle #1 (case vehicle) was a 2003 Toyota Rav4, four-door utility vehicle. There was only one occupant in the case vehicle with the front left occupant (driver) being our case study participant: Driver (case occupant)-was a 24-year-old female/ Frontal Advanced Air bag (dual stage/steering wheel mounted) Seat back mounted air bag and front side curtain with a manual lap and shoulder belt (with pretensioners) were available/ Frontal Advanced Air bag deployed and it appears the manual lap and shoulder belt was not in use (pretensioner fired in unused position). Side air bags did not deploy. This crash occurred in the evening, on a dry (level/bituminous) residential roadway with no adverse weather conditions. The crash occurred on a two-lane roadway (one northbound lane and one southbound lane) with parking on both sides of the street. The involved roadways had a speed limit of 40kmph (25mph). . Vehicle #1 (case vehicle) was initially traveling northbound when the driver steered off the right side of the roadway and impacted a tree with the front. After initial impact, V1 rotated clockwise 90 degrees before traveling to its final resting position facing east. The driver of V1was extricated from the vehicle after 23 minutes (due to injury precautions and mechanically restrained i.e. foot pedals) and transported from the scene to a local trauma center where she was admitted for 15 days for injuries received in the crash.
