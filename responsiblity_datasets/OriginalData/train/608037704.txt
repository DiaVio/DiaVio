The case vehicle a five-door 2007 Chevrolet HHR was northbound on a wet, level asphalt two-lane roadway approaching a three-leg intersection. It was dark and there was a fog. The driver traversed the intersection departed the road off the north shoulder and struck a 36-cm wooden utility pole with its front end. The case vehicle rotated counterclockwise slightly and came to a rest facing north-northeast. The case vehicle was towed due to damage. The 23-year-old female driver and the 23-year-old female right-front passenger were the only occupants of the case vehicle and were not restrained by a three-point belt. The driver sustained 'B' injuries was transported to an outside hospital and later transferred to a level-one trauma center. The right-front passenger was also transported to an outside hospital and later transferred to a level-one trauma center with severe injuries where she was enrolled as a case occupant. Note: The impact deformed the right-front longitudinal structural member of the case vehicle, but the top-instrument panel air bag did not deploy.
