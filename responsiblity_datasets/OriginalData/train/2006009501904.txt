This crash took place at a signal-controlled, T type intersection of two roadways. The north/south roadway was four lanes divided, with a tree-linedmedian. The roadway had a curve right with a measured radius of curvature of 161 meters. The posted speed limit for this roadway was 64kmph (40mph). The east/west roadway was six lanes divided by a grass median. The south edge of this roadway ran adjacent to a commercial construction site and the roadside surface was measured to be 62 centimeters lower than the edge of the road.

V1, a 1992 Honda Accord LX SW, was traveling south in lane one, negotiating a curve right, and approaching the intersection. V1 departed its lane to the left and entered lane two shortly before entering the intersection. V1 continued through the intersection, struck and displaced the newly formed curb on the south edge of the roadway. This vehicle then continued over the edge of the roadway and came to rest on a dirt berm in the construction area. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

V1 was driven by a 39 year-old male who stated that he had stopped at a local bar for a few drinks before continuing home from work. He stated that he remembered approaching the intersection and meaning to turn left, but did not remember the crash sequence. This driver also stated that he had realized his condition was affecting his driving prior to the crash.

The Critical Pre crash Event for this vehicle was coded this vehicle traveling end departure, and the Critical Reason was coded other critical non-performance - passed out.
