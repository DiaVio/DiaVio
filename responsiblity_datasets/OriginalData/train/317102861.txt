This case involved the 41 year old restrained female driver a 2009 Toyota Highlander four door sport utility vehicle (V1). She was restrained by the three-point manual safety belt and also had deployed airbags. The steering wheel mounted frontal airbag, the lower left instrument panel knee airbag, the left seat mounted airbag and the left roof side rail head curtain airbag all deployed in the crash. The crash involved impacts with a large boulder, two wire fences and a large tree. The crash occurred during daylight hours on a two lane dry asphalt roadway. The roadway was straight with a 5% uphill grade and the posted speed limit was 80 kph (50 mph). V1 was traveling west at approximately 56 kph (35 mph) as it reached the hilltop. The road forked at the hilltop with the intersecting roadway going off to the left and V1's roadway curving off to the right. V1 continued straight ahead and departed the roadway sideswiping a large boulder with its left side and impacting a fence with its frontal plane. V1 continued in a forward motion, traveling down a negative 12% grassy slope to impact another fence and a large tree with its front. V1 was towed from the scene due to disabling damage. The driver of V1 was transported by ground ambulance to a level one trauma center for treatment of injuries sustained in the crash.
