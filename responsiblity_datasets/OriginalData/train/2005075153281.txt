This single vehicle crash occurred shortly before mid-night on a dry, dark, two-lane, undivided state highway on an S-curve. The highway runs generally runs east and west; however, runs north and south at the location of the crash. The highway has one through lane in each direction and the speed limit is 64 kmph (40 mph). Approximately 0.3 kilometers (0.2 miles) before the crash, there is a warning sign, warning of curved roads and advising a speed of 40 kmph (25 mph). At the scene of the crash there are yellow warning arrows, warning of the curve. There were no adverse atmospheric conditions present at the time.

Vehicle one (V1), a 1999 Jeep Cherokee, driven by a 77 year-old male was traveling eastbound in lane one approaching a sharp left curve. V1 braked, and left skid marks departing the roadway and traveled off the right side of the road onto a soft dirt shoulder. V1 traveled on the dirt shoulder and began to swerve to the left. Its front contacted a small tree then continued and its front contacted a second small tree. It drove off a small ditch embankment and re-entered the roadway. V1 then began to rotate slightly clockwise as it crossed both lanes of traffic. V1 drove off the left side of the road onto another dirt shoulder. It continued until its front contacted a large rock and its right side contacted some bushes. V1 came to rest on the left shoulder on top of the large rock with its right side against the bushes facing north. V1 was towed due to moderate frontal and undercarriage damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 56 kmph for V1.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: this vehicle control loss due to- traveling too fast for conditions. The critical reason for the critical event was coded as a driver related factor: too fast for curve/turn.
