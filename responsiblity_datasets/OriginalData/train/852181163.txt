The case occupant is a 26 year old female driver, wearing a manual lap/shoulder belt, involved in a frontal collision with a frontal impact air bag deployment who sustained moderate injuries. This crash occurred on two westbound one way travel lanes of a divided four lane freeway during daylight hours with clear, dry conditions. Vehicle one (V1), a 2006 Ford Focus, four door sedan, was travelling in lane one behind vehicle two (V2), a 1995 Toyota T100 pickup truck. Ahead of V2 was a third vehicle, a four door sedan. V2 and V3 began to decelerate when the front of V1 impacted the back of V2 that then forced the front of V2 into the back of V3. All vehicles were towed. The case occupant in the driver of V1, a 26 year old female, who was wearing the manual lap/shoulder belt and the frontal steering column mounted airbag deployed. This driver sustained moderate injuries and was transported to the trauma center. Two other occupants were in V1. A two year old female was seated in the second row left using the manual lap/shoulder belt with a low back booster child seat. A six year old male was in the second row right seat and was not restrained. Both sustained only minor injuries and were transported to the trauma center and both were treated and released.
