A solo vehicle nighttime collision occurred during the evening hours on a Monday on an urban street. The collision occurred on a five-lane north/south roadway with parallel parking allowed on both sides of the street. The roadway consists of two lanes of travel in each direction with a median left turn lane. The asphalt surface was dry at the time of the collision. The roadway is designated a 56 kmph (35 mph) zone. The roadway was dark but lighted at this time.

Vehicle one (V1) is a 2000 Grand Cherokee Laredo 4 x 2 utility vehicle. V1 traveling southbound in the number one lane. Vehicle two (V2) is a 2004 Dodge Neon, four-door sedan, legally parked along the west curb. Vehicle three (V3) is a 1997 Volkswagen Jetta, four-door sedan, legally parked along the west curb. Vehicle four (V4) is a Honda Accord, 4-door sedan, also legally parked along the west curb. The three parked vehicles were parallel parked facing south. Police Reported Travel Speed is No PAR obtained (created) for V1 and No PAR obtained (created) for V2.

As V1 was traveling southbound, its front plane crossed over the right lane line and contacted the left plane of V2. V1 continued forward where the front plane contacted the rear plane V3. V1 then contacted the rear plane of V4 as V1 rotated clockwise. V1 then rolled one quarter turn onto its left side where it came to rest facing northwest adjacent to vehicle four. V1 was towed from the scene. The driver was not injured and left the scene with relatives. A police report was not generated as a result of the collision.

The driver of V1 was not available for an interview, a police report was not generated and minimal information is available on the driver. The driver refused to discuss the collision with researchers on scene. The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: this vehicle traveling, over the lane line on right side of travel lane. The critical reason was coded to V1 as a driver related factor: type of driver error unknown.
