This two-vehicle crash occurred just past noon during daylight hours on a dry, straight, level state highway at a on-colors controlled intersection. Southbound traffic lanes consist of a right turn lane, two through lanes, and a left turn lane that is separated by a painted median. The northbound traffic lanes consist of a right turn lane, two through lanes, and two left turn lanes. The posted speed limit 64 kmph (40 mph).

Vehicle one (V1), a 2004 Chevrolet Malibu sedan driven by a 65 year-old female was traveling southbound in lane two. Vehicle two (V2), a 1989 Saab 900-S two door hatchback driven by a 17 year-old female was traveling northbound in lane five. Both vehicles entered the intersection, V1 intending to go straight and V2 intending to make a left turn. The front of V1 contacted the right side of V2. V1 rotated clockwise and came to rest in the intersection facing southwest. V2 rotated counterclockwise and came to rest in the intersection facing southwest. Both vehicles were towed from the scene.. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: other vehicle encroachment; from opposite direction, over left lane line. The critical reason was not coded to this vehicle.

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded: this vehicle traveling, turning left at intersection. The critical reason for the critical event was coded to V2 as a driver related factor: false assumption of other's actions. An additional factor was inadequate surveillance.
