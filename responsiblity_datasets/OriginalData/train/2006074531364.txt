This single-vehicle crash occurred on a curbed-median-divided roadway. The two northbound lanes consist of two through lanes and are level and curved to the left. The speed limit on this roadway is 48 kmph (30 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.

Vehicle one (V1), a 1994 Nissan Sentra, was northbound in lane one. V1 traveled into the left lane and the driver steered right and applied the brakes. V1 rotated clockwise and the right front tire/wheel struck the east curb. V1 continued rotating and rolled over three quarter-turns. V1 came to rest in a driveway on its right side facing southeast and was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

The driver of V1, a thirty-year-old male, was traveling from a mall to his home. He was well rested, healthy and familiar with his vehicle. He travels this roadway daily. This driver's BAC was .128 two hours after the crash.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as: this vehicle traveling; over the lane line on left side of travel lane. The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as a driver related factor: overcompensation.
