This two-vehicle crash occurred shortly after noon on a four-lane, bituminous expressway, with a concrete barrier dividing the east and westbound lanes. The crash occurred in lane one of the eastbound roadway. The roadway is straight, level, has no visible deficiencies, has a speed limit of 89 kmph (55 mph) and has shoulders on the north and south sides. The weather at the time of the crash was clear and there was mild congestion due to merging of different expressways.

Vehicle one (V1), a 2001 two-door Ford Explorer driven by a 65-year-old male, was heading eastbound in lane one at a police reported excessive speed. Vehicle two (V2), a four-door 2000 Lincoln Navigator driven by a 33-year-old male, was heading eastbound in lane one ahead of V1. V2 slowed to about 16 kmph (10 mph) due to the traffic. V1 then rear-ended V2, they both came to final rest shortly after the impact. Both vehicles were towed due to severe front-end damage to V1 and significant rear-end damage on V2. Both drivers were transported to local area hospitals with reported injuries. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as: other motor vehicle in lane, traveling in same direction while decelerating. The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as: driver related factor, unknown recognition error.

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded as: other motor vehicle in lane, traveling in the same direction with higher speed. The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V2 was not coded to this vehicle.
