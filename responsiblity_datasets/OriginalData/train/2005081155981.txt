This single vehicle daytime collision occurred during early to mid-afternoon hours on a Monday. The collision occurred as the vehicle was heading north on a two way, two-lane, undivided roadway. Improved asphalt shoulders line both sides of the roadway. The pre-crash roadway is level, dry, bituminous, residential roadway. Access to the roadway is not controlled. The posted speed limit for the roadway is 56 kmph (35 mph).

Vehicle 1 (V1), a 1995 Honda Civic, was traveling north in lane 1. When V1 drove off the right side of the roadway where the front of the car impacted a mailbox. The vehicle then crossed over a private driveway, and impacted a large drainage ditch where it came to rest facing north. Both airbags deployed as a result of the collision, and the vehicle was towed due to disabling damage. The driver was not injured. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

The pre-event movement for V1 was coded "going straight." The critical pre-crash event was coded "this vehicle traveling, off the edge of the road on the right side." The critical reason for the crash was a driver related factor of Internal Distraction. The driver was looking at a bag of food inside the car trying to retrieve some French fries just prior to the road departure.
