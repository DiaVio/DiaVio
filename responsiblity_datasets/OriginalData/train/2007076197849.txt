This single vehicle crash occurred in a rural recreation area on a 2-lane primitive dirt road with washboard conditions. In the area of the crash, the westbound lane curves left and is downhill. The speed limit is 40 kmph (25 mph). The crash occurred during late afternoon daylight hours with no adverse weather conditions.

Vehicle one (V1), a 2000 Cadillac Escalade Sport Utility Vehicle, was westbound when it began to slide on the dirt road. The driver of V1 steered right then left and departed the right road edge rotating counter-clockwise. The vehicle rolled over to its right and down the embankment where it came to rest on its right side facing east after impacting a large rock at the bottom of the embankment. The deputy-in-charge estimated the speed of V1 was between 56 and 72 kmph (35 - 45 mph). V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

The driver of V1, a twenty one (21) year-old female, stated to police that she just started sliding on the dirt road. She and her three passengers had been camping near a lake and were traveling home. The driver was unable to be contacted.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: this vehicle traveling, off the edge of the road on the right side. The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event was coded as a driver related factor: too fast for conditions (specified as: speed too fast for primitive dirt road with wash board conditions).
