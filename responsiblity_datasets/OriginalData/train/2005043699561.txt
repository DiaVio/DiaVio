The crash occurred in the eastbound lanes of an undivided, four-lane, urban roadway with parallel parking spaces along the eastbound side. The roadway had a posted speed of 35 mph and a level grade. Conditions were daylight and dry with clear skies. The crash occurred in the early afternoon hours on a weekday.

Vehicle one was 2002 Hyundai Accent coupe traveling east in the first lane. A wasp flew in the window and the driver began swatting at the insect. V1 lost control and struck V2 a 1992 Chevy that was parked with its right side mirror. V1 continued forward and struck the rear of V3 a 2000 Ford that was also parked with its front. V3 was pushed forward into V4 a 2001 Mazda which was also parked. All vehicles came to rest off the right side of the roadway in the parking lane. Police Reported Travel Speed is 56 kmph for V1 and 56 kmph for V2.

Vehicle one was driven by a 40-year old female who was transported to a local trauma center with possible injuries. She stated that a wasp had flown in the window, she started swatting, and cannot remember any further events of the crash sequence. She also stated that the EMS personnel thought she had a stroke, but she claims that did not occur. She reports that the wasp did not sting her. Vehicle one was towed due to damage.

The Critical Precrash Event was this vehicle traveling - off the edge of the road on the right side. The Critical Reason for the Critical Pre-crash Event - was a recognition error - internal distraction - wasp inside vehicle. An associated factor for this driver was the possible occurrence of a stroke, as reported on the medical report.
