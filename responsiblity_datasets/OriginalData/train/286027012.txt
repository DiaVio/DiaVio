This case involves a frontal impact event resulting in an extremity injury to a second row right case occupant. Vehicle one (V1 - case vehicle), a 2002 Toyota Camry, 4-door sedan, was traveling south on a three-lane trafficway with paved shoulders. It was daylight hours, the weather was clear and the bituminous road was dry and level. The posted speed limit on the roadway is 80 km/h (50 mph). The trafficway consists of two northbound lanes and one southbound lane seperated by double yellow line. Vehicle two (V2) 2004 Chevrolet 3500, Express Cutaway Van was traveling north in the northbound lane. Vehicle three (V3) 2000 Honda Civic, 2door coupe was traveling behind V2. The crash occurred mid section of the roadway. V1 entered the opposite lane (north bound). The front left corner of V1 impacted the left side plane of V2 in a side swipe manner and V1 continued to move forward into the opposite lane. The frontal plane of V1 then impacted the frontal plane of V3. V1 came to rest facing northeast and V2 came to final rest facing west. The 37-year old female driver of V1 was restrained with the available lap and shoulder belt and sustained very minor injuries. The 15-year old female front passenger of V1 was restrained with the available lap and shoulder belt and sustained very minor injuries. The 7-year old female lying down in the right rear seat with her head behind the driver's seat was restrained with the lap and shoulder belt (shoulder belt was positioned behind the occupant) and sustained serious injuries. She was transported by ground transportation to nearby hospital and later transferred and admitted to the trauma center.
