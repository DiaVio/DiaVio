Vehicle 1 (V1) is a 1998 Pontiac Sunfire, four-door (case study vehicle). Vehicle 2 (V2) is a 1986 Ford Medium/Heavy truck. V1 was occupied by a female, age 26 as its driver (case study participant). The case study participant was wearing the available lap and shoulder belt and the frontal air bags did not deploy.

The crash occurred during the hours of daylight. The weather at the time of the crash was clear. The roadway surface was dry. The posted speed limit is 25 mph (40 kmph).

The crash occurred in an intersection. The roadway consists of one through lane and one right turn lane for the westbound direction. The eastbound direction has one through lane, a dedicated right turn lane and a dedicated left turn lane. The intersecting roadway is one lane in each direction. The intersection is controlled by a stop sign. The roadway is level in the area of the crash.

V1 was traveling southbound and was attempting to make a left turn. V2 was traveling westbound in the through lane. V1 turned into the path of V2. The front of V2 struck the left side of V1. The impact caused V1 to rotate counterclockwise coming to rest in the eastbound right turn lane. V2 came to rest in the intersection.

The case study participant was transported by air to a trauma center.
