This two vehicle crash occurred shortly past noon during daylight hours. The collision occurred at a four-leg intersection. The westbound and eastbound roadway is seven lanes, dry, level and has a left turn pocket. The southbound and northbound roadway is level, dry, and has two lanes. The intersection is controlled by a tri-color traffic signal that was working properly at the time. All four legs are comprised of asphalt. Both roadways are designated 56 kmph (35 mph).

Vehicle one (V1), a 2003 Mazda Protege sedan driven by a 18 year-old female, was traveling east in the number four left turn lane. Vehicle two (V2), a 2004 Chevy Impala sedan driven by a 40 year-old male, was traveling west in the number three lane. V1 was making a left turn at the intersection and the front plane contacted the front plane of V2. V1 rotated counterclockwise and headed in a southwesterly direction coming to final rest in the east bound lanes facing southwest. V2 continued northwest and was driven to the north curb. The driver of V1 said she was thinking of her high school graduation party that was planned for the upcoming weekend. She said she saw the other vehicle but thought that it would stop. Both vehicles were towed from the scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: this vehicle traveling, turning left at intersection. The critical reason for the critical event was coded to V1 as a driver related factor: false assumptions of other's actions. Inattention was an associative factor for this driver.

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded: other vehicle encroachment; from opposite direction, over left lane line. The critical reason was not coded to this vehicle.
