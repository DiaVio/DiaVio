This crash occurred on a two-way, two-lane, undivided roadway. The north/south roadway was bituminous, straight, and level with a posted speed of 48 kmph (30 mph). Conditions were daylight on a weekend morning with dry roads and clear skies. The traffic flow was normal.

V1, a 1993 Ford Escort with one occupant, was southbound in lane one. V1 drifted over the lane line into lane two (the northbound lane), before departing the left side of the street. The front of V1 made contact with a tree that was greater than ten centimeters in diameter. The back left corner of V1 then made contact with a wooden fence, coming to final rest on the sidewalk near the damaged tree. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 40 kmph for V1.

The driver of V1, a 46-year-old female, was transported to the hospital with minor injuries. She stated that she was on her way to work. The driver could not say with complete certainty what she was doing just before the crash. However, she did indicate that she was hearing a loud, strange noise coming from her vehicle. The motorist said she thought she might have been looking inside her vehicle for the source of the noise, drifting off the roadway in the process.

The Critical Precrash Event for V1 was coded this vehicle traveling, off the edge of the road on the left side. The Critical Reason for the Critical Event was a driver-related recognition error, an internal distraction. It is believed that the driver was distracted by a noise coming from inside the vehicle. The driver was under the influence of drugs at the time of the crash. She was using a combination of prescription painkillers, sedatives, and cocaine. Also coded was the fact that the driver suffered from rheumatoid arthritis, curvature of the spine, and bulging discs. The tires of the vehicle were also coded as an associated factor, as the tread depth was dangerously low.
