This single vehicle crash occurred on a divided freeway at night with no street lights in the area. Case vehicle one (V1), a 2001 Acura Integra four door sedan, was southbound in lane one of two, one way lanes of this freeway that had guard rails on both sides. V1 drifted to the right on the west shoulder and swiped right side of V1 down the guard rail. V1 then moved left and crossed over both lanes on the east shoulder and then contacted the front left corner into the east side guard rail. V1 was deflected clockwise and the left side back and left rear tire impacted the guard rail. V1 continued to roll forward and came to rest facing south on the east shoulder. V1 was damaged and towed. The driver of V1 is the case occupant with no other passengers in V1. The case driver was 40 year old female who was wearing a lap and shoulder belt and the frontal steering column mounted air bag deployed on the second impact into the east side guard rail. This driver had serious injuries and was taken to a local hospital and then later transferred and hospitalized at the trauma center.
