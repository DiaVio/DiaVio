This single-vehicle crash occurred during the morning dawn hours on a cloudy morning. The roadway is level, straight, two way, two-lane, undivided, bituminous that was dry at the time of the crash. The speed limit is 64 kmph (40 mph).

Vehicle one (V1), a 1992 Jeep Cherokee, was westbound with one occupant. The driver was traveling home to go to sleep after working through the night. The driver had to drive two and a half hours with only a two-hour sleep break in the last 24 hours. The driver started having chest pains, clutched his chest with his right hand and closed his eyes. When he opened them he had departed the right road edge. The front of V1 struck a 6"X 6" timber post with a mailbox mounted to it. This impact rotated the vehicle clockwise and the vehicle tripped and rolled left 8-quarter turns and came to final rest down an embankment facing east. V1 was towed due to disabling damage. The driver was transported to a local trauma center with incapacitating injuries. Police Reported Travel Speed is 64 kmph for V1.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: this vehicle traveling, off the edge of the road on the right side. The critical reason for the critical event was coded as a driver related critical non-performance factor: heart attack or other physical impairment of the ability to act.
