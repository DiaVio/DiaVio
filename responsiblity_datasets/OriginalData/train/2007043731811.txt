Case Summary-2007-43-203

This single-vehicle crash occurred in the morning of a weekday on a 2-lane undivided roadway. The bituminous road was flat, straight and wet after a recent rainstorm. The posted speed limit on the road is 72 kmph (45 mph). Police Reported Travel Speed is 72 kmph for V1.

V1, a 1996 Toyota Corolla with one occupant, was southbound on this road when it began to hydroplane on the wet road and departed the right side of the road. V1 struck three small trees with its front and then a big tree (90 cm in diameter) with its left side. V1 came to final rest against the big tree facing southwest. The car was towed due to damage and the driver was examined on scene by EMS.

The driver of V1 is a 17-year-old female that was not interviewed for this case. Her mother told me that her daughter lost control of the vehicle on the wet surface and that she tried to steer left, steer right, and brake but she could not regain control of the car.

The Critical Precrash Event for this vehicle was coded this vehicle traveling off the edge of the road on the right side. The Critical Reason for the Critical Event was coded as traveling too fast for conditions.
