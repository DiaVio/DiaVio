This case involves a moderate to severe small-overlap frontal impact with a large tree. The case occupant is the 60-year-old female center-rear passenger. She was restrained by a three-point belt, and no air bags were available to her. The case vehicle a 2007 four-door Kia Optima was southbound on a dry, level, asphalt, two lane road, negotiating a right sweeping curve while approaching a three-leg intersection with a raised median for north-south traffic. It was dark but lighted and foggy. The driver lost control of the case vehicle, traveled through the intersection departed the road at its end and the front-left corner struck a large tree. The case vehicle rotated counterclockwise, crossed a private drive and its right fender and the axle struck another large tree. The vehicle came to rest in the private drive facing south-southeast. It was towed due to damage. The case occupant is a 60-year-old female center-rear passenger. She was restrained by a three-point belt and no airbags were available. She sustained serious injuries and was transported to a level-one trauma center. The 28-year-old female driver, the 27-year-old male, right-front passenger, the 5-year-old female left-rear passenger and the 8-year-old female right-rear passenger all sustained police reported non-incapacitating injuries and transported to a medical facility.
