This two-vehicle crash occurred on a straight, undivided roadway. This roadway consists of one lane in each direction and a parking lane on the east side of the roadway. The southbound lane has a six percent uphill grade and the northbound lane has a seven percent downhill grade. The speed limit on this roadway is 48 kmph (30 mph). It was dark with streetlights on at the time of the crash and there were no adverse atmospheric conditions.

Vehicle one (V1), a 2001 Subaru Forester, was southbound in lane one. Vehicle two (V2), a 1995 Dodge Caravan, was northbound in lane one. V1 entered the northbound lane in an attempt to make a U-turn. The front of V2 struck the right side of V1. V2 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The driver of V1, an eighteen-year-old female, was traveling with two female teenagers. This driver was not interviewed; no further information is available for this driver.

The driver of V2, a fifty-nine-year-old female, was traveling from a library to the post office. She was healthy, well rested and familiar with her vehicle She rarely travels this route. This driver stated that V1 made the turn abruptly and there was no time to attempt any avoidance maneuvers.

The pre-event movement for V1 was coded as "going straight." The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "this vehicle traveling; over the lane line on left side of travel lane." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as " driver related factor; unknown decision error."

The pre-event movement for V2 was coded as "going straight." The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded as "other vehicle encroachment; from opposite direction - over left lane line." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V2 was "not coded to this vehicle."
