This case involved the 26 year old unrestrained male driver of a 2006 Toyota 4Runner four door sport utility vehicle (V1). The front steering wheel mounted airbag depolyed in the crash. The vehicle was not equipped with side impact airbags. The crash occurred at night on an unlit area of a divided interstate highway. The road ran north and south with a posted speed limit of 113 kph (70 mph). It was raining at the time of the crash and the concrete roadway was wet. V1 was traveling north in the center of the three travel lanes with a 2% uphill grade. V1 hydroplaned on the wet road and traveled across the left lane and departed the west side of the roadway. V1 continued through the grass median and behind the guardrail on the northbound traffic side. The southbound lanes were also protected by a guardrail. V1drove through the median and struck a sign support pole in the center of the median, coming to rest between the two guardrails. V1 was towed from the scene due to disabling damage. The driver of V1 was transported by ground ambulance to a level one trauma center and hospitalized due to injuries sustained in the crash.
