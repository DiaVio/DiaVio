This two vehicle crash occurred mid-afternoon during daylight hours on a north/south roadway. The road is a two way, two lane, undivided asphalt roadway with parking lanes on both sides of the roadway. There were no adverse weather conditions. The speed limit on the road is 50 kmph (30 mph).

Vehicle one (V1), a 1993 Buick Regal Sedan driven by a 64 year-old female, was traveling in the southbound lane of the road. The driver drove into and struck the back of Vehicle two (V2) while it was legally parked. V2 was a Ford F-250 4X2 pickup truck with no occupants. V1 was towed from the scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The driver of V1 had been driving for approximately seven years. She claimed that a non-contact vehicle was approaching from the rear at a rapid speed and trying to pass on her left side. The driver stated that she looked into her driver side mirror and pulled over into the parking lane to let this non-contact vehicle pass. She was familiar with the road and the area, but she had only owned the vehicle for three months. She was coming from one job and heading to another, both occupations are in the housekeeping field. The driver was rested with no shift in sleeping pattern. She also stated that she was in good health, with no deficiencies in hearing or vision.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: this vehicle traveling, over the lane line on right side of travel lane. The critical reason for the critical event was coded to V1 as a driver related factor: external distraction. Poor directional control was coded as an associated factor.
