The single vehicle crash occurred on an divided, north/south trafficway. There are four travel lanes for southbound traffic. The roadway was straight, dry and of concrete construction with a downhill grade. The posted speed limit is 56 kmph (35 mph). Traffic was light at the time of the crash. This crash occurred before sunrise with clear skies.

Vehicle one (V1), a 2000 Kia Sportage with one occupant, was southbound in the fourth travel lane. V1 departed the roadway on the left side and the vehicle entered the curbed paved median. The front of V1 struck a non-breakaway light pole in the median. V1 began to rotate counterclockwise and rolled over onto the right side and top. While skidding on its top, the left side of V1 struck another light pole in the median. V1 came to rest on its left side in the southbound lanes facing south. The vehicle was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

The driver of V1, a twenty-seven year old male, was heading home from work. This driver was unsure of how long he had been driving, drives the route daily and is familiar with the vehicle. The driver stated that he was tired and fell asleep. The driver did not recall anything until after the vehicle was rolling over. The driver stated he works two jobs and was drowsy at the beginning of the trip. He also stated he gets about 3 hours of sleep a night. He has been driving for 16 months.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: this vehicle traveling-off the edge of the road on the left side. The critical reason for V1 was coded: driver related factor-sleeping, that is, actually asleep.
