This two-vehicle collision occurred in the late afternoon during daylight hours. The collision occurred on a busy roadway. The roadway has five lanes, and the posted speed limit is 72 kmph (45 mph). The pre-crash roadway is straight and level, with bituminous paving. It was overcast, and the roadway was wet from a previous rain. Access to the roadway is not controlled.

Vehicle one (V1), a 1996 Ford Explorer 4-door SUV driven by a fifty eight (58) year old male, was traveling westbound in lane two. Vehicle two (V2), a 1993 Mercury Sable 4-door sedan driven by a twenty three (23) year old female with one passenger was eastbound in lane two. The driver of V1 blacked out while driving, crossed the center turn lane to the left, and impacted the left side of V2 near the rear with V1's front plane. V1 then continued on, jumping the curb, impacting a fence, and traveling down a ravine striking another fence. V1 came to final rest facing southwest in the ravine and was towed from the scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The driver of V2 was not reached for interview so most of her pre-crash factors are unknown.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: this vehicle traveling, over the lane line on the left side of travel lane. The critical reason for the critical event was assigned to this driver as a driver related factor: heart attack or other physical impairment of the ability to act. The driver was listening to the radio while driving and blacked out for unknown reasons. He takes the following medications: aspirin, vitamins, Lovastatin and Niaspan.

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded: other vehicle encroachment; from opposite direction, over left lane line. The critical reason for the critical event was not coded to this vehicle.
