The crash occurred on the south roadside of a residential roadway. The roadway was unmarked but wide enough for two-way traffic. It was set on a downhill grade of 8% with a 56 kmph (35 mph) speed limit. Conditions were daylight and dry with cloudy skies. The setting was early afternoon hours on a weekday.

Vehicle one was a 1999 Chevrolet Tracker compact utility traveling east in lane one. V1 traveled off the right side of the road, across a driveway, and into a mailbox. V1 then rolled left three quarter turns, coming to rest partially in lane one east of impact. Police Reported Travel Speed is 48 kmph for V1.

The Chevrolet Tracker (V1) was driven by a 33-year old male who was uninjured. He stated that he was heading home from his cousin's house. He looked down in the passenger floorboard for an item. The next thing he knew he had hit the curb and the mailbox, causing him to rollover. He estimated his travel and impact speeds to be 32 kmph (20 mph). The Chevrolet compact utility was towed due to damage.

The Critical Precrash Event for V1 was this vehicle traveling off the edge of the road on the right side. The Critical Reason was a driver related factor of internal distraction. This vehicle had a tire deficiency of inadequate tread depth on the left front tire. This driver had not driven this vehicle since two years prior.
