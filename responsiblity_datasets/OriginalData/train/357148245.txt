This case involves a small over-lap impact between the front of a passenger and a tree. The 81-year-old male driver is the case occupant. The case vehicle, a four-door, 2008 Chevrolet Impala was southbound on a dry, level, two-lane asphalt road. The case vehicle crossed the centerline and the northbound lane and departed the roadway at the intersection of a private drive, impacted a private lawn with the under-carriage and struck a tree with the right side of the front end. It was daylight and clear. After impact the case vehicle rotated clockwise off the tree and came to rest facing west. The 81-year-old male driver was the only occupant of the case vehicle. He was restrained by a three-point belt, but the steering-wheel air bag did not deploy. The case occupant was transported to an outside facility and later trnasferred a level-one trauma center with serious injuries and enrolled as a case occupant.
