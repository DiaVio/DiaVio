The crash occurred in the parking lane of a one-way roadway with one lane of travel. This was a straight, level, bituminous roadway with a speed limit of 48kmph (30mph). It was the afternoon hours of a weekday, and the road was dry and the sky was clear.

V1, a 2007 Ford E-350 cargo van with one occupant, was traveling northbound in lane one. V2, a 1995 Toyota Corolla four door, was parked and unoccupied in the eastern parking lane. V3, a 1996 Chevrolet Lumina four door, was parked and unoccupied in front of V2. V4, a 2006 Lexus RX330 sport utility vehicle, was parked and unoccupied in front of V3. The right side of V1 sideswiped the left side of V2, and V3 and then the front of V1 impacted the back of V4. V1 came to a final rest after impacting V4. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

V1, the Ford E-350, was driven by a 29 year-old male who reported no injuries. The driver advised that he was traveling and making deliveries to stores when a non-contact vehicle attempted to pass on the left side. He stated that he thought the other driver was going to contact him when he moved over to the right side of the lane, he felt confused and lost control of the vehicle.

The Critical Pre-crash Event for V1 was "This vehicle traveling, off the edge of the road on the right side". The Critical Reason for the Critical event was a Driver related factor, "external distraction". An Associated factor coded to the vehicle was an exterior source rearward in which a non-contact vehicle attempting to pass on his left. The driver tracked the other vehicle with his side view mirror while moving over to the right to allow him to pass. An Inadequate surveillance, failed to look far enough ahead was also coded to the driver.
