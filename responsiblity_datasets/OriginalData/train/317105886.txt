This case involved the 60 year old female driver of a 2011 Jeep Compass four door sport utility vehicle (V1). The front of V1 impacted the back of a 2005 GMC cutaway van (V2). The driver of V1 was restrained by the available three point lap and shoulder belt and had a deployed frontal airbag. V1 was also equipped with left and right roof side rail mounted head curtain airbags that did not deploy. The crash occurred during daylight hours on straight interstate highway with a 1% downhill grade. It was cloudy and the concrete roadway was dry. The posted speed limit was 97 kph (60 mph). V1and V2 were traveling in the left (inside) lane of the four northbound lanes. V2 had decelerated to approximately 16 kph (10 mph) due to a slowdown in traffic. V1 was traveling behind V2 going 119 kph (74 mph) when it collided with the back of V2. The impact propelled both vehicles forward and they came to rest still in the left lane of traffic. V1 was towed from the scene due to disabling damage. V2 was driven from the scene of the crash. The driver of V1 was transported by fire rescue to a level one trauma center for treatment of injuries sustained in the crash.
