The crash occurred in the signalized intersection of two roadways. The north/south roadway is divided with three lanes in each direction. The roadway was bituminous with a slight curve and had an uphill grade of 2/61. The speed limit was 48kmph (30 mph), and it was dawn on a weekday; the road was dry and it was clear with no adverse conditions.

V1, a 1995 Ford Aspire two door, with one occupant was traveling westbound in lane two. V2, a 2003 Mitsubishi Galant four door, with one occupant was traveling eastbound in lane three. At the intersection, V2 attempted to make a left turn at the intersection when the front of V1 impacted the right side of V2. Both vehicles came to a final rest in the intersection. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

V1, the Ford Aspire was driven by a 40 year-old female who reported incapacitating injuries and was transported to a medical facility. She stated to the Police that she was going through the intersection when V2 made a left-turn in front of her and struck her vehicle. The driver declined to participate in the survey.

The Critical Pre-crash event for V1 was "Other vehicle encroachment, from opposite direction - over left lane line". The Critical Reason for the Critical event was not coded to this vehicle. The possible associated factors were coded unknown because no interview was obtained.

V2, the Mitsubishi Galant was driven by a 66 year-old male who reported incapacitating injuries and was transported to a medical facility. The driver stated to the Police that he was making a left turn at the intersection when he struck V1.

The Critical Pre-crash event for V2 was "This vehicle traveling, turning left at intersection". The Critical Reason for the Critical event was coded as an unknown reason. The medical records show that he was taking medication for diabetes, high blood pressure and angina. The possible associated factors were coded unknown because no interview was obtained.
