This two-vehicle crash occurred just past noon during daylight hours along a 4-lane, two-way, non-divided, bituminous roadway with no traffic controls and a posted speed limit of 48 kmph (30 MPH). The road was wet, and there were no adverse atmospheric conditions.

Vehicle one (V1), a 1999 Chevrolet Malibu sedan driven by a fifty four (54) year old male with one passenger, was traveling eastbound in lane one. Vehicle two (V2), a 2000 Chevrolet Suburban SUV driven by a fifty (50) year old male with two passengers, was traveling east in the number two lane. V1 intending to turn right, and V2 going straight. As V1 started to make the right hand turn, witnesses confirm that V1 encroached into lane 2. The front of V2 struck the back of V1, sending V1 into a clockwise yaw. The left front tire of V1 struck the curb of the cross street, and V1 came to rest facing south on the north side of the cross street. V2 driver was able to bring V2 to a stop in eastbound lane 1, facing east past the intersection. V2 was able to drive away from the scene, V2 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: this vehicle traveling over the lane line on the left side of travel lane. The critical reason for the critical event was coded as a driver related factor: poor directional control (e.g., failing to control vehicle with skill ordinarily expected).

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded: other vehicle encroachment; from adjacent lane (same direction) - over right lane line. The critical reason was not coded to this vehicle.
