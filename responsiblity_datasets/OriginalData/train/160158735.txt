The focus of this case is on a 59-year old, female driver (case occupant), of a 2006 Toyota Corolla (V1), involved in a head on collision with a 2006 Ford F350 Super Duty Pickup Truck (V2). This two vehicle collision occurred during the evening hours on a straight level section of a two lane undivided roadway. This bituminous roadway runs north and south. The weather was cloudy and the roadway surface was dry. The speed limit for the east/west roadway is 72 km/h (45 mph). The 59-year old female driver (case occupant) was operating V1 in the northbound travel lane. She was wearing the available 3-point lap/shoulder belt. The driver frontal air bag deployed in this event. V2 was operating in the southbound travel lane and, for unknown reasons, V2 crossed over the center line and impacted V1 in an offset frontal crash configuration. As a result of the collision, V1 translated backwards and rotated counterclockwise approximately 20 degrees, coming to final rest on the gravel shoulder of the northbound travel lane pointed in a north westerly direction. V2 continued moving forward and to the right - coming to rest at the right edge of the southbound travel lane pointed in a southwesterly direction. The driver of V1 (case occupant) was removed from the vehicle by EMS and transported, by air rescue, to a trauma center and hospitalized with serious injuries. V1 was towed due to damage sustained in the crash. The driver V2 was transported to a local medical facility. The extent of his injuries and/or treatments is unknown. Both vehicles were towed due to damage sustained in the crash.
