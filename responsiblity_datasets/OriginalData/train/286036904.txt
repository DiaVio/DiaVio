This case involves a moderate frontal impact resulting in extremity injuries to the right front case occupant. Vehicle one (V1 - case vehicle), a 2005 Nissan Altima 4-door sedan, was travelling north in the northbound lane of a two-lane two-way roadway approaching a T-type intersection. Vehicle 2 (V2), a 1978 Ford F250 pickup was travelling west on the intersecting roadway. At the time of the crash, it was daylight, and the weather was cloudy, though it was not raining. The bituminous (asphalt) roadway was wet. The posted speed limit for the north-south portion of the roadway was 80 km/h (50 mph). A stop sign regulates the traffic for the west leg of the intersection. The crash occurred when V2 began a left turn at the T-intersection, intending to travel south. V1 was travelling straight through the intersection when V2 pulled directly into its path. The front of V1 impacted the front left corner of V2. Post-impact, V2 rotated clockwise approximately 120 degrees. Both V1 and V2 departed the intersection at the northeast corner and came to rest final rest facing northeast. Both vehicles were towed due to disabling damage. The 29 year old male right front passenger (case occupant) was not wearing his available lap and shoulder belt. The front instrument panel-mounted airbag did deploy for his seating position. The case occupant was transported from the scene to a local trauma center where he was treated for right arm and left ankle fractures. Also in the case vehicle was the 23 year old male driver. He was not restrained by his available lap and shoulder belt, however his frontal airbag deployed. According to the police report, he sustained no injuries. The driver of V2 was a 36 year old male who was restrained via lap and shoulder belt. According to the police report, he sustained non-incapacitating injuries, including a cut on the left side of his forehead and back pain. There were no other occupants in V2.
