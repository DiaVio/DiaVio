This case involves a small-overlap impact between the right side of a passenger car and a light pole. The 39-year-old male driver and a 40-year-old female right-front passenger are the case occupants. The case vehicle, a 2006 two-door Lotus Elise hard-top roadster travelling in northbound lane of a wet, concrete three-lane roadway approaching a drawbridge with a metal lifting-deck. As the case vehicle crossed the metal deck the vehicle entered a clockwise yaw crossed into the east bike-lane, mounted a barrier curb and struck a light pole at the front-right bumper corner. After impact, the vehicle rotated clockwise off the pole and came to rest facing east straddling the northbound traffic and bike lanes. The 39-year-old male driver and the 40-year-old female right-front passenger are both case occupants. They were the only occupants of the case vehicle. Neither occupant was restrained by a three-point belt, but the steering-wheel and mid-instrument-panel air bags deployed. The driver sustained multiple moderate injuries and the right-front passenger multiple serious injuries. Both occupants were transported to a level one trauma-center and enrolled as a case occupant.
