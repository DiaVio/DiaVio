This crash occurred on a curved southbound exit ramp with a speed limit of 56 kmph (35 mph) during the late afternoon hours of a weekday when the conditions were dark with cloudy skies. V1, a 1990 Mazda pickup was exiting off the expressway with excessive speed. V1 began skidding as its driver attempted to maintain control. V1 skidded off the road to the right rolling over on to its roof before coming to final rest in the culvert alongside the road.

V1 was driven by a 42 year-old male who refused to be interviewed. He sustained incapacitating injuries and was transported from the scene to a local trauma center. Police officers on scene stated that he was going to fast and the PAR stated that the driver was intoxicated. Police Reported Travel Speed is Unknown for V1.

The Critical PreCrash Event was this vehicle traveling, off the edge of the road right side. The Critical Reason was a decision error, traveling too fast for curve/turn. An associated factor was the fact this driver was intoxicated which contributed caused performance errors and could be the reason for his excessive speed.
