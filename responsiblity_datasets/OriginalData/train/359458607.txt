The case occupant is a 39 year old male front right passenger, not wearing the available manual lap/shoulder belt, who was involved in a severe frontal collision with a frontal air bag deployment, and who sustained serious injuries. This single vehicle crash occurred in darkness, under cloudy weather and dry roadway conditions. Vehicle one (V1), a 2004 Mazda RX-8 four door sedan, was northbound in lane one of a two lane, two way roadway. The asphalt travel lanes are straight, have no curbs, with trees present on both sides of the roadway. As V1 was traveling north, the driver drifted out of the lane and departed the roadway to the right. V1 traveled approximately 25 meters north and then the front of V1 impacted a small and large tree that were right next to one another, resulting in the deployment of both frontal air bags. V1 rotated clockwise and came to final rest with backend on the roadway, facing northeast. V1 was towed due to damage. The case occupant is the front right passenger of V1, a 39 year old male, who was not wearing the available manual lap/shoulder belt. His top instrument panel air bag deployed. This passenger sustained serious injuries and was transported to the trauma center for treatment. The driver is a 28 year old female, who was also not wearing the available manual lap/shoulder belt. Her steering column mounted front air bag deployed during the crash. She sustained possible injuries and was transported to an area hospital for treatment.
