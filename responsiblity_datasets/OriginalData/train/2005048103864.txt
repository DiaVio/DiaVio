This single vehicle crash occurred at on a four-lane, uphill, dry, divided bituminous roadway that curves to the right. There are two lanes of travel in either direction. The southbound lanes are raised above the northbound lanes. The posted speed limit for this roadway was 89 kmph (55 mph). There were no adverse atmospheric conditions present during this daytime crash.

Vehicle one (V1), a 1997 Mitsubishi Mirage was traveling southbound in lane two. V1 departed the roadway to the left. As V1 descended the median, its left front wheel dug in and tripped the vehicle. V1 rolled 4 quarter turns towards its left side upon its longitudinal axis. V1 came to rest facing east in lane two of the northbound roadway. V1 was towed due to disabling damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 97 kmph for V1.

The driver of V1, a male of unknown age, was driving home from work. This driver stated that a vehicle cut him off the roadway and that is when he crashed. This driver also indicated that as he was driving, he was opening an audio compact disc out of its plastic wrap. This driver indicated that he usually gets a good amount of sleep but feels fatigued when he awakes. This driver drives this vehicle daily and over this road daily.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: this vehicle traveling, off the edge of the road on the left side. The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as a driver related factor: internal distraction.
