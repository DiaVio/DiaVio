This is multiple event crash involving near simultaneous non-horizontal impacts to the undercarriage and front of the case vehicle with a resulting intra-unit back plane impact with a towed trailer. The driver (case occupant) sustained serious injuries. The case vehicle a 1999 Dodge Durango SUV 4x4 was towing a trailer. It was eastbound on a snowy asphalt roadway with a positive grade. It was dark and there was no adverse weather. The vehicle drifted off the south edge of the road, went down the embankment of a drainage ditch and struck a steeply graded culvert with its undercarriage and front end. On impact, the trailer it was towing became unhitched. The case vehicle went up the embankment of the culvert, traversed a private drive and came to rest, facing east on the north embankment of the drainage ditch on the opposite side of the driveway. The 39-year-old male driver was restrained by a three-point belt and the steering-wheel air bag deployed and was the sole occupant of the case vehicle. The driver sustained police reported 'B' injuries. The case occupant sustained serious injuries and was taken to an outside hospital before being transferred to a level-one trauma center.
