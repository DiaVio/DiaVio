This crash occurred off the left side of an undivided straight two-lane asphalt roadway with a posted speed limit of 89 kmph (55 mph). The crash occurred during the afternoon hours while daylight. It was snowing and the roadway was icy at the time of the crash.

V1, a 2002 Ford Taurus 4-door sedan, was traveling westbound when the vehicle began to rotate counterclockwise due to the icy road conditions. V1 departed the lane to the left and then departed the roadway to the left, where its front struck a large tree and then V1 rolled one-quarter turn to the right. V1 came to final rest on its right side. V1 was towed from the scene due to moderate damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

The driver of V1, a 63-year-old female, stated she was on her way home from her daughter's house. She stated she applied the brakes when the vehicle began to spin and felt the ABS engage. She continued to rotate until she departed the roadway, struck the tree, and began to roll over.

The Critical Pre-Crash Event coded for V1 was "This vehicle control loss due to: Poor road conditions." The Critical Reason for the Critical Event was coded "Environment Related Factor; Slick roads." Associated factors coded to the driver of V1 include traveling too fast for conditions and the snowy weather.
