This single-vehicle crash occurred on a median-divided, straight trafficway. The southbound lanes consist of two through lanes and have an uphill grade in the precrash area. The speed limit for this roadway is 105 kmph (65 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.

Vehicle one (V1), a 2006 Dodge Stratus, was southbound in lane one. V1 traveled off the right side of the road. V1 then returned to the roadway and began rotating counterclockwise. V1 traveled into the median and rolled over to the right two quarter-turns down a steep grass embankment. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 105 kmph for V1.

The driver of V1, a thirty-six-year-old female, was traveling from an appointment with an attorney to work. She was well rested and travels this route daily. V1 is a rental vehicle and this is the first time that this driver has driven it. This driver has been diagnosed with a seizure disorder and takes the medications Clonidine, Clonazepam, Norvasc, Keppra and Lortab. She did not provide details of the crash.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "this vehicle traveling; off the edge of the road on the right side." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "driver related factor; heart attack or other physical impairment of the ability to act."
