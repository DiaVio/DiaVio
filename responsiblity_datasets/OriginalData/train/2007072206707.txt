This single-vehicle crash occurred on straight, level, undivided roadway near an intersection. This roadway consists of two lanes in each direction, left turn lanes at the intersection and parking lanes on both sides. The speed limit for this roadway is 48 kmph (30 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.

Vehicle one (V1), a 2004 Nissan Armada, was southbound in lane one. V1 traveled off the right side of the road and the front struck a fire hydrant and a utility pole. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

The driver of V1, a thirty-seven-year-old female, was on her way home from work. She was well rested and familiar with her vehicle. She travels this route daily. This driver reported that she "blacked out" and later stated that she drove off the roadway to avoid a pedestrian. According to her medical record, this driver had an epileptic seizure. She takes the medication Dilantin.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "this vehicle traveling; off the edge of the road on the right side." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "driver related factor; heart attack or other physical impairment of the ability to act."
