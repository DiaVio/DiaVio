This head-on collision involves a 43 year-old male restrained driver with deployed airbags who sustained serious injuries and a 39 year-old female passenger with deployed airbags who sustained serious injuries. The crash occurred during the daylight hours in dry and clear weather. The collision occurred on an six lane trafficway that travels north and south. Two of the four southbound lanes on the north side of the intersection are straight through lanes and one is a dedicated left turn lane and one a dedicated right turn lane. Two of the four northbound lanes on the south side of the intersection are dedicated straight through lanes and one is a dedicated right turn lane and the other a dedicated left turn lane. The north and southbound lanes are separated by a raised grass median. The case vehicle (V1), 2009 Toyota Corolla, was traveling north and attempting a left turn from the dedicated left turn lane. The crash occurred when V1 turned into the path of V2, which had been traveling south in the left through lane. V1 was towed due to damage. V1 was driven by a 43 year-old male case study occupant. He was properly wearing a three point lap and shoulder belt and had front and side rail airbags deployed. The case vehicle also contained a 39 year-old female case study occupant. She was seated in the right front seat, was properly wearing a three point lap and shoulder belt and had the front and side rail airbags deploy. Both case study occupants were seriously injured and transported to a level-one trauma center for treatment.
