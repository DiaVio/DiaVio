This case involved a 23 year old female driver of a 2005 Ford Focus four door sedan (V1). There were no other occupants in the vehicle. The driver was not utilizing the available three point manual safety belt, but did have a deployed frontal airbag. The vehicle was not equipped with side impact airbags. The crash occurred at dawn on a two lane residential street. The dry asphalt roadway was straight with a 2% downhill grade. There were no adverse weather conditions at the time of the crash. The posted speed limit was 56 kph (35 mph) V1 was traveling north through a four-leg intersection when it drifted across the broken yellow center line into the southbound lane. V1, who was traveling at approximately 60 kph (40 mph), continued veering left across the southbound lane to the west side of the roadway. V1 sideswiped a utility pole with its left front quarter panel and then struck another utility pole with its frontal plane. V1 rotated approximately 45 degrees counterclockwise and came to rest partially off the roadway. V1 was towed from the scene due to disabling damage. The driver of V1 was transported by private vehicle to a local hospital and later transferred to a level one trauma center where she was hospitalized for treatment of her injuries.
