This two-vehicle crash occurred in a four-legged, traffic signal-controlled intersection. The east/west roadway is straight, undivided and consists of two lanes in each direction with a left turn lane at the intersection. Both approach roadways have an uphill grade. The speed limit on this roadway is 56 kmph (35 mph). Traffic was heavy at the time of the crash due to rush hour congestion. It was dark with streetlights on at the time of the crash and there were no adverse atmospheric conditions.

Vehicle one (V1), a 1990 Buick LeSabre, was stopped in westbound lane three, waiting to turn left (south) at the intersection. Vehicle two (V2), a 2006 Chevrolet Monte Carlo, was eastbound in lane two. V1 started to make the turn and the front struck the front of V2. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The driver of V1, a thirty-year-old male, was traveling from his accountant's office to his home. He was healthy, well rested and familiar with his vehicle. He rarely travels this route. This driver stated that he thought that V2 would stop to allow him to make his turn.

The driver of V2, a twenty-seven-year-old female, was traveling from work to her home. She was in fairly good health, well rested and comfortable with her vehicle. She travels this route daily. This driver has a broken jaw and takes the medications Flonase, Albuterol, Ortho-Cyclen, Claritin, vitamins and an unknown anxiety medication.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "this vehicle traveling; turning left at intersection." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "driver related factor; false assumption of other's actions."

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded as "other vehicle encroachment; from opposite direction - over left lane line." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V2 was "not coded to this vehicle."
