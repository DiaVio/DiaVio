This case involved an 18 year old restrained female driver of a 2008 Nissan Sentra four door sedan (V1). The driver was utilizing the available three point safety belt system and had a deployed frontal airbag. The left seat mounted airbag and left head curtain airbag also deployed in the crash. There were no other occupants in V1. The other vehicle involved was a 1999 Chevrolet 1500 regular cab pickup truck (V2). The crash occurred during daylight hours on a two lane dry asphalt roadway. There were no adverse weather conditions at the time of the crash and the posted speed limit was 89 kph (55 mph). V1 was traveling north at a reported speed of 113 kph (70 mph) negotiating a left hand curve with a downhill grade when it traveled across the center line into the southbound lane. V2 was traveling south at an estimated speed of 80 kph (50 mph). V1 and V2 collided in the southbound lane in an offset frontal impact. After impact, V1 rotated counterclockwise approximately 90 degrees and impacted a guardrail with its rear bumper. V1 came to rest partially on top of the guardrail. V2 also rotated counterclockwise and came to rest in the southbound lane facing northeast. Both vehicles were towed from the scene due to disabling damage. The driver of V1 was airlifted to a level one trauma center and hospitalized for treatment of her injuries.
