This crash occurred during daylight hours with clear, dry conditions on a two lane, two way roadway that had a posted speed limit of 35 mph/56 kmph. Vehicle one (V1), a 1997 Chrysler Sebring convertible was eastbound and drifted across the opposite lane then off road to the left into a ditch. V1 then impacted a fence post that split and flung upward and penetrated into the windshield into the driver position. V1 continued to strike two more fence posts with the fence swiping down the left side. V1 finally impacted a large tree with the front left corner before coming to rest in the ditch. The case occupant was the driver, a 61 year old female, who was wearing the manual lap/shoulder belt and none of the airbags deployed. The case occupant was airlifted to the trauma center. The vehicle was towed due to damage.
