The crash occurred off the right curve of a two-lane, rural roadway. The roadway had a posted speed limit of 88 kmph (55 mph). The westbound approach was on a 5% uphill grade. Conditions were daylight and wet with cloudy skies. The setting was late afternoon hours on a weekday.

Vehicle one was a 1992 Geo Prizm sedan traveling westbound negotiating the right curve. V1 traveled across the centerline and off the left side of the roadway. After traversing a steep embankment, V1 rolled over one half turn onto its top. V1 came to rest in the left roadside on its top. Police Reported Travel Speed is 80 kmph for V1.

The Geo Prizm was driven by an unknown driver who fled the scene of the crash. Her passengers were not able to be located for interview. The investigating officer estimated the travel and impact speeds of V1 to be 80 kmph (50 mph). The sedan was towed due to damage.

The Critical Precrash Event for V1 was this vehicle control loss due to traveling too fast for conditions. The Critical Reason was a driver related factor of traveling too fast for curve. The conditions included wet roads, low-treaded tires, and a moderately sharp curve.
