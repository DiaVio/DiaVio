This two-vehicle crash occurred in a four-legged, traffic signal-controlled intersection. The north/south roadway is undivided, straight and there are two lanes in each direction. The northbound lanes have a 2.5% uphill grade. The east/west roadway is level, straight and divided by a curbed median. The westbound lanes consist of three through lanes and a left turn lane. The speed limit on these roadways is 56 kmph (35 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.

Vehicle one (V1), a 1995 Mitsubishi Gallant, was northbound in lane two. Vehicle two (V2), a 2003 Ford Taurus, was westbound in lane one. In the intersection, the right side of V1 was struck by the front of V2. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The driver of V1, a thirty-four-year-old female, was traveling from her home on her way to pick up her child at school. She was well rested, healthy and familiar with her vehicle. She drives this route daily. This driver stated that her signal was green the last time she looked at it.

The driver of V2, a forty-six-year-old male, was traveling from his home to a store. He was in good health, well rested and comfortable with his vehicle. This driver travels on this roadway weekly.

The pre-event movement for V1 was coded as "going straight." The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "this vehicle traveling; crossing over (passing through) intersection." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "driver related factor; inadequate surveillance (e.g. failed to look, looked but did not see)."

The pre-event movement for V2 was coded as "going straight." The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded as "other vehicle encroachment; from crossing street, across path." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V2 was "not coded to this vehicle."
