Vehicle 1 (V1) is a 2000 Dodge Durango, sport utility vehicle, four-door (case study vehicle). Vehicle 2 (V2) is a 2003 Dodge Ram 1500, pickup truck. V1 was occupied by a male, age 39 as its driver (case study participant), a female, age 38 (occupant 2) in the front right position and a male, age12 (occupant 3) in the right position of row 2. All occupants were wearing the available lap and shoulder belts. Occupant 3 was asleep with his upper body tilted to the right. The frontal air bags did deploy as a result of impact. The crash occurred during the hours of dusk. The weather at the time of the crash was clear and dry. The roadway surface was dry. The posted speed limit is 55 mph (89 kmph). The crash occurred on a four lane trafficway. The trafficway is divided by a large grass median. The trafficway travels in a north-south direction. The northbound direction is at a higher elevation than the southbound direction. The northbound direction consists of two through lanes and an asphalt shoulder. The southbound direction consists of two through lanes and an asphalt shoulder bordered by a metal guardrail. The southbound direction has a positive grade. V1 was traveling southbound and V2 was traveling northbound. V2 crossed into the northbound lanes. V2's front struck the left front of V1. The impact caused V1 to rotate in a counterclockwise direction. As V1 rotated, the vehicle started to roll onto its right side, but prior to completing the roll the vehicle struck the metal guardrail with its right side. V1 came to rest against the guardrail. V2 came to rest in the left through lane of the southbound lanes. The driver of V1 was transported by air to a trauma center, and occupant 2 and 3 were transported to a local emergency room by ground.
