This two-vehicle crash occurred in a four-legged, traffic signal-controlled intersection. The east/west roadway is level, straight, undivided and consists of two lanes in each direction. The speed limit on this roadway is 56 kmph (35 mph). The north/south roadway is level, straight, undivided and consists of one lane in each direction. The speed limit on this roadway is 40 kmph (25 mph). It was dark with street lights on at the time of the crash with no adverse atmospheric conditions.

Vehicle one (V1), a 2002 Kia Sedona, was eastbound in lane one. Vehicle two (V2), a 2000 Ford F150 pickup, was southbound in lane one. In the intersection, the front of V1 struck the right side of V2. V2 traveled off the southeast corner of the intersection and the front struck a pole. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The driver of V1, a thirty-year-old male, stated that he looked down and reached to retrieve his phone. He further stated that he never saw V2 before the crash. No further information is available for this driver.

The driver of V2, a twenty-five-year-old male, was on his way home from a friend's house. He was well rested, healthy and comfortable with his vehicle. He travels this route weekly.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "this vehicle traveling; crossing over (passing through) intersection." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "driver related factor; internal distraction."

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded as "other vehicle encroachment; from crossing street, across path." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V2 was "not coded to this vehicle." This driver's assumption that the driver of V1 would yield the right-of-way was an associated factor.
