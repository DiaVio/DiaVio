This two-vehicle crash occurred on the shoulder of a divided, straight, level trafficway, with two lanes in each direction. The speed limit on this roadway is 105 kmph (65 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.

Vehicle one (V1), a 2006 Ford Mustang, was northbound in lane one. Vehicle two (V2), a 1990 Honda Civic, was parked (unoccupied) on the right (east) roadside. V1 traveled off the right side of the road and the front struck a road marker post. V1 continued northbound off the road and the front struck the back of V2. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The driver of V1, a twenty-seven-year-old male, was traveling from his home to have lunch with his co-workers in another city. The crash occurred approximately one hour after he left home. This driver was well rested and familiar with his vehicle and the roadway. He is diabetic and takes Novolog and Lantus. This driver stated that he remembered getting on the road and then remembers nothing until after the crash. A witness stated that V1 was swerving on and off the road prior to the crash. According to the driver's medical report, he had a hypoglycemia insulin reaction.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: this vehicle traveling; off the edge of the road on the right side. The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as a driver related factor: heart attack or other physical impairment of the ability to act.

V2 was legally parked on the roadside and was not considered a case vehicle.
