Vehicle 1 (V1) is a 1999 Hyundai Accent, two-door hatchback (case study vehicle). Vehicle 2 (V2) is a 2004 Ford medium/heavy truck. V1 was occupied by a male, age 27 as its driver and lone occupant (case study participant). The case study participant was wearing the available lap and shoulder belt and the frontal air bags did deploy as a result of impact. The crash occurred during the hours of darkness. The weather at the time of the crash was clear and dry. The roadway surface was dry. The posted speed limit is 40 mph (64 kmph). The crash occurred at a large intersection controlled by a traffic signal. The roadway consists of two through lanes, one dedicated right turn lane and one dedicated left turn lane for the nothbound direction. The southbound direction has two through lanes and one dedicated left turn lane. A raised concrete median divides the roadway. The roadway is level in the area of the crash. V1 was traveling nothbound in the left through lane. V2 was traveling southbound attempting to make a left turn. V2 turned into the path of V1. V1 struck the front end of V2 with its front end. Due to the size difference, V1 under-rode V2. The case study participant was transported by air to a trauma center.
