This two-vehicle crash occurred in a four-legged, traffic signal-controlled intersection. The north/south roadway is straight and level and consists of three through lanes and a left turn lane. Lane one in the southbound direction was closed for construction at the time of the crash. The speed limit on this roadway is 64 kmph (40 mph). It was dark with street lights on in the evening with no adverse atmospheric conditions.

Vehicle one (V1), a 1996 Toyota Camry, was southbound in lane three. Vehicle two (V2), a 1984 Chevrolet G10 van, was northbound in lane two. V1 turned left at the intersection and the right side was struck by the front of V2. The vehicles rotated and the right side of V1 struck the left side of V2. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The driver of V1, a thirty-five-year-old female, was not located for an interview. No further information is available for this driver. The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as: this vehicle traveling; turning left at intersection. The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as a driver related factor: type of driver error unknown.

The driver of V2, a fifty-four-year-old male, was a newspaper delivery driver. He was on his way from one job site to another. This driver was healthy, well rested and familiar with his vehicle. He drives this route daily. The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded as: other vehicle encroachment; from opposite direction - over left lane line. The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V2 was not coded to this vehicle.
