This single-vehicle crash occurred on a physically divided, five-lane, left curve interstate roadway. The interstate runs east and west at the location and has four lanes on each side with a lane to merge to another roadway. It had been snowing the day before and all night and the road were snowed packed with ice treatment. The road curves to the right with a radius of 141 meters. The speed limit is 105 kph (65mph) and it was snowing lightly at the time.

Vehicle one, a 1998 Ford Taurus was westbound in lane one negotiating the left curve. A non-contact vehicle that was ahead of and to the left of vehicle one suddenly changed lanes to the right. Driver one steered to the right and impacted a cement barrier with its front. Vehicle one, rotating clockwise impacted the end of the cement barrier and ran off the roadside and impacted a brick wall with its front. Vehicle one came to rest on its wheels facing north against the brick wall. Vehicle one was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 48 kmph for V1.

The driver of vehicle one, a fifty-year-old female was driving to work from home. This driver had been driving for about thirty minutes at the time of the crash. This driver stated that as she was driving she was thinking about the road conditions. This driver had only been up for about one hour at the time of the crash. This driver stated that the vehicle ahead of her suddenly changed lanes to the right and she swerved to avoid. This driver drives this vehicle daily and over this road on a daily basis.

The critical pre-crash event for vehicle one was coded as “this vehicle control loss due to: poor road conditions [puddle, pot hole, ice, etc.] [Specify]: snow packed and icy road conditions”. The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for vehicle one was coded as “Environment related factor: slick roads [low friction road surface due to ice]”.
