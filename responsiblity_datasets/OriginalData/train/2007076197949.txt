This single-vehicle crash occurred in a residential area on a 3-lane asphalt roadway with a center two-way left turn lane. The roadway is straight and level. There is a concrete irrigation ditch bordering the northbound lane. The speed limit is 40 kmph (25 mph). The crash occurred mid-afternoon during daylight hours with no adverse weather or road conditions.

Vehicle one (V1), a 1991 Nissan Pathfinder 4-door compact utility vehicle, was northbound when it gradually departed the right road edge and contacted the northbound curb with its tires. The vehicle traveled across the dirt shoulder then fell into the concrete ditch to the right where it came to rest facing north. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 24 kmph for V1.

The driver of V1, a forty four (44) year-old female, stated to police that she was lighting a cigarette and looking down inside the vehicle when she drove off the road. She stated to police that she takes several prescription drugs. The medical report indicates she uses Triamterene, a prescription drug used to control hypertension. The driver refused to be interviewed and refused to allow the vehicle to be inspected.

The critical pre-crash event was coded: this vehicle traveling, off the edge of the road on the right side. The critical reason for the critical event was coded as a driver related factor: internal distraction.
