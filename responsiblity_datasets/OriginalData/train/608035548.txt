This case involves a principal front end-plane to near side-plane impact to the case vehicle, resulting in serious injuries to the 78-year-old female right-front passenger. The case vehicle, a four-door 2002 Toyota Camry was westbound in the curb lane of an eight-lane dry, level, asphalt, divided underpass at an interchange of an interstate highway. Vehicle two (V2), a 1983 Chevrolet G-20 van was in the middle lane of the southbound off-ramp. It was daylight and there was no adverse weather. As the case the case vehicle entered the intersection of the off-ramp and the westbound traffic lanes, V2 entered its path and struck the case vehicle on the right quarter panel with its front end. The impact caused the case vehicle to rotate clockwise through the intersection, before it came to rest on the gore of the raised median in front of a traffic signal standard facing north. V2 also rotated clockwise and came to rest in the intersection facing southwest. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. The 56-year-old male driver and the 78-year-old female right-front passenger were the only occupants of the case vehicle. Both occupants were by restrained a three-point belts. In addition, the right-side side-curtain airbag deployed. The driver sustained police reported 'possible' injuries. It is not known whether he sought medical treatment. The right-front passenger was transported to an outside hospital and later transferred to a level-one trauma center with serious injuries and enrolled as a case occupant.
