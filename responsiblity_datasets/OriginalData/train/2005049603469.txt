A single-vehicle crash happened mid-morning. The location was a four lane divided (positive barrier, eight lanes overall) highway. Road surface blacktop, the weather was clear and the road was dry.

Vehicle one (V1), a 2003 Dodge Intrepid, driven by a 30 year-old female, was traveling north in the second lane of the roadway. The driver stated there was a white van traveling less than a car length in front of her. This Van slowed down significantly without coming to a complete halt. The driver of V1 hit the brakes and steered to the left to avoid a rear end collision. V1 crossed the third and fourth lane of traffic and impacted the concrete traffic median with the left front corner. The vehicle's back lifted up, rotated clockwise and impacted the traffic median with its left rear wheel. The vehicle bounced back from the median into the northbound lanes of traffic. V1 rotated clockwise and crossed lane four and before coming to rest partially in lane three and partially in lane two, facing southeast. The vehicle was towed from the scene. The driver was not injured. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: this vehicle traveling, off the edge of the road to the left side. The critical reason was coded as a driver related factor: overcompensation.
