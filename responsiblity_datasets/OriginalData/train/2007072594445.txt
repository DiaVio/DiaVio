This two-vehicle crash occurred on a straight, level, four-lane building underpass roadway. There are two lanes in each direction. In each direction, curbed separators and concrete support pillars divide the lanes. The speed limit for this roadway is 48 kmph (30 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.

Vehicle one (V1), a 2000 Plymouth Neon, was westbound in the lane to the right of the pillars. Vehicle two (V2), a 2001 Nova bus, was westbound in the lane to the left of the pillars. At the end of the pillars, V1 attempted to make a U-turn. The front of V2 struck the left side of V1. V1 rotated and the left side struck the right side of V2. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The driver of V1, a fifty-five-year-old male, had dropped his wife off and was running some errands. He was healthy, well rested and familiar with his vehicle. He rarely travels this route. This driver stated that he was focused on vehicles in the intersection ahead and did not see V2 prior to the crash.

The driver of V2, a thirty-seven-year-old female, is a city transit bus driver. She was not interviewed; no further information is available for this driver.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as " this vehicle traveling; over the lane line on left side of travel lane." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "driver related factor; illegal maneuver." This driver's failure to look to the left was an associated factor in this crash.

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded as "other vehicle encroachment; from adjacent lane (same direction) - over left lane line." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V2 was "not coded to this vehicle."
