This two vehicle daytime collision occurred on a seven lane, dry, level, asphalt roadway with a two-way left turn lane. The roadway is designated 56 kmph (35 mph). There is a stop sign for traffic entering the roadway from the private driveway on the north curb of the east/west roadway.

Vehicle one (V1) is a 1999 Toyota Avalon 4-door sedan, driven by a twenty eight (28) year old male who was traveling south in the number one lane of a private driveway. Vehicle two (V2) is a 2001 Saturn L200 4-door sedan, driven by a sixty three (63) year old female who was traveling west in the number two lane. V1 attempted a left turn out of the private driveway. As V1 made the left turn its front left plane was impacted by V2's front plane. V1 rotated clockwise and came to rest facing southwest in the two-way left turn lane. V1 headed southwest where it came to rest facing southwest in the two-way left turn lane. Both vehicles were towed with major damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: this vehicle traveling, turning left at intersection. The critical reason for the critical event was coded to V1 as a driver related factor: inadequate surveillance (e.g., failed to look, looked but did not see). The driver looked to his left but did not see V2 until just before impact. He & his passenger were on an work related errand going from a nail salon to a beauty supply house. It was his first time on this roadway and he has 18 months of driving experience.

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded: other vehicle encroachment; from driveway, turning into opposite direction. The critical reason was not coded to this vehicle. The driver of V2 has high blood pressure and takes Lotrel in the mornings.
