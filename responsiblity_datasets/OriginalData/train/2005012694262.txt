The crash occurred in the eastbound lane of a two-lane two-way roadway at the intersection. The conditions were dry and daylight on this weekend afternoon.

V1 a 1992 Oldsmobile Cutlass was traveling southbound on a two-lane, two-way asphalt roadway, approaching the intersection. He reported that he had stopped at the stop sign before proceeding, but did not see V2 coming from the adjacent street. V2, a 1983 Chevrolet Silverado was traveling eastbound on a similar roadway approaching the same intersection. The front of V2 contacted the right front of V1 before coming to final rest on the roadway. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The driver of V1 was a 31-year old male who reported that he had stopped at the stop sign for 5-10 seconds before proceeding. He had no health related problems, and had taken no medication prior to the crash. He was rested and was on his way home. He sustained no injuries.

The Critical Precrash Event for the driver of V1 was other motor vehicle in lane in crossover. The Critical reason for the Critical Event was coded as recognition error, inadequate surveillance (failed to look, looked but did not see.) The driver reported not seeing V2 before they hit. He did not attempt any avoidance maneuvers.

The driver of V2 was a man of unknown age. He took off after the crash and was not available for interview. The name on the police report may be incorrect, as the owner of the vehicle was not sure of his name.

The Critical Precrash Event for the driver of V2 was other vehicle encroachment from crossing street, a cross path. No Critical Reason was coded to the driver of V2 and he was thought not to have contributed to the crash. V2 did not have a stop sign.
