This crash occurred at night with street lights on a freeway with wet roadways and overcast conditions. Case vehicle one (V1), a 2003 Chevrolet Malibu four door sedan was westbound in lane four of five one way lanes of a divided freeway. Vehicle two (V2), a 1990 Acura Legend two door coupe was traveling eastbound in the opposite direction in the same lane. V1 swerved to the left just prior to impact with V2. The front right corner of V1 impacted the front right of V2. The damage on V1 swiped and extended down most its right side. V1 then rotated clockwise post impact across lane five and the left shoulder before the left back corner impacted the concrete barrier. V1 then came to final rest facing mainly east in lane five. V2 post impact also rotated clockwise almost one complete rotation and came to final rest facing northeast in lane three. Both vehicles were towed. The case occupant is the front right passenger of V1 who was a 29 year old female wearing the lap/shoulder belt and the instrument panel mounted air bag deployed. It should be noted that this case occupant had both feet on the front of her seat cushion with her knees to her chest. The case occupant had serious injuries and was transported and hospitalized at the trauma center. The only other occupant in V1 was the driver who was 28 year old female wearing the lap/shoulder belt and the steering column mounted air bag deployed. She was also transported to the trauma center and treated and released with minor injuries.
