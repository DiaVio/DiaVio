This moderate severity frontal crash involved a 40 year old female who was determined to be not belted and had frontal, side impact and side curtain airbag deployments. She sustained serious injuries. This crash occurred at night with street lights and clear, dry conditions. Vehicle one (V1), a 2014 Kia Rio four door sedan was travelling straight in the southbound lane of a street that became divided with a double yellow line bounded center lane and a single lane northbound lane. There were narrow paved shoulders on each side. Vehicle two (V2), a 2004 Gillig transit bus, was stopped southbound at a bus stop to the curb with the right half of the bus over the right paved shoulder and the left half of the bus partially in the southbound lane. The front right half of V1 then impacted the back left of the bus in the southbound lane. The front end of V1 sustained underride type damage mainly to the hood as it went under the back of the bus bumper. V1 rotated slightly clockwise and came to final rest engaged under the back of the bus. V1 was disabled and towed. V2 was driven away. The driver of V1 is the case occupant with no other passengers in V1. She was determined to be unbelted. Her frontal steering column mounted airbag, outboard seat back mounted side and left roof side rail mounted curtain air bags deployed during the crash. She sustained serious injuries and was taken to an outside hospital before being transferred to the trauma center.
