This two-vehicle crash occurred at a three-legged, traffic signal-controlled intersection. The north/south trafficway is straight, level and divided by a painted median. The southbound lanes consist of one through lane and a right turn lane. The speed limit for this trafficway is 72 kmph (45 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.

Vehicle one (V1), a 1990 Toyota Camry, was stopped southbound in lane two. Vehicle two (V2), a 2001 Ford Explorer, was southbound in lane two. The front of V2 struck the back of V1. V1 traveled off the road in the southwest corner of the intersection and the front struck a road marker sign and a fire hydrant. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 0 kmph for V1 and 0 kmph for V2.

The driver of V1, a fifty-seven-year-old male, was traveling from his twenty-five-year-old passenger's house to a store. This driver was well rested and comfortable with his passenger. He travels this route monthly. He has diabetes and a heart condition and takes the medications aspirin, Coumadin, Toprol - XL, Warfarin, Lisinopril, Lipitor, Glyburide and Metformin. This driver stated that he had been stopped at the red traffic signal for more than ten seconds and did not see V2 prior to the crash.

The driver of V2, a twenty-nine-year-old male, was not interviewed. No further information is available for this driver.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "other motor vehicle in lane; traveling in same direction with higher speed." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was "not coded to this vehicle."

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded as "other motor vehicle in lane; other vehicle stopped." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded as "driver related factor; type of driver error unknown."
