The crash occurred in the westbound lanes of a two-way, seven-lane roadway, which was divided by a concrete curbed median. There were four westbound lanes, with a posted speed of 56 kmph (35 mph). Conditions were daylight on a weekday afternoon, with dry roads and clear skies. Traffic flow was normal.

V1, a Saturn L200 with one occupant, was traveling westbound in lane two. V2, a city transit bus with one occupant, was also traveling in lane two, ahead of V1. V2 was stopped at a red light. The front of V1 made contact with the back of V2. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The Saturn L200 was driven by a 92-year old male, who was not injured. He declined to be interviewed. The Critical Precrash Event for V1 was "other motor vehicle in lane, other vehicle stopped". The Critical Reason for the Critical Event was an unknown driver-related recognition error. No associated factors could be determined for this motorist, due to lack of interview data.

The city transit bus was driven by a 37-year old female, who was not injured. She stated that she was driving her usual bus route. The driver was stopped at a red light, about one hundred feet from the intersection, when she was unexpectedly hit from behind.

The Critical Precrash Event for V2 was "other motor vehicle in lane, traveling in same direction with higher speed". The Critical Reason for the Critical Event was not coded to this driver. There were no associated factors present for the driver of the city transit bus.
