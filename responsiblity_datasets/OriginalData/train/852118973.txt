This crash occurred during daylight hours at an intersection. It was raining and the roadway surface was wet. Case vehicle one (V1), a 2001 Daewoo Lanos 2-door hatchback was in the northbound lane two, of a three lane, two way street and began to make a left turn at the intersection. Vehicle two (V2), a 2002 Chevrolet Tahoe four door utility vehicle was eastbound in lane two of a four lane, two way street that curved to right through the intersection. The front of V2 then impacted the left side of V1 in the intersection. With the roadway surface wet, V1 slid laterally to the right and the front right tire contact the raised curbed median. This rotated V1 counterclockwise and the left front side of V1 then lightly impacted the left fender of V2 as both vehicles came to final rest against each other with V2 facing east and V1 facing west just east of the point of impact. Both vehicles were damaged and towed from the scene. The driver of V1 is the case occupant who was a 20 year old male wearing a manual lap/shoulder seat belt and the frontal steering column mounted air bag deployed. This case occupant was seriously injured and transported to the trauma center. Another occupant in V1, included a 27 year old female who was wearing a manual lap/shoulder seat belt and the frontal instrument panel mounted air bag deployed. She was also transported to the trauma center with moderate injuries.
