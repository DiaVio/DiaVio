This two-vehicle crash occurred on a divided, straight, level roadway. There are two lanes in the southbound direction and a wide gravel shoulder. Outside the shoulder, there is a steep embankment leading down to a ditch. The speed limit on this roadway is 64 kmph (40 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.

Vehicle one (V1), a 1993 Lincoln Town Car, was southbound in lane two. Vehicle two (V2), a 1999 Dodge Ram pickup, was southbound in lane one. V1 moved to the right and the front struck the left side of V2. V1 then began rotating and departed the right side of the roadway, traveling down the embankment. The front of V1 struck the ditch and the left side of V1 struck a tree. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

V1 was driven by a male who left the scene before the police arrived. No information is available for this driver.

The driver of V2, a sixty-six-year-old male, was traveling from his home to a friend's home. He was well rested, healthy and familiar with his vehicle. This driver rarely travels on this roadway.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "this vehicle traveling; over the lane line on right side of travel lane." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "driver related factor; type of driver error unknown."

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded as "other vehicle encroachment; from adjacent lane (same direction) - over left lane line." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V2 was "not coded to this vehicle."
