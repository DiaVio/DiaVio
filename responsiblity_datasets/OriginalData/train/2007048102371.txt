This single-vehicle crash occurred on an undivided, two-lane, dirt roadway. The northbound lane has a downhill grade and curves to the right (the radius of curvature is 71 meters). The speed limit for this roadway is 56 kmph (35 mph). It was dark at the time of the crash with no adverse atmospheric conditions.

Vehicle one (V1), a 1991 GMC Jimmy, was northbound in lane one. V1 traveled to the left, the driver steered right and then V1 rotated clockwise off the right side of the road. The front of V1 struck a ditch and V1 rolled over to the left two quarter-turns. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 72 kmph for V1.

The driver of V1, a twenty-eight-year-old male, was on his way home from a friend's house. He was healthy, well rested and familiar with his vehicle. He rarely travels this route. This driver's alcohol breath test result was 0.046.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "this vehicle control loss due to traveling too fast for conditions." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "driver related factor; too fast for curve/turn."
