This two-vehicle crash occurred a little past noon during daylight hours on a straight, undivided, two-lane road under construction. The road runs north and south and has one through lane running in each direction. The speed limit on the road is 56 kmph (35 mph) and there were no adverse weather conditions at the time.

Vehicle one (V1), a 2001 Ford U. S. Postal truck, driven by a 36 year-old male was stopped southbound on the west shoulder at a roadside mailbox delivering mail. Vehicle two (V2, a 1996 Chevrolet Blazer SUV driven by a 36 year-old female with one passenger was traveling south in lane one going straight. V1 was leaving the mailbox and entering the road starting to make a U-Turn to go to the other side to deliver. V1 entered lane one as V2 got closer. Driver of V2 swerved to the left and entered lane two. V1 impacted with its front to V2's right side. V1 rotated slightly clockwise and came to rest on its wheels facing northeast. V2 rotated slightly counter clockwise came to rest on its wheels facing southeast. V2 was towed from the scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is 8 kmph for V1 and 8 kmph for V2.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: this vehicle traveling over the lane line on left side of travel lane. The critical reason for the critical event was coded to V1 as a driver related factor: inadequate surveillance (e.g., failed to look, looked but did not see).

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded: other vehicle encroachment, from parking lane. The critical reason was not coded to this vehicle.

Additional factors, driver of V1 was unfamiliar with the area, there was a 1 meter tall (3.2 feet) sign on the west shoulder warning southbound traffic of construction ahead that may have blocked his view.
