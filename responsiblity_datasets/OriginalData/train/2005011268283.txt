The crash occurred on a two-way, two-lane bituminous roadway during the morning hours of a clear, dry weekday morning. The straight roadway had a statutory speed limit of 89km/h (55mph). V1, a 1998 Mercury Mystique, driven by a 40 year-old male, was eastbound on a >2% downhill slope. The driver was observed by a witness slumped over the wheel prior to the crash. V1 left the roadway on the right side, and continued tracking until it contacted the left and right trees of three trees with one base, each >10cm in diameter. The left front door of V1 was removed to extricate the driver, and V1 was towed from the scene due to damage.

The Critical Precrash Event coded to V1 was “this vehicle traveling off the edge of the road on the right side. The Critical Reason for the Critical Event was coded as “driver related factor: critical non-performance error-other physical impairment”. The driver of V1 stated that he has epilepsy with a history of seizures and stated that he did have a seizure prior to the crash. The driver takes the prescribed medication Tegretol for his seizure disorder. This medication carries many possible side effects but the seizure itself was thought to be the only cause of the crash. There were no other Contributing or Associated Factors. There were no emotional, fatigue, stress or other health factors. The driver was familiar both with his vehicle and with the roadway. There were no environmental or mechanical factors thought to have contributed to the crash. Police did not consider either alcohol or illegal drugs to be involved and ordered no tests. The driver of V1 holds a valid driver's license. The police stated that the driver would be re-evaluated regarding the status of his driver's license. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.
