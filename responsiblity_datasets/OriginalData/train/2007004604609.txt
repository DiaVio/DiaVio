The crash occurred off the roadway on the southbound lane of a two-lane north/southbound roadway. The roadway was bituminous and level, a posted speed limit of 80 kmph (50 mph). It was afternoon on a weekend. The road was dry. Traffic flow was light.

V1, a 2001 Mercury Sable with three human occupants and one canine occupant, was traveling south when, after negotiating a curve, it departed the roadway to the right, striking a traffic sign, a retaining wall, a mailbox and a tree before coming to rest. V1 made no avoidance maneuver. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

V1 was driven by a 30-year-old female and was occupied by a 26-year-old male, a 23-year-old female and a small canine. All occupants reported injuries and were transported to the hospital: the canine was taken into protective custody by local emergency responders. The driver stated that she did not know what happened and that it was possible that her steering failed. Statements made by the passengers to local law enforcement official indicate that the driver may have been distracted by the puppy and failed to completely negotiate the curve. V1 made no avoidance maneuvers and appears to have simply driven off the road.

The Critical Precrash Event for V1 was "This Vehicle Traveling - Off the road on the right side". The Critical reason for the Critical Event was "Driver Related Factor- Internal distraction"; the driver was apparently distracted by the actions of the puppy riding in the back seat of the car and failed to finish negotiating the curve. The following associated factor was assigned, "Recognition Factor - Conversing with passenger"; the driver was conversing with both passengers and interacting with the puppy at the time of the crash.
