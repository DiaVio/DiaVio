This two-vehicle crash occurred during the daylight hours on a straight, dry, undivided, seven-lane roadway. The roadway runs north and south at the location of the crash and has a right turn lane, two through lanes and a left turn lane for northbound traffic. The speed limit on the roadway is 64 kmph (40 mph) and there were no adverse weather conditions at the time.

Vehicle one, a 1999 Chevrolet Lumina, was northbound in lane two, approaching a traffic signal-controlled intersection. Vehicle two, a 2000 Chrysler 300M, was northbound in front of vehicle one. Vehicle two stopped in traffic for a red light at the intersection. Vehicle one did not stop in time and the front struck the back of vehicle two. Both vehicles came to rest just north of the impact. Vehicle one was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 48 kmph for V1 and 48 kmph for V2.

The driver of vehicle one was a forty-nine-year-old male. There is no further information about this driver since he refused an interview.

The driver of vehicle two, a sixty-four-year-old male, was driving from his home to lunch on a route he was very familiar with. He was comfortable driving his vehicle and was well rested. He noticed vehicle one behind him, but was unable to attempt any avoidance maneuver due to traffic in front of him.

The critical pre-crash event for vehicle one was coded as "other motor vehicle in lane: other vehicle stopped." The critical reason was attributed to vehicle one and was coded as "driver related factor: type of driver error unknown." The critical pre-crash event for vehicle two was coded as "other motor vehicle in lane: traveling in same direction with higher speed." The critical reason was coded as "not coded to this vehicle."
