This crash occurred in the late evening of a weekday on right roadside of an undivided urban roadway. The east/west roadway consisted of two lanes in each direction separated by a two-way left turn lane. The bituminous roadway was dry, straight, and level with a posted speed limit of 72kmph (45 mph). The traffic flow was normal and there were no adverse atmospheric conditions at the time of the crash.

V1, a 1992 Geo Tracker was westbound, traveling in lane one. As V1 continued westbound the driver lost control of the vehicle and departed the roadway to the right. V1 crossed over an adjacent roadway and contacted a utility pole on the roadside. V1 came to final rest against the pole. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

The driver of V1, a 23-year old male, reported that he had been traveling between 67-80kmph (41-50mph) prior to the crash. He stated he was on his way to meet up with some friends and admitted to drinking before starting his trip. He was uninjured in the crash and refused treatment.

The Critical Precrash Event for V1 was coded this vehicle traveling, off the edge of the roadway on the right side. The Critical Reason for the Critical Event was coded as a driver related error, poor directional control. The presence of alcohol also factored into this collision.
