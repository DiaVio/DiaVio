This frontal crash case involves a 68-year-old restrained male with a deployed frontal air bag who sustained serious injury. The crash occurred during daylight hours in dry and clear weather. The collision occurred on a two lane, two way roadway with a downhill grade and a curve to the left. Vehicle one (V1), a 2005 Honda Accord sedan, was traveling south and downhill on the roadway. The crash occurred when the driver of V1, for unknown reasons, lost consciousness and drifted off the roadway to the right. where it struck the back of an open-topped trailer with its front. This impact rotated vehicle one clockwise and the left side of V1 swiped the left side of the trailer. After this impact V1 continued across both lanes of traffic and came to final rest facing north on the east side of the roadway. V1 was towed due to vehicle damage. V1 was driven by a 68-year-old male case occupant who was restrained by the available three-point manual belt. The available steering wheel mounted air bag deployed as a result of the impact. The case occupant was seriously injured and was transported to a level-one trauma center by ground.
