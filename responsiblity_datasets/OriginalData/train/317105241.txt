This case involved a 47 year old restrained female driver of a 2007 Toyota FJ Cruiser four door sport utility vehicle (V1). There were three other vehicles involved in the crash, a 2007 Hyundai Santa Fe four door sport utility vehicle (V2), a 1998 Honda Accord four door sedan (V3), and a 2011 Toyota Tacoma access cab 4X2 pickup truck (V4). The driver of V1 was restrained by the available three point manual safety belt and had a deployed frontal airbag. The crash occurred during daylight hours on a six-lane city street that was straight with a 2% uphill grade. The roadway ran north and south with a left turn lane and a right turn lane for each direction and two straight through lanes. There were no adverse weather conditions at the time of the crash and the asphalt roadway was dry. The posted speed limit was 64 kph (40 mph). V1 was traveling south in the second lane at an unknown speed. V2, V3 and V4 were traveling south in the same lane decelerating as they approached a traffic light that had turned red. The front of V1 struck V2 in the back, causing V2 to strike V3 and V3 to strike V4. V1 traveled off the right side of the road after impact and came to rest in a private business driveway. V2, V3 and V4 came to rest still in their original travel lane.V1, V2 and V3 were towed from the scene due to disabling damage. V4 was driven from the scene. The driver of V1 was transported by ground ambulance to a level one trauma center and hospitalized for treatment of her injuries.
