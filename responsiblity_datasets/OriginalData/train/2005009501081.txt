This crash took place at an intersection of two roadways. The east/west roadway was three lanes divided by a grass median; with a left turn lane beginning 25 meters from a signal-controlled intersection. The north/south roadway was three laned undivided. It had just began to rain in the early evening of a weekday. Both roadways had posted speed limits of 48kmph(30mph)

V1, a 1993 Toyota Camry, was traveling east in lane two approaching the intersection. V2, a 1997 Honda Accord, was traveling north in lane two approaching the same intersection. V1 and V2 made contact in the intersection. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

V1 was driven by a 40-year old male who was not injured. The driver stated that he had returned his attention forward from the rearview mirror to notice the signal light change. He attempted to stop but could not. The Critical Precrash Event for V1 was coded this vehicle traveling crossing over (passing through) intersection. The Critical Reason for the Critical Precrash Event was coded inadequate surveillance.

V2 was driven by a 30-year old female who was not injured but there was a second occupant transported. Both driver and occupant refused to be interviewed. The Critical Precrash Event for V2 was coded other vehicle encroachment from crossing street, across path. There is no Critical Reason coded to this vehicle.
