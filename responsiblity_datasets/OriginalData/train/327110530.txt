This case involves a moderate, frontal impact between the front of a passenger car and the rear end of a SUV. The case occupant is the 73-year-old female driver of the passenger car. She was restrained by a three-point belt and the steering-wheel air bag did not deploy. The case vehicle, a 2006, four-door Honda Civic was eastbound in the center lane of a dry, level, asphalt, five-lane road approaching a controlled four-leg intersection. Vehicle two (V2), a 1998 Jeep Cherokee, was stopped in traffic in the center eastbound lane. It was daylight, and cloudy. As the case vehicle was approaching the intersection the front of the case vehicle struck the rear end of the V2. After impact, the driver of V2 brought the vehicle to a controlled stop straddling shoulder and southbound bike path. V1 came to a controlled stop straddling shoulder and northbound bike path. The case vehicle was towed due to damage. The tow status of V2 was not reported. The restrained 73-year-old female driver was the sole occupant of the case vehicle. She sustained serious injuries and was transported to a level-one trauma center, where she was enrolled as a case occupant.
