This two-vehicle crash occurred in a four-leg intersection with five-lanes running east to west and two lanes entering from the north and south. The primary roadway runs east to west and is not controlled by a traffic control device. The north and south entrances are stop sign controlled. This intersection has a pedestrian crossing on the west side and is well marked. This crash occurred during daylight with no adverse weather and no obvious road defects. The posted speed is 48 kmph (30 mph) for the primary roadway with a -4% slope for the westbound travel lanes.

Vehicle one (V1), a 1986 Honda CRX was westbound in the first lane. Vehicle two (V2), a 2001 Ford Windstar was westbound in the first lane ahead of V1. V2 slowed down to stop for a pedestrian in the crosswalk. The front of V1 impacted the rear of V2. Both vehicles came to rest near the point of impact. V1 was towed due to damage. V2 was not towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 48 kmph for V1 and 48 kmph for V2.

The driver of V1, a thirty-one year old male was not interviewed. There is no known pre-crash information on this driver.

The driver of V2, a sixty-nine year old male was returning home after eating at a restaurant. This driver had been driving for about six minutes at the time of the crash. This driver was taking Flomax as a medication. This driver stated that he was driving and talking to his wife when he had to slow down to let the pedestrian cross. This driver drives this vehicle daily and over this road on a weekly basis.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: other motor vehicle in lane, traveling in same direction while decelerating. The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as a driver related factor: type of driver error unknown.

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded: other motor vehicle in lane, traveling in same direction with higher speed. The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event was not coded to this vehicle.
