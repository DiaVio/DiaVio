This case involves a minor side impact followed by a major frontal impact event resulting in chest injuries to the case occupant. Vehicle one (V1 - case vehicle), a 2000 Chrysler Voyager, minivan, was traveling north on a two lane, two way road approaching the intersection. It was day light and the bituminous (asphalt) road was dry and level. The posted speed limit is 40 km/h (25 mph). The crash occured at the four legged intersection. The north-south and east-west two way roads form the intersection. Vehicle two (V2), a1993 Toyota Camry, four door sedan, was traveling east intending to travel through the intersection. The crash occurred when V1 was traveling into the intersection and was impacted on the left side by the frontal plane of V2. After initial impact V1 traveled accross the south lane and traveled off the roadway to the left after the intersection and impacted a low brick retaining wall. V1 came to final rest facing west. The 49 year old female driver (case occupant) was not restrained and sustained significant chest injuries. She was transported to nearest trauma center via ground unit.
