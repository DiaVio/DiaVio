This single-vehicle crash occurred in a rural area on a two-lane asphalt roadway. The westbound roadway is downhill and curves left. The speed limit in the area of the crash is 88 kmph (55 mph). This road is newly built and has been open for public use for a short amount of time. The crash occurred during darkness with no lighting.

Vehicle one (V1), a 1997 Chevrolet Malibu was westbound in the first lane. V1 gradually departed the right lane, crossed the rumble strip and traveled along the paved shoulder before over correcting to the left. The vehicle rotated counter-clockwise as it crossed over both travel lanes and contacted the westbound guardrail with its front right side plane. V1 then contacted the guardrail with its right rear side. V1 continued to rotate counter-clockwise and impacted the guardrail with its front-end plane where it came to rest up against the guardrail facing southwest. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 89 kmph for V1.

The driver of V1, a twenty-seven year old male was traveling to a relative's house in another town from his own residence and had driven approximately thirty-two kilometers (twenty miles) when the crash occurred. He stated he works ten-hour shifts six days a week and had worked four days since his last day off. He also stated he consumed an excess amount of alcohol prior to starting this trip and did feel tired and sleepy while driving. He has been a licensed driver for only one year and has rarely driven on this road but has been a passenger several times while traveling on this road. The police indicated that this driver had a BAC of 0.18.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: this vehicle traveling, off the edge of the road on the right side. The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as a driver related factor: other critical non-performance [specified as: driver passed out due to alcohol consumption).
