This single vehicle crash occurred at the intersection of a five-lane roadway and a two-lane roadway. The five-lane road is straight and level. At the time of the crash, the five-lane road was stopped for a traffic light. This speed limit on the road is 56 kmph (35 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.

Vehicle one (V1), a 1997 Nissan Altima was westbound in the first lane. As V1 approached the intersection the driver departed the roadway to the right. V1 impacted a curb with its front left tire. V1 continued northwest to impact a fire hydrant with its front plane. V1 came to rest facing north on the sidewalk. V1 was towed with moderate damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

The driver of V1, a sixteen-year-old male was heading to school at the time of the crash. He said that he was making a right turn to avoid the traffic jam in front of him when a bicyclist came into the roadway from the east curb of the north/south roadway which caused him to veer to the left. However the street he was turning into is a dead end street. There was no bicyclist at the scene. Researcher believes the driver failed to see the traffic stopping ahead of him until it was too late and made a sharp right turn to avoid hitting the stopped traffic. Only a partial interview was obtained on this driver.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: this vehicle traveling, off the edge of the road on the right side. The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as a driver related factor: inadequate surveillance.
