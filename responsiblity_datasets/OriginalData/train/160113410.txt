Vehicle 1 (V1) is a 2000 Volkswagen Jetta, four-door (case study vehicle). Vehicle 2 (V2) is a 2004 Jeep Liberty, four-door. V1 was occupied by a male, age 25 as its driver and a female, age 22 as its front right passenger, (case study participant). The case study participant was wearing the available lap and shoulder belt and the frontal air bags did deploy as a result of impact. The driver of V1 was not wearing a belt and had the fronal airbag deploy.

The crash occurred during the hours of darkness. The weather at the time of the crash was clear and dry. The roadway surface was dry. The posted speed limit is 35 mph (56 kmph).

The crash occurred in an intersection. The roadway consists of three through lanes and two turn lanes for the northbound direction. The southbound direction has five through lanes and a dedicated right turn lane. A raised concrete median divides the roadway. The intersection is controlled by a traffic signal. The roadway is level in the area of the crash.

V1 was traveling northbound and was attempting to make a left turn. V2 was traveling southbound in lane two. The front left of V2 struck the right side of V1. The impact caused V1 to rotate counterclockwise coming to rest in the southbound right through lane. V2 rotated clockwise coming to rest in the intersection.

The case study participant was transported by air to a trauma center.
