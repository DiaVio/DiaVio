The crash occurred in the northbound lanes of a two-way roadway with two lanes in each direction and an emergency lane. This was a straight, level, bituminous roadway with a posted speed limit of 48 kmph (30 mph). It was the evening hours of a weekend and the road was wet, and it was dark but lighted.

V1, a 2005 Cadillac CTS four door with one occupant, was traveling northbound in lane two. As V1 attempted to change lanes to lane one, it lost control and spun clockwise and the left side impacted a tree located east of the roadway. V1 came to a final rest at the point of impact with the tree. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

V1, the Cadillac CTS, was driven by a 45 year-old male who had fatal injuries. A witness stated that she observed the driver of V1 driving at high speeds with another vehicle, and while going around her, changing lanes to lane one from lane two, lost control and went sideways into the tree. She stated that other drivers who stopped at the crash scene also claimed that this vehicle was previously racing with another vehicle on the highway. There was a long extrication to get the driver out of the vehicle.

The Critical Pre-crash Event for V1 was "this vehicle control loss due to: traveling too fast for conditions". The Critical Reason for the Critical Event was coded as a driver related factor, "aggressive driving behavior". The associated factor coded to the driver was a decision factor, which he was traveling too fast for conditions, reasons unknown. Speeding, tailgating, and rapid/frequent lane change/weaving were coded as driver's aggressive acts. The other possible factors were coded unknown because no interview was obtained.
