This near-side impact crash case involves a 78 year old restrained male with deployed frontal, side and curtain air bags who sustained severe injuries. The crash occurred during daylight hours at a four legged traffic signal controlled intersection with clear, dry conditions. The case vehicle one (V1), a four door 2008 Honda Civic four door sedan, was traveling north in lane two of two one way lanes, and Vehicle 2 (V2), a 1980 two door Ford pickup, was traveling east in lane one of a six lane, two way street. V1 started from a stop and began to make a left turn. V2 was crossing straight through the intersection and the front of V2 impacted the left side of V1. V1 rotated clockwise as V2 continued in a northeasternly direction and the left side of V1 side slapped the right side of V2. V1 continued to rotate as it traveled east and came to final rest facing northeast in the eastbound travel lanes. After the vehicles side slapped, V2 continued to in a northeastern direction and came to final rest in the westbound lanes, facing northwest. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. The case occupant is a 78 year old male driver of V1 in the front left position who was restrained with the manual lap/shoulder belt. This case occupant's seat belt retractor pretensioner actuated, the steering column mounted frontal air bag, the seat back mounted side air bag, and the left roof side rail mounted side curtain all deployed. This case occupant sustained severe injuries and was transported to the trauma center. There were no other occupants in the vehicle.
