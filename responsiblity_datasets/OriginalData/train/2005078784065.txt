This is a single vehicle, rollover crash that occurred on straight, level, rural, bituminous Interstate highway. The roadway had two eastbound and two westbound traffic lanes separated by a depressed, sand and gravel median. Improved asphalt shoulders line the outside edges of the traffic lanes. The posted speed limit was 121 kmph (75 mph). The crash occurred shortly before noon hours on a Saturday. There were no adverse weather or environmental conditions. The day was bright sunshine and 78 degrees. There was excellent visibility with no sightline restrictions

Vehicle one (V1), a white 2004 Nissan Xterra compact utility vehicle, was traveling eastbound in the #2 travel lane when the left rear tire experienced a blowout. This caused a loss of control and the vehicle entered an immediate clockwise rotation, the left rear rim gouged into the pavement while still in the #2 lane and the vehicle overturned on the roadway. V1 rolled off the roadway and came to final rest on the sand/gravel roadside, on its wheels, facing generally southwest. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: this vehicle loss of control due to, blow out/flat tire, (specified as: a blowout of the left rear tire). The critical reason for the critical event was coded as a vehicle related factor: tires/wheels failed.
