This two-vehicle crash occurred on a divided, level, straight roadway, near an intersection. The intersection was controlled by a flashing yellow traffic signal for north/south traffic. The southbound lanes approaching the intersection consist of two through lanes, a right turn lane and a left turn lane. The speed limit on this roadway is 89 kmph (55 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.

Vehicle one (V1), a 2006 Volkswagen Beetle, was southbound in lane three. Vehicle two (V2), a 1998 Jeep Cherokee, was behind V1. The driver of V2 saw the yellow light and assumed that it was a tri-light that was about to turn red. V1 stopped. The driver of V2 noticed V1 stopped and steered to the right in an attempt to avoid a collision. The left front of V2 struck the right back of V1. V2 then rotated counterclockwise and rolled one quarter-turn to the right. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 0 kmph for V1 and 0 kmph for V2.

The driver of V1, a twenty-six-year-old female, was traveling to a business appointment. She was well rested, healthy and familiar with her vehicle. This driver travels this route weekly.

The driver of V2, a thirty-four-year-old female, was traveling with a seven-year-old male, from her home to her friend's house. She was well rested, healthy and familiar with her vehicle. This driver travels this route weekly. This driver noticed the flashing yellow signal and did not realize that V1 would stop.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "other motor vehicle in lane; traveling in same direction with higher speed." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "driver related factor; too fast to be able to respond to unexpected actions of others."

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded as "other motor vehicle in lane; other vehicle stopped." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event was not coded to this vehicle.
