A single vehicle crash occurred on a Saturday evening during darkness with no street lighting in an urban residential area. . The crash occurred on a two lane undivided, dry, level, asphalt roadway in a right curve with a radius of 86 meters (283 feet). The roadway is designated 40 kmph (25 mph.).

Vehicle one (V1), a 2004 Dodge Durango 4x2 4-door sport utility, was traveling north in the number one lane. Acceleration marks indicated the path of V1 as it accelerated from a stop sign located at a Tee-intersection prior to entering the curve. The vehicle departed the roadway to the right and impacted a tree with its front right plane. The tree was located 0.9 meters (3 feet) from the curb line. V1 then came to rest facing north at the curb just past the struck tree. V1 was towed due to damage. The driver fled the scene. The investigating officer did not complete a report. Police Reported Travel Speed is No PAR obtained (created) for V1.

The driver of V1 could not be located due to the fact that it was a hit and run with a stolen vehicle. It was learned later that the officer did not file a hit and run report and only did a stolen recovery.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: this vehicle traveling-off the edge of the road on the right side. The critical reason was coded as a driver related factor: poor direction control. The driver accelerated at a rapid pace and failed to maintain control of the vehicle in the curve.
