This one-vehicle crash occurred on a two-way, two-lane, not physically divided roadway. The crash took place during daylight hours and there were no adverse atmospheric conditions at the time of the incident. The roadway upon which V1 was traveling was straight and level with a posted speed limit of 72kmph (45mph).

V1, a 1995 Dodge Dakota, was traveling east in lane one of the roadway. The vehicle left its original lane of travel to the left into the opposing traffic lane. V1 then continued off to the left leaving the roadway striking a tree stump, and continued down an embankment before coming to final rest in a drainage basin. The vehicle sustained severe undercarriage damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Unknown for V1.

V1 was driven by a 46 year-old male in good health who was headed to his friend's home. According to the driver, he was looking at a map, which was being held by the passenger. The next thing he knew, he was headed off of the roadway and up the embankment. However, the driver was suspected of consuming alcohol and was given a field sobriety test as well as a Breathalyzer test. The police determined that he was not over the legal limit and was able to operate a motor vehicle. The driver did not suffer from any injuries as a result of the crash and did not require any medical attention. The passenger of V1, a 34 year-old female sustained minor injuries but did not require transport to a medical facility. V1 was towed from the scene.

The Critical Pre-crash Event that was coded as this vehicle traveling, off the edge of the road on the left side. The Critical Reason for the Critical Pre-crash Event was coded as an internal distraction; he was trying to read a map instead paying attention to the roadway, which caused his vehicle to drift off of the road. The associated factors include a slight physical impairment due to alcohol and the driver's unfamiliarity with the road as he rarely drove it.
