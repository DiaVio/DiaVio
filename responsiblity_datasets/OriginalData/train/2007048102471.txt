This single-vehicle crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions. The roadway was divided, level and straight. The westbound lanes consist of two through lanes with shoulders on both sides. The speed limit for this roadway is 113 kmph (70 mph).

Vehicle one (V1), a 1998 Honda Civic, was westbound in lane one. The driver lost control of his vehicle due to engine failure. V1 skidded off the right side of the roadway and the front struck a guardrail. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 121 kmph for V1.

The driver of V1, a twenty-year-old male, was traveling from his home to work. He was well rested, healthy and drives on this roadway daily. This driver had just purchased the vehicle three days prior to the crash.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "this vehicle control loss due to disabling vehicle failure." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "vehicle related factor; transmission/engine failure."
