This case involves a moderate to severe, rollover crash of at least ten-quarter turns. The case occupant is the 35-year-old female driver. She was restrained by a three-point belt, but no air bags deployed. The case vehicle a 2004 GMC Envoy SUV was eastbound in the outside lane of a dry, level, concrete divided highway. It was dark and cloudy. The right-front passenger perceived an impending impact with construction barrels and reached over and made a steering input to the left. The driver reacted by steering right, hitting a construction barrel on the right front side of the case vehicle and then over-corrected steering left. The case vehicle departed the roadway off the south side, entered a counter-clockwise rotation and traveled along an embankment approximately fifty-feet. The right wheels dug into the ground, causing the vehicle to rollover at least ten-quarter turns before it came to rest on its top facing north. The vehicle was towed due to damage. The 35-year-old female driver and 23-year-old male right front passenger were restrained by a three-point belt. No air bags deployed. The 21-year-old male right-rear passenger was not restrained by a three-point belt. Both passengers were transported to a local hospital. The case occupant left the scene, and awoke the next morning with extreme pain and was taken to an outside hospital before being transferred to a level-one trauma center.
