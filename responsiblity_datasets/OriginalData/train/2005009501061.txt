This Crash took place at the intersection of two rural two-lane roadways. Conditions were daylight and dry in the early evening of a weekday. The roadway had a posted speed limit of 48 kmph (30mph).

V1, a 2001 Volkswagen Cabriolet, was traveling north approaching the intersection. This vehicle attempted to turn left and struck the northwest curb while departing the roadway. V1 continued off the roadway, made contact with its front plane to a tree, rotated counterclockwise making contact with two additional trees on its right plane, then came to rest. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

V1 was driven by a 19-year old male who was transported with incapacitating injuries. He stated that he had been following a friend to an unknown destination. Both vehicles were traveling at a high rate of speed. When the lead vehicle turned left at the intersection he attempted to do so also. V1 was towed due to damage.

The Critical Pre-crash Event for V1 was this vehicle off the edge of the road on the left side. The Critical Reason for the Critical Pre-crash Event was too fast for curve.
