This crash occurred off the right side of an undivided straight two-lane asphalt roadway with a posted speed limit of 89 kmph (55 mph). The crash occurred during the daylight hours in the early afternoon. The road was icy.

V1, a 2003 Dodge Ram pickup truck, was driven by a 57-year old female. V1 was traveling southbound when it lost control due to the icy roads. It rotated clockwise approximately 180 degrees and skidded off the roadway where it began a fall-over type rollover on the roadside. It rolled 2-quarter turns to the left and landed on its top. The driver of V1 was transported to the hospital due to minor injuries. V1 was towed from the scene due to moderate damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 64 kmph for V1.

The Critical Pre-Crash Event coded for V1 was "This vehicle control loss due to traveling too fast for conditions." The Critical Reason for the Critical Event was coded as "Slick roads," an environment related factor. Associated factors coded to the driver of V1 include the use of several prescription medications for high cholesterol, hypertension, and chronic back pain, traveling too fast for conditions, the slick condition of the roadway, and the weather (snow).
