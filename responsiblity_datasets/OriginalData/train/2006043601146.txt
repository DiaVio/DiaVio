This single vehicle crash occurred during a weekday morning commute on a main thoroughfare. The roadway runs north and south and is divided by a curbed grass median. At the location of this crash, there are three northbound lanes, two southbound lanes, and a posted speed limit of 89kmph (55 mph). The conditions were clear, dry and daylight.

V1, a 2000 Nissan Xterra with one occupant, was traveling south in lane two when an unknown vehicle from lane one swerved in front of it. V1's driver avoided colliding with the unknown vehicle by immediately steering to the left. V1 departed the left roadside and struck the curbed island. V1 entered the northbound traffic turn lane. The impact combined with a tire blowing out caused the vehicle to roll over 6 quarter-turns before coming to final rest roughly 50m (164 ft) from the initial trip point. Police Reported Travel Speed is 89 kmph for V1.

V1 was driven by a 17-year-old female high school student (senior) that was driving to school this morning when she was involved in this crash. She states she was traveling south in lane two at approximately 89kmph when a vehicle in the lane to her right (lane 1) cut in front of her. She immediately pulled the steering wheel to the left, lost control of her vehicle and drove into the concrete crossover divider. After she struck the concrete traffic island, an unknown tire blew out and her vehicle began to rollover. The Nissan Xterra she was driving belonged to her mother and this was only the seventh time she ever drove this vehicle. She was transported to a local trauma center where she was treated and released for whiplash.

The Critical Precrash Event for V1 was coded this vehicle traveling, off the road on the left side. The Critical Reason was coded as a driver related factor, too fast to be able respond to unexpected actions of others. Associated factors coded to this crash include poor directional control, driver inexperience, and a tire deficiency.
