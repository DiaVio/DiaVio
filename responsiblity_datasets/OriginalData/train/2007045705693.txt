The crash occurred on an interstate exchange ramp from eastbound interstate lanes to northbound interstate lanes. The road had 2 lanes, was one way and had paved shoulders on either side. The roadway was curved left with a radius measuring 354m and was super-elevated 5.2%while having a negative 4.6% grade. The right/east road edge was bordered by gravel for a few meters then grass for approximately 140 meters parallel to the roadway. The ground then had a brief positive grade at the edge of a wooded area approximately 200 meters north of the run off road point. Conditions at the time of the crash were daylight and dry in the early afternoon on a weekday. The speed limit was 64kph/40mph.

V1 was a 2004 Ford Taurus traveling north in lane 1 of the exchange ramp when it departed the right road edge traveling through the grass field and entering the woods and finally struck a tree with its front end coming to rest facing north at impact. V1's undercarriage was hung on a stump and had to be winched free before being towed away. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

V1 was driven by a fifty-nine-year-old-belted-male who was transported to an area hospital with possible injuries. In a brief statement to the researcher he (D1) stated that he recalled feeling dizzy or something awhile before the crash.

The critical precrash event for V1 was this vehicle traveling off the edge of the road on the right side. The critical reason was coded as an unknown critical non-performance. The driver told police he was taking heart and seizure medication. He also related a memory lapse of several minutes prior to the crash.
