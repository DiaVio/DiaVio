V1, a 1995 Pontiac Grand Am, was traveling south on a three-lane interstate divided by a concrete median barrier. The concrete roadway was dry, level, and curved to the right. The posted speed limit was 105 kmph (65 mph). The crash occurred in the late evening hours on a clear night. The roadway was illuminated by intermittent streetlamps.

V1 was initially traveling in lane two, coming up upon a slower-moving non-contact vehicle. The driver of V1 changed lanes to the left to pass the non-contact vehicle. The driver of V1 then attempted to change lanes back into lane two. While attempting this, the driver of V1 lost control. He crossed two lanes of travel and skidded off the roadway to the right. The right side of V1 struck a tree and then a fence. It continued in a southbound direction on the roadside and its left front then struck the leg of a sign. V1 then rolled to its right one-quarter turn and its top struck a bridge abutment. V1 spun on its right side counterclockwise and then landed on its wheels. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

The driver of V1 was a 35-year old male who admitted he had been traveling over 113 kmph (70 mph) prior to the crash. A witness who had been traveling in the first lane of travel reported traveling 75 mph and stated that V1 was going much faster than that. The driver of V1 reported that he had no health-related problems and had taken no medication prior to the crash. He was rested and traveling from his new house to his old house. He does not wear corrective lenses. He sustained moderate injuries and was transported to a local trauma facility. A BAC test conducted less than one hour after the crash revealed a BAC of .30.

The Critical Pre-crash Event for the driver of V1 was when he lost control due to traveling too fast for conditions. The Critical Reason for the Critical Pre-crash Event was aggressive driving. Associated factors included alcohol intoxication, excess speed, and poor directional control.
