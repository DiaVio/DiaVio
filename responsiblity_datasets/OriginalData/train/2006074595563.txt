This two vehicle crash occurred during mid-afternoon daylight hours in lane one of a 5 lane divided -4% grade, concrete roadway, with a posted speed limit of 64 kmph (40 mph). The weather was clear and dry.

Vehicle one (V1) was a 1994 gold Saturn 2 door coupe driven by a 21 year-old female. V 1 was traveling west. approached the intersection, the driver noticed a pollster on the right side of the roadway that the driver knew. While the driver of V 1 was looking to the right, traffic had come to a stop at the intersection ahead of her. When the driver looked forward again it was too late to stop and she ran into the rear of vehicle two (V2). At the time of the crash V1 was traveling about 48-56 kph (35-39 mph). V2 was a 2005 Nissan Pathfinder SUV driven by a 59 year-old female, V 2 was also traveling west in lane one, immediately in front of V 1. The driver of V 2 stopped the vehicle just west of the intersection due to heavy traffic up ahead. V 2 was struck in the rear by the front of V 1. Both vehicles came to rest in the same travel lane. V 1 was towed from the scene, V 2 was driven from the scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is 0 kmph for V1 and 0 kmph for V2.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: other motor vehicle in lane, other vehicle stopped. The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event was a driver related factor: external distraction. The driver was looking towards the right at a friend on the sidewalk.
