The crash occurred on a two-way, two-lane bituminous road with a posted speed limit of 72 kph (45 mph). There was an advisory/caution limit of 56 kph (35 mph) at the beginning of a left hand curve. Conditions at the time of the weekday morning crash were dark, unlighted, cloudy, and the roadway was wet.

V1, a 2002 Ford Taurus SES 4 door sedan driven by a 45 year-old male, was westbound on the road on his commute to work. The driver stated that as he came around the bend (left hand curve) and a deer ran out from his left. The driver braked (full ABS application) and swerved to the right, missing the deer. The right side tires contacted the gravel on the right shoulder and the driver steered back to the left, overcompensating. The vehicle crossed the centerline and spun counterclockwise. V1 returned to the right lane, existed the road on the right side and crossed the partially paved shoulder. V1 continued onto wet grass down a 30% slope with the backside leading. The right front of V1 contacted a 9cm road sign metal post, bending it downward. V1 slid rearward and the right side contacted two trees (<10cm diameter). V1 came to rest off the road facing east. V1 was towed from the scene due to damage. The fully restrained driver denied injuries. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

The Critical Precrash Event coded to V1 was 'This Vehicle Control Loss Due Too Traveling Too Fast for Conditions.' The Critical Reason coded to this driver was 'overcompensation.' Contributing factors coded were 'successful avoidance maneuver to a previous critical event' and 'animal (deer) in roadway.' Additional contributing factors coded were 'wet road,' 'dark and unlighted', and 'speed too fast for curve,' as the driver stated that he was traveling 40-45 mph. Associated factors coded were 'unfamiliarity with roadway as the driver usually travels a different route' and 'low tire pressure in the rear tires.'
