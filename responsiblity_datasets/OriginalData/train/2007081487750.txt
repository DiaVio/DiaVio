This single-vehicle crash occurred just past noon during daylight hours. The location was a rural residential roadway. The roadway was two-way and comprised of two-lanes. The roadway was a slight curve to the left with a 4% downhill grade in the northbound direction of travel. The weather was clear and dry. The posted speed limit was 56 kmph (35 mph). There were Chevron warning signs posted in the curve.

Vehicle one (V1), a 1990 Acura Legend coupe driven by a forty eight (48) year old female, was traveling westbound down a hill, and did not negotiate the curve to the left. V1 departed the roadway to the right, and impacted the rock wall, a street sign, a mailbox, and some garbage cans. V1 then re-entered the roadway coming to rest facing westbound in lane one. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

Most of the driver's pre-crash factors are unknown as she was unable to be reached for interview. She did admit on-scene to taking oxycontin for chronic pain. and was arrested for DUI.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: this vehicle traveling, off the edge of the road on the right side. The critical reason for the critical event was coded to V1 as a driver related factor: poor directional control (e.g., failed to control vehicle with skill ordinarily expected). Associated factors for V1 were: medication, alcohol, and too fast for curve/turn. Vehicle dynamics indicate V1 was tracking when it departed the roadway.
