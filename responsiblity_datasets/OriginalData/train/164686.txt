Vehicle one (V1 - case vehicle), a 2002 Chevrolet Cavalier, 2-door coupe was traveling east on the exit ramp of a 5-lane, divided freeway (two lanes westbound, divided median, two lanes eastbound, plus one lane exit ramp). It was daylight, the weather was clear and the asphalt road surface was dry. The driver of V1 entered onto the exit ramp too fast and the driver was unable to comlete the right curve. V1 departed the left side of the exit ramp while beginning a clockwise yaw and tripped over the left side wheels, rolling down the embankment a total of 4-quarter turns and coming to rest on its wheels facing southwest. The vehicle was towed from the scene due to disabling vehicle damage. The 24-year-old female driver (case occupant) was using the available three-point seat belt and no air bags deployed. She was transported via ground ambulance to a regional level-one trauma center with minor (AIS-1) injuries. The 42-year-old male right-front passenger (case occupant) was using the available three-point seat belt and no air bags deployed. He was transported via ground ambulance to a regional level-one trauma center with severe (AIS-4) injuries. The 5-month-old female left-rear passenger was reportedly seated in an unknown child restraint secured to the vehicle seat. She was transported via ground ambulance to a regional level-one trauma center with unknown injuries. The 2-year-old male right-rear passenger was reportedly seated in an unknown child restraint secured to the vehicle seat. He was reportedly not injured.
