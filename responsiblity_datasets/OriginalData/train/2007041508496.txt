The crash occurred in the northbound lanes of a two-way, four-lane roadway, which was divided by a curbed median. There were two northbound lanes, with a posted speed of 56 kmph (35 mph). This was a straight, level, and asphalt roadway. Conditions were daylight on a weekday afternoon with dry roads and clear skies. Traffic flow was normal.

V1, a 2007 Chrysler Sebring 4-door sedan with one occupant, was traveling northbound in lane one. V2, a properly parked 2002 Dodge Durango with no occupants, was occupying a parking space in the parking lane to the right of lane one. The driver of V1 veered to the right. The front right side of V1 sideswiped the back left corner of V2. V1 and V2 were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is No PAR obtained (created) for V1 and No PAR obtained (created) for V2.

The driver of V1 stated that she had just flown in that day for a weeklong business trip. She had worked that day, and was on her way back to the hotel. The driver indicated that she was initially heading west, and then turned north at the intersection. She said that she was in lane one, when a vehicle in lane two suddenly cut her off. She tried to avoid the other vehicle by steering right. In doing so, the motorist hit a parked vehicle.

The Critical Precrash Event for V1 was "this vehicle traveling over the lane line on the right side of travel lane." The Critical Reason for the Critical Event was a driver-related decision error, incorrect evasive action. One associated factor for this driver was experience-related. The driver was in a rental vehicle that she had rented that morning, and had only driven it once prior to the crash. No additional associated factors were coded to this driver.
