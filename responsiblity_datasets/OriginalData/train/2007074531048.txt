This two-vehicle crash occurred in a four-legged, traffic signal-controlled intersection in an interchange area. Both roadways are undivided. The southbound approach is level, straight and consists of a left turn lane and a right turn/through lane. The eastbound approach is straight, has an uphill grade and consists of two through lanes and a left turn lane. The speed limit for these roadways is 56 kmph (30 mph). Traffic was congested at the time of the crash. This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.

Vehicle one (V1), a 1985 Cadillac Fleetwood, was southbound in lane one. Vehicle two (V2), a minivan, was eastbound in lane one. In the intersection, the front of V2 struck the right side of V1. V1 was towed due to damage. V2 left the scene prior to police arrival. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The driver of V1, a fifty-five-year-old female, was traveling with three adult passengers. They were on their way home from a grocery store. This driver was healthy, well rested and comfortable with her vehicle and passengers. She travels this route daily. This driver stated that she was talking with her passengers and did not see V2 prior to the crash.

The male driver of V2 was not interviewed. No further information is available for this driver.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "other vehicle encroachment; from crossing street, across path." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was "not coded to this vehicle." This driver's inadequate surveillance as an associated factor in this crash.

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded as " this vehicle traveling; crossing over (passing through) intersection." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded as "unknown reason."
