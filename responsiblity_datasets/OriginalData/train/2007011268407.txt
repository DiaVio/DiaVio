The crash occurred on a two-way, two-lane, level rural road with a speed limit of 89 kmh (55 mph). Conditions at the time of the weekday morning crash were daylight, clear and dry.

V1, a 2000 Ford F150 2 door pick-up driven by a 25 year-old male on his way home from an appointment at a medical clinic, was westbound on the road. The driver of v1 dropped his personal listening device, reached down to retrieve it, taking his eyes off of the road. V1 left the road on the right and the driver looked back up just before impact. The driver took no avoidance actions. The right side of v1 struck a 35 cm diameter tree and continued and struck a 30 cm diameter tree with the front right. V1 then struck a 40 cm diameter tree with the front center. V1 rotated counterclockwise and came to rest facing northeast. V1 was towed from the scene due to damage. The fully restrained driver was transported from the scene to a local trauma center due to injuries. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

The Critical Precrash Event coded to the driver of v1 was 'this vehicle traveling off the edge of the road on the right side.' The Critical Reason for the Critical Event coded was a 'driver related factor of internal distraction.' A Contributing factor coded was for 'fatigue'. Associated factors were coded for possible side effects of prescribed medications for diabetes, back pain and drug addiction.
