This single vehicle versus a parked vehicle collision occurred on a two-lane two-way city street oriented in the north-south direction at dusk. The roadway is straight, level and paved with bituminous asphalt in the area of the crash. There is on-street parking on the west side of the street. The speed limit for the road is 48 kmph (30 mph). At the time of the crash the weather was clear.

Vehicle one (V1), a 1994 GMC Safari minivan driven by a sixty one (61) year old male, was traveling south on the city street. Vehicle two (V2), a JLG lift/crane was parked on the west side of the street. There was a reflective traffic cone near the rear of V2. V1 had struck two parked vehicles north of the final collision site in a previous collision, impacting the rear of the first vehicle and sideswiping the second. V1 then continued south, finally striking the parked crane head on. V1 was then disabled and was towed. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: this vehicle traveling, over the lane line on the right side of the travel lane. The critical reason for the critical event assigned to this driver is coded as a driver related factor: heart attack or other physical impairment of the ability to act.

Additional factors for this driver are medication use for insulin, alcohol presence and insufficient steering inputs for an avoidance action. When the driver was first removed from the crashed vehicle, he exhibited the symptoms of a diabetic with low blood sugar. A blood draw confirmed that the driver had low blood sugar at the time of the crash, and he was operating the vehicle in a near-unconscious state.
