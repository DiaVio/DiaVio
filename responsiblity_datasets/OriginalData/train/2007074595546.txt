This two-vehicle crash occurred at a three-legged intersection on an undivided, straight roadway that consists of two lanes in each direction. The westbound approach has an uphill grade. The speed limit for this roadway is 56 kmph (35 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.

Vehicle one (V1), a 1995 Toyota Camry, was westbound in lane two. Vehicle two (V2), a 2000 Lexus ES300, was stopped westbound in lane two, waiting to turn left in the intersection. The front of V1 struck the back of V2. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The driver of V1, a seventy-seven-year-old female, was on her way from one store to another. She was well rested and familiar with her vehicle. She rarely travels this route. She takes the medications Levothroid, Effexor XR, Metformin, Aspirin, Tylenol and vitamins. This driver stated that she was looking left for the street sign. When she looked forward again and realized that V2 was stopped, she applied the brakes in an attempt to avoid the crash.

The driver of V2, a fifty-nine-year-old female, was on her way home from shopping. She was in good health, well rested and familiar with her vehicle. She travels this route daily. This driver has a thyroid condition and takes Synthroid and Advil. She stated that she noticed V1 in her rearview mirror at the last moment and released the brakes.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "other motor vehicle in lane; other vehicle stopped." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "driver related factor; external distraction."

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded as "other motor vehicle in lane; traveling in same direction with higher speed." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V2 was "not coded to this vehicle."
