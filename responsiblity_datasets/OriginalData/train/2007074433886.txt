This two-vehicle crash occurred on an undivided, straight, level roadway near a four-legged, traffic signal-controlled intersection. This roadway consists of one lane in each direction with parking on both sides. The speed limit for this roadway is 48 kmph (30 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.

Vehicle one (V1), a 1994 Ford Ranger, was southbound in lane one. Vehicle two (V2), a 1998 Ford Explorer, was southbound in lane one, stopped at the intersection. The front of V1 struck the back of V2. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The driver of V1, a fifty-four-year-old male, was traveling from his home to a store. He was well rested and familiar with his vehicle and route. He has high blood pressure, depression, sleep apnea (treated with CPAP) and restless leg syndrome and takes the medications Requip, Cymbalta, Lisinopril, Prilosec, Abilify, Walitin and Klonopin. This driver stated that he was looking at the houses on the right side of the road. When he returned his attention to the roadway, he realized that V2 was stopped and applied the brakes and steered right in an attempt to avoid the crash.

The driver of V2, a forty-three-year-old male, was on his way home from school with a female passenger. He was healthy, well rested and comfortable with his vehicle and passenger. He travels this route daily. This driver stated that he had been stopped "for about two minutes" and did not see V1 prior to the crash.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "other motor vehicle in lane; other vehicle stopped." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "driver related factor; external distraction."

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded as "other motor vehicle in lane; traveling in same direction with higher speed." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V2 was "not coded to this vehicle."
