This case involves a single front end-plane to side plane crash event resulting in serious injuries to the driver of the case vehicle (case occupant). The case vehicle a four-door 2003 Hyundai Sonata was traveling southbound in the center lane of a three-lane roadway that was part of a divided trafficway, approaching a controlled four-leg intersection. Vehicle two (V2) a 1993 4-door Oldsmobile Cutlass Cruiser station wagon was traveling westbound in the center lane of a divided five-lane roadway, approaching the same intersection. It was daylight and the road was snowy/slushy. As the case vehicle was passing through the intersection, it entered the path of V2. The front of V2 struck the left side of the case vehicle. The impact caused the case vehicle to rotate counterclockwise and come to final rest facing northeast. V2 rotated counterclockwise and came to final rest facing southeast. The case occupant was the sole occupant of the case vehicle. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. The 55-year-old female driver was the sole occupant of the case vehicle. She was restrained by a three-point belt and the head/torso side-impact air bag deployed. She sustained serious injuries and was transported to a level-one trauma center.
