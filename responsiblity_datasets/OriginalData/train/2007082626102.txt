This two vehicle daytime collision occurred in the intersection of a two lane arterial road. The road is paved with asphalt and is straight with an downhill grade of 9% for V1 and a uphill grade of 9% for V2. Traffic flow is controlled by on-color traffic signal. The speed limit for both vehicles is 48 kmph (30 mph). At the time of the crash the sky was clear.

Vehicle one (V1), a 1990 Cadillac Deville sedan driven by a seventeen (17) year old female with three passengers, was traveling south in lane one. Vehicle two (V2), a 1994 Kia Sephia sedan driven by a twenty five (25) year old female, was traveling north in lane one. V1 entered the intersection and began a left hand turn. V2 then entered the intersection and the front of V2 impacted the front of V1. V1 came to rest facing south/east and V2 came to rest facing north/east. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The driver of V1 was unable to be located and interviewed after numerous attempts. The driver of V2 refused to be interviewed and or take part in the study.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: this vehicle traveling turning left at intersection. The critical reason for the critical event was attributed to V1 as a driver related factor: type of driver error unknown. Without interview information it could not be determined if the critical reason was a recognition or decision factor.

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded: other vehicle encroachment; from opposite direction - over left lane line.
