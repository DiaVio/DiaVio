This single-vehicle crash occurred on a median-divided, level roadway. The eastbound lanes curve to the left and consist of two through lanes. The speed limit on this roadway is 105 kmph (65 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.

Vehicle one (V1), a 2000 Toyota Camry, was eastbound in lane one. V1 traveled off the left side of the roadway and the left side struck a delineator post. V1 returned to the roadway, then again off the roadway to the left rotating counterclockwise. The right side struck a small pole and then a large sign post. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 105 kmph for V1.

The driver of V1, a twenty-one-year-old female, was on her way home from work. She was healthy and comfortable with her vehicle. She travels this route daily. This driver stated that she has two jobs and generally sleeps four hours per day. She also stated that she looked down at her speedometer just before V1 moved off the left side of the road.

The pre-event movement for V1 was coded as "going straight." The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "this vehicle traveling; off the edge of the road on the left side." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "driver related factor; internal distraction." The driver's fatigue was an associated factor.
