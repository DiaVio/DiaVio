This single vehicle-crash happened on a weekday during early morning daylight hours. The location was the eastbound lanes of a divided trafficway. The eastbound lanes consisted of three lanes with a slight left curve and an uphill grade of 4.1 percent. The radius of the curvature was 563 meters (1846 feet). The weather was clear and the roads dry. The posted speed limit for the roadway was 89 kmph (55 mph).

Vehicle one (V1), a 2005 Chevrolet Impala, was traveling east in the first lane of the roadway. V1 drove off the road to the right and the front struck a metal guardrail and the right side struck a concrete barrier. V1 re-entered the eastbound travel lanes, crossed all three lanes of travel and the front left corner of V1 struck the concrete median barrier. V1 bounced back, rotating clockwise. It traveled further south and came to rest within lanes one and two, facing south. V1 was towed from the scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

A 21-year old male, was the driver of the vehicle. He was not interviewed. He stated to the police at the scene that an unidentified vehicle cut into his lane forcing him to steer right. The driver's parents indicated that this driver was in good health prior to the crash and does not take any medication. He works 12 hours every day and was on his second day off at the time of the crash. He was heading home after he spent the night at a friend's house.

The critical per-crash event was coded: this vehicle traveling, off the edge of the road on the right side. The critical reason was coded as a driver related factor: overcompensation.
