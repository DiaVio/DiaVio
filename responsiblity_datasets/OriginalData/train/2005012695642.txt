V1, a 1995 Chevrolet Cavalier was traveling northbound on a three lane, one way, dry, concrete highway in daylight conditions. V1 was traveling in lane one and then changed lanes to lane two. She noticed that another car was trying to merge into her lane, so she tried to change lanes back into lane one but overcorrected and lost control. V1 departed the roadway on the right side and rotated in a clockwise motion. V1 contacted its left front to the guardrail and then contacted it's left back to the guardrail before coming to final rest up against the guardrail.

The driver of V1 was a 47-year old female who had reported that she had been traveling between 98-113kmph (61-70mph) prior to the crash. She stated that she moved into the middle lane from the right and a van was moving into the middle lane from the left. She swerved to avoid the van . She was rested and traveling home from work. The driver was wearing her prescribed lenses that corrected a Myopic (near-sighted) condition. She sustained minor injuries from the crash but refused treatment. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

The Critical Precrash Event for the driver of V1 was this vehicle traveling off the edge of the road on the right side. The Critical Reason for the Critical Precrash Event was a performance error, overcompensation, because she tried to avoid another vehicle coming into her lane and she over steered and contacted the guardrail.

Note: the damage to the right side of the vehicle was from a previous crash.
