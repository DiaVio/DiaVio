This crash occurred in the morning of a weekday on the left side of a two-lane, rural highway. The westbound lanes were bituminous, dry, and level with a posted speed limit of 113kmph (70 mph). The highway curved to the right as one continued westbound. The traffic flow was normal and there were no adverse atmospheric conditions at the time of the crash.

V1, a 1993 Oldsmobile 98, was westbound in lane one negotiating the curve when it drifted off the roadway to the left. V1 continued down a negative 20% slope traveling roughly 190m (624 ft.) before hitting a stump and coming to final rest. V1 was towed from the scene with front-end damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

The driver of V1, a 71yr old male, stated that he does not remember leaving the roadway, and that he had a seizure. He stated that he came out of the seizure at the bottom of the hill right before he hit the stump. He sustained minor injures, but did not require transport.

The Critical Precrash event for V1 was coded this vehicle traveling, off the roadway to the left. The Critical Reason for the Critical Event was coded as a driver related factor, physical impairment of the ability to act (seizure). There were no other associated factors coded to this driver.
