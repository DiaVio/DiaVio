The case occupant is a 74 year old male driver who was involved in a severe rollover collision and sustained serious injuries. He was restrained with a manual lap/shoulder belt and the frontal airbag deployed This crash occurred at night with foggy conditions and snow/ice on the roadway. Vehicle one (V1), a 2005 Ford Excursion four door utility vehicle was traveling in the southbound lane two of a three lane, two way highway with a steep upgrade. V1 lost control due to the conditions and crossed over the northbound lane and departed the roadway to the left into the ditch. The front of V1 impacted the embankment that deployed the frontal airbags. V1 then began to rotate counterclockwise and rolled over to the right four quarter turns and came to final rest on its tires facing east in the ditch. V1 was towed due to damage. The driver of the V1 is the case occupant who is a 74 year old male. He was restrained with the manual lap/shoulder belt and the frontal steering column airbag deployed and the belt pretensioner fired. He sustained serious injuries and was transported to the trauma center.
