This crash occurred in the westbound lanes of a four-lane undivided roadway. There were two westbound lanes and two eastbound lanes. The roadway curved to the left, had an uphill slope, was bituminous and had a posted speed limit of 56 kmph (35 mph). The roadway was dry and conditions were clear.

V1 was a 1989 Ford F350 with a dump truck bed, being driven by a 54 year-old male. V1 was traveling westbound and had just finished negotiating the left hand curve when it suddenly left the roadway to the right side. The vehicle impacted a telephone pole, shearing it in half, and coming to final rest in a large hole in the ground with the pole resting on top of it. Police Reported Travel Speed is 80 kmph for V1.

The Critical Precrash Event for V1 was "this vehicle traveling, off the edge of the road on the right side." The Critical Reason for the Critical Precrash Event for V1 was "Unknown reason for critical event." The driver stated that as he was negotiating the curve, the steering wheel began shaking and he lost control of the vehicle. He attempted to steer to the left but was unable to and that braking was not working. The vehicle followed the curb until it came to what used to be an entrance to a house. The vehicle then left the road, pitched down into a whole in the ground and hit a utility pole braking it in half. Upon inspection of the curb at the scene there was no significant scuffing along the curb. The drivers' son, who was driving ahead of him, stated that he saw his father fighting with the steering wheel in his rear view mirror. It should be noted that the driver appeared to be intoxicated at the time of the interview. A blood alcohol test was administered to the driver with a result of 0.245. When the driver was talking about his avoidance maneuvers it also seemed that he began braking after the collision with the utility pole.
