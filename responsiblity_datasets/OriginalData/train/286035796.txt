Vehicle #1 (case vehicle) was a 2007 Ford Focus, 4-door sedan. There were three occupants in our case vehicle with the front right and rear left occupants as our case subjects. The case vehicle was equipped with lap and shoulder belts for all seating positions in the vehicle. The driver and front right passenger seating position was equipped with frontal and side seatback mounted air bags. The driver's side seatback mounted air bag was the only one to deploy. None of the three occupants in the vehicle were wearing their seatbelts at the time of the crash Vehicle #1 was traveling southbound on a two lane undivided residential roadway with a speed limit of 25 mph (40kmph). The roadway was snow covered and it was both raining and snowing at the time of the crash. As V1 entered a left curve in the roadway, the vehicle lost control and began to spin out of control. V1 began to rotate clockwise 80 degrees before impacting a parked vehicle. After initial impact, V1 rotated clockwise side slapping the parked vehicle with its left side. V1 continued rotating clockwise before traveling to its final resting position in the northbound lane facing south. The case vehicle was towed from the scene due to disabling damage. The front right case occupant was airlifted to a local trauma center for treatment of his injuries. The rear left case occupant was transported to a local hospital via ground transportation and then later airlifted to a local trauma center for treatment of his injuries.
