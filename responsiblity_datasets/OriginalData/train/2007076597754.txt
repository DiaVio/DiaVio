This single vehicle rollover type crash occurred on a four-lane divided roadway. This roadway is asphalt constructed with a posted speed limit of 89 kmph (55 mph) with various curves and grades. This crash occurred on a downgrade of 6 percent with a right hand curve. This roadway runs north to south with hillside cuts on the west side and guard railing on the east side. This crash occurred just past noon with good visibility and no adverse weather. There are warning signs prior to this curve warning of the curve and an advisory speed of 64 kmph (40 mph).

Vehicle one (V1), a 2004 Chevrolet Malibu sedan, was southbound in lane one. V1 departed the right side of the road, the driver steered left and V1 departed the left side of the roadway. The front of V1 struck a guardrail and V1 traveled back across all lanes of traffic and departed the right side of the roadway. The front struck a dirt/rock embankment and V1 rolled to the left four quarter-turns. V1 was towed from the scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is Unknown for V1.

The twenty-six-year-old female driver of V1 stated that she was not quite sure what had happened. She was healthy, well rested and familiar with her vehicle. She travels this route several times each month. She takes the medication Phentermine. This driver remembered going along at the speed limit (89 kmph/ 55 mph), then felt her vehicle begin to rotate. She stated that she did not remember anything else.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 coded: this vehicle traveling, off the edge of the road on the left side. The critical reason for the critical event was coded as a driver related factor: overcompensation.
