This single-vehicle crash occurred on a four-lane, undivided roadway. In the eastbound direction, the two through lanes have a 7.4% uphill grade and curve to the right. The speed limit on this roadway is 89 kmph (55 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours and it was snowing/sleeting. The roadway was wet and slush covered.

Vehicle one (V1), a 2006 Chevrolet C1500 pickup, was eastbound in lane one. The driver of V1 lost control and V1 traveled into lane two and back into lane one rotating clockwise. V1 rotated off the right side of the roadway and rolled over to the left in the ditch. The top of V1 struck an embankment and V1 came to rest. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 64 kmph for V1.

The driver of V1, a thirty-six-year-old female, was traveling with her two teenage sons, from a relative's home to their home. She was well rested, healthy and comfortable with her passengers and vehicle. She drives on this roadway weekly. This driver stated that she had lost control of V1 earlier on this roadway but was able to regain control. She further stated that she was driving below the speed limit, but was aware that the road conditions and weather were precarious.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "this vehicle control loss due to traveling too fast for conditions." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as " driver related factor; too fast for conditions (snowing/sleeting with wet, slushy roadway)."
