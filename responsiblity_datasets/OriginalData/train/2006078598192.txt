This single-vehicle crash occurred on a level rural roadway. This roadway consists of two lanes in each direction and a center left turn lane and curves to the right in the northbound direction. The fields on both sides of this roadway are cultivated with many deep furrows. The speed limit on this roadway is 89 kmph (55 mph). This crash occurred at dawn with no adverse atmospheric conditions.

Vehicle one (V1), a 2003 Ford Ranger, was northbound in lane one. The left rear tire sidewall blew out. V1 rotated counterclockwise and traveled off the right side of the roadway. The undercarriage of V1 struck the ground and the back struck a ditch. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

The driver of V1, an eighteen-year-old female, was driving her seventeen-year-old sister into town from their home. She was well rested, healthy and comfortable with her vehicle and passenger. She travels this route daily.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: this vehicle control loss due to: blow out/flat tire - left rear tire blowout. The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as a vehicle related factor: tires/wheels failed.
