This case involves a moderate narrow-overlap frontal impact involving the front-left bumper corner of the case vehicle, a 2008, four-door, Honda Civic and the right-front corner of a 2002 Chevrolet Silvarado 1500 pick-up truck. The case occupant is the 48-year-old female driver of the passenger car. She was restrained by a three-point belt, and the steering-wheel and curtain air bags deployed. The case vehicle a 2008, four-door, Honda Civic was northbound on a dry, two-lane, asphalt road with a 2% up-grade while negotiating a banked right-sweeping curve. It was daylight and clear. Vehicle two (V2) a 2002 Chevrolet Silvarado 1500 pick-up truck was southbound on the same road. V2 drifted off the east edge of the roadway. The driver of V2, over-corrected and V2 entered a counterclockwise yaw and crossed the centerline. The front-right corner of V2 struck the front-left corner of the case vehicle. V2 continued to rotate counterclockwise and departed the roadway off the west edge down an embankment and came to rest facing east in a field. The case vehicle entered a clockwise rotation and departed the roadway off the west edge and came to rest on the roadside facing west. The restrained 48-year-old female driver was the sole occupant of the case vehicle. She sustained serious injuries was transported to an outside hospital before being transferred level-one trauma center, where she was enrolled as a case occupant.
