This moderate severity frontal crash involved a 61 year-old female front right passenger who was restrained with a manual lap/shoulder belt with a frontal airbag deployment and who sustained moderate severity injuries. This four vehicle, rear end crash occurred on a five lane, one-way freeway during daylight hours and under clear and dry conditions. All four vehicles were travelling northbound in lane five, a dedicated car pool lane. Case vehicle one (V1), a 2012 Toyota Prius was following vehicle two (V2), a 2008 Subaru Outback. Vehicle three (V3), a 1999 Honda Civic was ahead of V2 and vehicle four (V4), a 2014 Honda Pilot was ahead of V3. V4, V3 and V2 began to decelerate in the lane. The driver of V1 braked, and then the front of V1 impacted the back of V2. The front of V2 was forced into the back of V3, and the front of V3 was forced into the back of V4. V1 and V2 were towed due to damage. The case occupant is a 61 year-old female who was the front right passenger of V1. She was wearing the manual lap/shoulder belt and the belt pretensioner actuated on impact. The front instrument panel airbag also deployed in her seated position. She was transported to the trauma center and hospitalized. The driver of V1 was also restrained and the frontal steering column and knee bolster airbags deployed in his position. He was not injured or treated.
