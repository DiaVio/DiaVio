This crash occurred on a multi-lane divided urban trafficway. The trafficway is situated in a north to south configuration and is comprised of three travel lanes for each direction. The trafficway is divided by a grass and tree covered median without a positive barrier. At the time of the crash it was dark and the streetlights were lit. The weather was clear and the road surface was dry. At the time of the crash traffic was light to moderate. Involved in the crash was the case vehicle (V1) a 1999 Ford Explorer 4-door SUV equipped with manual 3-point lap and shoulder restraints for each outboard seating position. In addition to the manual restraints, V1 is also equipped with a driver and right passenger frontal impact air bags, both of which deployed in the during the crash sequence. V1 is also equipped with seat mounted side impact air bags for the front seat occupants. Only the driver's seat mounted side impact air bag deployed during the crash sequence. The driver of V1 was a 62-year-old male (case subject). He was not using the available manual restraint at the time of the crash. The right front seat was occupied by a 58-year-old female. She was using the available manual restraint at the time of the crash. The right rear seat was occupied by a 96-year-old female (case subject). She was also using the available manual restraint at the time of the crash. V1 was traveling in a southerly direction in the third lane. As V1 proceeded south, the driver fell asleep at the wheel and V1 veered to the left. V1 struck and mounted the center median and continued forward in a southerly direction. V1 struck a palm tree with the left front corner of the bumper in a sideswipe type impact. V1continued in a southerly direction and struck a second palm tree again with the left side in another sideswipe type impact. The second sideswipe impact continued down the entire length of the left side of V1. V1 continued forward and struck a third tree. The third impact was to the left side of the front bumper. This narrow frontal impact caused the case vehicle to rotate in a counterclockwise direction where it came to final rest still engaged with the tree. The driver of V1 did not loose consciousness in the crash. He was found inside V1 by the paramedics. The LF Door was jammed shut. After extrication the driver was transported to the trauma center via helicopter. The right rear occupant was knocked unconscious in the crash. She was extricated from the case vehicle and transported to the trauma center also by helicopter.
