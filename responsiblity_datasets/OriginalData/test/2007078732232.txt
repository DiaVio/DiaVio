This three-vehicle rear end crash occurred on a two-way, five-lane dry asphalt roadway. There are two westbound lanes and two eastbound lanes that are separated by a continuous left turn lane. The speed limit on the roadway is posted 56 kmph (35 mph). The traffic at the time of the crash was congested. The weather was clear with no atmospheric conditions. It was daylight and mid-morning.

Vehicle one (V1), a 2002 Chevrolet Cavalier 2 door coupe with one occupant, was traveling eastbound in the left lane attempting to change lanes to the right. Vehicle two (V2), a 2005 Suzuki Forenza 4 door with one occupant, was stopped in the eastbound left lane. Vehicle three (V3), a 2003 Toyota Sequoia with two occupants, was stopped ahead of V2 in the eastbound left lane. The front of V1 struck the rear of V2, pushing V2 into the rear of V3. All vehicles came to rest in the eastbound left lane facing east. V1 and V2 were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 32 kmph for V1 and 32 kmph for V2.

The driver of V1, a twenty two (22) year old female, said that she was traveling eastbound and was looking over her right shoulder to change lanes. She said that she then looked ahead and saw V2 stopped in front of her. She said that she could not change lanes due to heavy traffic in the lane next to her. She said that she applied her brakes and thought her car skidded before she struck the rear of V2.

The driver of V2, a eighteen (18) year old female, said that she was stopped for about 5 seconds before V1 hit her from behind. She said that she saw the driver of V1 in her rear view mirror and that driver was looking over her right shoulder trying to change lanes to the right. She said that the driver of V1 then looked ahead just before the crash. The driver of V2 was transported from the scene by EMS.

The driver of V3, a thirty eight (38) year old female, said that she was stopped in the eastbound left lane for about 3 seconds before she was struck by V2. She said that she never saw the other cars before the crash. She said that traffic was heavy at the time of the crash. She said that she was on the way to the mall with her mother. She said that she picked up her mother just before the crash. This driver and passenger, a 62-year old female, did not report any injuries at the scene.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: other motor vehicle in lane, other vehicle stopped.. The critical reason for the critical event was coded to V1 as a driver related factor: external distraction. The driver was looking over her right shoulder to changes lanes. This took her attention off the traffic ahead of her.

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded: other motor vehicle in lane; traveling in same direction with higher speed. The critical reason was not coded to this vehicle.

V3 was involved in the first harmful event. The critical reason was not coded to this vehicle.
