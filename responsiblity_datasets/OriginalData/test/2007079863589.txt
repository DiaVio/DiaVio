This two vehicle daytime collision occurred early morning on a four lane, undivided, dry, level, asphalt roadway. The north/south roadway is designated 56 kmph (35 mph). There is a radar activated digital speed monitor device on the west side of the roadway. The road merges from two lanes to one lane for southbound traffic just before the impact area.

Vehicle one (V1) is a 1999 Toyota Camry Solara, 2-door sedan, driven by a forty three (43) year old female. V1 was traveling south in the number one lane. Vehicle two (V2) is a 2001 Toyota Corolla sedan driven by a thirty eight (38) year old male. V2 was traveling south in the number two lane. V1 had just left a parking space and was making a u-turn when the left front corner impacted the right front door of V2 that was headed south in lane number two. After impact V1 rotated clockwise coming to final rest facing south in travel lane, V2 continued south coming to final rest facing south. V1 was towed from the scene, V2 was driven from scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: other (specify) making u-turn. The critical reason for the critical event was coded to V1 as a driver related factor: inadequate surveillance (e.g., failed to look, looked but did not see). The driver of V1 stated that she never saw V1 prior to impact. She had been looking at another vehicle that had made a U-turn prior to the crash. She was on her way home after dropping her children off at school.

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded: other vehicle encroachment; from adjacent lane (same direction) - over right lane line. The critical reason was not coded to this vehicle.
