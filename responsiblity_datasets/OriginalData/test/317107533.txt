This case involved the 17 year old restrained male driver of a 2004 Mazda RX8 two door coupe (V1). V1 was struck in the left side by the front of a 2003 Chevrolet Silverado 4x4 extended cab pickup (V2). V2 was also involved in a head-on collision with a 1998 Chevrolet S10 regular cab pickup (V3). There were two occupants of V1, the driver (our case subject) and the front right passenger. The driver was restrained by the three point manual safety belt and had deployed side airbags. The front right passenger was unrestrained with no aiirbag deployment. The crash occurred at dark on an unlit dry asphalt two lane roadway. The road ran east and west and curved to the left with a 6% downhill grade for the eastbound traffic. It was clear with no adverse weather conditions at the time of the crash. The posted speed limit was 72 kph (45 mph). V1 was traveling east at the posted speed limit behind V3. V2 was westtbound at approximately 105 kph (65 mph) when it crossed the center line and struck V3 in an offset frontal impact. V3 rotated 180 degrees counterclockwise and came to rest on the south side of the road. V1 steered to the right and also departed the south side of the roadway. V2 continued forward and to the left, leaving the roadway and striking V1 in the left door with its frontal plane. V1 and V2 continued down an embankment with the front bumper of V1 impacting a small tubular post before coming to rest on a private driveway facing north. V2 came to rest on the far side of the driveway facing south. All three vehicles were towed from the scene due to disabling damage. The driver of V1 was transported by ground ambulance to a local hospital and later transferred to a level one trauma center. The front right passenger was also transported to the hospital for treatment.
