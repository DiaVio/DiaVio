This single-vehicle crash occurred on a jersey barrier-divided, straight, level trafficway. The southbound lanes consist of three through lanes with a paved shoulder on the right side. There is a curb and a grass downhill embankment next to the right shoulder. The speed limit on this roadway is 105 kmph (65 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions. Traffic was congested at the time of the crash.

Vehicle one (V1), a 2003 Jeep Liberty, was southbound in lane two. The driver of V1 started to change lanes to the left and realized that a vehicle was in lane three. This driver abruptly steered to the right to avoid this vehicle. V1 traveled to the right and the driver steered abruptly to the left in an attempt to avoid traveling off the right side of the roadway. V1 rolled over to the right four quarter-turns, traveling to the southwest. The right rear wheel struck the curb and V1 continued to roll two more quarter-turns and the left side struck a bush. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 105 kmph for V1.

The driver of V1, a twenty-four-year-old female, was on her way to work from her home. She was healthy and familiar with her vehicle. She travels this route daily. This driver stated that she felt fatigued when she left her home. Also, she stated that she is near-sighted and was not wearing corrective lenses.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "this vehicle traveling; over the lane line on left side of travel lane." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "driver related factor; overcompensation." This driver's fatigue (due to long work hours and only five-and-a-half hours of sleep), lack of corrective eyewear and inadequate surveillance were associated factors in this crash.
