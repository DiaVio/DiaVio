This crash occurred on a 7 lane highway with a median, 3 lanes were heading south and 4 lanes were heading north. I was late in the evening and there were no adverse weather conditions.

Vehicle 1 was a 1987 Chevy Pick-up truck traveling north on the highway and was in the left turn lane. Vehicle 1 turned left at the intersection. Vehicle 2 a 1997 Buick Lasabre was traveling south, struck Vehicle 1 on its right side. The two vehicles then side slapped causing damage on Vehicle 2's left side and then causing the driver of Vehicle 1 to lose control, jump the curb, and strike a large utility box completely damaging it. Police Reported Travel Speed is Unknown for V1 and Unknown for V2.

Vehicle 1 was driven by a 37 year old male. Attempts to contact driver of Vehicle 1 was unsuccessful since he is serving time in a correctional institute.

The Critical Precrash Event was coded this vehicle traveling turning left at intersection. The Critical Reason for the Critical Precrash Event was coded as unknown reason for critical event.

Vehicle 2 was driven by a 20 year old male who stated that he was going home from work when he saw Vehicle one sitting at the intersection waiting to turn left. The driver of Vehicle 2 went straight and as he crossed the intersection Vehicle 1 pulled out in front of him. He tried to turn right and brake but it was to late and he hit him in the right side.

The Critical Precrash Event was coded other vehicle encroachment from opposite direction - over left lane line. The Critical Reason was not coded to this vehicle.
