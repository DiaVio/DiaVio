This single vehicle crash occurred late afternoon during daylight hours. There was sun glare present for westbound traffic at the time of the crash. The crash occurred on a four lane physically divided, dry, level, highway comprised of concrete. The roadway is designated 104 kmph (65 mph). The number four lane is an HOV lane.

Vehicle one (V1) was a 1985 Isuzu Pup diesel two-door pick up truck with a large toolbox bed and a ladder rack. V1 was driven by a fifty one (51) year old male with one passenger who were traveling west in the number two lane. V1's right rear tire blew out causing the driver to loose control and rotate clockwise and cross over into the number one lane. V1's driver then steered to the left and V1 rolled over onto its right side in the travel lanes. V1 came to rest on its roof in the number two lane. V1 was towed with roll over damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: this vehicle loss of control due to - blow out/tire failure. The critical reason for the critical event was coded to V1 as a driver related factor: overcompensation. The driver stated he made no effort to regain control of the vehicle, although vehicle dynamics suggest he steered to the left. The shifting cargo also helped induce the rollover.
