Vehicle one (V1 - case vehicle), a 2001 Hyundai Accent, 4-door sedan was traveling west in the westbound lane of a two-lane, two-way rural road. It was dark, the weather was cloudy and the bituminous road was wet. As V1 approached a left curve, the driver of V1 continued straight and departed the right side of the road. The front of V1 struck a large tree (80 cm diameter) and V1 rotated clockwise apporaximately 90 degrees before coming to final rest. The 17-year-old male driver was using the available three-point seat belt and the steering-wheel air bag deployed. He was pronounced dead at the scene and transported to the local morgue. The 17-year-old male right-front passenger was using the available three-point seat belt and the dash-mounted air bag deployed. He was pronounced dead at the scene and transported to the local morgue. The 16-year-old female left-rear passenger was not using the available three-point seat belt. She was also pronounced dead at the scene and transported to the local morgue. The 17-year old male right-rear passenger (case occupant) was using the available three-point seat belt. He was transported via ground ambulance to a local hospital and later transferred to a regional level-one trauma center.
