This two-vehicle rear-end crash occurred at dusk on a weekday at a T-intersection. The bituminous roadway consisted of two lanes and was not physically divided. The dry roadway was straight and had a positive grade of two percent for southbound traffic. The posted speed limit was 64kmph (40mph). There were no adverse atmospheric conditions at the time of the crash and traffic flow was normal.

Vehicle one (V1), a 2002 GMC Envoy utility vehicle, was heading south in lane one on the roadway intending to continue straight. Vehicle two (V2), a 2001 Ford Focus 4-door sedan, was also heading south on the roadway in lane one in front of V1. The front of V1 contacted the rear of V2. After the initial impact, V1 careened off the right side of the roadway where it contacted a tree before coming to final rest. Vehicle two continued forward slightly before coming to final rest facing south on the roadway. Both vehicles had to be towed from the scene due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Unknown for V1 and Unknown for V2.

The driver of V1, a 31 year-old male, was not injured as a result of the crash and did not require any medical treatment. There were no other occupants in the vehicle when the crash occurred. A partial interview was obtained from the driver of this vehicle. He claimed that he was driving directly behind V2 when all of the sudden V2 slammed on the brakes. He said that he tried to avoid the collision by braking and steering to the right, but to no avail. After he hit the car in front of him, he veered off of the side of the road and hit a tree. The driver claimed that he was well rested at the beginning of the trip and was somewhat familiar with the roadway. The driver also related that he was in good health and did not take any medications on the day of the crash.

The Critical Pre-crash Event coded to this vehicle was: other motor vehicle in lane, traveling in same direction while decelerating. The Critical Reason was coded as following too closely to respond to other driver's actions. No additional associated factors were coded to this vehicle.

The driver of V2, a 25 year-old male, was not injured as a result of the crash and did not require any medical treatment. There were no other occupants in the vehicle when the crash occurred. A complete interview was obtained from the driver of this vehicle. He related that he was driving along the roadway when the car in front of him stopped suddenly to make a left turn at the intersection. Upon noticing this, the driver activated his emergency brake and was hit from behind by V1. The driver claimed that he was not fatigued and was familiar with the roadway. The driver also relayed that he was in good health and was not on any medication when the crash occurred.

The Critical Pre-crash Event coded to this vehicle was: other motor vehicle in lane, traveling in same direction with higher speed. The Critical Reason was not coded to this vehicle. No associated factors were coded to this vehicle.
