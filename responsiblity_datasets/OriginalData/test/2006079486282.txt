A two-vehicle collision occurred on a Thursday late afternoon. The collision occurred at a four-leg intersection. Both legs have five lanes including a left turn pocket. Both roadways are dry, level and made of asphalt. Both roadways are designated 56 kmph (35 mph). The intersection is controlled by a tri-light signal, which was working properly at the time.

Vehicle one (V1), a 2003 Ford Mustang 2-door coupe with one occupant, was traveling south in the number three lane. Vehicle two (V2), a 2004 Chrysler Town and Country 4-door minivan with one occupant, was traveling north in the number one lane. As V1 made a left turn its front impacted the left side of V2. V1 came to rest facing east in the intersection. V2 came to rest facing northeast in the intersection. Both vehicles were towed with major damage. The driver of V2 was transported. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The driver of V1 is a 21-year-old male, with 3 1/2 years of driving experience. He was on his way home from a store. He said he panicked when he saw V2 coming through the intersection and did not apply his brakes in time. He was stopped behind another vehicle that turned left in front of him. He detected V2 but failed to attempt any evasive maneuvers. He drives the route daily and is familiar with the vehicle. He has limited driving experience.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: this vehicle traveling, turning left at intersection. The critical reason was coded a driver related factor: panic/freezing. The driver admitted he saw V2 in time but did not attempt any evasive maneuvers.

The driver of V2 is a 68-year-old female with 52 years of driving experience. She was on her way home from work at the time. The driver has a hyperopic (far sighted) condition but was not wearing her glasses at the time. She does not have a corrective lenses restriction on her license. Her boyfriend taught her to drive and has been to traffic school more than five years ago. The driver stated she entered the intersection and was struck on the left side. She had no idea where the other vehicle came from.

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded: other vehicle encroachment; from opposite direction-over left lane line. A critical reason was not assigned to this vehicle.
