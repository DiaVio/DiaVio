This two vehicle crash occurred at an intersection during daylight hours with clear, dry conditions. Case vehicle one (V1), a 2000 GMC Savana Van 1500, was westbound on a five lane, two way street with a center left turn lane and curb lanes for merging and right turns. Vehicle two (V2), a 2004 Isuzu Box Truck was southbound in the left turn lane of a four lane, two way street with a single northbound lane. V2 began a left turn into the intersection as V1 was going straight and the front of V1 impacted the left side of V2. V1 was directed to the left post impact and came to final rest facing southwest in the middle of the intersection. V2 was directed to the right and came to final rest facing southwest also in the middle of the intersection. Both vehicles were damaged and had to be towed. The driver of V1 is the case occupant and the only person in V1. This case driver is a 49 year old male who was wearing a lap/shoulder belt with the vehicle equipped with a handicap seat and foot controls. The air bag was removed with a replacement steering rim. The case driver was transported to a local hospital and then transferred to the trauma center with serious injuries.
