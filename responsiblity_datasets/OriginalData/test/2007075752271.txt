This two-vehicle crash occurred on an undivided roadway. This roadway curves to the left in the precrash area and has a slight downhill grade. This roadway consists of one lane in each direction with parking lanes on both sides. The speed limit on this roadway is 40 kmph (25 mph). It was dark at the time of the crash with no adverse atmospheric conditions.

Vehicle one (V1), a 2000 Nissan Maxima, was northbound in lane one. Vehicle two (V2), a 2005 Chevrolet G2500 van, was legally parked (unoccupied) on the right (east) side of the road. V1 traveled to the right and the front struck the back of V2. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 32 kmph for V1 and 32 kmph for V2.

The driver of V1, a thirty-seven-year-old female, was not interviewed. The police report indicates that this driver was under the influence of drugs.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: this vehicle traveling; over the lane line on right side of travel lane. The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as a driver related factor: type of driver error unknown.
