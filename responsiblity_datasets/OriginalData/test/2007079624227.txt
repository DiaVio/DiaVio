This single vehicle daytime collision occurred a few hours before noon The collision occurred on the eastbound lanes of an east/west state highway. The eastbound lanes are divided, dry, level, concrete, and consist of five lanes. The number five lane is a high occupancy vehicle (HOV) lane. The HOV lanes are separated from the other lanes by double-yellow solid lines. South of the south edge exists an asphalt shoulder. North of the north edge exists a north median. North of the median exists a concrete center divider that separates the westbound and eastbound lanes. The highway is a 104 kmph (65 mph) zone

Vehicle one (V1) is a 1990 Honda Civic, four-door sedan, driven by a fifty seven (57) year old male who was traveling eastbound in the number three lane. As V1 was traveling, the driver decided to change lanes to the left. A non-contact vehicle approached from the number four lane as V1 attempted the lane change. The driver of V1 swerved back to the right into his lane to avoid contact. The driver then steered left, lost control, and traveled towards the concrete center divider. The front plane of V1 contacted the center divider as it rotated clockwise. The left plane of V1 contacted the center divider as it traveled towards its final rest. V1 came to rest facing southeast blocking the number five lane. V1 suffered major damage and was towed from the scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is 105 kmph for V1.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: this vehicle traveling, off the edge of the road on the left side. The critical reason was assigned to V1 as a driver related factor: overcompensation. The driver takes the medications Hydrochlorothiazide, uses an asthma inhaler and Vicodin for the conditions: peripheral vascular disease, asthma, and high cholesterol. He also attends therapy twice a week as a disabled vetran. He was coming from a store going to the hospital for a medical appointment and was not in a hurry.
