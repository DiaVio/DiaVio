This two-vehicle crash occurred in a four-legged, traffic signal-controlled intersection. This roadway is undivided, straight and was covered in slush at the time of the crash. The speed limit for this roadway is 56 kmph (35 mph). The southbound approach is level and consists of two through lanes and a left turn lane. The northbound approach has an uphill grade and consists of one through lane, a left turn lane and a right turn lane. It was dark with streetlights on at the time of the crash with no adverse atmospheric conditions.

Vehicle one (V1), a 1990 Oldsmobile Cutlass Calais, was southbound in lane three. Vehicle two (V2), a 1996 Infiniti I30, was northbound in lane two. V1 turned left in the intersection and the front struck the front of V2. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The driver of V1, a nineteen-year-old male, was on his way home from work. He was healthy, familiar with his vehicle and travels this route daily. He had one year of driving experience at the time of the crash. This driver had taken Percocet within the past twenty-four hours. He had slept for five-and-a-half hours the previous day and awakened eighteen hours prior to the crash. He had worked for seven-and-a-half hours and stated that he felt fatigued at the time of the crash. He further stated that he saw V2 as it approached the intersection but thought he had time to make his turn.

The driver of V2, a thirty-year-old male, was on his way to work from his home. He was healthy, well rested and familiar with his vehicle. He travels this route daily. This driver stated that he saw V1 but assumed that he would stop and allow V2 to pass through the intersection before making the turn.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "this vehicle traveling; turning left at intersection." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "driver related factor; misjudgment of gap or other's speed." This driver's fatigue and inexperience were associated factors in this crash.

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded as "other vehicle encroachment; from opposite direction - over left lane line." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V2 was "not coded to this vehicle." This driver's speed was an associated factor in this crash.
