This two-vehicle crash occurred in a four-way, traffic signal-controlled intersection. On the north and south sides of the intersection, the roadway is undivided and the northbound and southbound traffic lanes consist of a right turn lane, two through lanes, and a left turn lane in each direction. This roadway is straight, uphill in the northbound direction, and has a posted speed limit of 64 kmph (40 mph). This crash occurred during afternoon daylight hours with moderate traffic flow.

Vehicle one (V1), a 2004 Chrysler Sebring with one occupant, was northbound in lane four, attempting to turn left at the intersection. Vehicle two (V2), A 1996 Jeep Cherokee with one occupant, was southbound in lane two, approaching the intersection. The driver of V1 did not see V2 enter the intersection. Even though the driver of V2 applied the brakes and steered right to avoid a collision, the front of V1 impacted the front of V2. V1 rotated counterclockwise and came to rest facing west-northwest in the middle of the intersection. V2 came to a controlled stop near the point of impact, facing south-southwest in the middle of the intersection. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Neither driver was injured. Police Reported Travel Speed is 56 kmph for V1 and 56 kmph for V2.

The driver of V1, a forty-three-year-old female, was driving from her house to the store. She reported being well rested, in good health, and drives this route about three times a week. She feels very comfortable with her car. She offered no explanation for the crash except that she didn't see V2. The driver stated she had been stopped for 5-10 seconds before proceeding with the left turn.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: this vehicle traveling, turning left at intersection. The critical reason for the critical event for V1 was coded as a driver related factor: inadequate surveillance (e.g., failed to look, looked but did not see).

The driver of V2, a twenty-year-old female, was driving from the gym to her home. She was well rested, in good health, and very comfortable with her car. She saw V1 as she approached the intersection. The driver steered right and applied the brakes but could not avoid the crash.

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded: other vehicle encroachment; from opposite direction - over left lane line. The critical reason for the critical event was not coded to this vehicle.
