The crash occurred in the middle of a two-lane intersection controlled by a stop sign. Conditions were daylight in the afternoon on a weekday. The posted speed limit was 56 kmph (35 mph).

V1 was a 1995 Dodge Neon sedan traveling eastbound in the 1st travel lane approaching an intersection controlled by a traffic control signal intending on going straight. V2, a 2003 Chevy Impala sedan, was traveling northbound in the 1st travel lane approaching the intersection intending on going straight. The front of V1 struck the left side of V2 in the eastbound lane. As a result of the impact, V2 swerved right and departed the roadway. The front of V2 struck a fence then continued on and struck concrete steps. V1 also departed the roadway and drove over the struck fence. Both vehicles came to final rest facing northeast. The driver of V1 fled the scene on foot. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

An unidentified person drove V1. Authorities could not find the driver of V1. V1 was towed due to damage. The critical precrash event for V1 was this vehicle crossing over the intersection. The critical reason for the critical precrash event was unknown.

V2 was driven by a 23-year-old male who was not injured. He stated that he was traveling northbound when the other vehicle drove into the left side of his vehicle. The vehicle then swerved towards the right, departed the roadway and struck a fence and concrete steps. The driver of V2 did not take any avoidance actions and was traveling between 50-64 kmph (31-40 mph) at the time of impact. V2 was towed due to damage.

The critical precrash event for V2 was other vehicle encroachment from crossing street, across path. No critical reason for the critical precrash event was coded to this driver.
