This single-vehicle crash occurred on a median-divided, straight trafficway. The northbound lanes are level and consist of two through lanes. There is a concrete ditch in the median that is parallel to the travel lanes. The speed limit for this roadway is 89 kmph (55 mph). It was dusk at the time of the crash and it was raining.

Vehicle one (V1), a 2003 Jeep Cherokee, was northbound in lane one. V1 traveled off the right side of the road. The driver steered left and V1 returned to the road and into the median, rotating counterclockwise. The right wheels struck the ditch. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 89 kmph for V1.

The driver of V1, a twenty-year-old male, was on his way home from work. He was healthy and familiar with his vehicle. He travels this route daily. This driver stated that he fell asleep before he traveled off the right side of the road. This driver had only three-and-a-half hours of sleep the night before the crash. He stated that he works twelve-hour shifts and was fatigued at the time of the crash. This driver also stated he was concerned about his child who was ill.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "this vehicle traveling; off the edge of the road on the right side." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "driver related factor; overcompensation." An associated factor in this crash was the driver falling asleep while driving.
