This two-vehicle crash occurred on a jersey barrier-divided, straight trafficway. The westbound lanes consist of five lanes that have an uphill grade. The two left lanes were within a construction zone that was clearly marked with lighted signs and cones on the lane line. The speed limit for this roadway is 80 kmph (50 mph). At the time of this crash, it was dark with streetlights on with no adverse atmospheric conditions.

Vehicle one (V1), a 2006 Chevrolet Malibu, was westbound in lane three. Vehicle two (V2), a 2002 Ford F350 truck (a working vehicle), was facing westbound within the construction zone. V1 traveled to the left and the front struck the right side of V2. V1 continued to the west in the construction zone and the front struck two pedestrians (construction workers). V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The driver of V1, a thirty-eight-year-old female, was on her way home from helping her parents twenty-five miles east of the crash site. This driver stated that she was healthy, well rested and familiar with her vehicle. She travels this route daily. She had been awake for sixteen hours (after a seven-hour sleep) and had worked for eight-and-a-half hours before going to help her parents. This driver stated that she only remembers driving along after the beginning of the construction zone. This driver stated that she had one seizure in 1997. She does not remember this crash but denies having had a seizure. Her medical record indicates that this driver has a history of hypoglycemic seizures.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "this vehicle traveling; off the edge of the road on the left side." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "driver related factor; unknown critical non-performance."
