The crash occurred in the intersection of two roadways. Both were five-lane, two-way roadways with a posted speed of 72 kmph (45 mph). Both roadways were bituminous and level. One roadway was straight and had two eastbound lanes and two westbound lanes and a left-turn lane. The other roadway had two northbound lanes and two southbound lanes and a left-turn lane. The north leg of this roadway curved left. It was evening on a weekend and the roads were dry and the sky was clear. Traffic was flowing.

V1, a 2006 Dodge Caravan minivan with five occupants, was traveling westbound in lane three. V2, a 1999 Honda Accord was traveling southbound in lane three approaching the intersection. This intersection had a traffic light on colors. V1 approached the intersection and stopped for the red light. The driver stated that he was the third car back in the left-turn lane. V2 approached the intersection and attempted to turn left but stated that she accidentally applied the accelerator instead of the brake. The front of V2 contacted the front of V1. Both vehicles came to final rest on the east leg of the intersection. V1 was facing west and V2 was facing east. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

V1, the Dodge Caravan, was driven by a 37-year old male. He reported no injuries and refused treatment. He advised that he and the occupants had just left a soccer tournament and were on their way home. He stated that the occupants noticed their friends pull up in the car next to them. They were waving to their friends when they were struck by V2. They did not see V2 before impact.

The Critical Precrash Event for the driver of V1 was: "Other vehicle encroachment from opposite direction over left lane line". The Critical Reason was not coded to this vehicle. An associated factor was that they had an external distraction to the left.

V2, the Honda Accord was driven by a 41-year old female. She reported minor injuries and went per own vehicle to a local trauma facility. She advised that she had just left Meijer and was on her way home. She stated that at the time of impact she was traveling between 34-48 kmph (21-30 mph). She stated that she applied the brake but could not correct the loss of control.

The Critical Precrash Event for the driver of V2 was: "This vehicle traveling over the left lane line on the left side of travel. The Critical Reason for the Critical Precrash Event was a driver related factor: Poor directional control (e.g. failing to control vehicle with skill ordinarily expected. An associated factor was inexperienced driver; she was on a permit and had only been driving for 6 months.
