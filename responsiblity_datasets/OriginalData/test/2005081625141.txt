This two-vehicle crash occurred just past noon during daylight hours. The roadway is a seven lane, two way, undivided, level bituminous surface street with three lanes in each direction and a center turn only lane. The approach for both vehicles was a slight curve to the left. The weather was clear, and the street was dry. The speed limit for both vehicles is 80 kmph ( 50 mph)

Vehicle one (V1), a 1988 Volvo 740 series four door sedan driven by a 27 year-old female with two passengers, was traveling southbound in lane three. Vehicle two (V2), a 2000 Cadillac Catera four door sedan driven by a 69 year-old female, was traveling southbound in lane three ahead of V1. A vehicle in front of V2 made a sudden left turn causing V2 to brake and decelerate. V2 was still moving when it was struck in the rear by the front of V1. On impact V2 continued forward and stopped in the center turn lane facing south. V1 and V2 were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: other motor vehicle in lane, traveling in same direction while decelerating. The critical reason for the critical event was coded to V1 as a driver related factor: following too closely to respond to unexpected actions of others. An associative factor for V1 was an internal distraction. The police report states the driver was turned toward the rear seats distracted by the two passengers.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: other motor vehicle in lane, traveling in same direction with higher speed. The critical reason was not coded to this vehicle.
