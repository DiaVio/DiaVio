This single vehicle crash occurred on a three-lane asphalt constructed roadway. This roadway runs east to west with two lanes going west. The area of this crash has various curves and an uphill grade of +6 percent. Just before this crash the roadway curves left then straightens out before a right hand curve. This crash occurred during darkness with no street lighting and no adverse weather conditions.

Vehicle one, a 2000 Ford pickup truck was westbound rounding a left hand curve when a deer crossed the road from the right. The driver steered left to avoid. Vehicle one departed his travel lane, and the roadway to the left. The left front of vehicle one impacted a large rock. Vehicle two then impacted a fence with its front end. Vehicle one impacted a tree then went airborne and landed in a creek and did undercarriage damage. Vehicle one came to rest in the creek. Vehicle one was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 89 kmph for V1.

The driver of vehicle one, a forty-five year old male was not interviewed. The driver stated to police that this crash occurred around 05:00 a.m. and that he was not able to get out the vehicle for several hours because the doors were jammed. When he finally was able to get out (six hours later) he crawled up to the roadway where he flagged down a passing highway patrol officer.

The critical pre-crash event for vehicle one was coded as “object or animal: animal in roadway”. The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for vehicle one was coded as “driver related factor: type of driver error unknown”.
