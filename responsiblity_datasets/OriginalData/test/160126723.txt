Vehicle one (V1), a 2004 Mini Cooper 2-door, was traveling north in lane two of a five lane, two-way divided trafficway approaching an intersection. The roadway was wet but the weather conditions were dry. Vehicle two (V2), a 2000 Honda Accord 2-door, was traveling west in lane three of a seven lane, two-way divided trafficway approaching the same intersection. Both vehicles attempted to pass through the intersection and the front of V2 contacted the right side of V1. After impact, V1 skidded clockwise, coming to rest in the northwest corner of the intersection. After impact V2 also skidded clockwise, where it came to rest in the northwest corner of the intersection. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. The driver of V1 and the case study occupant was a 27-year-old male who was wearing a three-point lap and shoulder belt. The right side seat mounted and curtain airbags both deployed. The driver of V1 was transported to a level-one trauma center for treatment.
