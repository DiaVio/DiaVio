This case occupant is a restrained 15 year old female involved in a near-side impact crash with a large pickup truck and sustained severe injuries. This crash occurred at a traffic controlled intersection during daylight hours with overcast skies and dry conditions. Case vehicle one (V1), a 1997 Ford Explorer XLT, was eastbound on two lane, two way street and crossing straight through the intersection. Vehicle two (V2), a 2006 Dodge Ram Quad Cab Big Horn truck, was northbound on a three lane, two way street with a center left turn lane and was crossing straight through the intersection. In the intersection the front of V2 impacted the right side of V1. Both vehicles post impact rotated clockwise and slid laterally to the northeast corner of the intersection. V1 impacted the raised curb on the corner and then slid laterally on the sidewalk and began to tilt to the left as the top impacted a light pole. V1 then came to final rest facing southeast tilted against this light pole and the left side was not in contact with the ground. V2 came to final rest also facing southeast with the left side of V2 against the right side and front right tire of V1. Both vehicle were damage and towed. The case occupant is the front right passenger of V1 who was restrained with the manual lap/shoulder belt and the front instrument panel mounted air bag deployed. This case occupant sustained severe injuries and was transported to the trauma center. The driver of V1 was a 17 year old male who was wearing the manual lap/shoulder belt and the frontal steering column mounted air bag deployed. He sustained minor injuries and was transported to the trauma center. No other occupants were in V1.
