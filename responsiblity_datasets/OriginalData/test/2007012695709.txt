The crash occurred in the intersection of a five-lane roadway. There were two northbound lanes and two southbound lanes divided by a left-turn lane. This was a straight, level, bituminous roadway with a posted speed of 72 kmph (45 mph). It was early afternoon on a weekday and the road was dry and the sky was clear. Traffic was flowing.

V1, a 2004 Saturn Ion four-door with two occupants, was traveling southbound in lane three. V1 was attempting to turn left at the intersection. V2, a 1999 Oldsmobile Intrigue four-door with one occupant was traveling northbound in lane two. V2 was intending on going straight through the intersection. V1 pulled out into the intersection waiting for a clear chance to turn. The light had turned red for V1 and the driver of V1 stated that she did not want to be stuck in the intersection. The light was green for V2 when V1 turned left as V2 was coming through the intersection. The driver of V2 stated that she attempted to avoid V1 by applying the brakes and steering right. V2 was unable to avoid V1 and the front of V1 contacted the front of V2. V1 came to final rest at the northwest corner of the intersection. V2 continued to the northeast corner of the intersection where the undercarriage contacted the curb before coming to final rest. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

V1, the Saturn Ion was driven by a 19-year old female who was transported to a local trauma facility for her injuries. She advised that she had just left her house and was on her way to the store. She stated that she had been stopped at the intersection for more than ten seconds before proceeding at between 2-16 kmph (1-10 mph) when she was contacted by V2.

The Critical Precrash Event for the driver of V1 was This vehicle traveling: turning left at intersection. The Critical Reason for the Critical Precrash Event was a driver related error: misjudgment of gap or other's speed. Associated factors are that this was the first time she had driven this vehicle and was uncomfortable with it. She was not wearing her corrective lenses and she failed to stop for the traffic control device. She also proceeded with insufficient clearance. .

V2, the Oldsmobile Intrigue, was driven by a 17-year old female who was transported to a local trauma facility for her injuries. She advised that she had just left a job interview and was on her way to her boyfriend's house. She advised that she had been traveling between 50-64 kmph (31-40 mph) when she made the evasive action. She was unable to avoid V1.

The Critical Precrash Event for the driver of V2 was Other vehicle encroachment: from opposite direction over left lane line. The Critical Reason was not coded to this vehicle.

The witness was stopped at the light heading east. The witness stated that she was waiting for the light to turn green. A girl in a white car (V2) was coming through traveling north. There was a girl in the intersection also in a white car (V1) waiting to turn left. The driver of V1 had been waiting there for 30-40 seconds. Then all of a sudden the driver of V1 got nervous and turned left in front of V2. The witness thinks the driver of V1 turned because the light was turning red.
