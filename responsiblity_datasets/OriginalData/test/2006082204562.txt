This two vehicle collision occurred during morning daylight hours at the end of rush hour congestion. The crash occurred in lane two of a four lane interstate highway heading south. The road has four lanes of regular travel and has a right exit in the immediate vicinity of the crash. The lanes are paved with concrete and are straight, wet and level in the area of the crash. At the time of the crash there was a light drizzle, enough to wet the roadway but not enough to hamper vision. The speed limit for the road is 96 kmph (60 mph).

Vehicle one (V1), a 2000 Honda Civic driven by a 22 tear-old female, was traveling south in lane three. Vehicle two (V2), a 2006 Chevrolet Impala driven by a 75 year-old female, was heading south in lane two. V1 changed from lane one to lane two, and stabilized with lane two as its lane of travel. V2 was attempting to change lanes to lane three, and slowed to find a gap in traffic. V1 changed lanes from three to two. V1 braked with full lock-up and skidded into the rear of V2. Traffic was heavy in all lanes, with lane one fully blocked due to exiting traffic. Both vehicles were towed, but only V1 was fully disabled. Neither driver was transported due to injury. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: other motor vehicle in lane, traveling in same direction while decelerating. The critical reason for the critical event was assigned to this driver as a driver related factor: external distraction. The driver was looking for an opening in traffic to the left and right.

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded: other vehicle in lane, traveling in same direction with higher speed. The critical reason for the critical event was not coded to this vehicle.
