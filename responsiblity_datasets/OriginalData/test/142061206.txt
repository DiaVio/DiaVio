Vehicle #1 (case vehicle) was a 2001 Mazda Tribute, four-door compact utility vehicle. There was only one occupant in the case vehicle with the front left occupant (driver) being our case study participant: Driver - was a 28-year-old male/Redesigned frontal Air bag and front seat back mounted side air bag and a manual lap and shoulder belt were available/ Redesigned frontal air bag deployed (side seat back mounted did not deploy) and it does not appear the manual lap and shoulder belt was in use. This crash occurred in the early morning hours on a dark but lighted roadway. The roadway was snow covered and it was snowing at the time of the crash. This crash occurred on a two-lane roadway (one northbound lane with a parking lane and one southbound lane with a parking lane) with a level elevation. The involved roadway was of bituminous construction with speed limit of 40 km/h (25 mph). Vehicle #1 (case vehicle) was initially traveling northbound (while being chased by an unknown vehicle) when it traveled off the right roadway (beginning to slide) and impacted a building with its front bumper. After the initial impact, V1 rotated clockwise before traveling to its final resting position in the middle of the northbound sidewalk facing southwest. The driver (case occupant) of V1 was transferred from the scene to a local trauma center where he was hospitalized for 3 days for treatment of his injuries.
