This two-vehicle crash occurred at the intersection of a five lane median divided roadway that curves to the left when heading south. The intersecting roadway is a two-lane roadway. There are paved turn lanes through the median. This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions at the time of the crash. The speed limit is 89 kmph (55 mph).

Vehicle one (V1), a 1991 Buick Century Custom sedan was traveling northbound when it entered the turn lane. V1 stopped for the traffic control device for 5-10 seconds before crossing the intersection. Vehicle two (V2), a 2000 Dodge Durango (4 x 2) sport utility vehicle was traveling southbound; negotiating a slight curve to the left in lane one. As V1 entered the intersection, the front of V2 impacted the right side of V1. Post-impact, V1 rotated counterclockwise and the vehicles side slapped. Both vehicles then departed the left side of the roadway coming to rest facing southwest. Both vehicles were towed from the scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is Unknown for V1 and Unknown for V2.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: this vehicle traveling: crossing over [passing through] intersection. The critical reason for the crash was assigned to this driver and was coded as a driver related factor: false assumption of other's actions. The driver of V1 stated that he thought V2 was going to exit right to another roadway rather than continue its path through the intersection. He also said that the driver of V2 must have changed his mind and pulled from the exit lane to lane one at the last moment. He said he believed it was safe to cross the intersection because V2 was exiting right. The driver of V1 was a 51-year-old male who was unlicensed with over 30 years of driving experience. He was comfortable with the vehicle and drives the roadway at least 5 times weekly.

The driver of V2 was a 27-year-old male. He is a mechanic at a nearby auto repair shop. He was test-driving the vehicle because its transmission had just been repaired. This was his first time driving the vehicle. The driver of V2 stated that he had been on this trip for 3 to 5 minutes. While this driver admitted that he was unfamiliar with this particular vehicle, he has 11 years of experience driving this class of vehicle. The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded: other motor vehicle encroachment; from crossing street, across path. The critical reason was not coded to this vehicle.
