This single vehicle crash occurred in the morning hours on a weekday near the intersection of a north/south five lane raised median divided roadway and an east/west two lane two way roadway. The intersection is controlled by tri-color traffic control signals. The bituminous roadway is straight, level, and wet at the time of the crash. The speed limit on the roadway is 56 KPH (35 MPH). The weather condition at the time was cloudy, and traffic was light.

V1, a 1992 Pontiac Bonneville 4-door, was northbound in the first lane approaching the intersection. Per the statement given by the witness, who was stopped in the southbound left turn lane, V1 approached the intersection, crossed it, then veered to the right. V1 jumped the curb and struck a light pole with it's front end. V1 then rolled left two quarter turns and came to final rest on it's roof across the first northbound lane just north of the intersection, facing north east. Per the witness, two young females exited V1 and left the scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

The Critical Pre-crash Event for V1 is “This vehicle traveling, off the edge of the road on the right side”. The Critical Reason for the Critical Pre-crash Event is: Unknown Reason, Unknown reason for critical event.
