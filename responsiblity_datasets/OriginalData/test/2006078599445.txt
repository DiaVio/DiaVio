This is a two-vehicle, intersection, T-Bone type crash that occurred during late afternoon hours. The weather was clear with no adverse conditions. The road surface was level, dry asphalt. The East/West Street west of the intersection is a three lane, two-way roadway with no traffic controls. The North/South Avenue is a three lane, two way roadway controlled by stop signs and operating, flashing, red lights. Posted speed limit for east/west roadway 56 kmph (35 mph) and for the north/south roadway 80 kmph (50 mph).

Vehicle one (V1), a 2001 Toyota Corolla 4 door sedan driven by a 17 year-old female with two passengers, was traveling in the number one eastbound lane. V1 entered the intersection and saw vehicle two (V2) entering the intersection from the right. V2, a 1997 Dodge Intrepid 4 door sedan driven by a 41 year-old female with one passenger, was traveling north in the number one lane. V2 entered the intersection across the path of V1. The driver of V1 attempted to avoid the crash by hard application of the brakes causing lockup. The front of V1 struck the left side of V2 just behind the driver door. The impact caused V1 to rotate counterclockwise nearly 180 degrees. The final rest position of V1 was on it's wheels, facing generally west, on a painted divider north of the #1 lane eastbound, and east of the intersection. V2 after impact, rotated counterclockwise and came to final rest in the northeast quadrant of the intersection, on it's wheels, facing generally northwest. Both vehicles were towed from the scene. The drivers and passengers were all transported to a local medical facility. Police Reported Travel Speed is 80 kmph for V1 and 80 kmph for V2.

The driver of V1 was a 17-year-old female. There were two additional occupants in the vehicle of 16 years in age. The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: other vehicle encroachment; from crossing street, across path. The critical reason was not coded to this vehicle.

The driver of V2 was a 41-year-old female. There was one occupant in the vehicle who was a 35-year-old male. The driver & passenger were going from a friends house and on a trip to her home in an adjacent state. The driver was not ill or fatigued and takes the medication Clondine. The driver rarely drives this roadway, and looked left towards V1, but did not see V1 before proceeding into the intersection after stopping at the stop sign. There were no sight line restrictions for either driver at this intersection. The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded: this vehicle traveling; crossing over (passing through) intersection. The critical reason was coded to V2 as a driver related factor: inadequate surveillance (e.g. failed to look, looked but did not see). The driver of V2 driver stated she doesn't remember seeing the other vehicle.
