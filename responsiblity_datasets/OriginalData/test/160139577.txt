This case contains three case study occupants. A 41-year-old driver of a 2006 Mini Cooper coupe was properly wearing a lap and shoulder belt with frontal airbag deployment involved in a frontal, a 11-year-old male right front seat occupant wearing a lap and shoulder belt with frontal airbag deployment and a 3-year old female left rear occupant in a booster seat. Vehicle one (V1), a 2006 Mini Cooper coupe, was traveling south in lane one of a two lane, two-way roadway under dry, daylight conditions. V1 had just negotiated a curve to the left and for unknown reasons went off the roadway to the left where it struck a tree with its front. V1 came to rest off the roadway to the east and was towed due to damage. All three occupants in the vehicle are case study occupants. The 41-year-old driver was properly wearing the available three-point lap and shoulder belt and the steering wheel mounted frontal airbag deployed. The 11-year-old male right front seat occupant was also properly wearing the three-point lap and shoulder belt and the instrument panel mounted frontal airbag deployed. The 3-year old female left rear seat occupant in a booster seat and was restrained by a three-point lap and shoulder belt. All three occupants were transported to a level-one trauma center for treatment.
