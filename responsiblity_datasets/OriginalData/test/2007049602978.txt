This single-vehicle crash occurred on a jersey barrier-divided, straight trafficway. The westbound lanes have a downhill grade and consist of five through lanes with lane five designated as a high occupancy vehicle (HOV) lane. The speed limit on this roadway is 97 kmph (60 mph). It was dark with streetlights on at the time of the crash, with no adverse atmospheric conditions.

Vehicle one (V1), a 2002 Toyota Camry, was westbound in lane five. The driver of V1 steered left to avoid a box on the road and the front of V1 struck the median jersey barrier. V1 then traveled to the north and rolled over to the left two quarter-turns. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is No PAR obtained (created) for V1.

The driver of V1, a twenty-seven-year-old female, was on her way home from work with her twenty-year-old female coworker. This driver was healthy and comfortable with her vehicle and passenger. This driver and passenger travel this route daily. The tires on V1 were mismatched; the left rear wheel was a fifteen-inch wheel and the rest were eighteen-inch wheels. This driver normally works eight hours each day, but had worked twelve and one-half hours on the day of the crash. She had consumed energy drinks during the day "to stay awake." This driver was talking to her passenger just prior to the crash.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "object or animal; object in roadway." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "driver related factor; overcompensation." Associated factors were the mismatched tires, the driver's fatigue and this driver's distraction caused by conversing with her passenger.
