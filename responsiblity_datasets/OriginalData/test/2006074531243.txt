This single-vehicle crash occurred near the intersection of two straight, undivided, two-lane, level, gravel roadways. The speed limit on these roadways is 56 kmph (35 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.

Vehicle one (V1), a 1995 Oldsmobile Silhouette, was eastbound in lane one. V1 turned left at the intersection and the driver lost control. V1 rotated counterclockwise, then clockwise and then counterclockwise again. V1 traveled off the left side of the road and the front struck an embankment and a tree stump. V1 then rolled over to the right, coming to rest on its top. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

The driver of V1, a thirty-nine-year-old male, was traveling from a lake to his home. He stated that he had been drinking beer for more than four hours while at the lake. This driver stated that he had also smoked marijuana during the previous twenty-four hours. He further stated that he has been diagnosed with bipolar disorder and schizophrenia but does not take his medication because he does not like them. He also stated that he had just gotten out of a mental hospital and had not slept during the five days that he was there. He also indicated that he has personal issues with his living conditions because he lives with his mother and stepfather. This driver stated that he was traveling too fast for conditions. He further stated that he had recently replaced the right front tire of his vehicle with a different size tire and has felt "a slight pull towards the right" since that time. This driver travels this route weekly.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "this vehicle control loss; traveling too fast for conditions." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as " driver related factor; too fast for curve/turn." The driver's inappropriate choice of evasive actions is an associated factor.
