This single vehicle crash occurred on a six lane divided roadway when driver attempted an abrupt right turn into the parking lot of a private business establishment. Road surface dry, concrete pre-impact roadway had a 3% slope and leveled off prior to vehicle departing roadway on right side. This crash occurred mid-afternoon during daylight hours, posted speed limit 64 kmph (40 mph).

Vehicle one (V1), a 2001 Chrysler Town and Country mini van driven by a 18 year-old male with one passenger, was traveling north in lane two when the front passenger informed the driver that one of their friends was waving at them from the side of the road to the right . Driver noticed their friend on side of roadway and attempted to make a sudden right turn into a parking lot. Unfortunately, the speed at which the vehicle was traveling, distance to the driveway, and size and handling capabilities of vehicle caused driver to loss control and strike brick retaining wall and then rolling left. V1 was towed from the scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: this vehicle traveling, turning right at intersection. The critical reason for the critical event was coded as a driver related factor: too fast for curve/turn.
