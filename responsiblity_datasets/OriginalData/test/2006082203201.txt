This two-vehicle collision occurred on a dry, straight, bituminous, six-lane, two-way roadway that was level for northbound traffic in the approach area and a downhill grade for southbound traffic. The roadway has a posted speed limit of 48 kmph (30 mph). At the time of this crash, it was daylight and there were no adverse weather conditions, sight restrictions or road defects in the crash area.

Vehicle one (V1), a 2004 Kia Spectra was northbound in the second lane. Vehicle two (V2), a 2001 GMC Yukon was southbound in the first lane after entering the road from an interstate highway off ramp. V1 departed all the lanes to the left and the front of V1 impacted the front of V2. V1 and V2 were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The driver of V1, an eighteen-year-old male refused to be interviewed. This driver was in a stolen vehicle and was being pursued by police.

The driver of V2, a fifty-six year old female was heading home. It is unknown how long this driver had been driving on this trip. This driver drives this vehicle daily and over this road on a weekly basis.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: this vehicle traveling, over the lane line on left side of travel lane. The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event was attributed to V1 and was coded as a driver related factor: aggressive driving behavior.

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded: other vehicle encroachment; from opposite direction-over left lane line. The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event was not coded to this vehicle.
