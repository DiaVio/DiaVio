This near-side crash case involves a 60 year old restrained male seated in the front left seating position (Driver) with no deployed air bags who sustained severe injuries. The crash occurred in the morning hours in dry and clear weather. The collision occurred within the confines of a T-intersection with one northbound lane (curved to right on the south side of the intersection) and two southbound lanes (with one left turn lane). The north and south travel lanes are separated by a double yellow line. This road is intersected by a level, two lane asphalt road with one westbound lane and one eastbound lane. This intersection is controlled by a stop sign for the westbound travel lane only. The case vehicle (V1) a 2005 four-door Ford Five Hundred was being driven westbound and Vehicle #2 (V2), a 2006 four-door Ford F150 Extended cab pickup(4X4) was being driven northbound The crash occurred when V1 entered the T-intersection (westbound) in an attempt to execute a left turn in order to travel south. V2 had just exited a right curve when the front of its vehicle impacted the left side of V1. After initial impact, V1 and V2 side-slapped and then rotated to their final resting position. V1 and V2 were both towed from the scene due to damage sustained in the crash. V1 was driven by the 60 year old male case occupant who was restrained by the available 3-point manual belt (retractor type pretensioner actuated). The available steering wheel mounted, Seat back mounted and side curtain Air bad did not deploy as a result of the impact. This case occupants seat was positioned between middle and rear most and the seat back was in the slightly reclines position. The Case occupant was severely injured and transported to local Trauma Center by ground ambulance for treatment of his injuries.
