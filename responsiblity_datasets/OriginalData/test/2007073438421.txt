This two-vehicle crash occurred in a four-legged intersection. The east/west trafficway is straight, level and divided by a curbed median. In both directions, the approach roadway consists of two through lanes and a left turn lane. The speed limit for this roadway is 56 kmph (35 mph). It was dark with streetlights on at the time of the crash with no adverse atmospheric conditions.

Vehicle one (V1), a 2007 Toyota Camry, was westbound in lane three. Vehicle two (V2), a 1993 Cadillac Eldorado, was eastbound in lane two. V1 turned left in the intersection and the right side was struck by the front of V2. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The driver of V1, a thirty-nine-year-old female, was traveling from her home to a friend's house. She was healthy, well rested and familiar with her vehicle. She travels this route daily. This driver stated that she did not see V2 prior to the crash. According to the police report, this driver was impaired by alcohol.

The driver of V2, a thirty-six-year-old male, was not interviewed. According to his medical record, this driver tested positive for opiates and THC. No further information is available for this driver.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "this vehicle traveling; turning left at intersection." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "driver related factor; inadequate surveillance (e.g. failed to look, looked but did not see)."

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded as "other vehicle encroachment; from opposite direction - over left lane line." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V2 was "not coded to this vehicle."
