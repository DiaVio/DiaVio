The crash occurred on the west roadside of a southbound, rural roadway. There was a north and a southbound lane with a small ditch off the west side of the roadway. The roadway had a posted speed limit of 88 kmph (55 mph) and a level grade. Conditions were daylight and dry with clear skies. The setting was late afternoon hours on a weekday.

Vehicle one was a 1997 Ford Explorer sport utility traveling south in lane one. V1 lost control and crossed the center line. It then began rotating clockwise. During this rotation the left rear tire blew out causing the left rear wheel to gouge the roadway. V1 continued off the right side of the road and rolled to the left three quarter turns. V1 then came to rest in the west roadside. Police Reported Travel Speed is 97 kmph for V1.

The Ford Explorer was driven by a 25-year old female who was transported to a local trauma center with non-incapacitating injuries. She stated that she had been drinking prior to the crash. She was heading from home to her Dad's in a vehicle borrowed from her mother. The driver was reaching into her purse when the vehicle lost control. The driver steered both ways but was unable to regain control of the vehicle. The Ford sport utility was towed due to damage.

The Critical Precrash Event for v1 was this vehicle control loss due to traveling too fast for conditions. The Critical Reason was a driver related factor of internal distraction. This driver was under the influence of alcohol. She was wearing corrective lenses for a nearsighted condition. She was unemployed at the time. She was not comfortable with this vehicle due to its alignment problems. Her right hand was reaching in her purse prior to the crash.
