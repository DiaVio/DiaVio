This two-vehicle crash occurred mid-afternoon. The roadway a divided six lane the surface is concrete dry and free of defects. There were no adverse atmospheric conditions at that time. The crash occurred near a signalized intersection. The posted speed limit 64 kmph (40 mph).

Vehicle one (V1), a 2005 Ford Focus sedan driven by a 34 year-old male, was traveling westbound in the second lane of a three-lane, two-way, divided, concrete roadway without a positive barrier. Vehicle two (V2), a 1999 Cadillac Catera sedan driven by a 29 year-old female with one passenger, was stopped facing westbound in the second lane. The driver of V1 stated he had a seizure and lost consciousness prior to impact. The front of V1 impacted the rear of V2. V1 rotated clockwise and came to rest on the median, facing northeast. V2 was pushed northwest and came to rest in the intersection, facing northwest. V1 and V2 were towed from the scene due to damage sustained in the collision. The driver of V1 was treated at the scene, but was not transported to the hospital for treatment. The 34-year-old male driver of V1 was well rested and familiar with area. There was no indication of drug or alcohol use as a factor for this driver. He stated that he lost consciousness prior to the accident and has no recall of the crash events. He received a citation for no driver's license. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: other motor vehicle in lane, other vehicle stopped. The critical reason for the critical event was coded to V1 as a driver related factor: heart attack or other physical impairment of the ability to act. (epilepsy seizure).

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded: other motor vehicle in lane, traveling in same direction with higher speed. Critical reason not coded to this vehicle.
