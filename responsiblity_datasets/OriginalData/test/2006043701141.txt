The crash occurred on the north side of a two-way, two-lane, rural roadway. The eastbound lane curved left before its approach to a four-way intersection. The roadway had a posted speed limit of 72 kmph (45 mph) and a 2% downhill grade. There were also warning signs indicating road work and an intersection ahead. Recommended speed limit was 56 kmph (35 mph). Conditions were dark and dry with clear skies. The setting was late evening hours on a weeknight.

Vehicle one was a 2003 Ford ZX2 coupe negotiating a left curve while traveling east in lane one. V1 traveled partially off the roadway to the right, and then reentered the roadway abruptly. V1 rotated counterclockwise 90 degrees, traveling off the roadway to the left. The right plane of V1 struck two construction barrels. V1 continued eastbound striking a stop sign in an intersection median with its left plane. V1 continued further and struck a telephone pole with its left plane while in the northeast corner of the intersection. V1 came to rest facing south off the northeast corner of the intersection. Police Reported Travel Speed is 97 kmph for V1.

V1 was driven by a 22-year old male who fled the scene of the crash with no injuries. He stated that he was coming home from an oyster bar the day he had been fired from his job. He claimed a deer was in the roadway, forcing him to swerve right in avoidance. He estimated his travel speed to be 89 kmph (55 mph). The car was towed due to damage.

The Critical Precrash Event for V1 was this vehicle traveling off the edge of the road on the right side. The Critical Reason was a driver related factor of internal distraction. There were several associated factors present for this driver. The driver was adjusting the radio at the time of the crash. He was traveling above the posted speed limit. He was upset after being laid off from his job earlier in the day. He was taking the prescription medication for attention deficit disorder.
