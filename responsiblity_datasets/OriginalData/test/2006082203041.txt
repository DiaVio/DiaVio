This two vehicle daytime crash occurred in a 4-way intersection with no traffic controls. The intersecting roadways were dry, are two-way, have one travel lane in each direction, are straight, and have concrete construction. The eastbound roadway has an uphill grade, and the northbound roadway is level. At the time of the crash the sky was cloudy, and there were no sight restrictions or road defects.

Vehicle 1 (V1), a white 1987 Nissan Stanza 4-door, was traveling east on the roadway in lane one. Vehicle 2 (V2), a 2003 Ford Focus sedan with one passenger, was traveling north in lane one. V1 entered the intersection first. The front of V2 hit the front right side of V1 causing it to rotate counterclockwise. The right side of V1's rear bumper then side slapped V2s left rear door. Both vehicles came to rest at the northeast corner of the intersection; V1 was facing northeast, and V2 was facing generally east. V1 and V2 were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The V1 driver, a 31-year old male, stated that he was eating popcorn as he was driving. He failed to yield the right of way at the intersection with no traffic controls. He is familiar with the roadway and vehicle. The Critical Pre-crash Event for V1 was coded as: This Vehicle Traveling - Crossing over/passing through the intersection. The Critical Reason for the Critical Event was coded to the V1 driver as a: Driver Related Factor - Inadequate surveillance.

The V2 driver, an 18-year-old female, stated that she was looking downward at her cell phone as she approached the intersection. The driver also stated she was late for work. The Critical Pre-crash Event was coded as: Other Vehicle Encroachment - From crossing street, across path. The Critical Reason for the Critical Event was not coded to V2.
