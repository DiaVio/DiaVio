This two-vehicle crash occurred in a four-legged, traffic signal-controlled intersection. The east/west roadway is undivided, straight, level and consists of two lanes in each direction. The speed limit for this roadway is 56 kmph (35 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions. Traffic was congested at the time of the crash.

Vehicle one (V1), a 2006 Toyota Corolla, was stopped westbound in lane two. Vehicle two (V2), a 2000 Chevrolet Cavalier, was eastbound in lane one. A non-contact vehicle was stopped in eastbound lane two waiting to turn left. V1 turned left in the intersection and the right side was struck by the front of V2. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The driver of V1, a nineteen-year-old female, was traveling with her sixteen-year-old sister, from a restaurant to their home. She was healthy, well rested and comfortable with her vehicle and passenger. She travels this route weekly. This driver's father stated that this driver waited but turned left even though she could not see if there was any traffic in eastbound in lane one.

The driver of V2, a twenty-four-year-old female, was not interviewed. No further information is available for this driver.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "this vehicle traveling; turning left at intersection." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as " driver related factor; turned with obstructed view."

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded as "other vehicle encroachment; from opposite direction - over left lane line." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V2 was "not coded to this vehicle."
