The crash happened during early morning hours involving a passenger car on a divided roadway with a raised median. The speed limit for the roadway is 72 kmph (45 mph) The road surface concrete dry and there was no detrimental atmospheric conditions at the time of the crash. The roadway alignment prior to the crash site curves right.

The only vehicle in this crash, a 1994 Buick LeSabre, was traveling east in the third lane of a three lane divided (no positive barrier) roadway negotiating a decreasing right curve. The vehicle drove off the road to its left. Its front impacted a traffic impact attenuator in front of a bridge pillar, located on the raised median of the roadway. It then slightly rotated clockwise around the attenuator. The impact attenuator consisted of a collapsible guardrail cushion. The bottom was attached to a rail on the ground. The guard rails were all pushed backwards 2.4 meters. The vehicle came to rest on the median. The vehicle was towed from the scene. The driver was not injured. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

The driver was a 25-year old male. The driver intended to go straight. He stated he lost control after falling to sleep. He did not seem fatigued at the time of the interview taken less than 30 minutes after the crash was reported. He stated he was working 8 hours every night except on Monday to Tuesday nights. He could not exactly point out his sleep schedule, but said he would only get four to five hours of sleep each day during the daytime. He left his job site less than 15 minutes prior to the crash. His job site is located about 11.2 kilometers (7 miles) from the crash site.

The critical pre-crash event was coded: this vehicle traveling, off edge of road on the left. The critical reason was coded as a driver related factor: sleeping, that is actually asleep.
