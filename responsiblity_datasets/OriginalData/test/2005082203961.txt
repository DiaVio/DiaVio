This two vehicle daytime collision occurred at in the afternoon daylight hours. The collision occurred in a controlled intersection with a traffic control light and a blinking yellow light for the left turn lane. The pre-crash roadway for both vehicles straight and level,dry paved with bituminous. There is a posted speed limit of 56 kmph (35 mph) for both vehicles.

Vehicle one (V 1), a 2005 Mercedes 230 sedan, driven by a 19 year-old female with one passenger was traveling southbound on the roadway in lane 2 before getting into the left hand turn lane, with the intent on turning left at the intersection. Vehicle two (V2), a 1988 Buick Electra Park Avenue sedan, driven by a 81 year-old female was traveling northbound in lane 2. V1 had yielded for a moment then decided she had enough time to make the left hand turn. V2 assumed V1 would yield to the right of way but realized V1 continued with the left hand turn. The front of V1 hit the left front of V2, causing both cars to deflect and come to rest on the north/east corner of the intersection. Both vehicles towed due to damage. Driver of V2 stated she thought V1 was following the car that had left turned before V1 turned. She stated that she had locked up her brakes and tried to avoid the collision but there was not enough time. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: this vehicle traveling, turning Left at Intersection. The critical reason for the critical event was coded to V1 as a driver related factor: misjudgment of gap or others speed . An additional factor was this driver being an inexperienced driver.

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded: other motor vehicle encroachment; from opposite direction, over left lane line . The critical reason for the critical event is not coded to this vehicle
