This single vehicle, weekday, early evening crash occurred off the right side of a ice covered, straight, 3 lane, 2 way, bituminous roadway. At the time of the crash it was dark and snowing. The posted speed limit was 80 kmph (50 mph) and the roadway had a negative slope >2% in the northbound direction.

V1, a 1992 Chevrolet Lumina solely occupied by the 30-year-old male driver, was westbound in lane one. The driver of V1 lost control and V1 slid off the road to the right striking a utility pole with it's front. V1 came to rest against the pole facing northwest. The driver was injured and transported. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

The driver of V1 stated that he had been arguing with his girlfriend at their house and had left to go to his sister's who did not live very far away. He had only driven about a mile, was up near the posted speed, and then does not remember anything until after the vehicle had come to rest following the crash. He took no avoidance actions and does not remember leaving the roadway.

The Critical Precrash Event for V1 was coded this vehicle traveling, off the edge of the road on the right side. The Critical Reason for the Critical Precrash Event was a decision error of driving too fast for conditions. Associated factors include the weather, blowing snow and the icy conditions. The driver was also consuming alcohol while driving and losing consciousness due to fatigue. The driver also admitted to thinking about personal issues and stress caused from work. The driver was also not wearing his corrective lenses that are required for driving. The driver was also operating the vehicle on a suspended license.
