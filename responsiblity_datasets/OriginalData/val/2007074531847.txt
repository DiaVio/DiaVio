This two-vehicle crash occurred in a four-legged, traffic signal-controlled intersection. The north/south trafficway is straight and divided by a curbed median. This trafficway consists of two lanes in each direction with a left turn lane at the intersection. The northbound lanes have an uphill grade and the southbound lanes have a downhill grade. The speed limit for this trafficway is 64 kmph (40 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.

Vehicle one (V1), a 1996 GMC Yukon, was northbound in lane one. Vehicle two (V2), a 2000 Jeep Cherokee, was stopped southbound in lane three. V2 entered the intersection turning left and the right side was struck by the front of V1. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The driver of V1, a sixty-eight-year-old female, was on her way home from work. She was healthy, well rested and familiar with her vehicle. She travels this route daily. This driver stated that she saw V2 and assumed that the driver would wait until she had cleared the intersection. When she realized that V2 was turning, this driver steered to the left and applied the brakes.

The driver of V2, a fifteen-year-old female, was traveling with her forty-year-old mother from their home to a store. This driver was healthy and well rested. This was the first time this driver had traveled on this road and she had driven this vehicle less than twice in the past three months. She had twelve months of driving experience. This driver stated that she saw V1 but thought she had time to make her turn.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "other vehicle encroachment; from opposite direction - over left lane line." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was "not coded to this vehicle."

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded as "this vehicle traveling; turning left at intersection." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded as "driver related factor; misjudgment of gap or other's speed." This driver's inexperience was an associated factor in this crash.
