This two-vehicle collision occurred at the intersection of two two-way city streets. The intersection only has a yield sign controlling the east-west traffic and both roads are straight, level and paved with worn concrete. Both roads have parking lanes in either direction. The speed limit for both roads is 48 kmph (30 mph). At the time of the crash the sky was cloudy and the road was wet.

Vehicle one (V1), a 2002 Jeep Liberty was eastbound in the first lane. Vehicle two (V2), a 2002 Hyundai Santa Fe was northbound in the first lane. Vehicle three (V3), a 2005 Kia Sorrento was legally parked facing northbound. The front of V1 impacted the right side of V2. V2 rotated clockwise and the right side of V2 impacted the rear of V3. V2 was towed due to disabling damage, but V1 and V3 were driven from the scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The driver of V1, a sixty-one year old female was coming from work and was heading to a meeting. This driver had been driving for about eighteen minutes at the time of the crash. This driver indicated that she checked the cross street to both the left and right, but did not see any other vehicles approaching. This driver was a taking multi-vitamin as a medication. This driver drives this vehicle daily and over this road on a rare basis.

The driver of V2, a twenty-two year old female was unable to be contacted after the crash for an interview.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: this vehicle traveling, crossing over [passing through] intersection. The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event was attributed to this vehicle and was coded as a driver related factor: inadequate surveillance.

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded: other vehicle encroachment; from crossing street, across path. The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event was not coded to this vehicle.
