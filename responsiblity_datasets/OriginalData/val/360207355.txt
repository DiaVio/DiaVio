This crash involved a 32 year old female in a severe frontal single event crash. She was restrained with a lap/shoulder belt and the pretensioners actuated and the front steering column airbag deployed. She sustained serious injuries and was transported to a local hospital before being transferred to the CIREN trauma center. This crash occurred in the evening in a dark area and the weather was clear and dry. Vehicle one (V1), a 2005 Chevrolet Trailblazer four door utility vehicle was traveling westbound on a three lane, two way street with a center lane for left turns. V1 drove off the roadway to the right and struck the curb leaving tire scuffs and continued on a side walk and then over a grassy level landscape before the full front of V1 impacted a brick/concrete base of a resident sign. V1 came to final rest against this object. V1 was towed due to damage. The driver of V1 is the case occupant who was a 32 year old female who was wearing a lap/shoulder belt with pretensioners actuating and the front steering column airbag deploying on impact. The driver was transported to a local hospital before being transferred to the trauma center with serious injuries. No other occupants were in V1.
