This two-vehicle crash occurred near an interchange on a jersey barrier-divided, straight trafficway. The northbound lanes are level and consist of four through lanes with paved shoulders on both sides. The speed limit for this roadway is 97 kmph (60 mph). It was dark with streetlights on at the time of the crash with no adverse atmospheric conditions.

Vehicle one (V1), a 1996 Chevrolet Geo Prizm, entered the northbound lanes from the entrance ramp and moved into northbound lane two. Vehicle two (V2), an unknown automobile, was northbound in an unknown lane. The front of V2 struck the back of V1. V1 rotated counterclockwise and traveled to the northwest. The back of V1 struck the jersey barrier. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The driver of V1, a twenty-two-year-old male, was on his way home from a friend's house, with a twenty-two-year-old female passenger. This driver was healthy, well rested and comfortable with his vehicle and passenger. He travels this route weekly. This driver stated that he was looking to the left to change lanes into lane three when his vehicle was struck by V2. He never saw V2 prior to the crash.

The driver of V2 immediately left the scene was not interviewed. No further information is available for this driver.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "other motor vehicle in lane; traveling in same direction with higher speed." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was "not coded to this vehicle."

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded as "other motor vehicle in lane; traveling in same direction with lower steady speed." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded as "driver related factor; type of driver error unknown."
