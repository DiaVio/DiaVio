This two-vehicle crash occurred in a four-legged, traffic signal-controlled intersection. The east/west roadway is straight, level and divided by a curbed median. The westbound lanes consist of three through lanes and a left turn lane. The north/south roadway is straight, level and undivided. In the northbound direction, this roadway consists of one through lane, one right turn lane and two left turn lanes. The speed limit for these roadways is 72 kmph (45 mph). It was dark with streetlights on at the time of the crash with no adverse atmospheric conditions.

Vehicle one (V1), a 2002 Isuzu Rodeo, was westbound in lane two. Vehicle two (V2), a 1999 Honda Civic, was northbound in lane two. In the intersection, the front of V1 struck the right side of V2. The vehicles rotated and the left side of V1 struck the right side of V2. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Unknown for V1 and Unknown for V2.

The driver of V1, a twenty-six-year-old male, was traveling with two passengers, a twenty-five-year-old male and a twenty-seven-year-old male. They were traveling from work to lunch at the time of the crash. This driver was healthy, well rested and comfortable with his vehicle and passengers. He travels this route daily. This driver stated that he did not notice that his traffic signal was red. When he saw V2 just a moment before the crash, he applied the brakes and steered right in an attempt to avoid the crash.

The driver of V2, a twenty-year-old female, was on the way from her home to a friend's house with a twenty-year-old female passenger. This driver was healthy, well rested and comfortable with her vehicle and passenger. She travels this route daily. This driver stated that she was stopped and then proceeded into the intersection when her signal turned green.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "this vehicle traveling; crossing over (passing through) intersection." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "driver related factor; inadequate surveillance (e.g. failed to look, looked but did not see)."

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded as "other vehicle encroachment; from crossing street, across path." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V2 was "not coded to this vehicle." This driver's failure to look ahead to the side was an associated factor in this crash.
