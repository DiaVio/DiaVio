This two vehicle crash occurred mid-afternoon during daylight hours on a five-lane, east-west roadway divided by a concrete traffic barrier. There were no traffic controls. The roadway was straight, level, concrete with a posted speed of 96 kmph (60 mph). The road was dry and the sky was clear. Traffic flow was heavy.

Vehicle one (V 1), a 1995 Ford Taurus sedan driven by a 18 year-old female was traveling westbound in lane one. Vehicle two (V 2), a 1992 Toyota Pickup driven by a 55 year-old male was traveling westbound in lane one ahead of V 1. V 2 was stopping for heavy traffic ahead and the front of V 1 struck the back of V 2. V 1 came to rest in the first travel lane at the point of impact. V 2 came to rest in the first travel lane at the point of impact. V 1 was towed from the scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: other motor vehicle in lane, traveling in the same direction while decelerating. The Critical Reason for the Critical Event was driver related recognition factor: internal distraction. The driver admitted that she was eating prior to the collision.

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded: other motor vehicle in lane, traveling in the same direction with higher speed. The Critical Reason for the Critical Event was not coded to this vehicle.
