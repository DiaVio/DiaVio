This crash occurred in the morning of a weekday in the westbound lane of a two-lane roadway. The east/west roadway consisted of one lane in each direction with a posted speed limit of 64 kmph (40 mph). The bituminous roadway was dry, straight, and level. The traffic flow was a bit congested but there were no adverse atmospheric conditions at the time of the crash.

V1, a 2005 Chevrolet Silverado 4-door with one occupant was westbound in lane one. As V1 was traveling towards an intersection, a non-contact vehicle was stopped ahead it waiting to turn left onto another roadway. The NCV began its turn but suddenly stopped. The driver of V1 swerved right to avoid striking V2 in the rear. V1 then departed the roadway and struck a telephone pole. V1 came to rest against the pole and was towed due to damage. Shortly after V1 came to rest against the pole and the driver had exited the vehicle the telephone broke in half and fell on a large dump truck, which was traveling behind V1. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

The driver of V1, a 23-year-old male was uninjured in the crash. He stated that he saw a non-contact vehicle ahead of him attempting to turn. As the vehicle began slowly turning it stopped. The driver said he did not want to strike the other vehicle so he steered right. He slammed on the brakes but it was not enough to stop his vehicle and he struck the pole.

The Critical Precrash Event for V1 was coded this vehicle traveling, off the edge of the road on the right side. The Critical Reason for the Critical Event was a decision error, incorrect evasive action. The only associated factor coded to V1 was an internal distraction as the driver admitted to drinking a bottle of water while driving.
