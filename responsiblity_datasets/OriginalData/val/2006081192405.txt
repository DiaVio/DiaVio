This single-vehicle crash occurred on a three-lane, level, dry roadway. This roadway is curved left in the precrash area and then straightens out. This roadway consists of one lane in each direction and a center continuous left-turn lane. The speed limit for this roadway is 56 kmph (35 mph). This crash while it was dark with street lights on and with no adverse atmospheric conditions.

Vehicle one (V1), a 1989 Toyota Corolla, was northbound in lane one. V1 traveled off the right side of the roadway, through a parking lot and was heading toward a utility pole. The driver of V1 steered left to avoid the pole. V1 returned to the roadway, crossed all lanes and departed the left side of the roadway. V1 continue on and the front struck a metal pole/barrier in front of a gas pump. V1 then rolled backward, came to rest facing southwest and was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

The driver of V1, a twenty-six-year-old female, was not interviewed. This driver's mother refused to be interviewed except to state that this driver was intoxicated at the time of the crash. The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: this vehicle traveling, off the edge of the road on the right side. The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as a driver related factor: overcompensation.
