This three-vehicle crash occurred north of a four-way, traffic signal-controlled intersection. On the north side of the intersection, the roadway is undivided and the southbound traffic lanes consist of a right-turn lane, two through lanes, and a left-turn lane. This roadway is straight, downhill in the southbound direction, and has a posted speed limit of 72 kmph (40 mph). This crash occurred in the mid-afternoon hours on a weekday with moderate traffic flow.

Vehicle one (V1), a 1999 Honda CR-V with one occupant, was traveling southbound in lane four. Vehicle two (V2), a 1994 Toyota pickup with two occupants, was stopped behind traffic in lane four, in front of V1. Vehicle three (V3), a 2002 Chrysler Sebring with two occupants, was stopped in lane four in front of V2. The driver of V1 failed to stop for traffic and the front of V1 impacted the back of V2. V2 was pushed forward and the front of V2 contacted the back of V3. All vehicles came to final rest facing south in lane four near the points of impact. V1 was towed due to damage. No one was transported. Police Reported Travel Speed is 97 kmph for V1 and 97 kmph for V2.

The driver of V1, a sixteen-year-old male, was driving from his home to soccer practice. He explained that he checked his rearview mirror to see if it was OK to get over and before he knew it, he had run into the back of V2. He was well rested, very comfortable with his vehicle, and drives this route daily. He is in good health and takes no medications. The driver has had seven months of driving experience.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: other motor vehicle in lane, other vehicle stopped. The critical reason for the critical event for V1 was coded as driver related factor: external distraction. Inadequate surveillance and inexperienced driver were coded as associative factors.

The driver of V2, a thirty-four-year-old male, was driving from his father-in-law's house to his own home. He was well rested, very comfortable with his vehicle, and drives this route several times a month. He is in good health and takes vitamins on a regular basis. The driver was conversing with the passenger at the time of impact. V2 had been stopped for more than 10 seconds.

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded: other motor vehicle in lane, traveling in same direction with higher speed. The critical reason for the critical event was not coded to this vehicle.

The driver of V3, a thirty-seven-year-old female, was driving from work and going to lunch. The driver was conversing with the passenger. She was well rested, very comfortable with her vehicle, and drives this route daily. She is in good health and takes no medications.

V3 was not involved first harmful event. The critical reason for the critical event was not coded to this vehicle.
