The crash occurred off the right side of the roadway of a two-lane roadway. Conditions were daylight and dry in the morning on a weekday. The posted speed limit was 56 KMPH (35 MPH).

V1 was a 1993 Chevy S10 Blazer SUV traveling eastbound in the right lane. V1 departed the right side of the travel lane and struck the right curb. He then continued on and the front of V1 struck a utility pole. V1 came to final rest against the pole facing east. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

V1 was driven by an 80-year-old male who was unconscious minutes before the crash occurred. V1 was towed due to damage. The Critical Precrash Event was this vehicle traveling off the edge of the road on the right side. The Critical Reason for the Critical Precrash Event was a critical non-performance error; when the driver of V1 was unconscious with the inability to act.

Numerous attempts were made in order to obtain an interview from the driver of V1 but the outcome was unsuccessful.
