This single vehicle daytime collision occurred shortly after midday on the westbound lanes of an east/west state highway. The westbound side of the highway consists of five lanes. The number five lane is a High Occupancy Vehicle (HOV) lane. The westbound lanes are dry, level, concrete, and curve to the left. An asphalt shoulder borders the east edge of the highway. A concrete wall borders the east edge of the shoulder. A raised concrete center divider borders the west edge of the highway. The center divider separates the westbound and eastbound lanes of the highway. The highway is designated a 104 kmph (65 mph) zone.

Vehicle one (V1) is a 1998 Mitsubishi Montero, sport utility vehicle, driven by a twenty four (24) year old female with one passenger. V1 was traveling westbound in the number two lane. As V1 traveled, the driver attempted a lane change to the right. V1 returned into its original number two lane as it began to lose control. V1 rolled unto its left plane and came to rest facing northbound, on its roof, blocking the number two and three lanes. V1 was towed from the scene due to rollover damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 113 kmph for V1.

The critical pre-crash event was coded: this vehicle traveling, over the lane line on right side of travel lane. The critical reason for the critical event was coded as a driver related factor: overcompensation. The driver is a nanny to the 4 year old passenger, she just picked up the child at pre-school & was heading to the child's home. She drives the route weekly and had 7 years of driving experience. No problems with the vehicle were reported by the driver.
