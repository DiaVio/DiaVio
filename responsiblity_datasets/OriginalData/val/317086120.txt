This case involved a 33 year old female driver of a 2010 Honda Accord four door sedan (V1). She was restrained by the available 3-point manual safety belt. She had a deployed frontal airbag as well as the seat mounted side impact bag and the head curtain airbag. There were two other vehicles involved in the crash, a 2003 Dodge Durango sport utility vehicle (V2) and a 2001 Mercury Grand Marquis four door sedan (V3). The crash occurred during daylight hours on a straight and level dry asphalt two lane roadway. V1was traveling south behind V3. V2 was northbound. All three vehicles were reported to be traveling at the posted speed limit of 89 kph (55 mph). V2 traveled across the center line and impacted V3 in a swiping type configuration before continuing into the southbound lane and striking V1 head on. V1 was knocked backwards approximately 12 meters off the west side of the road and down an embankment. V2 continued off the west road edge and came to rest on the roadside. V3 continued forward and off the east side of the road for final rest. All three vehicles were towed from the scene due to disabling damage. The driver of V1 was transported by ground ambulance to a level one trauma center for treatment.
