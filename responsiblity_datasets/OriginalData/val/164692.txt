Vehicle one (V1 - case vehicle), a 2003 Chevrolet S-10, 2-door pickup truck was traveling south in the southbound lane of a two-way, two-lane road. It was dark, the weather was sleet/hail and the asphalt road surface was icy. The driver of V1 was negotiating a left curve when she lost control of her vehicle and it departed the right side of the road. The roadside drops away and below the road into a residential lawn and the front of V1 struck a 70-cm diameter tree stump. V1 rotated counterclockwise approximately 120 degrees and came to rest facing east-northeast. V1 was towed from the scene due to disabling vehicle damage. The 60-year-old female driver of V1 (case occupant) was using the available three-point seat belt and the steering-wheel air bag deployed. She was transported via ground ambulance to a regional level-one trauma center with serious (AIS-3) injuries.
