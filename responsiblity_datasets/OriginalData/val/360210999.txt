This moderate severity single vehicle frontal crash involved, a 67 year old male driver who was restrained with the lap/shoulder belt and the pretensioner actuated and the steering column mounted airbag deployed. This driver sustained serious injuries and was transported to the trauma center. This crash occurred during daylight hours with clear, dry conditions on five-lane, two-way level street with a center left turn lane. Vehicle one (V1), a 2002 Nissan Frontier pickup truck was westbound in lane one and departed the roadway to the right and the front of V1 impacted a large tree. V1 remained against the tree facing west at final rest with no post crash rotation. V1 was towed due to damage. The driver, a 67 year old male, is the case occupant with no other passengers. He was wearing the manual lap/shoulder belt with the pretensioner actuating on impact and the front steering column mounted airbag deployed. He was transported with serious injuries to the trauma center.
