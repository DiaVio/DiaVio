This crash occurred in the eastbound lanes of a four-lane highway with a guardrail-divided grass median. The roadway had a left curve and a level approach leading to a 3% uphill grade. It was bordered on both sides by paved shoulders with rumble strips. Conditions were daylight and dry with cloudy skies. The setting was morning hours on a weekday.

Vehicle one (V1), a 2000 Volvo S80 sedan, was eastbound in lane two negotiating the left curve. The driver began feeling ill and attempted to pull off onto the right shoulder. V1 traveled off the roadway to the right and impacted an up-sloping embankment with its undercarriage. V1 traveled a short distance off the roadway and then came to rest facing east. V1 was towed from the scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is 97 kmph for V1.

The driver of V1, a 56-year old female, was uninjured in the crash. She is currently undergoing chemotherapy treatment for cancer, which causes her to be very weak. She said that she began feeling ill and lost consciousness as she tried to pull off the road. She did not attempt avoidance maneuvers. Her estimated travel speed was 96 kmph (60 mph).

The Critical Precrash Event for V1 was coded this vehicle traveling, off the edge of the road on the right side. The Critical Reason was a driver related factor consisting of a physical impairment of the ability to act. The driver was in poor health and taking several medications for cancer. She was still working a full time despite the exhaustion of chemotherapy, which further contributed to her fatigue.
