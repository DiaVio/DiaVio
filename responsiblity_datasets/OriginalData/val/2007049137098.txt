This single-vehicle crash occurred on a southbound, four lane, one-way, straight roadway near the intersection of a six lane roadway. The speed limit on the roadways is 48 kmph (30 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with light traffic. There were no adverse weather conditions at the time of the crash.

Vehicle one (V1), a 2003 Honda Element, was turning left from the westbound lanes to the southbound lanes. V1 departed the roadway on the west side and the front of V1 struck a tree. The vehicle stopped at impact. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

The driver of V1, a forty-four year-old female, was traveling from home to work. She was well rested, in good health and drives the vehicle and the route daily. She stated she was not in a hurry and had driven 6 km (4 miles) prior to the crash.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "this vehicle traveling; off the edge of the road on the right side." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "driver related factor-poor directional control (e. g., failing to control vehicle with skill ordinarily expected)."
