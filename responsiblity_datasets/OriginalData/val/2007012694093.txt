This crash occurred on a five-lane roadway with two lanes in each direction and a center left turn lane. The roadway was straight level asphalt. The speed was posted at 64 kmph (40 mph). The crash occurred in the early morning at the end of the week. The weather was clear and the road was dry.

Vehicle #1, a 1997 Oldsmobile Bravada utility vehicle, exited out of a driveway without yielding the right of way to Vehicle #2, a 1998 Pontiac Sunfire coupe that was traveling north in the second lane of travel on the roadway. V1 was attempting a left turn onto the roadway and while crossing over the northbound lanes, the front of V2 struck the left side of V1. Both vehicles rotated counterclockwise to come to final rest blocking the left turn lane and the northbound lane #2. Both vehicles were towed from the scene. Both drivers were transported to local trauma facilities. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

A 31 year-old male drove V1. He reported being in good health and taking no medications. He stated he had been to his brother's home to see about his niece's dirt bike being taken. He stated that as he drove north on the roadway that he rarely drives he spotted a vehicle towing a trailer with dirt bikes. He pulled off into a driveway stopping at an angle. He also stated his view of oncoming traffic was blocked by his B-pillar. He stated that as he entered the roadway he was contacted at the left side of his vehicle and knocked unconscious and did not know what happened until a police officer was at the side of his vehicle.

A 62 year-old female drove V2. She reported being in good health and taking no medications. She stated she had traveled from home and stopped at the store for a soda before going to work. She stated that as she drove north in lane #2 she noticed a vehicle entering the roadway from her right side and before she could steer away, she had contacted the left side of the vehicle. She stated that she hit the brakes hard but could not avoid V2. She said she was knocked unconscious and when she came to, there was a young lady standing next to her vehicle holding her hand telling her to stay still. She stated she could see her vehicle was facing at an angle and she could see the other driver in his vehicle but did not know what angle they were facing (north or south).

Witness #1 stated that she was standing in the driveway of her apartment building waiting for her friend to come and pick her up for class. She stated that V1 pulled into the drive and asked where he could buy some alcohol. She said that he smelled of alcohol and told him it was too early to buy alcohol and that he should pull into the truck lot across the street and sleep it off. She stated he then pulled into the lot of her apartment and was stopped by security and told he could not be in their lot. He then turned around very fast and drove straight into the roadway without stopping for oncoming traffic. She also stated that after final rest, the driver of V1 tried to drive away.

Witness #2 stated he and his partner where driving south in lane #1 and noticed V1 entering the roadway without stopping. They stated that there were no vehicles in southbound lane #2 next to them so they had a clear view of V1 entering the roadway from the driveway. They stated that as V1 and V2 came to final rest, V1 accelerated and tried to drive away. They reported that when the driver of V1 realized he could not get away he just slumped down in his seat and stayed there until police arrived.

The Critical Pre-crash Event for V1 was coded this vehicle traveling, turning left at intersection. The Critical Reason for the Critical Event was coded as inadequate surveillance, a driver related factor. Associated factors coded to this driver include alcohol and drug use. Blood tests conducted at the hospital revealed a BAC of .15 less than two hours after the crash. The driver also tested positive for cocaine and benzodiazepines. Other associated factors include fatigue, inattention, aggressive driving, and unfamiliarity with the roadway.

The Critical Pre-crash Event for V2 was coded other vehicle encroachment from driveway, turning into opposite direction. The Critical Reason for the Critical Event was not coded to this vehicle. No associated factors were coded to this driver.
