This single vehicle crash occurred on a 4-lane asphalt roadway with a raised median. The eastbound lanes curve right then straighten out and are level. The posted speed limit for the area is 64 kmph (40 mph). The crash occurred during late morning daylight hours with no adverse weather or road conditions present.

Vehicle one (V1), a 1998 Chevrolet Camaro 2-door sedan, was eastbound in the #2 lane negotiating a right curve when it crossed both lanes to the right, departed the right road edge over the curb and with its front-end impacted a chain link fence with a concrete base and a metal fencepost. V1 rotated clock-wise and contacted the fence and a post again with its right rear corner. V1 came to rest in the #1 lane facing west and was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Unknown for V1.

The driver, a twenty nine (29) year-old male, stated he was trying to get around slower vehicles and was upset that they would not let him pass. He finally was able to pass on the left and was traveling at 89 kmph (55 mph) when he stated he just lost control and drove off the right side of the road. He left his house 10 minutes before and was going to get some lunch. He is unemployed looking for work. He drives the road daily and stated his vehicle was working properly prior to the crash.

The critical pre-crash event was coded: this vehicle traveling, off the edge of the road on the right side. The critical reason for the critical event was coded as a driver related factor: aggressive driving behavior.
