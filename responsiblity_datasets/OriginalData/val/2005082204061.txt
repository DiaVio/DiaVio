This single vehicle afternoon crash occurred at 1419 in the eastbound lanes of a dry, residential, undivided, three lane, two-way bituminous roadway. In the crash area, the road curves to the left and has a 5% downhill grade for eastbound traffic. Two eastbound roadways merge at a 5-way intersection; the road where the crash occurred does not have a traffic control, consists of one travel lane in each direction and a 2-way left turn lane in the middle and has a posted speed limit of 56 kph (35 mph). At the time of the crash, it was cloudy, but there were no adverse weather conditions, sight restrictions or road defects.

Vehicle #1 (V1), a 2003 Toyota 4Runner, driven by a 28 year-old male was traveling in lane one going east. Where the road curves to the left and enters the intersection, V1 continued straight, departed the road to the right, contacted a wooden sign pole with the left mirror, traveled down an embankment, the front struck the branches of a tree, then entered a counterclockwise rotation, struck a large (50cm diameter) sign pole with the right side, had a front left corner impact with a large tree, then contacted and came to rest with the right plane against a chain link fence. The driver stated that he had fallen asleep and did not know that a collision had occurred until he hit the first tree, and was, at that point unable to make an avoidance maneuver. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

The Critical Pre-crash event was coded as "This Vehicle Traveling - off the edge of the road on the right side." The Critical Reason for the critical event was coded to V1 as a "Driver Related Factor, Sleeping, that is, actually asleep."

Associative Factors: The V1 driver stated that he had been out very late the previous evening, had not gotten as much sleep as usual, and had started his trip feeling drowsy.
