This crash occurred in the northbound lanes of a two-way roadway with two lanes in each direction. This roadway was straight and level, daylight, dry and clear with no adverse conditions. The speed limit was 48kmph (30mph), and it was the morning hours of a weekday.

V1, a 2004 Ford Crown Victoria four door with one occupant was traveling northbound in lane one. The driver lost control and veered to the right where the front of V1 impacted a light pole located on the northeast corner of the intersection. V1 came to a final rest contacting the light pole. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

V1 was driven by a 23 year-old male who reported no injuries. He advised that while driving the cab, the accelerator pedal got stuck and he lost control of the vehicle and impacted the light pole. He attempted to brake without lock-up but was unable to stop.

The Critical Pre-crash event for V1 was "This vehicle loss of control due to: Other cause of control loss (Stuck accelerator pedal)". The Critical Reason for the Critical event was vehicle related factor, other vehicle failure (Stuck accelerator pedal). An Associated factor coded to the driver was an inadequate/incorrect evasion, a combination of insufficient steering and braking inputs. A Vehicle related factor was coded as a stuck accelerator pedal. No other Associated factors were coded to the driver.
