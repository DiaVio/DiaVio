This two-vehicle crash occurred on a straight, level, undivided, wet roadway. This roadway consists of two lanes in each direction. The speed limit for this roadway is 72 kmph (45 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours and it was raining.

Vehicle one (V1), a 1998 International school bus, was eastbound in lane two. Vehicle two (V2), a 1991 Honda Civic, was eastbound in lane two in front of V1. V2 stopped to turn left into a driveway. The front of V1 struck the back of V2. V2 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The driver of V1, a sixty-three-year-old female, had picked up thirteen students from school and was on the way to drop them off at their homes. This driver has hypertension and diabetes and takes Avandia, insulin and an unspecified diuretic. She was well rested and comfortable with her vehicle and passengers. She had eight years of experience driving school buses. This driver stated that she applied the brakes when she saw V2 stopped, but the brakes failed. According to the police report, the state inspector confirmed that a brake line had ruptured causing brake failure.

The driver of V2, a fifty-three-year-old female, was on her way from her full-time job to her part-time job. She was healthy, well rested and familiar with her vehicle. She has diabetes and takes oral insulin daily. This driver stopped and was waiting for traffic to clear to make the left turn. She saw V1 in her rear view mirror, but was unable to make any avoidance maneuvers.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "other motor vehicle in lane; other vehicle stopped." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "vehicle related factor; brakes failed."

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded as "other motor vehicle in lane; traveling in same direction with higher speed." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V2 was "not coded to this vehicle."
