The crash occurred in the westbound lane of a two-lane, two-way roadway. There was one eastbound lane and one westbound lane. This was a straight, level, bituminous roadway with a posted speed of 89 kmph (55 mph). It was late afternoon on a weekend. The road was icy and it was blowing snow.

V1, a 1993 Pontiac Bonneville four-door with one occupant was traveling eastbound in lane one. V2, a 2002 Chevrolet Cavalier four-door with two occupants was traveling westbound in lane one. V1 started swerving and lost control. V1 crossed the double yellow line its front contacted the front of V2. V1 came to final rest in the roadway. V2 departed the roadway on the right and came to final rest. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

V1, the Pontiac Bonneville, was driven by a 18-year old female who reported no injuries. She advised that she had just left her house and was on her way to pick up her sister from work. She advised that she was traveling 72 kmph (45 mph) prior to loosing control. She stated that a big gust of wind blew into her car, which caused her to start swerving. She tried steering left and right but could not avoid contacting V2.

The Critical Precrash Event for the driver of V1 was: This vehicle traveling; over the lane line on the left side of travel. The Critical Reason for the Critical Precrash Event for the driver of V1 was a driver related error: too fast for conditions (blowing snow and icy roadway). Some associated factors coded to this vehicle include low tire pressure, inexperienced driver, inadequate braking, poor directional control slick surface, blowing winds and severe crosswinds.

V2, the Chevrolet Cavalier, was driven by a 48-year old female who reported minor injuries. She advised that her and her friend had left a birthday party and was on her way home. She advised that she had been traveling between 18-32 kmph (11-20 mph) prior to the crash. She stated that she was going slowly due to the bad weather. She stated that she saw V1 swerving and attempted to steer right to avoid her, but she could not.

The Critical Precrash Event for the driver of V2 was: other vehicle encroachment; from opposite direction over left lane line. The Critical Reason was not coded to this vehicle. Associated factors coded to this vehicle include slick surface, blowing winds and severe crosswinds.
