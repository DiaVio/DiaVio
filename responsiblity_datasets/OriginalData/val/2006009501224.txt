This single vehicle crash took place during the afternoon hours of a weekday near an intersection of two roadways. A three lane undivided roadway that heads east/west was intersected by a two lane undivided roadway heading north/sough and was signal controlled. The northwest corner of the intersection was adjacent to a shopping center. There were no adverse conditions and the posted speed limit was 56kmph (35mph).

V1 was traveling west in lane one approaching the intersection. On a green signal, V1 continued traveling and entered the intersection. As V1 crossed through the intersection, it departed its lane to the right . V1 struck the curb, traveled onto the sidewalk and struck a tree with its front plane before coming to rest. Police Reported Travel Speed is No PAR obtained (created) for V1.

V1, a 1995 Nissan Pathfinder (4x4), was driven by a 35 year-old female who stated that she was en route to work. The driver stated that as she had been approaching the intersection, she had been in conversation on her cell phone about her work schedule with a co worker. Then, as she passed through the intersection she turned her head to the right to see if the drive thru bank was busy taking her attention from her direction of travel. She further stated that she returned her attention to the front only after striking the right curb.

The critical pre crash event for this vehicle was coded: Off the edge of the road on the right side, and the critical reason was coded: External distraction.
