This single vehicle rollover occurred in a rural non-divided asphalt constructed roadway. This road crosses a deep gorge with various curves and varying elevations. This road had a vertical rock embankment on the right side and a vertical drop off on the left side with guard railing protection. This crash occurred in the early morning daylight hours with no adverse weather and clear visibility. The posted speed is 56 kmph (35 mph) with a +5 percent northbound grade with two lanes going up hill and one for down hill.

Vehicle one (V1), a 1999 Ford Ranger pickup driven by a fifty nine (59) year old male, was westbound had just negotiating a right hand curve. V1 was in the straight part of the roadway when the driver dozed off and exited the right road edge. V1 exited the road edge at a gradual angle and continued off the right road edge for 30 meters (98 feet) before impacting a medium sized rock with its right front tire. This impact caused V1 to rotate clockwise a little then impacted a vertical rock embankment with its front plane. V1 then bounced upward and rolled one quarter coming to rest on its left sided still heading west. Police Reported Travel Speed is 56 kmph for V1.

The driver stated that he had worked all night and had been awake for 16 hours. He stated that he was feeling sleepy and was planning on stopping to rest at the top of this grade. He stated that he did not remember much before this crash and last remembered going at the bottom on this gorge some 4.8 kilometers (3 miles) prior to the crash. He was sure that he had fallen asleep while driving.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 coded: this vehicle traveling, off the edge of the road on the right side. The critical reason for the critical event was coded as a driver related factor: sleeping, that is, actually asleep.
