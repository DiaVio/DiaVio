This single-vehicle crash occurred on a straight roadway that consists of two lanes in each direction with a center left turn lane. The westbound travel lanes have a downhill grade. The speed limit on this roadway is 64 kmph (40 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.

Vehicle one (V1), a 2001 Toyota Tacoma pickup, was westbound in lane one. V1 traveled off the right side of the road and the front struck a pole. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

The driver of V1, a fifty-nine-year-old male, had just stopped to purchase coffee on his way to a medical appointment. He was in fairly good health, well rested and familiar with his vehicle. He has diabetes and takes insulin daily. He travels on this roadway weekly. This driver stated that he was holding the coffee cup with his right hand. The cup lid loosened and the coffee began to spill. This driver looked down and attempted to put the cup in the center console cup-holder. As he did this, he inadvertently steered to the right.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as " this vehicle traveling; off the edge of the road on the right side." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as " driver related factor; internal distraction."
