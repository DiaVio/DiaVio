This one-vehicle crash occurred during daylight hours on a two-way, two-lane roadway, which was not physically divided. The bituminous roadway was straight and level with a posted speed limit of 40kmph (25 mph). There were no adverse atmospheric conditions at the time of the crash and the roadway was dry. No traffic control devices were associated with this stretch of roadway.

Vehicle one (V1), a 2002 Nissan Maxima, was traveling east in lane one intending to continue straight. The vehicle departed the roadway to the right, continued off of the roadway for a bit and struck an embankment before coming to final rest. The vehicle had to be removed from the scene due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Unknown for V1.

The driver of V1, a 42 year-old male, suffered some non-incapacitating injuries and was taken to the hospital for observation. There were no other occupants in the vehicle when the crash occurred. The driver refused an interview while on-scene. Through information given to the researcher by the police, the driver initially claimed to have suffered a seizure before the crash occurred. After refusing medical treatment, the driver then changed his story, claiming to have fallen asleep behind the wheel. The driver was eventually persuaded into going to the hospital for observation.

The Critical Precrash Event for V1 was coded this vehicle traveling, off the edge of the roadway to the right. The Critical Reason was coded as a driver related factor, an unknown critical non-performance. It is uncertain if the driver fell asleep or had an epileptic episode.
