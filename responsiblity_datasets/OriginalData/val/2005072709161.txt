This one-vehicle crash occurred early afternoon on a two-way north/south highway during daylight hours. The road is divided and there are four lanes in each direction that are level, curved slightly the right, and the surface was dry. The speed limit is 75 kmph (45 mph) and the weather was clear at the time of the crash.

Vehicle one,(V1) a 2005 Volvo S60 sedan driven by a 17 year-old male with two passengers was traveling northbound in the third lane. The driver lost control, skidding laterally across the right two lanes onto the shoulder into a tree. Then the car spun in a clockwise motion and came to a final rest on the road facing northwest. The driver and the other two occupants were all classified with non-incapacitating injuries. All three were transported to a hospital and then released. The driver and passengers were treated for minor injuries and released. The driver's father stated that he was coming home from school by means of his normal route, and that he only had been driving that vehicle for six months. He also stated that his son regularly drove in excess of the speed limit. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

The pre-event movement for V1 coded negotiating a curve, category this vehicle traveling off the edge of the road on the right side. Critical reason for the critical event coded driver related factor "aggressive driving behavior" (speeding).

Additional factor, the police accident report stated that the driver was changing a CD when the accident occurred. The reporting officer also cited the driver for speeding in excess of 30 mph over the speed limit. The driver has been licensed for less than two years.
