Vehicle 1 (V1) is a 2004 Chevrolet Cavalier, two-door (case study vehicle). Vehicle 2 (V2) is a 1994 Kenworth, tractor-trailer combination. V1 was occupied by a male, age 41 as its driver (case study participant) and a female age, 35 as the front right passenger. The case study participant was unbelted and the passenger was wearing the available lap and shoulder belts and the frontal air bags did deploy as a result of impact. The crash occurred during the hours of dawn. The weather at the time of the crash was clear and dry. The roadway surface was dry. The posted speed limit is 45 mph (72 kmph). The crash occurred at an intersection of a roadway and a gravel driveway. The roadway is a two lane road separated by a double yellow line. The roadway travels primarily east and west. V1 was traveling westbound and V2 was attempting to make a left turn from the intersecting roadway onto the eastbound lane. V2 being a tractor trailer combination had cleared the westbound lane with its front but the trailer portion remained across the westbound lane. V1 struck the left rear wheel of V2 with its front end. The impact caused V1 to rotate counterclockwise coming to rest in the unpaved area off the north edge of the roadway. The case study participant was transported to a trauma center by air. The front right passenger was treated and released at a local hospital.
