This two-vehicle crash occurred mid-afternoon during daylight hours. The collision occurred at an intersection comprised of an east/west roadway controlled by stop signs and a north/south roadway. Both roadways are two-way, two lane. The north/south roadway is concrete prior to the impact area and has a -7% grade. The east/west roadway is bituminous and is controlled by a stop sign. Both roadways were dry, free of congestion, poor weather conditions, and/or road deficiencies at the time of the collision. Both roadways are designated as 40 kmph (25 mph).zones.

Vehicle one (V1), a 2005 Chevrolet Colorado pickup, driven by a 25 year-old male was traveling westbound. Vehicle two (V2), a 1994 Jeep Grand Cherokee, driven by a 39 year-old male was traveling southbound. As V1 entered the intersection disregarding stop sign the front of V1 impacted the left side of V2. V1 rotated counterclockwise and continued after impact in a southwest direction. V1 came to rest up on the northwest corner up against a chain link fence. V2 rotated clockwise continuing after impact in a southwest direction. V2 came to rest in the southwest quadrant of the intersection facing southwest. V1 had deployed frontal airbags and was towed due to damage. V2 driver changed the left front flat tire and drove the vehicle from scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: this vehicle traveling, crossing over (passing through) intersection. The critical reason for the critical event was coded as a driver related factor: illegal maneuver.

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded: other vehicle encroachment; from crossing street, across path. The critical reason was not coded to this vehicle.
