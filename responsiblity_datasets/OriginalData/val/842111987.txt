This crash occurred during daylight hours with clear, dry conditions. Case vehicle one (V1), a 1998 Buick Skylark four door sedan, was southbound on a two lane, two way roadway and was proceeding straight through a controlled intersection. Vehicle two (V2), a 1990 Peterbilt medium heavy single unit truck was eastbound through the same controlled intersection on a three lane, two way highway with a center left turn lane with the speed limit of 50 mph/80kmph. In the intersection the front of V2 impacted the right side of V1 and forced V1 laterally to the east side of the intersection where it came to final rest. V2 had locked up the brakes through the impact and came to final rest on the southeast corner of the intersection. The driver and back right passenger in V1 are case occupants. The driver is 49 year old female, who was wearing the lap and shoulder belt and no airbags deployed. The back right passenger was a 9 year old female who was wearing the lap and shoulder belt. Both of these occupants were transported to the trauma center. V1 was disabled and towed, and V2 was able to be driven away from the scene.
