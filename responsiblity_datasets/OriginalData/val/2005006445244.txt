The two-vehicle crash occurred in the middle of a four-way intersection. The asphalt road was dry, level, and there were no adverse weather conditions. The posted speed limit was 56 kmph (35mph). The crash occurred early evening on a weekend.

Vehicle one, a 2001 Nissan Maxima, was traveling southbound, and was turning left at the intersection when mid-way through vehicle one's front contacted vehicle two's front. Vehicle two is a 1994 Subaru Legacy. Police Reported Travel Speed is Unknown for V1 and Unknown for V2.

The driver of vehicle one is a 30-year old male. According to the brief interview he said he was in a hurry and thought that vehicle two was going to stop. This vehicle was towed due to damage.

The Critical Pre-Crash Event for vehicle one was turning left at intersection and the Critical Reason for the Critical Pre-Crash Event was coded as aggressive driving behavior.

The driver of vehicle two is a 26-year old male who stated during the interview that he was traveling north on the roadway, and the traffic light was green. He proceeded to cross through the intersection when vehicle one traveling south struck the front of his car. This vehicle was towed due to damage.

The Critical Pre-Crash Event for vehicle two was other vehicle encroachment from opposite direction - over left lane line. The Critical Reason was not coded to this vehicle.
