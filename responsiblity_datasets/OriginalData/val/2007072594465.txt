This two-vehicle crash occurred on a jersey barrier-divided, straight trafficway. The southbound lanes are level and consist of four through lanes. The speed limit for this roadway is 89 kmph (55 mph). It was dark with streetlights on at the time of the crash with no adverse atmospheric conditions. Traffic was congested at the time of the crash.

Vehicle one (V1), a tractor (unknown make with cab behind engine & sleeper) towing a semi van style trailer, was southbound in lane three. Vehicle two (V2), a 1994 Honda Accord, was southbound in lane two. V1 began to move into lane two and the front struck the left side of V2. V2 rotated counterclockwise and the front struck the front of V1. V2 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The driver of V1, a thirty-six-year-old male, was traveling from another city to another state. He was healthy, well rested and comfortable with his vehicle and cargo. He travels this route monthly and works as a driver for a moving company. This driver has been driving a truck for thirteen years. He stated that he had seen V2 earlier but did not see V2 as he changed lanes.

The driver of V2, a twenty-two-year-old female, was not interviewed. No further information is available for this driver.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "this vehicle traveling; over the lane line on right side of travel lane." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "driver related factor; inadequate surveillance (e.g. failed to look, looked but did not see)."

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded as "other vehicle encroachment; from adjacent lane (same direction) - over left lane line." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V2 was "not coded to this vehicle."
