This single-vehicle crash occurred at the intersection of two-six lane roads. The northbound road has three lanes in either direction. This road is curved slightly to the right on approach to the intersection, and is level with a posted speed limit of 48 kmph (30 mph). The intersection is controlled by a traffic signal with pedestrian controls. The northbound road is divided with a concrete barrier (planter). This crash occurred during nighttime hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions

Vehicle one (V1), a 1995 Ford Windstar GL, was traveling northbound in the third lane. As V1 approached the intersection, the driver that there were two pedestrians in the crosswalk. The driver of V1 stated that he applied the brakes, the parking brake and put the vehicle transmission in "park" but the vehicle would not stop. The driver of V1 steered left to avoid the pedestrians and the front of V1 struck a large planter in the median. V1 came to rest on the planter. V1 was towed due to damage. The driver of V1, a forty-year old male, was heading to pick up his wife at work after working out at a gym. This driver had been driving for less than thirty minutes at the time of the crash. He did not indicate during the interview if he had noticed brake problems in the past, but he claimed that he lost his brakes during this trip. This driver drives this vehicle daily and over this road on a daily basis.
