This single vehicle crash occurred mid-afternoon during daylight hours on a clear day. The pre crash roadway is straight with a -2/122 slope. The pre crash roadway is four-lane, median divided, and bituminous. The eastbound lanes intersect with a two-lane county road that is controlled with a stop sign.

Vehicle one, a 1995 Ford Explorer, was eastbound in lane one traveling at approximately 45 mph (72 kph). The driver turned right at the intersection but departed the left side of the intersecting roadway. Vehicle one entered the woods and impacted a tree with its front plane, then one on the left plane and finally one on the right plane. Vehicle one came to rest in the woods. Vehicle one was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 64 kmph for V1.

The driver of vehicle one, a forty-seven year old female stated that she was driving from her house to her parents home and was distracted internally with a conversation with the passenger and trying to keep a dog in the bag seat. The driver had just put the center armrest up when she initiated the right hand turn. The driver indicated that she was so distracted that she almost missed her right hand turn on a road that she drives weekly.

The critical pre-crash event for vehicle one was coded as "this vehicle traveling: turning right at intersection". The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event was attributed to this vehicle and was coded as "Driver related factor: too fast for curve/turn". Though this driver had an internal distraction, the last link was when the driver made the turn without slowing and with almost missing the roadway.
