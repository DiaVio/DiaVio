Vehicle one (V1 - case vehicle), a 2003 Chevrolet Trailblazer, 4-door utility vehicle was traveling north in the northbound lane of a two-lane, two-way road and was approaching a T-intersection with a road approaching from the west. Vehicle two (V2), a 2003 Ford Taurus, 4-door sedan was traveling south in the southbound lane of the same road as V1. As both vehicles approached the intersection, the driver of V1 attempted to turn left and the driver of V2 was unable to avoid stiking V1. The front of V2 struck the front of V1 in an oblique offset configuration. Both vehicles were towed from the scene due to disabling vehicle damage. The 95-year-old male driver of V1 (case occupant) was using the available three-point seat belt but no air bags deployed. He was transported via ground ambulance to a regional level-one trauma center with serious (AIS-3) injuries. The 93-year-old female right-front passenger (case occupant) was using the available three-point seat belt but no air bags deployed. She was transported via ground ambulance to a regional level-one trauma center with serious (AIS-3) injuries.
