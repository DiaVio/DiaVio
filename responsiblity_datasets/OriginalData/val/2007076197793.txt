This single-vehicle rollover occurred in a rural area on a two-lane asphalt roadway. The southbound lane curves left and is downhill. The speed limit is 105 kmph (65 mph). The crash occurred shortly before midnight with no street lighting and no adverse weather or road conditions present.

Vehicle one (V1) was a 1996 Kia Sephia 4-door sedan driven by a fifty seven year old male with two passengers. V1 was southbound negotiating the left curve when it crossed into the northbound lane. The driver over-steered to the right and the vehicle went into a clockwise rotation across the southbound lane to the right. V1 departed the right road edge and bounced over a shallow ditch where it rolled 4 quarter turns to the left landing on its wheels on the roadside dirt where it came to rest. V1 then rolled backward off of the shallow embankment and back onto the roadway crossing both lanes and coming to rest finally on the paved northbound shoulder facing north. V1 was towed from the scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is 109 kmph for V1.

The driver has an unknown eye problem which restricts his driving to daylight hours only Crash occurred shortly before midnight). He refused to be interviewed.

The critical pre-crash event was coded: this vehicle traveling, off the edge of the road on the right side. The critical reason for the critical event was coded as a driver related: overcompensation. Poor directional control and the driver's vision impairment were associative factors.
