This one-vehicle crash occurred on a weekday on an undivided two-lane roadway. The bituminous roadway was straight, dry and level. There were no traffic control devices associated with this stretch of roadway. The posted speed limit was 56 kmph (35 mph). There were no adverse atmospheric conditions at the time of the crash and traffic flow was normal. The crash occurred in the late afternoon during daylight hours.

Vehicle one (V1), a 1995 Dodge Ram pick-up, was heading west on the roadway intending to continue straight. Vehicle one departed the right side of the roadway and continued for approximately 62 meters before the first impact with a tree stump. The vehicle continued partially off the right side of the roadway for another 67 meters before the second impact with a mailbox. The vehicle continued to traverse on the side of the road for approximately 13 meters before hitting two trees. The last impact caused the vehicle to roll over on the driver's side where it came to final rest facing north. The vehicle had to be removed from the scene due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Unknown for V1.

The driver of V1, a 46 year-old male, suffered non-incapacitating injuries and was taken to the hospital for treatment. There were no other occupants in the vehicle when the crash occurred. After numerous attempts, an interview could not be obtained from this driver. For this reason, it is unclear if any fatigue or health related issues contributed to this crash. According to the medical report, the driver was taking a few prescription medications for depression and sleep deprivation. The medical report also revealed that one hour after the crash, the driver had a BAC of 0.298.

The Critical Pre-crash Event coded to this vehicle was: this vehicle traveling off the edge of the road on the right side. The Critical Reason was coded as poor directional control. Associated factors coded to the driver of V1 include alcohol intoxication, the medical condition of depression, and the use of three prescription medications.
