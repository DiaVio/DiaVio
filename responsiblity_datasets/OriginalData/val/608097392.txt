This case involves a severe impact to the front of the case vehicle with a tree in a left-offset mode. The case occupant is the 53-year-old male driver. He was not restrained by a three-point belt, but the steering-wheel air bag deployed. The case vehicle, a 2004 two-door Chrysler Sebring convertible was eastbound on a dry, level, asphalt traffic-way. It was dark, but lighted and clear. The case vehicle crossed the center-line and drove off left side of the road, entered the mouth of a private drive and struck a 26-cm diameter tree in a left, off-set, frontal-impact mode and came to rest straddling the roadside and the road facing northeast. The 53-year-old, male driver was the sole occupant of the case vehicle. He was not restrained by a three-point belt, but the steering-wheel air bag deployed. He was transported to a level-one trauma center and enrolled as a case occupant.
