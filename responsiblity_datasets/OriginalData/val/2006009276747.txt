The crash occurred in the eastbound lanes of an eight-lane roadway intersection that is divided by a median. There were four eastbound and four westbound lanes. This was a straight, slightly elevated on both sides roadway with a posted speed limit of 56 kmph (35 mph). It was the early evening and the road was dry and the sky was clear. Traffic flow is moderate for this section.

V1, a 1991 Lexus LS 400 4-door with one occupant, was traveling eastbound then turning left. V2, a 2000 Acura 3.2 TL with one occupant, was traveling west in lane 3 going at a constant speed. V1 turned left and V2's front contacted V1's right side. The impact pushed V1 over into the north side of the intersection. V2 after impact rotated clockwise about 30 degrees facing north. Both vehicles came to rest in the intersection. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

V1, the Lexus LS was driven by a 56 year old female who was transported due to possible injuries. She had just come from a department store. She advised that people always get hit in that intersection and its always people that are turning, so it must be a problem with the roadway. She also stated that people always try to beat the light and that is why they get hit.

The Critical Precrash Event for V1 was "this vehicle traveling turning left at intersection". The Critical Reason for the Critical Precrash Event was coded "type of driver error unknown".

V2, the 2000 Acura 3.2 was driven by a 28 year old female who was transported due to possible injuries. Driver has not returned any phone calls for interviews.

The Critical Precrash Event for V2 is "other vehicle encroachment from opposite direction over the left lane line". The Critical Reason was not coded to this vehicle.
