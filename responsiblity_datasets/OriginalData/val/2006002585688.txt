2006-061

The crash occurred in the eastbound lanes of a 5-lane roadway (three eastbound and two westbound lanes) divided by a box-beam guardrail. The roadway curved to the left with a radius of curvature of 1,125 meters and a superelevation of 1.8%. This was a level, bituminous roadway with a posted speed limit of 89 KPH (55 MPH). It was cloudy, the roadway dry and it was dark and unlighted at the time of this early morning weekday crash. Traffic flow was normal for that time of day. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

V1, a 2003 Chevrolet S-10 extended pickup, was traveling eastbound in the second lane (the right curb lane was a very long acceleration lane). V1 drove off the right side of the roadway and struck a breakaway sign post with its right side. V1 continued and struck a rock embankment with its front. V1 continued up the embankment, rolled over two quarter turns to the left and came to rest on its roof facing in a southeasterly direction. V1 was towed due to damage.

V1, the Chevrolet S-10, was driven by a belted 18-year-old male who was transported, treated and released for minor injuries. Driver stated to police that he was on his way to work and fell asleep. Additional information obtained from a relative was that the driver had been traveling between 51-60 MPH, fell asleep and veered off the right side of the roadway hit a rock wall and rolled over. He had gone to sleep at 11:00 p.m., gotten up at 6:00 a.m. when he left for work and had driven 14 miles before the crash. He also had his driver's license less than one year. He took no medications.

The Critical Precrash Event for V1 was this vehicle traveling off the edge of the roadway on the right side. The Critical Reason for the Critical Event was sleeping, that is, actually asleep, a driver related critical non-performance error. Driver stated to police that he had fallen asleep. Police did not consider either alcohol or illegal drugs to be involved and ordered no tests.
