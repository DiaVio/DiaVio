This two-vehicle crash occurred in a parking lane adjacent to a one-way, three-lane, straight roadway with a 6% downhill grade. The speed limit on this roadway is 48 kmph (30 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.

Vehicle one (V1), a 1995 Buick Regal, was eastbound in lane one. Vehicle two (V2), a 1990 Plymouth Acclaim, was parked (unoccupied) in the parking lane on the left side of the road. V1 traveled off the left side of the road and the front struck the back of V2. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The driver of V1, a forty-four-year-old male, was traveling with a twenty-year-old male passenger. They were returning to work after lunch. This driver was healthy, well rested and comfortable with his vehicle and passenger. He drives this route daily. This driver stated that he dropped his lit cigarette between the seats and was focused on retrieving it when his vehicle drifted into the parking lane. He further stated that he did not see V2 prior to the crash.

The pre-event movement for V1 was coded as "going straight." The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "this vehicle traveling; over the lane line on the left side of the travel lane." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "driver related factor; internal distraction."
