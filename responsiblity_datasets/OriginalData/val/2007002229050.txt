This crash occurred on a two lane undivided roadway at an intersection with a posted speed limit of 48KMPH (30MPH). The roadway had a curve radius of 188 meters and a super elevation of 3%. Weekday morning. The weather was clear and the roadway was dry.

Vehicle #1, a 1999 Mercedes-Benz CLK320, was making a left turn at the intersection, when it was impacted in the right front by Vehicle #2, a 1993 Chevrolet Suburban, with its left front. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The Mercedes-Benz (Vehicle#1) was driven by a belted 28 year old male who stated at the scene that Vehicle #2 was traveling fast. This driver has refused to return messages left on his answering machine and a message left at his door.

The Critical Precrash Event coded to Vehicle #1 was: This Vehicle Traveling, Turning Left at Intersection.

The Critical Reason for the Critical Precrash Event coded to Vehicle #1 was: Driver Related Factor, Misjudgment of Gap or Other's Speed.

The Chevrolet Suburban (Vehicle #2) was driven by a belted 20-year-old male and there was a belted 31-year-old male in the front right seat who was transported to an area hospital with back and neck injuries. This driver stated, in an interview, that Vehicle #1 had tried to make a left turn by cutting in front of his Suburban. He stated he did not have time to take any avoidance actions but did notice the front of Vehicle #1 lift up slightly during acceleration.

The Critical Precrash Event coded to Vehicle #2 was: Other Vehicle Encroachment, From Opposite Direction, Over Left Lane Line.

The Critical Reason for The Critical Precrash Event was not coded to this vehicle.

Police did not consider alcohol or illegal drugs to be a factor in this crash and ordered no tests for either driver. The driver of Vehicle #1, however, was issued a summons for Failure to Yield.
