This two-vehicle crash occurred in an intersection of a five-lane undivided bituminous level roadway, controlled by traffic signals with pedestrian crossings and a three-lane roadway. The speed limit on the roadway is 56 kmph (35 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.

Vehicle one (V1), a 2005 Ford Taurus was southbound in the left turn lane. Vehicle two (V2), a 1998 Mercury Tracer was northbound in the first lane. V1 turned left at the intersection and impacted V2 on its front plane with its front plane. Both vehicles came to final rest in the area of impact. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The driver of V1, a twenty-one year old female, was driving to the circus from work. This driver indicated that she had multiple stressor's. This driver was experiencing work stress due to starting a new job, along with being tired from some unknown reason. At the time of the crash the driver was transporting 3 children to the circus, which she was already late for. The circus started 50 minutes before the crash occurred and the driver was still 15 minutes from the circus. This driver drives this vehicle daily and over this road on a weekly basis.

The driver of V2, a nineteen-year old female, was heading to a friend's house and in no hurry. This driver had been driving for about five minutes at the time of the crash. This driver drives this vehicle daily and over this road on a daily basis.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: this vehicle traveling, turning left at intersection. The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event was coded as a driver related factor: inattention.

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded: other vehicle encroachment, from opposite direction-over left lane line. The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event was not coded to this vehicle.
