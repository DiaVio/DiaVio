This crash occurred off the right side of an undivided straight two-lane asphalt roadway with a posted speed limit of 72 kmph (45 mph). The crash occurred during the mid-evening hours while dark out. Streetlamps were present. There were no adverse conditions at the time of the crash.

V1, a 2001 Dodge Caravan minivan, was traveling westbound when the driver went into diabetic shock. V1 departed the roadway to the right, first impacting a culvert with the left front bumper and undercarriage. V1 continued on while westbound and struck a water line marker with its right front bumper, and then struck a fire hydrant with its front end. The driver of V1 was transported to the hospital due to minor injuries. V1 was towed from the scene due to moderate damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

The driver of V1, a 73-year old male, stated he was coming from his grandson's soccer game. The driver had little recollection of what happened and could not provide insight regarding the vehicle's movement during the crash.

The Critical Pre-Crash Event coded for V1 was "this vehicle traveling off the edge of the road on the right side." The Critical Reason for the Critical Event was "driver related factor, heart attack or other physical impairment of the ability to act." Associated factors coded to the driver of V1 include the physical condition of diabetes and the use of prescription medication. The driver had a hearing impairment but was wearing his hearing aid at the time of the crash.
