This two-vehicle collision occurred early evening during daylight hours at a four-leg intersection. The north/south roadway is undivided with five lanes including a left turn pocket. The east/west roadway is divided by a curbed median and is seven lanes including a left turn pocket. Both roadways are dry, level and made of asphalt. The intersection is controlled by a on-colors signal, which was working properly at the time. A photo enforcement camera is present at this intersection. The east/west roadway is designated 56 kmph (35 mph). The north/south roadway is designated 64 kmph (40 mph).

Vehicle one (V1), a 1992 Pontiac Bonneville 4-door sedan driven by a thirty six (36) year old male, was traveling east in the number three lane. Vehicle two (V2), a 2002 Chevrolet Tahoe, 4-door sport utility vehicle driven by a twenty eight (28) year old female, was traveling north in the number three left turn lane. V2 was stopped in the intersection on a green light waiting for pedestrians to clear the west crosswalk area. As V1 entered the intersection on a red light, its front plane impacted V2's left plane. Both vehicle's came to final rest in the intersection. V1 was towed from the scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: this vehicle traveling, crossing over (passing through) intersection. The critical reason for the critical event was coded to V1 as a driver related factor: sleeping, that is actually asleep. An associated factor for V1 was fatigue; the driver had been awake for 48 hours. For this trip he was commuting from work to his mother's house. He is required to work extended shifts, and worked 90 hours in the previous seven days. The driver has high blood pressure and high cholesterol and takes the medication Lipitor.

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded: other vehicle encroachment; from crossing street, across path. The critical reason was not coded to this vehicle.
