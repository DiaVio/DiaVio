This two-vehicle crash occurred on a curbed median-divided, straight trafficway. The southbound travel lanes are level and consist of two through lanes and a parking lane. The speed limit for this roadway is 48 kmph (30 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.

Vehicle one (V1), a Chevrolet Impala, was southbound in lane one. Vehicle two (V2), a 1993 Ford Ranger, was southbound in lane one in front of V1. The front of V1 struck the back of V2. V2 traveled off the right side of the road and the front struck a utility pole. V2 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is No PAR obtained (created) for V1 and No PAR obtained (created) for V2.

The drivers of V1 and V2 were not interviewed. No further information is available for these drivers.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as " other motor vehicle in lane; unknown travel direction/speed of other motor vehicle in lane." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "unknown reason for critical event."

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded as "other motor vehicle in lane; traveling in same direction with higher speed." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V2 was "not coded to this vehicle."
