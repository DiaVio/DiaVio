The crash occurred in the morning of a weekday on a one-lane, one-way urban roadway. The bituminous roadway was dry, straight, and level with a speed limit of 48kmph (30 mph). Traffic flow was normal and there were no adverse atmospheric conditions at the time of the crash.

V1, a 1992 Mercury Grand Marquis, was northbound when it impacted the back of V2, a 1988 Nissan Pathfinder that was legally parked on the side of the road. Both vehicles moved slightly forward but came to final rest near the point of impact. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

V1 was driven by a 48 year-old male who sustained non-incapacitating injuries but was not transported to a hospital. The driver refused to participate in the study (would not be interviewed or allow his vehicle to be inspected).

The Critical Precrash Event for V1 was coded this vehicle traveling, over the lane line, right side of travel lane. The Critical Reason was coded as an unknown driver error. There were no known associated factors that could be coded for V1.
