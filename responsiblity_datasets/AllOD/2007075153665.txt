This single-vehicle crash occurred on a four lane divided roadway. The southbound traffic lanes consist of two through lanes. This roadway is bituminous and downhill with a posted speed limit of 64 kmph (40 mph). The roadway curves to the east. This crash occurred during daylight hours with minimal traffic congestion. The road surface was dry, however there was a blanket of snow on the roadside.

Vehicle one (V1), a 1993 Dodge Caravan with two occupants present, was southbound in lane two. The driver of V1 attempted to pass a non-contact vehicle that was in front of him. In doing so, V1 changed lanes to the right as the vehicle exited the curve. V1 departed the roadway to the right where the front of the vehicle contacted a metal fence. The impact caused V1 to rotate clockwise before coming to rest on all four tires facing west. V1 was towed due to damage. The two occupants were not injured. Police Reported Travel Speed is 64 kmph for V1.

The driver of V1, a seventeen-year-old male, was on his way home from the recreation center when the crash occurred. He stated being rested, drives this route several times a month and is very comfortable with this vehicle. The driver has no medical issues and takes no medications. He has had 12 months of driving experience. He has had driver's education that consisted of classroom only.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: this vehicle traveling, off the edge of the road on the right side. The critical reason for the critical event was coded as a driver related factor: poor directional control (e.g., failing to control vehicle with skill ordinarily expected). The driver was inexperienced and attempted to pass a slower moving vehicle in font of him by changing lanes to the right in a left curve.
