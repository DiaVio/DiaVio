This two-vehicle rear end crash that occurred in the morning during daylight hours in the eastbound lane of a four-lane, two-way roadway separated by a painted centerline stripe. The crash occurred at a T-type intersection. The cross street is used to access a high school parking lot. The roadway has two westbound and two eastbound lanes. The posted speed limit for both the east/west roadway is posted 56 kmph (35 mph). The roads were dry and the sky was clear. The roadway is an uphill grade of 4% for eastbound traffic.

Vehicle one (V-1), a 1999 Jeep Cherokee suv driven by a 23 year-old male was traveling eastbound in the number 2 lane. Vehicle two (V-2), a 1997 Chevrolet Cavalier coupe driven by a 20 year-old female with one passenger was stopped behind two other non-contact vehicles. The driver of V1 apparently applied braking but failed to stop in time before striking the rear of V2. The vehicles came to rest in the traffic lane and were later removed from the roadway onto the cross street to permit traffic to flow eastbound. There was a glare from the sun that was rising from the east at the time of the crash. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 56 kmph for V1 and 56 kmph for V2.

The critical pre- crash event for V1 was coded: other motor vehicle in lane, other vehicle stopped. The driver of V1 was on his morning commute from home to work. He works an average of 12 hours per day, 60 hours per week. The critical reason for the critical event for V1 was coded as a driver related factor: following too closely to respond to unexpected actions of others (specified as: Driver did not expect forward vehicles to stop.).

The pre-crash event for V2 - other motor vehicle in lane traveling n same direction with higher speed. Critical reason not coded to this vehicle.
