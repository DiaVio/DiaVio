This crash occurred on a wet two-lane asphalt roadway early on a weekday afternoon. The speed limit was 35mph (56kph) and there was a positive grade of 1.6%.

Vehicle 1 was a 1995 Lincoln Continental driven by an 81 year-old male. There was a 2005 Chevrolet Colorado pickup legally parked on the right shoulder of the roadway. Police Reported Travel Speed is Unknown for V1 and Unknown for V2.

The driver stated that he took his eyes off the road for two seconds to look for something on the dashboard - the driver couldn't recall what he was looking for -

before departing the right side roadside and striking the LR of the parked pickup with its RF corner. The pickup was pushed forward into another parked vehicle as vehicle 1 crossed the roadway and departed the left roadside nstriking a boulder with its RF wheel.

The Critical Pre-crash Event was V1 traveling off the right roadside. The Critical Reason for the Critical Pre-crash Event was an internal driver distraction.
