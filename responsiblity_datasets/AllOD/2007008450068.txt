The crash occurred in the southbound lanes and in a parking lot just outside the five lane roadway. There were two northbound lanes plus a turning lane and two southbound lanes. This was a straight, level, bituminous roadway with a posted speed limit of 72 KMPH (45MPH). It was early evening on a weekday the road was dry and the sky was clear. The traffic was moving freely.

V1, a 2003 Ford Mustang convertible with two occupants, was traveling in lane two. V2, a 1996 Dodge Van, was legally parked in a parking lot just off the roadway. V2 was unoccupied. V3, 1993 Buick Century, was also legally parked and unoccupied in a parking lot just off the roadway. V1 was traveling at a high rate of speed, weaving dangerously through traffic. V1 turned right sharply into the driveway and impacted the left curb with its left wheels and undercarriage. V1 began to rollover and impacted V2's front with V1's front and V3's drivers door with V1's right tire. V1 came to rest on its roof. Police Reported Travel Speed is Unknown for V1 and Unknown for V2.

V1, the Ford mustang, was driven by a 52 year old male who was transported from the scene with serious injuries. No interview has been obtained from the driver of V1. It was learned that the driver had just purchased the vehicle hours prior to the crash.

The witnesses advised that the vehicle was being driven very fast and that it was weaving dangerously through traffic. It looked like they needed to turn into the driveway. When they started to turn the car flipped on its roof and hit the van. It came to rest on its roof.

The Critical Pre-crash Event for V1 was "this vehicle traveling off the edge of the road on the left side". The Critical Reason for the Critical Pre-crash Event was a driver related error, "traveling too fast for curve/turn". Associated factors were traveling too fast and turned from wrong lane.
