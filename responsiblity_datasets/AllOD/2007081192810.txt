This single vehicle crash occurred mid-afternoon during daylight hours. The location was a three lane inter-city arterial with one lane in each direction and a center left turn only lane. There are curbs and sidewalks on both roadsides with condominium complexes and driveways. The roadway was level, bituminous, dry pavement with no sight restrictions. The posted speed limit is 56 kmph (35 mph).

Vehicle one (V1), a 1968 Ford Econoline full size window van driven by a forty five (45) year old female, was traveling southbound in the curb lane. A witness stated V1 was drifting back and forth in its lane. V1 veered left across two lanes and drove up and over the east curb sideswiping a utility pole and continued traveling southbound on the sidewalk. V1 passed between a wooden power pole and a fence and struck a conduit tube attached to the pole. V1 continued, but veered right back onto the roadway, crossed all three lanes and rode up over the west curb. V1 struck a group of mailboxes, then traveled down an embankment into a condominium parking lot. V1 struck a stair rail and continued southbound jumped a curb and then struck a large tree head-on coming to final rest facing southeast. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

The driver of V1 could not be located to interview.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: this vehicle traveling, off the edge of the road on the left side. The critical reason for the critical event was coded to V1 as a driver related factor: poor directional (e.g., failing to control vehicle with skill ordinarily expected). The police report indicated alcohol was present for the driver and unknown if drugs were present.
