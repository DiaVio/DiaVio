This single vehicle crash occurred in the early afternoon on a Tuesday. The location was an urban/industrial area with no sight restrictions. The traffic way was divided State Route freeway. The roadway was level bituminous, with three northbound lanes, paved shoulders and a concrete Jersey barrier center divider. The weather was cloudy and the roadway was dry. The posted speed limit was 97 kmph (60 mph). Traffic was moderate and moving at the speed limit.

Vehicle one (V1), a 2006 Acura RSX three door with two occupants, was traveling northbound in the center lane (lane 2). The driver of V1 stated a large semi changed lanes in front of him cutting him off and causing him to brake and swerve left. V1 initiated a counter clockwise yaw as it crossed lane three and entered the shoulder before the front left struck the concrete barrier. The vehicle then rolled right two-quarter turns and it followed a path against the barrier before coming to rest on its top. There was one witness listed on the PAR, but he refused to share any information. V1 slid approximately 93 meters (305 feet) on its top. V1 was towed due to damage. Neither occupant was transported. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

The driver of V1 was an 18 year-old male. He stated he was traveling in lane two when a large semi cut him off. The driver was in good health with no medical problems. He was well rested and familiar with both the vehicle and the roadway. He has no prior driver training of any kind and has only been driving for two years. He stated he did not know how fast he was going prior to the crash.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: this vehicle traveling, over the lane line on left side of travel lane. The critical reason was coded as a driver related factor: overcompensation.
