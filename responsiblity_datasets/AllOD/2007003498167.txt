The crash occurred in the intersection of two roadways. The north/south roadway was two-way with one lane in each direction. The east/west roadway was two-way with one lane in each direction. This was a straight, level, bituminous roadway with a speed limit of 48 kmph (30 mph). It was the evening hours of a weekday and it was dark and light with no adverse conditions.

V1, a 2001 Oldsmobile Intrigue four door with four occupants, was traveling southbound in lane one. A non-contact vehicle was in front of V1, and stopped at the intersection attempting to make a left turn. As V1 went around the non-contact vehicle, another non-contact vehicle traveling northbound attempted to make a left turn in front of V1. V1 lost control, and the front of V1 impacted a sign pole, and then a bus stop shelter, both located on the southwest corner of the intersection. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

V1, the Oldsmobile Intrigue was driven by a 22 year-old male who reported no injuries. The three occupants, a 22 year-old male, a 22 year-old male and a 21 year-old male all reported no injuries. The driver advised that he was picking up friends and traveling to the movie theater. He stated that as he was going around a vehicle in front of him, another vehicle from the on-coming direction attempted to make a left turn in front of him. The driver attempted to turn right, but there was a pedestrian with a baby carriage in the crosswalk, then went into the bus stop shelter.

The Critical Pre-crash event for V1 was "this vehicle traveling off the edge of the road on the right side". The Critical Reason for the Critical event was coded as a Driver related factor, “other decision error - crossed with obstructed view”. The Recognition factors were an impending problem masked by traffic flow pattern, and that he was conversing with passengers. An other decision factors was coded as an crossed with obstructed view.
