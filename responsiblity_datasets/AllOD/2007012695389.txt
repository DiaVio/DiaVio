This crash occurred in the late morning of a weekend off the west (right) side of a two-lane, two-way roadway. The roadway consisted of one lane in each direction with a posted speed limit of 56 kmph (35 mph). The bituminous roadway had a positive slope in the southbound direction and curved slightly to the left. The roadway was dry but the sky was cloudy. The traffic flow was light at the time of the crash.

V1, a 1993 Nissan Sentra with one occupant, was southbound in lane one negotiating the curve. V1 departed the roadway on the right side and the driver steered hard to the left, which V1 to begin to rotate counter-clockwise. V1's right struck a signpost, ending its rotation. V1 continued forward where it struck a small tree with its front followed by a larger one where it came to final rest. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

The driver of V1, a 59-year-old male, was transported from the scene for treatment of non-incapacitating injuries. His wife stated that he had just left his home to look for their dog that had run away. She stated that she did not know how fast he was traveling but he did travel that roadway often. She stated that he had a history of heart problems but did not take medication for it.

The Critical Precrash Event for V1 was coded this vehicle traveling, off the edge of the road on the right side. The Critical Reason for the Critical Precrash Event was a driver related factor, overcompensation. The driver's medical records show that he tested positive for cocaine and opiates as well as having a BAC of .22. Other associated factors include traveling too fast for curve and the fact that the tires had little tread on them.
