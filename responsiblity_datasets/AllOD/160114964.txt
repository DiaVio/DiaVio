Vehicle 1 (V1) is a 2001 Honda Civic, four-door (case study vehicle). Vehicle 2 (V2) is a 1994 Mercury Sable, four-door. V1 was occupied by a female, age 26 as its driver (case study participant). The case study participant was wearing the available lap and shoulder belt and the frontal air bags did deploy as a result of impact.

The crash occurred during the hours of daylight. The weather at the time of the crash was clear and dry. The roadway surface was dry. The posted speed limit is 35 mph (56 kmph).

The crash occurred on a two lane roadway. The roadway travels in a north-south direction. The southbound direction is a straight-away with a negative grade. The northbound direction has a right hand curve with a positive grade. A double yellow line divides the roadway.

V1 was traveling southbound. V2 was traveling northbound attempting to negotiate a right curve in the roadway. V2 crossed into the southbound lane impacting V1 in the front with its front end. V1 rotated counterclockwise coming to rest partially on the gravel shoulder. V2 came to rest with its front end in the southbound lane.

The case study participant was transported by air to a trauma center.
