This two-vehicle collision occurred early afternoon. It was clear at the time of the collision. The collision occurred at a four-leg intersection. The north/south roadway is five lanes, undivided, dry, level and made of asphalt. The east/west roadway is two lanes undivided, dry, level and made of asphalt. The intersection is controlled by a on-color signal, which was working properly at the time. The north/south roadway is designated 56 kmph (35 mph). The east/west roadway is designated 40 kmph (25 mph).

Vehicle one (V1) is a 1993 Toyota Camry 4-door sedan, driven by a thirty eight (38) year old unlicensed female. V1 was traveling north in the number one lane. Vehicle two (V2) is a 2002 Honda Accord 4-door sedan, driven by a fifty two (52) year old female. V2 was traveling west in the number one lane. As V1 entered the intersection, its front plane impacted V2's left plane, a corner to corner impact. V2 rotated clockwise and came to rest in the north crosswalk area in the northbound lanes. V1 continued northwest impacting a curb with its front plane and then a fence with its front plane. V1 came to rest facing northwest on the west curb. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The driver of V1 is 38 year-old female. No interview was obtained for this driver. The driver of V2 refused the interview.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: this vehicle traveling; crossing over (passing through) intersection. The critical reason for the critical event was coded to the driver of V1 as a driver related factor: type of driver error unknown, due to lack of interview. Inadequate surveillance and an illegal maneuver were considered associative factors.

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded: other vehicle encroachment; from crossing street, across path. The critical reason was not coded to this vehicle.
