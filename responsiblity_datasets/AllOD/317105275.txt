This case involved a 29 year old restrained female driver of a 2007 Chevrolet HHR four door wagon (V1). She was restrained by the available three point manual lap and shoulder belt. The vehicle was equipped with frontal airbags only, which did not deploy in the crash. The crash occurred at night on an unlit rural two-lane roadway. The asphalt roadway was level and curved to the left. It was raining at the time of the crash and the road was wet. The posted speed limit was 80 kph (50 mph). V1 was southbound traveling at a reported speed of 97 kph (60 mph) while negotiating the left hand curve. The right side tires of V1 dropped off the right side of the pavement and the driver steered back to the left The driver overcorrected and crossed the center line of the roadway in a counterclockwise yaw. V1 continued off the left side of the road and impacted a 30cm tree with its right side aft of the C pillar. V1 rotated clockwise and rolled onto its left side into a ditch. The impacted tree was sheared off approximately 1.6 meters above ground and fell over onto the right side of V1. V1 was camouflaged by the darkness and the tree branches and was not discovered for three hours. V1 was eventually towed from the scene due to disabling damage. The driver of V1 refused treatment after she was extricated from the vehicle. The following day, she sought treatment at a local hospital emergency room and was hospitalized overnight. She was then transferred to a level one trauma center due to the severity of her injuries.
