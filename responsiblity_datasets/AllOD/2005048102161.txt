This single vehicle crash occurred several hours before midnight on a clear night in darkness. The roadway is a bituminous two-lane that is curved to the left. The posted speed limit is 56 kmph (35 mph)

Vehicle one (V1), a 1997 Mitsubishi Montero, was westbound negotiating the left-hand curve at a high rate of speed. V1 was traveling at a police reported speed of 80 kmph (50 mph). The vehicle departed the right road edge and traveled down an embankment and rolled over. The vehicles top and left plane struck two trees and came to final rest on the left side. The vehicle was towed due to disabling damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 80 kmph for V1.

V1's driver is a sixteen year old with only two months of driving experience. She had gotten off from her part-time job and went by her boy friends house (without her parents permission) to visit with him before she went home. She was in a hurry to get home and was driving too fast. This driver left the boyfriends house five minutes before the crash. This driver has also been up for sixteen hours at the time of the crash.

The critical pre-crash event was coded: this vehicle traveling, off the edge of the road on the right side. The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event was coded as a driver related factor: too fast for curve.
