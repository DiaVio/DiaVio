This two vehicle crash occurred at dawn on the two westbound lanes of a rural interstate roadway. There were two lanes westbound and two lanes eastbound separated by a depressed earth median. The roadways are straight and level asphalt with no traffic controls. The posted speed limit for each roadway was 121 kmph (75 mph). The roadside north of the westbound lanes was very heavy small shrubbery and small mesquite trees.

Vehicle one (V1), a 2001 Ford Focus sedan driven by a 22 year-old male was westbound in the number two lane. Vehicle two (V2), a 2006 Nissan Altima sedan driven by a 46 year-old male with four passengers was traveling west in the number one lane. V1 entered a clockwise rotation and crossed into the number lane. V1 struck the left front bumper corner of V2 and continued in its clockwise rotation. V1 departed the roadway into soft earth and tripped causing the vehicle to overturn five quarter turns. V1 came to rest on the left side on top of a barbed wire right-of-way fence facing generally northeast. V2 came to a controlled stop on the north paved shoulder facing west. V1 was towed due to damage. V2 was driven from the scene. Witness from V2 stated the driver of V1 said, "don't call the police, they will arrest me because I'm drunk". The driver then ran into the open desert terrain and disappeared. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: this vehicle traveling, over the lane line on right side of travel lane. The critical reason for V1 was a driver related factor: poor directional control.

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded: other vehicle encroachment; from adjacent lane (same direction) over left lane line. The critical reason not coded to this vehicle.
