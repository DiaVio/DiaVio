This single vehicle crash occurred at a T-intersection. There is one northbound and one southbound lane that are separated by a painted centerline stripe. There is a through street that has one eastbound and one westbound lane that is separated by a painted centerline stripe. The intersection is controlled by a stop sign for northbound traffic. The stop sign was visible and in proper working order at the time of the crash. This was a straight, level, and bituminous roadway with a posted speed limit on both roadways of 72 KPH (45 MPH). It was a mid afternoon on a weekday and the road was dry and the sky was clear. Traffic was light at the time of the crash.

Vehicle one (V1), a 1998 Ford Explorer 4X2 with three occupants, was traveling northbound approaching the intersection. The vehicle traveled beyond the stop sign and entered the cross street. The driver then steered to the left and the vehicle departed the north edge of the westbound lane and struck a directional sign. The vehicle then struck a barbed wire fence. The vehicle came to rest down a 4-foot embankment on its wheels facing in a northwesterly direction. The driver sustained injury and was transported by private conveyance. The vehicle was towed from the scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is 40 kmph for V1.

The driver, a 28-year-old female, told police that she was traveling northbound and her brakes failed just as she approached the intersection. She was not available for an interview. Post crash inspection of the vehicle found the brake pedal pressure firm. The driver was not available for interview.

The critical pre-crash event was coded “this vehicle traveling-end departure”. The critical reason was coded “driver related factor-type of driver error unknown”.
