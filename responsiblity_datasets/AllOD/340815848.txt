This case involves a frontal impact between the front of a passenger car and the side of a Bluebird School Bus. The impact involved the front of the passenger car under-riding the right side of the bus. The case vehicle a 2008, four-door, Mercedes-Benz E-350 was traveling in the inside westbound lane of a divided, level, four-lane, asphalt, trafficway approaching a four-leg intersection. It was daylight and clear. Vehicle two (V2), a Bluebird School Bus, was in the eastbound left-turn lane of the same intersection. As the case vehicle was crossing through the intersection, it entered the path of V2. The front of the case vehicle stuck the right side of V2 and under-rode it. V2 came to a controlled stop in the intersection facing north. The case vehicle came to rest facing west. The sole occupant of the case vehicle was the unrestrained 31-year-old male driver. He had the benefit of a deployed frontal impact air bag. The driver sustained multiple moderate injuries and was transported to a level one trauma center and enrolled as a case occupant.
