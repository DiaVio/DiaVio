This single vehicle crash occurred in the southbound lane of a two-lane undivided roadway. This was a straight, level, and bituminous roadway with a posted speed limit of 56 kmph (35 mph). It was early morning daylight hours on a weekday; the road was dry and the sky was clear. The traffic flow was light. The impact occurred on an approach to a narrow bridge. The guardrail on each side was to protect errant vehicles from striking the culvert or concrete wings. A black and yellow warning sign alerted motorist of the upcoming bridge.

Vehicle one (V1), a 1996 Mazda Miata two door convertible with only the driver present, was traveling southbound in lane one. The driver of V1 fell asleep and the vehicle departed the right side of the roadway and contacted a guardrail on the right side. The vehicle was deflected to the left, which then caused V1 to cross the roadway and contact the guardrail on the east side of the roadway. The vehicle came to rest against the guardrail facing southeast. The vehicle was towed and the driver was not injured Police Reported Travel Speed is 56 kmph for V1.

A 50-year old male was driving V1. He stated that he had fallen asleep while driving. The driver also stated that he is diagnosed with sleep apnea and receiving CPAP treatment. He also stated he received three hours of sleep in the last twenty-four hours and works an average of eleven hours per day. The driver is currently taking medication, which includes Hydrochlorothiazide, Lisinopril, and Verapamil.

The Critical Pre-crash Event for V1 one was “this vehicle traveling, off the edge of the road on the right side.” The Critical Reason for the Critical Event was coded “driver related factor - sleeping, that is, actually asleep”. Associated factors coded to this vehicle include driver fatigue and sleep apnea.
