This case involves a frontal impact between the front of a passenger car and the front of another passenger car. The case occupant is the restrained driver of a passenger car. The case vehicle a 2007, four-door, Honda Accord was traveling in the westbound lane of a dry, level, two-lane asphalt roadway negotiating a right-sweeping curve. It was daylight and clear. Vehicle two (V2) a 2003, four-door, Honda Accord, was in the eastbound lane of the same roadway negotiating a left curve. The case vehicle drifted across the center-line and entered the path of V2. The front of the case vehicle struck the right side of the front-end of V2. Both the case vehicle and V2 rotated clockwise before coming to rest in the eastbound lane. The case vehicle came to rest, headed northwest and V2 came to rest headed southeast. The sole occupant of the case vehicle was the restrained 71-year-old male driver. He had the benefit of deployed frontal-impact air bag. The driver sustained serious injuries and was transported to a level-one trauma center and enrolled as a case occupant.
