The two-vehicle crash occurred at an intersection of a five-lane undivided roadway and a two way, two lane undivided roadway. For southbound traffic there is three travel lanes. The road was dry, traveled, and level, straight with an asphalt surface. Cross traffic is controlled by stop signs. The posted speed limit for both roadways is 56 kmph (35 mph). Traffic was light at the time of the collision. This crash occurred in dark but lighted conditions with clear skies.

Vehicle one (V1), a 1997 Dodge Ram with three occupants, was westbound stopped at the stop sign with the intention of going straight across the intersection. Vehicle two (V2), a 1998 Chevrolet Astro with one occupant, was southbound in the second travel lane. As V1 attempted to cross the roadway, the front of V2 impacted the right side of V1. V1 rotated clockwise and came to rest facing northeast in the roadway south of the point of impact. V2 continued southbound and came to rest in the roadway south of the point of impact. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. None of the occupants were injured Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The driver of V1, a seventy-six year old male, was heading home from a friend's house at the time of the crash. This driver had been driving for about five minutes. He drives the route daily and is familiar with the vehicle. This driver stated that he saw V2 prior to impact and attempted to accelerate past the path of V2 but was unsuccessful.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded "this vehicle traveling-crossing over (passing through) intersection". The critical reason was coded "misjudgment of gap or other's speed".

The driver of vehicle two, a thirty-three year old male, was unable to communicate with the researcher to give any information about the crash. The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded "other vehicle encroachment-from crossing street, across path". A critical reason was not coded to this vehicle.
