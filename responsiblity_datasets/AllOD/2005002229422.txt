This crash occurred on a two lane undivided roadway, slight curving to the left with a positive 3.27% grade and a posted speed limit of 72KMPH (45MPH). It was raining lightly and the roadway was wet. This crash occurred on a weekday afternoon.

Vehicle #1, a 1993 Subaru Loyale was traveling southbound on the two lane undivided roadway, negotiating a curve to the left when it lost control, skidded counter-clockwise and departed the roadway on the east side impacting a utility pole with its right rear side. The vehicle came to rest facing in a northerly direction. Police Reported Travel Speed is Unknown for V1.

The driver of Vehicle #1 was a 17 year old belted female who was slightly injured but did not get transported to a hospital. This driver could not be located or contacted for an interview. There is no evidence of any legal or illegal drug use or of any illness. A witness stated that this vehicle was traveling at an excessive rate of speed for this curve and for road conditions. It should be noted that this driver was inexperienced.

The Critical Precrash Event coded to this vehicle was: This Vehicle Control Loss Due to Traveling Too Fast for Conditions. The Critical Reason for the Critical Precrash Event was coded as: Driver Related Factor Decision Error, Too fast for conditions, specified as speeding on a wet roadway.

The police did not consider illegal drugs or alcohol to be involved and ordered no tests for this driver.
