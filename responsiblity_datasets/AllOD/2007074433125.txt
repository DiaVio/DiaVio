This two-vehicle crash occurred in a four-legged, traffic signal-controlled intersection in an interchange area. The north/south roadway is straight and divided by a curbed median. The southbound roadway has a five percent downhill grade and consists of two through lanes and a left turn lane. The northbound roadway is level and consists of two through lanes and a left turn lane. The speed limit for these roadways is 64 kmph (40 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.

Vehicle one (V1), a 1999 Buick LeSabre, was southbound in lane two. Vehicle two (V2), a 1993 Chevrolet S10 pickup, was stopped northbound in lane three, waiting to turn left. There were non-contact vehicles stopped in southbound lane three. V2 turned left and the right side was struck by the front of V1. The vehicles rotated and the left side of V1 struck the right side of V2. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The driver of V1, a seventy-three-year-old female, was on her way home from a car repair shop. She was in good health, well rested and familiar with her vehicle. She travels this route monthly. She takes Synthroid for a thyroid condition. This driver stated that she did not see V2 prior to the crash.

The driver of V2, a sixty-eight-year-old male, was on his way to a store on his way home from work. He was fairly healthy, well rested and familiar with his vehicle. He travels this route weekly. He takes Lipitor and Toprol for his high blood pressure and high cholesterol. This driver stated that he did not see V1 prior to the crash. He also stated that he waited for two other vehicles in southbound lane two but thought there were no other vehicles in that lane.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "other vehicle encroachment; from opposite direction - over left lane line." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was "not coded to this vehicle."

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded as "this vehicle traveling; turning left at intersection." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded as "driver related factor; turned with obstructed view."
