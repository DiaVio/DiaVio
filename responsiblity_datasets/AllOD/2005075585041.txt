This two-vehicle crash occurred during daylight on a straight, dry, level, physically divided, six-lane highway. The highway runs east and west at the location of the crash and each direction consists of three through lanes. The speed limit is 105 kmph (65 mph) and there were no adverse weather conditions at the time.

Vehicle one (V1), a 1996 Oldsmobile Aurora was westbound in lane three. Vehicle two (V2), a 2005 Hyundai Santa Fe was westbound in front of V1, behind a tractor-trailer. The tractor-trailer slowed for traffic and V2 decelerated. The front of V1 struck the back of V2. V1 then traveled off the left side of the highway into a grassy median and the left side struck a delineator pole and the front struck a guardrail. V1 came to rest in the median facing southwest. V2 rotated slightly clockwise and came to rest facing northeast in lane one. V1 was towed due to damage. V2 was not towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 97 kmph for V1 and 97 kmph for V2.

The driver of V1, a thirty-nine-year-old female, refused to be interviewed. Medical records indicate that this person was taking these medications: Paxil, Albuterol and birth control pills.

The driver of V2, a thirty-year-old female, was driving from the airport to a nearby city in a rental vehicle. This driver had been driving for about thirty minutes at the time of the crash. She was well rested, rarely drove on this roadway and although she had never driven the vehicle previously, she was comfortable driving it.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: other motor vehicle in lane, traveling in same direction while decelerating. The critical reason for critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as a driver related factor: type of driver error unknown.

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded: other motor vehicle in lane, traveling in same direction with higher speed. The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V2 was not coded to this vehicle.
