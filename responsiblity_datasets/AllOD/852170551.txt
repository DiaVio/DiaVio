This near side impact crash case involves a 21 year old restrained female driver with a side impact air bag deployment who sustained serious injuries. This crash occurred at night with street lights during the early morning hours at a four legged intersection controlled by traffic signals. At the time of the crash, the level, asphalt roadways were dry under overcast weather conditions. The case vehicle one (V1), a 2005 Hyundai Sonata four door sedan, was traveling west in lane two on a five lane, two way roadway and was approaching the intersection moved into lane three, a left turn only lane. Vehicle 2 (V2), a 2006 Ford F150 Supercab four door pickup, was traveling north in lane two of five on the intersecting street. V1 began to make a left turn as V2 was crossing straight through the intersection and the front of V2 impacted the left side of V1. V1 rotated counterclockwise and came to final rest near the southwest corner of the intersection, facing east. After the initial impact, V2 traveled in a northwesterly direction and came to final rest in the center of the intersection, facing northwest. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. The case occupant in V1 is a 21 year old female driver in the front left position who was restrained with a manual lap/shoulder belt and the side impact seat back mounted air bag deployed. The case occupant sustained serious injuries and was transported to a local hospital then transferred to the trauma center. A second occupant, a 52 year old male, in V1 was seated in the front right position and was restrained with a manual lap/shoulder belt and sustained minor injuries.
