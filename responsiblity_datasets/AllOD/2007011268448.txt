The crash occurred on a two-way, two-lane, dirt/slag rural roadway with an un-posted speed limit of 89 kmph (55 mph). Conditions at the time of the weekday evening crash were dark, unlighted, cloudy and dry.

V1, a 2005 Dodge Ram 2 door 2WD work pick up truck, was driven by a 39 year-old male on his way home from work. V1 was traveling westbound on the roadway and accelerating after turning onto the road, and was going 35 mph. The driver stated that two deer came into the road from the left side. The driver braked (ABS rear brakes) and swerved to the right to avoid the deer. The truck began to bounce on the bumpy road and rotated slightly counterclockwise. V1 went off of the roadway on the right side into a 34% sloped ditch. The right side of V1 struck a 35 cm diameter tree. V1 came to a stop in the ditch off the right side of the road, facing west. V1 was towed from the scene due to damage. The fully restrained driver refused treatment at the scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

The Critical Precrash Event coded to V1 was 'this vehicle traveling off the edge of the road on the right side'. The Critical Reason coded to this driver was 'a driver related performance error of overcompensation'. The contributing factors coded to this driver were 'successful avoidance maneuver to a previous critical event', 'animal in roadway' and 'roadway conditions of dark, unlighted'.
