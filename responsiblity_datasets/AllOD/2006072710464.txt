This one vehicle crash occurred mid-afternoon on a four-lane roadway. The road consists of two lanes that run southbound and two lanes that run northbound. Both directions of the road consist of concrete barriers that separate local traffic and non-local traffic. The non-local traffic is elevated and overpasses the local traffic, but the overpass was not part of the crash. The speed limit is 58 kmph (35 mph). There were no adverse weather conditions or traffic flow problems.

Vehicle one, a 1999 Cadillac Deville with one occupant, was traveling northbound in the first lane. The driver of vehicle one claimed he was cut off by another vehicle and was forced to swerve right. The vehicle then rode up onto the concrete barrier and flipped the vehicle rolled two-quarter turns to the right. The left side of the vehicle struck a fire hydrant before coming to rest on its roof. The vehicle was towed due to damage and the driver was not transported. A PAR was not obtained. Police Reported Travel Speed is No PAR obtained (created) for V1.

The critical pre crash event for vehicle one is coded: this vehicle traveling-over the lane line on right side of travel lane. The critical reason was coded: incorrect evasive action.
