This single-vehicle crash occurred in the median of a divided, level, straight roadway. The eastbound lanes consist of three through lanes. The speed limit on this roadway is 64 kmph (40 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours and it was raining.

Vehicle one (V1), a 1997 Ford F150 pickup, was eastbound in lane two. As V1 changed lanes to its left, it rotated counterclockwise and traveled off the left side of the roadway. The right side of V1 struck and sheared a utility pole on the median. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

The driver of V1 left the scene. No information is available for this driver. The vehicle inspection revealed the left & right rear tires were underinflated. The pickup truck did not have any cargo to aid in traction of the rear tires.

The pre-event movement for V1 was coded as "going straight." The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as: this vehicle control loss due to; traveling too fast for conditions. The conditions were the wet roadway and underinflated rear tires. The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "driver related factor; type of driver error unknown."
