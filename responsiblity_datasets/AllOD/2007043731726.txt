This single-vehicle crash occurred in the afternoon of a weekday on a 2-lane divided highway. The bituminous roadway was a north/south thoroughfare with a grass median dividing the north from the south direction. The posted speed limit was 89 kmph (55 mph). The crash occurred after a right hand curve with a superelevation of 7 percent. The conditions were daylight, clear, dry and the traffic level was moderate.

V1, a 2000 Ford Explorer 4 x 4 SUV with one occupant, was southbound in lane two when it skidded off the left side of the road, entered a ditch/culvert and rolled over (3-quarter turns). The Explorer came to rest in northbound lane two on its left side and had to be towed from the scene due to disabling damage. The driver was not seriously injured and refused treatment. Police Reported Travel Speed is 89 kmph for V1.

The driver of V1 was a 21-year-old female and full time college student who was on her lunch break this afternoon and was returning to campus when she was involved in this crash. This driver reported she was driving south in lane two, and while talking to her girlfriend on a mobile phone (handheld) she realized that the traffic ahead of her was stopping. Her immediate response was to switch into lane one but when she looked to her right she saw a car in that lane. Rather than slamming on her brakes and trying to stop in her lane, she swerved to the left and into a grass median where her Ford Explorer rolled over. She received a small scratch on her neck from the seat belt and refused treatment from EMS after they arrived on scene.

The Critical Pre-crash Event for V1 was coded this vehicle control loss due to sharp steering maneuver. The Critical Reason for the Critical Event was coded as an internal distraction because she was talking on her phone. An associated factor was traveling too fast for conditions, did not realize that caution was required.
