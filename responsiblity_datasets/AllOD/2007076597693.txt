This two-vehicle crash occurred on a straight, level, undivided, two-lane, wet roadway. There are parking lanes on both sides of this roadway. The speed limit on this roadway is 56 kmph (35 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.

Vehicle one (V1), a 2000 Dodge Ram pickup, was westbound in lane one. Vehicle two (V2), a 1983 Ford F150 pickup, was parked (unoccupied) on the right (north) side of the road. V1 traveled to the right and the front struck the back of V2. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 48 kmph for V1 and 48 kmph for V2.

The driver of V1, a fifty-two-year-old female, was on her way home from a store. She was well rested and familiar with her vehicle. She travels this route weekly. She has high blood pressure and high cholesterol and takes Metformin and Zocor. This driver stated that she was looking down at and adjusting the windshield wiper control. When she looked up, she realized that she had moved over into the parking lane and applied the brakes, skidding longitudinally.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: this vehicle traveling; over the lane line on right side of travel lane. The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as a driver related factor: internal distraction.
