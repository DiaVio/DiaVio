This two-vehicle crash occurred in a three-way, stop sign-controlled intersection that joins with a private driveway. On the north side of the intersection, the roadway is a two-lane, undivided private driveway with no controls and the southbound travel lane may turn right or left or travel through. On the east side of the intersection, the roadway is undivided with four lanes and the westbound travel lanes consist of a right turn lane, a through lane, and a left turn lane. This roadway is straight and level, with a posted speed limit of 48 kmph (30 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with rush hour traffic congestion.

Vehicle one (V1), a 1993 Ford Taurus with one occupant, was southbound in lane one, attempting to turn left from the private driveway. Vehicle two (V2), a 2003 Hyundai Accent with two occupants, was traveling westbound in lane two, approaching the intersection. Two non-contact vehicles westbound in lane one approaching the intersection were stopped, waiting to turn right into the private drive that leads to a shopping center. The driver of V1 slowly exited from the private driveway and proceeded to turn left. The driver of V2 saw V1 and applied the brakes, but was unable to avoid a collision. The front left of V1 contacted the front of V2. V1 rotated clockwise and came to rest facing south in the middle of the intersection. V2 rotated counterclockwise and came to rest facing south-southwest in close proximity to V1. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. The driver of V2 was transported. Police Reported Travel Speed is 8 kmph for V1 and 8 kmph for V2.

The driver of V1, a thirty-two-year-old male, was driving from the store to his home. He stated that there were two vehicles in the westbound right turn lane and that he was pulling out slowly in order to see around them before he made his turn. He accelerated to turn and that was when the crash occurred. He was well rested, drives this route weekly, very comfortable with his vehicle, and takes no medications. The driver was driving with a suspended license. He stated he had been stopped 3-4 seconds before attempting the left turn.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: other; leaving driveway, turning left. The critical reason for the critical event for V1 was coded as a driver related factor: turned with obstructed view.

The driver of V2, a thirty-four-year-old male, was driving his girlfriend from her job to her mother's house. He was well rested, drives this route daily, very comfortable with his vehicle, and takes no medications. He was conversing with his passenger at the time of the crash. He detected V1 and braked prior to impact. This driver was driving with a suspended license also. The non-contact vehicles also obscured his vision of V1.

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded: other vehicle encroachment - from driveway, turning into opposite direction. The critical reason for the critical event was not coded to this vehicle.
