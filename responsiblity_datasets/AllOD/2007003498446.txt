This crash occurred on the southbound lanes of a two-way roadway with two lanes of travel in each direction. This was a straight, level, and bituminous roadway with a posted speed limit of 48 kmph (30 mph). It was daylight and clear with no adverse conditions, the traffic flow was normal, and it was the afternoon hours of a weekday.

V1, a 2006 Mercedes-Benz ML320 with one occupant, was southbound in lane one. V2, a 1998 Infiniti QX4 with one occupant, was southbound in lane two. In an attempt to avoid a non-contact vehicle, V1 swerved into lane two and the left plane of V1 struck the right plane of V2. V2 then rotated clockwise and rolled left one-quarter turn on to its left plane. V1 came to a final rest approximately ten meters south from the point of impact. V2 came to a final rest on its left side in lane one approximately 15 meters south of the point of impact. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The driver of V1, a 55 year-old male, reported no injuries. He reported to the Police that he swerved to avoid another vehicle when he contacted V2 causing it to overturn. This driver could not be reached for an interview.

The Critical Precrash Event for V1 was coded this vehicle traveling, over the lane line on left side of travel lane. The Critical Reason for the Critical Event was coded as an unknown type of driver error. There were no known associated factors that could be coded to V1.

The driver of V2, a 31 year-old male, reported minor injuries and was transported to a medical facility. He also could not be located for an interview.

The Critical Precrash Event for V2 was coded other vehicle encroachment, from adjacent lane (same direction) - over right lane line. The Critical Reason for the Critical Event was not coded to this vehicle. There were no known associated factors that could be coded to V2.
