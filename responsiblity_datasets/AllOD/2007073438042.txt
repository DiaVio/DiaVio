This two-vehicle crash occurred in a four-legged, traffic signal-controlled intersection. The north/south roadway is undivided, straight, level and consists of two lanes in each direction. The speed limit for this roadway is 48 kmph (30 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.

Vehicle one (V1), a 2007 Nissan Altima, was northbound in lane two. Vehicle two (V2), a 2006 BMW 750LI, was southbound in lane one. Non-contact vehicles were stopped in southbound lane two. V1 turned left in the intersection and the front struck the front of V2. V2 then traveled to the southwest corner of the intersection and the front struck a signal control box. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The driver of V1, a forty-seven-year-old female, was familiar with her vehicle and travels this route weekly. She stated that the vehicles stopped in southbound lane two blocked her view of lane one. She further stated, "... there didn't seem to be anybody coming, so I started to turn."

The driver of V2, a twenty-two-year-old male, was on his way from his home to a tire store. He was healthy, well rested and familiar with his vehicle. He travels this route monthly. This driver stated that he did not see V1 until the last minute due to the vehicles in lane two.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "this vehicle traveling; turning left at intersection." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "driver related factor; turned with obstructed view."

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded as "other vehicle encroachment; from opposite direction - over left lane line." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V2 was "not coded to this vehicle."
