This two-vehicle crash occurred just past noon on a dry, straight, undivided, six-lane, bituminous roadway at the intersection of a private driveway. The roadway runs north and south with three lanes in each direction. Northbound lanes consist of a right turn lane, and two through lanes. Southbound lanes consist of a left turn lane and two through lanes. The private driveway is undivided and has one through lane in each direction. The speed limit on the roadway is 56 kmph (35 mph) and there was no posted speed limit in the private driveway. There were no adverse atmospheric conditions present at the time of the crash.

Vehicle one (V1), a 1998 Toyota Camry, was traveling west in lane one of the private driveway. Vehicle two (V2), a 1980 Harley Davidson motorcycle, was traveling north in lane three, the outside through lane. V1 entered the roadway intending to turn left. Once the driver of V1 pulled into the roadway, he saw V2 and stopped. V2 was intending to go straight, impacted with V1's front with its front. V1 came to rest at impact facing southwest in lane three of northbound traffic. V2 rotated counterclockwise, fell to its side and came to rest at impact facing northwest. Both vehicles were towed due to severe damage to the front. Police Reported Travel Speed is 8 kmph for V1 and 8 kmph for V2.

The driver of V1 was conversing with his passenger and stated he looked both left & right to monitor for cross traffic and did not see V2 before pulling into the intersection to turn left. The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: this vehicle traveling, turning left at intersection. The critical reason for the critical event was coded to V1 as a driver related factor: inadequate surveillance (e.g., failed to look, looked but did not see).

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded: other motor vehicle encroachment; from driveway, turning into opposite direction. The critical reason was not assigned to this vehicle.
