This crash took place on a two-lane, two-way roadway. Conditions were daylight, dry and cold during the morning hours of a weekend and there was a patch of ice and water adjacent the curb in the eastbound lane one. The roadway had a posted speed limit of 64kmph (40mph).

V1 was traveling east in lane one. V1 traveled over the patch of ice adjacent to the curb and lost control. V1 departed its lane to the left, returned to its lane to the left, departed its lane to the right, and then departed the roadway to the right. This vehicle then came into contact with a chain link fence first with its front right plane, then with its front left plane, while rotating clockwise. V1 continued until it made contact with a parked truck and came to rest in the front yard of a residence. Police Reported Travel Speed is 80 kmph for V1 and 80 kmph for V2.

V1, a 1997 Toyota Camry, was driven by a twenty-six year-old male who stated that he had been traveling ten to fifteen mph over the posted speed limit prior to traveling over the patch of ice. The driver stated that he had attempted avoidance manuvers but could not avoid the collision.

The critical pre crash event for this vehicle was coded Off the edge of the roadway on the right side and the critical reason was coded Too fast for conditions.
