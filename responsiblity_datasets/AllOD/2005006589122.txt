The two-vehicle crash occurred on three-lane southbound highway. The roadway was asphalt, dry, and level. The posted speed limit was 80 kmph (50 mph). The highway was fully controlled and there was no control device. The crash occurred in the early evening during the daylight hours on a weekday. The traffic flow was normal and the weather was clear.

Vehicle two 1996, a Chrysler Sebring, was traveling south in lane two when a car came by her at a fast speed and cut her car off hitting her in the left rear bumper. Her car spun around and hit the cement divider by the bridge support with its front. Vehicle two then rotated counter-clockwise traveled a few feet and came to rest facing eastward. Vehicle one, a 2003 Nissan Sentra, traveling in lane one contacted the left side of V2 with its front. Vehicle two was towed due to disabling damages and all three occupants were transported to the hospital. Police Reported Travel Speed is Unknown for V1 and Unknown for V2.

The drivers for vehicle one and vehicle two were transported to a nearby hospital and attempts to reach them were unsuccessful. For that reason, it is unsure what other circumstances may have played a role in the crash. That is, why vehicle two lost control of the vehicle and contacted the guardrail.
