This severe frontal crash involved a 51 year old male driver who was restrained and sustained serious injuries. This crash occurred in the curve of a two lane, two way roadway during daylight hours with clear conditions at dusk. Case vehicle one (V1), a 2004 Ford Focus four door sedan was northbound approaching a sharp curve to the right. The driver lost control of the vehicle and departed the roadway to the left and struck the front of V1 into a large tree. V1 was disabled and towed. The case occupant is the driver of V1 with no other passengers. He was a 51 year old male who was restrained with the lap/shoulder seat belt. On impact the belt retractor actuated and the frontal steering column mounted air bag deployed. He sustained serious injuries and was transported to the trauma center where he was hospitalized for three days.
