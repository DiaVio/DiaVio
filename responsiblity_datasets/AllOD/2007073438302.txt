This single-vehicle crash occurred on one-lane, one-way interchange-area ramp. This roadway curves to the right (the radius of curvature is 113 meters and the superelevation is three percent) and has an uphill grade. This roadway was icy at the time of the crash. There is a deep ditch (49 percent downgrade embankment) outside the right shoulder. The speed limit on this roadway is 64 kmph (40 mph). It was dark with streetlights on at the time of the crash with no adverse atmospheric conditions.

Vehicle one (V1), a 2002 Ford Explorer, was northbound on the ramp. V1 traveled off the left side of the roadway. The driver steered right and V1 returned to the roadway, rotating clockwise. The driver then steered left and V1 traveled off the right side of the roadway, rotating counterclockwise. V1 rolled over to the right one quarter-turn into the ditch. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

The driver of V1, a thirty-three-year-old female, was traveling with a thirty-three-year-old male passenger, from her home to work. She was healthy, well rested and comfortable with her vehicle and passenger. She travels this route daily. This driver stated that she didn't realize that the roadway was ice-covered until she lost control as the road straightened out after the curve.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "this vehicle traveling; off the edge of the road on the left side." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "environment related factor; slick roads (low friction road surface due to ice, loose debris, any other cause)."
