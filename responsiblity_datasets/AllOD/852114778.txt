This crash occurred at dusk with clear, dry conditions at an intersection with posted speed limits of 40 mph/64kmph for all directions of travel. Vehicle one (V1), a 2000 Pontiac Montana Van, was westbound on a two lane, two way street and had stopped for a stop sign at the intersection. Vehicle two (V2), a 1989 GMC Pickup, was travelling southbound on a two lane, two way street and was approaching the same intersection with no traffic controls for southbound traffic. V1 started to cross straight through the intersection and V2 tried to swerve left and locked up the brakes before the front of V2 impacted the right side of V1. V1 rotated slightly clockwise and then rolled over to the left side and slid on the left side to final rest on the southwest corner of the intersection in the eastbound lane. V2 rotated slightly clockwise and remained in the intersection near the point of impact. Case occupant is the driver of V1, a 30 year old female, was not wearing a seat belt and tried to brace with her hands and arms into the driver side window as the van rolled over to the left side. She then partially ejected out the shattered window while impacting the ground with her upper extremities. Evidence was located across the top and outside of the window frame as dents, fabric transfers and skin was located along with a blood found on scene on the pavement. No other occupants were in the case vehicle. The case occupant was transported to a local hospital before being airlifted to the trauma center. Both vehicles were disabled and towed from the crash scene.
