This single-vehicle crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions. The east/west roadway is undivided and consists of one lane in each direction. The westbound travel lane curves to the right (the radius of curvature is 563 meters and the superelevation is seven percent) and has a three percent downhill grade. The speed limit for this roadway is 89 kmph (55 mph).

Vehicle one (V1), a 1993 Ford Mustang, was westbound in lane one. V1 began to rotate clockwise and traveled off the right side of the road. V1 rolled over to the left two quarter-turns and was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 80 kmph for V1.

The driver of V1, a thirty-seven-year-old male, was on his way home from a co-worker's house. He was healthy, well rested and familiar with his vehicle. He rarely travels on this roadway. This driver's alcohol breath test result was .240.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "this vehicle control loss due to traveling too fast for conditions." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "driver related factor; too fast for curve/turn." This driver's poor directional control was an associated factor in this crash.
