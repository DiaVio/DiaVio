Vehicle 1 (V1) is a 2000 Honda Accord, four-door (case study vehicle). Vehicle 2 (V2) is a 1994 Honda Accord, four-door. V1 was occupied by a female, age 52 as its driver (case study participant). The case study participant was wearing the available lap and shoulder belt and the frontal air bags did deploy as a result of impact.

The crash occurred during the hours of darkness. The weather at the time of the crash was clear and dry. The roadway surface was dry. The posted speed limit is 50 mph (80 kmph).

The crash occurred in an intersection. The trafficway's north left consists of two through lanes, a right turn lane and left turn lane. The southbound leg has two through lanes, a right turn lane and a left turn lane. A raised concrete median divides the north and south legs of the intersection. The intersection is controlled by a traffic signal. The roadway is level in the area of the crash.

V1 was traveling northbound and was attempting to make a left turn. V2 was traveling southbound in the left through lane. As V1 began its turn, the front of V2 struck the front of V1. The impact caused V1 to rotate counterclockwise coming to rest in the middle of the intersection. V2 came to rest in the southbound left through lane.

The case study participant was transported by air to a trauma center.
