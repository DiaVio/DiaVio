This single-vehicle crash occurred on an urban roadway with no controls. This roadway is undivided with two lanes of travel; there is one lane in each direction, northbound and southbound. This roadway is straight and level, and has a posted speed limit of 48 kmph (30 mph). This crash occurred shortly after noon on a weekday with light traffic flow.

Vehicle one (V1), a 2006 Honda Civic with one occupant, was traveling northbound in lane one. The driver of V1 became distracted because she felt something on her arm and looked at it to scratch it. V1 departed the roadway on the left and began traveling with its left tires on grass and its right tires still on the roadway. With its front, V1 impacted a fire hydrant located on the southwest corner of a four-way, stop sign-controlled intersection. V1 crossed the west side of the intersection while skidding longitudinally. V1 entered the northwest corner of the intersection and came to final rest with its left tires on a sidewalk and its right tires on grass, facing north. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 48 kmph for V1.

The driver of V1, a sixteen-year-old female, was driving from a mall to her home. She was well rested, very comfortable with her vehicle, and drives this roadway daily. She is in good health and takes no medications. She had been driving vehicles for just two months.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: this vehicle traveling; off the edge of the road on the left side. The critical reason for the critical event for V1 was coded as a driver related factor: internal distraction. Inadequate surveillance, poor directional control and inexperienced driver were coded as associative factors.
