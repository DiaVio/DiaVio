This single vehicle collision occurred early morning at dawn. The collision occurred on the southbound lanes of an interstate highway. The highway is divided, dry, level, asphalt, and consists of five lanes. The east edge of the highway is bordered by an asphalt median. East of the median exists a raised concrete center divider that separates the southbound lanes from the northbound lanes. The highway is designated a 104 kmph (65 mph) zone.

Vehicle one (V1) is a 2000 Hyundai Elantra, four-door sedan, driven by a twenty six (26) year old female with one passenger. V1 was traveling southbound in the number five lane. As V1 traveled, the tread on the left rear tire partially separated. V1 lost control and traveled towards the center divider. The front plane of V1 contacted the center divider. V1 rotated counter-clockwise contacting the center divider with its left plane. V1 came to rest in the number five lane facing north and was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 113 kmph for V1.

The critical pre-crash event was coded: this vehicle traveling, off the edge of the road on the left side. The critical reason for the critical event was coded as a vehicle related factor: tires/wheels failed.
