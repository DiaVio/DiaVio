Vehicle one (V1 - case vehicle), a 2002 Ford Windstar, minivan was traveling east in the right eastbound lane of a six-lane divided freeway (three lanes eastbound, protected median, three lanes westbound). Vehicle two (V2), a 2003 Kenworth tractror pulling one trailer was also traveling east in the right eastbound lane. V2 was ahead of V1 and traveling a lower steady speed. It was dark, the weather was clear and the concrete road surface was dry. The driver of V1 reportedly fell asleep and the front of V1 struck and underrode the rear of V2's trailer. V2 came to a controlled stop and V1 was still engaged under the trailer. V1 was towed from the scene due to disabling vehicle damage V2 was driven away. The 31-year-old female driver of V1 (case occupant) was using the available three-point seat belt and the steering-wheel air bag deployed. She was taken by air ambulance to a regional level-one trauma center with severe (AIS-4) injuries.
