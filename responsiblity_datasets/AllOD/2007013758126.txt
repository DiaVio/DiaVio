2007-13-032

This crash occurred in the eastbound lane of a two-lane, two-way, non-divided rural, wet asphalt roadway with a posted speed limit of 89Kmph (55Mph). The crash occurred during the hours of dusk and there were no adverse conditions present. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

V1 a 1989 Chevy Beretta was traveling west bound in the right curb lane. V2 a 2000 Toyota Camry was traveling eastbound in the right curb lane. V1 attempted to make a left turn into a parking lot, when it came into contact with V2. Both vehicles were towed from the scene with moderate damage, and two occupants from V2 were transported to the hospital with minor injuries.

The driver of V1 a 24 year-old male stated at the scene that he was trying to turn left into the parking lot. He then stated that he saw that there was not enough time to make the turn so he steered back to the right to try and avoid the collision but could not.

The Critical Pre0Crash Event Coded for V1 is “this vehicle traveling over the lane line on the left side”. The Critical Reason for the Critical Event Coded for V1 is “Driver related, looked but did not see”.

The driver of V2 a 22 year-old male stated that he saw V1 cross the centerline, and hit his brakes but could not avoid the collision.

The Critical Pre-Crash Event Coded for V2 is “Other motor vehicle encroachment, from opposite direction over left lane line”. V2 was not assigned a Critical reason.
