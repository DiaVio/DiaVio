The crash occurred on the west roadside of a north/south urban roadway. The roadway was five-lanes wide and undivided, with two southbound lanes and three northbound. The roadway had a posted speed of 56 kmph (35 mph) and a level grade. The police reported speed limit was 15 mph, in reference to the parking lot on the roadside. Conditions were daylight and dry with partly cloudy skies. The crash occurred in the mid-morning hours on a weekday.

Vehicle one was a 2000 Honda Accord sedan traveling in the second lane. V1 had just negotiated a left curve in the roadway. V1 traveled across lane one and departed the roadway, traveling across a parking lot. V1 traveled off the edge of the parking lot and impacted a small building with its front left corner. V1 then came to rest on its left plane in a gap between the parking lot and the building. Police Reported Travel Speed is 24 kmph for V1.

The Honda Accord was driven by an 18-year old female who was accompanied by a 20-year old female. Both were checked out at the scene of the crash by EMS personnel. The driver stated that she has a very weak stomach and experiences frequent bouts of nausea and light-headedness. She had a stomach ache the morning of the crash. Just prior to the crash she told her passenger that she was feeling dizzy. The driver then fainted and the passenger steered the vehicle right and to its position of final rest. The driver estimated her travel speed to be 40 mph, and the passenger estimated the impact speed to be 35 mph. The vehicle was towed due to damage.

The Critical Precrash Event was V1 traveling off the edge of the road on the right side. The Critical Reason was a physical impairment of the ability to act (101). No associated factors were coded to the driver of V1.
