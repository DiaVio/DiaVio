This crash occurred on a level bituminous four-lane undivided east/west roadway at an intersection with a driveway to a retail establishment. The posted speed limit was 56 KMPH (35 MPH). The weather was cloudy and the roadway was dry. This crash occurred on a weekday afternoon.

Vehicle #1, a 2002 Chevrolet Impala 4-door sedan, was making a left turn from a retail driveway when it was impacted in the left rear by Vehicle #2, a 2003 Honda Odyssey minivan. Vehicle #2 was traveling east in lane one when its front left struck Vehicle #2. After impact, Vehicle #2 continued forward and to the right where its front right then struck a utility pole. Vehicle #1 came to final rest in the roadway facing in a northwesterly direction. Vehicle #2 came to final rest at the utility pole facing in a southeasterly direction. Vehicle #2 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

Vehicle #1, the Chevrolet Impala, was driven by a belted 64 year-old female who was not injured. There was a belted 60 year-old female passenger in the front right seat who was slightly injured. This passenger was treated at the scene. The driver of this vehicle stated she had come to a stop, looked both ways, and waited for some westbound traffic to pass before proceeding with her left turn to the west. She stated that the eastbound lanes were free of traffic. It should be noted that this driver was on disability and was taking several medications, which have the side effects of drowsiness, dizziness and sleepiness.

The Critical Pre-crash Event coded to Vehicle #1 was: This vehicle traveling - turning left at intersection. The Critical Reason for the Critical Pre-crash Event was coded as: Driver Related Factor, Inadequate Surveillance. Associated factors coded to the driver were the medical conditions of chronic back pain, high blood pressure, ulcer, and a sleep disorder, along with the use of several prescription medications to treat these conditions.

Vehicle #2, the Honda Odyssey, was driven by a 52 year-old female who was not injured. This driver stated she had seen V1 stop before proceeding in front of her. She stated she tried to avoid the crash by braking and steering to her right, but was unsuccessful.

The Critical Pre-crash Event coded to Vehicle #2 was: Other vehicle encroachment from driveway, turning into opposite direction. The Critical Reason for the Critical Pre-crash Event was not coded to this vehicle. One associated factor coded to this driver was a false assumption, because she assumed that V1 would wait for her to pass before initiating its left turn.
