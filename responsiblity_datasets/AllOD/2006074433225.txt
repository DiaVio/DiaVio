This two-vehicle crash occurred on a one-way, four-lane, straight roadway. This roadway has a 6.5% uphill grade. The speed limit on this roadway is 56 kmph (35 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.

Vehicle one (V1), a 1998 Gillig bus, in use as a metro transit bus, was stopped westbound in lane one, loading passengers. Vehicle two (V2), a 1995 Dodge Intrepid was westbound in lane one. The front of V1 struck the back of V2. V2 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 0 kmph for V1 and 0 kmph for V2.

The driver of V1, a fifty-year-old male, was traveling from one bus stop to the next on his normal route. He was healthy, well rested and familiar with his vehicle. He drives this route daily. At the time of the crash, this driver was paying attention to the incoming passengers and the fare machine.

The driver of V2, a twenty-four-year-old male, was traveling from his home to work. He was well rested, healthy and comfortable with his vehicle. He drives this route daily. This driver stated that he was late for work and was looking to the left at the time of the crash because he was trying to move into lane two. He made no avoidance maneuvers.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "other motor vehicle in lane; traveling in same direction with higher speed." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was "not coded to this vehicle."

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded as "other motor vehicle in lane; other vehicle stopped." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded as "driver related factor; external distraction."
