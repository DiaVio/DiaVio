This crash occurred in the southbound lanes of a four-lane highway divided by a grass median. There were two southbound lanes and two northbound lanes. This was a straight, level, bituminous, roadway with a posted speed limit of 89 kmph (55 mph). It was early morning on a weekday, and the road was icy and snow covered. Traffic flow was heavy due to the morning rush hour and it was snowing.

V1 was a 2002 Honda Accord, driven by a 56 year-old female. V2 was a 2006 GMC Yukon, driven by a 47 year-old male. V1 and V2 were traveling southbound, V1 was in lane one and V2 was in lane two. Both vehicles were traveling approximately 30 mph due to the road conditions, and were side by side. V1 lost traction on the roadway and crossed into lane two, where it was struck by V2 in the driver's door. V1 rotated clockwise and caused sideswipe damage on the right side of V2. V1 then traveled back to the right where it came to rest off of the roadway. V2 traveled off of the left side of the roadway, through the grass median and came to final rest facing north. Police Reported Travel Speed is 48 kmph for V1 and 48 kmph for V2.

The Critical Precrash Event for V1 was coded this vehicle control loss, due to poor road conditions, icy roads. The Critical Reason for the Critical Precrash Event was a driver related factor, traveling too fast for conditions (icy, snow-covered roadway). The driver was familiar with this roadway and was attempting to proceed at a safe speed for the conditions. Associated factors include the poor road conditions and the rush hour traffic.

The Critical Precrash Event for V2 was coded other vehicle encroachment, from adjacent lane over the right lane line. The Critical Reason was not coded to this vehicle. Poor road conditions and the rush hour traffic were the only associated factors.
