This crash occurred on a five-lane, non-divided asphalt city roadway with a posted speed limit of 80 kmph (50mph). The crash occurred during the early evening hours while dark, and there were no adverse conditions present at the time of the crash. The roadway was illuminated by streetlamps.

V1, a 1995 Chevrolet K1500 pickup truck, was stopped in a business driveway waiting to turn left onto the five-lane roadway. V2, a 1991 Chevrolet Lumina coupe, was traveling westbound in the right curb lane approaching the private driveway. V1 pulled out and started to make its turn when it contacted the right side of V2. V2 was towed from the scene with moderate damage and both occupants were transported to the hospital. V1 was not towed away from the scene and neither one of V1's occupants were injured. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The driver of V1, a 53 year-old male, stated that he stopped and looked both ways but did not see V2. He then stated that by the time he saw V2 it was too late and he did not have time to hit his brakes.

The Critical Pre-Crash Event coded for V1 was "this vehicle traveling, turning left at intersection." The Critical Reason for the Critical Pre-Crash Event coded for V1 was "driver related, inadequate surveillance - looked but did not see." Associated factors coded to the driver of V1 include back problems and alcohol use. A police Breathalyzer conducted on scene revealed a BAC of .07.

The driver of V2, a 21 year-old female, stated that she did not see V1 before she felt a bang. She then stated that she did not have any time to avoid the collision.

The Critical Pre-Crash Event coded for V2 was "Other vehicle encroachment from driveway, turning into opposite direction." V2 was not assigned a Critical Reason and no associated factors were coded to the driver.
