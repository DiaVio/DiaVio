This two vehicle daytime collision occurred in the intersection of a four lane arterial road, and a driveway from a business parking lot. The roads are paved with asphalt. The southbound intersecting roadway is controlled with a stop sign and has an uphill grade of 15%. For east/west traffic the roadway level. The speed limit for southbound traffic was posted at 7 kmph (5 mph) and for westbound traffic the posted speed limit was 48 kmph (30 mph). At the time of the crash the sky was cloudy and misting with wet roadways.

Vehicle one (V1), a 1987 Cadillac Deville coupe driven by a eighty four (84) year old male, was traveling south in lane one in the parking lot. Vehicle two (V2), a 1991 Subaru Legacy sedan driven by a twenty one (21) year old male, was traveling west in lane one. As V1 pulled into the roadway to turn left, the front of V2 impacted the left side. V1 then rotated clockwise 180 degrees coming to rest facing north across lanes one and two of the eastbound lanes. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: this vehicle traveling, turning left at intersection. The critical reason for the critical event assigned to this driver is coded driver related factor: inadequate surveillance (e.g., failed to look, looked but did not see). The driver of V1 told the interviewee that he did not see V2 until after he had started to make his turn.

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded: other vehicle encroachment; from crossing street, turning into opposite direction. The critical reason for the critical event is not coded to this vehicle.
