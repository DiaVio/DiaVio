This is a single vehicle crash occurred late afternoon during daylight hours in a rural area on a 4-lane, dry, asphalt roadway with an extended median. In the area of the crash, the northbound travel lanes curve left . The speed limit for this area is 104 kmph (65 mph). There were no adverse road conditions or weather conditions present.

Vehicle one (V) a 1999 Ford Contour 4-door sedan driven by a 19 year-old female was traveling northbound in the #1 travel lane headed west. The driver attempted to lower the sun visor in an attempt to eliminate the sun glare and the vehicle wandered to the right road edge. The driver overcompensated as she steered left, the vehicle crossed the center line of the roadway, ran off the south road edge before the front of the vehicle struck the embankment maintaining contact for approximately 6 meters. the vehicle continued to rotate counter-clockwise as the front tires re-entered lane #2 and the vehicle came to rest partially on the shoulder and partially on the #2 lane facing north. The vehicle was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 105 kmph for V1.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: this vehicle traveling, off the edge of the road on the left side. Critical reason for the critical event driver related factor: Internal distraction. Associative factors, include: sun glare, internal distraction pulling sun visor down and overcompensation.
