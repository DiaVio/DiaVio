The crash involving two vehicles occurred during mid-afternoon hours on a one-way, four lane roadway. The road was dry, traveled, level asphalt. The posted speed limit is 48 kmph (30 mph). Traffic was light at the time of the collision. There were no adverse weather conditions.

Vehicle one (V1), a 1991 Chevrolet Silverado 2500 pick-up truck, was driven by a 18 year-old male with two passengers was traveling southbound in the second travel lane. Vehicle two (V2), a 1995 Toyota Camry, was driven by a 50 year-old male who was traveling southbound in the third travel lane. V1 attempted to make a left turn across two lanes and across the path of V2. V1 was struck on the left side by the right side of V2. Both vehicles moved southeast and came to rest off the roadway to the east. Both vehicles were towed from the scene and there were no reported injuries. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: this vehicle traveling, over the lane line on the left side of travel lane. The critical reason was coded to V1 as a driver related factor: illegal maneuver (turning left from wrong lane).

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded: other vehicle encroachment from adjacent lane-over the right lane line. The critical reason was not coded to this vehicle.
