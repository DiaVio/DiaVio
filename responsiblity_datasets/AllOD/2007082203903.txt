This single vehicle daytime right roadside-departure collision occurred on a downtown city street. The roadway consists of four lanes of straight downhill one-way travel paved with bituminous asphalt. There is parking on both sides of the street, and a bicycle lane on the left side of the road. The speed limit for the road is 48 kmph (30 mph). At the time of the crash the weather was clear and the roadway was dry.

Vehicle one (V1), a stolen 2005 Dodge Grand Caravan minivan driven by a unknown age female, was traveling south in lane one. Vehicle two (V2), a 1997 Mercury Villager minivan, and vehicle three (V3), a 1996 Ford F250 pickup, were legally parked on the right side of the road in the parking lane. V1 veered off the road to the right, sideswiping V2 and knocking off the driver's side mirror. V1 continued into the rear of V3, making solid contact and pushing V3 forward into other parked vehicles. V1 and V3 were disabled and were towed from the scene. The driver of V1 reported possible injuries but was taken into custody. The driver of V1 was unable to be interviewed because incorrect contact information was given to the police and she could not be later contacted. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: this vehicle traveling, over the lane line on right side of travel lane. The critical reason for the critical event assigned to this driver is coded as a driver related factor: unknown critical non-performance. It was unknown if the driver simply fell asleep, possibly from an extended period of taking drugs & alcohol, or if there was a genuine blackout. The police report indicated the driver had consumed drugs & alcohol and tests were pending. A witness listed in the police report indicates V1 just traveled off the right side of the travel lane and impacted the parked vehicles without any brake lights coming on or attempted evasive actions taken.
