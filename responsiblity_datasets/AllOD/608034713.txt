The case vehicle, a two-door 1999 Ford Escort was traveling in the southbound lane of a dry two-lane asphalt roadway with a downgrade. It was dark and there was no adverse weather. The case vehicle was entering a left-sweeping curve when it drove off of the right side of the road went down an embankment, and struck an 18-cm tree with its front-end and came to a rest facing south-southeast. The vehicle was towed due to damage. The 22-year-old female driver was the sole occupant and was restrained by a belt restraint and the steering-wheel air bag deployed. The driver was transported to a level-one trauma center with severe injuries and enrolled as a case occupant.
