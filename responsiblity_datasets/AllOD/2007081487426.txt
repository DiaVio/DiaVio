This single-vehicle collision occurred shortly after noon during daylight hours on a busy interstate highway. The collision occurred on a four-lane divided roadway as the roadway begins a slight curve to the left. The area of the collision also had a slight uphill grade and concrete paving. The roadway was wet and it was raining heavily. The posted speed limit for the roadway is 97 kmph (60 mph). Access to the roadway is controlled.

Vehicle one (V1) was a 1998 Honda Accord 2-door driven by a thirty two year old female with two passengers. They were traveling westbound in lane two or three, when the vehicle lost control and departed the roadway via the right shoulder. The front left corner of V1 impacted the concrete barrier, and V1 began to rotate counterclockwise. The left side door panel was torn off of the vehicle, and the left rear tire was damaged on the concrete barrier. The vehicle continued to rotate, and came to rest facing north up against the barrier. The vehicle was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

The driver was not available to be interviewed.

The critical pre-crash event was coded: this vehicle traveling, off the edge of the road on the right side. The critical reason for the critical event was coded as a driver related factor: too fast for conditions (specified as: heavy rain).
