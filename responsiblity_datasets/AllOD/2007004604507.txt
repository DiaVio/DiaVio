The crash occurred in the westbound lane of a "T" intersection composed of a single-lane one-way northbound driveway, and a three-lane east/westbound roadway. The east/westbound roadway was bituminous, level with a posted speed limit of 80 kmph (50 mph). It was early morning on a weekday. The roads were dry and the traffic flow was moderate.

V1, a 1994 Mitsubishi Diamante with one occupant, was traveling east in lane two when it attempted to turn left at the intersection and struckV2 on the left side. V2, a 2001 Nissan Sentra with one occupant, was traveling west in lane one. As V2 passed through the intersection it was struck V1. V2 attempted to avoid the collision by turning right and applying his brakes. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

V1, driven by a 20-year-old female who reported an injury to the arm and was transported, stated that she decelerated before attempting to turn left, however the sun was in her eyes and she never saw V2. She attempted the turn and struck V2. V1 made no avoidance maneuver.

The Critical Precrash Event for V1 was "This Vehicle Traveling-Turning left at intersection". The Critical reason for the Critical Event was "Environmental Related Factor-Glare". The following associated factor was assigned. "Other decision factor-turned with obstructed view".

The Driver of V2, a 33-year-old male, reported a complaint of pain was transported. Driver stated that he was traveling at approximately 80 kmph (50 mph) as he entered the intersection. He saw V1 approach the turn and assumed V1 would stop before turning. V1 then turned without stopping striking V2. V2 attempted to avoid the collision by turning right and applying his brakes.

The Critical Precrash Event for V2 was "Other vehicle encroachment from opposite direction-over left lane line". No Critical Reason was coded to this vehicle. No associated factors were assigned.
