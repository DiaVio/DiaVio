This single-vehicle crash occurred in a rural residential area on a two-lane asphalt roadway. The northbound lane curves left and is uphill prior to the crash location. The speed limit is 56 kmph (35 mph). The crash occurred at mid-day daylight hours. The weather was cloudy and the road had dried after an early morning light rain.

Vehicle one (V1), a 1997 Ford Escort sedan driven by a nineteen (19) year old female, was northbound when the driver applied the brakes in order to slow for an approaching intersection. The brakes did not respond and V1 rotated clockwise as it departed the right road edge. V1 contacted a small bush and a dirt embankment before flipping over 2 quarter turns to the left coming to rest on it's top facing north. The driver stated when she tried to brake, the pedal went to the floor and did not respond. Brake fluid was found leaking at the crash location. She stated she had driven 250 miles earlier in the week with no problems with her vehicle. She had purchased the vehicle 2 months prior. V1 was towed from the scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is 48 kmph for V1.

The critical pre-crash event was coded this vehicle traveling off the edge of the road on the right side. The critical reason for the critical event was coded as a vehicle related, "degraded braking capability". The officer on scene checked the brakes to verify driver's statement.
