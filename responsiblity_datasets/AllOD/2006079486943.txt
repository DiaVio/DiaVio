This two vehicle crash occurred shortly past noon during daylight hours. The collision occurred at a four leg intersection. The north/south roadway is a five lane, dry, level, asphalt roadway. For east/west traffic the roadway is two lanes, dry, level and made of asphalt. The north/south roadway is designated 56 kph (35 mph). The east/west roadway is designated 40 kmph (25 mph) and is controlled by a stop sign.

Vehicle one (V1) a 1993 Toyota Camry sedan, driven by a 19 year-old female was traveling east in the number one lane. Vehicle two (V2) a 1998 Ford Windstar, driven by a 54 year-old male with one passenger was traveling north in the number two lane. As V 1 was making a left turn its right plane was impacted by V2's front plane. V1 and V2 headed in a northeasterly direction. V1 came to rest on the northeast curb facing northeast. V2 came to rest at the northeast corner facing northeast. The driver of V1 stated that when she was pulling out she was focused on a large truck crossing in the number two lane traveling northbound and did not recheck northbound traffic behind the large truck while negotiating her left turn. She intended to enter the number two northbound lane. V1 was towed from the scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: this vehicle traveling, turning left at intersection. The critical reason for the critical event was coded to V1 as a driver related factor: inadequate surveillance (e.g., failed to look, looked but did not see). The driver failed to look back to her right to check for cross-traffic before pulling into the intersection to turn left.

The critical pre-crash event: for V2 was coded: other vehicle encroachment; from crossing street, turning into same direction. The critical reason was not coded to this vehicle.
