This crash occurred on the roadside of an icy two-lane, two-way, non-divided rural asphalt roadway with a posted speed limit of 89 kmph (55 mph). The crash occurred during daylight hours, when the roadway was covered in a sheet of ice.

V1, a 1992 Pontiac Grand Prix with one occupant was westbound in lane one. The driver of V1 lost control of the vehicle and departed the roadway to the right side. V1 struck the side of the ditch that ran parallel to the roadway and continued rotating clockwise. V1 than rolled left two quarter turns before coming to rest on its roof in the ditch. V1 was towed from the scene due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

The driver of V1, a 20-year-old male, suffered non-incapacitating injuries and was transported for treatment. He stated that he lost control of his vehicle on the icy roadway and that he tried to steer left to correct while braking, but he could not regain control.

The Critical Precrash Event for V1 was coded this vehicle loss of control, traveling too fast for conditions. The Critical Reason for the Critical Event coded for V1 was coded as driver related factor too fast for ice-covered roads. An associated factor was an internal distraction as the driver was smoking at the time of the crash.
