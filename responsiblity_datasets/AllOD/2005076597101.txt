This single vehicle crash occurred during early morning hours around dawn on a two-lane non-divided asphalt constructed roadway. This roadway has various curves and grades with guard railing on both side in places. At the area of this crash the roadway curves left and goes up hill with a grade of +7 percent. The posted speed limit is 80 kmph (50 mph) with no obvious defects or weather conditions.

Vehicle one (V1), a 1995 Toyota Corolla sedan driven by a 27 year-old male was traveling southbound going up the grade and negotiating a left hand curve when it began drifting across the right edge of the roadway. The driver applied left hand steering but V1 continued off the right road edge. The driver applied braking and V1 began sliding on the dirt shoulder and slid into the end of a guardrail. V1 was towed from the scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is 80 kmph for V1.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: this vehicle traveling, off the edge of the road on the right side. The critical reason for the critical event was coded as a driver related factor: poor directional control. An associated factor was fatigue. The driver indicated he has difficulty sleeping. He had been driving for several hours when the crash occurred.
