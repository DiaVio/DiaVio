This two vehicle crash occurred shortly before noon during daylight hours at the intersection of a four-lane, two-way, east-west, undivided roadway with a three-lane, one-way northbound roadway. The intersection was controlled by a tri-color traffic signal with pedestrian controls. The roadways were straight, level, concrete with a posted speed of 56 kmph (35 mph) northbound and 48 kmph (30 mph) east and westbound. the road was dry and the sky was clear. Traffic flow was moderate.

Vehicle one (V 1), a 1998 Mitsubishi Montero Sport sport utility vehicle driven by a 28 year-old female was eastbound in lane two. Vehicle two (V 2), a 1993 Ford F-150 pick-up driven by a 32 year-old male was traveling northbound in lane two. V 2 entered the intersection without the right-of-way and was struck on the left side by the front of V 1. V 2 rotated counterclockwise and came to rest in the roadway north of the intersection. The post impact movement of V 1 is unknown. V 1 was pushed to a nearby parking lot by the fire department prior to researchers arrival. V 1 was towed from the scene,driver refused to be interviewed. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The driver of V 2 advised that he was traveling between 32-48 kmph (20-30 mph) at the time of impact. He did not see V 1 prior to the collision and took no evasive action. V 2 was driven from the scene.

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded: this vehicle traveling, crossing over/passing through intersection. The Critical Reason for the Critical Event was driver related factor- unknown recognition error.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: other vehicle encroachment; from crossing street- across path. The Critical reason not coded to this vehicle.

Associated factors for the driver of V 2 was illegal maneuver, failed to stop for traffic control device. also inadequate surveillance- the driver of V 2 did not report seeing V 1 prior to impact.
