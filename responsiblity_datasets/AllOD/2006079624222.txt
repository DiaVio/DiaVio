A two-vehicle collision occurred on a clear day at dusk. The collision occurred on a four-leg intersection comprised of a north/south roadway and an east/west roadway. The north/south roadway is undivided, dry, level, asphalt, and consists of seven lanes. The east/west roadway is undivided, dry, level, and asphalt. The west leg consists of five lanes. The east leg consists of three lanes. Both roadways are designated 56 kph (35 mph) zones. The intersection is controlled by a tri-light signal that was functioning at the time of the collision.

Vehicle one (V1), a 1997 Volkswagen Passat, four-door sedan with one occupant, was traveling southbound in the number four (left-turn) lane. Vehicle two (V2), a 1986 Honda Prelude, two-door sedan with one occupant, was traveling northbound in the number one lane. As V1 made its left turn, the front of V2 struck the right side of V1. V1 rotated counter-clockwise and came to rest facing the northeast corner. V2 came to rest facing northeast blocking the number one lane. V1 received major damage and was towed from the scene. V2 received moderate damage and was reported as not towed from the scene, however a tow truck was observed removing it from the scene. Neither driver was transported. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The driver of V1 is a 26-year-old male traveling to a cousin's home from his mother-in-law's home. The driver was lost and had realized that he past his destination. The driver was making a left so that he could turn around and head north. The driver is on vacation from his work. He has been on vacation for approximately a week. The driver is a medium/heavy truck driver with endorsements for hazardous materials, tankers, doubles, and triples. The driver takes vitamins. The driver played soccer for two hours the day before the collision. He has only driven through the collision's area three times in three months. The driver stated that he was stopped for approximately 5 to 10 seconds prior to making his turn. He stated that traffic in all of the northbound lanes stopped except for V2. The driver assumed that V2 would also yield.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded “this vehicle traveling-turning left at intersection. A critical reason was not coded to this vehicle.

The driver of V2 is a 49-year-old male traveling home from a friend's home. The driver left his job four hours earlier and headed to a friend's home to unwind after a long day at work. The driver stated he had approximately four 12-ounce beers. He finished his last beer approximately 30 minutes prior to heading home. Breath samples approximately 40 minutes after the collision showed a BAC of 0.164. The driver was thinking about getting home for dinner as he was driving. The driver has high cholesterol, but has not taken medication for a long time. The driver takes vitamins. The driver bought the car approximately three to four months ago for his daughter. He has only driven the car approximately six times. This is the second time he has borrowed the car to go to work. The car's engine was recently repaired. The car also recently passed a smog check. The driver was convicted of driving under the influence about five years ago. His license was suspended at the time for one year, but is currently valid. The driver detected V1 in the intersection but assumed the vehicle would yield the right of way. The driver stated that V1 suddenly turned in front of him as he entered the intersection. The driver applied his brakes in an attempt to avoid the impact.

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded “other vehicle encroachment-from opposite direction-over left lane line”. The critical reason was coded “illegal maneuver”. The driver failed to stop for the traffic signal. Other northbound vehicles stopped prior to entering the intersection.
