This four-vehicle chain reaction crash occurred on a two-way, two-lane roadway. There are parking lanes on each side of the roadway. The posted speed limit on the roadway was 56kph (35mph). There was some rain, just prior to the crash. The gradient for all the vehicles involved was level.

Vehicle one (V1), a 2000 Ford Explorer, was traveling in the southbound lane, at an unknown speed, intending to continue straight on the roadway. Vehicle four (V4) was a legally parked vehicle, a 2005 Subaru Outback. Vehicle three (V3) was a 1992 Ford Ranger, which was stopped in the roadway in front of V1. Vehicle two (V2), a 2004 Mercury Mountaineer was stopped in front of V3 on the roadway. The right side of V1 made contact with the parked car, V4. V1 left front struck the right rear of V3. From the chain reaction, the front of V3 struck V2 in the rear. V1, was in the parking lane for final rest.
