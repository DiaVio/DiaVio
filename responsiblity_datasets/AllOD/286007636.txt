This case involves a rear impact to the case vehicle resulting in the serious injuries to the right rear seated case occupant. The crash occurred at night at an illuminated four-way intersection controlled by traffic signals. The roadway was clear of debris and the weather was clear at the time of the crash. Vehicle 1 (V1 - case vehicle), a 2003 Hyundai Elantra four-door sedan, was traveling west on a four-lane roadway separated by double yellow line. Vehicle 2 (V2), a 1992 Ford Bronco SUV, was traveling west behind V1. The crash occurred when V1 stopped at the intersection to make a left turn and the frontal plane of V2 impacted the rear plane of V1. The front two tires of the V2 over-rode the trunk of V1. Both the vehicles remained engaged facing west at the final rest position. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. The case vehicle was driven by a 37 year old female restrained by the manual lap and shoulder belt. A 33 year old male was seated in the right front seat restrained by the manual lap and shoulder belt. Both front seat occupants sustained minor injuries and were not transported to the hospital. A 2 year old male (case occupant) was in the right rear seat restrained by the 5 point harness of a forward facing child restraint. The safety seat was attached to the vehicle using the LATCH system and the top tether was used. He sustained serious injuries and was transported to the nearest trauma center by flight unit.
