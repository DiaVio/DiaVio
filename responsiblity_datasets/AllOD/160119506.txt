Vehicle 1 (V1) is a 2004 Toyota Camry, four-door (case study vehicle). Vehicle 2 (V2) is a 1994 Ford Crown Vic, four-door. V1 was occupied by a female, age 80 as its driver (case study participant). The case study participant was wearing the available lap and shoulder belt. The air bags did not deploy as a result of impact. The crash occurred during the hours of daylight. The weather at the time of the crash was clear and dry. The roadway surface was dry. The posted speed limit is 35 mph (56 kmph). The crash occurred at an intersection controlled by a traffic signal. The roadway travels in an east-west direction. The westbound direction consist of two through lanes and a dedicated left turn lane. The eastbound direction consist of two through lanes and a dedicated left turn lane. The roadway is level in the area of the crash. The roadway is divided by a raised concete median on the west side and a double yellow line on the east side. V1 was traveling eastbound in the left turn lane. V2 was traveling westbound in the left through lane. V1 turned into the path of V2 and V2's driver steered to the right in an attempt to avoid the crash. V2's front struck the right side of V1. This impact caused V1 to rotate counterclockwise and V2 to rotate clockwise until V1's right side struck the left side of V2. V1 came to rest on the west left's right, westbound through lane, facing west, while V2 came to rest on the north left's west side, facing northwest. The case study participant was transported by ground to a trauma center.
