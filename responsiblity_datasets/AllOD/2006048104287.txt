This two-vehicle crash occurred late in the afternoon on a five-lane, level, straight, dry concrete, roadway that runs east to west and supports two-way traffic. This roadway intersects with a five-lane, level, straight, concrete, median divided roadway that runs north to south. This tee intersection is controlled by a control signal on colors with no pedestrian signals. There were no adverse atmospheric conditions present at the time of the crash.

Vehicle one (V1), a 2002 Chevrolet Tahoe driven by a 21 year-old female was traveling west in lane three. Vehicle two (V2) a 1995 Toyota Corolla driven by a 30 year-old male with one passenger was traveling east in lane two. V1 attempted a left turn to travel south as V1 began its turn the front of V1 contacted the front of V2. Post-impact, V1 rotated clockwise and came to rest in lane one of the eastbound roadway facing east. Post-impact V2 rotated counterclockwise and came to rest facing east in lane one of the eastbound roadway. Both vehicles were towed from the scene. The driver and passenger of V2 were transported to a local medical facility, Police Reported Travel Speed is 8 kmph for V1 and 8 kmph for V2.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: this vehicle traveling, turning left at intersection. The critical reason for the critical event was coded to V1 as a driver related factor: false assumption of other's actions. The driver stated that she assumed V2 was going to yield the right of way to her.

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded: other vehicle encroachment; from crossing street, across path. The critical reason was not coded to this vehicle.
