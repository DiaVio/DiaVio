This single vehicle roll-over crash occurred during early evening daylight hours on a two lane county roadway. This roadway is asphalt constructed with various curves and grades with a posted speed limit of 48 kmph (30 mph). At the area of this event the roadway curves left then straightens out a short distance then goes into anther left hand curve with a down grade of -6 %. This roadway is 7.6 meters wide with a double yellow centerline and .8 meters of shoulder on both sides. This roadway runs north to south with a drainage ditch on the east road edge and no adverse weather. There are warning signs posted before the crash warning of the curves and speed.

Vehicle one (V1), a 2000 Chevrolet C2500 series pickup driven by a 20 year-old male with one 16 year-old female passenger was traveling north in lane one then changed lane to the left to pass slower vehicles over corrected while returning to lane one V1 then departed roadway to the right and rolled on to roof. V1 was towed from the scene due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

The critical precrash event for V1 was coded: this vehicle traveling, off the edge of the road on the right side. The critical reason for the critical event was coded as a driver related factor: Internal distraction. The driver & passenger were having an argument & were wrestling. An associate factor was also coded to inattention for arguing with passenger.
