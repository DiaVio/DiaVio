This two vehicle daytime collision occurred at a four-leg intersection. The northbound and southbound roadway is seven lanes, dry, level and has a left turn pocket. The south leg is curved to the right for northbound traffic. The eastbound roadway is six lanes, dry, level and has a left and right turn lane. The westbound roadway has five lanes, dry, and level. The westbound roadway has a left turn pocket and a bike lane near the south curb. The intersection is controlled by a on-colors signal that was working properly at the time. All four legs are comprised of asphalt. Both roadways are designated 56 kmph (35 mph).

Vehicle one (V1) is a 2007 Toyota Prius hybrid 4-door hatchback. V1 was driven by a thirty seven (37) year old female who was traveling north in the number four left turn lane. Vehicle two (V2) is a 1999 Toyota Avalon 4-door sedan, driven by a fifty four (54) year old female who was traveling south in the number three lane. As V1 made its left turn its right plane was impacted V2's front plane. V1 rotated clockwise and came to rest facing north in the intersection. V2 came to rest facing south in the intersection. V1 was towed with moderate damage. V2 was towed with moderate damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: this vehicle traveling, turning left at intersection. The critical reason for the critical event was coded to V1 as a driver related factor: type of driver error unknown. The driver of V1 is a 37 year-old female who refused to be interviewed, so it could not be determined if the critical reason was a recognition or decision factor.

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded: other vehicle encroachment; from opposite direction, over the left lane line. The critical reason was not coded to this vehicle. The driver of V2 has high blood pressure and takes Hydrochlorothiazide.
