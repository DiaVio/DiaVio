This crash two vehicle crash occurred in the middle of the afternoon on a clear day during daylight hours, at an on-colors controlled 4-leg intersection. The posted speed limit 48 kph (30 mph) roadway surface level bituminous. Each leg of the intersection was comprised of two travel lanes with one in each direction.

Vehicle one (V1) was a 1997 Chevrolet C1500 pickup driven by a twenty one (21) year-old female with two passengers. V1 was traveling east in lane one. looking for an address. Vehicle two (V2) was a 1991 Ford Explorer 4x4 driven by a thirty one (31) year-old male. V2 was traveling straight in the northbound travel lane. As the driver of V1 conversed with the passenger, she failed to see traffic control signal, entered intersection on a red light. The front of V1 struck the left side of V2. After impact V2 rotated counterclockwise coming to final rest at the northeast corner of the intersection. V1 continued in a northeasterly direction and came to final rest in the eastbound lane. Both vehicles were towed from the scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: this vehicle traveling, crossing over (passing through) intersection. The critical reason for the critical event was coded to V1 as a driver related factor: inattention (i.e., daydreaming), for talking to the passenger. The driver was also somewhat distracted looking to the left for a street address. It was her first time on this roadway and she was uncomfortable with the location they were in.

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded: other vehicle encroachment; from crossing street, across path. The critical reason was not coded to this vehicle.
