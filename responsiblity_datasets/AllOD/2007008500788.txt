This crash occurred on a two-lane roadway. The roadway was curved to the right, had a downhill grade, was bituminous, and had a posted speed limit of 56 kmph (35 mph). It was mid-evening on a weekday, the roadway was wet, and conditions were foggy.

V1 was a 1997 Nissan Sentra, driven by a 46 year-old female. V1 was traveling downhill and had was beginning to enter a right hand curve after exiting a left hand curve, when it began to rotate counterclockwise. The vehicle exited the roadway to the left and climbed up an embankment. Upon contact with the embankment the vehicle rolled over six quarter turns to its left and came to rest on the roadside. Shortly after comng to final rest the engine compartment caught on fire but was quickly extinguished. Police Reported Travel Speed is Unknown for V1.

The Critical Precrash Event for V1 was coded this vehicle traveling, off the edge of the road on the left side. The Critical Reason for the Critical Precrash Event was a driver related factor, too fast for conditions, wet roads. On the PAR the driver stated that she had been traveling below the speed limit, however the curve could have been easily traversed at a slower speed. Another related factor was that there was heavy fog at the time of the crash.
