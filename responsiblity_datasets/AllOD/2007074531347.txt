This two-vehicle crash occurred in a four-legged, traffic signal-controlled intersection. The north/south roadway is undivided, level and straight and consists of one lane in each direction. The speed limit for this roadway is 40 kmph (25 mph). The east/west trafficway is straight and divided by a curbed median. The westbound approach is level and consists of two through lanes and a left turn lane. The speed limit for this roadway is 64 kmph (40 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.

Vehicle one (V1), a 2001 Saturn SL2, was northbound in lane one. Vehicle two (V2), a 2001 Ford Focus, was westbound in lane two. Non-contact vehicles (NCVs) were stopped at the intersection in westbound lane two. The driver of V2 steered to the right to avoid the NCVs and continued into the intersection. The front of V2 struck the right side of V1. The vehicles rotated and the right side of V1 struck the left side of V2. V2 continued to the northwest and the front struck two short wooden poles and a fence. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The driver of V1, a forty-eight-year-old female, had picked up her thirteen-year-old niece on her way home from work. This driver was healthy, well rested and comfortable with her vehicle and passenger. She travels this route daily. This driver stated that she did not see V2 prior to the crash.

The driver of V2, a nineteen-year-old male, was traveling with two male passengers. He had picked up a friend and they were on their way to a park. This driver was healthy, well rested and comfortable with his vehicle and passengers. He rarely travels this route. This driver has attention deficit disorder and is prescribed Ritalin although he stated that he had not taken his medication in three days. He tested positive for marijuana. This driver stated that he was reading a text message on his cell phone and conversing with his passengers prior to the crash. When he returned his attention to the roadway, he saw the stopped NCVs and applied the brakes and steered to the right, moving into lane one prior to entering the intersection. He further stated that he did not see V1 prior to the crash.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "other vehicle encroachment; from crossing street, across path." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was "not coded to this vehicle." This driver's inadequate surveillance was an associated factor in this crash.

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded as "this vehicle traveling; crossing over (passing through) intersection." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded as "driver related factor; internal distraction." This driver's inadequate surveillance and drug use were associated factors in this crash.
