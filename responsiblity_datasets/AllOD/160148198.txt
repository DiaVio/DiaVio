This left sided crash involves a 27-year-old restrained female driver with no deployed air bags who sustained serious injury. The crash occurred during daylight hours in dry and clear weather. The collision occurred at the intersection of a four lane, two way roadway and a five lane, two way trafficway divided into three southbound lanes and two northbound lanes. The case vehicle (V1) is a 2003 Honda Civic sedan and was being driven southbound. Vehicle two (V2) is a 2001 Lincoln Navigator that was traveling westbound. The crash occurred when V1 attempted to make a turn at an intersection from the southbound roadway to the eastbound roadway. While making the left turn, V1's left contacted V2's front. Both vehicles came to rest in the southwest portion of the intersection and both vehicles were towed due to damage. V1 was driven by the 27-year-old case study occupant who was restrained by the available three-point manual belt. No airbags deployed in the crash. V1 also contained a right front occupant, a 27-year-old male, who was also wearing the available three-point manual belt. The case occupant was seriously injured and was transported to a level-one trauma center for treatment. The passenger in V1 was uninjured.
