This case involved a 68 year old restrained male driver of a 2007 Jeep Wrangler 2-door compact utility vehicle (V1). He was restrained by the available three point manual safety belts and also had a deployed frontal airbag. The other vehicle in the crash (V2) was an 18 wheeler tanker truck of unknown make and model. The crash occurred during daylight hours on a four lane interstate highway. The dry asphalt roadway was straight and level and there were no adverse weather conditions at the time of the crash. The north and southbound lanes were separated by a concrete barrier. The posted speed limit was 97 kph (60 mph). V1 and V2 were traveling south with V1 in the 4th (left) travel lane and V2 in the 3rd lane. Traffic was coming to a stop in all four lanes when V1 attempted to merge right into lane three. V2 had already come to a complete stop and the front of V1 struck the back of V2. V1 and V2 came to rest at impact. V1 was towed from the scene due to disabling damage. V2 was driven away from the crash scene. The driver of V1 was transported by ground ambulance to a level one trauma center and hospitalized due to serious injuries sustained in the crash.
