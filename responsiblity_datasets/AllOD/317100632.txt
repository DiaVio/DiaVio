This crash involved a 49 year old female driver of a 2007 Ford Mustang two door coupe (V1). She had a deployed frontal airbag, but was not using the available 3-point manual lap and shoulder belt. There were no other occupants in the vehicle. The crash occurred at night on a lighted two lane asphalt rural roadway. The road was wet and it was misting rain at the time of the crash. The posted speed limit was 72 kph (45 mph). V1 was traveling south with a 7% downhill grade as the road curved to the right. V1, reportedly traveling at approximately 137 kph (85 mph), traveled across the center line and departed the left side of the roadway. V1 struck a wooden mailbox post and a metal sign post with its frontal plane. The mailbox was dislodged from the post and struck the top of V1. V1 continued down an embankment and struck a double tree with its front. V1 rotated slightly clockwise and came to rest next to the tree. It was towed from the scene due to disabling damage. The driver of V1 was transported by ground ambulance to a local hospital and later transferred to a leve one trauma center for treatment.
