Vehicle one (V1 - case vehicle), a 2005 Chevrolet Trailblazer, 4-door utility vehicle was traveling east in the eastbound lane of a two-lane, two-way, rural road. It was dark, cloudy, and the dirt road was wet and muddy. The driver of V1 reported that she lost control of her vehicle due to the deep mud which caused her vehicle to pull to the left and depart the road. V1 departed the road while yawing/rotating in a counterclockwise manner and the right side of V1 struck a large tree on the side of the road and V1 came to rest against this tree. The front of V1 also sturck an electric fence that was directly behind the tree. V1 was towed due to disabling vehicle damage (non-tracking wheelbase). The 45-year-old female driver of V1 (case occupant) was not using the available three-point seat belt and no air bags deployed. She was transported via ground ambulance to a regional level-one trauma center and hospitalized for treatment.
