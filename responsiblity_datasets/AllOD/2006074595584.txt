This two vehicle crash occurred during early afternoon daylight hours on a intersection controlled by tri-color traffic signal. There are seven east/west lanes with a raised center median, road surface dry, level concrete with a posted speed limit of 64 kmph (40 mph). The north/south roadway consists of five lanes also concrete with a -3% grade going north. weather was overcast with no sight restrictions.

Vehicle one (V 1) a 1998 Chrysler Town & Country van driven by a 15 year-old male with two passengers was traveling east in lane one. Vehicle two (V 2) a 1998 Honda Accord sedan driven by a 86 year-old female was traveling north in lane two. As V 1 and V 2 approached the intersection V 1 changed lanes to the left and on the red light entered the intersection at the same time as V 2. The front of V 1 collided with the front of V 2 both vehicles then went off in a northeasterly direction causing a side-slap. V 1 came to final rest facing north in the westbound lanes, V 2 came to rest in the westbound curb facing northeast. Both vehicle were towed from the scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: this vehicle traveling, crossing over (passing through) intersection. The critical reason was coded as a driver related factor: illegal maneuver (disregard of traffic signal). Associate factors, V 1 driver inexperience of five months driving, inattention distraction due to passengers giving directions & also yelling at the driver that he was in the wrong lane.

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded: other vehicle encroachment; from crossing street- across path. The critical reason not coded to this vehicle.
