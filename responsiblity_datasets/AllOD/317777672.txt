This case involves a 17 year old unrestrained female driver in a 2010 Toyota Yaris (V1). V1 was equipped with dual side curtains, dual frontal and side airbags. The frontal steering wheel mounted airbag and left side roof rail mounted curtain both deployed during the crash. The crash occurred on the roadside of a dry, asphalt, two lane, two-way curved roadway that had a negative slope. There were no adverse weather conditions; however, the sky was cloudy. The posted speed limit was 40 kph (25 mph). V1 was traveling north downhill in the northbound lane of the roadway while negotiating a sharp left curve. As V1 entered the curve the driver attempted to slow her vehicle by applying the brakes. V1 began to slide causing the driver of V1 to release the brakes. V1 traveled off the right roadside. The driver of V1 attempted to steer left and maneuver her vehicle back onto the roadway. While on the roadside the right side of V1 swiped a small tree. V1 then continued a short distance and contacted another tree with its front right corner. After impact, V1 rotated clockwise and came to rest while partially on the roadway and partially on the roadside facing northeast. V1 came to rest on the roadside while facing northwest. The driver of V1 was partially ejected through the windshield during the crash sequence. She was transported from the crash scene to a level one trauma center by air. While at the trauma center she was treated for serious injuries that she sustained in the crash.
