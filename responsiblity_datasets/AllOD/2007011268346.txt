The crash occurred on a two-way, two-lane, straight, level, bituminous road with a statutory speed limit of 89 kmh (55 mph). Conditions at the time of the weekend nighttime crash were clear, dark, unlighted and dry.

V1, a 2001 Ford Taurus SES 4 door sedan was driven by an 80 year-old female. She had just been out to dinner with her friends and she was on her way home after dropping off her friends at their homes. She was traveling eastbound on a rural road. A deer entered the road from the driver's left and crossed in front of her. The driver stated that she swerved to the right but clipped the deer with her front left corner. V1 left the road on the right side as she went down into a ditch she contacted a small tree with her left side. The front right corner of V1 then struck a tree 54 cm in diameter. V1 came to rest against the tree facing west. V1 was towed from the scene due to damage. The fully restrained driver was transported from the scene due to injuries. The mailboxes were knocked down from a previous crash. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

The Critical Precrash Event coded to V1 was 'animal in roadway.' The Critical Reason for the Critical event coded was 'other highway related condition-dark-no street lights.' An Associated Factor coded to this driver was for possible side effects to her prescribed medication.
