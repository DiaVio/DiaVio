This single vehicle crash occurred at a T-intersection of two two-lane undivided roadways. The crash occurred in the late afternoon hours while still daylight. There were no adverse atmospheric conditions, and both roadways were dry, straight, and level. Both roadways had a posted speed limit of 40 kmph (25 mph). The intersection was controlled by stop signs for all directions.

Vehicle one (V1) was a 1997 Ford Explorer driven by a 17-year old male. V1 was traveling south on the roadway that ended at the T-intersection. V1 proceeded into the intersection without stopping at the stop sign and skidded across the east/west roadway, departing the roadway on the south edge of the road. The front left of V1 impacted a utility pole on the roadside. The front of V1 sustained moderate damage and had to be towed from the scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is Unknown for V1.

The driver of V1 complained of pain but refused medical treatment. There were two other teen-aged occupants in the vehicle at the time of the crash, and each of them complained of pain, but both also refused medical treatment. The driver did not want to provide an interview and told police that his wheels locked up and he lost control of his vehicle. Based on scene evidence and information from police, the Critical Pre-crash Event was coded as this vehicle traveling through end departure. The Critical Reason for the Critical Pre-crash Event was coded as too fast for conditions. It was believed that the driver was traveling at an excessive rate of speed as he neared the intersection, began braking too late, and skidded into and through the intersection and off the south edge of the intersecting roadway. Associated factors coded to the driver include an illegal maneuver, aggressive driving, and inexperience.
