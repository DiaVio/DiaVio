This crash occurred in the morning of a weekday on median separating a turn lane from the through lanes of a north/south roadway. The roadway consisted of 3 northbound lanes and 2 southbound lanes divided by a small median. The bituminous roadway was dry, straight, and level with a speed limit of 56 kmph (35 mph). The traffic flow was normal and there were no adverse atmospheric conditions at the time of the crash.

V1, a 2000 Mercury Mystique with one occupant, was northbound approaching an intersecting east/west roadway. As V1 continued north it drifted right over a small median located between a turn lane and the through lane and struck a pole. The driver of V1 then put the vehicle into reverse and struck a curb. The vehicle came to a rest partially on roadway. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

The driver of V1, a 28-year-old female, was not injured in the crash. The driver does not speak English very well and stayed with a police officer in her vehicle. After finding an interpreter, it was determined that the driver had fallen asleep at the wheel of the vehicle. She awoke after her vehicle struck the pole.

The Critical Precrash Event for V1 was coded this vehicle traveling, off the edge of the road right side. The Critical Reason for was a driver related error; the driver was asleep at the wheel. Driver had been driving for an extended period of time and was still fatigued.
