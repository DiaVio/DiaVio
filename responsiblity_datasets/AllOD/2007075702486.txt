This single-vehicle crash occurred in the westbound lanes of a state highway. A concrete barrier divides this roadway and the westbound traffic lanes consist of three through lanes. This roadway is straight and the crash occurred in a sag in the roadway. There was standing water in lane three. There was no drainage or run off for the water. The posted speed limit is 105 kmph (65 mph). This crash occurred during nighttime hours. It was raining and traffic flow was light.

Vehicle one (V1), a 1997 Pontiac Trans Am with a single occupant, was westbound in lane three. Because of a slight dip in the roadway, there was standing water in lane three. V1 started to hydroplane and began to rotate counterclockwise. V1 departed the roadway on the left and the front of the vehicle impacted the concrete barrier for a distance of 4.3 meters (14 feet). V1 continued its counterclockwise rotation through an estimated 540Â° from the original travel direction and came to final rest facing east in lanes one and two. V1 was towed due to damage. The driver was not injured. Police Reported Travel Speed is 89 kmph for V1.

The driver of V1, a twenty-two-year-old male, was commuting from work to his home. He was well rested, drives this route daily, very comfortable with his vehicle, and in good health. He was driving with a suspended license. He stated it was raining at the time and the windshield wipers were on steady.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: this vehicle traveling-off the edge of the road on the left side. The critical reason for the critical event for V1 was coded as an environment related factor: other highway related condition (specified as: standing water in sag with no drainage caused V1 to hydroplane). There was no drainage for the water that accumulated in the sag.
