This single-vehicle crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions. The roadway is undivided, straight, level and consists of two lanes in each direction. Lane one in both directions was covered in slush and the roadsides were snow-covered. The speed limit for this roadway is 89 kmph (55 mph).

Vehicle one (V1), a 1992 Pontiac Grand Am, was eastbound in lane one. V1 traveled off the road to the right. The driver of V1 steered left, returned to the road and then steered right. V1 rotated clockwise and traveled off the left side of the road. V1 rolled over to the left four quarter-turns and was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

The driver of V1, a seventy-five-year-old male, was traveling with his seventy-four-year-old wife from their home to a casino. This driver was healthy, well rested and comfortable with his vehicle and passenger. He travels this route daily. This driver stated that he applied the brakes and steered in an effort to regain control of his vehicle.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "this vehicle traveling; off the edge of the road on the right side." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "driver related factor; overcompensation." This driver's poor directional control and the slushy condition of the roadway were associated factors in this crash.
