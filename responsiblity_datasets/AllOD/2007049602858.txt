This single vehicle-crash occurred on a weekend during late morning daylight hours. The location was a three lane divided (with a positive barrier) left curved roadway. It was a downhill (slope unknown) highway. The radius of the curvature is not known. The weather was clear and the roads dry. The posted speed limit for the roadway is 97 kph (60 mph). There was medium traffic on the roadway.

The only vehicle (V1), a 2004 Chevrolet Avalanche, was traveling west in the third lane of the roadway. V1 began to rotate clockwise and then traveled off the right side of the roadway. The front of the vehicle struck the W-beam guardrail. The vehicle then rolled over to the left two-quarter turns. V1 landed on its roof on the pavement. The vehicle slid about 30 meter (98 feet) before it came to rest in lane one, facing west. V1 was towed from the scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

A 24-year old male was the driver of the vehicle. This driver was not interviewed. The police report indicates that the driver stated that he was traveling 16 kmph (10 mph) above the speed limit and steered right to avoid other vehicles.

The critical per-crash event was coded "this vehicle control loss due to traveling too fast for conditons." The critical reason was coded "driver related factor - too fast to be able to respond to unexpected actions of others."
