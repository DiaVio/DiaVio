V1, a 1995 Ford Contour 4 door, was westbound in lane 3 of 3 straight, level, bituminous westbound lanes of a 6-lane interstate divided by a median with a positive barrier. The posted speed limit was 65mph/105kmph. At the time of the crash it was daylight but the roadway was wet and it was raining. There was a lot of spray and he said it was hard to see. The driver of v1 was uncomfortable driving in inclement weather and did not think the vehicle could be controlled well on slick roads so he attempted to slow. down. As he tried to slow down, the driver lost control of v1. V1 fishtailed to the left then went into a counterclockwise yaw and left the roadway to the left. V1, front, contacted the median guardrail, continued to rotate counterclockwise and struck the guardrail again with it's back right. V1 came to rest next to the guardrail facing east. The driver of v1 was uninjured. V1 was towed due to damage.

A complete driver interview and vehicle inspection was obtained for v1. The 16 year old male driver was properly licensed for the vehicle and was not in a hurry. He was coming from school, had not driven very far and was on his way to a friend's house. Fatigue, drugs or alcohol did not appear to be involved as contributing factors in the crash. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

The Pre-event movement of v1 was going straight, the Critical pre-crash event was this vehicle traveling off the edge of the road on the left side and the critical reason for the critical pre-crash event was poor directional control. Some factors that may have contributed to the cause of the crash include the driver's inexperience (had only been driving for 3 months), the driver's unfamiliarity with the roadway, his lack of comfort with the handling of the vehicle on slick roads, driving too fast for conditions, the wet roadway and a possible mechanical problem with the vehicle's steering.
