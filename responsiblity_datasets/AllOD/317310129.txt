This case involved the 57 year old female driver of a 2005 Chevrolet Equinox four door utility vehicle (V1). The front of V1 impacted the front of a 2001 Ford F-150 pickup (V2). The driver of V1 was unrestrained and had a deployed dual frontal airbags. The crash occurred during nighttime hours on a curved and leveled roadway. It was raining and the asphalt roadway was wet. The posted speed limit was 48 kph (30 mph). V1 was traveling south and negotiating a left hand curve on the two-lane roadway. V2 was traveling north and negotiating a right hand curve. V2 crossed the center lane line and traveled a few meters. The front of V2 contacted the front of V1. Both vehicles came to rest partially in the southbound lane and partially off the roadway in their respective travel directions. Both vehicles were towed from the scene due to disabling damage. The driver of V1 was transported by fire rescue to a level one trauma center for treatment of injuries sustained in the crash.
