The crash occurred in lane two of a four-lane southbound interstate. The interstate was straight and level with four lanes in each direction divided by guardrails. The posted speed limit was 89 kmph (55 mph). This was a bituminous, dry roadway. The sky was clear during this early afternoon weekday. The traffic flow was light on this busy well-traveled roadway.

V1, a 2007 Chevrolet HHR sedan, was driven by a 38-year-old male traveling with a 37-year-old female friend. The driver stated that he was traveling in lane two at a speed of 105 kmph (65 mph) when he spotted, from the corner of his eye, a four-door beige sedan traveling in lane three overtaking his vehicle. He claimed that the beige sedan struck the left passenger door of V1 with an unknown portion of the beige vehicle. V1 began to skid to the right and in an unsuccessful attempt to regain control of V1, the driver steered left without braking. V1 continued to skid right across the first travel lane, the right shoulder and off the road. V1 continued traveling off the road to the right at which time the left side of V1 struck a signpost. V1 pivoted counterclockwise 180 degrees, and then while traveling backwards, the rear of V1 traveled up an embankment and onto a guardrail where it came to rest facing north. Neither the driver nor passenger was injured. Inspection of V1 failed to provide evidence that V1 was struck by another vehicle. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

The Critical Pre-Crash Event for V1 was "This vehicle traveling off the edge of the road on the right side." The Critical Reason for the Critical Pre-Crash Event was "Performance error - overcompensation." Associated factors include conversing with passenger and traveling too fast (at least 10 miles over the speed limit).
