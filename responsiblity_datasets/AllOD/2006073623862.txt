This single vehicle crash occurred early evening during dark hours on a two-lane east/west roadway. A solid yellow line for eastbound traffic divides the roadway and a dotted yellow line for west bound traffic.

The road for westbound traffic has a negative 4% grade and was a dry, straight, bituminous asphalt roadway. It was early evening the sky was clear. The speed limit for this roadway is 80 kmph (50 mph). Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

Vehicle one (V 1), a 1923 Ford Model-T sportster kit car, driven by a 31 year-old male with one passenger was traveling westbound in the first lane. A non-contact vehicle was traveling in front of V 1. V 1 accelerated and changed lanes to the left in an attempt to pass the non-contact vehicle. Due to the light rear end of V 1 it went out of control and off of the roadway left. V 1 rolled four quarter turns down an embankment that had a negative 98% grade and came to final rest facing north on it's wheels.

The driver advised that the non-contact vehicle was driving too slow and that he lost control of V 1 during the lane change. V 1 was towed from the scene due to damage.

The critical precrash event for V1 was coded: this vehicle traveling off the edge of the road on the left side. The Critical Reason for the Critical Event driver related factor "aggressive driving behavior". V 1 was accelerating at a high rate of speed in an attempt to pass another vehicle. Driver one stated that he knew better than to drive that particular vehicle in that manner.
