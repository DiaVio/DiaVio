This two-vehicle daytime collision occurred in an urban intersection. The North/South roadway has four travel lanes and one additional lane (lane 5) dedicated to bus only travel. The East/West roadway has four travel lanes. Traffic flow controlled by on-colors signal. The concrete roadway is straight and level in the area of the crash. The speed limit for the roadway is 48 kmph (30 mph). At the time of the crash the sky was cloudy and the roadway was wet.

Vehicle one (V1), a 2005 Chrysler PT Cruiser driven by a twenty one (21) year old male, was headed east in lane one after having made a left turn at the previous intersection.. Vehicle two (V2), a 1999 Toyota Camry sedan driven by a fifty four (54) year old female with one passenger, was headed north in lane four. V1 went though the intersection against the control signal and his front impacted the left plane of V2. Both vehicles came to rest slightly north and east of the point of impact. V2 was towed due to vehicle damage, the driver of V1 was treated on scene for possible neck injury, but neither driver was transported from the scene for further treatment. The driver of V1 was unavailable for interview or vehicle inspection. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: this vehicle traveling, crossing over (passing through) intersection. The critical reason for the critical event is coded a driver related factor: unknown reason for critical event. No interview was obtained with this driver and it is unknown why he didn't recognize the traffic light was red or the presence of V2 coming from his right. Also, V1 inspection was refused, so vehicle factors could not be determined.

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded: Other vehicle encroachment, from crossing street across path. The critical reason for the critical event was not coded to this vehicle.
