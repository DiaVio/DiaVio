This crash occurred during the early morning hours of a weekday on a two-lane, divided highway. The bituminous, straight and level roadway featured a posted speed limit of 113kmph (70mph). The conditions at the time of the crash were very poor, it was snowing very hard, and the roads were very slick.

V1, a 2002 Pontiac Grand Prix, was traveling south in lane one, when the driver lost control of the vehicle causing it to depart the roadway on the right side. V1 front struck a breakaway signpost. V1 traveled over the pole causing significant damage to its undercarriage. V1 came to final rest in the ditch. The driver of V1 left the scene of the crash and went to work, where she reported the crash to central dispatch because she did not have a cell phone. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

V1 was driven by a 31 year-old female in good health on her morning commute to work. This researcher contacted the driver of the vehicle and she stated that she was traveling between 82-96kmph (51-60mph) when she lost control of the vehicle on the slick snow-covered roads. The driver also stated that she was picked up by a co-worker and did not have a cell phone to report the crash. The driver was not injured but V1 was towed due to damage.

The Critical Precrash Event for V1 was coded as this vehicle traveling, off the edge of the roadway, to the right. The Critical Reason coded for this crash is a driver related factor, a decision error, traveling to fast for conditions. Associated factors for this crash include traveling too fast for conditions, as the driver did not realize that caution was required, as well as slick roads and poor visibility brought about the wind and snow.
