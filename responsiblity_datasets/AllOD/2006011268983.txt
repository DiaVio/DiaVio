The crash occurred on a two-way, two lane, straight, rural road with a >2% +slope. The dirt/slag road has a statutory speed limit of 89 kmh (55mph) and was recently graded, coated with brine and was in good shape. Conditions at the time of the weekday afternoon crash were clear, daylight and dry with very light traffic.

V1, a 2000 Ford Crown Victoria 4 door sedan, driven by 41 year-old male, was westbound on the road. The witness was traveling west at approximately 72 kmh (45 mph). V1 passed the witness on the left at an estimated speed of 96 kmh (60mph). The witness stated that 50-75 meters (20-30 feet) past him V1 began to spin clockwise moving from the left side of the road to the right. The witness stated that he observed V1 braking. V1 left the road facing north. The left side driver's door of V1 struck a tree 58cm in diameter and rocked up into the tree. A limb fell off of the tree and struck the top of the passenger compartment. V1 came to rest against the tree facing east. V1 was towed from the scene due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

The fully restrained driver was trapped in the vehicle due to left side, toe and floor pan intrusion. A fire ignited in the engine compartment and spread to the driver's leg area. Responding emergency personnel extinguished the fire, which re-ignited and was extinguished a second time. The driver was extricated and transported by helicopter to a local trauma center.

During an interview with the drivers mother it was discovered that he had asked his mother for money. His mother, uncle and cousin had confronted him about his additions and they argued about it. They refused to give him any money and he left the house angry to go somewhere else to get money. They said he smelled like alcohol and his eyes were bloodshot before he left the house.

The Critical Precrash Event coded to the driver of V1 was 'this vehicle traveling off the edge of the road on the right side.' The Critical Reason for the Critical Event coded was 'traveling too fast for conditions.' Contributing factors for this driver include the presence of alcohol (BAC .15) and cocaine use. The driver has a history of alcohol abuse, and anxiety and seizure disorders. In addition, the driver was somewhat unfamiliar with both the vehicle and the road. The driver was driving with a suspended and expired license. An associated vehicle factor was coded for the low tread on the rear tires.
