This single vehicle crash occurred early morning hours on a straight, divided, asphalt eight-lane, U.S. highway with no controls. There are three though lanes and a right merge lane to enter the highway for eastbound traffic. The highway runs east and west at this location and the speed limit is 89 kmph (55 mph). It had snowed the day before and it was lightly snowing at the time of the crash.

Vehicle one (V1), a 1997 Toyota RAV 4 SUV driven by a 25 year-old female, was traveling on an entrance ramp and merging onto the highway in lane one, the right merge lane. The driver lost control on the slushy roadway and started to rotate counter clockwise. V1 crossed all travel lanes and ran off the left side of the road impacting a jersey median barrier with its front. V1 came to final rest facing northwest on the left shoulder and lane four. V1 was towed from the scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: this vehicle traveling, off the edge of the road on the left side. Critical reason fro the critical event coded environment related factor: slick roads (low friction road surface due to ice, loose debris, any other condition). Associative factors were: too fast for the conditions and poor directional control. The driver did not adapt her driving to the adverse weather and roadway conditions.
