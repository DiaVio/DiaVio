The crash occurred on a dry, level, straight two-lane, undivided rural roadway during the mid-evening hours. It was dark out, and the roadway was not illuminated. The posted speed limit was 89 kmph (55 mph). Vehicle 1, a 1994 Chevrolet S-10 pickup, was traveling eastbound when it departed the roadway first to the left then to the right before rolling over two quarter turns. It came to final rest on its roof in the roadway.

The driver of V1, a 24-year old male, stated that he fell asleep for a second and when he awoke, he was off the road on the left side. He then stated that he steered hard to the right to get back onto the road and the truck rotated clockwise and then counterclockwise. The vehicle skidded off the roadway to the right and then tripped over in the soft dirt, rolling two-quarter turns to the right before coming to final rest on its roof in the original travel lane of travel. The vehicle was towed due to moderate damage. The restrained driver was transported to an emergency room with moderate injuries. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

The critical pre-crash event for this vehicle was when it traveled off the edge of the roadway to the right. The critical reason was coded as overcompensation. The driver was awake when he steered out of the ditch, but he steered too hard. Associated factors coded to the driver of V1 include fatigue and alcohol use. As per medical reports, the driver of V1 told medical staff that he had consumed “2 beers” prior to the crash. No alcohol test was conducted, however. The driver of V1 also reported that he was under stress from his job due to his having to work extended work shifts.
