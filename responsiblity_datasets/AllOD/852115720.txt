This crash occurred on an interstate highway with two lanes for both the northbound and southbound directions that are divided by a concrete traffic barrier with a grassy sloped median between the east edge of the southbound lanes and the barrier. The posted speed limit is 70 mph/113kmph. Vehicle one (V1), a 2001 Chevrolet Lumina foor door sedan, was northbound traveling in lane one. Vehicle two (V2), a 1990 Audi 100 four door sedan was southbound in lane two and lost control and departed the roadway to the left went up the grassy slope median and impacted the cement barrier with the front of V2. V2 climbed over the barrier and rolled over into the northbound lanes landing on the driver's side. V2 continue to slide on the driver's side into lane one when the front of V1 impacted the front of V2 while it was on its driver's side. V1 rotated clockwise and forced V2 toward the north. V1 came to rest in lane one and partially on the east shoulder facing southeast. V2 rolled over to the roof post impact with V1 and ended up in the grassy ditch on the east side of the northbound lanes and was facing west. Both vehicles were disabled and towed. Case occupants in V1 included the driver and a second right seat occupant. The driver was a 48 year old female who was wearing a lap/shoulder belt and the frontal airbag deployed and transported to the trauma center. The second row right seat occupant was a 19 year old male who was wearing a lap/shoulder belt and possibly leaning to the left and was airlifted to the trauma center. A third passenger, a 49 year old male was restrained in the front right position and transported to a local hospital.
