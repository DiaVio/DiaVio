This single-vehicle crash began in a one-way, two-lane, right-curved, level roadway in a tunnel. After the tunnel, the roadway is straight and is bordered by jersey barriers outside the shoulders. The speed limit for this roadway is 97 kmph (60 mph). This crash occurred during dark hours with street lights on. There were no adverse atmospheric conditions at the time of this crash.

Vehicle one (V1), a 1995 Volkswagen Golf, was eastbound in an unknown lane. V1 traveled off the right side of the roadway and the front struck the tunnel wall. V1 continued eastbound and the back right side struck the tunnel wall. V1 exited the tunnel, traveled across both lanes and the front struck the jersey barrier on the left side of the roadway. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is No PAR obtained (created) for V1.

The driver of V1 appeared to be under the influence of alcohol and was not interviewed at the scene. Further attempts to contact this driver were unsuccessful.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: this vehicle traveling, off the edge of the road on the right side. The critical reason for the critical event for V1 was coded as a driver related factor: type of driver error unknown.
