The crash occurred in the right lane of a four-lane roadway. Conditions were daylight and dry in the early evening on a weekday. The roadway had a posted speed of 64 KMPH (40 MPH) with a curve left.

V1 was a 2003 Jeep Wrangler Sport intending on negotiating a curve in the southbound lane. As V1 was negotiating the curve, an unknown vehicle passed this vehicle on the left and cut in front of V1. V1 then swerved right and struck the curb. V1 then drifted left across three travel lanes and the right side of V1 struck a curb then a tree. V1 then rolled left two-quarter turns coming to final rest onto its roof facing southwest. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

A 26-year-old female drove V1 and sustained minor injuries but was not transported. She stated that an unknown vehicle cut her off and she swerved right and that is when she lost control of V1. After striking the tree, the driver of V1 does not recall what had happened. V1 was traveling between 50 and 64 KMPH (31 and 40 MPH) at the time of impact. V1 was towed due to damage.

The Critical Precrash Event for V1 was this vehicle traveling off the edge of the road on the right side. The Critical Reason for the Critical Precrash Event was a decision error; too fast for conditions to be able to respond to unexpected actions of other road users; unknown vehicle cut off V1. The driver felt rested before the crash sequence and was familiar with the area. The police officer in charge on the investigation conducted a field sobriety test, which the driver passed.
