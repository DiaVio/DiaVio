This two-vehicle crash occurred in a four-legged, traffic signal-controlled intersection. The north/south trafficway is straight, level and divided. Both approaches consist of two through lanes, two left turn lanes and a right turn lane. The speed limit for this trafficway is 72 kmph (45 mph). It was dark with streetlights on at the time of the crash with no adverse atmospheric conditions.

Vehicle one (V1), a 2001 Jeep Cherokee, was northbound in lane three. Vehicle two (V2), a 1997 Ford Escort, was stopped southbound in lane five, waiting to turn left. V2 entered the intersection, turning left and the front struck the front of V1. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 40 kmph for V1 and 40 kmph for V2.

The driver of V1, a thirty-eight-year-old male, was not interviewed. According to the police report, this driver was distracted by his radio. No further information is available for this driver.

The driver of V2, a fifty-eight-year-old female, was on her way from a gas station to her daughter's home. She was in good health, well rested and familiar with her vehicle. She travels this route daily. This driver has arthritis and takes the medications Potassium Chloride, Sulfusalizine, Celexa, Zocor, Triamterene/HCTZ, Trazodone, aspirin, Nexium and Cyclobenzaprine. This driver stated that she entered the intersection when her traffic signal arrow turned green and saw V2 enter the intersection at the same time.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: other vehicle encroachment; from opposite direction - over left lane line. The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event was not coded to this vehicle.

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded: this vehicle traveling; turning left at intersection. The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded as a driver related factor: false assumption of other's actions.
