This single-vehicle crash occurred on a divided trafficway. The southbound lanes curve to the left (the radius of curvature is 173 meters and the superelevation is seven percent), have an uphill grade and consist of three through lanes with paved shoulders on both sides. There are snow banks on both sides of the road and the road was wet. The speed limit for this roadway is 72 kmph (45 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.

Vehicle one (V1), a 2000 Jeep Cherokee, was southbound in lane three. V1 traveled off the right side of the road and up the snow bank. The right side of V1 struck a sign post and then V1 rolled over to the left two quarter-turns. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 72 kmph for V1.

The driver of V1, a sixty-five-year-old male, was on his way home from a store. He was healthy, well rested and familiar with his vehicle. He travels this route several times each week. This driver stated that he was watching some deer that were off the left side of the road and "just wandered over". He applied the brakes but was unable to avoid the crash.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: this vehicle traveling, off the edge of the road on the right side. The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as a driver related factor: external distraction.
