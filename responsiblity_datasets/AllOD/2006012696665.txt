V1, a 2003 Chevrolet Trailblazer, was traveling eastbound on a four-lane undivided asphalt road in dark-lighted conditions. The roadway was level, straight, and snow-covered. The posted speed limit was 56 kmph (35 mph). V1 was traveling in lane two intending to go straight. The driver of V1 stated that she hit some ice and lost control of her vehicle. She crossed over two lanes off traffic on the left side, and departed the roadway where the front right side of V1 contacted a light utility pole. V1 then came to final rest off the north road edge.

The driver of V1 was a 29-year old female who reported that she had been traveling between 50-64kmph (31-40mph) prior to the crash. She had no health-related problems and had taken no medication prior to the crash. She was rested and traveling home. She does not wear prescription lenses. She was uninjured and refused treatment. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

The Critical Pre-crash Event for the driver of V1 was this vehicle traveling off the edge of the road on the left side. The Critical Reason for the Critical Pre-crash Event was a decision error - too fast for conditions (icy roadway). The EDR reading was obtained and it revealed that five seconds before the crash, V1 was traveling at 67 mph. Associated factors were the condition of the roadway and the weather (snow).
