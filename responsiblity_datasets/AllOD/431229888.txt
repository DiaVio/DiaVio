This frontal crash case involves a 28 year old restrained female seated in the front left seating position (Driver) with a deployed air bag who sustained serious injuries. The crash occurred during the early morning hours in dry and clear weather. The collision occurred on a level (at point of loss of control) two lane bituminous road way that curves to the left. This roadway has one northbound travel lane and one southbound travel lane that are separated by a double yellow line. There were no traffic control devices present and the speed limit was 45 mph (72kmph). The case vehicle (V1), a 1999 two-door Convertible Volkswagen Cabrio was being driven southbound. The crash occurred when V1 (tried to avoid a deer in the road) lost control, in a left curve crossing over the northbound travel lane (yawing) and off the road edge. V1 traveled down a -13% slope impacting a tree (42 cm wide) with its front right corner. After initial impact, V1 rotated counter clockwise 40 degrees and traveled rearward southwest about 25 feet before traveling to it final resting position partially back onto the north travel lane facing northeast. V1 was towed from the scene due to damage sustained in the crash. V1 was driven by the 28 year old female case occupant who was restrained by the available 3-point manual belt (retractor type pretensioner actuated). The available steering wheel mounted air bag deployed as a result of the impact. . This case occupant's seat was positioned between middle and rear most and the seat back was in the slightly reclined position. The case occupant was seriously injured and transported to a level 1 trauma center by air ambulance for treatment of her injuries
