A single-vehicle collision occurred a couple hours after noon during daylight hours. The collision occurred on a busy interstate highway in the southbound HOV bypass. The pre-crash roadway is a 10 lane divided interstate highway with 5 lanes in each direction. The pre-crash roadway is a slight downhill grade, and straight with concrete paving, the HOV bypass does make a jog left in the area of the collision. The weather was clear and the roadway was dry. The roadway has a posted speed limit of 97 kmph (60 mph).

Vehicle one (V1), a 2007 Toyota Scion TC 2-door sedan with one occupant, was traveling southbound in lane five. V1 traveled on the roadway to the left number five lane, which is an HOV bypass, and for unknown reasons lost control of the vehicle. The front left of V1 impacted a concrete barrier, and then the vehicle shot off to the right and impacted the front right corner on the opposite concrete barrier. Vehicle was towed due to damage. The driver was not transported. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

The driver of V1 is a 20 year-old female, whose pre-crash factors are unknown, as she was not interviewed. The critical pre-crash event for this vehicle was coded: this vehicle traveling, off the edge of the road on the left side. The critical reason for the critical event was coded as a driver related factor: unknown performance error. The driver failed to maintain control of the vehicle as it struck the concrete barrier on the left side of the roadway before it careened off the left barrier and struck the barrier on the right side of the roadway.
