This crash occurred through a curve on a two lane, two way roadway during daylight hours with wet roadways and overcast conditions. Case vehicle one (V1), a 1999 Saturn SC2 three door coupe was northbound through the curve right in the roadway and departed the lane to the left. Vehicle two (V2), a 2000 Toyota Tundra access cab pickup truck was southbound through the curve to the left. The front left corner of V1 impacted the front left corner of V2. The damage to V1 swiped down the left side to the driver's door along with direct contact to the A-pillar due to the height of V2. V1 was deflected to the right post impact and then continued north across the southbound lane and departed the roadway to the left off the west edge. V1 then dropped down a slight embankment where the front of V1 impacted the ground with minor damage, and came to final rest facing northwest. V2 was deflected slightly to the right post impact and continue south before coming to final rest on the west shoulder and road edge. Both vehicles were disabled and towed. The driver of case V1 is the case occupant and the only occupant in the vehicle. This case driver is 68 year old female who was wearing the manual lap and shoulder belt and the frontal steering column air bag deployed on the impact with V2. This driver received severe injuries and was transported to the trauma center.
