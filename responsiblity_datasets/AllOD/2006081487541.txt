A two-vehicle crash occurred mid-afternoon during daylight hours. The collision occurred in an intersection, in a residential neighborhood. The north-south leg of the roadway is controlled by a stop sign. The east-west leg of the roadway is uncontrolled. The pre-crash roadway is a two-way, two-lane undivided residential roadway. The pre-crash roadway is level and straight, with bituminous paving. The posted speed limit for the roadways is 40 kmph (25 mph).

Vehicle one (V1), a 1998 Dodge Stratus sedan, driven by a 49 year-old male with three passengers was traveling south in lane one. Vehicle two (V2) a 1998 Chevy C-1500 Pickup driven by a 41 year-old male was traveling east in lane one. V1 stopped at the stop sign, then entered the roadway without yielding to V2. The front of V1 impacted the left side of V2, causing V2 to roll right three quarter turns. V2 came to rest on the left side facing northwest on the east side of the intersection. V1 was driven from the scene. V2 was towed due to damage, and the driver of V2 was transported to local medical facility. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The driver of V2 had just left a job a few blocks away and was heading to another job in another city, he was heading to a main road that would take him to the other city, this was a scheduled trip, and he was not in a hurry. The driver of V2 claimed that V1 came out of the side street very fast.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: this vehicle traveling; crossing over (passing through) intersection. The critical reason for the critical event for V2 was a driver related factor: inadequate surveillance.

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded: other vehicle encroachment; from crossing street, across path. The critical reason for the critical event was not coded to this vehicle.
