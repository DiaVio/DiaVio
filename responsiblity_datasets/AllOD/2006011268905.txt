This crash occurred on a controlled one-way, one-lane ramp from one roadway to another, with a posted speed limit of 89 kmph (55 mph). The bituminous ramp curves to the right. Conditions at the time of the weekday nighttime crash were cloudy, dark and unlighted.

V1, a 2000 F-350 Supercab 4x4 pick-up truck driven by a 41-year-old male, was southbound on the ramp when the driver lost control. V1 began to rotate clockwise and left the road on the right side and crossed the paved shoulder to a grassy ditch. The front of the pick-up struck a group of small trees. V1 rolled two quarter turns to the left and rotated clockwise. The cab of V1 came to rest on the shoulder facing northeast, with the back of the pick-up near the ditch. V1 was towed from the scene due to damage. The unrestrained driver was partially ejected through the left front window, and was transported from the scene due to injuries. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

The Critical Precrash Event for V1 was coded this vehicle traveling, off the edge of the road on the right side. The Critical Reason for the Critical Event was coded as an unknown driver error. In talking with the driver's mother it was discovered that he was upset upon recently learning of a family member's declining health. The driver also takes anti-depressant and anti-seizure medication which when combined with alcohol (as indicated on the PAR) can cause fatigue.
