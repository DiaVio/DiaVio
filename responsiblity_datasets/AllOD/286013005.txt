This frontal crash involves a serious injury to the left front seated driver. The Vehicle one (V1- case vehicle) a 2003, Saab 9-3, 4-door car was traveling west (on lane 1) on west bound, two-lane plus left turn lane roadway and was intending to go straight through the intersection. The roadway is a two-way road separated by solid double yelow line. The Vehicle two (V2) a 2000 Ford Taurus, 4-door car was traveling south on southbound on a two-lane two-way roadway separated by solid double yellow line. The crash occured at the intersection of these roadways on a clear daylight. The west bound has an upward elevation at the intersection and slightly bends to right at the intersection. The frontal plane of V1 impacted left side plane of V2 in L-type configuration and both the vehicles side slapped each other. V2 most likely rotated clockwise (approximately 100 degrees) before both the vehicles came to final rest facing west on the west bound road on the north-west corner of the intersection. The posted speed limit for the west bound roadway is 52 km/h (35 mph) and south bound has 64 km/h (40 mph). The 75 year old male driver was restrained by 3-point manual belt and the frontal air bag deployed due to the crash. He sustained serious injuries and was air lifted to the nearest trauma center. There were no other occupants in the case vehicle at the time of the crash. The 65 year old driver of the V2 was reported to be restrained by manual lap and shoulder belt and the steering mounted airbag deployed. He was transported by ground unit and his injuries were reported as injuried with unknown severity.
