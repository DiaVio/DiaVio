This crash occurred in the southbound lanes of a two-way, ten-lane interstate roadway, which was physically divided by a concrete traffic barrier. There were five southbound lanes, with a posted speed of 105 kmph (65 mph). This was a straight, asphalt roadway, with an uphill slope greater than two percent. Conditions were daylight on a weekend afternoon with dry roads and clear skies. The traffic flow was normal.

V1, a 2004 Infinity G35 with one occupant, was southbound in lane two. V2, a 1987 Mazda B-series pickup truck with one occupant, was also southbound in lane two, ahead of V1. The front of V1 made contact with the back of V2. V2 veered off the right side of the roadway, making contact with a guardrail. V2 then veered back to the left, crossing all southbound lanes of travel. The front of V1 made contact with the concrete barrier wall. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 105 kmph for V1 and 105 kmph for V2.

The driver of V1, a 46-year old male, was not injured. He stated that he was on his way to visit his parents. After careful consideration, the driver had no apparent recollection of the crash events. He did not know what his precrash movements were. He could not remember the precrash movements of the other vehicle either.

The Critical Precrash Event for V1 was coded other motor vehicle in lane, traveling in same direction with lower steady speed. The Critical Reason for the Critical Event was a driver-related recognition error, inattention (daydreaming). There were numerous concerns on the driver's mind at the time of the crash, including employment, family, and personal relationships. Some associated factors for this driver related to his physical condition. The driver stated that he was fatigued from the long drive, lack of adequate sleep, and a recent long distance move, which required heavy lifting for five hours. Other physical factors included hypertension and severe insomnia, for which he takes medication. Another associated factor for the driver was the emotionally upsetting events taking place in his life at the time of the crash.

The driver of V2, a 65-year old male, was transported to the hospital due to non-incapacitating injuries. The driver declined to be interviewed and no further information could be obtained.

The Critical Precrash Event for V2 was coded other motor vehicle in lane, traveling in same direction with higher speed. The Critical Reason for the Critical Event was not coded to this vehicle. There were no known associated factors that could be determined for this driver.
