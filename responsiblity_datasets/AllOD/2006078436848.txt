This single vehicle, rollover crash, that occurred during early morning daylight hours in the westbound lanes of an interstate roadway. There were two lanes westbound and two lanes eastbound divided by a depressed earth median. The roadways are straight and level asphalt with no traffic controls. The posted speed limit is 121 KPH (75 MPH). The surrounding terrain is open desert.

Vehicle one(V-1), a 1997 Honda Civic LX sedan driven by a 35 year-old female with two passengers was traveling westbound in the number two lane. V-1 rotated counterclockwise while traveling in the number two lane for approximately 26 meters before it departed the left side of the roadway in a southwesterly direction. V-1 continued to travel off the roadway while rotating counterclockwise for an additional 7 meters before it changed rotation, rotating clockwise down a slope in the median. V-1 traveled for approximately 15 meters while rotating clockwise before it tripped and rolled to it's left. V-1 rolled left for approximately 12 meters before it came to rest on it's roof facing east. The vehicle was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

The Pre Event movement for V-1 coded going straight, category this vehicle traveling off the edge of the road on the left side. Critical reason for the critical event driver related factor "Overcompensation".
