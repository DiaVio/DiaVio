The crash occurred in the northeast corner of two roadways. The north/south roadway was a two-way divided roadway with three lanes in each direction. The east/west roadway was two-way with two lanes in each direction. This was a straight, level, bituminous roadway with a speed limit of 48 kmph (30 mph). It was the morning hours of a weekday, and the road was wet and it was raining.

V1, a 1996 Nissan Maxima four door with two occupants, was traveling northbound in lane two. A non-contact vehicle traveling southbound attempted to make a left turn in front of V1. V1 lost control and its front impacted an iron fence on the northeast corner of the intersection. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

V1, the Nissan Maxima, was driven by a 29 year-old male who reported no injuries. A 26 year-old male occupant also reported no injuries. The driver advised that he was commuting from home to work, and going straight through the intersection, a van (non-contact vehicle) attempted to make a left turn in front of him. He attempted to steer right while braking with lock-up, lost control and impacted an iron fence on the northeast corner of the intersection. He successfully avoided the van (non-contact vehicle), but was unable to avoid the fence.

The Critical Pre-crash event for V1 was "this vehicle traveling off the edge of the road on the right side". The Critical Reason for the Critical event was coded “overcompensation”. A weather-related factor was coded as cloudy and raining and wet roads.
