The crash occurred in the southbound lane of a two-lane, rural roadway along a left curve. The roadway had a posted speed of 72 kmph (45 mph) and the southbound lane had a 5% downhill grade. Conditions were dry and cloudy. The crash occurred at dawn on a weekend morning.

Vehicle one was a 2005 Nissan Sentra sedan traveling south and negotiating the left curve. V1 braked and traveled off the right side of the roadway. V1 continued off the roadway and struck a tree with its right rear quarter panel. V1 continued further, and upon reaching a ditch rolled over one complete turn to the right, coming to rest on its wheels. Police Reported Travel Speed is 97 kmph for V1.

V1 was driven by a 26-year old female who was transported to a local trauma center with non-incapacitating injuries. She was unable to be located for an interview. The investigating officer estimated her travel and impact speeds to be 97 kmph (60 mph). V1 was towed due to damage.

The Critical Precrash Event for v1 was this vehicle traveling off the edge of the road on the right side. The Critical Reason was unknown. Police report information suggested that this driver was speeding. Medical report information revealed a history of asthma, seizure, and several prescription medications.
