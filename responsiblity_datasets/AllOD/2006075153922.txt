A two-car crash occurred in the third southbound lane of a five-lane roadway divided by a painted median. There were three through lanes in the southbound direction and two through lanes in the northbound direction. The roadway was straight, bituminous, with an uphill grade in the southbound direction of travel. It had a posted seep limit of 72 kmph (45 mph). It was mid-afternoon on a weekday, the road was dry and the sky was clear. The traffic flow was moderate and congested.

Vehicle one (V1), a 2006 Ford Five Hundred sedan with only the driver present was traveling southbound in lane three. Vehicle two (V2), a 1999 Dodge Durango SUV with only the driver present was stopped in lane three in front of V1. V2 was stopped behind another non-contact vehicle waiting for a traffic light to turn green when V1 contacted the back end of V2 with its front end. V2 was pushed forward and came to rest in lane three on all four tires facing south. V1 also came to rest in lane three on all four tires facing south. Police Reported Travel Speed is 64 kmph for V1 and 64 kmph for V2.

The driver of V1, a 44-year old female, was reported as having possible injuries but refused transport to a medical facility. Little information is known about this driver due to lack of an interview. She was reported by the investigating officer as going approximately 64 kmph (40 mph) before contacting V2. V1 was equipped with both driver and passenger airbags, which did not deploy during the crash. She was also reported as wearing a lap and shoulder belt. This vehicle was towed due to moderate frontal damage. The Critical Precrash Event for V1 was coded: other motor vehicle in lane, other vehicle stopped. Vehicle and environmental factors for the critical reason were ruled out, as were driver critical non-performance and performance factors. It could not be determined it the critical reason was a driver decision or recognition factor. The Critical Reason for the Critical Event was coded as an unknown driver error due to the driver not wanting to talk to the researcher and no further information

A 29-year old male who reported no injuries drove V2, the Dodge Durango. He stated that he was listening to the radio and going home from work for lunch. He was stopped in traffic at the light and then was hit from behind by V1. He received eight and a half hours of sleep the night before and works approximately eight hours a day for an average of forty to forty five hours total a week. He also stated that he has had recent back problems but it does not affect his driving. He stated that he had taken Prilosec medication in the last twenty-four hours. This vehicle was driven from the scene. The Critical Precrash Event for V2 was coded: other motor vehicle in lane, traveling in the same direction with higher speed. The Critical Reason for the Critical Event was not coded to this vehicle.
