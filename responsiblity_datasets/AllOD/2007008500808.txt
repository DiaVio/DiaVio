This crash occurred in the northbound lanes of a six-lane highway divided by a concrete barrier. There were three northbound lanes and three southbound lanes. The roadway curved to the right, was level, bituminous, and had a posted speed limit of 89kmph (55 mph). It was almost midnight on a weekday, the roadway had some icy patches, and conditions were clear.

V1, a 2006 Ford E 150 driven by a 44-year-old female, was northbound negotiating a curve. As V1 continued northbound in lane two, it lost traction on a patch of ice. The vehicle began to rotate clockwise. It then left its travel lane to the left and lightly contacted a concrete barrier with its back. The vehicle then crossed all three lanes of the roadway, and struck a guardrail with its front, where it came to final rest. Police Reported Travel Speed is No PAR obtained (created) for V1.

The Critical Precrash Event for V1 was coded this vehicle control loss due to, poor road conditions. The Critical Reason for the Critical Precrash Event was a driver related factor, traveling too fast for conditions. There were many roadways that were experiencing poor conditions on this evening, including the major highways, and the ice was not readily apparent. Associated factors include inexperience as this was the driver's first time operating this vehicle and she had not been on this particular roadway in quite a while.
