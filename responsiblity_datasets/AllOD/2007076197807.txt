This single-vehicle crash occurred in a rural residential area on a two-lane asphalt roadway. The eastbound roadway is downhill and curves left, then curves right slightly. The speed limit is 40 kmph (25 mph). The crash occurred during daylight on a weekend. There were no adverse weather or road conditions present at the time of the crash.

Vehicle one (V1), a 2000 Chevrolet Impala 4-door sedan, was eastbound traveling at a driver reported speed of 80 - 89 kmph (50 - 55 mph) when it crossed the yellow line with its left tires. The vehicle was steered right and skidded off the right side of the roadway where it impacted a dirt and rock embankment with its right front tire and right side plane. The vehicle came to rest on the right dirt shoulder facing east. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 89 kmph for V1.

The driver, a sixteen (16) year-old male and his passenger, a fifteen (15) year-old female, were driving around in order to dry the car after washing it at the car wash. They had traveled 6.4 kilometers (4 miles) from the car wash when the crash occurred. The driver stated he was steering with his left hand while swatting at the hands of the passenger with his right hand. The two occupants were playfully arguing about which CD they were going to listen to. The driver stated he was looking inside the vehicle towards the CD player and when he looked up he discovered he was going too fast. He braked and lost control of the vehicle. This was the first time he had driven on this roadway and he had been driving for only 2 months.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: this vehicle traveling, off the edge of the road on the right side. The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event was coded as a driver related factor: internal distraction.
