The crash occurred off the right side of the roadway on a suburban highway with a raised concrete curb median. There were four southbound lanes, including a right and left turn lane prior to a T-intersection. After the intersection, the roadway had only two southbound lanes. The roadway had a posted speed limit of 89 kmph (55 mph) and the southbound lanes had a downhill grade of 2 percent. Conditions were dark and wet with rain. The setting was early evening hours on a weekday.

Vehicle one was a 1996 Chevrolet S10 pickup traveling southbound in lane one. V1 traveled past a cross street to the right and then skidded off the right side of the road abruptly. V1 rotated clockwise 180 degrees before rolling left 2-quarter turns into a large, rock-strewn, drainage ditch. V1 came to rest on its top in the drainage ditch. Police Reported Travel Speed is 89 kmph for V1.

The Chevrolet S10 was driven by a 20-year old male who was uninjured. He was unable to be interviewed on scene due to a language barrier. Further attempts for a translator to locate him or his passenger were unsuccessful. The investigating officer estimated his travel and impact speeds to be 89 kmph (55 mph) and 80 kmph (50 mph), respectively. The Chevrolet pickup was towed due to damage.

The Critical Pre-crash Event for V1 was this vehicle control loss - unknown reason for control loss. The Critical Reason for the Critical Pre-crash Event was a driver related factor of an unknown performance error. Associated factors that were coded were the rainy weather and the wet roadway. No other factors could be coded due to the lack of interview. The driver did not have an operator's license.
