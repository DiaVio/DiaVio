The crash occurred in the westbound lanes of a 5-lane undivided roadway. There were three lanes westbound and two lanes eastbound. The roadway was straight and level. Traffic flow was normal. The posted speed was 48 MPH (30 MPH). The crash occurred 32 meters from a four-way intersection. The conditions were dry and clear on a weekday evening.

V1 was a 1990 Nissan pickup truck that was traveling westbound, having just made a right hand turn at the intersection. The truck then departed the right hand side of the roadway and hit a palm tree, causing front right damage. V1 was towed due to damages. The driver was cited for the crash for failure to use due care. No one was injured. Police Reported Travel Speed is 40 kmph for V1.

The driver of V1 was a 21-year-old female who was on her way home from the mall. The driver stated she had just made a right hand turn from the intersection and then just lost control and hit the tree. The driver had illegal tires on the truck as they were too wide and not the recommended tire size. She was cited for having illegal tires as well as failure to maintain control. The driver stated she had no idea what caused her to lose control. She did admit to having an argument with her boyfriend, who was also in the vehicle, at the time of the crash. The boyfriend also had a suspended license and the truck was registered in his name. The police speculated that he may have been the driver of the truck but did not want to get in trouble so the girlfriend took the blame as the driver. There were no witnesses to the crash so this could not be proved. The recommended tire size for V1 was P195/75R14 whereas the tires that were on the truck were P225/60R16.

The Critical Pre-crash Event for V1 was this vehicle traveling off the right side of the roadway. The Critical Reason for the Critical Event was a driver related factor, poor directional control. Associated factors include the argument with the passenger prior to the crash and the use of non-recommended tires.
