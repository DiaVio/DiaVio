This single-vehicle crash occurred on a four-lane, east/west, undivided, bituminous, urban roadway. There are two through lanes in each direction. The roadway is straight and slopes down for westbound traffic and has no visible deficiencies. The speed limit on this roadway is 56 kmph (35mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.

Vehicle one (V1), a 1993 two door Mercury Cougar was westbound in lane one traveling above the speed limit. An unknown white, non-contact vehicle merged from westbound lane two into lane one. The driver of V1 turned right and applied the brakes as an evasive maneuver. V1 departed the right side of the road and struck a non-breakaway light pole with its front. It came to rest at the point of impact facing west. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

The driver of V1, a seventeen-year-old male, was familiar with the vehicle and traveled the route daily. He was well rested and not in a hurry. He has two years of driving experience but has had no formal driver education.

The pre-event movement for V1 was coded as "successful avoidance maneuver to a previous critical event". The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "this vehicle traveling, off the edge of the road on the right side". The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "incorrect evasive action".
