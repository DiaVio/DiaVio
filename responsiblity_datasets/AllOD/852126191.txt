This crash occurred on a divided freeway during daylight hours with clear, dry conditions. Case vehicle one (V1), a 2004 Toyota Prius four door hatchback was traveling in lane two of the four southbound through lanes. Vehicle two (V2), a 1993 Nissan Pathfinder was traveling in the same lane ahead of V1 and began to decelerate for stopped traffic ahead in the lane. Case V1 began to brake, but the front of V1 impacted the back of V2. With V1 braking and the back bumper height of V2 above the front bumper of V1, the damage of V1 was above bumper with majority of the crush into the hood and grill of V1. Both vehicles continued forward and came to final rest in the same lane facing south. Both vehicles were damaged and towed. The case occupant is the driver of V1 and was the only occupant in V1. This driver was 76 year old female who was wearing the lap and shoulder belt with a pretensioner and the frontal steering column mounted air bag deployed. The driver was using an aftermarket Loopo clip on the seatbelt for possible positioning. The driver had a few gallons of paint in the rear cargo that entered into the passenger compartment and splattered paint over the interior surfaces. This case driver had serious injuries and was transported to the trauma center.
