The case vehicle a four-door 2000 Dodge Stratus was westbound on a dry, level, asphalt two-lane roadway approaching the intersection of a six-lane divided highway. Vehicle two a 2007 Chevrolet Uplander minivan was in the outside southbound lane of the intersecting highway. As the case vehicle was crossing the highway, it entered the path of V2. The front of V2 struck the right fender of the case vehicle. The impact caused the case vehicle to rotate counterclockwise and V2 to rotate clockwise, resulting in a side-slap impact. The case vehicle continued to rotate counterclockwise and came to rest in southwest corner of the intersection facing northwest. V2 departed the roadway at the southwest corner struck a wooden stop-sign post went down a slope into a private lawn and stuck a tree and came to rest against it facing south-southwest. The 76-year-old female driver and the eight-year-old male left-rear passenger were the only occupants of the case vehicle. The driver was restrained by a three-point belt and the steering-wheel air bag deployed. The left-rear passenger was restrained by a three-point belt. The driver sustained serious injuries and was transported to a local hospital and later transferred to a level-one trauma center. The left-rear passenger was not injured. Both vehicles were towed due to damage.
