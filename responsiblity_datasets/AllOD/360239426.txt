This case occupant is a 32 year old male driver, wearing the available manual lap/shoulder belt, who was involved in a moderate near-side impact. This occupant's seat back mounted side air bag deployed during the crash and he sustained serious injuries. This crash occurred during evening hours under rainy weather and wet roadway conditions. Vehicle one (V1), a 2005 Ford Focus four door sedan, was westbound in lane one of a three lane, two way roadway. This roadway is comprised of one westbound and two eastbound straight and level asphalt travel lanes. Adjacent to the south side of the roadway is a sidewalk, followed by a grassy area containing several poles, street signs and train tracks. While traveling west, the driver of V1 lost control of the vehicle, crossed the centerline and began to rotate counterclockwise. V1 continued across the eastbound travel lanes and then V1's right front tire impacted the curb, resulting in tire damage. V1 continued over the curb while in a yaw, crossed the sidewalk and then V1's right front impacted a pole, resulting in moderate damage. V1 rotated slightly counterclockwise and the right side of V1 impacted a smaller pole, resulting in minor damage. Then V1's right side impacted a street sign, breaking it off its base and resulting in minor damage. V1 continued rotating counterclockwise, traveled across a set of train tracks and then the left side of V1 impacted a pole, resulting in the deployment of the driver's left side air bag. V1 rebounded off the pole and came to final rest, still off the roadway, facing northwest. V1 was towed due to damage. The case occupant is the driver of V1, a 32 year old male, who was wearing the available manual lap/shoulder belt. His left seat back outboard mounted side air bag deployed during the crash. This driver sustained serious injuries and was transported to the trauma center where he was hospitalized for ten days. There were no other occupants in the vehicle.
