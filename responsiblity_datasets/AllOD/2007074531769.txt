This single-vehicle crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions. The roadway is undivided and level and consists of two lanes in each direction. The speed limit for this roadway is 56 kmph (35 mph). The southbound lanes in the precrash area are curved to the left.

Vehicle one (V1), a 1998 Honda Civic, was southbound in lane one. V1 traveled off the right side of the roadway and the back right tire/wheel struck the curb. V1 continued southbound, out of control and then off the right side of the road again. The right side struck a utility pole and a sign and the left side struck a utility pole guy wire. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

The driver of V1, a sixteen-year-old male, was traveling with his fourteen-year-old female friend and a fifteen-year-old male friend. This driver had borrowed this vehicle without permission and picked up his friends. They were on their way to a store to buy snacks. This driver was healthy and well rested. This was his first time traveling this route and he had driven this vehicle only a few times. At the time of the crash, this driver had six months of driving experience. He also stated that he was not comfortable with this vehicle since the correct size tires/wheels were on the back but larger size tires/wheels had been installed on the front axle.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "this vehicle traveling; off the edge of the road on the right side." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "driver related factor; too fast for curve/turn." Associated factors in this crash include this driver's inexperience and his poor directional control as well as the vehicle defect created by mismatched tires/wheels.
