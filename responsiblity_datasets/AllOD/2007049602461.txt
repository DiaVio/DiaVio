This three-vehicle intersection type crash occurred at an intersection of two one-way streets. The intersection is controlled by traffic lights. The east/west road is a four-lane (one way) straight, level, concrete roadway. It is a four lane, concrete, level, straight roadway from north to south. The two service roads are part of a major connector between to major highways. The speed limit is 64 kmph (40 mph) for both roadways. The road surface was dry and there were no detrimental atmospheric conditions at the time of the crash. It was early afternoon on a weekend and there was light traffic on the roadways.

Vehicle one (V1), a 2008 Chevrolet Malibu with one occupant was traveling north in the second lane of traffic approaching the intersection of both roadways. This driver was traveling the wrong way on the one-way street. Vehicle two (V2), a 1999 Lexus ES 300 with three occupants, was traveling west in the lane two. Vehicle three (V3), a 1997 Ford Explorer with four occupants, was traveling west in the first lane. At the intersection of both roadways, the front of V1 impacted the left side of V2. V2 rotated counter clockwise and moved farther west until it came to rest within lane two and one of the westbound lanes, facing northeast. V1 rotated counter clockwise and continued to move north. Its right front corner impacted the left side of V3. V1 and V3 both came to rest within the intersection, facing west. All vehicles were towed from the scene. The driver and occupants of V1 and V2 were treated at the scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

A 58-year old male drove V1. The driver was from out of state. The vehicle was a rental car, equipped with a portable GPS unit provided by the rental car company. The driver stated at the scene that he was confused by the way the traffic way was constructed. He took the wrong left turn and ended up traveling the wrong way despite using a GPS unit. It was the first time for him driving in this area. He planned to move to the area from out of state at a later time. This driver was not interviewed. No further information is available for this driver.

The critical pre-crash event was coded "this vehicle traveling-passing through an intersection." The critical reason for the critical event was coded "illegal maneuver." The driver drove the wrong way on a one-way street.

A 34-year old male drove V2. He and his wife were heading to a friend's house for a visit. Their 15-month-old daughter was seated in a child seat in the center seat of the second row. He stated he was talking with his wife about some evening classes she and a friend were going to take. Since the crossroad in front of him was a one-way roadway, he did not look to his left but only to his right side, where the cross traffic was coming from. In his recall of the events, he was stopped at the red light and after the light turned green preceded. The dynamics of the crash and the final rest position of V2 indicate V2 was driving through the intersection at about the same range of speed the driver of V3 advised.

The critical pre crash event was coded "other vehicle encroachment-from crossing street, across path." No critical reason for the critical event was coded to this vehicle.

A 41-year-old male was the driver of V3, He was heading for a nearby church with his family, after he stopped by a super market. He did not see V1 prior to the first harmful event. He stated the light was green for him and V2 when he approached the intersection. A witness, traveling behind V2 and V3, was stopped at a red light located a block east of the crash site. This witness stated V2 and V3 had a green light and proceeded into the intersection when V1 came at normal speed and impacted both vehicles without taking any evasive action.

A critical pre-crash event and reason was not coded to this vehicle, because it was not involved in the first harmful event.
