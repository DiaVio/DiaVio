This two-vehicle crash occurred in a four-leg intersection. The north/south roadway is divided, level and straight. The northbound lanes consist of three through lanes and a left turn lane. The speed limit on this roadway is 72 kmph (45 mph). The east/west roadways are divided parking lot entrances. The eastbound approach lanes have a 7.4% downgrade and consist of two lanes controlled by a stop sign. It was dark with streetlights on at the time of this crash and there were no adverse atmospheric conditions.

Vehicle one (V1), a 2004 Nissan Pathfinder Armada, was stopped eastbound at the stop sign. Vehicle two (V2), a 1999 Pontiac Sunfire, was northbound in lane one. V1 entered the intersection and the front of V2 struck the right side of V1. The vehicles rotated and the right side of V1 struck the left side of V2. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The driver of V1, a thirty-nine-year-old female, was traveling from work to a nail salon. She was healthy, well rested and familiar with her vehicle. This driver travels on this roadway daily. This driver stated that she looked left and right before entering the intersection but did not see V2.

The driver of V2, a thirty-six-year-old male, refused to be interviewed. No information is available for this driver.

The pre-event movement for V1 was coded as "stopped in traffic lane." The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "this vehicle traveling; crossing over (passing through) intersection." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "driver related factor; inadequate surveillance (e.g. failed to look, looked but did not see)."

The pre-event movement for V2 was coded as "going straight." The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded as "other vehicle encroachment; from crossing street, across path." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V2 was "not coded to this vehicle."
