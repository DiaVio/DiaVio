This single vehicle crash occurred just past noon in a residential area on a two-lane asphalt roadway. The roadway is straight and on a downhill elevation in an east/west direction. The crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse weather or road conditions. The posted speed limit was 24 kmph (15 mph).

Vehicle one (V1) a 1992 Chevrolet van-based light truck vehicle used as a mail delivery vehicle driven by a 60 year-old male. was delivering mail when his left knee bumped emergency brake handle allowing V1 to move forward and ejecting the driver V1 then departing the road to the right climbing onto a cinder block wall and then striking a wooden utility pole with the right front bumper. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 32 kmph for V1.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: this vehicle traveling, off the edge of the road on the right side. The critical reason for the critical event was coded as a driver related factor: other performance error (specified as: driver, who was delivering mail, accidentally hit emergency brake with knee and released it, ejecting the driver. V1 then departed right side of roadway).
