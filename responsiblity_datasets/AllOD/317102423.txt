This case involved a 44 year old unrestrained female driver of a 2006 Mercury Milan four door sedan (V1). There were no other occupants in V1. The driver was not utilizing the available three point manual safety belt, but did have a deployed frontal airbag. For unknown reasons the steering wheel became separated from the steering column during the crash. The crash occurred during daylight hours on a two lane rural roadway that curved to the right. The asphalt roadway was dry and it was cloudy at the time of the crash. The posted speed limit was 72 kph (45 mph). V1 was traveling east on the downhill grade when it traveled across the center line into the westbound lane. A non-contact vehicle was going west at an unknown speed approaching V1. V1 swerved to the right to avoid impact with the non-contact vehicle. V1 continued off the right side of the road in a slight clockwise yaw and into a ditch. As the front bumper of V1 impacted the ditch, it began to roll to the left. V1 rolled four quarter turns striking a 44 cm tree with its left front quarter panel. V1 rotated clockwise around the tree, then continued to roll two quarter turns more to final rest.on its top. V1 was towed from the scene due to disabling damage. The driver of V1 was transported by ground ambulance to a hospital and subsequently transferred to a level one trauma center.
