This case involves an impact between the front-end of a pick-up truck and a large tree. The 35-year-old male driver is the case occupant. He was not restrained by a three-point belt, but the steering-wheel air bag deployed. The case vehicle a 2006, GMC Sierra extended cab pick-up was southbound on a two-lane, dry, asphalt road. It was dark and clear. The case vehicle crossed the center-line into the northbound travel lane , departed the road off the east side and struck a large tree on the roadside. The case vehicle came to rest facing south. The 35-year-old male driver was not restrained by a three-point belt, but the steering-wheel air bag deployed. He was the only occupant of the case vehicle. The driver sustained serious injuries and was transported to a level-one trauma center and enrolled as a case occupant.
