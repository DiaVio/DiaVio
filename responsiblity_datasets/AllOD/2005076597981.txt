This single vehicle crash occurred on a four-lane divided asphalt roadway in a rural mountain area. This roadway runs north to south and has various curves and grades. At the area of this crash the roadway is straight with a downgrade of -4 percent. The posted speed limit is 105 kmph (65 mph). This crash occurred during daylight with clear conditions and good visibility.

Vehicle one (V1), a 2002 Ford Explorer sport utility vehicle was northbound in the number two lane traveling at 80 kmph (50 mph) when the driver felt the left rear tire begin to vibrate and wobble as the tread separated. The driver braked lightly and the vehicle rotated clockwise. V1 crossed the number one lane and exited the right road edge. V1 traveled off the shoulder and struck a dirt embankment. V1 then rode the embankment horizontally and struck a medium size tree where it rotated clockwise and came to rest down in a ravine. V1 was towed for damages. Police Reported Travel Speed is 80 kmph for V1.

The six occupants of V1 were on their way to go camping. The driver was a 36 year-old female who reported feeling well rested and in good physical condition. She drove this particular roadway monthly and had been driving the Explorer for three years. The tread for all four tires appeared to be in good condition. The reason for the tread separation on the left rear tire is unknown. The tire remained inflated and contained 35 psi at the post-crash inspection, the recommended tire pressure.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 is coded: this vehicle control loss due to, blow out/flat tire/tread separation. When the separation occurred, the vehicle initiated a clockwise yaw as it ran off the right road edge. The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event is coded as a vehicle related factor: tires/wheels failed.
