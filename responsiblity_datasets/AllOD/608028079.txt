The case vehicle, a 2005 Chevrolet Trail Blazer SUV was headed west on a level, wet two-lane asphalt roadway, approaching a four-leg intersection. Vehicle two (V2), a 1993 Ford Explorer SUV was traveling north on the intersecting two-lane roadway. It was dark, but the roadway was lighted. As the case vehicle entered the intersection the front of V2 struck the left side of the case vehicle. After impact both vehicles rotated counter-clockwise. V2 came to rest in the southbound lane facing south. The case vehicle departed the roadway off the northwest corner of the intersection and rolled one-quarter turn before it came to rest on the roadside facing northeast. The case occupant the 65-year-old female driver was restrained by a three-point belt. The 36-year-old female right-front passenger was also restrained by a three-point belt. No air bags deployed during the crash. The driver sustained severe injuries and was taken to an outside hospital and later transferred to a level one trauma center. The right-front passenger, sustained police reported 'B' injuries and was taken to an outside hospital.
