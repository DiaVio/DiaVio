This one-vehicle crash occurred at night on a weekday in the middle of a two-lane, one-way roadway, which was physically divided. The bituminous roadway was straight and level. There were no traffic control devices associated with this particular stretch of roadway. The posted speed limit was 72kmph (45mph). It was raining heavily at the time of the crash and the roadway was wet. There were no traffic flow factors at the time of the crash.

Vehicle one (V1), a 2003 Chevrolet Impala, was heading southbound on the roadway in lane one intending to continue straight. A deer had entered the roadway and V1 successfully avoided hitting the animal. Unfortunately, the driver lost control of the vehicle and swerved off the right side of the roadway. He struck the guide rail and rolled over onto its roof where it came to final rest in lane one facing south. The vehicle had to be removed from the scene due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 72 kmph for V1.

The driver of V1, a 17 year-old male suffered non-incapacitating injuries and was taken to a medical facility for treatment. There were no other occupants in the vehicle when the crash occurred. After numerous attempts the driver could not be contacted for an interview. For this reason, it is uncertain if any fatigue or health related issues contributed to this crash.

The Critical Precrash Event coded to this vehicle was: object or animal (deer) in roadway. The Critical Reason was coded as: Traveling too fast for conditions (i.e., deer in road and wet road surface). Associated factors coded for this vehicle were inexperienced driver and wet road surface..
