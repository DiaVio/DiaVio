This single-vehicle crash occurred near a four-legged, traffic signal-controlled intersection. The westbound approach is straight, one-way, has an uphill grade and consists of four lanes. The speed limit for this roadway is 56 kmph (35 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.

Vehicle one (V1), a 2001 Hyundai Santa Fe, was westbound in lane four. V1 turned left in the intersection, traveled off the right side of the road and struck three pedestrians. V1 continued on, traveling back toward the roadway. The front of V1 struck a wooden utility pole that sheared and struck the top of V1. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

The driver of V1, a thirty-nine-year-old female, was on her way home from an aunt's house. She was healthy, well rested and familiar with her vehicle. She travels this route daily. This driver stated that the brakes failed. The brakes were checked and seemed to be fully operational.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "this vehicle traveling; off the edge of the road on the right side." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "driver related factor; poor directional control (e.g., failing to control vehicle with skill ordinarily expected)."
