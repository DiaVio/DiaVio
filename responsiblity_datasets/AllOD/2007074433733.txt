This two-vehicle intersection crash occurred during early morning daylight hours. The roadway surface is dry,bituminous, straight, and a 2% slope. Posted speed limit is 72 KPH (45 mph) and roadway is controlled by on-colors traffic signal with pedestrian crossing. No adverse weather conditions present.

Vehicle one (V1), a 2004 Ford Focus sedan driven by a seventeen (17) year-old female driver with two passengers was traveling south in lane two with the intention of turning left. Vehicle two (V2) a 1998 Toyota Sienna mini van driven by a forty (40) year-old female. V1 was commuting to school, she was stopped at intersection waiting to turn left. Once traffic signal started to turn yellow, she observed northbound vehicles stopping, especially a large school bus, and proceeded to turn. Having failed to notice approaching V2, the right front corner and side of V1 was struck by V2 front. After impact, V1 deflected counterclockwise and came to final rest in intersection facing northeast. V2 came to final rest in the intersection facing northeast. Both vehicles were towed from the scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 coded this vehicle traveling turning left at intersection. Critical reason for the critical event coded driver related factor "false assumption of other's actions".

The critical pre-crash event for V2 coded this vehicle traveling crossing over passing through intersection. Critical reason not coded to this vehicle.
