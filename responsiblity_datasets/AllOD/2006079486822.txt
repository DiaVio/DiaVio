This single vehicle crash occurred shortly past noon during daylight hours. The crash occurred on a seven lane undivided, dry, level, asphalt roadway with a two-way left turn lane. The roadway is designated 56 kmph (35 mph).

Vehicle one (V1) was a 1997 Toyota Camry sedan driven by a 65 year-old female with one passenger, a 12-month old baby. V1 was traveling west in the number one lane. An unknown large truck traveling west in the number two lane, which changed lanes to the right, cut off V1. V1 then steered right and departed the roadway. V1 struck the curb then a light pole with its front plane. V1 then rotated clockwise and continued northwest to impact an iron fence and a chain link fence with its front plane. V1 came to rest on the sidewalk at impact with the fence facing north. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: this vehicle traveling, off the edge of the road on the right side. The critical reason for the critical event was coded to V1 as a driver related factor: poor directional control.
