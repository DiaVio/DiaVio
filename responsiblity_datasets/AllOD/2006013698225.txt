This crash occurred in the center turn lane of a five-lane, non-divided, dry, asphalt city roadway with a posted speed limit of 80Kmh (50Mph). The crash occurred during daylight hours and there were no adverse conditions at the time of the crash. The roadway has a positive grade of 5.6% at the point of impact.

V1 a 1991 Chevy Lumina was pulling out of a gas station parking lot attempting to turn left. V2 a 1993 Ford Explorer was traveling west bound in the second lane. V1 pulled out in front of V2, and contacted V2 with it's front bumper. V2 was hit in the front right side. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The driver of V1 a 20 year old female driver stated that she looked to the left but did not see V2. The driver also stated that she would not have been contacted if V2 had not steered into the turn lane.

The Critical Pre-crash event coded for V1 is this "vehicle traveling turning left". The Critical Reason for the Critical Pre-crash Event coded for V1 is "inadequate surveillance".
