This single-vehicle crash occurred on a straight, access-controlled exit ramp. This trafficway is divided by a jersey barrier, has a downhill grade and consists of one lane in the eastbound direction on each side of the barrier. This roadway was covered with ice and slush at the time of the crash. The speed limit on this roadway is 105 kmph (65 mph) with an advisory speed of 64 kmph (40 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.

Vehicle one (V1), a 1997 Toyota 4Runner, was eastbound in lane one. V1 began to rotate. The driver steered left and right but V1 traveled off the left side of the roadway and the front struck the jersey barrier. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 64 kmph for V1.

The driver of V1, a thirty-year-old female, was traveling with a thirty-year-old female passenger, from a day of skiing to her home. She was healthy, well rested and comfortable with her vehicle and passenger. She rarely drives this route. This driver stated that she was traveling at 64-72 kmph (40-45 mph) when she lost control on the icy road and was unable to regain control to avoid the crash.

The pre-event movement for V1 was coded as "going straight." The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "this vehicle control loss due to traveling too fast for conditions." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "driver related factor; too fast for conditions (too fast for icy conditions on the road."
