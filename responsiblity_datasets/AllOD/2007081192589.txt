This single vehicle crash occurred during early evening hours . The location was on an East/West four lane, two way, and undivided, inter-city state route with a T-intersection. Traffic control at the intersection was overhead on-color signals with crosswalks. The northbound roadway was a two lane, two way, and undivided bituminous street joining the state route from the south. It was raining, the street was wet and it was dark with street lights present. The posted speed limit is 80 kmph (50 mph).

Vehicle one (V1), a 2004 Cadillac CTS sedan, was stopped northbound at the intersection. When the light turned green the driver pulled into the intersection and negotiated a left turn to head westbound. The driver stated as she turned into the outside lane, something went wrong with the steering on the vehicle. She stated she lost control of her vehicle. She departed the right side of the roadway and hit the barrier. The officer investigating the crash checked the steering and the brakes and both were in good working order. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: this vehicle traveling, off the edge of the road on the right side. The critical reason for the critical event was coded as a driver related factor: poor directional control (e.g., failed to control vehicle with skill ordinarily expected). Associated factors: all four tires were under inflated by at least 34 kpa (5 psi), wet roadway and overcompensation.
