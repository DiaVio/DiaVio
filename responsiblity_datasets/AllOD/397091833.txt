This crash occurred on an exit ramp for a limited access trafficway. The exit ramp is comprised of 2 travel lanes and is situated in a north to south configuration. At the time of the crash it was early morning dark and the streetlights were lit. The weather was clear and the road surface was dry. Traffic for that time of the day is moderate to light. Involved in the crash was the case vehicle (V1) a 1999 Chevrolet Malibu 4-door sedan equipped with manual 3-point lap and shoulder restraint for each outboard seating position. In addition to the manual restraints the case vehicle was also equipped with driver and right passenger frontal impact air bags, both of which, deployed in the crash. The driver, and lone occupant, of the case vehicle was a 52-year-old male. He was using the available manual restraints at the time of the crash. The case vehicle was traveling in a southerly direction. The driver was exiting the limited access trafficway and was traveling in the second lane of the off ramp. For an unknown reason the case vehicle departed the exit ramp at the left side. The case vehicle continued south in the grass. Contact evidence inside the vehicle indicated that the driver had his right foot on the brake at impact. The case vehicle struck a palm tree that was located in the grass bordering the east side of the exit ramp. After the impact, the case vehicle rotated counterclockwise approximately 90 degrees and came to final rest still engaged with the tree. It is not known if the driver loss consciousness in the crash. He was awake upon arrival of the paramedics however his glascow coma score was 14. He was removed from the vehicle by the paramedics and transported to the trauma center via helicopter under a "High Suspicion of Injury" criterion. He arrived at the trauma center 48 minutes after the crash.
