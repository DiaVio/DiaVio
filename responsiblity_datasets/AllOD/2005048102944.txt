This single-vehicle crash occurred on a two-lane, bituminous, non-divided roadway. This roadway curves to right and has a speed limit of 64 kmph (40 MPH). This crash occurred during early morning hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.

Vehicle one (V1), a 2000 Lexus RX300 was southbound. The vehicle's right tires departed the right road edge. The driver steered left to re-enter the roadway and departed the left side of the traffic lane into the northbound lane. The driver then braked and steered right. This caused the vehicle to go into a clockwise rotation. The vehicle departed the right road edge leading with the driver's side. The tires tripped on the ground and the vehicle rolled 1/4 turn onto the drivers side and came to final rest on the drivers side facing SE. As the vehicle rolled its roof came into contact with a utility pole. V1 rotated and came to rest off the roadway facing east on its left side. Police Reported Travel Speed is 64 kmph for V1.

The driver of V1, a forty-two year old female was driving to work and had only been driving for about twenty minutes at the time of the crash. This driver received enough sleep the night before. This driver drives this road daily and in this vehicle on a daily basis.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: this vehicle traveling, off the edge of the road on the right side. The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as a driver performance factor: poor directional control.
