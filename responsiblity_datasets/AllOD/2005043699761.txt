The crash occurred at an intersection in the northbound lanes of an eight-lane suburban roadway with a concrete median. The roadway had a posted speed of 50 mph and a level grade. Conditions were daylight, wet, and raining with cloudy skies. The crash occurred in the mid-afternoon hours of a weekday.

Vehicle one was a 2003 Honda Accord sedan. Vehicle two was a 1994 Pontiac Grand Prix sedan. V1 was traveling south in the fifth lane left turn lane approaching the intersection. V2 was traveling north in the third lane. V1 proceeded to turn left at the intersection. The front of v1 impacted the left side of v2. Both vehicles came to rest in the intersection. Police Reported Travel Speed is 32 kmph for V1 and 32 kmph for V2.

V1 was driven by a 34-year old female who was uninjured. She refused to be interviewed. The investigating officer estimated her travel and impact speeds to be 20 mph, with no slowing down prior to impact. V1 was towed due to damage.

The Critical Precrash Event for v1 was turning left at an intersection. The Critical Reason was Unknown.

V2 was driven by a 35-year old female who was transported to a local trauma center with possible injuries. Her two passengers were also transported. She stated that she was taking her daughter and a friend to dinner that afternoon. A car turned in front of her at the intersection. She steered right and braked, but was unable to avoid the crash. She estimated her travel and impact speeds to be 35 and 25 mph, respectively. V2 was not towed due to police-reported damage.

The Critical Precrash Event for v2 was other vehicle encroachment from the opposite direction over the left lane line. No Critical Reason was coded to this driver. An associated factor for this driver was a conversation with her passenger at the time of the crash.
