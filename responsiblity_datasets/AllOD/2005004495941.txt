The crash occurred off the right side of a two-lane roadway. Conditions were cloudy and dry in the afternoon on a weekday. The posted speed limit was 35 MPH.

V1 was a 1995 Volkswagen Passat sedan which was turning right and intending on going straight. The driver of V1 lost control of his vehicle and departed the right side of the roadway and the front of V1 struck a tree. V1 came to final rest up against the tree facing southwest. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

V1 was driven by a 17-year-old male who sustained moderate injuries and was transported to a local hospital. He stated he just got finished washing his car and his feet were slippery from the armarol cleaner. He also stated that his hands were oily from eating a sandwich. He was stopped at the stop sign then made a right turn onto the roadway and while he was straightening out his vehicle his foot slipped and his hands slipped off of the steering wheel. The vehicle then departed the roadway and struck the tree. V1 was traveling between 11 and 20 MPH at the time of impact. V1 was towed due to damage.

The critical precrash event for V1 was this vehicle turning right at the T-intersection. The critical reason for the critical precrash event was coded as a performance error, overcompensation. Associated factors coded to this driver were inadequate/incorrect evasive action combination of insufficient steering and braking inputs and the driver was inexperienced only having his license less than 1 year.
