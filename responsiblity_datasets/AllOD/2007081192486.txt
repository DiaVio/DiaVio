This single vehicle crash occurred mid-morning during daylight hours. The location was an urban arterial with curbs and sidewalks on both sides. The location of the crash was a marked crosswalk with a center median. The roadway was a bituminous five lane with two lanes in each direction and a center turn lane. The road was compact snow and ice and it was a negative grade for southbound traffic. The weather was overcast . The temperature was approximately -6.6 degrees Celsius (20 degrees Fahrenheit). The posted speed limit was 56 kmph (35 mph).

Vehicle one (V1), a 1991 Infiniti Q45 four door sedan driven by a thirty (30) year old male, was traveling southbound in lane two approaching the crosswalk. V1 started to slide and the driver lost control. V1 traveled up over the curb of the median and struck a small tree with the front center of the vehicle. On impact V1 rotated counter-clockwise approximately 90 degrees and came to rest facing east in the southbound lanes. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

The critical pre-crash event was coded: this vehicle control loss due to - poor road conditions snow and ice. The critical reason for the critical event was coded as a driver related factor: too fast for conditions (Specified as: snow & ice , in addition both rear tires had a minimum tread of 1/32 & 2/32").
