This case involved a 50 year old restrained female driver and lone occupant of a 2009 Honda Civic four door sedan (V1). The crash type was a sideswipe with a 1995 Dodge Dakota club cab 4x2 pick up truck (V2). The crash occurred on a two-lane roadway during daylight hours. The dry asphalt roadway was level and there were no adverse weather conditions at the time of the crash. V1 was traveling south and V2 was going north. Both vehicles were reported to have been traveling at the posted speed limit of 89 kph (55 mph). For unknown reasons, V2 traveled across the center line and sideswiped the left side of V1. V1 then departed the west side of the roadway, rotating clockwise and traveling backwards over a private driveway and down a steep embankment. V1 struck a small signpost with its back before traveling down the embankment to final rest. After impact the Dodge Dakota continued northbound 132 meters to final rest off the west side of the roadway. Both vehicles were towed from the scene due to disabling damage. The driver of V1 (our case subject) was transported to a local hospital and later transferred to a level one trauma center for treatment of injuries sustained in the crash.
