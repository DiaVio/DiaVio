This three-vehicle collision occurred on the northbound lanes of a divided highway in the second of two lanes. The pre-crash roadway for all vehicles is a straight and level bituminous paving. The roadway has a posted speed limit of 97 kmph (60 mph). At the time of this late morning crash, it was daylight, raining, and the roadway was wet..

Vehicle one (V1), a 1989 International Tractor with an empty trailer was traveling northbound on the roadway in the first lane. Vehicle two (V2), a 2000 Ford Ranger was traveling northbound in lane two. Vehicle three (V3), a 1999 Jeep Cherokee was traveling north in lane two ahead of V2. V2 and V3 were slowing in the second lane due to traffic, while traffic in the first lane was traveling at speed. V1 braked hard when a vehicle ahead of it slowed and went into a skid. The empty inter-modal trailer swung into lane two, and the wheels of the trailer stuck the rear of V2. The cab of V1 slid to the left of its lane, and struck the right mirror of V3. V2 was towed due to disabling damage. V1 and V3 were not towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The driver of V1, a forty-year old male refused a complete interview, but stated that a small car had darted in front of his truck, forcing him to slam on the brakes. He began to skid, and had no control over the vehicle. The driver of V3 stated that a car had pulled out in front of V1.

The driver of V2, a forty-seven year old female was not interviewed. There is no known pre-crash information on this driver.

The driver of V3, a fifty-six year old male was driving from one client to a second client. It is unknown how long this driver had been driving but he had only driven about 1.6 kilometers (1 mile) on this trip. It is unknown how often this driver drives this vehicle or over this roadway.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: this vehicle vehicle loss of control due to, jacknife event. The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event was attributed to V1 and was coded as a driver related factor: too fast to be able to respond to the unexpected actions of others.

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded: other vehicle encroachment; from adjacent lane [same direction] - over the right lane line. The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V2 was not coded to this vehicle.

V3 was not involved in the first harmful event. The critical reason was not coded to this vehicle.
