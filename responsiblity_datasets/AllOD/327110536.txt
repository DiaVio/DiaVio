This case involves a moderate to severe frontal impact between the front of a passenger car and the rear of a tractor-trailer combination. The case occupant is the 33-year-old male driver of the passenger car. He was not restrained by a three-point belt, but the steering-wheel air bag deployed. The case vehicle a 2007, four-door Chevrolet Cobalt was northbound in the curb lane of a dry, level, concrete, five-lane traffic way, divided by a raised median, approaching a four-leg intersection. Vehicle two (V2) a 1999 Mack Maxi Cruise tractor-trailer combination, was stopped westbound, on the intersecting road at the controlled intersection. It was day-light and cloudy. As the case vehicle approached the intersection, it reportedly made a right turn onto the intersecting eastbound lane and then made a U-turn into the westbound lane and struck the rear of V2. The unrestrained 33-year-old male driver was the sole occupant of the case vehicle. He sustained critical injuries and was transported to a level-one trauma center, where he enrolled as a case occupant.
