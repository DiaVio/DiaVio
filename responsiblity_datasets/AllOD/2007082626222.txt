This two-vehicle crash occurred in a four-leg intersection. The north/south roadway is level, straight, consists of two lanes in each direction and is controlled by a traffic signal for pedestrian crossing. The east/west roadway is straight, consists of one lane in each direction and is controlled by stop signs. The eastbound approach roadway has an 8% downgrade. This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.

Vehicle one (V1), a 1991 Ford Explorer, was stopped eastbound at the intersection. Vehicle two (V2), a 2006 Chevrolet G3500 van, was southbound in lane two. V1 entered the intersection, turning left (north), and the left side was struck by the front of V2. V1 then rolled onto its right side and slid to a stop in the intersection. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The driver of V1, a twenty-four-year-old female, stated that she didn't remember the details of the crash. She refused to answer interview questions regarding her health, activities or experience.

The driver of V2, a sixty-one-year-old male, was traveling from his home to work. He was healthy and well rested. He uses his van for work and is comfortable with the cargo inside the van (electrical supplies and tools) as well as the ladder and other supplies in the roof-mounted cargo holder. He travels this roadway weekly. This driver stated that he saw V1 stopped at the intersection and applied the brakes (ABS) when he noticed that V1 was entering the intersection.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as: this vehicle traveling, turning left at intersection. The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as a driver related factor: type of driver error unknown. Due to limited memory of the driver about the crash it could not be determined if the type of driver error was in the Recognition or Decision categories.

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded as: other vehicle encroachment; from crossing street, turning into opposite direction. The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V2 was not coded to this vehicle.
