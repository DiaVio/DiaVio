This single vehicle crash occurred during early morning daylight hours in a rural area on a 4-lane divided roadway with an extended median. The straight asphalt roadway is on a slight downhill grade for the northbound traffic and was wet at the time of the crash. Rumble strips are present on both sides of the roadway. The posted speed limit is 104 kph (65 mph).

Vehicle one (V1)a 2004 Nissan Sentra 4-door sedan driven by a 26 year-old male with one passenger was traveling northbound in the lane two when it gradually departed the left road edge onto the dirt median where it traveled 60 meters before impacting a mesquite bush with its left front bumper. V1 continued forward and down a gradual embankment for another 65 meters where it came to rest facing northwest in the extended median. V1 was towed from the scene due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 113 kmph for V1.

Information received from the officer-in-charge the driver had been driving since approximately 05:00 hours that morning when he fell asleep at the wheel after driving for 1 1/2 hours. The impact with the mesquite bush woke him and that is when he applied the brakes and came to a gradual stop.
