This crash occurred on a straight four lane divided trafficway, median with a positive barrier, with no grade and no super-elevation. The posted speed limit was 105KMPN (65MPH). The weather was clear and the roadway was dry. This crash occurred on a weekday afternoon.

Vehicle #1, a 1994 Saturn SC1, was traveling south on the four lane highway when it went into the passing lane to pass slower traffic. This vehicle then impacted with an animal (deer) that was crossing the highway from the east side heading west, with its front. The vehicle came to final rest on the east side median, facing in a southeasterly direction. The driver was treated at the scene. The drivers airbag deployed and the vehicle was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

The 1994 Saturn, (Vehicle #1) was driven by a belted 24 year-old female who stated she was looking to her right to see if she could get back into the curb lane after passing slower traffic. She stated she did not see the deer and had no chance to avoid it or use her brakes before impact. She stated she braked after impact and steered her vehicle to the left onto the median.

The Critical Precrash Event coded to Vehicle #1 was: Object or Animal, Animal in Roadway. The Critical Reason for the Critical Precrash Event was coded as: Driver Related Factor, Recognition Error, External Distraction.
