This crash occurred at the intersection of an east-west roadway with two lanes in each direction, and a one-way northbound roadway. The roadway was straight and level, and it was daylight and clear with no adverse conditions.

Vehicle # 1 was a 1991 Mazda Navajo, which was traveling westbound on the left lane. Vehicle # 2 was a 2003 Honda Accord, which was traveling northbound. At the intersection, the front of Vehicle # 2 contacted the left side of Vehicle # 1. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The driver of Vehicle # 1 was a Forty-eight year old female. She did not sustain any injuries. The driver declined to participate in the study.

The Critical Pre-crash Event was coded other vehicle encroachment from crossing street, across path. The Critical Reason was not coded to this vehicle. No other associated factors were coded to the driver and she was not thought to have contributed to the crash.

The driver of Vehicle # 2 was a Twenty-three year old male. He did not sustain any injuries. Vehicle # 2 had a Forty-two year old male passenger seated in the right front seat, did not sustain any injuries. The driver declined to participate in the study.

The Critical Pre-crash Event was coded as this vehicle traveling crossing over (passing through) intersection. The Critical Reason for the Critical Pre-crash Event was coded unknown reason for critical event.
