The crash occurred on a two-way, four-lane, not physically divided intersection controlled by a stop sign. The following conditions were present at the time of the crash: it was early morning, it was partly cloudy, the roadway was wet and there were no adverse atmospheric conditions. The gradient for V1 had a grade of -2%. The posted speed limit for vehicle one (V1) was 64 Kph (40 mph).

A non-contact vehicle (NCV) and was northbound approaching an intersection controlled by a stop sign and was intending to turn left to head westbound. V1 was traveling west in lane one of a four-lane roadway and was passing through the same intersection. As the NCV turned left it entered V1's path of travel. V1 traveled off the right side of the road and struck a brick light post, coming to final rest on the right roadside. V1 was towed from the scene. The NCV was never contacted but did stop following the crash. Police Reported Travel Speed is 64 kmph for V1.

The driver of V1 refused EMS on scene, but was taken to a hospital by a relative shortly after the crash. The interview from the driver of V1 was not obtained. Although the interview was attempted numerous times, all attempts through the driver's attorney were unsuccessful. The Pre-event Movement was a successful avoidance maneuver; the Critical Pre-crash Event for V1 was coded as off the road on right side; the Critical Reason for Pre-crash Event was coded as overcompensation. No associative factors could be determined.
