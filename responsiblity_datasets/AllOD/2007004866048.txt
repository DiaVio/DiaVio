** Case Note: Unable to contact Driver 2. Information supplied by on scene personnel and researcher observation.

The crash occurred in the center of a “T” intersection composed of a two-lane east/west roadway, and a five-lane north/southbound roadway. Both roadways were bituminous and level. The both roadways had a posted speed limit of 72 kmph (45mph). It was a weekend morning. The roads were wet and the traffic flow was light. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

V1, a 1998 Ford Contour with one occupant, was traveling north when it attempted to turn left and was struck by V2 on the right side. V2, a 1996 Toyota Avalon with two occupants, was traveling south in lane two. As V2 passed through the intersection it struck V1 on the right side.

V1, driven by an 18-year-old female who reported a Complaint of Pain and was transported, stated that she slowed down to turn left, did not see any oncoming traffic and proceeded to make the turn. V1 made no avoidance maneuver.

The Critical Precrash Event for V1 was “This Vehicle Traveling-Turning left at intersection”. The Critical reason for the Critical Event was “Driver Related Factor- Inadequate surveillance”. The following associated factors were assigned. “Other driver related experience factors-Inexperienced driver” and “Weather-Rain and cloudy”.

The Driver of V2, a 25-year-old male, and his passenger, a 22-year-old femal, reported Complaints of pain and were transported. Driver stated that he was traveling at approximately 72 kmph (45 mph) as he entered the intersection, when V2 turned across his path and he struck V1 on the right side. V2 attempted no avoidance maneuver.

The Critical Precrash Event for V2 was “Other Vehicle Encroachment-From opposite direction over left lane line”. No Critical Reason was coded to this vehicle. The following associated factor was assigned, “Weather-Rain and cloudy”.
