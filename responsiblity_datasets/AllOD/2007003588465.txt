The crash occurred in a signalized intersection of two roadways. The east/west roadway was a two-way with one lane of travel in each direction. The northbound roadway was a one-way with one lane of travel. The roadway was straight, level, bituminous, and the speed limit was 48kmph (30mph). It was the morning hours of a weekday, and was daylight and clear with no adverse conditions.

V1, a 1996 Plymouth Voyager minivan, with two occupants were traveling westbound in lane one. V2, a 2004 Mercury Grand Marquis four door, with two occupants were traveling northbound in lane one. At the intersection, the front of V2 impacted the left side of V1. The left side of V1 then contacted the right side of V2, and both vehicles came to a final rest on the northern crosswalk. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

V1, the Plymouth Voyager, was driven by a 39 year-old male who reported no injuries. A 63 year-old female occupant reported minor injuries and was transported to a medical facility. The driver stated to the Police that he was going through the green light when he was struck by V2. The driver declined to participate in the survey.

The Critical Pre-crash event for V1 was "This vehicle traveling, crossing over (passing through) intersection". The Critical Reason for the Critical event was coded as an Unknown Reason. The possible associated factors were coded unknown because no interview was obtained.

V2, the Mercury Grand Marquis was driven by a 56 year-old male who reported no injuries. A 54 year-old female occupant also reported no injuries. He advised that he was driving his wife to the train station, when crossing across the intersection with the green light, and V1 crossed the intersection with a red light. He advised that he attempted avoid the crash by braking without lockup and steering left.

The Critical Pre-crash event for V2 was "Other vehicle encroachment, from crossing street, across path". The Critical Reason for the Critical event was not coded to this driver. No associated factors were coded to the driver.
