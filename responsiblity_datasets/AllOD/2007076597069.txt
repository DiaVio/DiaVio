This single vehicle rollover occurred on a two-lane rural asphalt constructed roadway with a left hand curve on a downgrade of -6%, with a guard rail on the right side. This crash occurred during daylight with light traffic conditions and good visibility. This roadway runs east to west with a posted speed limit of 89 kmph (55 mph). Before the curve is posted an advisory speed limit sign suggesting a speed of 80 kmph (50 mph) for this curve.

Vehicle one (V1), a 2002 Lexus RX 300 SUV, was eastbound when it drifted off the right road edge. The driver steered left to avoid the end of a guard rail but V1 clipped the railing with its right front bumper. V1 rotated counter-clockwise and impacted the railing with the right rear wheel. This caused V1 to rotate clockwise and to impact the railing with its front bumper. V1 bounced up the railing then rolled over to the left rolling 6 quarters sliding down the roadway on its roof to final rest. This driver was transported to a local hospital where she was treated and released this same day. V1 was towed for disabling rollover damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 105 kmph for V1.

The driver, a sixty six (66) year old female, stated that as she was negotiating this curve that she looked over at her dog who was harnessed in the passenger front seat. As she looked up her vehicle was headed for the railing. She stated that she was in good health and had slept good the night before. She woke about 3.5 hours before the crash. and was on her way to her summer cabin. She stated that she does not drive this roadway on a regular basis but was comfortable with the road. She attributed this crash to her looking away from the roadway and down to her dog. The officer in charged estimated her speed at 105 kmph (65 mph).

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: this vehicle traveling, off the edge of the road on the right side. The critical reason for the critical event was coded as a driver related factor: internal distraction.
