The crash occurred while V1 was heading North on a 2 lane roadway, not physically divided, but has a single yellow dash line. Conditions were as follows: it was early in the morning, with clear skies, and on an almost completely dry roadway. The roadway was wet from the melting snow. Just prior to the crash location, a posted speed limit sign stated 72kph (45mph), and the roadway had a negative 8% grade.

V1 was 1997 Purple Dodge Neon that was traveling down a hill. V1 traveled off the roadway to the right side. The car went for 47.7m, after departing the roadway. Over this distance, V1 struck a concrete water drainage system, and then came to rest after striking a utility pole. Police Reported Travel Speed is Unknown for V1.

A 19-year old female drove V1, with 2 passengers. The front seat passenger was taken to a local hospital via an ambulance. The driver and back seat passenger were taken via their father to a local hospital for evaluation. The driver stated that the front passenger distracted her because he was playing with a balloon. She estimated her speed to be 50mph. The car was towed due to damage.

The Critical Precrash Event for V1 was this vehicle traveling off the edge of the road on the right side. The Critical Reason for the Critical Precrash Event was a recognition error, an interior distraction: the front seat passenger was playing with a balloon. Driver of V1 stated that she did not have any other problems or other occurrences that contributed to the crash. She has no vision or hearing problems.
