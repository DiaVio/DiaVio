This crash occurred just past noon involving three passenger cars at an intersection with stop signs. The speed limit for the roadway is 48 kmph (30 mph) for the east-west roadway. This roadway was an overpass over a highway. It was angled in relation to the north-south roadway.

The roadway from north to south is a 2-lane undivided concrete roadway. It is a one-way traffic roadway, serving as the service road for the highway. The speed limit on this roadway was 64 kmph (40 mph). There was no stop sign for this road at the intersection. The road surface is concrete dry and there were no detrimental atmospheric conditions at the time of the crash. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

V 1, a 1996 Mitsubishi Eclipse driven by a 51 year-old female was traveling south in the first lane of the service road, passing through the intersection of both roadways. V 2, a 2003 Chrysler Sebring,driven by a 49 year-old male was traveling west in the first lane. V 3, 2004 Dodge Neon, driven by a 44 year-old female was stopped, facing east in the first lane. The front of V 2 impacted the left side of V 1. V 1 rotated counter clockwise and moved down the southbound lanes. The rear section of the right side of V 1 impacted the front of V 3. V 3 rotated clockwise and came to rest within its lane.

V 1 continued to move southeast, crossing both southbound lanes of travel and departed the roadway. V 1 went down a steep embankment, approaching the southbound lanes of the nearby highway. V 1 rotated clockwise before coming to rest beside the southbound lanes of the highway.

V 2 rotated counter clockwise after the impact, and came to rest within the intersection, facing west.

V 1 and V 3 were towed from the scene. The driver of V 3 was transported to a medical facility treated and released. V 2 was able to driver away from the scene.

The pre-event movement of V 2 was coded going straight. The critical pre crash event was coded this vehicle traveling crossing over passing through intersection, reason for critical event driver related factor inadequate surveillance looked but did not see.
