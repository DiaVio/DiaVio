The crash occurred in the intersection of two roads. Both were two-lane, two-way roads. One went east and west and one went north and south. Both roads were bituminous with a level grade and a posted speed of 72 kmph (45 mph). It was evening on a weekday and the road was dry and the sky was clear. Traffic was flowing.

V1, a 2005 Ford Five Hundred four-door with one occupant was traveling northbound in lane one. V2, a 2001 Chevrolet Blazer with 4 occupants was traveling westbound in lane one. It was a four-way stop intersection. V2 stated that she stopped at the stop sign and looked both ways before proceeding. She stated that she saw V1 approaching but thought she was slowing down and thought that V1 was going to stop. V1 did not stop completely and the front of V2 contacted the right of V1. V1 then departed the roadway on the left side and came to final rest in a yard facing northwest. V2 came to final rest on the west side of the intersection, facing west, off the roadway on the right shoulder. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The driver of V1 was a 59-year old female who reported minor injuries but refused treatment. She advised that she had been on the road all day traveling from another state. She advised that she had just dropped off her mother at her house and was on her way up north to her home. She stated that she did not remember how long she was stopped at the intersection before proceeding. She stated that she was traveling between 2-16 kmph (1-10 mph) when she was struck by V2. She stated that she did not have time to react.

The Critical Precrash Event for the V1 was: “ther vehicle encroachment from crossing street across path”. The Critical Reason was not coded to this vehicle. An associated factor for the driver of V1 was that she had been on the road all day and she was fatigued.

The driver of V2 was a 39-year old female who reported injuries and was transported to a local trauma facility. She stated that she had just left her house and that she was traveling to her mother's house. She stated that she had stopped for more than ten seconds before proceeding. She stated that at the time of impact she was traveling between 2-16 kmph (1-10 mph). She stated that she did not have time to react.

The Critical Precrash Event for the V2 was: “this vehicle traveling: passing over crossing through intersection”. The Critical Reason for the Critical Precrash Event was a driver related error: “false assumption of other's actions” because she thought V1 was going to stop and yield the right-of-way.
