This case involves an impact to the front end of a passenger car with the front end of another passenger car. The case occupant is the unrestrained driver of the case vehicle. The case vehicle, a 2007 four-door Chevrolet Impala, was northbound in the inside lane of a level, wet, divided, concrete six-lane traffic-way. Vehicle two (V2), a 2000 four-door Chevrolet Impala was southbound in the northbound lane, heading in the wrong direction. It was daylight and foggy. The case vehicle and V2 collided head-on and came to rest in the inside northbound lane. The unrestrained 24-year-old female driver was the only occupant of the case vehicle. She was 32-weeks pregnant at the time of the crash. She had the benefit of a deployed frontal-impact air bag. She sustained serious injuries and was transported to a level-one trauma center where she was enrolled as a case occupant.
