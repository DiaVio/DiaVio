The single vehicle crash occurred on a seven-lane divided roadway. For eastbound traffic there are four travel lanes. Eastbound traffic has a curve to the left with a radius of 938 meters (3077 feet) and a negative 6.6% grade. The road was dry, traveled, asphalt. The posted speed limit is 56 kmph (35 mph). Traffic was light at the time of the collision. This crash occurred after sundown with clear skies. The roadway was lighted.

Vehicle one (V1), a 1995 Honda Accord with one occupant, was eastbound in the second travel lane. The driver of V1 stated another vehicle was going to pull out into his lane of travel from a crossing street. The driver steered the vehicle to the left and applied both the foot and hand brakes. V1 initiated a counterclockwise rotation, departed the lane on the left side, departed the roadway on the left side and struck a light pole located on the median. V1 continued rotating counterclockwise crossed back across the three eastbound lanes and came to rest facing southeast in the first travel lane. The vehicle was towed due to damage and the driver was not injured. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

The driver of V1, a seventeen-year-old male was heading home from a park at the time of the crash. This driver had been driving for about four minutes at the time. He stated that he saw a vehicle on his right side moving toward the roadway. The driver is not licensed to drive. This driver is familiar with the vehicle and drives the route daily.

The critical pre-crash event for vehicle one was coded as "this vehicle traveling: off the edge of the road on the left side". The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event was coded as "driver related factor: incorrect evasive action". He was an inexperienced driver, took inappropriate evasive maneuvers to the non-contact vehicle and lost directional control of the vehicle.
