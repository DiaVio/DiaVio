This crash occurred on a two lane undivided roadway with a curve to the left. The posted speed limit was 56KMPH (35MPH); however the speed limit sign was marred by green paint. The weather was blowing snow with white out conditions, and the roadway was snow-covered. This crash occurred on a weekday in the late afternoon.

Vehicle #1, a 1992 Ford F150 pick-up truck, was traveling east on the roadway when the driver suddenly noticed a stopped vehicle in his lane. The front of V1 impacted the rear of Vehicle #2, a 2004 Toyota RAV-4 sport utility vehicle. Both vehicles came to final rest in their original lane. The police did not consider alcohol or illegal drugs to be a factor in this crash and ordered no tests for either driver. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The Ford pick-up (V1) was driven by a belted 57-year old male who was not injured. The driver stated he only saw V2 when he was about 20 feet from it and although he applied his brakes, his vehicle slid into the rear of V2. He stated that due to white out conditions, visibility was very poor. This driver refused complete inspection of his vehicle.

The Critical Pre-crash Event coded to V1 was: Other Motor Vehicle in Lane, Other Vehicle Stopped. The Critical Reason for the Critical Pre-crash Event was coded as: Environmental Factor, Snow. Associated factors coded to the driver of V1 were his feeling sick with the flu and the slick roadway.

The Toyota RAV-4 (V2) was driven by a belted 51-year-old female who was treated at the scene. This driver could not be reached for an interview and did not answer mailed requests.

The Critical Pre-crash Event coded to V2 was: Other Motor Vehicle in Lane, Traveling in Same Direction with Higher Speed. The Critical Reason was not coded to V2. Associated factors coded to V2 were the slick roadway and the weather (blowing snow).
