The crash occurred in the southbound lanes of a two-way, ten-lane interstate roadway, which was physically divided by a concrete traffic barrier. There were five southbound lanes, with a posted speed limit of 105 kmph (65 mph). This was a straight, level, and bituminous roadway. Conditions were dark with streetlights on a weekday evening. The road was wet, and it was raining. Traffic flow was congested due to rush hour.

V1, a 1999 Dodge Neon with one occupant, was southbound in lane five (HOV lane). A phantom vehicle was traveling in front of V1, also in lane five. The phantom vehicle braked suddenly. V1 steered left, to avoid the other vehicle. The front left corner of V1 made contact with the concrete traffic barrier. V1 rolled right, two quarter-turns, and was redirected back onto the interstate. V1 came to final rest on its rooftop in the HOV lane. The vehicle was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 89 kmph for V1.

The driver of V1, a 19-year old male, was not injured. He stated that he was on his way to his vocational school to sign up for a program. The driver said that traffic was going slower than usual due to the rain and rush hour. He was traveling in the HOV lane, at 45 mph (72 kmph), when the vehicle in front of him hit the brakes. The driver of V1 applied his brakes and steered left, to avoid the other car. As the vehicle headed toward the wall, the driver tried to straighten out, but the vehicle continued to rotate counterclockwise. The driver indicated that the vehicle flipped onto its roof after impacting the wall.

The Critical Precrash Event for V1 was coded this vehicle traveling, off the edge of the road on the left side. The Critical Reason for the Critical Event was a driver-related decision error, following too close to respond to unexpected actions. There was less than five meters following distance between V1 and the other vehicle. Associated factors for V1 include the rain and wet roads.
