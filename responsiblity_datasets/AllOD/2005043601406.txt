This single vehicle crash was dispatched early in the morning on a dark, wet, two-lane undivided roadway. The roadway runs north and south with a westbound intersecting road at the location of the crash. The speed limit on the road is 72 kmph (45 mph).

Vehicle 1, a 2002 Chevrolet Silverado 4 x 4, was traveling south on the roadway and for an unknown reason departed the left side of roadway. V1 continued down an embankment and landed on its left side in a creek. The driver of V1 fled the scene before EMS, Fire, and Police arrived. It took several minutes for the Fire Department to find the exact crash site. The dispatcher tried to reach the initial caller of the crash to get better information on the crash location but the witness did not stick around to give information on what she saw. Police Reported Travel Speed is 89 kmph for V1.

The vehicle was moved to an impound lot for stolen vehicles. The owner's address listed on the crash report was not correct and the lender of the vehicle told the tow yard operator that the owner of the vehicle has not made payments on the car in a long time.

The Critical Pre-Crash Event was off the edge of the road on the left side. The Critical Reason for the Critical Pre-Crash Event was coded as unknown.
