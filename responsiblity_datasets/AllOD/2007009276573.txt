This crash occurred in the southbound lanes of a 4-lane highway divided by a median with a positive barrier. The roadway was bituminous and level with a slight right curve. The posted speed limit was 80 kmph (50 mph). It was the early morning on a weekday and the traffic flow was moderately heavy.

V1, a 1998 Dodge Durango utility vehicle, was driven by a 33-year old female traveling by herself. V1 was traveling in lane 1 of the highway at a rate of speed of approximately 60 mph when she had a seizure, lost control of her vehicle and departed the roadway. On the roadside, V1 struck a guardrail where it partially rode on top of it and then down the other side, onto the roadside. V1 then struck a sewer canal and a reflector sign and then came to rest under the bridge of the highway. The driver reported being unsure of how the events took place but remembered a lot of shaking and hard hits and hitting her head on top of the vehicle. She said there was a lot of smoke in the car once it finally stopped. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

The Critical Pre-crash Event for V1 was this vehicle traveling off the edge of the road on the right side. The Critical Reason for the Critical Event was a driver related critical non-performance error. The driver had a seizure, which impaired her ability to act. The driver has multiple pre-existing health conditions, including a history of seizures, panic attacks, hypertension, asthma, and a previous head injury and abdomen surgery. She takes several prescription medications. The driver said she was on her way to give back some personal belongings to someone. An inspection of the bags in the vehicle revealed mostly men's clothing. The driver may have been upset at a husband/boyfriend and was returning belongings to him. The driver only had 4 hours sleep the night before and reported being fatigued. The driver was also unfamiliar with the roadway, this being her first time driving on it.
