The crash occurred in the northbound lanes of a 4-lane highway that was divided by a grass median. There were 2 northbound and 2 southbound lanes. The northbound lanes were straight, level, and bituminous with a posted speed limit of 89 kmph (55 mph). It was the early afternoon on the weekend. Traffic flow was becoming heavy due to police presence.

V1, a 1999 Nissan Altima four door sedan, was driven by a 31-year old female who was traveling alone. V1 was following V2, a 1989 Toyota Supra convertible. Traffic flow had slowed down because of the presence of State Troopers who had pulled over a vehicle earlier for a traffic citation. V2 had slowed down with the rest of traffic. V1 had not slowed down and struck V2 in the rear. Both vehicles went forward and departed the roadway onto the median on the left. The driver of V1 reported that she was dialing a number into her cell phone. She said traffic was moving steadily. She said she had only looked away for 1 or 2 seconds and when she looked up, she saw that she was about to hit V2. She said she slammed on the brakes but it was too late. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The Critical Pre-crash Event for V1 was other vehicle in lane, traveling in same direction while decelerating. The Critical Reason for the Critical Event was internal distraction, a driver related error. An associated factor was the traffic flow interruption due to police presence.

V2, the 1989 Toyota Supra, was driven by a 17-year old male who was not injured. The driver was on his way to a nearby town and was following two friends who were traveling in a separate vehicle that was in front of him. He stated that he was traveling in lane 2 when traffic started to slow down. He said it was slowing down because the State Troopers were over on the right shoulder because they had pulled over a speeder. He said when he slowed down, he heard the screeching of tires from behind and then he felt the hit. He said his car then lunged forward and it went onto the median.

The Critical Pre-crash Event for V2 was other vehicle in lane, traveling in same direction with higher speed. The Critical Reason was not coded to this vehicle. Associated factors coded to the driver of V2 include unfamiliarity with both the vehicle and the roadway, and the traffic flow interruption due to police presence.
