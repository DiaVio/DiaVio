This two-vehicle crash occurred on a northbound three-lane expressway during nighttime conditions. The expressway lanes run straight and level. There is a shoulder and a median wall on its left side that separates the northbound and the southbound directions of travel. The speed limit on the expressway was 89 kmph (55 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.

Vehicle one (V1), a Ford Taurus of unknown year was northbound in the third lane. Vehicle two (V2), a 2003 Honda Accord was northbound in the second lane. V1 crossed over the right lane line and impacted with the left rear corner of V2. This caused V2 to rotate counterclockwise out of control and impact with the center median wall on the left side of the roadway with its front, then V2 came to a final rest facing west. V1 was a hit and run vehicle and did not stop after the first impact. V2 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Unknown for V1 and Unknown for V2.

The driver of V1 is unknown as a hit-and-run driver. There is no known pre-crash information for this driver.

The driver of V2, a twenty-five year old female, was heading home from a school trip. This driver had been driving for about two hours and thirty minutes at the time of the crash. This driver stated that before the crash she was looking in her side mirror and saw the other vehicle coming up on her side. This driver drives this vehicle daily and over this road only rarely.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: this vehicle traveling, over the lane line on right side of travel lane. The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event was coded to V1 as: type of driver error unknown.

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded: other vehicle encroachment; from adjacent lane [same direction] - over left lane line. The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event was not coded to this vehicle.
