This crash occurred in the northbound lanes of a four-lane undivided roadway. There were two northbound lanes and two southbound lanes. This roadway curved slightly to the right, had an uphill grade, bituminous, and had a posted speed limit of 56 kmph (35 mph). It was afternoon on a weekday and traffic flow was moderate.

V1 was a 1999 Pontiac Grand Prix was heading west. V2 was a 1997 Ford Taurus was heading north in lane two. V1 was entering the roadway from a business entrance on the east side of the roadway. The traffic traveling southbound was stopped for a red light. A vehicle to the left of V1 had allowed V1 to enter the roadway in front of it. As V1 entered the roadway V2, which was traveling southbound in lane two, struck V1 in the left front. Both vehicles came to final rest in lane two. Police Reported Travel Speed is Unknown for V1 and Unknown for V2.

V1, the Pontiac Grand Prix, was driven by a 24 year-old male. The driver stated that he was attempting to turn left into the southbound lanes. A van that was stopped at the light waved him through to make his turn. As he entered the second lane V2 hit him. He never saw V2 from behind the van.

The Critical Precrash Event for V1 was "this vehicle traveling, turning left at intersection." The Critical Reason for the Critical Precrash Event was "driver related factor, turned with obstructed view."

V2, the Ford Taurus, was driven by an 18 year-old female. She stated that she was driving north in lane two. V1 pulled out in front of her from behind another vehicle on the right side of the road. She was not able to make any avoidance actions.

The Critical Precrash Event for V2 was "other vehicle encroachment, from driveway, turning into opposite direction." The Critical Reason was not coded to this vehicle.
