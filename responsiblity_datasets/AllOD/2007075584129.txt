This two-vehicle crash occurred on an undivided, six-lane, straight roadway. The northbound lanes have an uphill grade and consist of two through lanes and a left turn lane. The speed limit on this roadway is 64 kmph (40 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions. Traffic was congested at the time of the crash.

Vehicle one (V1), a 2001 Hino flatbed truck, was northbound in lane two. Vehicle two (V2), a 1994 Pontiac Grand Prix, was northbound in lane two in front of V1. V2 stopped for traffic. The front of V1 struck the back of V2. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 48 kmph for V1 and 48 kmph for V2.

The driver of V1, a twenty-five-year-old male, was traveling from a job site to a warehouse. He was healthy, well rested and familiar with his vehicle. He rarely drives this route. This driver stated that he reached down to the floor to move the ratchet bar under his feet. When he looked back to the road, he saw that V2 had stopped and applied the brakes and steered left in an attempt to avoid a crash.

The driver of V2, a twenty-year-old female, was on her way to work from her home. She was well rested, in good health and comfortable with her vehicle. She travels this route daily. This driver stated that she looked in the rearview mirror and saw that V1 was not stopping but could not move forward due to stopped vehicles in front of her.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: other motor vehicle in lane; other vehicle stopped. The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as a driver related factor: internal distraction.

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded: other motor vehicle in lane; traveling in same direction with higher speed. The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V2 was not coded to this vehicle.
