This crash occurred in the afternoon of a weekday on the right roadside of a two-lane undivided rural roadway. The east/west roadway consisted of one lane in each direction with a posted speed limit of 64kmph (40 mph). The bituminous roadway was dry, level and featured a curve to the left. The traffic flow was normal and there were no adverse atmospheric conditions.

V1, a 2000 Dodge Town and Country minivan, was westbound negotiating the curve left. V1 departed the roadway to the right and struck a tree (74cm diameter) with its front left. The vehicle was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

V1 was driven by a belted 32-year-old female who stated she fell asleep behind the wheel. At the time of the crash the driver was 8.5 months pregnant but was injured. She stated that she had been shopping all day at various stores with her four children (passengers in the vehicle; an 8 year old, a 6 year old, a 5 year old, and a 3 year old who where belted in seats or in child/booster seats). In addition to shopping she also home schools all her children and normally only sleeps 5 to 6 hours per night. She does not remember anything after falling asleep until the deployment of the airbag waking her up.

The Critical Precrash Event for V1 was coded this vehicle traveling, off the edge of the road on the right side. The Critical Reason for the Critical Precrash Event was coded as driver related factor, sleeping, that is actually asleep. Associated factors include the driver being 8.5 months pregnant and being fatigued at the time of the crash.
