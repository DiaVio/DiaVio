This two-vehicle crash occurred early morning during daylight hours. The crash occurred at the four-leg intersection of a north-south roadway and an east-west roadway. The north and south edges of the east-west roadway are bordered by bike lanes. The bike lanes are bordered by parking lanes. Road surface dry,level asphalt. Both ends of the north-south roadway are controlled by stop signs. The north-south roadway is designated a 40 kmph (25 mph) zone. The east-west roadway is designated a 56 kmph (35 mph) zone.

Vehicle one (V1), a 2006 Honda Odyssey mini-van driven by a 30 year-old male, was traveling southbound in the lane one. Vehicle two (V2), a 1995 Mitsubishi Montero sport utility vehicle driven by a 58 year-old male, was traveling eastbound in the number one lane. As V1 entered the intersection the front contacted the left side of V2 causing V2 to rotate clockwise striking the south curb and a street sign before rolling onto it 's left side coming to final rest facing northwest on it's left side. V1 after impact came to final rest in the intersection facing southeast. Both vehicles were towed from the scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: this vehicle traveling, crossing over (passing through) intersection. The critical reason for the critical event was coded to V1 as a driver related factor: illegal maneuver. The driver of V1 failed to stop for traffic control devise.

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded: other vehicle encroachment; from crossing street, across path. The critical reason was not coded to this vehicle.
