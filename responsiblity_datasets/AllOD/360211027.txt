This moderate severity single vehicle frontal crash involved, a 60 year old female driver who was restrained with the lap/shoulder belt with a frontal steering column mounted airbag deployment. This driver sustained serious injuries and was transported to the trauma center. This crash occurred during daylight hours with clear, dry conditions in the southbound lanes of a six-lane freeway divided by a grassy median with a series of light poles located down the middle Vehicle one (V1), a 2007 Chevrolet Uplander van was in the southbound lane three of three lanes. V1 drove off the roadway to the left across the paved shoulder and into the grassy median. V1 then reacted and began to brake just before the front of V1 impacted one of the light poles in the median. V1 rotated slightly clockwise and the light pole broke away and landed across the hood and right A-pillar. V1 came to final rest facing southwest and was towed due to damage. The driver, a 60 year old female, is the case occupant and was wearing the manual lap/shoulder belt and the frontal steering column mounted airbag deployed. She was transported with serious injuries to the trauma center. No other occupants were in V1.
