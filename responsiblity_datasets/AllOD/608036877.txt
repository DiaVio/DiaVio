The case vehicle a 1999 Volkswagen Jetta was northbound on a dry, level two-lane asphalt roadway. Vehicle two (V2) was southbound on the same roadway. It was dark and there was no adverse weather. The case vehicle drifted across the center line into the path of V2. The driver of V2 steered to the left avoid a collision and crossed the center line into northbound lane. The driver of the case vehicle steered to the right to re-enter the northbound lane at the same time. The front of the case vehicle struck the front of V2 in an off-set to the right frontal mode. The impact caused the case vehicle to rotate clockwise approximately 140 degrees before it came to rest straddling the west shoulder and southbound lane. V2 rotated clockwise approximately 40 degrees and came to a rest straddling the east shoulder and northbound lane. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. The 49-year-old male driver was the only occupant of the case vehicle. He was not restrained by a three-point belt, but the steering-wheel air bag deployed. The driver was transported to an outside hospital and later transferred to a level-one trauma center with serious injuries and enrolled as a case occupant.
