This single vehicle daytime collision occurred mid-day, shortly after noon. The collision occurred on the southbound lanes of an interstate highway. The highway is dry, level, concrete, and consists of five lanes. The east edge of the highway is bordered by a temporary concrete divider. The concrete divider separates the southbound lanes from a construction site that is between the northbound and southbound lanes. The west edge of the highway is bordered by an asphalt shoulder. The highway is designated a 104 kmph (65 mph) zone.

Vehicle one (V1) is a 2001 Toyota Sequoia, sport utility vehicle, driven by a thirty six (36) year old female with six passengers. They were traveling southbound in the number four lane. As V1 traveled, the left rear tire's tread partially separated. V1 lost control and traveled left towards the center divider. The front plane of V1 contacted the center divider. The left plane of V1 then contacted the center divider as it bounced off the divider. V1 came to rest facing southbound in the number five lane. V1 was towed from the scene with moderate damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 97 kmph for V1.

The critical pre-crash event was coded: this vehicle traveling, off the edge of the road on the left side. The critical reason was coded as a vehicle related factor: tires/wheels failed. The left front tire had a sidewall blowout, then the left rear tire had a partial tread separation.
