This single-vehicle crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions. The roadway was dry, level, straight, undivided and consists of one lane in each direction. The speed limit on this roadway is 72 kmph (45 mph).

Vehicle one (V1), a 2003 Ford Explorer, was eastbound in lane one. V1 traveled off the right side of the roadway. The driver steered left and returned to the roadway. He then steered right and V1 departed the right side of the roadway rotating clockwise. V1 rolled over to the left four quarter-turns and the front struck a chain-link fence. V1 was towed due to the damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 80 kmph for V1.

The driver of V1, a twenty-year-old male, was traveling from a restaurant to his home. He was well rested, healthy and familiar with his vehicle. He drives this route daily. This driver stated that he was eating a sandwich while he was driving.

The pre-event movement for V1 was coded as "going straight." The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "this vehicle control loss due to traveling too fast for conditions." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "driver related factor; internal distraction." Associated factors were overcompensation and poor directional control.
