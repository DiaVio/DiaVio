The crash occurred in the southbound lanes of a two-way, eleven-lane interstate roadway that was divided by a concrete curbed median. There were six southbound lanes. This was a straight, level, asphalt roadway, with a posted speed of 105 kmph (65 mph). Conditions were daylight on a weekend afternoon with dry roads and clear skies. Traffic flow was normal.

V1, a 1989 Mercedes-Benz, was traveling in lane six (the HOV lane). V2, an unknown pickup truck, was traveling in lane five, slightly ahead of V1. A plastic or metal u-shaped object came loose and flew out of the bed of the pickup truck. The object crashed into the windshield of V1. V1 pulled off the road onto the shoulder under control. Due to the windshield damage, the vehicle was rendered inoperable by the police officer. There were no injuries. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

V1 was driven by a 21-year old female. She stated that she was coming from her house to a shopping mall with her mother. She said that she saw a large metal or plastic object come out of the bed of a gray pickup truck. She indicated that the object was u-shaped, and that it went up ten to twenty feet in the air before coming down and hitting the windshield of her vehicle.

The Critical Precrash Event for V1 was coded as “other, hit by flying object from V2”. The Critical Reason for the Critical Event was not coded to this vehicle. There were no associated factors for this driver.

The Critical Precrash Event for V2 was coded as “other. Losing cargo from pick-up bed”. The Critical Reason was coded as a “vehicle related factor, cargo shift”. There are no known associated factors for this vehicle. The vehicle was not inspected and the driver was not interviewed because V2 continued driving unaware of the piece of cargo that flew out of the pick-up bed.
