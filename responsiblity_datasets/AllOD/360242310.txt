This moderate severity near side impact crash involved a 64 year of female driver who was wearing a lap/shoulder belt and sustained serious injuries. This crash occurred in a T-type intersection during daylight hours when it was raining and the roadway surface was wet. Vehicle one (V1), a 2006 Scion Xb four door hatchback, was westbound on a two lane, two way street that intersects with a four lane, two way street. V1 was facing west and was stopped at a stop sign at this intersection preparing to make a left turn. Vehicle two (V2), a 2008 Toyota Prius four door hatchback, was traveling northbound through the intersection when V1 began to make the left turn. The front of V2 impacted the left side of V1. V1 was pushed in a northwesterly direction and came to final rest facing southwest in lane two. V1 continued forward and came to final rest facing mainly north in lane one. Both vehicles were disabled due to damage and were towed. The driver of V1, a 64 year old female, is the case occupant. She was restrained with the manual lap/shoulder belt. She sustained serious injuries and was transported to the trauma center. There were no other occupants in the vehicle.
