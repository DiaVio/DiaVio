This single vehicle crash occurred on a three lane undivided roadway. The westbound direction of the roadway consisted of a single through lane. This roadway was curved to the left with a radius of 94 meters (308 feet), level, bituminous with a posted speed limit of 56 kmph (35 mph). This crash occurred in the early morning daylight hours on a weekday. The road was dry.

Vehicle one (V1), a 1989 Volvo 740 with only the driver present, was headed westbound negotiating the left curve. V1 began to rotate counterclockwise. In an attempt to avoid the rotation, the driver of V1 overcorrected to the right and the vehicle started to rotate clockwise. This caused the vehicle to leave the roadway on the right side. In doing so, the left side wheels impacted the curb before the vehicle climbed a concrete embankment where the left side struck a concrete pillar. V1 came to rest facing north on the embankment and sidewalk. The vehicle was towed due to damage. The driver was not transported. Police Reported Travel Speed is 72 kmph for V1.

The driver of V1, a nineteen-year-old male, had dropped a friend off at school and was on his way to his school when the crash occurred. He stated getting seven and a half hours of sleep the night before, which is a little less than he likes to get. He also said that he was speeding because he was worried about being late for school. He reported being in good health aside from some strained muscles which he had taken Advil for. He has been driving for only three months. This driver overcompensated as the vehicle started to yaw.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: this vehicle traveling, off edge of the road on the right side. The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event was coded as a driver related factor: too fast for curve/turn. Associated factors coded to this driver include overcompensation, in a hurry and inexperience.
