This two-vehicle crash occurred on an undivided, two-lane roadway. This roadway has a slight left curve and a 2.5% downgrade. The speed limit on this roadway is 72 kmph (45 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.

Vehicle one (V1), a 2003 Chevrolet Express van, was eastbound in lane one. Vehicle two (V2), a 2005 Caterpillar backhoe, was eastbound in front of V1. The front of V1 struck the back of V2. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 97 kmph for V1 and 97 kmph for V2.

The driver of V1, a forty-two-year-old male, was traveling from his workplace to make a delivery in another town. He was well rested, healthy and familiar with his vehicle. He travels on this roadway monthly. This driver stated that he was drinking a soda while driving. He further stated that he was in a hurry to make this delivery. This driver also stated that he did not see V2 due to the sun glare until the last minute and applied his brakes.

The driver of V2, a -year-old male, was traveling from his workplace to a construction site. He was healthy, well rested and drives this vehicle often. This driver travels this route weekly.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: other motor vehicle in lane, traveling in same direction with lower steady speed. The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as a driver related factor: inadequate surveillance (e.g. failed to look, looked but did not see). An associated factor was the glare from the sun.

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded: other motor vehicle in lane, traveling in same direction with higher speed. The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event was not coded to this vehicle.
