This two-vehicle daytime collision occurred on a four-lane two-way road with a parking lane along the south/east curb. The roads are paved with asphalt. The roadway for both vehicles is straight and level. The speed limit for both vehicles is 48 kmph (30 mph). At the time of the crash the sky was cloudy with no adverse conditions.

Vehicle one (V1), a 2003 Saturn Ion sedan driven by a fifty eight (58) year old female, was parked in the parking lane facing east. Vehicle two (V2), a 1999 Chevrolet Silverado pick-up truck driven by a seventy two (72) year old male with one passenger, was traveling east in lane one. V1 initiated a u-turn across the eastbound lanes to go westbound. The front of V2 contacted the left side V1. Both vehicles came to rest at approximately the point of impact. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The driver of V2 was unable to be interviewed due to no contact after several attempts.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: this vehicle traveling, over the lane line on left side of travel lane. The critical reason for the critical event was assigned to V2 and coded as a driver related factor: inadequate surveillance (e.g., failed to look, looked but did not see). An associated factor for V1 is for this driver initiating an illegal U-turn, and also being unfamiliar with area.

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded: other vehicle encroachment, from parking lane. The critical reason for the critical event is not coded to this vehicle.
