This nearside crash case involves a 78 year old restrained female seated in the front right seating position who sustained serious injuries. The crash occurred during the early evening hours (dusk) in dry and clear weather. The collision occurred within the confines of a 4-legged intersection. There are five, bituminous level northbound lanes (four straight and one left turn only lane) separated by a raised curb on the south side of the intersection and five, bituminous level southbound lanes (four straight lanes and one left turn only lane) separated by a concrete positive barrier on the north side of the intersection. This roadway is intersected by three bituminous east/west travel lanes. The case vehicle (V1), a 2005 four-door Toyota Camry, was being driven northbound (left turn lane) and vehicle two (V2), a 2008 four-door Nissan Maxima, was being driven southbound in the far right straight lane.. The crash occurred as V1 entered the intersection attempting to execute a left turn to travel west and was impacted on the right side by the front bumper of V2. After initial impact, V1 and V2 rotated and side slapped each other (V1's right with V2's left) before traveling to final rest in the southwest quadrant facing southwest. V1 and V2 were towed from the scene due to damage sustained in the crash. The 78 year old female case occupant was seated in the front right seating position was restrained by the available 3-point manual safety belt (retractor type pretensioner actuated). The available passenger front IP air bag did not deploy as a result of the impact. This case occupant's seat was positioned between middle and rear most and the seat back was in the slightly reclined position. The case occupant was seriously Injured and transported to a level 1 trauma center by ground ambulance for treatment of her injuries. V1 was driven by a 78 year old male non-case occupant who was restrained by the available 3-point manual belt (retractor type pretensioner actuated). The available steering wheel mounted air bag did deploy as a result of the impact. This occupant's seat was positioned between forward and middle and the seat back was in the slightly reclined position. The non-case occupant was not injured in the crash.
