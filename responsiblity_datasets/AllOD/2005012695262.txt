This crash occurred in the merging area of an entrance ramp onto an eastbound interstate. The speed is posted at 113 kmph (70 mph) with a flashing advisory of 64 kmph (40 mph). This is a curved ramp with a radius of curvature of 163.8 and super elevation of -6/122 to inside of curve. The off road super elevation from road edge to bottom of the ditch is -17/122. It was a rainy weeknight. It was dark with no lights.

V1, a 1999 Ford F-250 Super Cab, was merging onto the interstate from the ramp. As the driver applied the brakes to slow down the vehicle rotated clockwise and slid off the road tripping on the soft shoulder rolling 4 quarter turns to land on it's wheels at final rest facing in a southwesterly direction. This vehicle was found some 24 meters off the road at the interstate fence line. There was no driver or passenger with the vehicle upon arrival at the scene as they had already been taken to a local ER by a passer by. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

V1 was driven by a 28 year-old male in good health but does suffer from asthma (the medication for which is not thought to have contributed to the crash). This driver stated that as he merged onto the interstate he applied the brakes and than stated it felt like the right rear tire locked and that is what stated the clockwise rotation and loss of control. The driver suffered minor injuries while the occupant in right front seat suffered non-incapacitating injuries. V1 was towed due to damage.

The Critical Precrash Event was coded: this vehicle control loss due to: traveling too fast for conditions. The Critical Reason for the Critical Event was coded as decision error: too fast for conditions- it was raining and a down hill right hand curve. The associated factors were the speed of the vehicle, the wet pavement and the vehicle had a 6 ft lift to the under carriage. This vehicle also had over sized tires and rims. The driver also admitted his own inattention as he was thinking about other things, as well as conversing with a passenger.
