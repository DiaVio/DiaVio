This crash occurred on a two-lane undivided roadway. There was one southbound lane and one northbound lane. This roadway curved to the left, had a downhill grade, was bituminous, and had a posted speed limit of 56 kmph (35 mph). It was mid-evening on a weekday, it was snowing, and the roads were icy.

V1 was a 2002 Volkswagen Jetta, driven by an 18 year-old female. There was a 17 year-old male passenger in position 13. V1 was traveling southbound when it drifted across the roadway to the left and departed the roadway. When the vehicle departed the roadway it struck and embankment and climbed up it. It continued along the embankment until it slid back down onto the left side of the roadway where it came to final rest. Police Reported Travel Speed is Unknown for V1.

The Critical Precrash Event for V1 was coded this vehicle control loss due to traveling too fast for conditions. The Critical Reason for the Critical Precrash Event was a driver related factor, too fast for conditions, icy roads. One factor to this crash was that the driver was relatively inexperienced. Another factor was that the road conditions had changed quickly, becoming icy with snow being blown onto them.
