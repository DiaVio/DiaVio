This crash occurred on a straight stretch of a four-lane, positively divided expressway. The roadway is concrete with asphalt shoulders. It was dark at the time of the crash, there were no adverse weather conditions, and the roadway was free of defects. In the area of the crash, the speed limit is 105 kmph (65 mph).

Vehicle one (V1), a 1993 Buick Regal four-door sedan, was traveling eastbound in the first lane of the four-lane expressway. The left front tire of V1 blew out. The driver attempted to steer the vehicle and lost traction. V1 rotated counter-clockwise as it crossed all lanes to its left. V1 departed the road over the left shoulder, and the front of V1 impacted the concrete barrier. V1 rotated counter-clockwise and struck the barrier again with its back plane, bringing the vehicle to rest in contact with the barrier. Police Reported Travel Speed is No PAR obtained (created) for V1.

The 42-year-old female driver of V1 reported that she was rested at the time of the collision, and that she has untreated high blood pressure. She has been driving for twenty-six years. At the time of the crash, she was singing along with the radio when the left-front tire blew out. She attempted to correct the vehicle's path by steering right, then left. The critical precrash event for V1 was: this vehicle loss of control due to- blowout. The critical reason for the event was attributed to V1 and was coded as a driver-related performance error: overcompensation.
