This crash occurred in the eastbound lanes of a four-lane undivided roadway. There were two eastbound lanes and two westbound lanes. This roadway curved slightly to the leftt, had an uphill grade, was bituminous, and had a posted speed limit of 56 kmph (35 mph). It was evening on a weekday and traffic flow was moderate.

V1 was a 1994 Chevrolet Cavalier convertible, driven by a 31 year-old male. V2 was a 2002 Volkswagen Beetle, driven by a 64 year-old female. V1 was traveling eastbound in lane two and was beginning to make a left hand turn into a gas station, when it impacted V1, which was traveling westbound in lane two. The driver of V1 attempted to steer the vehicle back to the right but was unable to avoid V2. The driver of V2 attempted to brake but did not have sufficient room to come to a stop before impacting V1. Police Reported Travel Speed is 40 kmph for V1 and 40 kmph for V2.

The Critical Precrash Event for V1 was "this vehicle traveling, over the left lane line on left side of travel lane." The Critical Reason for the Critical Precrash Event was "driver related factor, inadequate surveillance." The driver of V1 attempted to cross the travel lanes and realized that he was not going to be able to make it and attempted to steer back to the right. It should be noted that the driver of V1 was arrested for possession of an illegal substance at the crash scene. This is not reflected in the police report.

The Critical Precrash Event for V2 was "other vehicle encroachment, from opposite direction - over left lane line. The Critical Reason was not coded to this vehicle. There were no other factors coded to this vehicle.
