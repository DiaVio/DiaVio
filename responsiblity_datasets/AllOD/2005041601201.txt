This crash occurred in the northbound lanes of a divided high-speed, high-volume interstate. The straight, level, bituminous roadway featured six lanes and two emergency paved shoulders with a posted speed of 105 kmph (65 mph). Conditions were daytime and mid afternoon on a weekday.

V1, a 1997 Toyota Land cruiser was traveling northbound in lane three at approximately 89 kmph (55 mph). A non-contact vehicle cut across two lanes of traffic into lane three. The driver of V1 then jerked the wheel to the left causing him to lose control of the vehicle and roll over coming to final rest in lane six, (the HOV lane) facing south. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

V1 was driven by a 19-year-old male, in good health. He stated a phantom vehicle cut him off despite his beeping horn, causing him to lose control and rollover. The driver and two passengers were transported from the scene with moderate injuries and the vehicle was towed due to damage.

The Critical Precrash Event for V1 was coded as vehicle control loss due to traveling too fast. The Critical Reason was coded as overcompensation. The driver jerked the wheel, which caused the vehicle to lose control and rollover. The associated factors related to this driver are also age and the frequency of times he has traveled this roadway as he stated in the interview that he only travels the roadway monthly.
