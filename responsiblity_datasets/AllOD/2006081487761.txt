A single-vehicle collision occurred in the early afternoon hours on a Thursday. The collision occurred on a rural two-way, two-lane undivided roadway, in lane one. The pre-crash roadway is level, and straight with bituminous paving. The weather was clear and the roadway was dry. The roadway has a posted speed limit of 89 kmph (55 mph).

Vehicle 1 (V1), a 2000 Chevrolet S-10 extended cab pickup, was traveling southbound in lane one. V1 departed the roadway via the right shoulder, went down a non-transversable slope and the front of V1 impacted a large tree, the vehicle rotated clockwise, and came to rest against the tree. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

The driver of V1 was a 72-year-old male who was transported due to internal injuries. Per medical records he has a history of diabetes, hypertension and hyperlipidemia. He takes the following medications: dobutamine, sulcrafate, colace, selenium, vitamins E & C, insulin, cefazolin, and Albuterol. During an interview with the daughter of the driver, she indicated the driver had a diabetic reaction and fell asleep or passed out. The daughter could not answer questions about recent work or sleep habits. She did indicate that for this trip he was on his way home from work. The police report narrative states the driver was having a diabetic episode and drifted off the right side of the road.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as: this vehicle traveling, off the edge of the road on the right side. The critical reason was a driver critical non-performance factor: heart attack or other impairment of the ability to act.
