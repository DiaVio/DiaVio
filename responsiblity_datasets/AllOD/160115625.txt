Vehicle 1 (V1) is a 2000 Volvo S70, station wagon (case study vehicle). V1 was occupied by a female, age 41as its driver and a male age 6, as a left rear passenger (case study participant). V1's occupants were wearing the available lap and shoulder belts and the airbags did not deploy.

The crash occurred during the hours of daylight. The weather at the time of the crash was clear and dry. The roadway surface was dry. The posted speed limit is 25 mph (40 kmph).

The crash occurred on a two lane roadway. The roadway bed is made of hard packed gravel. The roadway travels primarily north and south. The travel lanes are bordered by a ditch and a wooded area. The southbound direction has a negative grade.

V1 was traveling southbound when the driver lost control for unknown reasons. V1 started to rotate in a counterclockwise direction. V1 ran off the left side of the roadway profiling the right side of the vehicle. The vehicle started to roll onto its right side. But, prior to completing the rollover V1 struck a tree.

The case study participant was entrapped because the vehicle came to rest on the right side. A passerby righted the V1 onto its wheels and then the case study particitpant was transported by air to a trauma center.
