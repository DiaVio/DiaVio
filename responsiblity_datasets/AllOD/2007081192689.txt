This single vehicle crash occurred during the early afternoon during daylight hours .The location was an urban area in a freeway interchange and was on-ramp related. The on-ramp was bituminous, and had a single lane with a standard right curve and a negative grade. The weather was cloudy and the road was wet. There was no advisory speed limit posted on the on-ramp or curve warning signs. The freeway has a posted speed limit of 97 kmph (60 mph).

Vehicle one (V1), a 2003 Ford Ranger pickup, driven by an unknown aged male was traveling northbound on the on-ramp negotiating a right curve. The driver was driving too fast for the wet road conditions and lost control of his vehicle. The vehicle rotated in a clockwise yaw off the right road edge and struck a ditch with the front bumper. V1 continued to rotate clockwise and struck the ditch a second time with the left rear wheel. V1 came to rest facing southwest off the roadway. V1 appeared to have a damaged rear axle and was towed from the scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is No PAR obtained (created) for V1.

The driver of V1, refused to be interviewed. He was out of the vehicle walking around and did not appear to be injured. The tires on V1 were slightly over-inflated and had good tread depth.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: this vehicle traveling, off the edge of the road on the right side. The critical reason for the critical event was coded as a driver related factor: too fast for conditions (specified as: wet roadway and curve).
