The crash occurred off the east side of an undivided two-lane north/south roadway. This was a level, bituminous, left curved roadway with a posted speed limit of 80 kmph (50 mph). It was the early afternoon on a weekday and the road was dry, the sky was clear, and traffic flow was light.

V1, a 2003 GMC Savanna large van, was heading southbound when the driver fell asleep at the wheel. V1 departed the roadway on the right side of road and then struck a telephone pole. The telephone pole fell on top of the vehicle and slid off onto the roadway. V1 then proceeded into a parking lot of a gas station and sideswiped V2, a 2002 Chevrolet Blazer utility vehicle that was parked near the entrance. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

V1 was driven by a 50-year old male who reported no injuries except for some minor burns he said he received from the airbag. He stated that he fell asleep at the wheel. He stated that he had just come from a friend's house whom he was giving estimates for a floor repair job. He also stated that he stopped at a shop and got a sandwich and a drink. Shortly after that he said he was on his way home when he "simply fell asleep at the wheel." He said the sound of the impact and the airbag awoke him and that was when he gained control of his vehicle.

The Critical Pre-crash Event for V1 was this vehicle traveling off the edge of the road on the right side. The Critical Reason for the Critical Pre-crash Event was a driver related error, asleep, that is, actually asleep. The driver claimed he took no medications and suffered from no medical conditions. No additional associated factors were coded to the driver of V1.
