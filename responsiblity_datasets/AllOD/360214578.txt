This moderate severity single-vehicle near-side impact crash involved a 17 year-old male front right passenger who was restrained with the lap/shoulder belt with side impact seatback-mounted and roof curtain airbag deployments. This occupant sustained serious injuries and was transported to the trauma center. This crash occurred at night with street lights and clear, dry conditions on a two lane, two way east/west street. Vehicle one (V1), a 2007 Volvo S40 four door sedan, was in the eastbound lane. V1 crossed over into the westbound lane rotating counterclockwise and continued though an intersection with another two lane, two way street. V1 then departed the roadway on the northeast corner of this intersection sliding almost laterally and both rear tires impacted the curb edge. V1 continued across a sidewalk before the right side of V1 then impacted a fire hydrant and a small sign post. V1 then continued down the sidewalk with the front end over a lawn, and the front of V1 impacted a landscape rockery with minor damage. V1 continued to rotate counterclockwise down the sidewalk and came to final rest facing northwest across this sidewalk. V1 was towed due to damage. The front right passenger, a 17 year-old male, is the case occupant and was wearing the manual lap/shoulder belt with side impact seatback mounted and roof curtain airbag deployments. He was transported with serious injuries to a trauma center. The 16 year-old male driver was wearing the lap/shoulder belt and was reported to have no injuries A 17 year-old male passenger, who was not belted in the second row right position, also sustained no reported injuries.
