Vehicle one (V1), a 2003 Honda Element SUV, was traveling north in lane three of a four lane, one way roadway, part of a roadway six lane, divided trafficway, under dry, daylight conditions and was approaching an intersection. Vehicle two (V2), a 1995 International tractor-trailer, was stopped at a red light facing north in lane three of the same roadway. For unknown reasons, the front of V1 contacted the rear of V2. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. The driver and sole occupant of V1 (case study occupant) was a 25-year-old male. He was wearing a three-point lap and shoulder belt and had both frontal airbags deploy as well as both seat-mounted side airbags deploy. The buckle mounted pretensioner also deployed. The driver of V1 was transported to a level-one trauma center for treatment.
