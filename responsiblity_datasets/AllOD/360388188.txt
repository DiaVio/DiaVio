This case occupant is an 18 year old male driver, wearing the available manual lap/shoulder belt. He was involved in two frontal impacts with a frontal air bag deployment and he sustained serious injuries. This crash occurred during evening hours in a parking garage with overhead lights. Vehicle one (V1), a 2011 Volkswagen GTI four door hatchback, was traveling down the private driveway leading to the garage. The driveway consists of two lanes, one for vehicles entering and one for vehicles exiting the garage. The lane V1 was traveling on is comprised of concrete, has a downhill grade and curves to the left, leading to a metal gate that opens for vehicles with access. After the driver of V1 negotiated the curve, V1 continued forward and its front impacted the gate, knocking it approximately 2.4 meters (8 feet) from its original location. V1 traveled approximately 56 meters (184 feet), and its front impacted the garage wall, resulting in the actuation of the driver's seat belt retractor pretensioner and the deployment of his frontal air bag. V1 rebounded slightly from the impact and came to final rest near the wall, facing north. V1 was towed due to damage. The case occupant is the driver of V1, an 18 year old male, who was wearing the available manual lap/shoulder belt. His seat belt retractor pretensioner actuated and his steering column mounted frontal impact air bag deployed during the crash. This driver sustained serious injuries and was transported to the trauma center. There were no other occupants in V1.
