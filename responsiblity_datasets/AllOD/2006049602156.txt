This single vehicle crash occurred on a divided, straight roadway in an interchange area. The southbound lanes have a slight upgrade and consist of three through lanes and a right exit lane. The speed limit on this roadway is 97 kmph (60 mph). It was dark with street lights on at the time of the crash and there were no adverse atmospheric conditions.

A non-contact vehicle, an unknown pickup, was southbound in lane four. Vehicle one (V1), a 2006 Chevrolet Malibu, was southbound in lane four behind the pickup. A dresser drawer fell out of the back of the pickup trucks bed. This object was struck by the front of V1. V1 then traveled off the left side of the roadway and the front and then the right side struck the steel cable guardrail. V1 was towed due to damage and the pickup truck continued to drive from the scene. No information is available for the pickup truck or its driver. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

The driver of V1, a twenty-four-year-old male, was traveling from work to his home. He stated that he was in good health. He stated that he was unable to avoid the object that fell from the pickup. This driver refused to complete the interview; no further information is available.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as: object or animal; object in roadway. The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as a driver performance factor: Other performance error (specified as: Too fast to avoid object that fell from forward non-contact in-transport vehicle.).
