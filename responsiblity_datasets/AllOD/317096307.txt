This case involved a 26 year old restrained female driver of a 2005 Dodge Neon four door sedan (V1). The driver was restrained by the available three point lap and shoulder belts and had a deployed frontal airbag. There were three other occupants in the vehicle, a 9 year old male seated in the second row left position restrained by the manual lap and shoulder belt, a 1 year old female restrained in a child safety seat in the center of the 2nd row and a 9 year old female in the second row right restrained by the manual lap and shoulder belt. There were no available airbags for the second row. The crash occurred at night on an unlit two-lane roadway that curved sharply to the left. The asphalt roadway was dry with a negative 5% grade. V1 was traveling south at the posted speed limit of 72 kph (45 mph) as it came into the curve. The driver of V1 braked and began skidding in a longitudinal motion. V1 skidded off the roadway and into a ditch, striking the far side of the ditch with its frontal plane. V1 came to rest in the ditch and was towed from the scene due to disabling damage. The driver of V1 was transported to a local hospital and subsquently transferred to a level one trauma center for treatment. The 2nd row left and right passengers were initially transported to a local hospital and later transferred to a level one children's trauma center. The center occupant of the second row was treated and released at a hospital.
