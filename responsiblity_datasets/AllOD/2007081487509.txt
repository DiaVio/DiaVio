This single-vehicle crash occurred mid-morning, during daylight hours. The roadway was a dry bituminous two-way, four-lane roadway with two lanes in each direction. The roadway was curved to the left with a 7% uphill grade. The weather was cloudy, and visibility was clear. The posted speed limit was 56 kmph (35 mph).

Vehicle one (V1) was a 1992 Toyota Camry sedan driven by a forty four (44) year old male. V1 was traveling eastbound up a hill when it departed the roadway via the right shoulder. V1 then re-entered the roadway, crossed all four lanes to the left, while rotating counter clockwise and departed the left side of the roadway. The right side wheels then impacted the left curb and V1 rolled over to the right. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: this vehicle traveling, off the edge of the road on the right side. The critical reason for the critical event was coded as a driver related factor: overcompensation. An associative factor was an internal distraction, the driver was listening to the radio and was changing the radio station. The driver said he was a bit drowsy that morning, so fatigue was also considered a factor. He has a nerve problem in his leg and stated that he takes the medications Fentanyl and Xanax.
