The crash occurred in the intersection of two roadways. The north/south roadway was undivided with one lane in each direction. There was no traffic control device on this roadway. The westbound roadway was one-way with one lane of travel and a stop sign. It was late afternoon on a weekend, and the road was dry and skies were clear with no adverse conditions. The traffic flow was normal.

V1, a 2002 Hyundai Accent four door sedan with two occupants, was traveling southbound in lane one. V2, a 2005 Ford Crown Victoria four door taxi with one occupant, was traveling westbound in lane one. At the intersection, the front of V1 impacted the right front side of V2. V1 came to a final rest at the southwest corner. V2 came to a final rest near the southwest corner. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

V1, the Hyundai Accent, was driven by a 26 year-old female who reported minor injuries and was transported to a medical facility. A 7-month-old male occupant also had minor injuries and was transported to a medical facility. The driver stated to the police that she was going straight when V2 proceeded from a stop sign and causing the crash.

The Critical Pre-crash Event for V1 was "other vehicle encroachment, from crossing street across path." The Critical Reason for the Critical Event was not coded to this driver. She had the right-of-way, and possible associated factors were coded unknown because no interview was obtained.

V2, the Ford Crown Victoria, was driven by a 60 year-old male who reported no injuries. The driver did not make any statements to the police.

The Critical Pre-crash Event for V2 was "this vehicle traveling, crossing over (passing through) intersection." The Critical Reason for the Critical Event was coded as an unknown driver error. He did not have the right-of-way, and possible associated factors were coded unknown because no interview was obtained.
