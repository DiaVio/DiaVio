It was dark with no adverse atmospheric conditions at the time of this single-vehicle crash. The trafficway is straight, level, divided by a grass median and consists of two lanes in each direction. There is a ditch with a steep embankment on the south side of the eastbound lanes. The speed limit for this roadway is 105 kmph (65 mph).

Vehicle one (V1), a 1998 Oldsmobile Silhouette, was eastbound in lane one. V1 traveled into lane two, back into lane one and off the right side of the roadway. The front of V1 struck a small tree. V1 continued eastbound on the embankment and the right side struck two trees. V1 then rolled over to the left two quarter-turns into the ditch. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is 89 kmph for V1.

The driver of V1, a thirty-eight-year-old female, was moving with her three-year-old daughter from a nearby state to another state. This driver stated that this was the first time she drove on this road. She also stated that she turned around to look at her child and steered right abruptly when she realized that she had left her lane. This driver provided no further information.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "this vehicle traveling; over the lane line on left side of travel lane." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "driver related factor; internal distraction." This driver's overcompensation and poor directional control were associated factors in this crash.
