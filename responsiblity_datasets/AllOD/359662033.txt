The case occupant is a 73 year old female driver who was involved in a head on collision with another vehicle. This driver was wearing the available manual lap/shoulder belt. Her frontal and knee air bags deployed and she sustained serious injuries. This crash occurred during daylight hours under clear weather and dry roadway conditions. In the precrash area, the roadway is comprised of one north and one southbound asphalt travel lane, with narrow paved strips present on both sides. For northbound vehicles, the roadway has an uphill grade and there is a shallow ditch present adjacent to the lane. For southbound vehicles there is a downhill grade and just west of the roadway is a steep drop-off with large rocks present. Case vehicle one (V1), a 2011 Toyota Highlander compact utility vehicle, was traveling south in the southbound lane. Vehicle two (V2), a 2009 Honda Civic hybrid four door sedan, was traveling north in the northbound lane. As V2 continued north, it traveled across the center lane lines and into V1's lane of travel. V1's front struck the front of V2, resulting in the deployment of V1's driver side frontal and knee air bags. V1 rotated counterclockwise while traveling towards the shoulder. V1 traveled down the steep drop-off and the back of V1 impacted a large rock, resulting in minor damage. V1 came to final rest completely off the roadway, facing east. After the initial impact, V2 rotated counterclockwise as it traveled towards the east shoulder. V2 came to final rest partially off the roadway, facing southwest. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. The case occupant in V1 is the 73 year old female driver who was wearing the available manual lap/shoulder belt. The driver's retractor pretensioner actuated and her frontal steering column mounted air bag and lower left instrument panel mounted knee air bag deployed during the first crash event. She sustained serious injuries and was transported to the trauma center. There was an 84 year old male occupant seated in the front right seating position who was restrained with a manual lap shoulder belt . His instrument panel mounted frontal air bag deployed during the crash. He sustained injuries and received some type of medical treatment, but his treatment status is not known. The driver and sole occupant of V2 was injured in the crash but his medical treatment status it is also unknown.
