This moderate severity frontal crash case involved a 46 year old female in the front right position who was wearing the available manual lap/shoulder belt and a frontal air bag deployed and she sustained serious injuries. This single vehicle crash occurred in a dark area with clear, dry conditions. Case vehicle one (V1), a 2007 Toyota Tundra four door pickup truck, was eastbound on a two lane, two way road that came to a T intersection with another two lane road. V1 attempted to turn right at the intersection and then departed the east side of the intersecting roadway. V1 crossed over a shallow ditch before the left side swiped a large tree stump and the front impacted a tree. V1 rotated slightly clockwise after impact with the tree and came to final rest facing southeast. V1 was towed due to damage and during this removal damage occurred on the left back side and bumper. The case occupant is a 46 year old female who was seated in the front right position wearing the lap/shoulder seatbelt and the retractor pretensioners actuated on impact with the tree. The frontal instrument panel mounted airbag also deployed. She was transported to a local hospital before being transferred to the the trauma center. The driver of V1 was a 50 year old male who was wearing the lap/shoulder seatbelt and the retractor pretensioners actuated and the frontal steering column mounted airbag deployed. He was transported to a hospital and treated and released with minor injuries. An 18 year old female was seated in the second row right position and wearing the manual lap/shoulder seatbelt. She was also treated and released at a local hospital with minor injuries. No other occupants were in V1.
