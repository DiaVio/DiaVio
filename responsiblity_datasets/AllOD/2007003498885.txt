The crash occurred on the eastbound lanes of a one-way roadway with three lanes of travel. The roadway was bituminous, dry, curved right and was downhill. It was daylight and clear with no adverse conditions. This was the morning hours of a weekday holiday.

V1, a 1997 BMW 325i four door with three occupants, was traveling eastbound in lane two. While V1 was negotiating the right turn, he lost control and rotated counter-clockwise and the front impacted the guardrail north of the roadway. Then V1 continued rotating counter-clockwise and the right side of V1 impacted the guardrail. V1 came to a final rest on lane three. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

V1, the BMW, was driven by a 19 year-old male who reported no injuries. The 27 year-old male occupant and a 23 year-old male occupant also reported no injuries. He advised the Police that a non-contact vehicle cut him off causing him to loose control and impact the guardrail.

The Critical Pre-crash Event for V1 was "This vehicle traveling off the edge of the road on the left side". The Critical Reason for the Critical event was coded as “incorrect evasive action”. An associated factor coded to the driver was a Decision factor; he was traveling too fast for conditions, and did not realize that caution was required. The other possible associated factors were coded unknown because no interview was obtained.
