This single vehicle crash occurred on a one-way westbound connector on-ramp. The transition connects an eastbound state highway and a westbound state highway. The westbound transition is wet, curves right with a radius of 81 meters, concrete, has an upgrade slope, and consists of two lanes. The transition has a warning sign, a speed advisory sign, and chevrons indicating the approaching curvature. The highway has a speed of 105 kmph and an advisory speed of 40 kmph (25 mph). It was cloudy and raining at the time of the collision. Overhanging lights illuminate the highway.

Vehicle one (V1), a 2001 Toyota Corolla was westbound in the number one lane. As the vehicle was traveling through the curve, it changed lanes to the left. The vehicle lost control and traveled towards the concrete divider located on the left edge of the roadway. The front plane of V1 contacted the concrete divider. V1 then proceeded to travel across both lanes and the back plane impacted the barrier on the other side of the road. V1 came to rest facing north blocking the number one lane. V1 received moderate damage and was towed from the scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is 56 kmph for V1.

The driver of V1, a forty-two year old female, was traveling from a friend's home to her residence. The driver was listening to music as she was driving. She takes vitamins for her health. She wears glasses, but only while reading. The driver works eight hours per day Monday through Friday. She attends night school on Monday nights. The driver had her windshield wipers on in the "slow" position. This driver drives this vehicle daily and over this road on a weekly basis.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: this vehicle traveling, over the lane line on left side of travel lane. The critical reason for V1 was coded as an environmental related factor: slick roads.
