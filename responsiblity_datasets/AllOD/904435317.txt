This frontal impact crash case involves a 49 year old female driver, wearing the available manual lap/shoulder belt, with a deployed frontal air bag who sustained serious injuries. This crash occurred during daylight hours on a seven lane, two way street. The north-south roadway consists of three northbound, three southbound and one center left turn only lane that are straight, level and comprised of asphalt. There are curbs and business driveways on either side of the roadway. At the time of the crash, there were clear weather conditions and dry travel lanes. The case vehicle one (V1), a 2010 Hyundai Elantra four door sedan, was traveling south in lane one. Vehicle 2 (V2), a 2000 Ford F250 extended cab large pickup truck, was stopped facing north in the center left turn lane, waiting to make a left turn into a business driveway located on the west side of the street. Most of the southbound traffic had slowed to a stop and V2 began to turn left through a gap that was left between vehicles. V2 entered V1's lane of travel and the front of V1 impacted the right side of V2, resulting in the actuation of V1's driver's seat belt retractor pretensioner and the deployment of the frontal driver air bag. After the impact, V1 traveled a short distance south and came to final rest facing southwest in lane one. V2 rotated counterclockwise and departed the roadway to the right. It traveled across a sidewalk and the front of V2 scraped a utility pole located on the southwest corner of the driveway. V2 came to final rest near the pole, facing southwest. V1 was towed and V2 was able to be driven from the scene. The case occupant is the 49 year old female driver of V1 who was wearing the available manual lap/shoulder belt. This case occupant's steering column mounted frontal air bag deployed. She sustained serious injuries and was transported to an outside hospital, prior to being transferred to the trauma center. There were no other occupants in the vehicle.
