This one vehicle crash occurred on a one-way two-lane expressway ramp. The ramp has small shoulders and concrete barriers on each side, and is used as a feeder from one expressway to another. The road curves to the right and runs on a downhill slope, the speed limit is 48 kmph (30 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.

Vehicle one, a 1993 Chevrolet Cavalier was traveling in the second lane of the ramp, when he departed the roadway on the left side and struck the concrete wall with the left front side of the vehicle. The vehicle then struck the left wall with the left rear side of the vehicle. Vehicle one then rotated, clockwise and rolled over two-quarter rolls left longitudinally. The vehicle came to final rest on its top facing north. Vehicle one was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

The driver of vehicle one, an eighteen-year-old male was heading home from work at the time of the crash. This driver had been driving for only a few minutes at the time of the crash. This driver did not say if the steering had been a problem before the crash but did state that once he got into the curve, he could not turn the wheel. This driver drives this vehicle daily and over this road on a weekly basis.

The critical pre-crash event for vehicle one for was coded as "this vehicle traveling: off the edge of the road on the left side". The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for vehicle one was coded as "vehicle related factor: steering failed".
