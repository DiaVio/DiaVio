This crash occurred at an intersection during daylight hours with clear, dry conditions and posted speed limits of 25mph/40kmph. Vehicle one (V1), a 2003 Cheverolet Trail Blazer, was northbound on a two lane, two way street and was travelling straight through an intersection. Vehicle two (V2), a 2002 Kia Sedona compact utiility vehile, was eastbound on a two lane, two way street crossing through the same intersection. The front of V2 impacted the left side of V1 in the intersection. V1 began to rotate counterclockwise post impact leaving tire scuff marks as evidence. V1 then began to roll over to the right and then on to its roof top as it departed the northeast corner of the intersection landing in a ditch. V1 came to final rest on its roof in the ditch as the front of V1 slightly impacted the front into a fire hydrant. V2 rotated slightly counterclockwise and came to rest facing east/northeast in the intersection. The case occupants are the driver of V1, who was a 47 year old female wearing a lap/shoulder belt and no airbag deployment, and the back right passenger, who was an 8 year old female who was seated on a bath towel folded four way layers on a low back booster seat while using the lap/shoulder belt. Both case occupants were transported to the trauma center. Both vehicles were towed due to damage.
