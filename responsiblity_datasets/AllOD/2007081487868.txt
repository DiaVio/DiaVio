This two-vehicle crash occurred in the morning daylight hours The location was a busy arterial in an area of commercial business. The roadway was a bituminous five lane with two lanes in each direction and a center left turn only lane. The crash is driveway related. The roadway is straight with a 2% grade. The weather was clear and dry and visibility was clear. The posted speed limit was 56 kmph (35 mph).

Vehicle one (V1), a 2000 Honda Accord sedan driven by a twenty nine (29) year old female, was stopped facing east with the intention of turning left into a private driveway. The westbound traffic was backed up, a non-contact vehicle left a gap and waved V1 to cross and turn left. Vehicle two (V2), a 2002 Saturn LWS300 4 door station wagon driven by a thirty four (34) year old male, was traveling westbound in lane one. As V1 attempted to cross lane one, the front of V2 impacted with the right front of V1. After the collision V1 was driven to a separate driveway and stopped to wait for aid and police. V1 was towed due to damage. V2 came to rest on the north sidewalk and was towed due to damage. The drivers of both vehicles were treated at the scene for injuries but not transported. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: this vehicle traveling, turning left at intersection. The critical reason was coded to V1 as a driver related factor: turned with obstructed view.

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded: other vehicle encroachment; from opposite direction, over the left lane line. A critical reason was not assigned to this vehicle.
