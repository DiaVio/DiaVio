This two-vehicle crash occurred in a four-legged intersection. Both roadways are straight, level, undivided and consist of one lane in each direction with parking on both sides. The speed limit on the east/west roadway is 40 kmph (25mph). This roadway is controlled by stop signs. The speed limit on the north/south roadway is 48 kmph (30mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.

Vehicle one (V1), a 2004 Volkswagen Jetta, was stopped eastbound in lane one. Vehicle two (V2), a 1996 Toyota Camry, was southbound in lane one. V1 entered the intersection and the front struck the right side of V2. V2 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The driver of V1, a forty-two-year-old female, was traveling from her home to a client's home. She was healthy and familiar with her vehicle. This driver works long hours at two jobs (fifty hours each week) and this was the first time she drove on this road. She stated that she was irritated at the time of the crash. She was trying to read the directions and thinking about how to get to her client's home.

The driver of V2, a forty-two-year-old female, was not interviewed; no further information is available for this driver.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "this vehicle traveling; crossing over (passing through) intersection." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "driver related factor; inadequate surveillance (e.g. failed to look, looked but did not see)." Associated factors were trees and a parked vehicle causing a sightline restriction.

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded as "other vehicle encroachment; from crossing street, across path." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V2 was "not coded to this vehicle."
