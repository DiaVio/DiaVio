This crash occurred on a two-lane two-way roadway. The roadway was straight, level, dry asphalt. The speed was posted at 89 kmph (55 mph). The roadway had gravel shoulders and the crash occurred at mid afternoon at midweek. The weather was sunny and clear.

As the vehicle a 2006 Pontiac G-6 traveled south in its lane the vehicle crossed the centerline the driver then over corrected moving back into the southbound lane over correcting again to rotate counterclockwise sliding off the left side of the roadway contacting the east side of the ditch rolling 2 quarter turns traveling 30 meters to come to final rest on the vehicles roof. The driver was removed from the vehicle and transported to a local trauma Unit due to injuries. The vehicle was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

A-17- year-old female stated she is in good health and does not take any medications drove the vehicle. She also stated she does not wear corrective lenses. She stated she had dental surgery to remove her wisdom teeth the day before and had taken prescribed medications after the surgery. She had not taken any medications in some 15 hours before the crash. She had gone to her work place to set up her work hours and then had gone to the hair salon. She stated she called her mother to let her know she was on her way home. Her mother stated the driver rarely drives home on this roadway and does not know why she drove this way on the day of the crash. The driver stated she does not remember any thing as to how the crash occurred.

The Critical Pre crash Event was coded "this vehicle control loss due to traveling too fast for conditions". The Critical Reason for the Critical Event was coded “inattention”. The associated factors were an unknown distraction because the driver had crossed the center yellow line before losing control of the vehicle.
