This single-vehicle crash occurred around dawn on a single lane exit ramp off of a six-lane interstate system. The exit ramp has a radius of 55m, a super elevation of 2%, it is bituminous, level and was dry at the time.

Vehicle one (V1), a 1997 Buick LeSabre with one occupant, was traveling west and exiting north to travel south. On the exit ramp, V1 lost traction and departed the west road edge in a clockwise yaw. V1 stuck a curve chevron sign with the left from before the wheels tripped on the sod and V1 rolled left four-quarter turn about its longitudinal axis. V1 came to final rest on its wheels facing east. The driver was transported to a local trauma center and the vehicle was towed due to disabling damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is No PAR obtained (created) for V1.

The 31 year-old male driver of V1 advised of his frustration with large trucks always being in his way at this exit. He attempted to accelerate around a truck and then enter the exit ramp at a high rate of speed. He is familiar with the vehicle and drives the route weekly. Medication consists of Methadone and Welbutrin SR. He was coming from his residence to a doctor's appointment and had driven about 15 miles when the crash occurred.

The critical pre-crash event was coded as: This vehicle control loss due to traveling too fast for conditions. The critical reason was coded: Driver related factor-too fast for curve/turn.
