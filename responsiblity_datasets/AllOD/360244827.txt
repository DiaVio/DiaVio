This severe frontal crash involved a 47 year old passenger who was seated in the second row right position, restrained with the manual lap/shoulder belt with a side curtain airbag deployment. He sustained serious injuries and was transported to a local hospital and then transferred to the trauma center. This severe off road crash occurred in a dark area while it was raining and the roadway surface was wet. Vehicle one (V1), a 2006 Honda Ridgeline, was travelling northbound in lane one of two one way lanes of a divided freeway with paved shoulders. V1 drove off and departed the roadway to the right into a grassy area with a slight downward slope. V1 continued to track in the grassy area and first impacted a shrubbery/bush (minor) before the front right of V1 impacted a tree (severe). V1 then rotated clockwise about 100 degrees and came to final rest facing east. V1 was towed due to damage. The case occupant is a 47 year old passenger in the second row right position. He was wearing the available manual lap/shoulder belt and was leaning to the right and sleeping at the time of impact. He sustained serious injuries and was transported to a local hospital and then transferred to the trauma center. The driver and another passenger were in V1. The driver was a 29 year old male who was wearing the manual lap/shoulder belt . His retractor pretensioner actuated and the frontal steering column mounted airbag deployed. He sustained minor injuries and was treated and released at the local hospital. The other passenger was seated in the front right position. He was a 21 year old male who was wearing the manual lap/shoulder belt and the retractor pretensioner actuated and the front instrument panel mounted, the side seatback, and the side roof rail airbags all deployed in his position on impact. This passenger was fatally injured and was not treated. Due to the severe integrity loss this front right passenger was also fully ejected and found near the vehicle at final rest.
