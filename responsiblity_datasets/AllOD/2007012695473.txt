The crash occurred on an undivided north/south two-lane bituminous roadway. This was a straight level roadway with a posted speed of 40 kmph (25 mph). It was early afternoon on a weekday and the road was dry and the sky was clear. Traffic was sparse.

V1, a 1996 Chevrolet Lumina four-door sedan with one occupant, was traveling northbound in lane one. The driver of V1 stated that he was traveling down the road and a litter of puppies ran into the roadway and he tried to avoid them by applying the brakes and steering left. The driver of V1 stated that the steering wheel and brakes locked up and he could not steer. V1 departed the roadway on the left side and contacted a tree with its front. It then spun counter-clockwise 90 degrees and came to final rest in the southbound lane facing southwest. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

V1 was driven by an 18-year old male. He reported injuries and was transported to a local trauma facility. He stated that he had just left the store and was on his way to his sister's house. He stated that he had been traveling between 50-64 kmph (31-40 mph) prior to impact.

The Critical Pre-crash Event for the driver of V1 was: This vehicle traveling off the edge of the roadway on the left hand side. The Critical Reason for the Critical Pre-crash Event was a driver related error: incorrect evasive action. Associated factors coded to this driver include traveling too fast, inexperience with the vehicle (he had only driven this vehicle twice), and driver inexperience. He did not have a driver's license. Also, the medical report indicated that the driver tested positive for THC.
