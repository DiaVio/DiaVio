This single-vehicle crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions. In the precrash area, the east/west roadway is undivided, straight and has one lane in each direction with parking on both sides. The eastbound lane has a downhill grade. In the crash area, this roadway passes through a tunnel under railroad tracks, with concrete pillars on both sides and in the center of the roadway. The speed limit for this roadway is 56 kmph (35 mph).

Vehicle one (V1), a 2007 Nissan Sentra, was eastbound in lane one. V1 traveled off the right side of the road and the front struck a curb and a concrete pillar. V1 then rolled to the left two quarter-turns, sliding across the travel lane. The front of V1 then struck the curb in the median. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is No PAR obtained (created) for V1.

The driver of V1, a forty-two-year-old male, was on his way to his grandma's house. He was healthy, familiar with his vehicle and travels this route weekly. This driver had been sleeping in his car for a couple of weeks since he was evicted from his house. He stated that he was sleeping when his vehicle traveled off the road but was unsure how long he had been sleeping.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "this vehicle traveling; off the edge of the road on the right side." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "driver related factor; sleeping, that is, actually asleep."
