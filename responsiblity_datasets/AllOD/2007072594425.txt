This two-vehicle crash occurred on a jersey barrier-divided, straight trafficway. The northbound lanes are level and consist of five through lanes with shoulders on both sides. The speed limit for this roadway is 89 kmph (55 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.

Vehicle one (V1), a 1998 Nissan Maxima, was northbound in lane one. Vehicle two (V2), a 2006 Ford Econoline van, in use as an ambulance, was northbound in lane one in front of V1. V2 stopped and the front of V1 struck the back of V2. V1 was towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The driver of V1, a thirty-year-old male, was not interviewed. No further information is available for this driver.

The driver of V2, a twenty-year-old male, had picked up two passengers and was on the way to a hospital. This driver was healthy, well rested and comfortable with his vehicle and passengers. He travels this route daily. This driver stated that he did not see V1 prior to the crash.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "other motor vehicle in lane; other vehicle stopped." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded as "driver related factor; type of driver error unknown."

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded as "other motor vehicle in lane; traveling in same direction with higher speed." The critical reason for the critical pre-crash event for V2 was "not coded to this vehicle."
