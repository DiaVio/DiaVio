This crash involved a single vehicle that rolled over on a two way, two lane divided asphalt roadway. The time of the crash was approximately 1330 hours. There were no roadway deficiencies and sight line restrictions noted. There is a depresses dirt median separating the westbound and eastbound lanes. There were no adverse weather conditions and the weather was clear. The speed limit is 104 KMPH/65 MPH

V1, a 2005 GMC Canyon SUV, driven by a 23 year-old male was traveling westbound in the number two lane when it traveled off the left side of the roadway. The vehicle then entered the roadway to the right and began to rotate clockwise. The vehicle continued to travel across both westbound lanes and eventually traveled off the right side of the roadway. The vehicle traveled up the soft dirt slope when it furrowed in the soft dirt and tripped at the base of a large bush and rolled to the left longitudinally 2 quarter turns. The vehicle then rolled to the right 1 quarter turn coming to rest on it's left side. The vehicle was towed due to damage. The driver was transported to a medical facility. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

The critical pre crash event was coded "this vehicle traveling off the edge of the road to the left." The critical reason for the critical pre crash event was coded "Driver related factor-External distraction." The driver was looking down to his left at an unknown noise coming from his vehicle. The driver stated that he heard a grinding noise coming from his vehicle as he was driving.
