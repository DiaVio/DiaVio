This moderate severity near-side impact crash involved a 35 year old male driver who sustained serious injuries and was wearing a manual lap/shoulder belt, This crash occurred at night with street lights on a two lane, one way roadway that curves to the right with an upgrade. It was raining and the concrete surface was wet. The left edge of the travel lanes are bordered by a narrow paved shoulder with a concrete traffic barrier and the right side with a raised curb and a sidewalk. Vehicle one (V1), a 2004 Toyota Tacoma four door pickup truck was traveling northeasterly in lane two and the driver lost control and V1 began to rotate clockwise. V1 crossed over lane one and departed the roadway over the curb and sidewalk continuing to rotate clockwise almost 90 degrees. The front of V1 impacted a breakaway pole just before the left side impacted a second utility pole directly into the passenger compartment area of V1. V1 was towed due to damage. The driver of V1 is the case occupant who is a 35 year old male who was wearing a manual lap/shoulder belt with no airbag deployments. He sustained serious injuries and was transported to a trauma center. No other occupants were in the vehicle.
