This crash occurred in the intersection of two similar roadways, both being two-lane, two-way, non-divided asphalt rural roadways with a posted speed limit of 40Kmh (25Mph). The crash occurred during daylight hours, and there were no adverse conditions present at the time of the crash.

V1 a 1999 Ford Windstar was stopped facing northbound at the intersection waiting to turn left. V2 a 2006 GMC Yukon XL was traveling southbound approaching the intersection. V1 turned left at the intersection in front of V2, and was contacted in the right side of the vehicle by the front bumper of V2. V1 was towed due to damage, and V2 was towed by owners request. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The driver of V1 a 79yr old male driver stated that he saw V2 coming, but thought the intersection was a four-way stop. He then stated that he had driven on the roadway before, but it had been awhile since he was last on it.

The Critical Pre-Crash event coded for V1 is "this vehicle traveling, turning left at an intersection". The Critical Reason for the Critical Pre-Crash Event coded for V1 is "False Assumption (thought other vehicle would yield the right of way)".

The driver of V2 a 37yr old female driver stated the she saw that V1 had stopped at the intersection, and did not think that they were going to turn in front of her. She then stated that they turned at the last second in front of her, and she hit the brakes but could not avoid the collision.

The Critical Pre-Crash event coded for V2 is " other vehicle encroachment from opposite direction over left lane line". V2 was not assigned a Critical Reason.
