V1, a 2000 GMC Jimmy was traveling westbound on a seven-lane, two-way, wet asphalt roadway, with a level grade in daylight conditions. V1 had made several lane changes, passing slower traffic. On the last pass, another non-contact vehicle was encroaching into his lane. He swerved right to miss the vehicle and departed the roadway on the right side. V1 rolled left; one quarter turn and spun counterclockwise on his left side before coming to final rest off the north side of the road.

The driver of V1 was a 23-year old male that reported that he had been traveling 82-97kmph (51-60mph) prior to the crash. He had a medical history of brain tumors and was taking medication to treat it. The medication has possible side affects of drowsiness, mental confusion, dizziness, insomnia, double vision and tiredness. He was rested and traveling to the bank. He stated that he was in a hurry to get to the bank before it closed. He sustained minor injuries from the crash, but refused treatment. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

The Critical Precrash Event for the driver of V1 was this vehicle control loss due to poor road conditions, rain and wet roadway. The Critical Reason for the Critical Precrash Event was a decision error, too fast for conditions to be able to respond to unexpected actions of other road users.
