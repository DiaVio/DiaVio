This case involves a frontal impact between a passenger car and a concrete barrier, which led the car to role onto its right side. The case occupant is the unrestrained 50-year-old female driver. She had the benefit of a deployed steering- wheel mounted air bag. The case vehicle, a 2008 four-door Toyota Camry was traveling in the inside northbound lane of a dry, uphill grade >2%, concrete, divided six-lane traffic-way. It was daylight and clear. The driver lost control of the vehicle crossed though the center and outside northbound lanes entered the distress lane and struck a concrete barrier in an oblique, off-set frontal mode. After impact the vehicle rolled right-side leading three-quarter turns. The driver was the only occupant of the case vehicle. She was not restrained by a three-point belt but she had the benefit of the deployed steering-wheel mounted airbag. She was transported to a level one trauma center with serious injuries and enrolled as a case occupant.
