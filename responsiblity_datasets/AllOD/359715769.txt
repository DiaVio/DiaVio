This near side crash involved a case occupant who is a 48 year old female driver, involved in a moderate left side impact. At the time of the crash she was wearing the available manual lap/shoulder belt. On impact her outboard seat mounted side impact and left roof rail side curtain air bag deployed. She sustained critical injuries. This crash occurred on a divided interstate freeway during daylight hours, under rainy weather conditions and wet concrete travel lanes. In this area, there are three westbound lanes that are separated from the two eastbound lanes by a wide grass and dirt median. Vehicle one (V1), a 2013 Honda Civic two door coupe, was westbound in lane two of the three westbound lanes. Vehicle two (V2), a 2008 Chevrolet Silverado large pickup, was traveling west in the inside westbound lane. Both vehicles were traveling uphill and as the vehicles neared the top, the driver of V1 lost control. V1 began to rotate counterclockwise as it moved south into V2's travel path. The front of V2 impacted the left side of V1, resulting in the actuation of V1's driver's seat belt retractor pretensioner and the deployment of the left side seat back air bag and left side curtain. Both vehicles traveled in a southwesterly direction, off the south edge of the roadway and into the grass and dirt median. V1 and V2 both came to final rest in the median, facing southwest. Both vehicles were towed due to damage. The case occupant is the driver of V1, a 48 year old female, restrained with the available manual lap/shoulder belt. Her outboard seat back mounted side air bag and left roof side rail mounted curtain air bag deployed during the crash. This driver sustained critical njuries and was transported to an outside hospital prior to being transferred to the trauma center, where she was hospitalized for thirteen days. There were no other occupants in the vehicle. The driver and front right passenger in V2 were reportedly not injured.
