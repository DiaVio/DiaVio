This case involves a right side crash event resulting in serious injuries to the right front seated case occupant. Vehicle one (V1 - case vehicle), a 2001 Saturn SL, 4dr passenger car, was traveling south in the southbound lane of a level four-lane roadway, two lane each way and vehicle two (V2), a 1993 Toyota Corolla, 4dr passenger car, was traveling north in the opposite direction of the same roadway. The collision occurred at a T-type intersection, the cross road connects the main road on the east side. The intersection is controlled by a stop sign for the connecting roadway and no traffic control for north-south roadway. It was daylight, the weather was clear and the bituminous (asphalt) road was dry during the afternoon hours. The posted speed limit on this road is 35 mph (56 km/h) both ways. The crash occurred when V1 made a left turn at the intersection onto the eastbound roadway. The frontal plane of V2 impacted the right side plane of V1. Upon impact, V1 rotated clockwise and came to rest facing south in the northtbound lane at the north-east corner of the intersection. V2 came to rest facing east on the northbound roadway at the intersection. The79-year-old male driver of V1 (non-case occupant) was using the available three-point seat belt and the steering-wheel frontal air bag deployed. The 77-year-old female in the right front passenger seat (case occupant) was using the available three-point seat belt and the passenger frontal airbag deployed. Only the case occupant was transported by the ground transportation to the nearest hospital with serious injuries and was later transported to trauma center.
