This single vehicle crash occurred on a two way, two-lane, undivided asphalt constructed roadway. This roadway runs north to south with a posted speed of 80 kmph (50 mph) and has a right hand curve with a radius of 375 meters (1,230 feet) just prior to this event. This crash occurred during the early afternoon hours with cloudy skies and a light rain. This roadway has various curves and grades with rock-faced embankments on both sides.

Vehicle one (V1), a 2005 Dodge Ram 1500 pickup with one occupant, was traveling south. A 62-year-old male was driving the vehicle when the right front tire blew and the vehicle veered to the right, off of the roadway. Once off the road edge this vehicle impacted a vertical rock embankment with its front right plane. V1 rotated clockwise and came to rest against the embankment straddling the southbound travel lane. The driver was slightly injured and was transported to a local hospital where he was treated and released. The driver stated that he is retired military and was traveling home from a visit with his mother. He is single and was not under any stress and was not ill prior to this crash. He does suffer from a sleep disorder for which he does take a medication. Medication includes Glipizide, Prednisone, Trazodone, Lorazepam and Strtaline. He stated that he was not in any hurry to arrive home and that he was familiar with this roadway and the vehicle. He stated that he was doing around the speed limit when he heard then felt the blowout of the right front tire occurring. The vehicle was towed from the scene. A witness to the crash stated that she was going in the opposite direction and witnessed the tire failure just as she was passing V1. Police Reported Travel Speed is 80 kmph for V1.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded “this vehicle loss of control due to- blow out/flat tire”. The critical reason was coded “vehicle related factor- tires/wheels failed”.
