V1, a 2002 Chevrolet Impala, was traveling southbound on a three-lane interstate divided by a concrete median barrier. The roadway was mostly dry with intermittent patches of ice. The crash occurred in the pre-dawn hours of the morning, and the roadway was illuminated by streetlamps. The posted speed limit was 105kmph (65mph). V1 was traveling in lane two when it encountered a patch of ice and lost control. V1 departed the roadway on the left side and its left side struck the barrier wall before coming to final rest off the roadway.

The driver of V1 was a 44-year old female who reported that she had been traveling between 98-113kmph (61-70mph) prior to the crash. She had no history of health-related problems and claimed that she had taken no medication prior to the crash. She said she was rested and traveling to work. She does not wear prescription lenses. She sustained minor injuries and was transported to a local trauma facility. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1.

The Critical Pre-crash Event for the driver of V1 was this vehicle traveling off the edge of the road on the left side. The Critical Reason for the Critical Pre-crash Event was a decision error, too fast for conditions (ice). The EDR reading indicated that five seconds before the crash, V1 was traveling at 80 mph. Associated factors coded to this driver were the use of opiates (this showed up in the drug screen test), relative unfamiliarity with the vehicle, low tread on the tires, and the slick roadway.
