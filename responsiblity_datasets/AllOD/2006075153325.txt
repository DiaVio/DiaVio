This single-vehicle rollover occurred during daylight hours on a weekday afternoon. The crash occurred on a curved, level stretch of a three-lane, positively divided, concrete, north-south expressway. At the time of the crash, it was snowing and the roadway was covered with snow and slush. The speed limit in the area of the crash is 105 kmph (65 mph).

Vehicle one (V1), a 1986 Ford Ranger regular-cab 4x4 pickup, was traveling northbound in the second lane, curving rightward. V1 changed lanes into the first lane and lost traction on the slick surface. V1 rotated clockwise and departed the road to the right, where V1 rolled five quarter-turns towards its left side, coming to rest on the roadside with its left plane in contact with the ground. Police Reported Travel Speed is 72 kmph for V1.

The 39-year-old male driver of V1 reported that he was on his way to school from a friend's house when the crash occurred. He stated that a car moved in front of him from the third lane, and he was trying to get into the right lane to get clear of them when he lost control. He stated being rested, drives this roadway weekly and is very comfortable with the vehicle. The driver is in good health and does not take any medication. He does, however, need to wear contacts, which were in at the time of the cash. The critical precrash event for V1 was coded: This vehicle loss of control due to poor road conditions (ice, snow). The critical reason for the event was attributed to V1, and was coded: Environmental-related factor, slick roads (low friction road surface due to ice, loose debris, any other cause).
