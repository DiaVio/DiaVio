This two-vehicle crash occurred mid-morning during daylight hours. The location was a junction between a busy interstate highway, and a state route highway. The roadway was a bituminous divided roadway, with five lanes in the northbound direction, with two of those lanes exiting to the other highway. The weather was clear and dry with good visibility. The posted speed limit was 97 kmph (60 mph).

Vehicle one (V1) was a 2002 BMW 325i sedan driven by a twenty nine (29) year old male. V1 was traveling northbound exiting an interstate highway onto the on-ramp to a state route highway, in lane one. Vehicle two (V2), a 2006 BMW 330i sedan driven by a thirty three (33) year old female, was traveling northbound in lane one, ahead of V1. V2 slowed down quickly as the traffic ahead did, V1 was looking down at a ringing cell phone and when he looked up, he was unable to stop before impacting the rear of V2. V1 drove over to the right shoulder, and was towed from there. V2 was driven from the scene. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.

The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: other motor vehicle in lane, traveling in the same direction while decelerating. The critical reason for the critical event was coded to V1 s a driver related factor: internal distraction, for reaching for the cell phone. The driver only had four hours of sleep the previous evening and worked 80 hours the previous seven day period, so fatigue was considered a factor.

The critical pre-crash event for V2 was coded: other motor vehicle in lane, traveling in same direction with higher speed. A critical reason was not assigned to this vehicle.
